User Manual

General Information:

Decidedly slim and sleekly refined, the PowerShot SX210 IS packs a feature set that reads like a wish list. The 14.1 Megapixel CCD joins a 14x 28mm Wide-Angle Zoom for imaging power to spare. Frame your shots in the bright 3.0-inch widescreen PureColor System LCD, with the Low Light mode for sharp images in dimly-lit situations. It also captures stunning HD movies with Dynamic mode for enhanced image stabilization when shooting movies using wide-angle settings. Capture smooth HD video (720p) with stereo sound for a truly natural, high-quality HD experience. The PowerShot SX210 IS not only goes wherever you do, but takes your photography and video to a whole new place, too.

    I've had the camera for about a month, it replaces an Olympus SP-350. I liked the Oly with the exception of long shutter lag in low light conditions and still use it for work photos.

The metering system and autofocus work well. I love the 14x zoom and the stabilization works well at full zoom.

    The video is suprisingly good although the sound is a little weak.

    Overall I am very happy with this purchase.

    3.0

    Review from: www.amazon.com
    review date: 2010-09-23

    I got this camera to "upgrade" from my Canon sd 780is. What my main priority was a camera with HD video capability, low light photos, and the quickness in which i can take photos. Unfortunately, for this camera it only excelled in the video category. It takes great HD video and the speaker quality is great as well, but the video quality wasn't that much better than my old Sd 780 (if at all). While video recording it tended to focus in and out often which was very frustrating. It was a pleasant addition to have the opportunity to be able to have manual controls on a digital camera since i'm a die hard SLR fan. The megazoom was really great, and fun to play with. But that is where the positives end with me.

    The camera quality sucks, the photos came out pale and washed out, even soft looking (indoors and outdoors). I tried playing with the settings and I could get better shots, but for those quick shots, it fails. FYI: it will NOT catch anything that is moving, trying to catch up with my 2 yr old goddaughter was near impossible. I got better photos from my 2 year old Canon SD 870. The flash is terrible: it's terribly placed and its frankly annoying to have to push it down when i don't want an overbearing flash to flood my photos. It is a larger camera, fairly heavy, and my hands are small so I found myself holding it in an awkward position and fidgiting often to find a comfortable position to take photos. While the screen is large, I found that the photos always looked grainy.

    This camera is perfect on paper, it has all the specs to be a great camera. But the quality of it just isn't any better than Canon's older digital cameras which simply doesn't justify the price. I'd go grab the Canon S95 instead.
