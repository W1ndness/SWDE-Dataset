General Information:

Soaking. Freezing. Dropping. Adventurous meets clumsy in the waterproof, coldproof and now shockproof Optio W80. Dunk it, freeze it, drop it, and it will keep coming back for more. Featuring 12 megapixels, a 5X internal optical zoom, a slim one inch body and a wide angle 28mm lens. The Optio W80 also captures widescreen HD movie capture that records up to 1280x720 pixels at full-speed 30 frames per second. Add to this Faster Face Detection, Super Macro mode, and a 2.5 inch screen with anti-reflective coating that makes it easy to view in bright sunlight.

    5.0

    Review from: www.amazon.com
    review date: 2010-08-03

    I bought this camera thinking I was getting a decent value for my money. I purchased it, based on reviews, thinking that I was making a sacrifice in picture quality, especially in low light settings. I am very pleased that this camera has a lot more versatility than I originally expected. If left on the default settings, yes, the pictures come out a bit noisy in low light, but if the ISO is dropped down and the camera is placed steadily to avoid blur during a longer exposure time, it does well. I prefer flash-free shots when I can get them, and I have even taken some shots that I really like, indoors and flash-free with this little camera. Another thing to keep in mind that I've noticed is sometimes a shot looks a lot noisier on the little built-in LCD screen than it will on the computer, so that isn't always a good judge! I have not printed any images yet, so I'm not sure how the quality will be there. There are also a lot of fun built-in editing tools, like image filters, red-eye removal, and some silly frames (my 5-year-old thinks these are hilarious). So even when I get a shot that is so-so, I find I can doctor it just a little using the built-in filters and end up with an image that I love.

    Another thing folks mentioned was battery life, but I am getting more than a day of shooting (and that is with me experimenting, so I've been taking lots and lots of pictures), and using the camera for picture editing (another really cool feature). To me that's plenty of use on a battery, but I suppose it is a personal preference thing. I do have a back-up, which makes life easier, but I don't think it's absolutely necessary.

    Sure, it is no SLR, but I think it performs great considering a)the cost, b)the compact design, and c)the fact the it is marketed as a weather resistant camera, not necessarily as a quality camera. This is by far the best point and shoot camera I've ever owned, and I think it will do a great job as our 'easy' camera when our SLR is inconvenient to use.

    Overall: I feel that I got a great value with this camera! Among all the weather resistant cameras out there, I am very glad I chose this one.

    PROS: Waterproof works great, Decent battery life, Lots of options to operate manually makes it possible to get decent image quality, Literally fits in my pocket, My daughter can use it and I don't worry about it breaking, Built-in editing, etc...

    CONS: Lots of menus to go through to get the settings that work...must practice with it before going out to take serious pictures

    4.0

    Review from: www.amazon.com
    review date: 2010-07-01

    I liked most of the features of the W80. Like others, I noticed short battery life is the worst thing. Pretty easy to fix, just buy a couple of extra batterys and keep one on the charger.

    Fits in a shirt pocket (or those shoulder pockets of an ACU, hua!) Don't have to worry about splashes by the pool, but watch out for high temps in the sun. Of course that was true in the 35mm days, so no real difference.

    One of the one-star reviews said all pictures were fuzzy. Low-light without flash is not the greatest. But low-light (high ISO) is a problem for the high dollar DSLRs. There is a setting on W80 besides the pixel setting that affects quality. It makes a sharper picture, but uses more memory. No problem, with an 8GB chip, I can shoot about 2,000 before I run out of memory. It really does make the picture sharper.

    I used a card reader to get the files into my computer. I like having a $10 card reader + regular USB cable instead of the $200 camera + proprietary USB cable for that purpose.

    The camera makes JPGs for stills and AVIs for movies. The files are compatible with everything. I was able to edit the AVIs with Nero and make a DVD. When you make a DVD, you lose a small amount of quality. If I had a BluRay burner, I would make BluRays to keep the HD quality.

    Don't expect this to replace a Nikon D or Canon EOS camera. It won't. But if you want a camera that will survive the water park, canoeing, beach, hiking, etc., this is a good buy.

    4.0

    Review from: www.amazon.com
    review date: 2010-06-27

    I'm a bit of a camera junkie - I've owned at least a dozen cameras including SLRs as well as point-and-shoot (P+S). My biggest gripe with P+S is that eventually dust or moisture will get inside the camera resulting in total failure or at the very least, dust will land on the image sensor rendering the camera useless. A sealed water-proof camera is the answer for me even though I have no intention of going underwater with it... still, I hike and backpack so mist and rain are just as much an issue for me.

    As for the graininess of the images - most of this can be avoided by learning how the camera settings affect the final image. For such a small camera, there are an amazing (daunting?) number of options so the beginning user may not find this a good match for them.

    The triple-shake-reduction method was a pleasant surprise for me (but yes, it does introduce a fair amount of image noise). I often shoot in low-light conditions without the flash and using other cameras, my usability success rate was under 50%. With this camera it's upwards of 95% success. For me, I'd rather have sharp yet "grainy" images than blurry "fine-grained" images that end up totally useless. It's a choice I can live with.

    About battery consumption - with all the image processing that goes on to get sharp images, it does eat through the batteries. BUT - the batteries are incredibly small and to carry three or more extras is just not an issue. I bought the "generic" brand of batteries so really they didn't cost all that much.

    Bottom line is that this camera is probably NOT meant for the beginner. It has features and functions that make it invaluable in certain conditions and if you need those features, you'll love this camera.
