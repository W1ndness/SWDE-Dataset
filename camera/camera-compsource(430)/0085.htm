    5.0

    Review from: www.amazon.com
    review date: 2010-08-09

    I bought this as an everyday complement to my Canon DSLR, which is excellent, but too big to carry around most of the time. I wanted a high quality, high zoom camera that would fit in my pocket. The size of the Panasonic is great- it's one of the smallest cameras with a 7x or larger zoom. I liked the Canon SX210, but with its widescreen LCD, I thought it was a little too big. 8X is at the low end of the zoom range I was looking for, but it works pretty well. With a 14MP image, I can always crop a little in software to zoom in tighter and still maintain good quality.

    The quality of the ZR3 is excellent. Outdoor shots in sunlight are excellent. Indoors shots are very good. Like most compact cameras, you don't have as much control as you'd like. There are about 20 scene modes, but no easy way to simply set the aperture to its widest setting to get a fast shutter speed. One feature I like is that you can put a max limit on the ISO speed so the camera won't jack it way up in its search for a fast shutter speed. That usually inserts considerable noise in the picture. I set the max ISO at 400 and noise is excellent. I haven't shot much above that, so can't comment on noisiness at higher ISOs. However, almost all compacts start to degrade above 400.

    Video quality is very good. You can zoom while filming, as some newer cameras can now do, which is a great feature. It has the space-saving ACVHD format, although I use the motion JPEG because it's hard to find software to edit the ACVHD.

    The wide zoom of 25mm comes in very handy when trying to get all of an indoor scene or a big group of people.

    I like this so much and the portability is so good, that I find I use this camera for most of my needs. I'll use my SLR for shooting my kids sporting events or if it's low light and I need the high ISO capability of the SLR. But it's so nice to have a high quality option that fits in my pocket. I'd highly recommend this.

    4.0

    Review from: www.amazon.com
    review date: 2010-07-29

    I previously owned an earlier generation Panasonic Lumix compact camera and was very happy with it. I did a lot of research prior to purchasing the DMC ZR3 and have learned that over the past few years Panasonic has made quite a name for itself in the camera industry. The primary reason for me upgrading was that newer cameras have HD recording capabilities as does the DMC ZR3 and also I was attracted to the large optical zoom capability (8X) with such a compact camera. It is only slightly larger (hardly noticeable) than my previous Lumix, which I bought largely for its compact size.

    Pictures are nice quality. Like any camera you buy -- especially compact -- pictures with a lot of natural light are going to turn out the best. If you plan to take a lot of pictures of scenery or the outdoors during your traveling I would recommend this camera. It takes very nice quality photos outdoors. Indoor photos are not quite as sharp generally, but sometimes they can turn out pretty good if you are able to have a decent light source. honestly, sometimes I force the flash off if there is enough artificial light to make due.

    The HD video pretty much follows suit with the still photo quality. Best in good natural light situations, tending to be grainy in low light. And because it is video as opposed to still frame, the granularity is more pronounced as is to be expected with probably any camera until you get into professional grade (and big bucks). I find the ability to connect to an HD TV using a mini-HDMI cable very handy. You should be able to find a generic mini-HDMI cable online for less than $10. You should realize that when using the HDACV Lite mode of recording, it works nice for TV playback, but is not as PC friendly as it requires the included Panasonic software to load that format onto your PC. You do have the option of recording in the less memory-efficient motion-JPEG format which will allow you to load directly to a PC without software, making videos more conducive for sharing.

    I have found the strong zoom to be good quality in terms of maintaining sharpness of the subject. When you have the zoom at maximum or a very high level, you have to keep it very steady -- especially in lower light. I have not yet attempted using it with a tripod, but I am confident that would produce quality results.

    Bottom line is that I paid less for this camera than I did my first Panasonic Lumix (which I really liked) and it has a lot of nice recent technological improvements which I find very useful. It's well worth the money.
