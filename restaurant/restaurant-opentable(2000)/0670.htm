Las Palmas Restaurant

writes...
  • Las Palmas is a family owned and operated business with strong ties to the city. From East Rogers Park to Wicker Park, we identify with the strain of this urban metropolis and feel a vested interest in providing a communal place for the essentials of human activities. Tucked into a tiny alcove in one of this city’s trendiest neighborhoods, Las Palmas remains one of Bucktown’s best-kept secrets. Upon entering the restaurant one’s senses are inundated with the vibrant colors of local and international artwork and the festive or melancholy sounds of live traditional music. Las Palmas serves spirited Mexican cuisine in a decidedly upscale, but refreshingly unpretentious environment. Chef Armando Gonzalez provides an exquisite selection of courses made from the freshest ingredients with solid roots in Mexican culinary heritage yet with a penchant for inventive whimsy.

Pick any of our stunning salons and from any of our various dinning packages to throw a party that your friends, family and acquaintances will always remember. Rooms La Cueva (Front dining room): For those of you who prefer not to be too far from the bar; this cozy front dining room, warmed by a fireplace, is affectionately called La Cueva, the cave, because of its dim romantic lighting at night. La Cueva has become famous for its art. Alfonso Nieves’s feathered serpent Aztec Deity, Quetzalcoatl, winds its way along the ceiling and doubles as a fallen palm. Pick out the famous and controversial faces on a Frida Kahlo’s Moises and the Solaris. Atrium: Entirely encased in glass windows. You can dine or dance in this sun bathed room by day or under the city lights by night. Outdoor Patio: Available weather permitting.
