Saddle Peak Lodge

writes...
  • Saddle Peak Lodge where rustic ambiance meets a sophisticated palate. Saddle Peak Lodge has existed in some form or another for nearly 125 years. Rich history, timeless style and contemporary cuisine. Chef's Adam Horton and Chris Kufek prepare French influenced contemporary American cuisine. With an emphasis on hearty wild game preparations. There is something for everyone with light flavorful seafood dishes as well as delicious vegetarian options. Our resident Mixologist prepares classic prohibition era cocktails as well as modern versions of some classics. The wine list focuses on American boutique wines difficult to find on many other lists. We also offer a selection of French Champagne and european dessert wines. *Saddle Peak Lodge dress code is business casual. Jacket and ties are not required but absolutely no shorts or beach wear. Please be sure to share this information with all members in your party. Thank you, we look forward to seeing you soon.

Saddle Peak Lodge Reviews
 

Facilities 1 of 5
  • Main Dining Room

    The main dining room is our most popular room. This room is known for its large stone fireplace and cozy seating. The French doors at the end of the room can be opened, allowing you to combine the space with our beautiful scenic patio.

    Capacity
    12575
  • Patio

    The patio possesses cascading fountains, serene gardens and gorgeous mountain views. Get away from the hustle and bustle of the city under the stars with the quiet hum of the fountains in the background. The patio can host parties of up to 100 and is positively charming for Sunday brunch events.

    Capacity
    130100
  • Library

    The library has gotten its name for the impressive collection of unusual antique books found on its shelves. When holding an event in the library we can include the adjoining terrace which serves as a classy location to sip cocktails and watch the sunset.

    Capacity
    6045
  • Den

    The den is our most intimate and cozy room. Perfect for small gatherings or business meetings, the den overlooks the mountains from the second floor and has a unique collection of novel boats adorning its walls.

    Capacity
    2016
  • Loft

    The loft is most private of our spaces and occupies the top third floor. The loft has a panoramic view of the Santa Monica Mountains and an array of old knick-knacks displayed on its shelves. The loft may be set as a private changing room for a bride and her party during weddings.    French doors in the loft open onto a stone path leading you past our flowing waterfall to the romantic ceremony site.

The beautiful location, paired with our award-winning cuisine and decor of rustic elegance, is the perfect location for your wedding ceremony and or wedding reception.
