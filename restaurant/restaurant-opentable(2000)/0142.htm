Grey Social Bar + Restaurant

writes...
  • Grey is spinning nostalgia sexy, rumored to have operated as a speakeasy for the elite who worked & lived in the city. Exposed brick is a glimpse into the building’s past, but the dining room is a warm, contemporary space. The 2nd floor has been opened up to the soaring tin ceiling 40’ ceilings with a one of a kind chandelier above the dining room. Custom fixtures for the tables recall early 20th century. The interior is handmade fabrications made locally & abroad. The highlight of the dining area is a 2 story wall fabricated of leather panels, & etched glass. The 2nd level is accessible via a door adjacent to the host stand at the entry, or by separate entry at the storefront for private & special events. Upstairs glass panels open into a front room designed for large parties & private events. Vintage wallpapers & photos surround a leather banquette perfect for small plates & sharing. A glass wall overlooks the room below & the two-story leather & glass feature wall.

Dinner is Italian Tapa's or as we are call "cicchetti" Small servings of fish, tiny sandwiches, plates of olives, and very small servings of typical full-course plates. Like Spanish tapas, one can also make a meal of cicchetti by ordering many different plates. Venice’s many cicchetti bars are quite active during the day, as Venetians typically eat cicchetti in the late morning, for lunch, or as afternoon snacks. Cicchetti is usually accompanied by a small glass of white wine, which the locals refer to as an “ombra” or Shadow.
