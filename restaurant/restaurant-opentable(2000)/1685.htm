Benjamin Steakhouse

writes...
  • Peter Luger chef of 20 years, Arturo McLeod left to open his own restaurant - Benjamin Steakhouse. For serious steak-connoisseurs, Benjamin is a must visit. The restaurant, located in the heart of Manhattan, offers the finest and carefully selected prime USDA meat, dry aged to perfection. Wide selection of wines and vintages for pairing. The 5,000 square foot restaurant features a ten-foot working fireplace, rich chocolate brown leather seating, oak wood paneling, vaulted decorative ceiling, and modern brass amber toned chandeliers.

Benjamin Steakhouse Reviews
 

Facilities 1 of 3
  • Main Dining Room

    • DescriptionOur main dining room, which seats up to 120 guests, features a 10-foot roaring fireplace along with high vaulted ceilings and dark wood paneling. To book the entire main dining room, a guarantee of 100 guests is required. Alternatively, you may book a portion of the dining room, and thus not have to commit to guaranteeing a minimum larger than your group.

  • Mezzanine Level

    The Mezzanine Level, seating a maximum of 66 guests, is situated on the 2nd floor of our restaurant, overlooking the main dining room in the form of a balcony. While the Mezzanine event space feeds from the energy of the main dining room, at the same time it offers a sense of intimacy and warmth, which is ideal for all manner of celebratory occasions, as well as casual dinners.

  • Third Floor Conference Room

    The Third Floor Conference Room, seating a maximum of 50 guests, is ideal for events where a “behind closed doors setting” is required, such as business meetings where speakers are involved, presentations dinners, etc. The Third floor conference room also offers full AV equipment upon request, and is always subject to availability, as it is in fact property of the Dylan hotel.
