Four Square Restaurant

writes...
  • Four Square is located in the Bartlett Mangum House, a restored 1908 Victorian home listed on the National Register of Historic Places. Outside, massive Doric columns flank the entrance. A wraparound porch, second floor balcony, and slate roof capped by iron scrollwork complete the picture. Inside, the intimate dining rooms each have their original coal burning fireplaces with tiled, carved wood and mirrored mantelpieces. Leaded and stained glass windows and beautiful woodwork can be found throughout. Our menu changes bi-monthly to reflect our commitment to bringing diners the freshest and most seasonal products available. We rely on local famers and purveyors to bring us the region’s best, from vegetables and grains to seafood and meats. We have a passion for food at Four Square, and we hope you sense it when you dine with us. Food nourishes both body and soul. It comforts, titillates, and engages four of our five senses. It has history, tradition and brings people together!

  • Private Party Facilities: Four Square is unique in the Triangle area in its ability to offer flexible private dining areas for groups of varying sizes. Our smallest private room seats 8 to 10 guests at a large round table, perfect for board meetings or small family gatherings. Parties of 12 to 24 can be accomodated in each of our two mid-sized rooms, and parties of 25 to 40 may reserve our largest private room. For larger parties, the second floor can accommodate as many as 75 and the entire restaurant may be reserved for up to 125 for a seated dinner. If used as a stand-up cocktail/reception the restaurant can accommodate 200. Please call the restaurant directly to book private parties.
