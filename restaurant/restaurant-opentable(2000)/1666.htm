Aureole - Bar Room

writes...
  • Charlie Palmer's signature Progressive American cuisine first took root in the original townhouse location of Aureole, where the chef made an early commitment to farm over factory food. Today, Aureole continues to shine at One Bryant Park under the kitchen leadership of Michelin-starred Executive Chef Christopher Lee. Dining at the contemporary Aureole is staged in two venues: the formal dining room and more casual bar room. Designed with modern conviviality in mind, the smart, casual bar room offers a la carte access to many selections that share the same style and ingredients as tasting menu dishes from the Dining Room. Or, select from a list of inventive “bar snacks” for lunch and dinner, followed by indulgent desserts from pastry chef Jennifer Yee. Reservations can be made here for the Bar Room only. To be seated in the Dining Room, please visit the Aureole Dining Room.

Consistent with the fundamental idea of radiance, light plays a key role in the design, with bespoke sculptural back-lit flame sconces wrapping the room, creating a warm glow. Behind these elegant sconces, walls are rhythmically composed of fabric panels and silver leaf wall covering. A recessed ceiling cone—13 feet in diameter and 7 feet deep—houses a custom chandelier constructed of silver acrylic tiles.
