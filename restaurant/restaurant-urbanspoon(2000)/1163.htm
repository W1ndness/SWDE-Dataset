The Name Pretty Much Says It All
by David from Culver City (61 reviews)
June 13, 2010 - Likes it
Kudos to Extraordinary Desserts for picking an immodest name for their establishment and living up to it consistently. Everything tastes as incredible as it looks, and the pastries, especially the cakes, do look incredibly delicious. The one drawback of the place, especially the one in Hillcrest, is that if you show up after 7pm, you'll probably have a long wait (up to an hour) in line. As long as you've got company and no kids waiting, it's still worth it. One of the best lines I overheard while waiting in said line is between two men remarking that in the early days, most of the clientele seemed to be gay, and now there are many more breeders, probably because there's a better place somewhere that they don't know about yet. If there is a better place for dessert in San Diego, I have yet to find it.

Not a fan...
by Sarah (9 reviews)
November 17, 2009 - Doesn't like it
I've had some bad luck at this place the last three or four visits. Stale cupcakes, flies all over the baked goods, waiting an excessive amount of time, and very limited selection. These four things all happened each visit. Years ago (2004-ish), I used to stop by here all the time for lemon merigue pie and it was phenomenal. My favorite dessert.
The past couple of years they have disappointed me to the point that I won't go back. I feel a little like they are just resting on past great reviews instead of striving to stay the best.
I actually get birthday/occasion desserts at Vons now...which sounds ridiculous, but it's true. I can count on their quality (with no flies) and their chocolate cake is awesome.

One of 5 places in America to be buried!
by Vendorcloud (12 reviews)
May 19, 2009 - Likes it
I love this place. I love the owner Karen and her entrepreneurial prowess. I love the whole damn thing.
We spent too much on a cab ride the other night after an awful dinner at JRDN just to taste the wonderful yumminess of ED.
This place has been successful for more years than most of San Diego's restaurants have been around. It always proves to be a good surprise each time we go.
The staff isn't always friendly but I liken it to Barney's. You know... cool coldness. Not so great but the food punctuates like an expensive suit and any drag you get from their tude is gone swiftly.
Karen is a wunderkind and I hope this place is open forever. Her desserts amaze. They seem like she cut and made each one even though I know she didn't. There are lots of ice creams available to a-la-mode-it. Her coffee is high test and robust. The line out the door at 11 PM should tell all. There's not a restaurant within miles to feed it. This is a true destination.
