Much Improved
by Trip Klaus (109 reviews)
March 03, 2010 - Likes it
A year ago I asked for help from Victoria's and I' m happy to say that help has arrived. First, Alex the beer guy continues to provide excellent rotating taps with accurate lists. Second, the overall quality of the food has improved greatly. During the recent Restaurant Week, Victoria's offered the best 12.00 lunch in the county. Unfortunately,. the duck fat fries continue to be inconsistent (just let them cook already) and the standard paprika fries range from very good to bbq Lays. This is the only place in Howard County where someone who loves craft beers can also get a good meal without discussions of IBU's, Malt Bills or how many hit points an orc has.

Help!
by Trip Klaus (109 reviews)
February 26, 2009 - Likes it
Update July 2009
I have now returned many times and I have seen baby steps. The poutine has been sometimes better and sometimes the same, the chorizo metaloaf is gone but nothing of interest has been added. On a recent Sunday brunch I found the bartender to be the most knowledgeable person ever in the restaurant despite the continued incorrect beerlist. Perhaps the structure of the Clyde's driven management is stifling the menu which has no seasonal change. Half of my recent meal was good and half not. Pretty much my experience since they opened.
I have been torn by this restaurant since it opened. While, at the beginning there was a level of professionalism brought by the former Clyde's staff/ management the service has become abomidable.The former Iron Bridge chef opened with an innovative gastropub menu however, after customer comments most of the duck dishes left the menu and it was scaled back. (Except for the inexplainable Chorizo meatloaf). While the duck fat fries may seem a luxury, often they are so undercooked that the presumed benefits are lost. And forget about the perfect sounding Canadian poutine with duck, cheese and fries (Perfect right) The fries are so soggy by the time they reach the ramekin that what could be the perfect heart stopping combination becomes a wet mess of great ingredients.
Don't ask about the wine list that has been threatened to be improved since opening.
Beer...Beer..Beer
While the list is more impressive than the average bar in the Columbia area, it pales in comparison to Phoenix in Ellicott City and the prices in many cases for the imports are three times the retail price. As for American micros, why are they so in love with California when great breweries like Brewers Art and Victory can be found so much closer. PS can we please get an accurate beer list when we visit.

Up and Down
by Adam Nettina (6 reviews)
June 22, 2010
On the positive side, the biscuits here are the best I have ever had in my life. Likewise, the Cuban sandwich has always been oustanding. Like many have mentioned, however, the duck fat fries can be hit or miss in terms of texture. I really love their seasoning, and find the rosmary and sea salt combo to be oustanding. Likewise, I do think their is a unique savoriness that comes from the use of duck fat. However, they were incredibly greasy and many were limp the last time I visited. On my last visit I also tried the frog legs, just for kicks. The breading was not as crispy as I would have liked, but the meat within was cooked just right. Still not something I'd get again, but more on account of it being frog legs as opposed to anything kitchen fault.

Food and beer are great, but bring your wallet!
by JohnM (2 reviews)
March 12, 2010 - Doesn't like it
As others have noted, both the beer selection and food here are first rate. VGB has an extensive draft and bottle list, and there are a number of creative choices on the food menu. Ambiance and atmosphere are fairly decent, and this is the kind of place where you can either dress up a bit or arrive in informal attire. Service has been polite, attentive and friendly the times I've stopped by.
All the positives aside, it's difficult to overlook the extremely high prices here. $7 for a medium size portion of fries is just a bit on the high side, no matter how well prepared they are (and they really are pretty good here), and while I applaud the fact that beer here comes in an imperial pint, charging $7 or $8 a pop (before tax) just seems like hiway robbery (even for Columbia). That's for local craft beer by the way (think troegs, clipper city and Victory), not the German, Italian and Belgian imports (you don't want to know or ask what those beers run here).
Sigh... this is a solid enough place, but who can afford to go here on more then a very occasional basis?
