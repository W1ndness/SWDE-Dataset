Maybe they were really great tippers or something but after they arrived, we may as well have been two liverwurst sandwiches. And for a bill that was around $100 for two, that was a little bit of a letdown in Norfolk.
I'll go back but would refuse the waiter.

Veal Chop Heaven
by hmptnrdsfoodlver (2 reviews)
April 17, 2010 - Likes it
I have dined at several Norfolk restaurants, having recently moved to the area, and my experienxce at The Green Onion is one to be remembered. My wife and I heard of this place from a few people we met at an SPCA function where the Chef wa being auctioned for a private wine dinner, so we decided to give it a try. We dined yesterday and were amazed that place could pack so many people in there. The lounge was very chic, and to me a little posh but my wife of course loved it. We started with a glass Plunger Head.... Delicious. As we waited for the hustle and vustle to subside we got our table and started with an Arugula salad and a Tomato bisque. The arugula salad was very refreshing served with a strawberry mint vinaigrette and blue cheese crumbles.... awesome summer salad! We then decidied to split their addition of the evening which was a veal "tomahawk" chop served with sun dried tomato mash potatoes and grilled asparagus. COOKED wonderfully with remarkable sides to boot..... We will definitely return to The Green Onion.

I do like it, but....
by Kim (3 reviews)
September 13, 2009 - Likes it
It has the potential to be a lot better. It has a great atmosphere, with patio seating, and the food is delicious, yet where it is lacking is in the attentiveness of the waitstaff and it's prices. There were a total of 5 tables seated when I was there, and it seemed that the waitstresses were having difficulty catering to all of them in a timely manner. I ordered the special, the pulled pork sandwhich with pommes frite. The sandwhich was not anything special, but the pommes frite were fantastic. Although the food was satisfactory all together, I ended up paying $14 for a pulled pork sandwhich, and I'm sorry, it was barely worth the price. Perhaps, if I had had outstanding service the price would have been looked over.

Disappointed in their Service and Food
by The Lowrys (2 reviews)
January 22, 2010 - Likes it
My foodie hubby and I used to think Green Onion was one of the best restaurants in Ghent, but we recently went there and had a terrible experience. The blonde hostess had horrible people skills and customer service. She totally forgot about us and our "20 minute wait" turned into a 30 minute wait. We were offered no drinks/wine while waiting until the best server there, Erin, came over. We really like the soups and small plates menu here, but my husband got the ribeye and it was overcooked and I got a pork tenderloin special that was just ok. The wine selection is great, but for the price of our dinner and the quality of our food and service we were unhappy. Will not return anytime soon.
