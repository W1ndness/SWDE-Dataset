Over Rated - Wasn't Impressed
by scondley (1 review)
July 06, 2010
Ate at Malone's Hamburg - first time to dine at any Malone's establishment. I was very impressed by the service. Rory and Josh could not have done a better job. However, the food left alot to be desired. I ordered my steak done (which they described as no pink), broccoli, and baked potato. My husband ordered a steak medium (described as red warm center) asparagus, and caesar salad. Rory assured me done meant no pink - but it arrived and there was pink in the middle. My husband's steak arrived medium rare. The flavor of the steaks was not that good either. I've had better steaks at Texas Roadhouse and Logan's for alot less money. Both the broccoli and asparagus were basically raw with absolutley no seasoning. The baked potato and caesar salad were nothing special. The french bread is served on a cutting board with a large steak knife through it along with a small container of butter - just like at the Outback Steakhouse. I don't know if I'll bother trying Malone's again or not. What I am sure of is I'll never have steak at Malone's again.

Average Dining, Reasonably Priced
by CKYDiner (5 reviews)
April 16, 2010 - Likes it
While Malone's has earned a reputation in Lexington the cuisine is far from superb. What is apparent is that one can find quality food at very fair prices. For beef entrées the food may even be classified as better than quality. That said, be aware the Malone's is hit or miss. Not every meal will be good. I have experienced food poisoning with a fish entrée unevenly prepared.
Compared to traditional "chain" dining Malone's should win every time. Try the Tates Creek location if quality food is your priority.
