Great Times @ the Frog!
by jboatright (33 reviews)
July 23, 2010 - Really likes it
An impromptu trip to KC found us at Le Fou Frog (the Crazy Frenchman ... which supposedly refers to the chef)! The experience was in fact a little crazy, but indeed worth our time and resources. Atmospherically speaking, LFF is quaint and cozy, yet flamboyant ... not what everyone might expect in a fine French restaurant. However, if you are reading this, then you most likely do know what to expect regarding the level of fare ... and there was no disappointment as we absorbed 7 courses with a lite followed by dark French wine. Service was cleverly attentive ... before the evening was complete, an amusing relationship was established with the staff. The Frog provided dinner along with unforeseen drama ... we will be back for both.

Le Fou Flawed
by Moochie (4 reviews)
April 11, 2009 - Doesn't like it
I get that it's 'French provincial' and an extremely 'sensitive artist' type of place. That is usually what we like about it. We eat here about once or twice a month, but that's going to change after last night.
Our waiter (could not really call him a 'server' b/c he provided little service) ignored us for the first 10 min, then practically encouraged us NOT to order b/c he was obviously engaged w/ friends of his at a nearby table and didn't want to be bothered with our order. When I persisted in requesting drinks and two appetizer plates, he seemed quite put-off that we wanted anything at all.
Only one of the appetizers ever made it to our table, and it sat there cooling off as we waited for one of the two people (not our waiter) to bring requested silverware.
There were many wait staff wandering around, but no one seemed to be on a clear task. The table next to us received THREE baskets of bread before we ever received one, again after requesting it. The last drink my husband ordered waited to even be placed w/ the bartender b/c after we made the request THE WAITER PROMPTLY WENT TO HIS FRIENDS TABLE, SAT FOR A DRINK AND A SMOKE w/ THEM BEFORE going inside to request our beverage. Our water glasses were never refilled and when our friends joined us they were met w/ the waiter's insistence that they order promptly as happy hour was about to end. When we expressed our interest in eating indoors rather than be so rushed, then our waiter was all attention to us - presumably b/c if he received our table indoors the prices would double for the food and his assumption of a tip as well.
We were SO glad to leave, and I was SO embarrassed we asked friends to drive up from Lawrence to dine here. I'm not sure when we'll think this is worth our money again. We will go back b/c disappointing service has only happened once or twice when we've been there. But this last incident was so egregious when we do go back, we will refuse to sit in this person's area.
I appreciate the well-priced happy hour, but don't offer this to customers if you don't plan on treating them as paying customers. The other restaurant we went to for dinner was more than happy to accept our $200 and we were all more than happy to dine there.
It's unfortunate when above average cuisine is diminished by selfish wait staff who feel customers are getting in their way.
My mistake, not getting up and speaking to the mgr immediately. We just couldn't believe the downward spiral went so fast.

by Rick Brown (4 reviews)
August 08, 2010 - Really likes it
This place is soo great...I think of it as KC's best little gem. It's intimate, casual and upscale all at once. The owners are super nice and the live french music is a must hear.
Typically their steaks are best I can find in KC. They also have 2 of the best dishes I have ever had. Their Lobster with a vanilla champagne sauce served over Risotto and the Duck Confit Salad with lentils.

Le Fou Frog
by kaylovesmusic (1 review)
September 15, 2008 - Likes it
Our first time dining here. The food was very good and the ambience was delightful. Impressive wine list and lots of top shelf liquor at the bar. I ordered the escargot, and was struggling to remove them from the shells with the slightly too-wide fork they gave me. Not sure if it was the owner or the manager, but a very thoughtful female staff member came over and graciously provided me with what looked like a giant toothpick to help me wiggle them out. Our server was fabulous. The cheese tray was divine with ample amounts of walnuts, compote, strawberries, grapes and apples and bread. We sat at the bar for an after-dinner drink and enjoyed our conversation with Jeremy, the bartender. Great place! Romantic and artistic environment without the snobbery.

Our "goto" place for a superb dinner where we know we can't go wrong.

We have been to Le fou Frog quite a few times now and we have yet to have a bad experience there. It's the kind of place that is obviously about the food not the food scene or what is trendy now.
Teri always gets the Warm Goat Cheese Salad and the Lobster tails in a champagne Vanilla sauce.
