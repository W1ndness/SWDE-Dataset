Next best thing to the island...!
by Thomas (1 review)
March 30, 2010
First of all, not sure what El Coqui the previous rater was talking about or rather what planet they were on, but I, a new resident of Tucson, have been searching for an authentic Puerto Rican restaurant for quite awhile now and am pleased to have found this one! Small yet providing a home-type feel, as soon as a patron walks in, the smell of fresh sofrito almost overwhelms you and you quickly find yourself asking "where is the menu?!" For an establishment of it's size, the menu surpasses satisfaction. The range of entrees covers not only the basics of what a curious hungry patron would look for, (menu is in spanish and english for newbies) but also the favorites that one would think would only be offered on the island. I was beyond satisfied after enjoying an appetizer of sweet plantains, a main dish of mofongo con camoron (fried plantains with shrimp), only to dig down deep to find room for perfectly prepared tembleque. Lets just say for the rest of the day, I sat at my desk in the reclined position! The recipes all exceeded my expectations. Great portions for a reasonable price. Absolutely fantastic. Most of the staff are boricua and the atmosphere directly reflected it. My wife who is boricua, has trained my palette for authentic Puerto Rican cuisine, and this establishment fits the bill. This far out west, your selections are many for other latin cuisines, but very few for Puerto Rican and I must say the service, cuisine, prices and atmostphere are dead on par. I defintely recommend it and will frequent there again! Perfect!

Fantastic food and service
by jekshopping (1 review)
May 31, 2010
I'm so glad we stopped by on Sunday as I had known it was coming but hadn't been since they opened.
We sat at the bar and had some appetizers and drinks as I had just wanted to stop by to check out their pasteles and gandule rice to get a fixing for some old family cooking. It was a great experience and the bartender, Raul, was great. He infused the evening with descriptions of what he's making and explained all of the other goodness on the menu. it was a real treat.
The Palmas del Mar drink he was working on was great - maybe a bit more frizz or a cirtrus would make it epic but it was really good and perfectly refreshing for the summer months here. That could easily be their signature drink.
The food was great, service was great, and I can't say enough about the chocolate flan that was available. It was simply beyond compare.
I look forward to going back with friends to share this place and some Sangria on a Sunday in the near future.
I highly recommend giving this a try. If you have a question or concern just ask.

mobile review
by RayH (3 reviews)
July 16, 2010
First I have to say I don't know what Peurto Rican food is supposed to be like. That said, I and my two guests enjoyed the food. We were expecting spicier, but this was on the salty side. Unfortunately, I was unimpressed with the service andvthe ambiance. Everything took twice as long as it should, from the wait at the door (at 3pm on Friday, it was mostly empty) and the time it took our waiter to finally come to our table, to the length of time for my drink refill to come back to the table - the waiter dropped it off at the bar, it was filled quickly, and sat there for many minutes while the waiter futzed around outside the kitchen doors. The most prominent display is the prolific array of booze at the bar; and folksy Peurto Rican music, while not totally obnoxious, was consistently too loud. If a friend offered to take me here, I wouldn't refuse. But I'm not likely to go back with my usual Friday afternoon bunch.
