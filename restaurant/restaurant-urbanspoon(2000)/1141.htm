A Treasure!! Excellent Home Made Moroccan food cooked with TLC
by Mia (1 review)
June 18, 2010
this place is AMAZING!!! while in san antonio for work, i was looking for some good moroccan food and found Moroccan Bites. I cannot express how wonderful this place is. It is a family run restaurant who makes you feel like part of the family. Wafa and her mother, Al-Hajja, exemplify true moroccan hospitality and kindness.
the restaurant is intimate, seats maybe 30 people. Its busy regardless of what time you go, lunch or dinner.
the food is yummy. as a moroccan who has grown up eating moroccan food and cooks it at home, eating here was like eating at an aunts house. portions were good, food was fresh and hot, Wafa & Al-Hajja actually see you as a person and not an "order".
i had to get lunch for 15 employees at a company function. i called the night before and they had couscous and moroccan salad ready for me by noon. my employees never had couscous or moroccan food before and loved it!! It was a huge hit.
Al-Hajja also told me that she also does moroccan sweets for special orders.
this will be a place that i will be visiting everytime i'm in san antonio.

NOT GOOD!
by girlinsa (1 review)
June 05, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I have no idea how this place is getting such great reviews. I know Moroccan food, and I don't know what is good about this place. The people are the only thing that is good.
Not only does it have only 6 tables, you can barely sit without the person behind you ramming into your chair. It is so hot (do NOT go in the summer) without air conditioning and the sun sets right into the windows as you are trying to eat. Bring sunglasses and a personal fan.
Now for the food. They bring out their special Moroccan bread. It was at least 1-2 days old and re-heated. So bad we could not eat it. The kids dishes were tasty. The lamb tagine was pathetic to say the least. There were like maybe 2-3 bites of worthwhile meat, no veggies? and not much sauce. Came with NOTHING. No rice, no couscous that was extra. I have made home made tagines and my husband was shocked that this place is getting such high reviews. Maybe people don't know what real Moroccan food should taste like. The couscous was good. However, the chicken they placed on top was just BOILED chicken (so disgusting with yellow skin shriveled on it). The veggies on top of the chicken we stewed and flavorless.
We will not be back. Too bad because I had such high hopes from the previous reviews.

What's not to like
by Jerry and Doris (1 review)
June 07, 2010 - Likes it
If you are looking for a place to eat that will delight your palate and soothe your soul this is the place. We found this place after a great review in the Express News several months ago. My wife lived in Morocco for over 3 years and loved the food of this beautiful country and Moroccan Bites has brought the same wonderful food to San Antonio.
I have traveled and eaten foods in Europe and the Far East and my wife has prepared several of the North African/Moroccan dishes, so we were very happy when we discovered Moroccan Bites.
Wafa and her mother Chef Haja are two of the most delightful people we have ever met and when you come to Moroccan Bites please take the time to get to know them, you won't be disappointed. We now count them as friends and love them dearly. Just a couple of days ago my wife told me that the reason she enjoyed going to Moroccan Bites was that it was not like going to a restaurant, but was like going to a close friends house to eat.
Please remember that you are not visiting a fast food restaurant, but you are there to relax, unwind and have a great dining experience so relax, have a pot of their exceptional mint tea and enjoy.
I am slowly working my way through their menu but it may take a while as I am constantly returning to several of my favorites. By the way if you visit them on a Friday please try their couscous, it is the best I have ever eaten and I am willing to bet you will say the same. Unfortunately because of the time and effort it takes to properly prepare the couscous they only serve it on Fridays, but for 4 people or more and 24 hours notice they will prepare most if not all of their specialty dishes for you any day of the week.
My wife and I will often stop by mid-afternoon for a pot of tea and to visit. It beats a spa and is cheaper.
Please join us at the best restaurant in San Antonio, "Moroccan Bites"

OBSESSED!
by Camilla Morrison (1 review)
April 14, 2010 - Likes it
I recently spent some time living in Morocco and the night I came back I went in search of authentic Moroccan food and immediately found the PERFECT place here! Speaking as someone who was eating actual Moroccan food for quite a while, this place gives you what is legitimately served over there and it is delicious. For a soup to start I would say get the Herrera and then a safe but tasty main dish is the Chicken Tagine. The lentils and the beans are also SO good but really everything is. Also every person who goes needs to get some Moroccan Mint Tea to sip on- in Morocco I couldn't go two hours without a cup of it being graciously poured for me by some store owner or just random Moroccan. The ladies who run things at this place also display the typical Moroccan hospitality, from the moment I walked in they have treated me as one of the family- I DEFINITELY insist that you try to get to know them some while you're there because it is worth it. Finally about the comments on here about the wait- disregard. It really doesn't take that long, people who complain are apparently just used to places like MacDonalds and Burger KIng. In morocco the ladies who made food at the place I was staying would wake up at 4 to begin breakfast that was served at 6:30 and then would immediately start on lunch served at 1:30 and (no joke) would start preparing dinner while we were eating lunch and it would be ready till about 7.... so think twice before you complain about it taking 25 minutes for a main course because I dont know how they create dishes that taste just as good in a fraction of the time.
