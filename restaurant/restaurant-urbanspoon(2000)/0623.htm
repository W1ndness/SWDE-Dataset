Dissapointing
by Jen Allen (9 reviews)
June 22, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I went to Hiko-a-Mon yesterday evening at 6:30pm. I was with my infant son (9 mths). I was sat promptly, and got setteled in with my kid. A waitress came by, asked what I wanted to drink, and about 2 minutes later, I recieved an ice tea. That is where it started to go sour. I did not see the waitress for 10 minutes. My baby started to get ancy, so I fed him all of his food. Once that was gone, I tried to keep him entertained, but I was getting pretty irritated. He started to get alittle loud, so I decided that since it was taking so long, I would just order to go. So I went back up to the hostess and asked if I could order to go. I asked her about the tuna tiaki, and she said she didn't know much about it, since it was only her second day. So she went and got someone that knew about the menu. A couple minutes later, a young man came up there with her. I asked him the same question, and he said he didn;t know, so HE had to go ask someone. He left, came back a few minutes later, and then told me (almost word for word) the description on the menu, which was not enlightening at all, as I can READ. So, I went ahead and ordered the tuna and a california roll. I sat back down at my table, and waited. About 5 minutes later, the hostess cam to me and said that it would be 20 minutes for my food. On a Monday night. In a restaurant that was not full. So, I said ok. And I waited. And I waited. And I paced the place. I had to frquently go outside becuase by this time, my child was ready to have a nervous breakdown. I waited 35 minutes for my food. No discount. The hostess apologized several times. I got home (at 7:45pm...1 hour 15 minutes later), opened my food, and ate the tuna. It was pretty decent (overpriced at 11.00 for the amount and sloppy-ness), but the california roll was the most dissapointing of all. Only one roll had crab in it. They were falling apart. And the cucumber was in big chunks, not uniform, and had the skin on it still. I spent 17.00 for one of the worst restaurant experiences I have ever had. I will not return, which is sad because I live right next to the WestPort Village. I hope no one else has to deal with this. NOTE: The employees were nice, and did not act insulted that I was alone just with a baby.

Excellent Japanese
by Roger Short (1 review)
September 28, 2009 - Really likes it
By far the best place in town for Authentic Japanese. The decor and ambiance are what you would expect from an upscale restaurant. This place is not just another typical Louisville Japanese Steakhouse(In fact no hibachi grills here).
The menu was well thought out and offers a unique and diverse offering of authentic cuisine. The presentation is excellent and the service is good.
It seems that they took the time to realize that people would appreciate the "extra little things" to make the entire experience a great one and everything including the service, the food quality and presentation and the even the decor( look for the little accent cushions ) represent this.
The Lunch menu is priced right and the servings are generous. Dinner is priced more in line with what you would expect to pay for decent Japanese but come early to get the Happy Hour specials!
You will not be disappointed.
