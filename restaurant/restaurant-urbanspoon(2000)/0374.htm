Indifferent
by Sean Boudreau (6 reviews)
April 21, 2010 - Likes it
I love Cuban food and pretty much all food with a Latin background. So I was excited to finally try Cafe Habana myself. First, the service was terrible. Not just "okay" not "adequate" but terrible. It was a Tuesday evening at 7, so they were not busy and there was a hostess and 2 waitresses, so there was PLENTY of help for the 4 or 5 tables. We were seated right away and I first noticed the lack of Cuban/Latin music playing, instead it was pop/rap music, which I think took away from the ambiance of a Cuban restaurant.
Our waitress brought us water, then went away for 10 minutes without asking if we'd like to order drinks. This is odd, drinks are big money for restaurants and Cafe Habana has a great mix of freshly made Cuban/Latin cocktails. I actually had to flag her down to order drinks and an appetizer.
For drinks my friend ordered the mojito and I ordered the (actually one of my favorite drinks) Caipirinha. The mojito was rather boring and nothing was muddled, same with the Caipirinha it just looked thrown together. Disappointing for drinks that were $6 and $7. While enjoying our drinks, 1 table of about 6 women sent ALL of their drinks back, this is very rare and something I don't think I've ever seen. Also, just a small point. While mojito's and Caipirinha are certainly Latin drinks, they are not exclusively Cuban cocktails. I'm surprised Cafe Habana didn't push the daiquiri a little more, as it is the official cocktail of Cuba.
We ordered the plantains appetizer with mojo sauce and they too were rather bland, but some salt and a good lathering of mojo helped with that. They were made tasty.
Finally, we both ordered the Carne Asada since we are both huge fans of hanger steak ($14 is a great deal for hanger steak). My friend ordered it medium (he's kinda slow) and I ordered it rare (rare - medium rare is recommended for hanger steak). Neither of ours came our right. His was overcooked (surprise, surprise) and mine was also overcooked. HOWEVER, the flavor was good, and the chimichuuri sauce was awesome on it. It was served with the most amazing saffron rice, I mean I could eat a whole plate of just that! Also greens were served on the side, which on that night was spinach. The greens really didn't work with the dish, but I love spinach so it was alright with me.
Bottom line, I think they were just having an off night. They share a kitchen with B'astone and some other places that are all connected and someone was off their game, certainly the waitstaff was (for sure) awful. I will however come back to Cafe Habana and probably sooner then later, I LOVE a great Cuban sandwich and I cannot wait to try theirs and some of the other food passing by looked great.
All in all, give it a try, I'm going to give them a second chance. If you love hanger steak theirs is pretty tasty, but the best hanger steak in Detroit, possibly Michigan all together is at Roast.

Cafe Habana cures tired taste buds
by dave m (4 reviews)
June 01, 2009 - Likes it
Bold colors set the stage for an array of bold flavors awaiting diners at Royal Oak's Café Habana.
Entering the restaurant from the partially hidden door on Fifth, the first things we notice are the open kitchen, the stark brick walls meeting colorfully upholstered booths, and finally, the bright painted mural in the back. On the way to our seat, the pulse of Cuban music and the chatter of happy diners fills the air.
Café Habana shares a kitchen and bar with Bastone, so any of Bastone's micro brewed beers are available. I tried the Dubbel Vision, a Belgian style amber ale that was really tasty. There's also the Cuba Libre (rum and coke), Mojito, and Cuban Sangria, in addition to assorted martinis and daiquiris.
My dining companion's Mojito, was a refreshing blend of rum, lime juice, sugar, mint leaves and a splash of soda, served up in a tall frosty glass. The secret to a perfect mojito is to crush together the mint leaves and sugar, releasing mint oil into the mixture. She pronounced the mojito as the "best" she's ever had.
For a starter, try the Fried Plantains with mojo (garlic/citrus sauce). They are tender and tasty, and a treat you won't often find in metro Detroit.
For dinner, we tried the Ropa Vieja, a large portion of slow cooked shredded beef in a cumin-laden spicy tomato sauce, served over yellow rice. The cumin made this unforgettable. We also had Carne Asada, a mojo marinated flank steak, grilled medium-rare and sliced thin, displayed around a mound of rice, and settled atop a thin layer of chimichurri. Both were delicious. Other popular dinners include Chicken Mole, a large grilled chicken breast with a sweet mole sauce, and grilled Ahi fish tacos.
Also available are authentic Cuban salads, appetizers, and several sandwiches. The Cuban sandwich, a generous helping of roast pork, ham, Swiss cheese and pickles on press-grilled Cuban bread, is outstanding. For light eaters, or the post-theater crowd, a smaller version of the Cuban sandwich Medianoche, is available.
With a friendly knowledgeable wait staff, and a kitchen with an eye for detail, Café Habana has the recipe for success in downtown Royal Oak.
