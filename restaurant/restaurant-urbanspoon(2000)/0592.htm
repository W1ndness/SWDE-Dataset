Well now - here is the deal. Merrick has good food and good service and the ambiance is.... well a little like eating in your rich aunts house during the civil war. She has started feeding the soldiers to make enough money to keep her house during the war. Your aunt isn't a flashy cook, but gets the right flavors needed from the food. The soldiers are a bit of a bore and the bar they gather around outside seems to distract from any elegance your aunt might actually be trying to maintain...making her restuarant seem more like a house rather than an estate.

Mediocre
by Haven Woo (6 reviews)
December 20, 2009
While Merrick may be the place to see and be seen in Lexington, the food is nothing to write home about. The nearly $20 fried chicken isn't in any way superior to what you would get for a quarter of that price at KFC. I suppose that there are enough older and traditional people in Lexington who keep Merrick packed because they like a relatively uninspiring menu, however Merrick has the potential for much more. With the new movement into nouveau southern cooking, a revision of the menu to reflect more of a fusion in its cuisine could take this restaurant out of the old days and bring it into the 21st century. If it wasn't for the bar and patio crowd, Merrick would have already gone the way of The Coach House.
