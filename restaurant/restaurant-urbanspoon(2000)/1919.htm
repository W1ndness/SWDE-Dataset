bestest pho in boston c-town
by FreeWilly (175 reviews)
January 24, 2010 - Really likes it
i try to avoid this place knowing: 1) there's always a wait at peak times; 2) the fact that it's so famous it has multiple locations (franchise or expanded family biz?). but at the end i still go frequently because they do make the BEST pho (beef noodle in soup) compared to the other ones in c-town (there are at least 5 others w/in the 2-block radius). i don't usually drink the soup in soup noodles but theirs is SOOO good i really can't help it.
grilled pork chop on rice (served hot) or dry vermicelli (cooked but served "cold")--both served w/vinegar sauce--are great as well. i always have a hard time choosing between the two.
i rarely steer off these routine orders but once i craved a pad thai so i ordered it. not the greatest nor authentic, but not bad either. vietnam and thailand are but neighbors anyway.
oh they do make the best durian shake as well. but don't get it if you can't stand the pungent smell (and taste) of the fruit. it takes some getting used to, even tho some ppl fall in love w/it instantly. other shakes (ex. red bean) are good as well. but i do ask them to skip the sugar.
yes i agree, the service is by far not the best at all, sometimes bordering rude as wait staff kind of "rush" you to order (ex. return 3 times within 5 minutes and expect you be ready), esp during peak times. but it's an everyday asian restaurant so i really don't hold them up to the same level of service standard than other fine dining establishments. if you're not happy w/how you're served (as i do sometimes), just tell them what you think by leaving a smaller tip.
:)
