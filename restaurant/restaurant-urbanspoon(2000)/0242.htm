Horrible!
by Buckibbq (1 review)
August 30, 2009 - Doesn't like it
For my wife's 40th birthday, I decided to surprise her with a trip to the highly acclaimed "Lolita". I would have been much more satisfied with a drive thru the local Burger King. We sat at the bar, even though we had reservations. The service from the bartender was at least adequate. We ordered the roasted dates and stuffed banana peppers as appetizers. The dates were nothing special and the peppers, BOTH of them, were charred beyond recognition. The sausage within the peppers was made from equal parts salt and pork. Seven dollars for two of these was a waste of money. I ordered the pizza and it might as well have been a Tombstone. My wife ordered the gnocchi which resulted in a burst of laughter when it arrived. Picture a small ice cream scoop of "stuff" plopped in the middle of a huge bowl. My wife ate both bites of the gnocchi and I ate a sliver of the pizza just to quell our hunger. I asked the bartender if I could somehow relay my sentiments to the "Iron Chef" owner, and she replied that the manager would be right over to console us. That didn't happen. I waited 7 minutes, paid the fine and left.

Good food in a cool neighborhood
by brianv684 (3 reviews)
May 19, 2010 - Likes it
Took my mother-in-law to this place for her birthday. I called and made a reservation a week in advance assuming that since it was a Saturday, they would fill up quickly. We got there a little early so we strolled down the street, peeking into the art galleries and shops down the street from the resturant. When we returned we were greeted by a friendly host who sat us immediately. Being a trendy Cleveland hotspot my expectations were high and they did not dissapoint. Our server was over immediately to take our drink order and to explain the house specials. They have a great wine/drink list and I started with a glass of Malbec. We also started with an apetizer. All of them looked great but we settled on the lamb keftedes.
The atmosphere is pretty cool, not too pretentious but a trendy spot to see and be seen. The focus of this place of course is the food, but I do want to point out we had excellent service. Our water glasses were filled and the server was able to strike the ellusive balance of being attentive without hovering.
The lamb keftedes were great, the sauce complimented the meat perfectly. For dinner I went for the roasted chicken, the MIL had the Ravioli and my wife went for the pork sausage pizza. The ravioli is very light so if you in the mood for pasta you can order this dish and not have to worry about having that feeling like you just ate a loaf of bread. My chicken was cooked perfectly, juicy, flavorful and very savory. The pizza was typical gourmet pizza, thin crust and fresh ingredients. All in all dinner was excellent and we will be back for sure.
