Nice addition to PV
by foodsnob (421 reviews)
April 09, 2010 - Likes it
I agree that it's a kickin' & lively place and a wonderful addition to PV! But, I agree with Food Carpenter, burger wise it's not even in the top 5, but it's good and fun and you should check it out. We started out at the bar and they had my husbands' "Bud", my Cape Cod was just right. After a wait we sat for dinner, husband had a burger and it was good though luke warm, his onion straws had some parmesan sprinkled on the top and we have to say that they beat Smashburgers' onion straws. I did the sausage and peppers-though I would have preferred sweet banana peppers like they use on the East coast, the sandwich was awesome and I'd have it again, the bun complemented the sausage. The home made condiments were nice and tasty though the bar-b-que sauce served with the onion straws was way too sweet for me. Worst thing about the meal was the mac & cheese, served luke warm the first time, sent it back and the heat didn't help much, it was just not great, not even good, of course I make really good mac and cheese so, maybe we are partial to mine but neither of us liked it at all. Our complaints would be way too creamy and overpowering with the white cheddar, I like to put about 5 cheeses in mine so one cheese doesn't overpower another, also there should be some spots where you run into some melted cheese it shouldn't be all cheese sauce, and doesn't everyone like to get some of the drier crusty baked macaronis-none here.
All in all, it's PV's latest hot spot and worth a try, we'll go back, particularily since it's close to the kids-but burger-wise we'll stick with Blanc or Smashburger.

Creative Thinking
by The Food Carpenter (157 reviews)
March 15, 2010 - Likes it
The lady and I partook in the BRGR scene on a busy Friday night and although it was packed, the entire front-of-the-house was running extra hard, which made the experience very smooth from beginning to end. Looking over the menu, it was easy to see that many of the selections were aimed at capturing a part of the ever so popular modern burger market that Kansas City loves so much. On paper it sounded on point, but when plated the flavors were a little less accurate. Ordered the Out-N-In burger, and while on paper the idea of a burger containing your common toppings stuffed inside sounds fun, it was a bit lacking. The burger was not made larger to compensate for its tenants, thus there was less lettuce (which I don't like hot and wilted), tomatoes, pickles, onions and cheese to go with my burger. To go with the burgers, we ordered a trio of fried sweet potato fries, truffle fries, and onion rings, all of which were good, but not the best. The BRGR was very tasty, but nothing I would rank in my top 5 in Kansas City. For dessert, we had to try the Chocolate Burger and the Guinness Shake and when it came out, the burger was by far one of the coolest looking desserts I had seen at a burger joint. Sadly, it was way too sweet to finish and the shake was fairly mute in flavor. All-in-all, it was a wonderful evening with good food and friendly service, just not sure how willing I would be to go out of my way to eat here again, especially if there is a Blanc in the near vicinity.

Off to a good start
by Mookman (42 reviews)
March 10, 2010 - Likes it
Sparkle Aggie and I tried this place out on their first official day open to the public. The atmosphere is very nice... solid wood tables, several large, communal tables, and a nice, warm color. There is a great outdoor patio, but unfortunately, it was raining, so we couldn't take advantage of that.
Since it was opening day, there were a few kinks to work out, but all in all, it was very good. The Out N In burger was a hit, and my traditional BRGR burger was just as I expected... nothing I haven't had before, but solid.
They have a nice selection of sides, and a trio of fries or onion rings (they have 4 types of fries and 2 types on onions) is only $6.
The best deal was the $2 for a salad. It was a great size and very delicious.
The service was good, but the kitchen took about 40 mintues to get the food out... to be expected on Day 1. Can't wait to go back and see how everything is after a week or 2.

Wow!
by nutmegballs (1 review)
March 16, 2010 - Likes it
My girlfriend Kelly and I hit BRGR Kitchen + Bar last night for dinner and I am looking forward to going back. The experience was all-around great, and the burgers really impressed us.
We liked it right off the bat because the atmosphere is modern but still comfortable and homey. We sat inside, since we were late enough that it was cool outside, but we plan to go back when it's warm so we can sit on the patio by the firepit-a really cool touch.
We ordered the pickle and zucchini spears for an appetizer, and were really pleased. The dip was great, and they were a light way to start off.
For dinner we had burgers (The Pittsburger for me and The San Antonio for her) and fries (actually truffle fries, sweet potato fries and onion straws) and beers. My burger was awesome. The slaw on it was the perfect touch on a burger that was already delicious. Kelly's was great too, but I admit I wasn't expecting it to be. The description has cheese whiz, black beans and fritos, which I would never have thought to put on a burger, but she let me try it and I'm for sure going to order it on our next trip.
The fry combo was good too. Great flavor and crispiness, and the home-made ketchup made it. I'm a ketchup guy (Heinz is my favorite) and this stuff was great even though it was different from my expectations (again). Whoever designed the menu and the sauces did a good job, because I'm pretty picky about food usually, and everything we tried impressed me.
I also dug the beer selection. They only serve taps and cans, but the selections that they have for both are great. They have a bunch of really good Colorado micro-brews on tap, and plenty of cans to choose from too, some as low as $2.00.
We didn't have room for dessert, but we were seriously tempted. Maybe next time we'll pass on the appetizer and save room.
Also, our waitress was great. I wouldn't have guessed that they had been open less than a week based on how efficient they were.
I'm looking forward to the next dinner trip, and also to hanging out in the bar, which seemed like a really inviting place to go with friends.
I'm really glad they opened up so close to us.
All in all, there's not much I could come up with to say that isn't positive, so there it is.
I highly recommend BRGR!
