They're VERY nice people there.
by O. William Marshall III (1 review)
February 08, 2009 - Likes it
In my judgement, the restaurant business is a people business. These are really VERY nice people. General Manager, Dan is a gem. Executive Chef, Gregg was very helpful to me recently on an important matter. Of course, I miss Manager Phil and Salvatore, the waiter (everyone knows Sal). Speaking of waiters, I usually like to request my favorite. In this place, that isn't necessary. ALL waiters are equally more than competent. If you want to choose, that would be on you. It would be a personality issue, not a competence issue. Last Mother's Day, I was leaving the restaurant and I had to negotiate my way through a large crowd of people that were gathered together in front of the bar. Within a second, I felt that these people were TOGETHER. I asked whether they were all friends or family. As it turned out, they were family and that is how I met one of the owners. I think that she said that her name was Toni, but I really don't recall now. I just call her Raquel Welsh. As it was Mother's Day, I asked her if she was a mother. She has two full grown boys. WHOA! If you've seen those magazines where they feature women in their forties or even fifties that are still looking good on their cover, you might have an idea of what I'm talking about. Rather than waste your money on those silly magazines, come down to Casa Dante's. She's an absolute KNOCKOUT. You just won't believe it until you see it, if then. Whenever I leave Casa Dante's, I have a feeling of sadness and regret in my heart, and I don't mean because of the excellent cuisine and service either.
~ O. William Marshall III

A Surprising Special Night
by hardworkinglady (1 review)
March 25, 2010 - Likes it
We dined at Casa Dante on March 20, 2010. It was a Saturday night and after the prior weekend of the storm with no electricity we needed a night out. We had reservations and we were promptly seated. We ordered a cocktail and found it to be a generous size. For an appetizer we asked if a dinner special could be split into two, and they were happy to accommodate us. It was a rissotto with shrimp and porcini mushrooms. We found it to be creamy, cooked perfectly and delicious. For our entree I had the bronzini fish. The fish was very fresh, and the taste was flavorful yet light. For dessert we had a cannolli. The outside of the cannolli was crispy (they must fill them fresh as you order), and the inside was smooth and not too sweet, just the way I like them.
As an added perk, there was entertainment that night. We actually danced once or twice! Normally, when we go out for dinner, it is over in an hour or two. Instead, this turned out to be a full evening out, for the price of a dinner.
So, we expected a routine dinner out and ended up having a very unexpected special night. We recommend Casa Dante and will definitely go back again.
