+ 2 more photos from R. E. P. A unique sandwich eatery
by R. E. P. (279 reviews)
June 14, 2009 - Likes it
I wasn't moving very fast on Sunday and at 3:00 PM still had not had lunch. I had put Wallace Station on my list to try sometime back and this seemed as good a time as any to go. Luckily we didn't wait past 3:00 because we found out they close at 4:00 on Sunday. But we got there in time and found this little restaurant on an out of the way road not far from Lexington, Midway, and Versailles. We also found some very delicious sandwiches. I had the "East Hampton Hot Ham & Brie" and loved it. If you go anytime other than lunch, call to be sure they're open because they have some odd hours. By the way, the Google map in UrbanSpoon places this restaurant almost in Frankfort; this is incorrect. They are really very close to the intersection of Old Frankfort Pike & US 62, about 6 miles closer to Lexington than Google would have you believe.

A Great Sandwich
by Kaintuckeean (9 reviews)
July 25, 2009 - Likes it
I've been to Wallace Station a few times. Twice for burgers and beer outside on a weekend evening with live music. A lot of fun.
Most recently, I drove down Old Frankfort Pike from Lexington for a lunch break. We arrived late - about 1:20 p.m. - and it was still very busy inside. Of course, the weather was terrific so we took advantage of the wonderful deck outside.
I ordered the inside-out hot brown which was a great twist on a Kentucky classic. Inside the pressed, buttered and grilled bread slices were all of the ingredients to a perfect hot brown. The tastes came together perfectly and the melted Gruyere did not overpower the sandwich as it sometimes does with the classic dish. Served with not-special potato chips, the meal was a success.
Also, they serve Pepsi (a plus for me) with a little extra syrup. Very nice.
