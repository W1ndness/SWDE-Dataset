Liked it...but. 6.5/10 pt Review
by RestaurantSnob (21 reviews)
March 28, 2010 - Likes it
The oldest, biggest, most historical, most haunted, most visited by celebrities, home of mardi gras royalty, and on and on. Ok, that said, it is truly a one of a kind experience even for just a regular diner on a regular evening. The building is phenomenal and elegant. The servers are professionals. The mystique of Dinner at Antoine's even has a book written about it by Frances Parkinson Keyes. The all French menu is traditional and good, but completely dated. Most of the recipes are decades old and heavy on the cream. The prices are extremely high. After reading some of the other reviews, I saw people complaining of it taking 10 minutes to see a server. While this is true and must go into your review, have you seen this place. The trip from the kitchen to one's table is a marathon. It would take me fifteen minutes. You have to go. It is an icon and one of the most integral parts of dining history available for dining still today. BUT, without attending an event or being invited by another , that may be your last voluntary visit, because all the negatives in the previous reviews are accurate.
