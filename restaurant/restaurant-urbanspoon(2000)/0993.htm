Oh. My. God.
by Doug C (1 review)
July 24, 2010 - Likes it
So I venture into Gravy for the first time today. My good friend Shari, who has quite the discerning palate, told me awhile back that she heard it was just so-so, so she has yet to go. But Urban Spoon brought it up on my phone today as I was looking for a good breakfast and, not to be daunted by hearsay, I was like "Oh yeah, Gravy. I'll give it a go." I have to wait outside for about twenty minutes, even just for a seat at the counter, but that's okay because it's a beautiful day in P-town and the people-watching on Mississippi is always good. Finally I'm seated on a flattish bar stool which is like a thick iron trashcan lid molded into the negative-space shape of someone's butt (but clearly not my own) -- but I'm easy-going so I don't care. The view is spectacular: three dewy, naturally beautiful servers (hey -- maybe THAT's the gravy) bustling about behind the counter in the stifling heat providing top-notch hospitality, and a full view of the man's-men cooks in the back layin' it down in black cast-iron pans over gyrating tongues of stove flame. Good stuff. The menu is chock full of what you want in breakfast fare -- scrambles, omelets, hash, pancakes, french toast, oatmeal, with sides of potatoes and fresh fruit. I decide on the Dalise scramble and, instead of a gratis side of potatoes or fruit, go with a short stack of pancakes for an extra buck. Yeah, like I needed those. Not that I needed the potatoes either. Next time I'll go with the fruit. My order in, the wait is a little longer than I'm used to for breakfast in Portland, but I have a good book and lots in front of me to watch, so I don't mind one bit. As I salivate I see what is brought to the patrons seated on either side of me. To my left, I espy a gigantic load of salmon hash, a pile of fluffy scrambled eggs, and a potato pancake the size of Montana -- all on one huge plate. I'm like "Crap, that's what I'm getting next time," thinking that what I had ordered couldn't possibly compare. One of the darlings behind the bar overhears me and she asks "What did you order?" When I tell her the Dalise, she says "Don't worry, you won't be disappointed." To my right, my neighbor is served his half-order of french toast. That's right, HALF-order. Now I'm thinking that clearly I'm on another planet where everyone is ten feet tall and weighs four hundred pounds, because this HALF order is more like the equivalent of HALF a loaf of bread. It was HUGE. The guy who ordered it is medium height and pretty skinny -- he almost finished it but couldn't quite get there, and he only had three bites of his giant potato pancake that came as a completely superfluous side. I'm actually sweating by the time my food arrives, and when it does, I say "Wait, is that really the Dalise?" "Yep" replies my lovely server, with a slight smile of "ahhh, he's never been here before" satisfaction. Much to my greatest pleasure, it is not what I had envisioned at all. The plate is huge, like the salmon hash plate I saw to my left moments before. The best way to describe the Dalise is this: picture a luscious juicy pizza topped with bacon, sausage, ham, mushrooms, onions, and swirls of melted cheese (I held the peppers); now scrape the entire load of toppings off the pizza onto a giant plate, leaving only the pizza crust and marinara sauce behind; now mix in a bunch of fluffy scrambled eggs (about five eggs, said the lead cook); and then cover the whole deal in COUNTRY GRAVY. There's your Dalise. "Your short stack is coming right up" is what my sweet and salty server says when she plops my main dish down. Like I needed two pancakes on top of this heap. All I can say is "Really? Holy f#%&." They all laugh as they welcome me onto the Gravy train. And people, let me tell you....it is friggin' delicious, and scorching hot to boot (and stayed hot until I caved). The outer rim of the food mass is lined with the juicy-greasy goodness that seeps out from within. Finally, finally, I am home. I eat about a fourth of the Dalise and three bites of pancakes before I throw in the towel. All the while I keep saying out loud to anyone who will listen: "This is friggin' delicious." I take the remains home in two separate doggy boxes. I look forward to eating the rest of it over the next few days, possibly inside a warm, soft tortilla. Total bill? $11.75 American (or was it $11.25?). That's it. Un-friggin-believable. Granted, I only drank water (of which you'll need copious amounts if you get the Dalise). I leave a $5.00 tip which, even at about 45%, still feels ridiculously cheap and triggers my codependent guilt. By the time I take my leave, I am covered in sweat from the kitchen heat and full of joy from the whole damn experience. I will go back again and again. My only concern about Gravy is the amount of food that goes to waste if patrons don't request doggie-boxes, because nobody -- I mean NOBODY -- can finish what they start. See you there, if you dare. And please -- take your leftovers home.

Boneheaded Hospitality
by Janet O'Connor (2 reviews)
May 23, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I have had a few good breakfasts there and went back even though I am no fan of the to-go box culture of over serving your customers... although the preponderance of chubby folks in the room probably don't agree.
So I decide to make the trip and plan my route to get get there before they close at 3. There were four or five tables eating at 2:45 but upon entering I was met and told that they decided to close early and wouldn't seat me. I complained that I made a special trip (I don't live in the area) and was told simply, "Sorry about that." I said that this would be my last trip to Gravy and got, "OK"
I own a restaurant. This is the kiss of death. A business with this attitude toward its customers, or one with staff who embrace the culture of indifference, won't survive. Perhaps the owner didn't know his staff decided to take off early. If that's the case, fire everybody who worked at closing on Friday the 21st of May.

mmm....Gravy!!!
by Justin Timmons (10 reviews)
September 01, 2009
There are a lot of hipsters lined up on a lot of sidewalks that can spend all Sunday-Late-Morning-Early-Afternoon debating over the best breakfast in town. I think if they're all honest with themselves, they'd have to put Gravy in the top 3.
Of course you should try something with gravy on it. The Chx Fried Steak - Awesome. The Biscuits - Fantastic. The Scramble with Gravy on Top - Paradigm Shifting. I must shamefully admit my favorite menu item doesn't have gravy on it. The homemade corn beef hash is MIND BLOWING!!! Pour some of the Aardvark sauce on that plate of goody-goody-goodness and you're in for 30 minutes of taste sensation!
If you're not into gravy, there's probably something wrong with you but, the oatmeal brulee is a unique and tasty menu item as well.
