Read the menu closely
by Bianchi (48 reviews)
September 05, 2009 - Likes it
Denino's Pizzeria, located in Staten Island, has to be quite possibly the furthest I have traveled for my quest for the best pizza. Staten Island you ask? Yeah, you know, the place no city dweller goes to, the place only tourist go when they take the free ferry ride by the Statue of Liberty, the place only a few poor souls live and work in, yeah, Staten Island. Denino's is off the beaten path in more ways that one, so expect to get lost. Thankfully the locals are willing to help out, and the fact that at the mere mention of the name Denino's brought a sort of clarity to their faces got me excited, "Oh yeah, yeah, of course I know Denino's! Just go down...."
Now, don't make the same mistake I did. I sat down in this friendly little family joint, admits the kids talking loudly over their coke filled plastic cups, yellow clay residue still on their baseball uniforms, perhaps a bit too excited. I quickly perused their menu and ordered a large cheese. When it was brought out I was, I proceeded to analyze the crust, sauce, cheese, etc. when all of a sudden, a smell hit my nostrils that would have woken up a dead man.
**Sniff**, **Sniff** ... Hmm, fresh mozzarella, basil.....what the hell is that, I asked myself. I turned my head in the direction of the smell and saw it, Denino's margarita pie. Dammit, I thought to myself. What the hell am I to do now? Well, I did what any self respecting man on a mission would do. Complete the mission at all cost, thus I ordered their margarita. How did I miss the margarita on the menu you ask? Simple, their margarita is listed all the way on the bottom of the menu. I have no idea why.
Your first bite will be met with that familiar crunch sound, but wait, their crust is quite unique. For some reason, the term par baked comes to mind. The crust is simple, thin, crispy, and yet not too hard. Then there's the sauce...oh and what a sauce. Now, this is not your tradition pizza sauce, rather, Denino's uses a marinara sauce made from olive oil, ripe tomatoes, garlic, and herbs. Considered a little spicy compared to your traditional tomato sauce, this red sauce and crust combo is Denino's saving grace because their mozzarella cheese is well, mediocre at best.
Overall, Denino's makes a damn good pie. Is it the best? Hardly, but if you're tired of your run of the mill Manhattan pizza joint and are looking to travel off the beaten path, then I suggest to try Denino's.
