Really? Did you taste it first?
by Robe (10 reviews)
July 07, 2009 - Doesn't like it
Starter was Spicy Chicken Chile Relleno -- arrived hot and fragrant, but the pico was flat and the pepper was bitter. The pith (white bitter part) was still mostly present, but occasionally that happens. The filling was homogeneous in texture and generically "spicy."
At this point the Raspberry "Mojito" sugar-shake got passed to my date and I tried to recover my palette with water. The wine helped, but the "wine guy" was pretty damn clueless about grapes or regions. Fine, my own damn fault for thinking there would be a sommelier.
Next was the Baby Spinach Salad. It's all about tart and salt and some pine nuts slugging it out with the greens, and completely uninspired. The dressing was your basic vinaigrette, but it somehow managed to aggravate rather than marry the tastes.
The Chef special Buffalo loin was cooked rare as requested, and tiny. The surprisingly unbalanced chipolte sauce threatened to ruin the meat, but at least it was on the side. "But it's healthy" does not excuse a near inedible sauce. Even so, I tried two bites of the black bean relish/concoction before giving up. It was bitter, sour, under seasoned and frankly horrible.
We wound up going somewhere else for dessert, and saved the evening, thankfully. 2 out of 5.

WE HAVE A WINNER!!
by Bev (8 reviews)
February 27, 2010
If you follow my reviews, you'll notice we haven't had many good experiences of late. Well last night turned the tide. Our group of 12 had a marvelous meal at Season's 52 in Altamonte. The overall experience was great ... lovely setting, piano music in the background, the most attentive service we've had in a long time. The food was terrific. Many folks ordered the fish dishes and they looked and tasted great. I had the filet mignon and was blown away by the tenderness and the flavor. The veggies were perfect (baby carrots and asparagus) .. not overcooked and delicious. Of course the little shot glasses of dessert were a huge hit. :) While Season's 52 is a bit pricier than most, it is worth every penny. We highly recommend Season's 52 in Altamonte Springs.
