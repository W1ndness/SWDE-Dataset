Provence in Oklahoma
by A Tevington (707 reviews)
December 29, 2008 - Really likes it
A couple of French brothers own and cook at this restaurant. This is not high-class, snooty Parisian food. It's more a taste of Provence both in the food and the atmosphere--casual. If you're lucky enough to be there when osso buco is a special, order it and be sure to use the provided marrow spoon. The chicken crepes are comfort food with their mushrooms and bechamel, but may not be filling enough for hearty appetites. The boudin blanc is also nice--very mild, compared to its Cajun cousin. For Sunday brunch, I love the smoked salmon. Pretty much everything they cook is good. The desserts are excellent, but go fast. I understand the wine list is very good, though I don't imbibe. Service is good. If you have to wait for a table, check out the baked goods in the front room or the meats in the attached butcher's shop owned by the same proprietors.

Magnifique!
by Jim Surdick (1 review)
June 28, 2010
I have been a patron since the early 1990's at the Nichols Hills location, and have found the food to ALWAYS be excellent. The service can be a bit slow at times, but I have always felt that I am willing to wait a bit longer and pay more for a well prepared and very tasty meal. If you are looking for a "cheap" meal or a buffet (i.e. you're a "big" hungry guy) then this is probably NOT the restaurant for you. In the almost 20 years that I have been eating there, I had only one wait staff experience that was anything less than perfect. And, even in that situation, it was I that was a little insistent and demanding in that I only wanted a lunch item that day, even though it was, in truth, after the lunch menu had expired. The waiter checked with the kitchen, and indeed did provide me with lunch. I thanked him profusely for accomodating my request, and after he could tell I was truly thankful instead of just being a demanding customer, his attitude was fantastic. This is the PERFECT restaurant for a first date, an intimate evening, or just a good time at the bar. I have not had their breakfasts, but their lunches and dinners are always prepared to perfection, hot, and fresh. I really find the bad reviews to be suspect, and had to go and read the bad reviewers' other reviews. I suspect many that have complained are indeed difficult customers, at least if they were referring to the Nichols Hills location. In addition, I fairly recently went on the wine trip via train group to Ft. Worth with the owner, and he is really an entertaining and gracious host. I will always be a customer of this fine establishment.

French & Fabulous
by Jessica Lothrop (18 reviews)
February 08, 2010 - Likes it
We keep odd hours, and so we typically end up at a restaurant during the post-lunch and pre-dinner lull. It's led to some disasters in dinning. La Baguette Bistro was in one small way no exception to that rule, when we walked in no one seated us, the waiter looked right at us and then proceeded to ignore us for a good ten minutes. When a bartender overheard us grumbling, he stepped up and took our drink order.
When our waiter did take notice of us, he was an excellant server. We had no more complaints of La Baguette for the rest of our stay.
The food, wow. Exceptional French cuisine. The menu has a really good range of dishes at every price level too. from cheaper sandwiches, quiche, pasta, and salads; to main dishes of upper moderate price.
To start we had the Smoked Salmon, carpaccio style. It was served up beautifully with lemon oil, lemon zest, capers and toasted bites of baguette. For main courses, I had the Quiche Florintine, which came with nice side salad and the house dijon vinegrette; and my husband had the Beouf Fromage sandwich, an open faced affair with a side of their silky, buttery cream of asparagus soup. For dessert we both shared some Panna Cotta with a strawberry sauce and sliced strawberries. Hands down the best panna cotta I've ever had. But I expected nothing less from La Baguette, as they are renown as the place for desserts.
While La Baguette is not a place to go if you're on a tight budget, I would urge to save up your food dollars and go there if you are budgeting. Plan on spending at least $50 for two people, more if it's a special occasion. ;)
