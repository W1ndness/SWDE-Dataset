Plesantly surprised!
by Lynsey Van Nevel (16 reviews)
June 30, 2009 - Likes it
As someone who is new to barbecue and allergic to pork, I didn't have high expectations when my friends said we'd be dining at Jim 'n Nick's. However, I was very surprised by the variety of salads available, as well as the beef, chicken, and turkey options in addition to the traditional pork. I ordered the sliced beef brisket, potato salad, and collard greens. I loved the brisket -- tender, yet still full of wonderful smoky flavor. The potato salad was also very surprising -- full, vinegary flavor while still being very creamy, almost like mashed potatoes. I'd definitely go back again!

Southern BBQ Goodness
by jasminbaby (3 reviews)
May 27, 2008 - Likes it
Jim n' Nicks looks like a chain store, and basically it is, but the food is definately NOT chain food, nor the staff and their presentation of it.
The first time I went, I ordered from the bar for take-out. When I told the handsome bartender I'd never been there before, he presented me with a sample plate of their wares, along with a charming and detailed explanation of how and why they do things the way they do.
Everything on the menu is made in house, and fresh daily, right down to the hand-squeezed lemonade and salad dressings. The meats, including brisket, pulled pork, chicken and turkey, are smoked on the premises every morning and if they run out of something, they mean it! That's all for till tomorrow.
Traditional side dishes (too many to choose from), huge salads and sandwiches too big to put for even the biggest of bites, round out the menu with many options.
Don't forget to save room for the key lime pie!
