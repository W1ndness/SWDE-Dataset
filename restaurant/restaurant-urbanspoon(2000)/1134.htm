Specials were right up my allley...wonderful night!
by Nathaniel Neugent (5 reviews)
July 25, 2010 - Likes it
This is 4-star cuisine at 3-star prices! First-off, I think the pun is intended in the name because the Silo is the Elevator. Traveling up it reveals a quaint yet posh little restaurant that is intimate yet not claustrophobic. The waiter had us intrigued immediately when he listed the specials and they were 3 of my favorites: potato-leek soup, foie gras (on french toast), and lamb tenderloin. How many times do you go some place and none of the specials sound worth the money? Well not here. All 3 were well-prepared and wonderfully presented, in addition to the wife and kid's beef tenderloin. The soup had a touch of smokey bacon (maybe prosciutto) in it that made it distinct from others we've tried. It was not snooty place at all...in that the waiter allowed by wife to subsitute a side and he brought 3 spoons to let us share one soup. Oh, and nice shorts were acceptable. Portions were large for the price (the lamb tenderloin, priced at $34, was a full-length cut which would be at least $25 in the store).
What kept it from being 5-star in my book? Well little things like the red wine being almost hot/room temp instead of slightly chilled. The foie gras wasn't quite as cripsy on the outside as I've had at 5-star places. The food took a tad long but not to an annoying degree.
Bottom-line, I would defintely eat here again. Its is worth the drive from anywhere around San Antonio.
