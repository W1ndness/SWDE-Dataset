Duck was so greasy, I couldn't even eat it. yet the duck meat was dry and not properly roasted, I love pecking/crispy duck, but this was unacceptable. Their noodles were bland and greasy as well, vegetables suck as bok choy were not cooked well and were dripping with oil. I even gave the place a second chance by ordering the classic general tso, which was a disaster, the sauce had the consistency of Jello, and had no flavor. That's it, based on the fact that this place has a 90%+ rating on urban spoon, I'm convinced that the vast majority of people don't know what they're talking about.

Sang Kee 2nd to none!
by SaganRand (1 review)
February 15, 2010
I was first introduced to Sang Kee over 20 years ago. I've had Peking Duck and noodle-soups at many different places/cities over the years and Sang Kee is simply the best. Forget your 'neighborhood' chinese! Put it this way, your neighborhood chineese is to Sang Kee as Franco American canned pasta is to an authentic Italian restaurant. When you see 10 or 15 Chineese people waiting outside in the cold of winter just to get a table, that should tell you all you need to know. Get the Peking Duck, and definitely any of the soups that have their delecate won-tons and noodles. Enjoy!!!
