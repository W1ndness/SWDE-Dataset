Mediterranean Tapas
by goeschei (75 reviews)
March 26, 2009 - Likes it
I love small plates. I enjoy the variety and social aspects of sipping cocktails and nibbling appetizers with friends while shooting the breeze. Zaytinya offers Greek and Turkish specialties that are both pleasing to the palette and the pocket.
The restaurant is large and spread out over two floors. A large cutout allows everyone to see everyone else so it's definitely a place to see and be seen. The place is beautifully appointed too. There's space out front for patio dining under the stars when weather permits.
I love the grilled octopus. I am generally not a fan of octopus...raw, cooked, or otherwise but the grilled octopus here is amazing. The resulting texture is very appealing and not at all octopus-like and the flavor is a bit smoky and delicious. Recommended.

Very Hit or Miss
by Marissa (7 reviews)
August 10, 2009 - Doesn't like it
I wanted to like it. I really did. And some dishes were well-done, but overall I just can't say I loved it, particularly for the money. We ordered six dishes, hummus, and dessert. Hummus was good but not stellar. First dish - heirloom tomatoes (it was a special.) I was very underwhelmed - the tomatoes were underripe, and the dish lacked salt. Second dish, mushrooms, was good, but it would have been better with more dates. Third dish, scallop, was Excellent and the scallop was perfectly cooked. Fourth dish, calamari, was fine. Needed some spice and definitely needed more lemon. Fifth dish, veal cheeks, was quite good - we loved the spicy sauce. Sixth dish, veal sweetbreads - the sweetbreads were a little overcooked, but the fennel that accompanied them was wonderful. Dessert was some unmemorable ice cream thing. It's hard to hate, because the dishes that we liked were genuinely excellent, but for all the hype and money, this place should have been better.
