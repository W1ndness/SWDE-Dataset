Not worth the money
by Josh Gonzalez (2 reviews)
October 20, 2008 - Doesn't like it
The dishes at Filomena's are good; just not $28-$32 an entree good.
Filomena also has something of a try-hard feel to it, the menu proudly boasting familiar A-List names which have dined at Filomena's (it makes me wonder if they ever came back, and why I never see any of them when I go).
The hostess was a little bit weird and a touch unfriendly (bossy, even) and the servers knew very little, if anything, about the dishes or cuisine.
We began our meal with Mozzarella in Carrozza, breaded and friend mozzarella cheese with tomato sauce. Everything we expected it to be, but perhaps too heavy to began a meal with; we had trouble fitting our entrees after sharing that one app.
The $20 cavatelli in a tomato cream sauce was delicious and everything you'd expect from a decadent dish packed with so many calories; nice big portion too. Veal marsala for $32 was also nicely portioned and enhanced by beautiful cheese, mushrooms, and a thicker marsala wine sauce.
The wine list had some pretty standard faire listings with familiar Italian and Napa wine offerings, even a value-price section. No sommelier to be seen.
For a restaurant that doesn't offer particularly pleasing ambiance and decor, doesn't give high-end service, and doesn't serve high-concept or interesting food I was disappointed to see (and pay) higher prices than restaurants that are truly offering something special and unique. Central Michel Ricard, Brasserie Beck, Ceiba, DC Coast, Acadiana, Oya, Mie n Yu, etc... could have been better dining experiences with similar, if not smaller, price tags.
Pass up Filomena's from something truly inspired.
