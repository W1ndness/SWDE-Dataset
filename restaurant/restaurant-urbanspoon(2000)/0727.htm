Atmosphere and service top notch....
by Gulf Coast Guy (820 reviews)
January 03, 2010 - Likes it
Food, not so much. Although I would rate the overall experience here good and will go back for dinner, my wife and I were a little bit disappointed in the food for our New Year's Day lunch. Of course we had the big build up in mind of it being Emeril's.
We drove up and valet parked.....watched the guy pull my car up fifteen feet and put a cone behind it. LOL.
The atmosphere was superb and just like I would do it if I had a place. Woodsy inside, bistro like decor and very inviting. Once seated our servers showed up and from the first to last minute they took great care of us. Like Mr. B's USED to be.
Now on to the food.....
Ordered a Bloody Mary and it was nicely done with spiced green bean and olive garnish. Great tasting too. A bit pricy at $9 (Mr. B's is $5) but did not flinch since we were here.
Wife ordered sweet tea and they only have unsweet which was not a big deal.
Salad for moi: A baby spinach with goat cheese, nuts and some really fine crunchy potato sticks with a nice light dressing. A little heavy on amount of dressing but I cleaned my plate.
Soup for my lady: Shrimp and Punkin Bisque. That is how the server was saying it so....Nice rich soup with great flavor and some shrimp in the mix.
Entrees: I had to have the Fried Chicken and Waffles since I never have and really wanted to try it sometime. My wife went for the most expensive item she could as usual. a 14 oz Prime Rib for lunch. With broccoli and mashed taters.
This was where the meal fell down a bit IMO. My waffle, a corn waffle, was overcooked, dry and actually burned in places and out of the two chicken pieces, one was also very dry. My side was a slaw and seemed to be an afterthought plate filler.
The Prime was very fatty and my wife picked her way around it and ate what she could but left about half on the plate. Sides were great. For $32 this was a big letdown.
I got a nice specialty Cappuccino afterward with some great espresso.
Now, we WILL be back because there are a lot more dishes we want to try and the desserts looked divine.
We saw a couple of the other dishes coming out since we were near the kitchen and the house pork chop looked really good. They also had a scallops benedict on the lunch menu I almost ordered.
Overall for our first trip:
Atmosphere: 4.0
Service 4.0
Food 2.0
Overall 3.0
Out of four seashells.

A Special Night Out
by Stinky (196 reviews)
September 22, 2009 - Likes it
We had dinner here for our 8th Anniversary, and I am sure we couldn't have made a better choice. The detail that goes into each dish is just astounding. The depth of flavor is not something you get just anywhere. We did the Degustation Menu and were just absolutely blown away dish after dish.Wait staff is attentive, but discreet. They know exactly when to come see you, and when to stay away. The restaurant is laid out perfectly and theres not a bad table in the house. The wine list is like a New York phone book. If your training allows, peruse it at your leisure and make your selection, but I was all too happy to have the sommelier do it for us, and I gotta say - WOW!!! When you want to experience something beyond a "Steak & Potato", Emeril's is the place. Valet parking made this a much easier experience as well.
