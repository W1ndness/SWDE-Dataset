Wonderful Experience
by Cris A. (82 reviews)
October 25, 2009 - Likes it
I love eating in little ethnic restaurants. I recommend the Bamya - their version of friend okra. My husband who does not like okra enjoyed it. I also had the vegetable wrap made with their injera bread (Ethiopian sourdough flatbread). The mixture of spices was really good. The highlight was the Kahlua chocolate cake which was layered with chocolate mouse - to die for. Muzita has this nice, quaint atmosphere, and the service was excellent (the waitress explained the dishes to us and had great recommendations).

Amazing Food..Friendly Staff...wow Bathroom
by KristenC (1 review)
November 19, 2009 - Likes it
Listen guys the word is out ..the best Ethiopian food anywhere. Period. I finally found a spot to call my own (I moved here from DC area about 4 years ago). The freshness you can smell and the authenticity you can taste. Homemade because their mom is the cook. The Hamli and the Alitcha are to die for. I was thinking what makes this restaurant kick ass…attention to details. These guys are all about details. Let's see: Warm moist towels to clean your hand. Everyone greets when you walk in, when you leave. The bathroom is amazing. It is VERY clean and nicely decorated. The servers are the best I have seen anywhere. Knowledgeable, attentive and very friendly. It does get crowded on weekends. So maybe reservation.
