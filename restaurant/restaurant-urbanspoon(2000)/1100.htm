Delicious but a little Pretentious
by Koishii (4 reviews)
October 04, 2008 - Likes it
I had wonderful food at Ella.  It is definitely a place to see and be seen if you are into the local hipster scene, but unfortunately I felt that it detracted from the food itself and made the whole experience seem somewhat superficial.  That being said, everything about the food is superb...the quality and the presentation are top notch.  I should add that the entree price listed here is misleading.  Dinners start at 15 and most are in the 25-35 range. The portions are not huge, as is common with finely prepared and presented food, and you'll want to try a couple of appetizers because you won't be able to make up your mind--the ceviche was wonderful.  Our party averaged $75 each and we didn't drink a lot.

Go for drinks, not the food
by aric (1 review)
March 17, 2010 - Doesn't like it
Choosing between several restaurants I wanted to try in Sac, including the Grange, Waterboy, Mulvaneys and Biba, I looked forward to fine dining at Ella because of the reputation of Selland.
Shoulda gone with one of the others. The service was attentive. The food highlights were the fresh bread, cauliflower soup and the celery/date salad. Our waiter was in training to be a sommelier, so I allowed him to select a wine. He just hadn't been in school long enough and after bringing a dry sauvignon blanc which clashed terribly, he quickly exchanged it for a Riesling. My main course was sea bass with a lemon beurre blanc and artichoke. The sauce was great. The fish was pretty, but flavorless. I had to add salt to find any flavor beyond the sauce. The waiter brought a nice oaky chardonnay which, again, clashed with the lemon of the sauce. Strike 2 on the wine. Moved to the chef's suggestion of a pinot and all was fine from there.
My wife's dish, the handkerchief pasta, was again, pretty, but shy on flavor. The porcini mushrooms didn't come through and the pasta was past al dente - not mushy, just squishy.
Altogether, a $150 meal that coulda, shoulda and oughta have been better.
Aric gives it: ambiance ***
service ***1/2
food **1/2
