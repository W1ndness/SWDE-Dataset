Support small businesses
by bunni (4 reviews)
May 02, 2010 - Likes it
Liz has made the Dairy Godmother more than just custard. She's a friend and neighbor and a great business owner. She supports the community, the schools and all kinds of programs. She buys Girl Scout cookies from my troop for her thin mint custard. The girls even were able to come in and help crush the cookies (with sanitation in mind) and talked about being a female business owner. Above and beyond. Then there's the custard, sorbets and cobbler. Seriously? This is the best!!!

You want it to just be all hype but it's not
by jmg (25 reviews)
January 16, 2010 - Likes it
Yeah the long lines can be frustrating and make you want to not like it but taste it and see if you can keep that mindset. Having lived in Del Ray for a few years, I can attribute a good portion of my mid-30s weight gain to the Godmother. The only real thing to whine about is the limited selection---vanilla, chocolate, and the flavor of the day; but if the flavor of the day doesn't grab you, there's usually a baked good available or you can get one of the many sundae variations.

wonderfull
by JohnW (5 reviews)
September 05, 2008
so glad to have Liz in Del Ray.  i admit at first i just didnt get it.  three flavors?  huh?  but i am fully converted.  it's got a chef's devotion to quality and flavor and the place has a point of view.  all those things are sadly lacking in many food establishents in alexandria.  the baked goods are incredible too.  there are quirks but i gladly tolerate them for the wonderfulness of the product!  nowadays i get chocolate with toasted salted almonds, and whipped cream.  or if they have coffee custard i get that same as above but with hot fudge.  out of this world.   ... someday i'll get used to calling it custard instead of ice cream!
