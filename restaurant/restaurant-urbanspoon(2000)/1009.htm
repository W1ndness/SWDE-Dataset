February 16, 2010 - Likes it
(review dated 3/08):
been there couple of times, and even though they have a huge seating area, wait is inevitable.
food is surprisingly great, it being a mixed asian fusion that has a wide range of dishes from japanese (ex. sushi) to southeast asian (ex. pad thai). not sure if they have japanese chefs amongst them, but seems that they've got the sushi right. other food items are good; drinks are good too. (sorry it's been a while.) service is good with respect to their workload; we got our water glasses refilled promptly.
now if you can only get over that wait, which usually would be an estimated 20-45 minutes, depending on size of party and day of week, you should be all set. seems to be me they open till quite late too.

I can't understand any of the negative reviews about Seven Moons. The menu is extensive. Never been disappointed with the food. My Favorite Restaurant
by Laura (4 reviews)
April 03, 2009
I live in North Kingstown so this was a big big treat. Finally some decent dining! I have never felt unsatisfied after eating at Seven Moons. I have never tried a dish that I haven't liked there. My husband and I had never tried many of the Vietnamesel, Laos, or Thai dishes they have available before Seven Moons opened and since we have been trying different things like the sizzling rice wraps and hae kainge - I know not necessarily exotic but we're rural and choose not to go to the city.
The menu is big enough to bring just about anyone and they will enjoy. Seven Moons offers some of the best prices in dining hands down. Two people could easily each lunch there for $12 and be completely satisfied. The food is fresh, the waiters are very nice, and they have a nice ambiance. It's what N. Kingstown needed, so much so I believe they have added-on twice since opening a few years ago.
