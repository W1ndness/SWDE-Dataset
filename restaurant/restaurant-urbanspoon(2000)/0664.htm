Seattle Fish Market of the Sub World
by Jackie (81 reviews)
August 24, 2009 - Really likes it
In the world of fish there is the Seattle Fish Market, which is known for throwing the fish through the air and caught by a co-worker to wrap in newspaper. In the sub world there is Laspadas. When you place your order, the appropriate meat is sliced, layered and then thrown through the air to the sub maker at the other end of the counter who catches it in a sub roll (freshly baked). You then choose your condiments and other toppings and after which another layer of meat is laid on top to seal the sandwich. These are some of, if not the best subs I have encountered.

The Hoagie to end all Hoagies
by Kevin Poole (10 reviews)
March 18, 2009 - Really likes it
There is nothing like a Laspada. This is a local shop only found in South Florida, and at that there are only three. In true Deli form, Laspadas cuts your meat to order. Their signature is a covering of meat that tops the whole thing.
I prefer the Monster which is Ham, Roast Beef, and Turkey. Every time I'm in So Fla, I hit this place up. It wouldnt be the same if they were to franchise, but oh how I wish they would.
A Regular can feed two people, if you think your starving, try the large, but have a wheel barrel ready...
