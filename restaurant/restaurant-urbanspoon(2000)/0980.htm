Overall great food
by ellothielen (1 review)
July 06, 2009 - Likes it
I was very impressed by the level of French food at Paris 66. I realize that yesterday there were two cooks missing, or that's what the hostess said when we mentioned we'd been waiting for 30 minutes for our food. The serving staff didn't seem to be like the reviews I've read on this site. I realize they probably are much busier than the day after the 4th of July, and I can see how you could wait an hour since the restaurant was rather small, even with patio seating outside. To be honest, as a frequent visitor to France, this little bistro was just what I was expecting. If you want fast food, this is America. If you want an experience of France, you have to realize that a bistro is where family and friends come together to eat good food and have a good time. Waiting for food or running out of items on the menu isn't truly any of the servers fault.
My crepe was the Pigalle, if I remember correctly, which had cubed chicken and mushrooms in a creamy sauce on top of a buckwheat crepe. I really liked it, and my wife thought that her (i won't even attempt to spell it) French pizza was excellent. The French onion soup was just right, not too salty but just enough to make it quite tasty.
My wife and I were laughing at the menu, since it says that it speaks English with a French accent. Many of their servers there speak French, and you can hear the chef calling for objects or saying table numbers in French from where we were sitting. It was a little disconcerting at first, but it really fits with the Paris decor and feel of the place.
Finally, our server was quite hospitable, and regardless of what might have been missing (there was no more French onion soup for my wife when she decided she wanted a bowl after tasting my own), he kept us well watered and fresh.
Paris 66 is sure to be one of regular places to eat on Centre Ave. and Penn Circle South. I'll be sure to get there right when they open to get the best food and no wait. I would simply suggest for the management to get a larger kitchen and/or new establishment. They are much too popular for that size of a restaurant if they have people waiting an hour to be seated!

Great Food, Great Price, Great Service
by pgheats (2 reviews)
July 11, 2009 - Likes it
My husband and I went to Paris 66 Bistro for lunch on July 10, 2009. The food was outstanding, as was the service.
The Food: We ordered the Champs Elysees crepes and were delighted with taste and the flavor of the crepes. The highlight of the meal, however, was the red pepper soup (this was the soup of the day).
The Atmosphere and the Price: The bistro reminds me of bistros in France, but the price is FAR more reasonable at Paris 66. It is truly authentic French cuisine and the entire meal cost less than $15 per person.
The Service: Although we had to wait for a table, the wait was only 5 minutes. We've had to wait longer to eat at TGIFriday's. The waitstaff was very pleasant and fast. The service is more "down home" style (in other words, the waitstaff is responsive and treats you like you are an old friend rather than treating you like a walking wallet). Even the chef came by to say "bon appetite"! If you are expecting the type of service you would get at a hoitey-toitey, expensive French restaurant, you won't find that here. I find that to be a good thing.
Overall: I would recommend Paris 66 to all of my friends and can't wait to go back!

A fun evening
by Brits abroad (6 reviews)
August 17, 2009 - Likes it
Four of us went on the spur of the moment. We were welcomed warmly and asked if we wanted to eat in or out - we chose out. Felt like a city cafe - the owner has lots of interesting plans to develop it.
The menu looked pretty authentic, and our server was helpful and friendly (though he described one dish as coming "with au jus". We pointed out that a Pittsburgh restaurant might not know correct French but a restaurant owned by a Frenchman, with a French chef and French pastry chef, has no excuse.)
One of us ordered French soup, one ordered salad, 2 ordered soup and salad; everything, including the main courses, arrived at the same time; not good. The French onion soup was tasty though not the best I've had, and it had chunks of bread on the top rather than the more usual slice.
I had the pissaladière, which was excellent and very authentic. I also tried someone's quiche lorraine which was probably the best I've had in America (not that I've had many). Another of our number had a buckwheat crepe with leeks and smoked salmon and said it was excellent; as remarked elsewhere, the crepes do not arrive rolled or folded but with a topping rather than a filling. The portions were just right, though small by American standards, so we didn't get overfaced, leave with a doggy bag - or have to pass as usual on pudding! Don't expect a really filling meal, this is French style serving.
For sweet I had the crème brulée, which was exactly right. 2 of the others had crepes, the house crepe which was very like a Suzette, and which were flambéed at table with Grand Marnier. Would have worked better if inside, no breeze, and they'd warmed the GM a little first. Tasty though. The owner came round and chatted to us all afterwards and talked of his plans (including making a cidre bouché , which would be wonderful - currently it's a bring-your-own).
So - we loved the atmosphere, very bistro-like. The food was pretty good. The service was friendly and competent. The prices were fine.
Terminator-like, we'll be back.
