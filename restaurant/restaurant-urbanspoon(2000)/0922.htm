mobile review
by kewlfocus (1 review)
June 15, 2009 - Really likes it
As someone who has moved from Washington, DC, the foodie in me was curious about JJ Fins. We started off with their famous pigs in a blanket, which are huge, and appeared to be fried instead of baked, but quite delicious. I moved onto the JJs basket combo (14.99), where I chose the "blue moon" grouper, which is battered with the Belgian ale of the same name. This was a great take on the traditional fish and chips, though I had a hard time tasting the orange zest that was supposed to be added. Along with the grouper my second item was the fried sea scallops. The scallops had a nice texture, but the breading was falling off, still very tasty though. As sides I chose the macaroni and cheese and the kettle chips. Both were homemade and tasted great. As for the service, JJ Fins is a new restaurant, so I will give them the benefit of the doubt when my appetizer came out with the entrees. Other than that, the wait staff was very cheerful and helpful. I look forward to eating here again and trying some of their other items that stood out on the menu like the aged steaks and the fried chicken and waffles. With JJs and Casa Mia Italian, Tavares may be giving Mt. Dora a run for its money.

by BillYeag (1 review)
September 06, 2009 - Doesn't like it
We've been to J.J.Fins on 3 occasions. We love the atmosphere and the menu. As mentioned in an article from the Orlando Sentinel, the variety is great. It's apparent that they are tooling the drink policy which, earlier on, did not have pitchers of beer, nor happy hour. A pricey policy if you want to hang out and catch an NFL game.
Apalachicola oysters are the amongst the best to be had. Unfortunately, all 3 times we ordered them, they were gritty and in a puddle of water, which was previously ice, but sat about 20 min. Before arrival. They were also not freed from the bottom shell, and did not come with knife nor fork .
On 2 occasions, we were fortunate to meet chefs from other restaurants who were giving the new place a try. One liked his rockfish and lobster fritter, albeit the absence of lobster. The second sent his food back, as well did his wife. His reasoning was an inappropriate wait, yielding cold food.
We wish the best of success for this and any restaurant during this tough economy, but Fins need some attention to their problems. My e-mail was answered with a phone number from the manager, but when I called, she wouldn't come to the phone.

Save your money!
by Tavares_mom (1 review)
August 29, 2009
My husband and I went to JJ Fins on a recommendation by my parents who raved about their food! We were more than disappointed! For starters...the menu has WAY too many options, which I realize is now a red flag if you are looking for fresh food! How on earth could a place cook to order than many fresh items?
We ordered the Bait Bucket from the appetizer menu which consisted of 5 snow crab legs, a few shriveled oysters, super dry overcooked tasteless shrimp, and very bland crawfish! We had to ask for the drawn butter which should have come with it and when it arrived we found it was nothing more than butter flavored oil! The smell was also horrendous!
We saw good portions on plates coming out of the kitchen and after ordering what seemed like a huge appetizer we decided to share an order of fish and chips. Again, big disappointment! We ordered the entree about 15 minutes after we ordered the bait bucket and it still arrived first. It consisted of 2 fairly small pieces of minced "grouper" that was covered with a salty thick coating of batter and it was accompanied by overcooked potato chips that tasted similar to cardboard and a small cup of tasteless cole slaw!
Almost 10 minutes later the appetizer arrived which didnt really bother us so much because we had higher expectations and after that 1 shrimp I couldnt bring myself to eat anything else.
Now some people might ask why we didnt send it back...well we arent complainers. We ordered the food and we ate it. Although my husband pretty much ate all of it on his own...food is food to him and he was hungry after a long tiring day. Still, we wont be going back to JJ Fins again and I would really recommend going someplace else if you are looking for non frozen, fried, tasteless food. You could get fresher cooked food at McDonalds!
Oh...and my husband just reminded me that the bloody mary he ordered was very watered down and we had to send it back and the 2nd was a little better. Good sweet tea though! :-)

Never Again
by Liz (1 review)
January 08, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I took my dad to JJ Fin's to celebrate the purchase of his new home. We went for the steamed Maine Lobster promoted on their online menu. The waiter said they had a new chef and menu and they hadn't updated accordingly. Since the weather tonight is so dismal, we decided to stay.
We started with the blue corn tortilla chips and fish dip. The plate was stone cold, as were the chips and dip. The dip was on a red lettuce leaf that was so wilted it was soggy. The dip tasted of only cold relish. It was obviously sitting in a fridge already plated. It might have been good with fresh dip and warm chips.
The entrées took so long that 15 minutes after the waiter apologized for the delay, another staff member came from the kitchen to apologize and hand me a $20 gift card. 10 minutes later the food arrived.
Dad's Rock Lobster was exactly that - rock hard. It was so over baked that it had the consistency of a chicken breast. My Salmon was burned on the bottom and both ends and dry in the middle. The crab topping was drowning in pepper. They even managed to ruin the mashed potatoes. And they forgot the vegetables, which arrived later covered in pepper.
Yet another staff member came out to apologize for the delay and asked how our meals were. We told the truth, but I made it a point to give kudos to the staff for trying so hard and tipped them well.
I'm giving the gift card to a college student who is sick of Ramen noodles. I won't be returning to JJ Fin's.
