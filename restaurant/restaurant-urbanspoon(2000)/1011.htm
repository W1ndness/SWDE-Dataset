Sad State
by AJ (3 reviews)
October 26, 2009 - Doesn't like it
Fellini Pizzeria used to be a palace of thin crust pizza. A once cavernous and hip meeting place with ample couches and pinball machines with a espresso bar that served fresh cannolis, Fellini's has been stripped bare and converted into a small space. And along with the ratty and comfortable spacious charm and the old owner went evidently the formula for good pizza as well. The pizza when hot is not as good as it used to be... buf if a slice is what you are after be prepared for something with the taste and texture of reheated food. Sadly, there are no supurb places to get pizza on the east side. But the closest thing to it is Nice Slice on Thayer street apparently run by a former Fellini employee from it's LONG passed heyday.

Recommend | Not helpful
