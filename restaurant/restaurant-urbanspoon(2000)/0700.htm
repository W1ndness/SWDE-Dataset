yIKES... more flavor, please
by jessie (32 reviews)
November 04, 2009 - Doesn't like it
I stopped in to Ike's for lunch and ordered the French Dip Sandwich with a side of fries and coleslaw. Sandwich roll: stale. Meat: tough. Coleslaw: flavorless. Fries: cold and soggy. I don't usually send things back, but these fries were clearly made hours ago and I wasn't interested in another serving, so asked to replace it with soup. The waiter brought out a cup of the minestrone, which tasted like a can of progresso plus some salt & pepper. With the check, I was given a complimentary warm chocolate chip cookie, which was also stale. The service was friendly, but that was really the only positive part of the dining experience. Wouldn't go back, nothing seemed to work.

Grilled ahi
by foxspit (27 reviews)
March 24, 2010 - Likes it
It's been a couple of years since I ate there, but Ike's in the MSP airport is the best airport dining I've ever had. I ordered the grilled ahi that had a ginger glaze that I've been trying to recreate on my own ever since. The fish was thinly sliced and cooked to a medium rare (just seared and removed from the grill, apparently). The wasabi mayo was a nice touch.
Bill, the bartender, was a character. With 40 years of bartending experience, this guy was a pro. He recommended a local brew, so I had a Summit Pale Ale that was quite good.
I can't wait to have a layover in Minneapolis again so I can go to Ike's. I hope the ahi is still on the menu.

Huge Portions
by Terrae Hudek (12 reviews)
April 29, 2010 - Likes it
The burgers are the thing at Ike's and I have to say that they are pretty good but nothing spectacular. They range from $13 - $15 each but they are huge and you get a lot of shoestring fries on the plate as well. The best part of the meal is when they bring a small, warm chocolate chip cookie out for each person.
I didn't look at the wine menu but the beer and cocktail menu is extensive with a few unique options. Cocktails are in the $10 range.
The atmosphere is casual upscale with dimmed lighting. I found the service lacking for a place that is trying to be upscale.

Nothing Extraordinary
by ED (20 reviews)

I've been to Ike's 3 times now. Once for brunch, once for happy hour and once for dinner. A common theme I noticed with my three experiences is that quantity generally exceeds quality and the price to value ratio is not compelling. Brunch was okay but nothing memorable. Appetizers are good and drinks are strong. Dinner was okay but there are better burgers at a lower price easily available in the twin cities. I would avoid the battered walleye. It was mushy, overpriced and uninventive. Overall, I would say it's okay but over priced. Also, service left a lot to be desired.
