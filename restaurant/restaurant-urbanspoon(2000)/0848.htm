Thai volcano
by A Tevington (707 reviews)
December 30, 2008 - Really likes it
This small, strip mall restaurant serves one of the best soups known to humankind. It's tom kha gai, with chicken broth, coconut milk, lemon, chicken, mushrooms and assorted Thai vegetables. This soup alone is worth the visit. It's very peaceful and comforting, so as to get you ready for the heat to come in the main meal. The number of entrees is very limited, but they're all good. The server, who owns the place, will ask you whether you want level 1 to 3 of spicy heat. If you haven't eaten there before, she'll refuse to serve you No. 3. Thank her profusely. No. 2 will singe your tonsils. No. 3 should be reserved for industrial processes like smelting ore. Listen to her when she suggests what you should eat on your visit. She really does know best and will offer definite suggestions--almost commands.

Tana Thai - Great Authentic Thai Food
by BB (1 review)
May 05, 2009 - Likes it
Although it's always a tough choice between Tana Thai and Papa Dio's each I pull into the parking lot - Tana Thai has never let me down, not even once! The cuisine has a great depth of flavor and the selection, while limited allows you to explore many dishes during each visit. Everything is made to order, no goopy, starchy left-overs at TT. The quality is comparable to that of a swanky, hotel-like Thai restaurant, but the food is better as are the prices! I would however like to have the option of enjoying a cold beer to help with some of the heat (I like #2, but think I could handle #3) but no alcohol is served here. For those of you who are "Thai shy" - try the crab fried rice, it won't let you down!
