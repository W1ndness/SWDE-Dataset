Greasy Disaster ...
by Larry McGoldrick (128 reviews)
October 20, 2009 - Doesn't like it
There are many excellent Asian restaurants in the Duke City environs.
Banana Leaf is not one of them.
I ordered a noodle dish with some kind of seafood. I asked for it to be very spicy. The only flavor I could detect was some kind of tasteless hot stuff that completely overpowered any other spicing. Horrible -- I couldn't finish it.
Jane ordered something (it matters not just what it was) that turned out to be tasteless and very greasy. Fundamentally inedible.
This place gives Asian food a bad name. I cannot understand its popularity. Beware. Stay away.
We left and decided to have dessert and coffee just down the road at the Village Inn, hoping to get the bad taste to go away. Second bad mistake of the evening (read my review of Village Inn on these pages).
Zero-and-two for this Friday evening.

Lunch
by RDS (16 reviews)
December 10, 2009 - Likes it
My only experience at Banana Leaf was at lunch, but my co-worker and I missed the lunch time specials, so we had to just order normal "sized" dishes.
I ordered some spicy chicken dish (I cannot remember the name of it - could have been General Tzo's chicken) and it came out spicy and still had the flavor I expected from the dish. The meal was filling, and not lacking quantity.
I need to check this place out again, to see if it has changed or is still a good place to eat Asian type dishes. But I did like my dining time here, so I voted "Like it".

Good if you know what to order
by Arthur (1 review)
December 18, 2009 - Likes it
My co-workers and I eat here for lunch once a week. There are two Asian restaurants closer, but this one is better than most - you just need to know what to order. The menu is not just Chinese, or just Vietnamese, it has variety. Some dishes are excellent - such as the House Lo Mein, others are not much better than average such as the Pad Thai. In general, I find that Thai-only restaurants do curry better than the Banana Leaf and I steer clear of dishes that mention brown or white sauces. Sometimes you can find their coupons in the Value Pack that comes in the mail.

This place does use msg, i for one am fine with that i think for what it is (neighborhood chinese joint with under $entrees) it is good.
