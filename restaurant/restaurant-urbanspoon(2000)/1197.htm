Worth the Drive from Indianapolis.
by Corey Dalton (28 reviews)
April 06, 2009 - Likes it
While visiting St. Louis, a friend and I decided to give Niche a try. I'm happy to report that the glowing reviews are right on the money! Eating at Niche represents the best dining experience I've had in years. The ambiance was cozy and warm, the staff was friendly and unpretentious, and the food was amazing. Every dish that we sampled (including cheese bread, pig's head, sweetbreads, the pork duo, and the scallops ) made us love the place a little more. The chef even sent out an extra appetizer and some palate-cleansing lemongrass-chamomile sorbet as a way of saying thanks for making the trip, which was quite generous. There was little to no wait between the various courses; everything came out perfectly timed and cooked. Our waitress was on-the-ball and a pleasure to chat with -- even if she never did manage to smuggle a spoonful of the bacon ice cream away from the kitchen for me! For dessert, we sampled three types of house-made ice cream (horchata, vanilla malt, and orange julius) as well as the liquid chocolate cake. They were all as great as they sound. At the end of our meal, the chef came out to ask how we liked our food, a gesture that we really appreciated. As for the price, it was surprisingly reasonable given the artistry of the food. Our combined bill was less than half what it would have been at many fine dining establishments, including most of those cheesy chain steakhouses (I'm looking at you, Ruth's Chris). I can't wait to get back to St. Louis some time soon so I can eat at Niche again. My only questions is -- when can we get an Indianapolis branch?

Outstanding meal
by Spidersva (10 reviews)
July 03, 2010 - Likes it
In short the food was outstanding. From the mains (pork belly, skirt steak) and their accoutrements (my wife had some miso parsnip puree that I was basically licking off her plate) to the sides to the desert, each part showed a creativity and a skill that is not frequently seen.
The wine list is a nice length (not a one pager but not a gaudy book) with some creativity as well. We had a Sequana pinot (which was lovely - Bommarito distributors has good stuff in MissourI) and a Palazzo Cab Franc (gotta love cab franc as a stand alone).
Only slight knock was the service - our waiter was super nice and knew the food well, but there would be stretches where we wouldn't see him. They were probably down a waiter...no biggie.
Special meal, glad we went, and you will be too.

Why No Michelin Rating?
by LetsGoOut (8 reviews)
April 10, 2009 - Really likes it
This is the best restaurant in St. Louis. The menu is half somewhat common items and half edgy dishes. Don't let the word "common" fool you, however. Everything is special - from the risotto to the local trout to the pigs head. There's a combination of love and talent mixed into every dish. This is the perfect place to impress a date or a business colleague. It's also a great place for dinner any day of the week. This is the one restaurant in St. Louis that deserves a Michelin three star rating.
