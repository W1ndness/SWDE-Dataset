Soul-Nourishment
by Miranda (22 reviews)
December 08, 2008 - Likes it
Al di La is open for lunch now and since my husband and I have been wanting to check the place out but were: a. afraid the hype would lead us to be let down, and b. afraid our wallets would spontaneously explode if we ate dinner here....we were delighted at the new lunch option.
I have to say, I was very happy with the food, the service, and the decor.
Al di La definitely has an Old World down-at-the-heels charm from a design point of view. The floor tiles are chipped and the ceiling paint is peeling, but somehow this all feels intentional. Soothing opera-esque music plays, the tables are worn butcher blocks and the walls are hung with interesting artwork.
The food is the draw, though. My husband had the tagliatelle with ragu and I had the lasagna al forno. Yum. The ragu sauce on the tagliatelle seemed to be the same sauce on the lasagna, but it was so good that we didn't mind the overlap ;)  The bechamel sauce was mingled nicely with the meat and tomato sauce, the pasta was perfectly cooked, and the lasagna had a dark, crispy crust on it. My favorite touch: the lasagna had small kernels of roasted garlic and rosemary in it.
The perfect thing for a cold day.
We decided it wouldn't be fair to review a place if we'd only tried two dishes, so we *forced* ourselves to order a dessert (such sacrifices...).
We got the apricot almond crostatta, which came with a vanilla cream. It was good in a very Italian-dessert sort of way (crumbly crust, subtle flavors, etc.).
Having dipped our toes in for lunch (which ran us nearly $50), I think I can safely say that we'll be back for a special dinner.

what we expected
by MMMMMM (3 reviews)
February 01, 2009 - Likes it
Coming from NYC to Park Slope on a Saturday night for dinner at Al Di La we knew would have a long wait.  When i checked in with our mairtre d i was immediately consumed by his traditional italian accent that reminded me of the Fellini Movies my mother always spoke about.  he said, "its very crowded tonight.  about an hour and a half."   
Luckily the owners of Al Di La have a wine bar next store where you can sip wine while while you wait for your table.  for the lucky ones the wine bar has some tables where you order food from the restaurant, but we decided we wanted experience if eating in the restaurant.  
after picking at our cheese plate which was very good despite having more pear slices than cheese and nibbling on assorted olives it was our turn to feast.
From my prior reading and from my interrogation of the waiter i concluded that  the 2 best dishes are their Taggletini with Ragu and a pork scallopini with proccuito.  The Ragu turned out to be the hit of the table yet everyone else still loved their food. The ravioli filled with winter squash in a butter and sage sauce was a not to distant  2nd.     While delicious it was very rich.
The Ragu was cooked perfectly and had just enough meat to flavor the sauce without overpowering the actual tomato portion of the sauce.  the pasta was cooked perfectly to the extent it had the right amount of firmness to make the twirl in my spoon every more enjoyable.  Hello godfather
The appetizers were all very good but none were outstanding. The salad with Jerusalem artichoke hearts in a warm sherry vinagrette was extremely satisfying.  the vegetables in the salad had a nice mixture of bitter and sweet that was tossed playfully in crunchy and leafy Boston lettuce..
we all left happy, full, and ready for bed..  a round of drinks, 4 appetizers, 4 meals, and 2 deserts ran us $120.00..
the wait, the decor, the food and the price were all what we expected.  if you can stand the wait the night turned out to be exactly what we expected.  a nice night out in Brooklyn.
