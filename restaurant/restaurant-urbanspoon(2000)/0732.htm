Great Po-boys and so much more
by Bill Mac (18 reviews)
May 11, 2009 - Likes it
Located in Old Jefferson, just over the parish line from NOLA, sits a little restaurant with cafeteria style seating and a heck of a menu. The largest section of the menu is devoted a local lunch favorite, the Po-boy. Po-boy varieties here range from a very delicious fried shrimp to an especially delectable Cochon de lait (roasted pork). Po-boys are not all that is on the menu. Briskets, blackened gulf fish, stuffed mirliton, etc are available as plate specials. There has yet to be an entree or sandwich that I have not loved. Some have complained about the "long table / bar seating" when it is crowded (usually between 1130am -1230 pm) but just take a seat where you can and make a friend. The prices are not cheap by local standards but manageable and well worth it. The quantities are large and the quality is excellent. That being said, if you are visiting New Orleans this lunch favorite is highly recommended. Get a po-boy/special, order a side of dirty rice, and leave room for a homemade brownie or cookie. You will thank me as your friends have to roll you out of the place. It's New Orleans... half the reason you came here was to eat.

Worth the Trip

July 07, 2010 - Really likes it
I visit NOLA frequently from NJ. I found this place through urbanspoon, in search for real local food, instead of chains or tourist traps. The place is a dump, but the food is outstanding. Crabby Jack's specializes in Po-Boys (subs on french roll for the rest of us outside of NOLA). This place has become a mandatory stop for me when I'm in the NOLA area. Why you ask? In NJ the line would be out the door 24/7. Favorites - Brisket Po-Boy dressed (lettuce, tomato and mayo). A great sandwich full of flavor that melts in your mouth. The ultimate sleeper, the Turkey Po-Boy. A turkey sub you say. I say yes. This is the best turkey sub in the US.
