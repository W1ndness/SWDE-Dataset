INSULTED AND DISRESPECTED ON FATHERS DAY JUNE 21, 2009
by edtroulhac (1 review)
June 21, 2009 - Doesn't like it
I am a father and a grandfather, who resides in Blue Bell, Montgomery County. I am a school administrator with a masters degree. I am a American of African, European, and Native American descent.
On Sunday morning June 21, 2009 I happily drove with my wife from Blue Bell, Pennsylvania to enjoy a Fathers Day Breakfast/Brunch. We arrived at Cakes at 10:30 a.m. and my wife put her name on the list and we were told there is a 45 minute wait. We poured ourselves some coffee and waited. the person who took our name came to speak with us about 50 minutes later and said we still had to wait. We said fine. Fifteen minutes later we were seated. We couldn't wait to enjoy the food cooked at Cakes. I ordered the steak entree medium well and a chocolate croissant. My wife ordered a scone, an order of bacon, and coffee.
The coffee, scone, and chocolate croissant came fairly quickly, but from there the service when extremely bad.
We waited and waited for my steak entree and my wife's bacon, but it never came as people around us who were seated after us received their food. The young blond lady who took our order came to our table and said 'sorry for the delay, your food should be right out" and "the manager is giving you your chocolate croissant and scone on the house". We said fine but still was looking forward to the rest of our food. I had just taken my diabetic medicine and had planned to eat within a prudent time of taking my medicine.
We continued to wait and still other people seated after us were receiving their food too. My wife and I decided to leave. The young lady who took our order didn't offer any words of don't leave the food is on the way out, but told us we had to pay for our coffee.
I looked into the kitchen and could see two cooks who seemed to be amused and laughing. I asked the same young lady who took our order and took our money for the coffee who the manger was. This time she replied to me that she was the manager filling in for the real manager who was out sick.
Now I feel that I'm being lied too and truly being given the run around. I don't know what my wife and I did to bring about this atrocious treatment.
We left Cakes at 12:30 and drove to Hymies Too in Lafayette Hill, and finally enjoyed a Fathers Day meal at 1:25 p.m.
I planned on contacting my congressman, state senator, and civil rights organizations for being insulted and disrespected on Fathers Day 2009. I will never forget the treatment my wife and I had to endure.

Best Eggs Madame ever!!
by Amy Lynn Saborsky (1 review)
July 21, 2010
My husband and I went to Cake on 7/18/10 and were very happy with the food. We did have to wait 30 minutes for a table which was a downside, but it was crowded and the wait was totally worth it. We love how you can put your name in and then walk around town while you wait-the restaurant staff just calls you when your table is ready. It was a while before we got our food after ordering, but it's clear that everything was fresh and made to order. My eggs madame came out on a croissant so buttery it was amazing. The poached eggs were fluffy and the hollandaise sauce was insanely amazing. On the way out we got two cupcakes which were the perfect ending to a perfect brunch :). I highly recommend Cake-you may have to wait a while to sit but it is well worth it!
