mobile review
by Kelly (14 reviews)
June 24, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I'm on the fence about this place. We experienced very good service, professional, but not as friendly as we're used to even in the nicest places in Portland. Prices are very high, which was expected, however, I still want to feel like I "got what I paid for". Yes, the tableside-prepared Caesar was excellent, the cocktails perfect, the steaks wonderful, but there are many other places in town that easily compare for far less money. One major error with our dinner, the shrimp in our starter were NOT cleaned properly, which at this caliber of restaurant is just plain embarassing. The wine list is very good, spendy ofcourse, but staff was knowledgeable. I'm glad we tried ElGaucho, but my husband prefers Ringside. For $350+ for two, I expect more (we had a bottle of "cheap" wine, at about $75), so if it is going on an expense card, go for it! If you want a special dinner for 2, try Ringside, Higgins, Andina, or Blue Hour.

El Gaucho - Grande Experience / Grande Price
by CulinaryCat (16 reviews)
April 30, 2008 - Likes it
El Gaucho has romantic atmosphere, a smart and friendly staff, valet parking, live music and a wonderful menu Spanish/Latin influenced, American, gourmet food. Definitely make reservations many days in advance just to insure that you get a seat. You will leave El Gaucho very satified, entertained and with taste buds singing, but if you decide to eat at El Gaucho make sure that your wallet is fat because the bill will most likely be in triple digits even for two people
