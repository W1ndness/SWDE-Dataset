Great Seafood
by jacques (4 reviews)
May 08, 2009 - Likes it
I've eaten here several times since they've opened the new retail/restaurant and each time the experience and the food has been fantastic. What the online menu doesn't show is that they must have a dozen varieties of fish that they can broil, grill, pan-sear or fry and a bunch of different spices or rubs that can go on it. It's very popular at the moment and seating is fairly limited so be prepared for that. High recommended.

Rochester's Best Fish Fry!
by pat (2 reviews)
May 31, 2010
I ventured to Palmer's for take-out on a recent Friday at around 5:00 PM. The place was booming, both for sit-down & take-out orders. Although I would probably avoid being there on Friday nights in the future, the wait was worth it! My husband & I each ordered a "traditional" fish fry (breaded haddock, fries, cole slaw, roll) & agree it was the best we have had in years. The generous portion of fish was mild, (not fishy), flaky, meaty, delicious, & not at all greasy. The fries were perfectly cooked (crispy on the outside & soft in the inside). The side of cole slaw was a perfect combination of sweet & sour. The white roll was yummy (not the cottony, "Wonder" bread type often thrown in with fish fries). I look forward to trying a different side next time, as there are many from which to choose. Despite the hectic environment that evening, service was efficient & friendly.

Fantastic
by eb (10 reviews)
October 30, 2009 - Likes it
My wife and I just had an early dinner at Palmer's. It was our first time in their newly opened sitdown restaurant on Jefferson Road. We had the haddock fish fry, with the smashed potatoes and mac salad. The fish was perfectly done, and the coating was yummy. The Tartar sauce was really, really good, as I normally don't eat Tartar sauce, but I had all of this and wanted more. The potatoes were perfectly done and the mac salad which looked way too slippery, wasn't! It was also the best mac salad I've had (other than my own) in years.
We also sampled the green beans almondine and Tony Carrettas Cajun sausage, both were excellent. We brought some of the sausage for tomorrow night's seafood and sausage gumbo dinner.
Palmer's is a treat not to be missed! Highly recommeded.
