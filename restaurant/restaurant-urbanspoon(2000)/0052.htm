Message from Baraonda

Stylish Midtowners snack on Euro-style pizzas at this romantic trattoria.

  • Woodfire Pizzas
  • Private dining room
  • Serving lunch and dinner

This Italian cafe in the heart of Midtown, adjacent to the famous Fox Theatre, dishes up classic Italian dishes and delicious brick oven pizzas. Baraonda serves both lunch and dinner and is open late: great for a post-theatre snack. A long list of pizzas, variety of salads, antipasti and wine list is sure to please every guest. Because of our convenient location a block from the Fox Theater, our reservation policies are in place to accommodate the many theater-goers that dine at Baraonda before a show. Please note that all parties must be present to be seated and we hold reservations for 15 minutes before welcoming the next party. We strive to provide the best service for every customer and look forward to serving you!

Fantastic Wine Bar!!!
by JVC (66 reviews)
January 20, 2010 - Likes it
On our way to the Fox and had 30 minutes to grab a bite, walked by the Wine Bar storefront of this restaurant and decided to grab a seat at the bar. Friendly staff and we were told we could easily be out on time. The menu is limited and very fried...but you could still get things from the main restaurant. We started with the Fried Gnocchi with Prosciutto that were really fried dough balls with a thin slice of prosciutto on top (do not attempt to bite into these as you will end up with a piece of ham hanging from your mouth...it happened to both of us!) these were wierd but good. Next up we ordered two Fried Stuffed Pizzas which were really mini calzones that were suprisingly light and delicate...I think I just got goose bumps! We had the goat cheese with spinach and a chicken one...both came with the most incredible marinara, so simple and crazy good...the perfect meal and we were out in 30!! Again, the staff is great and very accomodating, the wine menu is good...but could be written in a more user friendly way as it's kinda hard to read if your in a rush...we had one of my standbys...the Qupe Syrah.
