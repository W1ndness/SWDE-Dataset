Message from Charro Tacos & Tequila

The premiere Latin Fusion restaurant in downtown Milwaukee

  • Mexican & Latin Fusion Entrees
  • Fresh seafood Ceviches
  • Happy Hour

Charro Restaurante is the premiere Latin restaurant in downtown Milwaukee.

Like a steady drum beat in sync with the pulse of the city, Charro transforms its guests' expectations of Latin cuisine. This Latin restaurant fuses Latin culture with only the freshest ingredients to deliver extraordinary culinary creations. Hand-selected seafood from Hawaii makes up a variety of Ceviches. Entrees include Cuban favorite Ropa Vieja, Spanish-Style Seafood Paella and 'as big as your head' Burritos.
Charro incorporates some of the classics, including tacos, enchiladas and chimichangas, but with an exotic twist and quality unsurpassed. With specials including $2 Tacos on Sundays and $1 Frozen Margaritas on Thursday nights, this Milwaukee restaurant?s progressive atmosphere and outstanding service is sure to spice up the night with some delicious Latin culture and cuisine.

Charro Restaurante - Kobe & Crab Appetizers
by Your Milwaukee Dining - Reviews (4 reviews)
April 25, 2010
It's increasingly becoming more and more common that people are watching what they spend but still wanting to enjoy a night out. This was my thought process Friday night as we decided to make a stop at Charro on Milwaukee Street. The restaurant opened at 5pm and we walked in a few minutes after to a rather empty with the exception of a group of employees finishing up what appeared to be a meeting in the back of the restaurant.
Knowing that we were there for only a few drinks and appetizers we decided to grab a seat at the bar and look over the menu. The bartender was attentive and provided his recommendations, details of what tequila was used in which drinks and provided general good conversation. We decided on two margaritas, the El Charro made with Herradura Silver Tequila, Grab Marnier, fresh lime juice and their own Charro mix, ($12) and the San Lucas Margarita which was the same as the El Charro except prepared with Cabo Wabo Silver Tequila ($12). The Margaritas were great recommendations from the bartender and in no way had an overpowering Tequila flavor. It turned out to be a perfect blend of drinks to enjoy and relax after a long week. These are two drinks you can order with confidence, but if looking for something a little more exotic just check with the bartender and he/she can point you on the right direction.
As always drinks usually lead to dinner, but we kept our pledge and decided to just grab two appetizers or tapas as Charro refers to them. We spent about 5 minutes looking over the menu before we made our choices. The bartender at this point was prepping drink ingredients and making small talk with the waitstaff and appeared to have forgotten about us at the bar. I decided to not say anything and just wait it out and see how long it took for him to return. Approximately 10 minutes later he and I made eye contact and I think at that point he realized we had been waiting and came right over.
The first appetizer ordered was the Crab Empanadas, serving of 5 ($12) followed by Kobe Flautas ($8). When the appetizers were brought out the kitchen had made a small mistake and had prepared us Pork Sliders instead of Kobe Flautas, we politely told the server what we ordered and she said she would correct the order. We dug right into the Crab Empanadas and were surprised to get a nice portion of crab inside each perfectly fried empanada. The portion size was satisfying and turned out to be a great bar snack. It was served with two small sauces drizzled on the plate accompanied with tiny pieces of melon. There was no problem finishing everything on the plate and I would gladly order this appetizer again to satisfy a crab craving.
Shortly after we finished the Crab Empanadas the Kobe Flautas arrived with a small apology from the waitress. The error seemed to work out well. Not having the dishes come out at the same time allowed us to enjoy each item separately without rushing to make sure we still ate everything while it was hot.
The Kobe Flautas were just that when they arrived, hot! Once the temperature subsided and the first bites were taken you could find a generous amount of Kobe Beef inside each of the 4 flautas. Charro cut no corners with this appetizer which could have easily been prepared heavy on the peppers and light on the Kobe, but the exact opposite was true as they didn't skimp on the Kobe. The beef was tender and the peppers added a nice spicy kick which only added additional welcomed flavors!
Charro provided a queso dipping sauce to with the Flautas which worked extremely well. The queso was a mixture of white and yellow cheese with a spicy kick. I was afraid we would run out of dipping sauce but we showed restraint and made it last!
Granted their were issues with bar service when placing our order and having the wrong appetizer brought out, the appetizers themselves were excellent and would be ordered again without question.
Charro has served me dinners in the past and I always seemed to enjoy myself. I was glad to see that they provide an excellent dining experience at the bar as well. Be sure to check out their appetizers every now and then. They tend to keep it interesting and change up the menu periodically so you may be surprised by what you will find.
Check it out and let me know your thoughts.

Perfect bar experience!
by ALS-Milwaukee (2 reviews)
May 26, 2010 - Likes it
A girlfriend and I were on our way to a sporting event and needed a fab place to grab a couple cocktails and some appetizers without having to make the time commitment of sitting down at a table.
Charro fit the bill completely! The bartender was attentive and greeted us with a smile. After ordering my somewhat complicated margarita-substitute, Milagro tequila, seltzer, fresh lime juice with a half-salted rim (aka Real Housewives star Alexis's version of the "skinny girl margarita"), I was a little nervous as to what this middle-aged male bartender was going to pour. My fear drifted completely as I took my first sip of the delightfully refreshing (and low-cal) cocktail.
What's a Latin fusion restaurant without the guacamole?! Of course we had to try the table-side appetizer, and were pleasantly surprised with the result. The chips crispy, not too salty, accompanied a HUGE portion of perfectly seasoned guacamole. (They ask you exactly how spicy you prefer your guac). The bartender also offered to serve the guacamole with plantains, to which we immediately accepted. A great alternative to traditional corn chips!
Other tasty appetizers included Kobe Flautas (spicy and delicious!), Chicken Empanadas, and my absolute favorite, Torta Sliders. If you're a pork belly fan (or even if you're not), you MUST try these sliders! Absolutely delicious and crave-worthy!
The bartender knew we were in a hurry, and made sure we had our check in hand as soon as we were finished eating. I must say, VERY affordable!
Look forward to returning to Charro again soon!
