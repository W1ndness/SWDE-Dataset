If I had to recommend one pizza to try in Louisville,
by blackbird (52 reviews)
December 16, 2009 - Really likes it
it would be Impellizzeri's. I've eaten pizza in every city I have visited and Impellizzeri's is unique. I'm talking about the Sicilian deep dish. They also offer a hand tossed and a thin crust, but I've never tried them. When I think about Impellizzeri's, I only envision that 2 inch high square slab of cheese and homemade Italian sausage. Literally, one piece of Sicilian will take you down for the count. I hear the Wick's comparison being thrown around, but Wick's does not offer any pizza this thick. It is a unique flavor -- you might like it, you might not -- but you should try it at least once. The sauce is not sweet at all, but rather robust and spicy.
If you do get the Sicilian, be prepared to wait at least a half hour because this beast will take a while to cook. As thick as it is, I've never had one come out gooey or undercooked.
We had a slight problem with the veggie hoagie Annie ordered, but the server was extremely conscientous about making sure everything was ok.
Pizza Pantheon Comparison: I have never seen any pizza as thick as the Sicilian. It is unique so far as I know. Maybe some of the Chicago style pies are a similar size, but the flavor is totally different. I'm not crazy about Chicago style in general.
Bottom line: I cannot say that I have a favorite Pizza place. Sometimes I want Bearno's. Sometimes Papa John's. Sometimes even Mr Gatti's buffet. A few times a year, I really want Impellizzeri's, and it never disappoints.
Update - 3/20/10 I recently found out this is not the 'original' Impellizzeri's. This location is owned by a group of investors and the recipes are not exactly the same. You should support Tony Impellizzeri's restaurant in near Hurstborne.

Not what I expected
by Little Italy (1 review)
May 09, 2010 - Doesn't like it
After reading many good reviews for Impellizzeri's, I drove out to HWY 22 to try it. I have a hard time believing that this pizza was ever voted Best of Louisville. If this is the best Louisville has to offer, I would rather not eat pizza. I know pizza from Boston North End and New York - The pizza at Impellizzeri's was even difficult to chew and swallow. The sauce tastes like sweet tomato paste. Nothing about it tasted fresh. The crust did not taste homemade. Too much gummy cheese made it difficult to chew. The service was not the best. The lemonade tasted like it was served from a dirty cooler.Sadly, I will not recommend to friends and will not return. Pizza should never be made with a sweet sugary sauce and too much is never a good thing. I guess we will keep looking for a true italian pizzeria. Don't let the name fool you, Impellizzeri's on 22 is not real italian. (I have not been to the other restaurant and have been told they are not owned by the same people)
