Albuquerque Mainstay with Art, Too
by David from Culver City (61 reviews)
August 14, 2010 - Likes it
I have been going to Frontier on my occasional visits to Albuquerque since the mid-80s, and it's always been consistently good. There is certainly better food around, but it's tasty and cheap, and some of the offerings are excellent. As with anywhere in New Mexico, the quality of the chile determines the quality of the food, and Frontier's is pretty good. I like the enchiladas with chopped green chiles or with red, but I have other friends who prefer them topped with their green chile stew. I think their best features are their tortillas, which, plain as they are, are fresh, homemade, and quite good.
Back in the day, Albuquerque was where we'd drive down to run errands and go dancing over the weekend, and Frontier was always our rendezvous point. It's big, centrally located, and it's always possible to find a seat. Oh, and you can still figure out how to get there when you're drunk.
After so many years, I never really noticed the walls, other than a couple paintings of John Wayne, until somebody told me recently that a professor for his daughter's Southwestern Art class noted that Frontier has a very nice, comprehensive collection of Southwestern art. I went back, and though I'm not fond of all the pieces, I was impressed and could appreciate their collection. So even if you're not particularly impressed with the food, just consider it an art gallery with free admission where they allow eating on the premises, and it sounds like a much better deal.
