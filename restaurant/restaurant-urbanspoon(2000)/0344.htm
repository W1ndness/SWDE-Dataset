Good experience, mediocre food
by Patrick (22 reviews)
August 10, 2010 - Doesn't like it
Taki can be a great place to go with a group of friends. The place is generally lively, and the teppan chefs overall do a good job of entertaining.
The teppan food is prepared properly; no complaints there. Take your pick from a pretty standard hibachi menu (steak, scallops, lobster, shrimp, etc.). If that's what you're going for, you should have a good experience.
That's the good news. The bad news is that the sushi is average at best. It's edible, but there are much better options within five miles (especially Miyabi 9 and Waterfront).
I've had inconsistent service. The last waiter I had there was...simply annoying. I won't get into why, as that would be boring, but he annoyed everyone at my table.
It's not a bad place to go, but there are just better options in the metro: Ohana's if you want hibachi and Miyabi 9 if you want sushi.
If you want to pound sake bombs with a large group of friends and have a little bit of everything to eat, then Taki is a good choice. In summary, Taki has good points, but I just can't say that I like it with the mediocre quality of sushi and the inconsistent service.

Still good.
by Ryan (6 reviews)
December 16, 2009 - Likes it
Although I've heard the sushi may not be as good as it used to be, it still is pretty decent. Taki also has some alternatives if you are bringing a group here which includes one or more people who do not prefer sushi - the Donburi (bowl of rice & cooked meat). The Oyokodon (chicken version of the Donburi) certainly hasn't lost any of its quality after the owner change. Also, the Mango Mochi (ice cream wrapped in a soft rice shell) is a highly recommended addition to any meal. The last time I was there with a friend over lunch we had very good service.
