+ 3 more photos from Chiffonade Sciabica Olive Oil Dinner @ Soho
by Chiffonade (1 review)
August 11, 2009 - Likes it
I love it when two forces join for the greater good. Established and beloved Atlanta institution, Soho Restaurant hosted Modesto's Sciabica Oil of the Olive in the form of an unforgettable themed dinner. Chef Joey Ahn outdid himself by matchmaking Sciabica Olive Oil with the perfect menu to showcase it.
The festivities began immediately upon entering the dining room with a lovely server bearing a platter of huge grilled shrimp anointed with Sciabica Basil Olive Oil. The oil is fragrant and brightly basil. I've tasted flavored oils but somehow the flavoring agent seldom maintains its "fresh" characteristic. There was no doubt about it - somewhere in California, a truckload of fresh, deeply green basil leaves rumbled with a great deal of robust olive oil. I limited myself to two shrimp because I knew we were in for a night of perfect pairings.
Chef Joey addressed the diners just before each course to explain which oil was used and how the food items were chosen. The amount of thought and consideration that went into each course was easily as important as the quality of the ingredients and the expertise with which they were prepared.
Our first course, Prosciutto-Stuffed Fresh Mozzarella with Heirloom Tomatoes, made a delicious platform for Mission Variety Limited Release Olive Oil. This is a playful foray into pure olive oil - unflavored by anything but the California sun, rich soil and perfect olive-growing climate. Not so strong as to overcome someone new to olive oil, one is always aware of its contribution to the dish. Soho managed to snag some of the best tasting ripe heirloom tomatoes in a rainbow of colors. Restraint was futile; I ate my entire first course. The wine pairing, 2006 "J" Pino Gris, complemented the delicate olive oil and the summer-perfect tomatoes.
In the center of each table was a four-compartment white dish into which the oils used in the dinner were poured. Plenty of crusty bread was offered for dipping, sampling and swooning.
Our next course, Oil-Poached Sea Bass with julienne veg, had a hurdle to leap with me as I am not a fan of sea bass. I know I'm in the minority, most love it - it's one of Bear's favorite fish. I've had rubbery, spongy, dry bass at places that purportedly knew how to prepare it. With some trepidation, I lifted the first forkful of bass to my lips; and was suddenly aware of the reason for all the fuss. The fish was a little white chunk of Heaven, bathed in potent, fruity olive oil. It was tender, moist, and should in no way be confused with "fried" fish. The bass was gently poached which means it was placed in a pan with oil to cover; then heated just enough to cook the fish. Sevillano Fall Harvest Olive Oil is a serious contender for "favorite" on my palate. Sciabica's Sevillano is the variety that collects awards like a magnet attracts loose nails. If you're new to olive oil and want to experience a seriously olivey-tasting oil, look no further than Sevillano. The wine served had to be flavorful enough to assert itself to the tasty oil and delicate bass without leap-frogging over it. Enter: 2007 Qupe Marsanne.
By this time, I was nearly giddy with delight. (Yes, I did drink some wine but just enough to sample each pairing. No, really!!) We enjoyed the oils poured into the center plate with crusty bread from Star Provisions between course after incredible course. I wanted to live at Soho that day. What could Joey do for breakfast with olive oil and eggs? And maybe some leftover Star Provisions bread?
Our final savory course arrived, Baby Lamb Chops with Purple Peruvian Potatoes. Surely, Chef Joey must be mad. What oil could possibly even register against the taste of lamb, undoubtedly among the most highly-flavored meats in the animal kingdom?? None other than the Mac Daddy of the Sciabica stable - Manzanillo Variety Fall Harvest. Manzanillo makes an entrance like Rocky and challenges the lamb to a throw-down. I'm happy to report that instead of warring for supremacy in my mouth, the perfectly roasted lamb and the potent Manzanillo oil found peace and harmony. This is a truly heady dish. Block-rocking Manzanillo (my current favorite with Sevillano nipping at its heels), a perfect foil for the lamb. And out of nowhere, tender roasted Peruvian Purples and haricots verts jump in. The wine pairing needed a big personality to join this fray and 2006 Penfolds Shiraz stood firm with a muscle-flex all its own.
Nick Sciabica and his cousin Patricia took turns introducing each of the oils as the courses were served and it was wonderful to have this type of insight. Nick showed a clip from Food Finds featuring Sciabica production methods and how they differ from other producers. These oils are not heated - they are cold pressed. Flavorings are added during the pressing stage - never by heat-infusion. Quality, attention to detail and family expertise all shine through in each of Sciabica's products.
Our final pairing of the night, Lemon Olive Oil Pound Cake with Basil Olive Oil Ice Cream was delightfully surprising. I've long been a proponent of baking with olive oil and I sincerely hope the lemon oil cake made believers out of skeptics. Our final wine of the night was a bit playful - NV Chateau Frank Sparkling Cremant into which was added a dash of Limoncello. How the beverage complemented the dessert illustrated how one component "rounds out" a course. You'd like to think dessert would be as perfect without the wine - but you know you'd be wrong.
Soho continues to demonstrate how to endure and excel as a long-standing leader in the restaurant community of Vinings and the whole of Atlanta. It's an easy formula - prepare unique, uncluttered food; then serve it adeptly in a warm, funky-chic, welcoming environment. Let your chef flex his culinary muscle. Bring in a fresh perspective to shine a light on new ideas. Susan, Frank, Bob and of course, Chef Joey - Thanks for making it all work.
Visit the Sciabica website and peruse the offerings. Order from their site as their oil is not widely distributed. Grab some Garlic, Basil or Jalapeno olive oil for salads and finishing dishes. Order up some Orange or Lemon oil for baking and sweet applications. And get yourself some Sevillano or Manzanillo - If you are ready to be "olived" right between the eyes. <3
