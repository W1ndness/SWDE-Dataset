Needs a Little Tweaking
by FoodieLA (1 review)
March 12, 2009
The menu items are a mix of standard Italian and some interesting combinations, using ingredients like Swiss chard, escarole (endive) and rapni (broccoletti) to liven things up. Patrons looking for mussels on the menu (like at other Fred Neuville restaurants), however, will be disappointed. On our visit, the menu items we sampled were only fair. One tagliatelle pasta dish came out swimming in about an inch of water (not broth), while a dish of seared flounder on a bed of risotto was way over-salted, and was returned to the kitchen, replaced by a slightly undercooked version. The tomatoes, reputed to be from local farms, delightfully perked up the otherwise "ho-hum" flounder dish.
On our visit, the front-office management & staff were knowledgeable, accommodating and attentive, and the food was brought out to the dining area quickly.
The menu is priced quite moderately, with pasta entrees starting in the $14 range. The most expensive entree tops out around $25.
The tables for two people, located in the "kitchen" dining room are about 3.5 feet long, meaning you and your date are going to have to yell at each other to be heard over the strangers at tables on either side. This seating concept may hold value in NYC, but on Maybank Highway in Johns Island, SC, it's just annoying, and it detracts from any dining intimacy you may have planned. Opt for a table in the quieter dining room or enclosed porch to the left of the reception area, beyond the bar.
We have enjoyed other Fred Neuville-touched restaurants "The Fat Hen" and "39 Rue De Jean" because of their inclusion of mussels in wonderful sauces on their menus. There are no "hook" items found (yet) on the otherwise standard Italian offerings here at Wild Olive, which is a disappointment. So, when considering a dining choice on John's Island, we will likely veer back toward the Fat Hen, and wait for Neuville to tweak Wild Olive's menu, and saw a foot off of those long tables!
