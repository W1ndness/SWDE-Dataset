Message from South City Kitchen Vinings

South City Kitchen - Sophisticated Southern in Smyrna/Vinings

  • Award-winning wine list
  • Patio dining in Smyrna/Vinings
  • Private dining room

South City Kitchen Vinings brings a combination of energy and comfortable metropolitan sophistication to your neighborhood.

Executive Chef Chip Ulbrich's training began in a comprehensive, European-style culinary apprenticeship program run by the Rock Resorts Company and accredited by The American Culinary Federation Education Institute. In 2001, The James Beard Foundation recognized Chef Chip achievements.
Traditional, fresh ingredients prepared in an open exhibition kitchen are used to create their version of contemporary Southern cuisine. It features an oversized bar with communal tables, cozy booths and a dining room lined with French doors that open to a spacious outdoor patio.
There is also a private dining room perfect for your next social or corporate event. Dine the way the new South intended at South City Kitchen in Vinings.

Gouda Fritters!
by Barney (486 reviews)
July 31, 2008 - Likes it
Stopped by the Vinings post of South City Kitchen last night (actually, pretty sure it's in Smyrna, but what's a little more "Vinings creep").
The place was hopping for a Wednesday night. The downside is that it can get a little loud....
The Good: My starter (Gouda fritters with roasted corn salad and bbq aioli) was fantastic, steaming hot molten fritters with a great crusty outside with the sweet/crunchy corn salad and just a dab of the aioli. I would get this again. The bread service, little biscuits and corn muffins, is always good. Finally, the table service was also very good, even on a busy weekday night.
The Disappointing: My main (sauteed shrimp and scallops w/ grits and garlic gravy) simply fell short. The one plus was that the scallops were not overcooked. In the minus column was that the shrimp were overcooked and like biting into a tire. The biggest problem, though, was the lack of any taste or seasoning in the "garlic" gravy. It seemed like a simple brown gravy without much indication of garlic, and the only discernible flavor being salt. Unfortunately, brown gravy over grits with no real attempt to flavor resulted in a pretty bland dish.
The conclusion: I still like SCK, but not sure I can count on EVERY dish to be a winner. I still love their fried chicken and the crab hash, but would probably avoid the shrimp/scallops/grits dish.

Some worth trying, Some worth passing
by Wes (152 reviews)
August 12, 2010 - Likes it
I have been here 3 times in the past couple of months, and all three visits were very enjoyable. However, I have enjoyed certain dishes much more in some cases than others. My most recent visit started with an order of those wonderful Gouda fritters, and my friend had a Caesar salad which he enjoyed. These were followed by a Smoked Turkey sandwich for him (which he devoured), and a Veggie Plate for me. The best parts of the veggie plate were the Asparagus (only because I know it's very healthy and I don't get it often enough) and the Macaroni and Cheese, which was delicious but nothing fancy like I've had at Fox Brothers, OK Cafe or Carver's. The Succotash, Okra and especially the Green Beans were mediocre to my palate. I think these are Northern preparations and I am used to how things are done in the South. That being said, in agreement with Barney, the Fried Chicken is excellent, my Stepfather loved his burger, and my Mom loved her She-Crab soup. I also had a Pork Chop dish with sweet potatoes once that was excellent. They have great cornbread and the Chocolate Terrine for dessert is outstanding. This is a wonderful restaurant to have in this area and I will definitely keep going back to try other dishes and Brunch.

Staff made it just OK...
by JVC (66 reviews)
February 28, 2010 - Likes it
Saturday night, 8pm and no reservation...we were told there was a 45 minute wait even though there were no people waiting. So we sat at the bar as there were quite a few open seats and decided just to eat there. The bartender gave a little attitude when I asked for a drink/wine menu...after a bit we placed our beverage order and started on the dinner menu. It is a nice menu with a great selection...there were some difficult choices to be made with all the homestyle choices. We opted for the following: Fried Green Tomatoes (tart and crisp with creamy cheese but the tomato sauce base was flavorless), Goat Cheese Fritters (perfect, served on top of a wonderful slaw), the Fried Chicken (their claim to fame and it was good...you get only a large airliner breast that has the base wing bone) and I had the Trout (strange cold crawfish vinaigrette topped on perfectly cooked trout that was on a a bed of veggies...julienne of sweet potato, fennel and maybe onion that were cooked to different textures..mushy-crunchy-yucky...). We ended the night with an amazing Banana Pudding..their vanilla wafer was sooo good!!
Overall it's a nice place outside of both bartenders attitudes that continued through the evening, then there were the 3 tables that remained unseated beginning 10 minutes after we arrived until we left at 10pm....45 minute wait...really??? Wine prices were good as were the cocktails with a very nice well rounded selection. Retrain some staff members to not roll their eyes when looking at the tips that other patrons left them or pour wine from a dirty glass (fellow bar patron had lipstick on glass and bartender simply poured it into a clean glass) and you have a destination here!
