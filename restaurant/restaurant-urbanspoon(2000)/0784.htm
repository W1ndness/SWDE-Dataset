Best Thai food in the area
by Travis Westfall (7 reviews)
May 06, 2010 - Likes it
The outward appearance is not endearing, but step inside and the atmosphere is lovely. It is a quiet, classy feeling restaurant. We started w/ appetizers which were somewhat disappointing, only given the extremely high expectations we had. They were still good, but not amazing. I also tried imported Thai beer. It was not a top tier beer.
When we moved on to the main course, this place established itself as one of the best. We ordered chili basil salmon, chicken pad thai, chicken green curry, panang curry duck, and drunken noodles w/ beef. All the dishes had an amazing range of flavor and quite the depth as well. The duck and chicken were cooked very nicely, tender and not overdone. The level of spice was simply perfect, no other way to put it. One more thing, some may find their dishes were slightly overpowered w/ basil.
I would recommend the duck and pad thai especially, and also any of their specials, as they seem to put the most TLC into those dishes.

So good, the taste transcends countries.
by James Watson (1 review)
May 26, 2010 - Likes it
I have been to the restaurant numerous times when I have been staying in America, me and my girlfriend both love the place. It is a nice small restaurant, comfortable and relaxing, perfect for either a casual lunch or a dinner date. The food is amazing and delicious every time and always leaves you wanting more, garunteeing your future satisfaction and their future business. I get cravings for Krungthai's dishes back home in England, nothing else compares to it. Their duck dishes are amazing and the 'Jungle Krungthai' dish is a sure hit for those who like it real spicey, like myself. I high recommend Krungthai to anyone. Amazing quality, service, pleasant atmosphere. A great place indeed. 5 Stars, 10 out of 10 and the 1st place ribbon.
