Cozy, fun, & casual....
by Lola (243 reviews)
May 28, 2009 - Likes it
I'm a fan of Matchbox., but (and it's a big BUT), NOT of its pizza.
What I like about Matchbox:
- cozy, fun, & casual atmosphere. It's a great spot to hang with friends or entertain out-of-town guests
- good drink options
- yummy mini-hamburgers (sliders) served with flavorful onion strings
- a menu that has something for everyone (salads, sandwiches, etc.)
- great location near the Gallery Place/Chinatown metro and the phonebooth!
What I don't like about it:
- the pizza..for the life of me, I don't get it. The crust is very, very thin and the few times I've had it, it was chock full of huge chunks of garlic (yuk!) and proscuitto that wasn't cooked enough. Seriously, for all you pizza fans out there, in DC 2Amy's or Pizzeria Paradiso is where you need to go, not Matchbox
- service is hit or miss. Downstairs is super-noisy too.
Still on the whole, Matchbox is a good bet when you're in Penn Quarter/Chinatown.

Great Pizza, burgers and sandwiches
by Bon Appetit Foodie (24 reviews)
July 12, 2010 - Really likes it
Anyway, I went down to their Capitol Hill location on Valentine's day eve with my boyfriend. We wanted something low key and not too expensive--just a great place where we could get a good meal and be able to enjoy each other's company. The 30 minute wait slid on by, while I sipped a strong margarita. Matchbox may not boast the crazy and extensive selection of beers Pizzeria Paradiso does...but their pizza is better (shhh! Sorry PP!). After seeing the ubiquitous "3.6.9" shirts on the wait staff, along with the piles of onion straws surrounded by scrumptious looking burgers, I decided to try 3 of the mini sliders which were seasoned perfectly and cooked exactly how I asked. I usually don't want pickles on my burger, but here, the cool crunch was a welcomed addition, and I only wish I would have asked for some cheese on the burgers, as gouda would have went perfectly. The onion straws were crisp and were dusted with a delicious, salty parmesan mixture that tasted pretty good on the burgers as well.
Now, as I've said, I've had their pizza before, several times before. And the one I'd really recommend is the coppa italian ham and arugula pizza. Slathered with pesto, ricotta, mozzarella and slices of roma tomato and coppa italian ham, this pizza is so satisfying. The pesto makes it a little greasy, but the crust remains crisp. The ricotta mixed with the mozzarella is a perfect marriage between two cheeses, and the ricotta gives the pizza a nice creamy texture under the layer of baked mozzarella. Two things that really stood out about this pizza: the slices of roma tomato were fresh and flavorful, which is impressive because it's hard enough to find a good tomato, let alone a good, fresh tomato in the middle of February on the East Coast. Secondly, the coppa ham was just crispy enough; no stringy, fatty pieces here. And then there's the bitter arugula that goes the extra mile to make this pizza stand out even more. The salt content here may have been a little steep between all the cheese, pesto and ham; but I'm not complaining.
The other pizza I tasted this past weekend was the matchbox meat. The tomato sauce Matchbox uses is slightly sweet, which goes really nicely with the spiciness of the pepperoni and the sausage. This is a meat lovers pizza; just the right amount of meat, huge lumps of sausage smothered in cheese and topped off with crunchy-around-the-edges pepperoni and bacon. To be honest, I didn't taste the bacon that much, but it was so good I didn't notice. If you like the matchbox meat pizza, you'll probably enjoy the spicy meatball one as well.
Although I've frequented Matchbox, this was the first time I've had dessert there, and was only disappointed that I had never had a third course there before. My boyfriend and I split the pecan pie. The pie is served with a bourbon butter sauce that is so thick, caramelly and sweet. I could have slurped down the entire shot of that sauce and fallen into a deep sugar coma. Presentation here was great--instead of a reheated slice of pie, Matchbox gives you a mini individual pie and dusts the rim of the shot glass and the pie with powdered sugar. The pie itself was piping hot and had a gooey filling that rolled over the pecans, creamy vanilla gelato and the flaky, crisp crust. I would have liked a few more pecans in the filling. I am not lying when I say this is the best pecan pie I've ever had.
Side note: If for some reason you wind up at Matchbox and are not craving pizza, their sandwiches are really tasty as well. They used to have this blackened chicken one that was so amazing (I keep begging via Twitter every now and then for them to bring it back), but have since replaced it with a different grilled chicken sandwich.
And so concludes another delicious meal I've enjoyed in DC. Onto the next pizza place! Although Matchbox is a really tough act to follow. Next time you're looking for good pizza or happen to be in Chinatown or Capitol Hill, head over to Matchbox...just be prepared for a bit of a wait. I assure you it's worth it and it is that good.

Ever since I visited Italy I searched long and hard for pizza that measured up to authentic European pizza; matchbox comes the closest. The atmosphere is light and modern, yet cosy with the wooden tables and brick oven in plain sight. The back dining area has a skylight and an upstairs, making it seem like your eating outside without the heat and noise of DC.
