It's all about the white sauce
by Eddie3 (38 reviews)
March 18, 2009
The atmosphere, food, service, and menu are all basically generic, American-ized Asian. However, it is worth two hours in what is essentially an Asian Applebee's (not to mention the chef's dog-and-pony, cook at your table show) to get to the main attraction - Kobe' white sauce.
This white sauce is amazing and makes even the most uninspired plate of rice and chicken a mouth-party. I don't know how to describe it other than to say it's a slightly sweet, tangy, shrimp sauce with what I'm guessing is a mayo. base. If you've never had it this may sound gross, but trust me, you'll be asking the knife-juggling chef for seconds and thirds.

Best Hibachi Grill I've been too!
by MeganA (16 reviews)
July 02, 2010 - Really likes it
And I've been to A LOT!!! First of all, I've always used urbanspoon but never wrote any reviews. Now, because of a couple of bad experiences I'm going back to give my opinion. I've been going to Kobe's for about 10 years at least 2-3 times a year! Every experience has been wonderful! Very entertaining, loud, fast, and expensive. But, if you can make it for their short happy hour you're in luck! It's Mon-Thrus 5-6pm with great prices!!! Be prepared to come with a group of people or you may be sat with other families. The tables hold 10-11 people. Reservations are good but don't always help because they require everyone to be there before sitting you. Other than all that... the white sauce is like liquid edible gold and the drinks are strong! Enjoy!!!

GREAT
by sguthrie (1 review)
August 21, 2009
You will wait forever (even with a "reservation" - make sure everyone gets there at the same time or they won't seat you) and will hear at least 15 rounds of gong-banging and happy-birthday-singing (everyone gets $15 worth of a free meal on their birthday), but it's worth it. Why? THE WHITE SAUCE.
I pretty much agree with what other reviewers have said. It's touristy and a tad overpriced, but the sauce is top-notch. You can buy bottles of it from the bar for $5 each (definitely worth it if you're there already). Other Japanese restaurants are closer to home and have more reasonable prices, but none have mastered the art of the white sauce like Kobe.
