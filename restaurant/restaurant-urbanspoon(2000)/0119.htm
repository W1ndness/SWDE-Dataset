Order wisely = fab meal
by umami (1 review)
October 28, 2009 - Likes it
I'm highly skeptical of pan-Asian restaurants that offer cuisine from more than one country, because that usually means none are done well. Here, the strongest cuisine is Chinese -- with some stellar dishes, if you know what to order.
The fried anchovies are one of the best items on the menu. Fried to a crisp and served with sauteed jalepenos and peanuts, they're as addictive as potato chips. Likewise, the crispy duck is the best I've had in years, with succulent meat and crunchy, salty golden-brown skin. It's large enough for two. We've ordered off the "traditional" menu (choose a protein and a sauce), and enjoyed the spicy garlic shrimp, though it wasn't a standout like the previous dishes.
Another highlight is the fact that they serve nigori sake (unfiltered, milky white, and served cold) and sparkling sake, a champagne-like twist on cold sake that's bubbly and slightly sweet. If you like Prosecco, you might like this. Both are hard to find in a town that mostly serves sake hot and cheap.
The service is great here, and the ambiance is quirky and fun, with karaoke some nights.
On the downside, the Thai and Japanese dishes are not terribly authentic. The bento boxes are an excellent value (they're probably serving too much and charging too little) and if you're not picky they taste perfectly fine. But if you are particular about Japanese food (I am, perhaps more than normal), there are a few things just "off" -- the sashimi, while fresh, isn't cut properly, and when I asked for a bowl of rice they brought out Jasmine rice (NO!). All three of the Thai dishes I tried (Thai beef salad, Tom Yum Gai, and pad Thai) were completely wrong.
That said, I do recommend this as a good lunch spot or date night outing. Just stick with the cuisine that goes with the restaurant's name.
