Really Neat!
by Fabienne (12 reviews)
January 09, 2010 - Really likes it
Small place packed with fun memorabilia. Very good waiters/esses and a friendly atmosphere. Terry's is the kind of a play you can't not like. It looks like an old house from the outside, one that has a bunch of junk in the front yard...the kind of place the East End has been noted for for years. Two (and maybe three) funky statues of cowboys and waitresses beckoning you inward in their mechanical rotations, and a line of old theater chairs lined up for the unlucky patrons to sit and wait for the treats within.
We were lucky. I called and found out that there was no line so we got right in (it was 1:00 p.m on a Cold Saturday). We sat at the same table as a pair of strangers and were immediately handed a bowl of peanuts to stem our appetites. There were lots of specials and add ons displayed around the tiny kitchette-like interior, and it is a mild shock to realize that the entire north wall is really a full bar!
The food, very good. My husband and I had what Terry's is noted for: burgers. These are BIG and come as either ham or cheese burgers for the same price. The only thing to watch out for is that they will arrive exceptionally pink in the middle. If you want them cooked all the way through, you have to remember to ask for that. It also tastes like someone has drizzled butter all over them. I shudder to contemplate the calorie content. They tasted like fresh top shelf beef and tangy swiss cheese and all the other things on there. I put only ketchup on mine...had to.
The fries? Done just the way they should be: more brown than you will find in most places. They are far better like that. Terry seems to keep them in the fat about 5 minutes longer than most people do and I think that is just the way they should be done.
There are other intriguing things on the menu involving ingredients like: fillet mignon with bearnaise sauce, fillet mignon chili, lump blue crab meat.
Yuppie? Yes. It's the EastSide...what do you expect? However, there is this working class sheen on the place which is just plain old well done. Let's you feel down home without your having to give up your J. Crew sweater.
I was very glad to have found Terry's Turf Club at last. I will be back and I will try to bring out of town friends because it's a unique treasure on the EastSide
