Makes you want to go to Nepal!
by Steph (21 reviews)
March 29, 2009 -
I never get tired of this place and I usually eat there weekly! Being that I am a poor college student, I LOVE the $9 lunch buffet!! Come hungry because the food is so tasty that you will have trouble controlling your intake (I usually end up eating 3 overflowing plates of goodness and then need to be rolled outta there). For veggie lovers, I highly recommend the navaratna korma, saag paneer and there is some potato thing with ketchup in it (says that on the menu) and it is rockin'. For chicken lovers, the tikka masala is always amazing and the tandoori chicken is extra special. The briyani rice is also mouth watering. Everything tastes better with a mango lassi or Taj Mahal beer! Don't forget the garlic naan, too! Oh, and the rice pudding for desert... I could pretty much live in a pool of that stuff. I have to disagree with one of the previous reviews about bringing a date there - I think it is a great atmosphere and the service is incredibly fun and friendly. Plus, they are moving a block away to a bigger, prettier place soon. Highly recommended!

again and again
by Robin-Elise Call (16 reviews)
March 03, 2010 - Really likes it
I am starting to lose count of the number of times I've eaten at Himalayan Kitchen, but I had usually stuck with the more familiar Indian entrees. This time, I decided to go a little Nepalese, and got the Vegetable Momo. It was good, but seemed more like an appetizer to me than an entree. The thing I'm noticing is the distinctness of vinegar that is not in the other Asian dishes I'm used to.
I got the mixed appetizer platter as usual, and a bread basket, which round out every meal. I am totally in love with Tamarind, and cannot escape it this year! (the appetizer platter comes with a Tamarind dipping sauce that I use on everything.
Don't forget about the Coconut Vegetable Korma, just because it's tasty, and vegan. I like getting the fancy rice and ordering enough to have leftovers when I come here.
Since I don't eat dairy I lose out on desserts and the special teas, but if I was feeling high maintenance one day, I might find out what they could make me.
One thing I noticed this last time was that the wait staff and host were exceptionally friendly. Could it be that we arrived before the dinner rush, and most of the time we arrive after the rush....when they are a little worn out or frustrated?
