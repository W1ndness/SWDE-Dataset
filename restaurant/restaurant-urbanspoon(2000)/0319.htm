Bad Waiter
by jaczarni (36 reviews)
May 25, 2010 - Doesn't like it
In terms of corporate fancy dining fare, the Melting Pot comes in about where you would expect. The food is indulgent, the gimmick is diverting, the atmosphere extremely calculated.
Our visit there was marred by a bad waiter. We came for the extended 5280 deal. While our waiter went through the opening shpeel, he went out of his way to tell us what the cheapest deal at that time was. He did not mention the 5280 deal until we asked about it. He was clearly not happy to have to be offering us this. Not a good start. He then went over the nonalcoholic drinking options and didn't mention wine or beer. Now, we look young, but not that young. I was borderline offended, but decided to let it slide.
At the end, we split the bill. He WROTE on the check how much we should each tip him. He not only assumed that we did not know how to tip on a split bill, he assumed he deserved a great tip. Now I'm pissed. I tip him and just try to get the hell out of there. As we left, I heard another waiter exclaim that "Our cheapest dining option right now is the 5280 deal!" What the hell.

We got kicked out!
by jillmyers6 (2 reviews)

The Melting Pot is not a quick meal, and we expect to linger over the many courses when we visit The Melting Pot. Which is why we were surprised when only minutes after paying out bill, we were approached by the general manager and in no uncertain terms asked to leave our table. He did mumble something about champagne in the bar, but we were so taken aback and embarrassed that we did not want to stay another minute. We felt that the situation was handled badly as our waitperson never said that she needed the table (it was 9:00 on a weeknight) and instead immediately elevated the situation to the manager, who quite frankly could use some sensitivity training in dealing with patrons. This was after we spent over two hundred dollars for a party of four and tipped our waitress 20 percent. We felt unwelcome at this establishment and our evening was ruined.

Worth the trip
by Bob (1 review)
July 28, 2009
I was recently entertaining a business associate from out of town (my boss to be exact) and we were looking for a place to do lunch. I saw the sign at The Melting Pot that they are now open for lunch so we went there. I have been there with my wife before so I figured it would be a good place to "brownie" up to the boss. The place has a real nice ambiance and is one of the very few places in Littleton that offers an upscale atmosphere. However, my boss wanted to sit on the patio (which may just be the nicest patio I've ever been on) and they gave us their lunch menu (I didn't even know they had one). Our server suggested the cheese, salad and chocolate combo which was only $20 a person because it would get us out in 45 minutes max. Not a bad deal…especially when it's on the corporate card!
While we were there my boss started talking about possibly doing our Christmas party at The Melting Pot this year (which would make my wife ecstatic). Our server called over their manager who gave us a copy of their banquet contract and took my number down to give to their banquet manager.
Overall I again had good food; good service and was in and out within an hour!
