Overpriced, mediocre food, poor service, culinary arrogance, cramped, noisy , confusing menu, and no reservations allowed..
by Ken Beilman (1 review)
May 27, 2009 - Doesn't like it
I like to frequent Louisville's best restaurants and we fortunately have many - like Jack Fry's, Lilly's, Vincenzo's, and Cafe Metro, among several others. 732 Social, however, is not one of them. I don't usually review restaurants online but I am compelled to remark on overpiced, low quality, expensive retaurants. To begin with, the restaurant does not accept reservations but does offer an ineffecitive "call ahead" serivce. Thus it is likely that you will arrive with no idea how long you will wait. Make sure you are not hungry so you can survive the indeterminate wait. The No Reservation Policy is, by itself, a major disincentive to visiting this establishment. Further, 732 Social offers poor service, mediocre food, is overpriced, and so noisy that conversation is difficult. These are my reactions to my first and last visit there. We had to wait an excessive time for service and then seemed to have gotten lost by the wait staff. The roast chicken was acceptable but certainly not worth the price. $50 for 2 modest cheese plate appetizers and the roast chicken entre far exceeded the meal's value. The volume of my martini was barely half the size of the standard volume. Beause the waitress didn't bother to write down the order, I received my martini with no olives. The accoustics of the room were terribly loud with an echo and make intimate conversation impossible. Tables are very small and so crowded together that it was difficult to be seated. The menu is confusing and needed an extensive explanation by the waitress. Oh, and one other thing - this restaurant represents the zenith of culinary arrogance. With there being no salt and pepper on the table (a typical statement of the chef's culinary conceit and elitism), I asked for more salt. Instead of being given a salt shaker, I was only allowed to use their salt grinder while the waitress waited to take it back to the kitchen. So I had to ask for each use of the salt shaker! How dare I season the food to MY taste , especially more than once. They must have paid off the Courier Journal for their biased review. While 732 Social is currently chic and hip because of its newness and location, I expect its popularity to be fleeting with the test of time. Highly NOT recommended. My experience there could not have been much worse.

it wasn't great, then it was great, and then... not so great again
by nik (1 review)
August 05, 2010
After all the buzz I was excited to go to 732 last Saturday with my best girlfriends. We tried to make a reservation the afternoon of, but no one was there to answer. We left a message requesting seating for 5 people at 7pm. When we got there we were told we were not on the reservations list and would not be able to be accomodated inside at any time that evening but we did have the option to sit outside. When we mentioned we had called she (the hostess) asked us if we had received a return call. When we said no, we were told "if you don't get a return call, you don't have a reservation". I think a simple return call to let us know of no availability would have not been too much to expect. Although we didn't like the initial vibe and attitude of the hostess, we didn;t want to let it spoil our evening or our chance to try out the intriguing menu and cocktails we'd been hearing about. We decided we would sit outside. Keep in mind it's been a scorcher here in Louisville lately so we really must have wanted to stay. When the hostess led us to our table it was a tiny wood block table with five seats she crammed around it. With all the plates, utensils, glassware, decor etc. how were five people expected to eat comfortably there? We commented that it was a little cramped and although we are best friends, we didn't want to have to eat on top of eachother. There was a two top table a few feet away from us that was empty but when we asked to pull that in with ours we were told that they wouldnt be able to do that. We were kind of shocked at the attitude and indifference. One of my friends said that maybe we should just have a cocktail here and go somewhere else to eat.The hostess response to that was "Well, I don;t mind if you guys want to eat here. We put five people around these tables ALL the time" That's nice that she didnt MIND us wanting to eat there! At this point I was getting mad and suggested that if they wouldnt pull the table over to ours then two of us would sit at the two top and the remaining three of us would sit at our original table. They were only a few feet away anyway I mean really- what's the difference? After that she said nothing and obviously annoyed, scooted the table over to ours and walked away. Gee thanks so much for the hospitality. Can really tell you want our business. Then our server arrived and EVERYTHING changed. He was funny, charming and had a passion and love for the restaurant and the food that was so obvious it was contagious. He spent lots of time explaining some of the more eclectic dishes and suggesting items he thought would suit our taste. He did the same for the drinks. I took his suggestion and got the Hemmingway Daquiri which was heavenly. His food suggestions were was also wonderfully unique and tasty. By the time dinner was over, we were having a great time and had nearly forgotten the rude reception we received when first arriving. Then it started to rain and as it was time to pay our bill anyway, we went inside in search of our awesome waiter. He gave us the bill, we paid, and while we were waiting for him to return with the credit card slips, we saw some friends seated at the other side of the restuarant. Two of us went over to say hello. We talked to them for about five minutes. At least three times servers were coming around us with food and grumbling loudly and rudely that they wished we would move. The restaurant is very small and crowded and with space at a premium, I can understand that we were in their way. There are nicer ways of speaking to your guests however. And heck, if you aren't sitting at your table you are pretty much getting in the way just by trying to go across to get to the one bathroom(unisex) in the place. We left after saying goodbye to our wonderful waiter and as we passed the surly hostess out the door we were all left wondering if we would return. There are two things this restaurant needs if it wants to sustain its success and I hope that it does. First, it needs better training for the people who open the door to greet guests. The young lady we dealt with was unpleasant, unaccomodating, and a bit condescending. Secondly, it is in dire need of some kind of expansion. There is simply not enough room to be comfortable in this restaurant. I hope they can get those things corrected soon. I support keeping Louisville weird and would love to see it succeed but there is more to a restaurant than ambience and food, you need great service too to keep people coming back.
