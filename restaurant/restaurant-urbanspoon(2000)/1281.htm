Downtown McFresh

December 08, 2009 - Likes it
Went to Elote the other day with a friend for lunch and I was pretty impressed. I've been looking for some places around Tulsa that emphasize fresh and local and Elote is one of them. My friend had the puffy tacos (your basic taco but with a bit of a puff to it...sounds complicated, I know, but really, it's not) and I had a chicken burrito. The quality of the food was really good, you could really tell how fresh everything was...and I've never seen spinach this fresh in a restaurant before. My only complaint was that the chicken burrito was lacking enough chicken, in my opinion. There was plenty of everything else, just not enough chicken.
They don't do servers during lunch, but the staff that are there are kind and offer to get you more fountain drinks, etc and clean up after you also. They seem nice and helpful.
Overall, Elote is a great place to eat downtown, and I can't say there are a ton of good places downtown. Friendly people and really fresh food makes for an enjoyable experience at a reasonable price.

Can't recommend this place
by k1b8sn1 (1 review)
January 20, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I want to start by saying that I am more of a Tex-Mex fan than any other styles of Mexican food.
OVERALL IMPRESSION. This is NOT anything near Tex-Mex or Original Mexican cooking as some have posted. It is someone's "healthy" version of Mexican food that way misses the mark for me. I would not recommend anyone that is expecting Tex-Mex or authentic Mexican food to try this establishment.
VALUE. Way over-priced in my opinion for the quality of food.
THE ATMOSPHERE. This is much cleaner than Nelson's but it still felt dirty. There was a bright pallet of color and decorations on the wall that didn't really make me feel as though I was in a Mexican restaurant or relaxed. It was more the bright yellow and red of McDonalds hurry-up-and-eat theme.
The music was loud but not obnoxious.
Having to walk through the restaurant to the register at the back to place your order was odd and uncomfortable for me. I feel that in a restaurant environment, you should place your order at your table and not "fast food style".
THE STAFF. I agree with another reviewer's comment about looking as though they just got out of bed and unable to tell who was an employee and who was there for a meal. I finally figured out to look for a towel in their back pocket or waist band. Whenever I tried to speak with one of the staff, she stood and listened but only responded with one word responses. I never had the "Welcome to our restaurant" feeling from anyone.
WHAT I ORDERED. I ordered a beef burrito, white queso and chips, water.
THE FOOD. The white queso came out and had a very watery consistency so I assumed it was very hot out of the warmer. This queso is not cheese based but rather tasted as though it was 80%+ Sour Cream based which threw my taste buds into revolt. I believe that Sour Cream sucks the flavor out of foods and only belongs on bad tasting dishes. If you are a fan of sour cream, simply ask for a side of sour cream on your order and you'll do much better than paying $3.00 for the queso.
The Beef Burrito came out and I was very surprised at the size. I was going to enjoy this Organic roast beef, cheese, sweet potato, jalapeño dish. The guajillo sauce was a little off-putting in that it was flavorless. On my plate, it did nothing more than make my burrito soggy. The first bite was a very large chunk of sweet potato and an after taste of potted beef. Assuming the after taste was caused from the guajillo sauce saturating the tortilla, I dug some of the inside out of the burrito and it wasn't the sauce. I know this was roast beef but it tasted over processed and "wrong". I only ate about a quarter of my burrito.
THE DRINK. My water was ice cold and refreshing.

Eloté is unique, creative, artsy, and fantastic.
by Joe Bob Food Fan (14 reviews)
June 06, 2010 - Doesn't like it
Eloté is a wonderful change of pace from most Mexican places.
If you're looking for standard Tex-Mex or authentic Mexican food, then this isn't the best place for you. (At the time of this writing, La Hacienda on Peoria is the best authentic Mexican spot in town.)
What Eloté does - and does properly - is what I like to call "artsy-fartsy Mexican." They have taken traditional Mexican dishes and added some creative flair to them. Some people like myself will love this concept; others, as has been made obvious by some of the reviews, will not.
The overall atmosphere is very bright and colourful, and the wait staff (mostly females, and mostly very attractive - not that it matters, but it almost seems like they're running a modeling agency on the side) are overall very friendly and quite attentive. An earlier reviewer noted that she had difficulting picking out which people were the employees. Most of the time, the staff dresses in Eloté T-shirts, many of which read (rather humourously) "I ♥ Puffy Tacos."
Speaking of which, the puffy tacos - which I have them make with beans instead of meat - are phenomenal. I also recommend the sweet corn tamales and the smokin' hot quesadilla (with jalapeno, poblano and serrano peppers).
Their queso isn't my favourite in town, but it's not awful. As others have noted, it is cream-cheese based, so it's a bit thicker than most, and to me it does seem a little bit on the bland side. It reminds me of the cream-cheese-based queso over at Cafe Olé, but I have to admit that Cafe Olé's is more flavourful.
One of the other appetizers, however, the Fresh Fruit Pico Bites, is a stunning endeavour. You get a set of little flour tortilla chips covered with a sweet jalepeno mousse and a "fruit pico de gallo." It's an absolutely remarkable appetizer; both my wife and I went nuts over it, and I was practically licking the plate when they were gone. The downside is that they were gone pretty quickly.
My wife and I have also tried a number of Eloté's desserts, which were good, but I would especially recommend Lily's Smokin' Hot Ice Cream Sandwich. They use a special Mexican "cajeta" sauce which adds a little spice to the dish. It's not too spicy for most people. (For me, it's not quite spicy enough, but I'm a bit of a masochist.) It's quite good and unique enough that you couldn't easily stumble onto such a dessert anywhere else.
Of course, as you probably know, Eloté uses local ingredients and they aspire to be extremely eco-friendly; even the cups, lids, and straws are made from corn and sugar beets. (Just try not to bite down on the straws; they're kind of brittle.) All of this is great and lends right in with the hipness and "artsy-fartsy" nature of the place... but I'm happy to report that the food quality is great as well.
If they keep up their level of food quality, service and innovation, my wife and I will be fans and customers of Eloté for a long time to come.
