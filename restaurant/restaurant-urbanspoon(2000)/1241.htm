Dependable Italian favorites
by Rommie Johnson (20 reviews)
February 10, 2009 - Really likes it
Full disclosure: I lived around the corner from Bella's for almost seven years, so I'm probably a little biased in its favor. But this casual-yet-classy Italian eatery had been a favorite of mine for well over a decade before I even moved into the 'hood, and the fact that I've been there literally hundreds of times should tell you everything you need to know. Sure, Bella's probably won't win any James Beard awards any time soon, but that's not what it's about. This is a place for Italian-style comfort food and friendly service in a warm atmosphere. Longtime favorites include a killer chicken lasagna, the colorful Confetti Spaghetti, an amazing lobster bisque and arguably the area's best Bolognese sauce. I'm also partial to the Pollo alla Fiorentina (breaded chicken breast stuffed with spinach and goat cheese, served with a peppercorn cream sauce) and the Pollo Romano, which is what I had tonight. A simple dish that lets the ingredients' natural flavors shine through, it's a pair of chicken breasts lightly breaded with Romano cheese, sauteed and topped with lemon basil butter and artichoke hearts. I've ordered it dozens of times, and tonight's was every bit as delicious as the first. And, of course, it should be mentioned that Bella's bartenders -- Kelly, Jessica and especially Larry -- are among Tampa's finest.

One of SoHo's Oldest & Best!
by Rob.M (114 reviews)
July 28, 2010 - Likes it
When it comes to upscale Italian food, one restaurant comes to mind...Bella's. Offering a wide and varied menu of excellent Italian themed creations Bella's is a must stop on any culinary tour-de-force in Tampa. Upscale but still casual the atmosphere is top notch, not pretentious or superficially casual as many other SoHo venues, Bella's is one of those places that's as fun to be seen at as it is to see. Lot's of south Tampa scenery abounds, just a stone's throw from Bay Shore Blvd or Hyde Park. Great food, reasonably expensive prices and terrific atmosphere make for a wonder dining experience in an often overly trendy area of town. Highly recommended.

Best Carpaccio!
by Scott A. Boyce (1 review)
July 20, 2009 - Likes it
One of the things my wife & I love most about Tampa is the vast number of great restaurants. We're more than happy to pay a premium for exemplary service and food (Armani's, Bern's, etc.), but equally appreciative of good value. Bella's serves some of the best food we've tasted at a price that's shockingly reasonable. We found Bella's when we first moved to Tampa over 9 years ago and have been back countless times since, never leaving disappointed.
Despite it's "trendy" location, there is always an eclectic mix of people inside. Bella's has a very relaxed atmosphere with attire ranging from shorts to suits. As it can get pretty loud, I definitely wouldn't recommend it for a quiet dining experience - about the only thing they don't offer.
The Carpaccio is outstanding - even better than Donatello's, in my opinion. My favorite entree is Confetti Spaghetti, except I substitute their starburst pasta. Service is equally good.

We ordered our drinks and were then presented with what tasted to be frost bitten bread, and unsoftened butter. While reviewing the menu, we observed the pantry chef microwaving numerous items, and countless servers passing by food in the window. I ordered chicken parmigiana while my brother ordered a Create-your-own Pizza. The food arrived rather quick, although we watched it sit in the window for easily 3 minutes. Upon its arrival i was instantly disappointed. My platter appeared as if something was missing. There were Two moderately sized chicken breasts swimming in a watery thin sauce, on what seemed like a never ending plate. Paired with my chicken was what appeared to be left over cappilini . Almost as offensive as my plate, was my brothers less than impressive $13 personal pizza. My first bite was of nothing but the sauce. I was sure at this point that salt was there only choice in seasoning. To alleviate his inconvenience our server , brought multiple drinks at a time, rather than refilling glasses.
