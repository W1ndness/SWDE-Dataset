Is it Jucy or Juicy?
by BizzNessMPLS (127 reviews)
March 10, 2009 - Really likes it
The battle for Jucy (or Juicy) supremacy is going to be hard fought from here on out. I am a huge fan of Matt's Bar, but I had my first Juicy Nookie a few weeks back, and it was good, not just good, but really good. Similar to Matt's, it's full of hot, molten cheese that will burn any exposed flesh in its steaming path. I am not a big fan of The Nook fries, but they have these Leprechaun Legs (deep fried green beans) that cannot be missed. If you are not in the mood for a Juicy, the Lodge burger on the menu is one of the best burgers I have ever had. The cheddar is so smokey and it has bacon, enough said.

Very Good Burger!
by DougMN (57 reviews)
December 21, 2009 - Likes it
Let me start out by saying- there was a time about 10-12 years ago when I went to the Nook regularly. A friend of mine bar-tended and cooked there (yes, at the same time because that's how busy they were) and even back then the burgers were fantastic. I moved out of the area (or I guess I should say my girlfriend- now wife did) and had not been back since. After picking up my jaw from the floor after seeing The Nook on Triple D, I had no plans to go there- especially after hearing about the waiting times. But this weekend, one in our group insisted, so I found myself standing outside the Nook waiting for a table around 7PM on Saturday. After about a 30 minute wait were seated and ordered. I had the Lodge Burger with Sweet Potato fries and was impressed. Overall- the burgers have not changed much since the days I frequented it. What has changed is the way these guys have figured out how to market it. Good for them and I hope they have continued success. Neighborhoods need places like the Nook. Also- do yourself a favor and swing into the Ran-Ham next door for either a drink or some bowling- classic and looks like a bar should. You will not be disappointed.

...and the burger search continues (success)....
by Karl D. Schubert (20 reviews)
October 23, 2009 - Really likes it
...and the burger search continues (success)....
We continued our search for the best burger & place in town -- and found the one we've like the best so far: The Nook. It's friendly, small, the burgers are outstanding, and what the menu says is on the burgers is on the burgers. (Since my wife likes hers "plain", this is a big deal; when a burgers said they just came with the bun it was like a new experience.) She had "The Mickey's Original" and I had the "Paul Molitor Burger" (stuffed with pepperjack cheese). And, they both came with fries (very tasty and perfectly done). The price was very reasonable...the burgers were juicy and cooked to doneness as requested...and served in baskets.
We went on a Saturday afternoon around 1:30pm-ish had had about a 30 minute wait for a table. Next time, we'll probably take a seat at the bar.
This is definitely one we'll be going back to.

Fantabulous!
by lkp (1 review)
January 21, 2009 - Likes it
I have lived in the Twin Cities for the past 26 years (and a few before that) and have searched the whole time for a great, well-cooked, fried-not-chargrilled burger. The Nook is the place to get the best hand patted, home-cooked-style burger that I have found (not only in the Twin Cities - but anywhere I've been). We've been there day, night, busy, slow and we have never had a bad burger. The fries are always fantastic also. I can't believe The Nook has received any negative reviews. The wait staff are fantastic, especially when you consider the number of people they put through there each and everyday. You usually have a wait, but it's a wait-well-worth-it!!!! We took my parents there (from out of town) and they agreed that it was the best they've had.
