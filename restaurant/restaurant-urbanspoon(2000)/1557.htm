Big Meaty Tacos!
by Barney (486 reviews)
August 14, 2009 - Likes it
Stopped in for lunch today on the nicely busy Decatur downtown square and I went with the taco lunch special (pick 2 tacos and choose between salad or rice and beans). I went with a fish taco (Sweetwater 420 Beer-battered fish, with cabbage, chipotle baja sauce and pico de gallo) and one brisket taco (slow-cooked beef, with onions, pico de gallo and cheese) and black beans and rice (also have the choice of pinto beans). Without a doubt, the grilled flour tortillas are a little unusual (not bad, simply a little different for tacos) but they do allow them to really stuff these full of tasty meat and "stuff." The fish taco was average to slightly above average with a good portion and nice variety of flavors, but the brisket taco was the winner as it overflowed with tasty tender brisket. Nicely priced lunch special that was plenty of food! Also nice selection of hot sauces on the table (regular, green, and REALLY hot) and very friendly service on the shade covered patio. I suspect that this is a great place to come when you can sit outside and have a margarita or a couple of beers with dinner...

What happened?
by fishsticks64 (20 reviews)
May 23, 2009 - Doesn't like it
I had been to Raging Burrito ("RB") many times in the past.
This time, I took my daughters the same day as the Decatur Arts festival. So, granted, it was crowded "in the streets" and on RB's patio, but we opted for indoors in the newer Raging Taco location which was half empty.
We ordered drinks which came quickly and then chips and lunch. The chips didn't arrive until I asked for them, about 40 minutes after we'd ordered them. My daughters were ready to eat the napkins. Lunch didn't arrive until after I asked for it, too, more than an hour after we ordered.
I think they were growing the beans in the burritos.
I ordered a burrito, one of my daughters had a children's quesadilla and my other daughter had two plain soft tacos with just beans. I give you this level of detail because I have to say that I was surprised at the lack of "accommodation" toward children eating.
Specifically, I always order a child's drink, 1/2 watered down. Places that want you to come back with your children, charge you for one drink when you've ordered two that way (i.e. 1/2 lemonade/1/2 water.) At Raging, where they never once the entire hour and a half we were there came back to refill our glasses, charged me about $2 or so per child's drink (or $4 for the two). Same for my unsweetened tea which was never refilled either.
The children's quesadilla, which is a half order that I ordered with chicken (so there was some type of exorbitant upcharge for other than just cheese) was minimal dried out chicken and a tiny bit of cheese. That's all. My daughter, who eats quesadilla's once a week or so - refused it. I tried it and I understand why she did.
My other daughter doesn't eat anything but either chopped meat or beans in this type of eatery. Since they don't have any chopped meat (better known as hamburger in some places), we ordered the beans only. But they didn't have this on the menu for children and a children's variation wasn't offered to me. So, I had to order the two taco meal off the adult menu - which was supposed to be accompanied by rice and beans. I asked that the rice and beans just be placed on a separate plate as my picky five year old might not like them touching her bean (only) burrito.
They did come separately, in two little bowls. However, the two taco meal with rice/beans (and sour cream on top but we opted not to have that) was $7 and change. I was charged $11.30 for them because they were separated. Two tiny soft tacos with beans only and barely a dixie cup full of rice and beans for $11.30 plus tax. That's highway robbery.
And to have to wait over an hour for a burrito lunch is a crime. An absolute crime.
The bill, with tip, was over $40 and I vowed we'd never go there again. There are so many places making good burritos and the like and serving them in a timely manner -- places that welcome children (and especially their parents bringing them there) . . . by making the menu somewhat flexible and inclusive of the younger appetite.
Too bad.
