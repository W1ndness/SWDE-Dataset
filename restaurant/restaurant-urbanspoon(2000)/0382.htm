Thai one on! Amazing FRESH Thai food!
by Mike Tayon (5 reviews)
May 01, 2009 - Really likes it
My Wife and I literally stumbled across this restaurant in Clovis (next to Fresno) while heading to Olive Garden, and of course, we skipped OG to try it, and are VERY glad we did! This little restaurant is owned by Native Thai's that stick to real Thai or traditional Thai food, as they know it, and of course, being from Thailand, you know you are getting "the real McCoy"! (some "Thai" Restaurants are not owned or operated by folks from Thailand!)
We had the pleasure of meeting the owner himself, and learned quite a bit about the authentic Thai ways of some dishes, and also his little added touches. The owner, whose name is "Wit", really is serious about FRESH food! My wife is a Vegetarian, and asked for some specific food, and Wit had no problem letting her know, that they can prepare any Vegetarian dish she would like based on food from the menu!
We were very pleased with the restaurant overall, but the food will really surprise you at just how good real, fresh, Thai food can be! :)
We had dinner, drinks, and appetizer for $37! I also want to note, the tempura vegetable appetizer we had was very good, not greasy tasting like I wind up making at home, not sure what the secret is :(
If you want a cool local restaurant, are in the mood for Thai Food, and want the freshest, not to mention best value, in Thai food, this place is for you! You will love the friendly owner, he's a blast, and loves people! :)
We can't wait to go back with our friends and family! :)

Great (cheap) Thai Food
by Don Incognito (14 reviews)

Thai Gem is one the best deals in town. Cheap entrees and pretty decent food. In terms of overall quality, I actually prefer Aroy Thai in northern Clovis or Sweet Ginger in northwest Fresno. If I were eating with friends I'd probably take them to one of those places. I think the curry's more flavorful at those places but you'll also end up paying more. But then again, I only get Panang when I eat at Thai restaurants, so I can't speak for the quality of the pad thai here. The panang here is still pretty good.
But for cheap take-out you can't beat Thai Gem.
