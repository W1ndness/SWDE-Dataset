Question?
by Trip Klaus (109 reviews)
April 01, 2009 - Likes it
I continue to be torn by Petite Louis which has hosted my favorite holiday luches in the US. While when busy the din can be obnoxious, the authentic French feel and open fire can welcome. The wine list remains acceptable but uninspiring, especially for Mr. Forman. However, the continuing degredation of the kitchen and serving staff after the opening of all the other Wolf/Forman restaurants lessens my appreciation every visit.
On my last visit the frites were so soggy (after the first batch they dropped on the ground) I couldn't enjoy a basic of Bistro dining. Don't get me started on the pate which used to be worth taking home.
Also, the cheese cart was cut in half and the addictive toast points were poor.
So, I'm torn. I have had many good meals here, but recently I'm disappointed.
What am I to think?

Wee Wee
by Matt S. (2 reviews)
November 20, 2008 - Really likes it
Petit Louis...one of my personal favorites in the Baltimore area.  The cafe-like setting is charming with the old wood, weathered mirrors, classic lighting and the marble-topped tables.  The wait staff is friendly and knowledgeable.  The food is very good and not overpriced, the focus is on a modest amount of food in most choices but the quality and taste allow you too see past the american-sized portions available elsewhere.  The 5 minute trip to Roland Park is well worth the "out of Baltimore experience."

Absolutely wonderful dining experience
by methrace2000 (6 reviews)
July 29, 2009 - Really likes it
My wonderful girlfriend surprised me and took me to Petit Louis for Valentine's Day. This was one of the best meals I've ever had. I've eaten at many great restaurants in France and this is up there with the best. We enjoyed an extremely rich and delicious foie gras on toast points for an appetizer. I ordered the confit du canard, which, while small, was delectable, falling off the bone on a bed of wonderfully prepared beans. She ordered the filet mignon with steak frites. The steak was cooked to perfection as ordered and the steak frites were prepared exactly the way I've experienced eating in Paris. The atmosphere and service were wonderful and the price is well worth it. This would be a $400 meal in NYC. Exceptional. Favorite restaurant in Baltimore.
