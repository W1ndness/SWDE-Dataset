Taki - A Fresh Experience
by JimIsAFoodie (1 review)
May 28, 2010 - Likes it
After numerous drive by's saying "I really want to try that restaurant", we actually did. We were early and didn't expect much in the way of service but we were seated (without reservation... we were that early) immediately and within just a few minutes had menus and fresh drinks. Good start!
We started simple with a ginger dressing salad and some miso soup. Both were fresh and exactly what you would expect. The ginger dressing was the standout here... very fresh tasting on very fresh lettuce.
There is a hibachi side to this restaurant but we were there for one thing, sushi. There was a daily special board for maki rolls along with a large combination of rolls, sashimi, and nigiri of all varieties. No Toro but I have learned to expect that in an Indy sushi restaurant. We ordered 4 rolls and 2 orders of hamachi (yellowtail)) nigiri. The rolls were of all non-traditional varieties with spicy tuna here, spicy snow crab there, shrimp tempura and scallops here, baked, etc. I am not sure this review can do the freshness justice. Every roll, every topping was perfectly fresh with a ton of taste. I would order every roll again with my favorite being the shrimp tempura topped with a spicy tuna, snow crab, scallion combo.... it was a special.
Now... the maki was fresh and extremely loaded with flavor but the hamachi stole the show for me. It was probably the best I have had, and I have eaten at some of the best sushi restaurants in the country. It was sliced perfectly and was so beyond fresh that you couldn't ask for a better piece of nigiri. I would say it was perfect and I can't wait to have it again.
The atmosphere is the best we have seen for a sushi restaurant in Indianapolis. The service was extremely good with everyone being attentive and ready to help. We waited nor wanted for nothing.
All in all this was a fantastic experience and we would go back in a heartbeat.

Could be better
by huiray (3 reviews)
February 09, 2010
Had lunch there:
Agedashidofu
Seared Scallop
Selection of sushi
Nabeyaki udon.
Small salad to start with (gratis), ho-hum.
The agedashidofu was inventive, with beech mushrooms included. However, the breading was curious - it became rubbery when soaked in the soyu, somewhat off-putting, although the tofu itself was firm and OK. The seared scallops were fine/good, nice pureed sauce with it - but there was very little that was 'Japanese' about it. The sushi was a mixed bag...the tuna, yellowtail and salmon slices were good, the mackerel was dreadful, the salmon roe was OK, the sushi rice was undercooked, also needs more vinegar. The nabeyaki udon broth was very salty, the udon rubbery, the tempura prawns too heavily breaded though crisp and tasty enough, veggies too sparse.
Conclusion - needs to get better.
