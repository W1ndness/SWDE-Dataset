All in, best value in the Triangle
by Brad (1 review)
April 25, 2010 - Likes it
I've eaten at the Fed, well, probably more than 100 times. I keep trying elsewhere, but nowhere matches the combination of outstanding food, great service, and reasonable (okay -- more than reasonable) prices. The Fed is the only place in the Triangle where you can get oxtail barbecue, rabbit stew, frogs legs, venison -- no telling what's going to be on the specials menu -- and never pay more than $15 for an entree. And the beer . . . . just think Foothills on draft, plus beers from Triangle, Highlands, Terrapin, Brooklyn, etc. etc. Get there early, or be ready to wait to sit in the main dining/bar area.

Creative food at bargain prices
by tdotson (17 reviews)
March 29, 2009 - Likes it
Downside: it's a dumpy, smoky bar, their Web site is horribly out of date, and the restrooms make those on airplanes look spacious. Upside: some of the best and most creative food in the area comes from the tiny kitchen, which turns out an amazing roster of daily specials at low prices. Frites are the real deal. One of the best bargains in the Triangle is their $5 soup and half sandwich lunch, to which a plate of frites can be added for $2. Far, far better than would be necessary to keep the college crowd happy. If there were enough Federals, we wouldn't need so many crappy chains.
