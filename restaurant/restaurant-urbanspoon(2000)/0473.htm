The Grand Dame of Galveston
by sandy (18 reviews)
July 11, 2010 - Likes it
Venerable Galveston seafood house can usually be counted on when ordering some of the old standards like bisque, gumbo, crab cakes, oysters (in season) and grilled snapper or mahimahi topped with lump crab.
Judging from many reviews (including Robb Walsh, former food critic extrodinairre of The Houston Press) Gaido's can be hit or miss. I've been lucky, having never had a bad meal. Back in the late 70s I moonlighted there on weekends as a waiter. There was always a member of the Gaido family on duty---any problem was resolved (customers were ALWAYS right). Most of the black waiters and kitchen staff had been there decades and really knew their business, so there weren't many problems that presented. Not sure who the managers are these days. It's a shame to see the negative reviews mixed in with the good ones. This place is expensive so there's no excuse for bad food or poor/uninformed service. So far, I've been satisfied. But I always stick with the dishes mentioned at the beginning of my review.

Average seafood
by slidsouth (25 reviews)
November 15, 2009 - Likes it
On a recent trip to Galveston I was looking for the "best seafood" and was directed to Gaido's. While it certainly was good, it also certainly was not the best and was less than I hoped for being 100 yards from the gulf.
The restaurant has a large dining room with ocean decor that created a good atmosphere. They do not take reservations which was not a problem for our early dining party, but would be during the city's tourist seasons.
Service was very attentive and prompt and the waiter had no problem handling our large table.
I started with the fresh oysters which were served with all the usual accompaniments. I will compliment Gaido's on the portion size. I ordered a dozen and was presented with nearly two dozen. They were fresh and well worth ordering.
A dinner salad was average however the dressing selection was both interesting and well prepared.
I had a grilled seafood platter which consisted of scallops, shrimp, and snapper. The snapper was well portioned and perfectly cooked. The fish was firm and flaky and had a great flavor.
The scallops were generously portioned as well. They were a small variety however, either bay or calico and appeared to have been steamed as they were white with no sear or brown. They had limited flavor but at least were fresh.
The shrimp were prepped/cleaned properly but the cooking process also left them very plain. They were also about as small as one could serve in a tail on style which was surprising. Flavor was very average.
This was all served over a bed of rice which was far too wet and ended up pasty. You also got a choice of sides. I chose the AuGratin potatoes which were gummy and had no real cheese flavor.
Only one person in our group had desert and chose a pecan pie slice which was reported to be excellent.
All in all the meal was like winning your dollar back in a scratch off lottery, you are glad but wish there was more..
