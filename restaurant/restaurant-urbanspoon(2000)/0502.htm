Consistency Is The Receipe For Success
by FoodMaven (31 reviews)
January 10, 2010 - Likes it
I first tried Red Elelphant in Tallahassee when my daughter attended FSU several years ago. We walked into a crowded storefront, yet the noise level was not too loud. Even with the array of flat screen TVs and kid-friendly atmosphere, the noise was tolerable. Kudos to the architects.
The location on San Jose is no different.
What makes this restaurant a winner? Good, consistent food and friendly service. The menu vareity is so diverse, you can eat at Red Elephant on a weekly basis and not get bored.
Their lunch special (ind. pizza w/ 1 topping, salad and drink) is fantastic deal. And you can order it during lunch or dinner.
Pizza is their signature offering, but you will not be disappointed with the burgers, salads or dinner entrees. Try the onion rings with the spicy dipping sauce. Crunchy outside and tender onion on the inside.
On a recent visit, we arrived at 9:25pm (they closed at 9:30pm that night). We walked in and was amazed at how crowded it was on a weekday. The hostess said, don't worry about the time, we can seat you immediately.
We ordered a large dinner for 2 pizza special (with 3 toppings and 2 side salads), Salad with Grilled Chicken, and the Classic Burger. Try the Zorba (Greek) or CJ's salad dressing. They are homemade with a unique flavor. You won't be disappointed.
Make it a point to ask for Casey, the General Manager. She is not your typical restaurant manager. She actually loves her job, greets you as you enter, and walks around to each table interacting with customers.
FoodMaven

Try the Burger
by FoodMaven (31 reviews)
June 03, 2010 - Likes it
Although we eat at The Red Elephant at least 4 times a month, yesterday I decided to change my order from the usual pizza, salad and drink to the Cheeseburger.
All I can say is...I found another item on the menu that is awesome. Not your usual fried on a griddle greasy mess, but made with fresh meat on a charcoal grill. And it was cooked to order - Medium Rare.
The bun was also toasted and loaded with shredded lettuce and tomato. There are 4 choices of sides, but the fries are great. I also get an additional side of their cole slaw. It's not your run of the mill slop with mayo & vinegar. It's light and really good.
Keep up the great work Casey and crew.

Economical and Tasty
by Megan (2 reviews)
May 22, 2009 - Likes it
Living within walking distance has lead my husband and me to enjoy the Red Elephant at least a half dozen times since it's opening. We could tell immediately that the food is made from fresh ingredients. We have tried all of the speciality pizzas and our favorite one is Papa Gus' Pesto pie. We also love the bucket of boiled peanuts and the elephant ears. Very tasty. We've tried salads, burgers, pizzas, and wings. Everything is very fairly priced for the food one receives. The wings leave more than a little to be desired, but when the craving calls - I'd rather go to the Red Elephant instead of Hooter's. We can go into the Red Elephant and get an appetizer, a couple of beers, two entrees, and a dessert for about $30. Nowhere else can you find that kind of deal in Mandarin. Fair food for a fair price.
