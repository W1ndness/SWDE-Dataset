Classy, Classic Chinese
by Captain Napkin (284 reviews)
February 10, 2010 - Likes it
Omaha is blessed with many fine Chinese restaurants. P.F. Chang's is the only full service chain Chinese eatery in town, and it lives up to it's reputation for classic quality cuisine at their Westroads location.
We enjoyed their Dinner for 2 for $40 menu, which featured your choice of 2 soups, a shared appetizer (about 8 choices), two entrees (from about 15 options), and two desserts (from 8 options). The hot and sour soup is very good, tending toward the sour side more than many other places around. The steamed pork dumplings were done just right. The Dali chicken was highly seasoned and spicy, perfect for a cold winter's night. The orange peel beef was also quite good. And our desserts...tiramisu and a raspberry chocolate cake, were just fine.
P.F. Changs is quite possibly the most attractive Chinese restaurant in the city, at least since the closure of Imperial Palace several years back. I would rate their food quality in the top 5 or 6 in the city. Prices are very reasonable for the quality and overall dining experience. Our service was excellent and unobtrusive. A friendly and knowledgeable server offered suggestions, our water glasses were refilled and used plates removed as if by magic, without us needing to ask. The manager stopped by to inquire about our meals, and to briefly chat, which was a nice touch.
This is a well run restaurant, and the crowd on a cold Wednesday night was testament to that fact.

Quality food with great customer service
by Randalph Bronson (4 reviews)
May 25, 2010 - Likes it
I've never been here before so i was pretty excited to go.
Upon walking in for dinner, we were greeted by the nice lady at the front and was immediately seated. Our waiter was very nice and took our drink orders and food orders as soon as we were ready. We ordered the Almond and Cashew Chicken, Beef with Broccoli, Lo Mein, Beef Fried rice and the Hot Fish. The Almond and Cashew Chicken was incredible. The bell peppers, mushroom, onions, chicken, almond and cashews paired nicely with each other in the garlic soy sauce. I was especially impressed with the Beef with Broccoli because im used to eating the cheap beef from the mom and pop chinese restaurant and was shocked that the beef was actually high in quality. It's probably the best i've tasted so far. The low mein and fried rice was good. Nothing too special about them, except that they used the high quality beef when cooking it all Not to sure on how much it all cost but i bet it was a lot. Ill definitely be coming back here.
On a side note, the Almond and Cashew Chicken tastes good the next day!

To put it into perspective each entree equals two KFC double downs. Don't get me wrong, the food is tasty and the sauces can make pig butt taste good but its just way too many calories and too much money for so little food. Rare Gluten-Free Chinese
by William Reveal (1 review)
September 28, 2009 - Likes it
Although the food is a touch on the expensive side - read that as you pay for the ambience - it is one of the few good gluten-free restaurants in Omaha and the only Chinese one that I know of.
They gave me a fairly extensive overview of how they maintain gluten-free and it is better in most regards than most restaurants that claim to be gluten-free.
I just wish they had some beef or pork dishes that were gluten-free.
I am especially addicted to the Spicy Chicken which my wife says is mild but is rather hot to me.
Check out their two for one deal if they still have it, full meal for two at a good discount. Desert isn't gluten-free but everything else can be.
