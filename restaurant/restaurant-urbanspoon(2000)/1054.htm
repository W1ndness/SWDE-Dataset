Just not up to par
by Matthew (2 reviews)
May 03, 2009 - Doesn't like it
I've tried this place twice.
I gave it a second shot tonight and it just failed. The fixed price menu sounds like a good idea but its really just an excuse to not serve a full menu on Sundays.
This place is tiny, 4 booths, 2 two tops and an excessively large bar which only serves wine. If your taller than 5'10 you will have to hunch down when going upstairs to the restroom.
The food is just not up to par especially for the price. Tonight I had a salad which was acceptable. The entree was served family style for two people - there's no reason to just plate it up on two plates especially on such a small table. The chicken was very bland and mostly dry - no flavor at all served with chopped potatoes and carrots - very disappointing.
The dessert was kinda gross - some apple based grain pudding. This could just be a personal taste but not good.
I walked in with optimism and walked out disappointed yet again. There are many good restaurants in richmond - this is not one of them.

NICE LITTLE SURPRISE,,
by Kevin (1 review)
June 17, 2010
Last night I was taken to Cafe Rustica,by a special friend,(who has been working in the downtown area for some 30 yrs.) and had never been.
We both "Love" great food,and fun little places to eat ,that are good food, good value , ..and off the beaten path.
This place was ....well none of the above , it is right on the Main st. had fantastic food,and great prices !

One of the best
by mr.rm (6 reviews)
June 18, 2010 - Likes it
This is by far one of my favorite places to go and dine alone. With the large bar, you don't have to feel like a jerk taking up a table. the food is simple, prepared well, and delicious. I don't go here as often as I should, about once a month, but I always leave happy. Expect a bit of a wait if it's busy, the kitchen is small and so is the staff. So enjoy a cup of their delicious coffee, or one of the many tasty wines and be patient. Good things come to those who wait, and good things is what you will get. Prices are very reasonable. If there were more eateries like this place and The Black Sheep, vs. places like 3 Monkeys, and Delux, Richmond would be a dining mecca. Those places may look slick and hip but they lack the one thing that these other places have, heart and soul.

Excellent food and experience. Great Find!
by lmyers (1 review)

It is a great spot for a good time with friends. The menu represents most of the Old European cuisines and desert list was great although we had to skip it this time.
There was a beer on tap, good wine selection too. Service was excellent. I am not sure why people complain about the setting.
