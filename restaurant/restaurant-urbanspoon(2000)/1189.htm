Cool atmosphere, stellar food
by Ken (22 reviews)
February 24, 2007 - Really likes it
My favorite among the Tom Douglas restaurant group, this is the place I bring out of town guests when I want Seattle to impress them.  The space is beautiful but not stuffy, the menu is sophisticated without being intimidating, and there's enough variety so everyone can find something to they'll enjoy (tip: the appetizers also make good entrees).  Personally I can't stop ordering the roast chicken (moist, delicious, and generously portioned), preceded by the "enormous crouton" salad (their novelty take on caesar).  For dessert you can't do better that the coconut cream pie (from the Dahlia bakery, a staple at all of the Douglas restaurants).

Fun
by Kristin C (5 reviews)
July 03, 2010 - Likes it
What a great place. We've been coming here for years and enjoy the ever changing menu. You don't see many kids here (usually) but we've found the servers to be more than friendly/helpful when we've gone with our (well-behaved) child (10) past several years. Just brought guests from out of town and had three excellent desserts, including the triple coconut cream pie (I know, you'd think we'd be tired of it by now, but no -- it was better than the last time), a delicious strawberry pie and a chocolate tarte (?). All were good. Very loud restaurant, which works for us, but just a heads up if you have anyone on hearing-aids. Consistently excellent, interesting, well-prepared food, much more complicated than I'd bother with at home (which is why we keep coming).

Since moving to Seattle, this place has been high on my list of favorites. My last visit however my experience was flawed by the snoot of the service. Yes this is a Tom Douglas resturant and as expected the food was more than delicious, however... My server Sean Liming wasn't up to Palace standards. He seems to know about the food and wine well enough but he was a little too suggestive on some dishes and almost pushy with some. We would discuss different menu items wich he "loved" then they would show up on my table without my ordering them.  To my supprise, he would simply bring these plates and add them to my bill. One such dish was the giant Palace wings, they're marinated in tobasco and soy sauce overnight then grilled with apple wood. The wings are then served drizzeled in a corriander cream sauce wich I loved. Overall I'd reccomend this resturant to anyone, I'd just try to avoid that Sean at all possible. Other servers I'd suggest, Dave P, or the beautiful and brilliant Cassie.
