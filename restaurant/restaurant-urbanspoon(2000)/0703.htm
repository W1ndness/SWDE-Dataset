Enjoyable Dinner
by BizzNessMPLS (127 reviews)
June 22, 2009 - Likes it
Four of us went to dinner at Tavern on France Saturday night. They offer call ahead, so we put our name on the list and were seated at a table immediately after arriving. The place is pretty big and relatively busy. It did get busier as the night progressed, but it didn't seem like the staff was affected. We started with the Farmers Market Pizza, which was good, nothing spectacular. I went with the build a burger option with a side of mashed potatoes. The burger was juicy and cooked to a perfect medium. The mash potatoes were okay, definitely better than what I had expected. They tasted fresh. I have had a lot that taste like they were made in the morning or the day before and just re-heated to order. My wife had the Patty Melt and had no complaints. For the four of us before tip, it came to $70, which included five Summits, a soda, the Pizza Appetizer, and four meals. That seems pretty reasonable to me.

Phony reviews don't get me going
by Bob McClain (1 review)
June 02, 2009
The first two reviews were obviously phony. As a professional restaurant reviewer, I can spot a fake review from across the bar.
While I realize that most restaurants try to "game" the system by posting fake reviews, no one is going to believe that a restaurant is that good. Especially from a person who has one review, this one, and a person with ten reviews says the place is lousy.
If a restaurant owner or manager is going to have someone post phony reviews, at least make them believable, What patron is going to claim that the restaurant food is "scratch made"?
Or say that a restaurant is "perfect for children" or simply talk that glowingly about everything?
Even when I've written reviews of exceptional restaurants, I always found a few things to quibble about or some improvements that could be made.
Let's get real people...
