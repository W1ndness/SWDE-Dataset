So very happy to have this tapas joint
by Soup Fork (69 reviews)
February 18, 2009 - Likes it
I really enjoyed my experience at Barcelona Tapas. The decor is bright and open and the international Chill music is not too loud. I started with the Sopa de Alubias (black bean soup) which was rich and piping hot on a cool day. Next up, Pincho de Pollo y Chorizo(chicken and chorizo on a stick) which were cooked perfect, small grill marks and not dried out. I tasted the cumin alioli, but found that eating the meats unadorned was more to my liking. The baked goat cheese(Queso de Cabra al Horno) had a tang and a bit of heat in the tomato sauce. I was rather stuffed at this point because I had been nibbling on the Pan con Alioli (warm garlic bread with olive oil). I want to try every item on the menu in the future. Indianapolis should consider it's self lucky to only have the second restaurant in this small chain.

Good Food - Questionable Service
by Katie (43 reviews)
May 25, 2009
Barcelona definitely has good food, and I really love the sangria, but the last time I was here my service was so bad that I left just plain mad.
My guess is that, in the early dinner hour of 6 ish, the bully bartender told the host to seat someone in his section. The restaurant was not busy at all, and she tried to seat us at a tall bar table. I told her I didn't want to sit in the bar, so then she moved us to a table next to the bar, right by the hallway on the way to the bathroom. When the bartender came by, I told him "I don't want to sit here, I want to sit in the dining room." His response was an angry "Fine, sit where ever you want." So we did.
I should have known better because the problem was that no one knew who's table we were... I understood this, and eventually we were greeted and our order was taken.
The next problem came when my fiance and I kept watching items that we had ordered be delivered to the next table, who were quick to pass them around. Finally when the asparagus and paella made it to the our table, it was delicious.
For dessert, the flan was excellent... The churros were dull, but I wasn't picturing what I had ordered.
All in all, we left Barcelona Tapas with a very bad taste in our mouth, and it wasn't from the food.

Great Tapas, Not So Great Service

November 23, 2009
I think one of the best trends in dining out these days is the tapas. Mini-courses served over the course of an hour or so, while you drink and chill out with your friend or friends. You eat nothing but get to taste everything. Best dishes: the curried chicken salad was to die for; the olives were great; pass on the gazpacho ( too acidic). Speaking of acidic, the service was rather that way. When I asked what was causing the delay of a particular tapa, that was ordered long before the others we had received, the waiter snapped at me. Also, there is just an air about the staff, like they know they have a good thing going in this one-horse town and you can take it or leave it and they don't care. It's not that the wait staff bothers you, it just that they kind of don't want to be bothered with you at all. Apathy, that's what it was. The wait staff was really apathetic.
That having been said, food was great, sangria DELICIOUS, not very pricey at all, and I can't wait to go back again. As a matter of fact, I did.

The Flavor of Spain in Indiana
by Jessica (3 reviews)
January 28, 2009 - Really likes it
BARcelona is not a restaurant, it's an experience. First of all, if you're not used to tapas, it's not a meal, but a series of "finger foods" brought out in courses. One central plate is brought out and your party takes from that plate (much like appetizers). The food itself is flavorful and has a variety from cold plates with fresh tomatoes and cheeses to hot tapas of meats. Their sangria, be it white or red, is delicious, as are their desserts. This is not a place for a quick in and out meal though. The atmosphere is laid back and the whole concept of tapas is getting together with family or friends and enjoying. So, if you have the time, they have the flavor.
