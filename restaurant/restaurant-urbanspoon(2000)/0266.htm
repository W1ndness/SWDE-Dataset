Wunderbar
by Icewolf (150 reviews)
August 18, 2009 - Likes it
I've been before but I had to go again. Just good food and good atmosphere. We sat in the adjunct room which was good because the Ooom-pah band was just setting up in the main room and would have made it hard to hold a conversation. Leiderhosen fans would have approved they sounded great from where we were.
we started with the pretzel bites. Which was a soft pretzel cut into pieces and then deep fried and served with a spicy horseradish mustard. Yum. For my meal I had the sampler platter with 1/4 of each Knockwurst, Bratwurst, Bahama Mama (Original spicy) and Bahama Mama (mild), plus sauerkraut and I opted for a double order of latkes & applesauce (known there as potato pancakes). Really really good wursts. Kraut was fresh and cooked with bits of pork embedded in it. Served with a fresh hot dog bun that was tasty to eat alone!. Of course we ended with the classic giant cream puff! One in vanilla, and one of the new peanut butter fluff w/chocolate ganache overtop. My god, both were so good and completely sinful.

I like it BUT...
by Penny (7 reviews)
July 10, 2010 - Likes it
I like Schimdt's BUT it use to be much better. At some point in time home made dishes became pre-made. You can go to Teejays and order the exact same cabbage rolls you get at Schmidt's, at half the price. What they do make in restaurant is good: sausages (not made in restaurant, but made by Schmidt's), German potato salad, cabbage & corn beef, cream puffs, tarts and pies. (FYI: The german chocolate is pre made too.) And stay away from the sausage stew, it's left over sausages. At the end of the night any sausages not served or left over from the buffet go into the next day's sausage stew. And trust me, they don't look pretty after baking under a hot lamp all evening on the buffet. ( Yes, I use to work there.)

Yah, Der Goot Potatoes...?
by The Big Cheez (3 reviews)
March 02, 2010 - Doesn't like it
This is an admittedly biased review so take it for what it's worth... I'm no fan of German food with all the potatoes and brats and kraut, however, that said, my two trips to Schmidt's Sausage Haus with my good German-food-loving friend has allowed me to mellow in my harsh critique of ALL things bratish. Because I wished to sample the many different Bavarian-style foods, I have had the buffet both visits. I was not impressed...except for the exceptional warm potato salad! It was "der goot!" Now, I can take the brats or leave them, esp. the "Bahama Mama" or whaterver it was called... not good to my palate. I was also not impressed with the cream puff. I had one with a "chocolate" filling (weak with little taste) and it was just ordinary.
Hey, I loved the atmosphere and excellent service/directions etc... Appreciated its historical value and it centainly enhances the ambiance of German Village. Everyone should try it once, maybe twice but for me, after two visits, I can take it or leave it and I think I will leave it for now. I give it a 6.5 on the lederhosen scale.
