Excellent Thai; Amazing Patio
by Chris (62 reviews)
October 23, 2008
It's hard to beat Teak. The food is always solid (despite the funkiest menu in the city). The terraced patio is a great place to eat outside (despite the fact that there is no view). And the atmosphere is funky and lively.
Best bets on the menu: Massman curry, chicken coconut soup, pad thai (best in the city I think) and anything they make with asparagus.
The service has varied widely during the last 12 years. Some of the younger servers have been indifferent at times. The older staff is outstanding, especially when dealing with families.
It's a great place to eat and then walk around to enjoy the rest of the Mt. Adams neighborhood.

damn good food!
by Mark H. (43 reviews)
March 29, 2010 - Likes it
Went Sunday night for dinner. My first time there, but my GF has been a few times. What an awesome atmosphere, seriously. Decorated nicely and just a great layout. Seemed a little slow, but it was Sunday so I wasn't too surprised. We were seated upstairs by the window giving a decent view.
We started off with the Kanisu appetizer. While it was good, I wouldn't order it again. Just bland. Would be 100x better if it had a nice spicy sauce on top, but that's just my preference.
I ordered the Spicy Fried Noodles after I asked the server his opinion and difference between that and another dish. WOW I am so glad I ordered this. The holy basil in the dish tastes like cracked black pepper just like he said. Spiced just right at a #7 and I'm glad I had leftovers for lunch today.
My GF is a sushi junkie and I think Teak is #2 on her list of the best just behind Kyoto. She got a few rolls, but the one she raves about and was so excited to get again is their Honey Bee roll.
All in all, it was a great meal with really quick, efficient, and friendly service all around.
Not huge on sushi, but I'm glad they have solid thai food, so I'll definitely look to go back again.

Quality Work, or Work on Quality?
by Christian (3 reviews)
March 18, 2010 - Doesn't like it
Teak, what can I say? Want atmosphere? Want good service? Want a beautiful restaurant to dine in and have a few drinks? If these are your staples, then by all means visit Teak because not many other Thai and Sushi places can compare. However, if you're think that you may be interested in the taste of the food, the quality work of the sushi, and exceptional pricing for all of the above, then maybe you should visit elsewhere.
I can't say that I really dislike this place, because I don't. The service is great and friendly, and the overall experience is decent, I just want more! I want food that doesn't taste like its been prepared for the masses, sushi thats been waiting for me for a while before I got there, and just plain quality in the areas that really matter to me.
To be genuinely fair, I would give this place a 3 out of 5, and if you're in the area, stop by. If this is out of your way, then you may just want to hit up one of the many other Thai and Sushi places in the area, because a craving should be properly satisfied.
