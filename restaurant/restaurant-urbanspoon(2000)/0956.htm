AMAZING
by Adam Piazza (33 reviews)
August 06, 2009 - Really likes it
There is your classic steakhouse and that rustic feel of steak, but than there is Mastro's. The excitement of the word just has a ring to the name. Although named after the family which founded it in 1999. That name put the signature on 400 degree plate that accompanies your USDA Prime cut of dry aged selected Steak.
Experience wins here, Opening a meal with bread, but not any bread a pretzel dough tube roll, makes for some change and sitting there the atmosphere and service is impeccable. I was there for my Culinary School Graduation dinner back in 06, and my stepbrother, not into dressing up wore his casual clothes and wasn't even frowned upon. His eyes were huge and he felt like he was back in Philly with the flare of the pretzel roll type bread, and awaiting our steaks, management was sure to come over and congratulate my accomplishments and check on us personally throughout our meal
Waiting for our steak selections, a tower of seafood can be witnessed passing by with cold steam pouring off it. I still use this dry ice effect inspiration in some of my plates.
Steaks arrive, plate were not the metal crap sizzle platters but normal plates, but HOT, my steak was ordered Med-Rare perfectly done, some parts were pretty rare, but all i had to do was hold the cut piece awaiting it's fate in my mouth, to the plate and bring it up to the temp of my liking. Oh and the Gorgonzola mac and cheese, Why not, ha ha, put a different spin to Kraft. Was extraordinary.
I talk we began in Philly and the meal ended in Philly, with a that signature warm butter cake to hammer at our full souls. This was comped by the Chef and Management with a congratulation note, Even beyond that, I was congratulated by my name, which show attention to detail...
The modern spin on an American steakhouse here was right on. Nothing fancy and petite about it. The beauty of this place is very intimidating to walk in but will never look down upon anybody and make all feel welcomed and treat everyone as they would want to be treated. You will arrive hungry and if you aren't, the air and ambiance will strike a nerve, departing you stuffed and most of all... Impressed and talking of it for weeks. I write this review now and this is off memory from years ago. My Favorite restaurant so far in Arizona...

Good, but very expensive
by JG (8 reviews)
February 03, 2010 - Likes it
My husband and I ate at Mastro's last night for our anniversary. The staff was a mix between great and inattentive, although our server was very good. The restaurant was very dark, and it was hard to even see our menus. Everything was clean and stylish with the decor and the place settings. The chairs were comfortable. My only real issue was the extraordinarily high prices. We eat out often and go to a lot of high end places such as Ruth's Chris and other venues in Las Vegas, Scottsdale and Southern California. This is by far the most expensive meal we have ever had. Our steaks cost $49-$50 for a bone-in filet (18oz) and a chef's cut new york strip (20oz). All sides were purchased ala carte and served family style. We ordered the creamed corn ($9, absolutely delicious), au gratin potatoes ($9, but not worth it) and lobster garlic mashed potatoes ($33, and very good). With an iced tea ($5) a strawberry daiquiri ($13), our total bill was just under $180. After $30 tip, our dinner cost $210. I'm not saying the food wasn't good - it was - but I just couldn't believe how high the prices were. If you want good food and don't mind paying over $100 per person, this is a good restaurant to try.

Great Steak, Great Service
by Tina (3 reviews)
August 21, 2009 - Likes it
Last month my husband and I were in Scottsdale on vacation and really were craving a prime rib dinner. Mastros was the only place we could find that served it on a Monday night. In fact, they serve an amazing prime rib every day of the week.
When we arrived we realized this was a very upscale place, which was a pleasant surprise. Our server was fantastic and the manager, bussers, etc could not have been more accomodating. The food was to die for. Yes, it is your usual steakhouse fare, but Mastros does it just a notch better than anyone else. Ruth's Chris has always been a staple destination for steak for my husband and I, but Mastros tops them by a mile. The shrimp on the cocktail can hardly be properly called shrimp. They are huge- really giving meaning to jumbo shrimp- and the sauce was the perfect compliment without overpowering the fresh taste of the shrimp. The prime rib we were craving was so huge (28 oz) that we had to share. The seasonings were spectacular. Usually, I don't think a prime rib should be over seasoned- salt, cracked pepper and garlic are generally enough. Mastros seasons its prime rib with a number of herbs and spices that, like the shrimp cocktail, add flavor without taking anything away from the beef. The martinis are also huge and delicious!
We heard from many of the resort guests where we stayed that Mastros was too expensive. In the words of one gentleman, "I had to take out a mortgage." This seemed odd to me since we were all staying at a rather luxurious, and not too cheap, resort. My husband and I are from New York and, perhaps, a little price jaded when it comes to restaurants but we felt the prices were right on target for the kind of restaurant Mastros is. You get what you pay for and Mastros does not disappoint.
