RE: Lunch Today
by FN (1 review)
September 10, 2009 - Likes it
I read these comments almost every day and bite my tongue most of the time. However, after reading the last comment, I really feel the need to respond. I am the daughter of the owners.
We go out of our way to buy tomatoes from Pungo instead of from a large distributor…my mother cleans buckets of fresh lettuce everyday and is extra particular about the way she stores the lettuce to keep it crisp. My father makes our soups from scratch. We roll our own stuffed grape leaves, make our own spanakopita, tiropita, moussaka, pastitsio, oven roasted potatoes, briam, green beans (the green beans are frozen, yes…but definitely not canned), all of the dips and sauces are made here - not store bought, we grind our own beef, marinate our meat, make our own tzatziki sauce, buy the best products that we can find, and all menu listed desserts are homemade. We skewer each stick of meat and vegetables that comes on your souvlaki dinner (by the way…it comes with 2 sticks of pork or chicken, a stick of vegetables, two sides plus tzatziki sauce and bread)…I'm not sure what you expect for $14, but I should fill you in on the fact that minimum wage these days is $7.25 per hour and we employ several people and have to pay for food, utilities, labor, and an expensive rent. That and we are not here working around the clock for nothing. We have to make some profit, too.
Trust me, there is no lettuce from a bag, we do not have a pre-made Greek dressing, and we certainly do not used canned green beans.
You have the right to your own opinion, but at least get your facts straight. There is a chance that maybe your green beans were overcooked. I'm not sure and apologize if things weren't to your liking. I have noticed that sometimes they are inconsistent… we are aware of this and are working on a way to fix it. They always turn out better in smaller batches, but when we are busy and need green beans in a hurry, we have to cook them in larger batches.
In addition, I have read and made adjustments from many of the comments from previous posts. Sorry, though… I have not changed the price of our beverages. I am not sure why people think that is so outrageous… I pay $2.50 all the time at several places. The next time we print menus, though, we will strongly consider reducing those prices.
Many of the complaints referenced below are server errors, too. While we try to weed out the weak servers or offer them better training, we cannot do that if we are not made aware of who needs it. Blasting the websites with news of poor service does not really help to fix anything.
We all work very hard to try and make things perfect. Sometimes it doesn't happen that way. There is always an owner on the premises, although usually in the back cooking. If you drive by - even on Sundays when we are closed - you will see our cars outside, because we are still at work trying to catch up on all of our homemade food…unlike the restaurants that you referenced which are using canned goods and microwaves. I am very offended that you would compare us to them.
We strive to offer the highest quality of food and service, the best way we know how. Many of our customers agree that our food is delicious, that it is authentic, and some have even said that it is better than the food that they had in Greece. I know that they are speaking the truth because I see many of the same faces several times per week. We hope to make this the experience of everyone that comes to Yanni's Casual Greek.
If you have any questions or concerns, feel free to email me directly at fayeinvb@cox.net

More lazy than casual.
by Jim Gitman (50 reviews)
May 14, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I think all of the complaints about Yanni's prices may have more to do with the care and quantity that one receives for their money. The prices aren't very high, but the quality and serving sizes are so low that one ends up feeling ripped off anyway.
I will concede that Yanni's spanokopita is amazing. Every bite offers a full range of textures, going from crisp to flaky to soft to creamy as you descend through the pastry's savory layers. Even so, $7 for a 4-inch cube of appetizer seems a bit pricey. The bruschetta was above average, herby and sharp in flavor. Unfortunately, you're only given half a dozen thin rounds of bread to eat it on (about an inch and a half in diameter). The entrees, whether meat or vegetable, were all overcooked and could have passed for appetizers in many restaurants. It's fine if they want to offer reduced portions and try to make us more fit, but don't continue to charge full price for food that is so simply prepared and features no expensive ingredients.
