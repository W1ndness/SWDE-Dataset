Good, a bit noisy
by Mike (8 reviews)
October 27, 2009 - Likes it
I ate here the other night for the first time. I sat at a teppanyaki table. The soup was basically beef broth with onion flavor, 2 or 3 tiny slices of green onion and 3 razor thin slices of mushrooms. Not very impressive at this price point. The salad had a ginger based dressing. The shrimp appetizer was very good, as was the fried rice. I had the filet mignon and scallops which were also pretty good. The meal was $30 which was about on par with similar teppanyaki places.
The decor was very modern, slightly dark and VERY noisy as others have mentioned. It's really hard to carry any kind of conversation and is more like a night club. There were a lot fo 20-30 y/o and it was a pretty attractive crowd. A few local NBA players were eating while I was there. Supposedly this is one of the restaurants in Scottsdale "to be seen" and the place was pretty packed. Reservations are recommended.

Recommend | Not helpful

Noisy but good
by Walt in Scottsdale (33 reviews)
August 06, 2009 - Really likes it
Just had dinner for the first time here and agree with other reviews that it is noisy. Very noisy. Not with music but with conversation. Some will find it too much a social environment. However, I went with friends and their 7 year year old daughter. Really good food, present with pleasant and quick servers. Las Vegas Rolls were great. The mussels in a spicy peanut sauce were my favorite but really wished they had cut the mussles from the shells prior to presentation. Like a lot of asian restaurants the price can get quite high if ordering singularly but generally reasonable when ordering in a group.
We had a great time and we will return.
