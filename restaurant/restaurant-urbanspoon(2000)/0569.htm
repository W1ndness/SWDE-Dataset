Quite the Surprise

May 12, 2009 - Really likes it
Having not tried Stone Crab before I wasn't sure what to expect of Joe's but was pleasantly surprised by the quality of the food. While not cheap, our party of four started with a full order of crab claws and legs ($140) and they were one of the most enjoyable seafood dishes I've ever tasted. Fish entrees and steaks were prepared perfectly and the drinks were excellent. I would consider service to be excellent not only due to the quality of the wait staff but also their honesty. When we tried to order more food than our table could enjoy our waiter politely informed us that we were likely overdoing it based on their portion sizes - something I always appreciate from a professional waiter who isn't out to inflate the check and his tip percentage. Very good meal. Bring a wad of cash (no joke, one appetizer is called the "2nd Mortgage") and prepare to be blown away.

Fish on Fridays
by Greggndc (74 reviews)
March 10, 2010 - Likes it
Trying to keep the Lenten abstention from meat, we "suffered" though a meal of stone crab at Joe's. We all shared a plate of crispy calamari (the cocktail sauce was pleasantly spicy and the creamy sauce had a zesty lemon flavor - but rather a small portion of each) before heading to the main attraction - the stone crab claws! I am not usually an eat-with-your-hands kind of guy but could not resist the chilled crab. Sweet and delicate we easily tugged the flesh from the shells - that had been expertly cracked in the kitchen. Joe's signature mustard dip was a wonderful contrast to the sweet crab. Service was lovely, adeptly headed by the very cute Scott who kept our glasses filled and table clean. The wine list has a pretty nice variety of bottles in both shades of nectar from the US and aboard, although it was a bit pricier than other restaurants of this caliber in LV. The bottle of Sancerre we had was amazing and even converted my red wine -loving family. Cocktails before were rather pedestrian however and questionably worth their rather steep price. Overall, a great experience.

Good seafood, not sure about the hype though.
by Mickeroo (4 reviews)
March 06, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I was very excited when a friend chose Joe's as the restaurant to go to for her birthday. I had not been and was intrigued by all of the good reviews here and on other sites. After going, I'm not sure I get the hype.
The restaurant itself is very nice, if a bit loud. There were six of us in our party and we were seated at a large circular table in the dining room. At times it was often difficult to hear the people on the other side of the table over the din of the other diners and service staff- I think if you had a smaller table or one of the booths it would be less of an issue. The tables are a bit close as well, which I'm sure contributes to the sound level.
I wouldn't say the service was the best I've ever had as I've been to other restaurants of this price point and my glass never got below half-full and all it took was a glance to get some assistance. The service was certainly adequate, but again, nothing memorable.
Most of us opted for the Crab Biqsue, which was reported to be good if a bit salty. I went with the BLT Wedge salad and I thought it was very good- the bacon was very smoky, something I enjoy. For dinner, most of us chose the medium claws, which- although they come pre-cracked- do require you to get in and pick out the small shell bits and occasionally spit the errant shell into your napkin, so it's not for the person who doesn't want to get a little dirty. I thought the stone crab was very nice and the seven claws you get in that order are plenty, really. I think I honestly prefer King Crab for its flavor, but these were a nice treat as you just don't find them everywhere.
Unfortunately, the sides were pretty mediocre. We ordered the broccoli, asparagus, Lyonnaise potatoes, and the pan roasted corn. Of those, only the pan roasted corn was something I went back for seconds on. The other veggies were bland and the potatoes were a little overdone and over-salted for my taste.
We split some desserts and I've got to say that the Key Lime pie didn't seem to live up to its hype either. It was very firm, so not sure if it had recently come out of the freezer or what, but I've definitely had better. The coconut cream pie was tasty, though.
All together our dinner for six came to right at $400. That's not bad for a high-profile Strip restaurant, but it's certainly no bargain and I'd argue that with some of the prix-fixe specials at other Las Vegas restaurants, you can have a better meal and a nicer overall dining experience for the same money.
