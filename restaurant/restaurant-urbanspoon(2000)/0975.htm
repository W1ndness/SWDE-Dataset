I don't get it.
by mrsriffle (1 review)
March 09, 2010
We were looking for some place new for breakfast. Called early on a Sunday morning and they had no line yet so we went. By the time we arrived there was a line out the door. We took two seats at the counter, which was a dangerous decision. You have to keep your arms and legs close to you as if on a roller coaster ride, to keep the buss boys from ripping them off as they fly by with huge carts. We ordered an omelette and a breakfast burrito, both were huge but lacking. And the "signature sauce" that was smothering our food was no more than marinara, which I would never put near my breakfast. If you love greasy, oversized portions of low quality food served in a loud dirty diner by waitresses that couldn't be paid to smile, then head on down. Otherwise the best breakfast in Pittsburgh can be found at the Door Stop in Dormont. There you will find the best pancakes you've ever had, consistant quality and free samples while you wait.

Average Food, Poor Service
by sarasster21 (2 reviews)
April 19, 2009 - Doesn't like it
We have been to Deluca's four times now, and each time it has gotten progressively worse. Based on Deluca's appearance on Man vs. Food, we tried the mixed grill on this occasion. It was bland and oily. We also ordered pancakes. They brought the wrong thing and then tried to argue with us about what we ordered. They reluctantly brought out a new plate of food and tried to charge us for our original order, which cost twice as much. Only after arguing with them for several minutes did they correct the bill.
We should have gotten out of line after hearing multiple people grumble about it not being worth the wait as they were coming out of the restaurant. We should have listened to the people walking by telling us that Pamela's (around the corner) is much better.
We personally recommend the Dorstop in Dormont over Deluca's. The Dorstop;s food is by far superior, and the people there are much more friendly. It's a little farther out of the city (across the Liberty Bridge, through the tunnels, up West Liberty and right on Potomac), but it is worth the drive.
We do not anticipate revisiting Deluca's again...ever.
