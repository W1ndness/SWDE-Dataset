Great For Sunday Brunch
by Julia (35 reviews)
October 20, 2009 - Likes it
Went back to the Waldhorn this past Sunday for brunch and had a great visit. Normally, I avoid buffets, but their brunch buffet is nice. I love the cooked-to-order omelette station as well as the selection of traditional breakfast foods and traditional German foods. The people are friendly, service was good and quality of the food is excellent. I would definitely recommend giving them a try for brunch even if you are not typically a fan of "German" food -- I don't eat beef, pork, cabbage, etc but I can certainly find plenty of other options here that are excellent (Spätzle, Chicken, French Toast, Cheese Grits, Home Fries, Fresh Vegetables, Fresh Soup of the day, and delish fresh breads). Brunch is also an excellent time to try some of their various desserts such as fruit cobblers, Napoleons, Bienenstich Kuchen (Honey cake with cream in the middle and honeyed almonds on top), Black Forest Cake, Apfelstrudel, Eclairs, and many others.

Authentically German, authentically fun.
by hungrywanderers (24 reviews)
December 28, 2009 - Likes it
My wife and I were very excited to see that Charlotte had a German restaurant, but were skeptical about how good a "German" restaurant out in the suburbs of Charlotte could be. We were pleasantly surprised the first time we went, and have been back more times than we can count. The food is good and authentic, lacking a lot of super-rich, over-the-top sauces and dishes that other "German" restaurants have. The beer selection is top-notch for any beer aficionado, including only one domestic draft from Charlotte's original German brewery, OMB - http://www.oldemeckbrew.com/. Eat, drink, enjoy.
