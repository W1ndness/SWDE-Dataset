Testa
by Amy Boyce (10 reviews)
January 05, 2007 - Likes it
The first time I went to Lupa, we ordered the Affetati Piccolo, an assortment of house-cured meats.  There was one plate, the Testa, a sort of meaty paste on a plate.  My companion asked our jovial waiter what was in it - he replied "I'll tell you when she's had some," pointing to me.  I had some and (it was good) and was quickly told (with some relish) that they made it by taking a whole pig's head, boiling it on the stove for a day, scooping out the inside and adding some nutmeg.
That's Lupa in a nutshell - a place that can even make THAT taste amazing.
Their ricotta gnocchi are light and yummy, their carbonara exquisitely nutty and the sunchoke appetizer is a fresh tasting surprise.

Restaurant Review: Lupa, the she-wolf gets it right
by Dan Shapiro (29 reviews)
February 25, 2009
Lupa-The She Wolf gets it right
We are just back from Rome and where we have essentially ate our way across the city, so going to an Italian restaurant in New York is definitely taking our chances. BTW, there is no bad pasta in Rome, but there is terrible Pizza.
Lupa, the she wolf, symbol of Rome, is wonderful bistro, on Thompson Street, nyc, that gets it right. The waiters are extremely knowledgeable, (do you know the difference between Borsci and Lucano digestifs) and are actually helpful, rare in New York.
Appetizers are served in soup bowls and are tasty and distinctive. I liked the Winter Squash alla Romana. Pastas are bountiful and have interesting twists; the Bavette Cacio has a bread crumb topping. The major compliant is that the selection is small. The main courses fish or meat are seasoned just so. Sorry, Vegans need not apply. While the wine list is not cheap the variety is amazing and half carafes are available. Desserts are definitely Italian style and even the Tartufo, an ice cream bombe, has a certain savior faire.
Italians entertain at restaurants not usually at home and Lupa always has several large groups going at one time. Rather than assign a single waiter to a large table teams of waiters work together and that way everyone gets served together.
Warning the front room is noisy and reservations are not only a must, but weekend reservations are hard to get. Dinner for two with an inexpensive wine comes to about $140.
for more see www.ditmasestates.com

Perfect
by ASL (24 reviews)
September 09, 2009 - Likes it
You come to the big city with your heart filled with hope, but you know how it is. You worry, just a little, that maybe the fancy pants restaurant people will talk down their noses, give you a crappy table, make you wait even though you have a reservation. And worst of all, you worry, just a little, that the food will not live up to the hype.
Banish all fear and go to Lupa. We were a party of four, obviously out of towners (OK, maybe from the outlying boroughs, but we're talking deep Queens or even the dreaded Staten Island!). We were directed to the bar for a drink while our table was readied and were seated within ten minutes of our reservation time. Waiter was helpful, polite but not obsequious, professional and just the right amount of human-element.
And the food. Oh my gosh. This is how we eat in heaven, except with no calories or cholesterol. You have to get the meats -- housemade soprasetta, head cheese, speck. Gorgeous prosciutto. The gnocchi were quite simply sublime, like little clouds, with fennel and sausage in the sauce. Also good pasta, plate du jour specials. Another highlight -- amazing dessert of panne cotta with just a hint of cracked black pepper, served with what must have been wild blueberries. The combination of the perfectly made milky white panne cotta, the black pepper and the blueberries, one of the best things I have ever eaten.
