Fancy Dinner spot downtown.
by dfwdean (253 reviews)
February 14, 2010 - Likes it
If you remember the old location, this is not as fancy or dressy, but it is still very elegant, and waitstaff are very attentive, and the food is still upscale/fancied-up "cowboy chow".
Make reservations or plan on sitting at the bar for a while (which is a good thing if you enjoy drinking good wine and cocktails: Reata Rita and Lemonades are favorites).
The waiters seem to be 'lifers': they have worked for here for years and have their regular customers but never ignore a new customer. You feel well taken care of and they are not over-bearing.
The food is always good here: the appetizers are well done. If you can't make your mind up, go for the sampler (menu says 2-minimum, but as a party of four we found there was enough for all of us to share and we are not by any means light-eaters). You get two samples of everything listed (this could be a big meal for one person too): Bacon Wrapped Shrimp with Onion Jam and Cornmeal Star, Tenderloin Tamales with Pecan Mash, Fried Calamari with Cowboy Cocktail Sauce, Wild Boar Ribs with a Peanut Dipping Sauce, Tumbleweed Onion Rings with Spicy Serrano Ketchup, Jalapeno and Cheese Elk Sausage, Smoked Quail with Jalapeno Cheddar Grits with Molasses BBQ Glaze, Southwestern Chicken Tacos, and finally the Calf Fries with Cream Gravy.
For the main course, two of us went with two of the evening's specials (Buffalo New York Strip, Baked Sea Bass), the other two went for menu staples that never disappoint: Martha's Vegetable Plate and the Chicken Chile Relleno. All cooked to perfection, served in a timely fashion, and all very filling. I've never had such an amazing Vegetable Plate. Wow! Mac-and-Cheese for a side was very tasty.
We left room for dessert of course: Dessert Tacos (crispy sweet taco shells, a little difficult to eat gracefully), Pecan Pie (quite thin on phillo dough crust sitting on a bed of melted white chocolate), and Apple Crisp were all good.
Highly recommended for dining with very close friends and those family members you really like.

Wild Game Paradise
by yotababy (13 reviews)
November 19, 2009 - Really likes it
Oh, this place was great. When we got there we were told there would be a 30-45 minute wait, so we went up to the fourth floor rooftop bar and got drinks. Ten minutes later our table was ready, so we tromped down the stairs (the elevator in that place was ridiculous slow) and were lead to our spot. First they brought us some jalepeno cornbread muffins and almond bread with some sort of flavored butter. The cornbread muffins were fabulous! Next we went with the wild boar ribs with peanut sauce and it was FANTASTIC--the meat was tender and juicy and I could have eaten a bowlful of that peanut sauce ALONE it was so good! Then I got one of the specials--pheasant ravioli with chili-rubbed red onion rings--and that was pretty amazing too. I actually ended up taking that home with me and having it for two more meals! My husband got the buffalo lasagna, and said it was delicious as well. For dessert we split a bananas foster creme brulee and OMG, I don't even know what else to say about that! The atmosphere is great--sort of a fancy hunting lodge meets old west museum feel. We were seated next to a case of saddles we joked must have belonged to Ghengis Khan (they really did look like they could have) and they had several private rooms (with clear glass walls) available for larger parties. The prices were higher than we typically pay when we go out, but in my opinion, MORE than fair, considering the quality of the food, service and atmosphere. Highly recommended!
