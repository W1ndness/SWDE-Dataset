Message from Japonais

  • Chic and Elegant Setting
  • Diverse Sushi Selection
  • Global Wine List

Since opening in 2003, Chicago's Japonais has received three stars by both the Chicago Sun Times and Chicago Tribune, and was recently named 'Best Restaurant' by Chicago Magazine in 2005. With a vibrant and exciting scene, Japonais offers Chicago residents and travelers alike modern Japanese cuisine in a setting that is at once intimate and exciting.

Renowned for creating the award-winning menu of Mirai Sushi, Chef Ichikawa oversees the restaurant's astounding sushi selection as well as all cold items, while Chef Kato handles the remainder of the menu.
The extensive Sushi menu has both traditional and contemporary items. Hot entrees such as Kobe Prime Rib and Teriyaki Salmon are featured as part of this unique Japanese fusion.
The well-thought out wine list is global, featuring selections primarily from France and the United States, with Australia, Germany and Chile as well.

Treated Well
by John Epp (12 reviews)
March 29, 2010 - Likes it
First, The service the whole night was excellent. From the Bartender downstairs to our waiter at the table.
Second everything was running late. Had reservations for 9:30 and didn't sit down till after 10. If your in a hurry this might not be the best place. Once seated though food came out in a timely fashion and service again was excellent.
Third, The food was uniformly excellent. The Nagiri and Rolls we had were excellent and priced about right, the Shrimp Tempura roll, was the best of the evening. The appetizers were also very good, but I felt were a bit overpriced. Don't go looking to only spend a few bucks, this is going to be an expensive but very tasty dinner.
Last, Ambiance was a bit loud. People are here to see and be seen. Lots of beautiful people and lots of noise. The music could have also gone down a few notches which could have helped.

Wonderful dinner
by CMF (3 reviews)
March 17, 2010 - Likes it
I lucked out and had a friend bring me here for a birthday dinner. The bar downstairs has incredible drinks and better sushi. We had dinner in the dining room, and it was incredible. The "Rock" appetizer seems a little silly, but the sauce was perfect, and being able to cook the meat as much (or as little!) as you want was amazing.
My duck entree was perfectly cooked and tender, and my friend's surf and turf had the best cooked steak I have ever had. We split a bottle of sake (their sake list is excellent) and I had the creme brulee for dessert.
HIGHLY recommend this restaurant for any special occasion. The service was flawless, never hovering but always there when we needed something.
