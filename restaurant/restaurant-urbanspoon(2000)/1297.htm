Lots of hype for an OK burger
by Haz-Matt (61 reviews)
May 11, 2010
Given all the hype, I was expecting much more of a restaurant when I arrived but was a bit taken aback at the atmosphere (think: college dive) and the crowd as a whole. It became apparent that the correct mode of operation is to stand in line - no sign, just a herd of people lowing gently like cattle en-route to the slaughterhouse.
The menus are at the very end of the line, just before ordering so it helps to know what you want ahead of time or you'll cause a hold-up. The pre-fixe burgers are a nice touch for those (such as myself) who have a tough time making a culinary decision.
Once you've paid for your order (cash only), it's strictly self-service. The cashiers offer little direction other than to take the orders so go ahead and take your chips or drink if you ordered one since you won't be prompted.
Finding a seat can be a challenge and there are signs on the wall admonishing anyone who would dare to move a table or chair without prior permission. Seating is very tight and the furnishings are reminiscent of a flop-house - nothing fancy.
Listen closely for your number to be called out as the wait staff walks around the dining room. The burgers are quite thick and juicy which is a very good start. However, it's not the "be-all, end-all" burger that everyone seems to tout. Yes, it's a GOOD burger and the value is excellent but it's not so good that I'd want to run the gauntlet again to experience.
Overall, I'll give it a second chance - now that I know the place is truly a dive and that courtesy from the patrons is nil, I won't be astonished at the shabby appearance or the rudeness of the people who mill about, waiting for their burgers "to go".
If you're in the area and want to reminisce about college days and scream at each other to be heard during dinner, this is the place to go. Otherwise, there are other burger "joints" that offer similar products, albeit not with the Ray's name, at similar prices in a much cleaner and pleasant environment.

The "Hell" must have something to do with the indoor temp.
by Johnny Nuckols (12 reviews)
August 11, 2009 - Likes it
I was trying to figure out what the "Hell" in "Ray's Hell Burger" was for. The only thing I could figure was that it was hotter than hell in that place! If you're lucky, you can grab a table near the front and away from the kitchen.
Despite the selection and names, these guys don't play. You order, you grab a table (don't try to get a table until you order and get a number first), and wait to have your food brought out to you. The place is pretty shabby even for a burger joint; 20 oz sodas in bottles, chips in bags, water dispensed out of a large jug, and no large menu board to look over, but I like it. It might be a little awkward ordering if you've never been there. Just look for a copy of the menu hanging on the wall to the right by the counter, and know what you want and how you want it when you order.
I've had 2 burgers from there so far, and didn't even bother with any sides. They weren't to die for, but they were pretty damn good. Tastier than Fuddruckers and Elevation Burger, and a much more adult choice than Five Guys. First time there, I built a burger: Au Poivre (peppercorn crust) bloody with Gruyere, roasted garlic, and cognac sauteed mushrooms. My second visit, I had "The Dogcatcher" mainly because I figured it would be what Bourdain would get if he went there. Talk about a rich, unhealthy burger! 10 oz of beef topped with a good 2 oz of roasted bone marrow. Sure, I might as well have eaten a stick of butter, but I wanted something unique and deadly. Maybe next time I'll go for something pretentious like their "Burger of Seville" with Foie Gras and White Truffle Oil.
This place stays busy. While Ray's is packed, the other restaurants in that strip mall are dead. Definitely worth trying if you've never had it, but be ready for a pretty serious burger.
*Note: The address is 1713 Wilson Blvd., but the actual entrance is at 1725 Wilson under the "Ray's the Steaks" awning.
