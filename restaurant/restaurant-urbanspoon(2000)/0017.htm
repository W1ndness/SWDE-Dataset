Everything
by Lily Mas (29 reviews)
December 25, 2009 - Likes it
I used to go to this place after an evening class with friends all the time when we were constantly broke. But....we could get a big bowl of soup and all the rolls we wanted to go with it - and it was great soup. This was all for around $5.00 if we drank water! Since I've graduated, I've tried a lot of the menu - Buddha Bowls (vegetarian or add meat) for $8.59 straight, Southwest Turkey, Lo Mein ($8.59), and good old Mac & Cheese @ $5.99 - all are great food. I swear the spicy Chai there has cured my colds before. There's free Wi-Fi and lots of students working or just hanging out. One of the most endearing things the place does is allow - even encourage - you to take a magazine off the rack and look through it while you're there! The only problem with the place is the baked goods. This was originally a bakery and you can see big machines used and they still make the best baked goods I've had in N.M. So, the problem? You take a tray with your number and your drink...AND your dessert that you picked out of a large and varied display. Your food is delivered as soon as it's ready, but by then, I've eaten my coconut cream pie or chocolate eclair or..... Dessert first isn't a bad thing, though, right? I highly recommend Flying Star on Central.

Flying Star Cafe: The Crossroads on Route 66
by Randy Burge (2 reviews)
February 17, 2009 - Really likes it
A city's social heart can be measured best by the places where pedestrian traffic thrives. In Albuquerque, one of these places is the Flying Star Cafe in the epicenter of the Nob Hill vibe.
Morning, noon, and night, the place and pace is a humming orbit of eclectic diners and urban dwellers visiting amid the Flying Star's trademark pastel and neon hues. Business suits (execs) sit across from backpacks in flip-flops (UNM students) next to grandmother and her three pies (grandkids) near two murals (artists) - all partaking in the great upscale diner fare and convivial din.
The Nob Hill location is the original Star among the constellation of nine Flying Stars in the region, one of which will open in the Rail Yards in Santa Fe very soon. Each Star has its own substantial character and edifice, worthy of any metro area in the world. In addition to the Flying Stars, the proprietors have also created a five planet system of smaller quicker service Satellite Coffee houses.
http://www.flyingstarcafe.com/locations.htm
http://www.satcoffee.com/locations.html
The Flying Stars are hubs for local growers whose produce ends up fresh as fresh can be in a variety of dishes. The food and service is high quality and consistent.
The menu in each Flying Star is a broad, healthy range of sandwiches (hot and cold), salads, soups, burritos, through buddha bowls. My favorites are the Crave salad, a lively mixture of lettuce and fixings that lives up to its name; the Graburrito, a fast and tasty burrito wrapped and ready for on-the-go; and the ABC Patty Melt sandwich; accompanied by coffee or my personal half/half blend of lemonade and Red Stuff punch, no ice.
The many offerings in the dessert counter are made by the restaurant bakery under the guidance of a renowned European bakery chef. Cookies and pies, tarts and rolls, cakes and scones beckon you as you pass by the glass showcase during the ordering process - usually a fast-moving line seven or eight people deep to the left as you walk in the door. Buy the slice or take out, Flying Star desserts star in many celebrations in-house or on back patios at home.
Other major attractors to the Flying Star Cafes are the magazine racks that span two walls filled with several hundred magazine titles available for purchase or perusing. Also, Flying Star has the best free wifi of any restaurant in the region, attested by the glowing faces of mobile netters working/playing and eating at all hours. Indeed, Flying Star serves food for the body and the mind.
Comparatively the food prices are high normal for the region, perhaps one of the main complaints about the Flying Star Cafes, but a little extra is a small price to pay for the exceptional quality and care in the bustling joints all.
If you are in Albuquerque (and soon in Santa Fe) your trip will be complete only if you indulge yourself at least once at your neighborhood Flying Star Cafe, our city's home away from home. You get what you pay for still holds at the high quality Flying Star.
