One of BR's best
by Mike (15 reviews)
February 05, 2010 - Likes it
After Katrina, I quit trying to go anyplace near LSU -- the traffic at all hours and the lack of parking just aren't worth it -- so I was pleased when they opened the Coursey location. Nice, pleasant building, too. I always go for lunch, and I always try to get there just a whisker ahead of the noontime rush, and I've never had to wait. Like most of their customers, although I've tried most things on the menu at least once, there are a few items I really like and I tend to cycle through them. Best is the fried catfish, which comes with first-rate hush puppies. After that is the red beans & rice with sausage, with which I usually get a big tossed salad. That's a full, satisfying meal right there. I tend to judge "American" restaurants by their club sandwiches, and the Chimes Club is one of the best around; the blackened chicken breast makes it. Finally, the crab cakes is listed as an appetizer, but it's large enough to make a good lunch -- if you can keep other people at the table from insisting you share it. If I'm having lunch with two or three others, we generally get the onion rings to split among the group, too -- they're close to perfection. And whatever sort of beer I'm in the mood for, I know I'll find it on the Chimes's huge, multi-page beer menu. How can you beat a place that offers all of that?

EatRouge.com Review: Chimes East
by EatRouge (14 reviews)
July 29, 2009
For lunch today, I decided on one of Baton Rouge's favorite restaurants, The Chimes. Normally I go for the seafood poboys or pasta which are fantastic, but today I decided on the rabbit and sausage gumbo with side salad.
The gumbo here is served very thick, something unusual when it comes to gumbo, and served over a bed of rice. The taste is truly fantastic and definitely one of the best gumbos I have ever eaten. The big chucks of rabbit meat and andouille sausage work very well together and the thickness of the gumbo itself make the meal more filling. I'm stuffed for now, but I'm sure I'll be craving this meal again later.
Calling all beer connoisseurs! One really cool feature of The Chimes is the "Round The World Beer Challenge". This challenges guest to drink 60 beers from 20 different countries (not is one trip of course). If you succeed in doing so, you will be immortalized on the wall plaque.
Food Rating: 5 out of 5 Claws Up!
Service
The Chimes is a very popular place to eat. Being constantly packed, it can make it hard to get a seat and your food out quickly.
When I arrived, there was a wait so I decided to go sit at the bar area for my meal. We sat straight down and ordered our drinks. The waitress was friendly but a little slow to take out order once we were ready because of all the drinks she was making for people at the bar. But all in all she did a pretty good job for all the people she had to serve and it really didn't take long for the food to come out. Whatever wait I had, it was well worth it.
Service Rating: 4 out of 5 Claws Up!
Atmosphere
The Chimes has a great atmosphere featuring a patio, bar area, televisions, upper and lower deck and the around the world beer challenge. Its clean, open, and fun.
Atmosphere Rating: 4.5 out of 5 Claws Up!
Overall: 13.5 Out of 15
