+ 7 more photos from Gulf Coast Guy First trip in and not the last....
by Gulf Coast Guy (820 reviews)
January 15, 2010 - Likes it
Despite being in a bit of a funky location on Nebraska, Ella's holds much promise and judging by the crowd already amassed when we left at 7:00 P.M. it is already a big deal.
Atmosphere is all at once, funky and homey and very comfortable. Outside tables even looked inviting and will be in the temperate weather to come.
We were in with five of us and a little one and seated in the back in a large booth with tall bench backs. Actually liked the table as much as anything.
Nice bustle in the place without being too loud and distracting.
Started with a hummus app and also ordered the Northern Italian salad with pecans, granny smith's, gorgonzola and a great light vinegarette mixed into a plentiful plate of spring greens and lettuces. Shared with three people and nada left in the bowl.
Also nice hot bread in the form on pistolettes came out with hummus and salad.
Hummus was unique in that it had chickpeas and black beans in it with a nice "cup" full of hot toasted pita strips for the dipping. Ran out of the pitas with plenty left and Nick, our fine server this evening brought out some more to our table. The only rap on the hummus was that is lacked some flavor and was thought to be a bit bland. It was tasty though.
On to the entrees,,,,,,,,,
Badd Burger with 1/2 lb. angus patty, roasted red peppers, goat cheese and garlic aioli. Ordred with zucchini fritters. Came out nice and a tasty treat although to my pallette, I thought the goat cheese overwhelmed the meat a bit too much. Fritters were crunchy outside and soft inside. Nice change from fries.
Daughter got the Good Burger which is a veggie patty and she chose the sweet potato fries which were delish in crinkle cut style.
Son got the pasta with chicken and mumbled approval when asked about it. It was the Seminole Heights pasta. Nice looking dish.
Wife ordered the shrimp wrap app and noshed on everyone else's plates too. The app had tempura shrimp and yams and was a bit too "fried" for her taste but good nonetheless.
Son's girlfriend and future wife(Right son? ;0)) got the cheese pizza and added spinach and it was given two or three or maybe four thumbs up.
We had enough to take home three boxes with pizza, pasta and burger bits.
Nice eclectic selection of brews and some wines. Try the St. Bernardus ABT12 and the Samurai Rice Ale. Different ends of the spectrum and good complements to the food.
Ernie, one of the owners stopped by and I told him I looked forward to Soul Food Sunday visit and some chicken and waffles. Nick told us to come after 4 PM on Sunday and we would also be treated to live music as well as the soul food. Deal.
Service was great and friendly and Nick an asset to the place.
So here we go on the GCG four seashell rating scale:
Atmosphere: 3.50
Service: 3.50
Food: 2.50
Drinks: 3.00
Overall 3.25
Look forward to next visit and to get to know folks here on a first name basis. Nice bar too for relaxing.

Just Plain GOOD!
by SeminoleHeightsCouple (2 reviews)
April 03, 2010 - Likes it
I have dined at Ella's many times. I have yet to have a bad entree item or experience. I know that Sundays can be a little crazy with the music, and they can run out of BBQ by late afternoon, early evening, but I don't care. I understand that they prepare fresh food daily, and for that I will deal them running out of items on occasion. I have learned the reason they are not open for lunch is because the kitchen staff preps the entrees for the evening shift during the day time. When it's gone, it's gone. I would rather them run out of food then to serve me something that was prepared three days ago. This is why the regular menu is not available TO ANY ONE on Sundays. Don't be so quick to judge before you know the whole truth about Ella's. Ask questions. Get answers.
By asking, I have also learned that the upstairs area is not normally open for regular dining. It is only used when the weather doesn't cooperate. And for that matter, hats off to the service staff that has to climb those stairs a thousand times a day. Let the complainers try your job for a day.
Take it, or leave it. This is one of the best little affordable places to dine in Tampa.
