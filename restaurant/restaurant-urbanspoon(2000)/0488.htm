Haute Hoosier
by Greggndc (75 reviews)
April 21, 2009 - Really likes it
My party hopped in cabs to go to Oakley's Bistro from downtown Indy…. $80 dollars later and a few detours for directions we arrived. While we all adjusted our minds to the price our outing was going to cost, we were suddenly greeted by a blonde angel. She showed us to our seats and we settled in to our discretely elegant surroundings. Cocktails were ordered and were the prefect tonic (although the Manhattan martini was neither Manhattan nor martini - want a fruity drink? The cosmos were great). One of our group was commenting about the expense and the news somehow made it to our blonde angel. She told the hunky chef (we presume) and he provided nourishment in the form of shrimp corn dogs and a bacon tart with spring peas of such delight I am at a loss to describe it except it was splendid. The corndogs…who would have though? A large shrimp was encased in cornmeal dough and lightly fried… was that a hint of curry in the dough? It was drizzled with honey and served on wooden planks and we gobbled them down like hungry fair goers. The knowledgeable and adorable waiter described the entrees and starters and also gave sage advice regarding the wine list. A surprising number of us opted for the duck done two ways. A tender breast was pan roasted and served with its companion - duck confit - with a surprising twist as it was served under a basil-infused waffle with haricots vert. I modestly started with a romaine salad that provided a light and perfect start to the rich duck but other opted for the sweet pea soup - a perfect spring green. Other diners ordered fish - a well grilled salmon that looked crisp yet tender. We almost skipped dessert since we were stuffed but relented on our waiter's suggestion that a bit of sweetness might be the right note to end the night. He was again a seer. Molten carrot cake with a rice krispie wedge and a scoop of ice cream gave each of us just the right bit of sugar for the ride home. There were no complaints about the cost of the ride home since this was a singularly delicious journey for a group of foodies from both coasts visiting the heartland.

Oakley's
by Mariposa (59 reviews)
May 06, 2009 - Really likes it
Simply put, the best restaurant in Indy.
I really enjoyed dining at Oakley's. It'd be easy to drive by this random restaurant in a northside strip mall, but the interior is cozy and nicely decorated. I split the crab cakes with the bf, and we ended up fighting over them. There were six mini cakes in a tiny cast iron skillet, topped with corn salsa and andouille sausage. It was an unexpected and seriously tasty combination.
For dinner, I ordered the duck two ways, and the bf ordered the filet mignon. The roasted duck was tender and had an amazing smokey flavor, and the confit was equally moist and yummy. The filet was a perfect medium rare, so needless to say, we were both quite pleased.
We split the chocolate dome cake for dessert, and while it was quite good, it was the least memorable part of the meal. It was too dense and chocolatey for my tastes, but splitting it was the perfect amount.
The service and atmosphere were great, and the bf and I are definitely planning to return.

Absolutely Delicious...
by Sherrel Harris (72 reviews)
January 17, 2010 - Really likes it
Wow, what a meal. I have been here several times, and the food has never disappointed. Tonight, my wife and I started out with the Butternut Squash Gnocchi. This was going to be hard to beat, I thought.
For my next course, I went with the French Onion soup and it was very good. It had a nice carmelized onion flavor. There was also a bit of playfulness with the presentation. Rather than floating a slice of baguette on the soup and then covering the top with melted gruyère, they served this soup with a hot grilled cheese sandwich. And to change up the flavors a bit, the cheese is actually cheddar. It was a tasty second course.
Then came the main course -- beef short ribs with portobello mushroom purée, potato mash, vegetable giardiniera relish, and blue cheese tater tot. The potato mash was good but not too rich. It was probably meant to be a foil for the very rich short ribs and the blue cheese tater tot.
The blue cheese tater tot was interesting. When I first bit into it, I did not get much of a blue cheese flavor up front. Just when I was beginning if I had bitten into the correct item, the flavor of the blue cheese unfolded. I am a blue cheese fan, so I thought this was also very good.
Finally, the short ribs were braised to absolute perfection. I have never had any this good.
Overall, my ratings for the evening were:
Food: 4
Service: 3
Atmosphere: 3.5
