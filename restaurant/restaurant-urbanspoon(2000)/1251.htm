A new fave of the wife and I.....
by Gulf Coast Guy (820 reviews)
February 10, 2010 - Likes it
....when we head down to AMI and venture out for some good seafood. Surprisingly there are no places on AMI with raw or steamed oysters. Weird I know. So we did some adventuresome driving and using Urbanspoon and Google found this gem. Right on 41 and right before the turn to Siesta Key. have visited a couple of tiems now and sat at the bar. Got some steamed oysters and raw ones too. Attentive service and a boisterous crowd inside and out. Place sits on a little lake and is really reminiscent of the old time seafood houses. Prepare to wait for a table during peak.
They have an extensive menu and we want to go back and expand our samping to other items. Lots of interesting looking dishes.
Rated on my four seashell scale:
Atmosphere 3.50(keeping in mind the place)
Food 3.00
Service 2.75
Overall 3.00
Only issue is to be careful when ordering the steamed oysters bucket as the literally hand them to you with a shucker. Can be dangerous if you do not know what you are doing.

Good Eats + Waterfront Dining
by Steve M (140 reviews)
April 22, 2010
I have been coming here for years and years and the quality has always been pretty good though I admit - I haven't really eaten here lately. You could not ask for a better location though. PCVOB is located right on picturesque Phillippi Creek with moorings that you can even pull right up to in your boat!
While you sit at your semi-outdoor or outdoor table on the water enjoying cocktails and apps such as steamed mussels and peel & eat shrimp, you'll have a great bar at your disposal that features beer, wine and liquor while you anticipate your fried, broiled, blackened or grilled seafood. You can even feed the mullet and the 'gators in the creek while you sit at your table on the dock admiring the nature.
When the food comes out, you'll find it pretty decent. What I really love are the steamed dinner pots they serve that are filled with oysters, mussels, FL lobsters, stone crab claws, shrimp, corn on the cob, onions, celery and a wonderful broth. I pretty much always have a decent meal here and all the servers and bartenders have always been as nice as they possibly could have been to me, while being efficient and pleasant too.
Truth be told, it ain't exactly cheap, but the food's good, plus the incredible ambiance here can't be beat and compared to similar places (Wait - there aren't any similar places in SRQ), it's just about the going rate. If I were recommending anyplace seafood for Sarasota visitors or even locals, this would probably be one of my top five venues.
Phillippi Creek brings back SO many great food & fun memories, both recent and from long ago. I'll always go back at least for the nostalgia.

Not a tourist trap!
by Restaurant Lifer (15 reviews)
July 17, 2010 - Really likes it
I've been going to Philipi Creek for years despite my fathers protestations that the place is a tourist trap. The old guy just hates crowds. I've been to prettier seafood restaurants but for a place modeled on an old Florida fish camp this place is the tops. My wife and I went there twice on a weekend in June and we were very pleased both times. They have excellent oysters which was emphasized when we went to the competition down the road and paid more for much weaker shellfish. They were plump and briny, just the way you want them. The sandwiches were well built and the grouper was fresh. What really surprised me this trip was two things. First we heard some grumbles from a nearby table about the portions. I'm a big guy and I was stuffed so I'm not sure what the little sixty-something guy was carping about. Secondly I was really impressed with the service both visits. Over the years the service has always been attentive but not really excellent. Both visits this time we had servers that really, really knew their craft! I'm a 25 year restaurant veteran and these two women I would hire in a second!
Lastly, when people complain about the prices at a seafood store they generally have their heads up their ass. Good seafood isn't cheap if you don't have a boat no matter where you live. You get what you pay for. Cheap seafood usually equals old seafood.
