Good, but expensive
by reddot (27 reviews)
May 01, 2009 - Likes it
I like this place a lot. The concept is similar to Coldstone, but with a lot more variety. And they don't smash in the ingredients with spades, they use this kind of blender / auger device. There's a lot of mix-ins that you can get, perhaps 50? They typically have around 10 to 15 ice cream flavors. Chunky mix-ins work best, as anything semi-solid or liquid just gets totally dispersed. The ice cream comes out like soft-serve, as the mixer isn't feezy, like the stone counter at Coldstone.
The waffles are very good. But you can take a while.
I am always happy with the food, but it always seems to cost more than I expect. Also, they don't make their own ice cream, but do buy really nice quality stuff.
I find they are always generous with their servings.
The owner is often there and he's a really nice guy. You can tell that he takes a lot of pride in his work.

Oh Yeah, Love that PGH LATTE
by Joseph (2 reviews)
April 15, 2010
What I really want to talk about, is the PGH LATTE. Not sure if any other cafe sells this, or if it is an OH YEAH original. Billed as "short and strong", it is just that - a shot (or two?) of their fine espresso, with a bit of foamed half and half (instead of reg milk or god forbid skim/nonfat milk) and some raw sugar added - giving it a similar mouth-feel as a good salted margarita rim And, there is very little half and half added - so it is not your regular "tall latte" volume. Strong, velvety smooth from all that extra milkfat, with the cream adding almost as much sweetness as the sugar. Really a nice drink. Besides a straight espresso shot, this is my favorite cafe drink, period.
Interesting reading through the reviews prior to this - I have never had an issue with Ethan the owner, in fact the few interactions I have had with him have all been cordial, and all of the staff is very good and quick to serve.
