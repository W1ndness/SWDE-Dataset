What a Great Surprise
by Bob (1 review)
June 07, 2009 - Likes it
Dining at a Tapas restaurant is about good food, albeit in small portions, giving you a chance to relax, enjoy good conversation and live entertainment. This gem in downtown Waukesha does this with a great atmosphere and great food. You don't have to drive to Milwaukee or Chicago anymore.
The flavorful portions are small and will leave you wanting more, just when your next dish comes to the table to be shared by all. If you are looking for a "mega" burger, this isn't the place. If you are looking for a relaxed dining experience, this is the place to be.
Take a chance, sit down, relax, order a bottle of wine and enjoy.

Dear Todd...\
by Sandy (1 review)
July 27, 2009
I think that it is unfortunate that you didn't enjoy your visit to Generations. As a tapas restaurant, the food items are meant to be shared, and served in courses so that you can visit and enjoy everything as a group (which your server will happily explain to you before you order). As one of the kitchen staff I can tell you that we often get orders that have "same time" marked on the ticket for groups who do not wish to share, and can assure you that we always send the items out at once. We are more than happy to "bend" a bit to suit any of our guests needs, and I hope that you consider this before you decide not to return.
Tapas [n] The serving of tapas is designed to encourage conversation because people are not so focused upon eating an entire meal that is set before them. Also, in some countries it is customary for diners to stand and move about while eating tapas.
-wikipedia
