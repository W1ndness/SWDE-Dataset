Doesn't Measure Up
by Arielle Norman (30 reviews)
July 27, 2010 - Doesn't like it
Knowing I would be in Boston for just a weekend, I had been asking everyone I met who'd lived there where I should eat. One Bostonian told me Santarpio's pizza was the best he'd ever eaten (and we currently live in NY, where there's some GOOD pizza). Then I ventured to their website, where they've got a video posted that shows a poll that proclaims their's the best pizza in New England. So I had high hopes and expectations for the mind blowing culinary experience I was about to have.
Unfortunately, I wasn't really a fan. When my sister and I first walked up, there was a woman out front who asked if we wanted to sit at the bar or a table and upon receiving our preference of a table, said, "Hmm, let me see what I can do," then disappeared for a moment into the restaurant, looked around, and waved us in. There were several empty tables, so this made no sense except as a hokey way to seem quaintly exclusive or something. Then when we ordered "a carafe of Chablis," simply reading off the menu, the waitress responded, "The white? A carafe of the white? Gotcha," sort of instructing us in the "down-to-earth" little way they do things here at the institution that is Santarpio's Pizza.
All fine and adorable, but on to the pizza. We ordered the cheese and garlic. We received a cheese, garlic, and anchovy pizza. For my tastes, the cheese to tomato ratio was low, and the anchovies were so salty that they overpowered the garlic the waitress assured us was on there somewhere. We were also assured we weren't being charged the 2 bucks for the unwanted 'chovies, but it sort of ruined the experience for us. I had kind of wanted to try anchovies on pizza though, so at least my curiosity was satisfied. I also wasn't a big fan of the burnt crust that leads to a ridiculously thin center that doesn't even start to hold under the weight of the tomato sauce. I guess some people prefer this style, but I like the NY style better (and I wasn't raised on it at all, by the way. I've simply learned it's my favorite after much style sampling).
I do kind of think I should go back and give it another try, this time ordering extra cheese and sausage (and maybe some of that sausage and lamb appetizer that looked so good at the table next to ours), but this is on my agenda maybe one line above trying KFC's new Doublicious.

Best Pizza in Boston. Period.
by JP (1 review)
March 03, 2010 - Likes it
I am a Boston local born and raised. I have always heard of Santarpio's from certain people here and there, but I never set foot in East Boston, so I never had the motivation to try it out... Trust me, that all changed.
I like many different kinds of pizzas depending on what I am feeling. I like Ernersto's/Pizzeria Regina for a good quality pizza. I like the Upper Crust/Sweet Tomato pizza when I want fresh toppings with thin crust. And of course, I like Little Steve's when I've had more than a few pizzas in me. However, I have to tell you, Santarpio's pizza blows them all out of the water. The best pizza I've ever had was in Italy, and I must say, Santarpio's rivals that.
Santarpio's is a no frills Italian-American pizzaria that is all about good, fresh, high-quality food. Simplicity. Most locals don't even bother with the menu because they already know what to get.
When I bit into the pizza for the first time I was thrown back. It was so good, I was actually a bit shocked. Living in Boston all my life, I have grown accustomed to the same, lame, overdone pizza. It was a very nice surprise. The sauce and the cheese go perfectly with the toppings, and every bite feels just as good as the last. The crust is crispy and slightly on the thinner side... which is good. One gets the feeling this is exactly how the pizza tasted like when the Italian Americans really made it their own during the first half of the 20th century. True authenticity.
I recommend starting off with the homemade sausage and lamb appetizer (charcoal grilled to perfection). The hot cherry peppers and italian bread go perfect with this by the way. Try the red house wine, it's smooth and slightly sweet. Any of the pizzas are delicious, but I like the sausage & garlic.
Warning, if you go to Santarpios once, you will start having withdrawals within a week... ultimately forcing you to come back for more.
