Better than Graeter's but pricey!
by Closet Gourmet (80 reviews)
April 24, 2009 - Likes it
Cincinnati is fortunate to have two wonderful ice cream establishments. While Graeters has gone on to expand around the tri-state Aglamesis has remained small with only 2 locations. Walking into either you feel transported back to a simpler time before the days of convenience store ice cream. Only a couple of Graeter's older stores hold this magic. I love both ice creams but consider A's a notch above and have yet to have a flavor I would turn my tongue away from. A's also has Italian ice and candies.
Now the catch! This decadent treat and the ambiance come with a price. One annoyingly small scoop breaks the 3 dollar barrier and adding a second scoop is over the top. Judging by it's growth Graeter's has the pricing down for premium small batch hand packed ice cream. $3.25 for the first generous scoop and only 50 cents for the second. It is all about value even at this level. Perhaps this is a conscious choice by A's management but I know I would pass up Graeter's every time if the value were better at Aglamesis. Enjoy!

100 years of excellence
by GG (32 reviews)
March 02, 2009 - Likes it
Okay, sure, everyone knows that Graeters has great ice cream, but have you been to Aglamesis Bros.? Aglamesis has great ice cream too, served in a actual dish (if you eat in) and the place has tons of old world character. While you're there check out the original Louis Comfort Tiffany lamps and marble counter tops. After your done, stroll over to Blue Manatee children's bookstore and café (for a darn good cappuccino).
Bon Appetit magazine named Aglamesis Brothers as one of the last, authentic ice cream parlors remaining in the country. Let's support them!
MY TOP FLAVORS:
Cinnamon
Vanilla
Strawberry chip
Mocha chip
Banana chip
The classic thing to order at Aglamesis is a chocolate sincere soda with vanilla ice cream. Their "swiss raspberry" sundae can not be beat. It's REAL whipped cream mixed with raspberries. As far as the candy counter goes, dark chocolate turtles are the gold standard.

Rebuttle to the last review
by matt003 (1 review)
May 13, 2009 - Likes it
I happen to be an employee at this great store! I love all these great reviews and enjoy reading them. However, in the last persons review it states that a "small scoop" is pricey at over $3.00. That is very untrue! a single scoop (over a third of a pound with 0% over run aka air in it) costs only 2.25. a simple sundae with a larger scoop is 3.25 and 2 scoops is 3.70 making it comparable to greaters 2 scoops but with much greater quality. Sorry but I had to get the facts straight. Unless someone gave you the wrong price, there is not a single scoop over $3.
