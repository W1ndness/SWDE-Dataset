Little gem
by Little Spoons (15 reviews)
January 27, 2010 - Likes it
I try not to write reviews until I've been to a place a few times - so many reviews on this site (any, really) are either "Likes It" that are rife with exclamation marks, or "Doesn't Like It" that are just angry rants against one crappy server or sub-par dish. That said, I've been to Cooper half a dozen times since it opened in October, and it's definitely worth checking out. They're always hopping during typically busy restaurant hours, but if you go after about 8 pm during the week you'll (mostly) miss the cougar crowd. Its SLP, people, there are bound to be the cougars. Ask your server (I've never had bad service - take a second to recognize when it's superbusy, and calm down) for their menu recommendations. If you do order something you end up not liking, ask to chat with one of the managers - guest feedback goes a long way anywhere.

Great place -- Check out the secret room!
by lshallm26 (1 review)
November 15, 2009 - Likes it
I've been dying to check out Cooper ever since I drove by and saw people dining in the newly opened restuarant's cool pub-style windows. I finally stopped in for the first time last week and really enjoyed myself. Here are the highlights:
The nearby heated underground parking garage has an elevator that deposited me a short distance from the front door of the restaurant.
Great decor and interior design. Reminds me of charming European pubs I ate at in London and Dublin as a student. I went during a very busy lunch rush, but there was plenty of space, it was not overly loud or noisy, and the servers were very prompt and attentive.
I ate at the bar, which was shiny and clean with plenty of space to eat a meal. The bartenders/servers (even the ones not officially serving me) and the manager all stopped by to chat with me and were very friendly.
The food was fantastic. I got the open-face meatloaf sandwich, which includes a hearty portion of meatloaf with onion crunchies on top and "Champ" mashed potatoes and gravy. Highly recommended!
Here's the cool part: While waiting for my food, one bartender told me about their special room that can be rented out for private parties--it's hidden within the cylindrical wall of the bar and feels really VIP. Since it was not being used at the moment, the bartender invited me back to take a peek inside. If you go, ask your server if they'll let you sneak a peek, too!
All in all, a great experience. Cooper is the new neighbor this area has needed for a long time!
