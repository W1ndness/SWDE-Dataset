Good but tame
by A Tevington (707 reviews)
February 15, 2010 - Likes it
This tiny place produces very good, although tame, Thai food. The green curry is not as hot as you can find at various Thai restaurants in Oklahoma City, but it's still tasty, regardless of your protein choice (shrimp, beef, pork, chicken, seafood, or tofu). The Panang curry does not seem to include peanuts in its curry paste, as is typical. Nonetheless, the Panang curry has that characteristic creaminess and slight sweetness of the usual brown curry. The Tom Kha, a coconut milk-based soup, is good. My wife considers it better than Tana Thai's version, which serves as our exemplar. I believe Tana Thai's heavier use of lime makes for a more flavorful Tom Kha. In either case, Padthai's soup is still very good. Despite what an earlier reviewer wrote, I have not experienced any problem with greasiness. Service is pretty much provided by one person who does everything out front, so it may take a bit to get your order. Seating can accommodate about two dozen diners, so come early or late. One downside here is unusual: The menus are extremely hard to read. The main menu places black letters on dark pictures, hiding words. The take-out menu print is both too small and fuzzy. The specials menu is legible but, of course, limited.

Good but greasy
by Deepak (22 reviews)
October 10, 2009 - Likes it
This is a good place to grab an Americanized version of Thai food, they have all the usual dishes that you can find in any other Thai restaurant across the US. The food tastes good, the decor is nice, the service is adequate when not crowded. The only negative is that the food here tends to be somewhat more greasy (oily?) compared to other places I have eaten in and maybe one of the reasons why the food tends to taste as good as it does. If anyone disagrees with this assessment, order the chicken satay and see how much oil is left over and I dont understand how something that is grilled can have so much oil. The curries are good - especially the Evil Jungle Chicken - but then again, a layer of oil on the surface. Its good food but its artery clogging. Did I also mention Americanized?
