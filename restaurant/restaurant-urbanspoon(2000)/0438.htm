The Best Shrimp Truck in the Pacific
by The Food Carpenter (157 reviews)
November 04, 2009 - Really likes it
If Oahu were a treasure map then Giovanni's would be the big red X. Once you're in the neighborhood, you'll find there is a lot of competition when it comes to selling shrimp, but this is the hands-down best choice if you're going to make the travel. The garlic is the most popular, but if you go with friends I highly recommend sharing as many different types you can handle. So if you find yourself on the island, then head north to this marked-up shrimp truck, because it's worth discovering.

by Ant (222 reviews)
July 07, 2009 - Really likes it
Very tough to find this place if you have never been. If you are visiting, get a map of Oahu, and find the NORTHERN most tip. There are other shrimp trucks out there too so do NOT be fooled. Giovanni's is tagged up full of graffiti. No its not in a dangerous area, its just their trademark.
Their garlic shrimp plate was just absolutely mouth watering. Worth the 60-90 minute drive from Waikiki, or stop by if you are near the Polynesian Cultural center.
Maybe I was really hungry that day or the food was just that damn good, but I had to go back and order seconds. Seemed pricey as I was paying, but my stomach was happy that I did.

Ditto what others have said
by Kristen Spangler (5 reviews)
June 26, 2010 - Doesn't like it
On a solo trip to the North Shore, I'd stopped at Macky's in Hale'iwa for my first plate lunch and loved it. I decided to take my parents to a shrimp truck, hoping for roughly the same thing, as soon as we went on our North Shore drive. Since we were driving north from the Windward Side, we found Giovanni's first and, given that I'd heard so much about it, we stopped there. While it was nice having a pavilion and restrooms, I'd be loath to recommend the place for any other reason. Firstly, dad and I ordered plates whilst my mother ordered a garlic hot dog and mac salad. We were told her hot dog was coming and received all the rest of the food immediately. Dad got the lemon butter and I the garlic shrimp. My shrimp were drowning in the butter sauce--I couldn't de-shell them without squirting butter all over the table and virtually everything else. To boot, the excess spoiled the flavour of what is to be some of the best shrimp in the world. Dad liked the fact that he could control the amount of sauce on his shrimp, but wasn't impressed with the price--$13/plate--when I told him that Macky's had given me the same amount of rice and shrimp, plus a salad and a slice of pineapple for $12. Half an hour later, mum was still waiting for her hot dog. Numerous other patrons around her had order one and got it, but our number hadn't been called since. She had to stand in the queue all over again, was treated a little rudely when she asked if it'd been made yet and was finally given it just as the manager (I presume) decided to start closing down. As if that wasn't bad enough, the restrooms had no towels and one had no soap. I realise the food is the priority, but if you're going to provide facilities, check once in a while that they're stocked.
