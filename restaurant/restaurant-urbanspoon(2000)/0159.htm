by kelsfork (2 reviews)
September 22, 2009
We were seated outside in this so called " see and be seen" atmosphere, which was preferable on a warm spring evening to the dimly lit interior. But service should not play favorites to those inside. I ordered a glass of pinot noir- simply undrinkable. I had my dinner date sip it to make sure I wasn't crazy because I am not one to send back wine. Usually I send it down the hatch with haste and order something else the next go round. Not possible with this glass. I asked for the wine menu back and after 10 additional minutes with no service took the wine to the bar myself to ask for something else. That produced the desired attention, but come on! Bring me the wine menu asap if I let you know I can't drink the wine you served! Then dinner arrived. My dates red curry was saved only because anything with coconut milk cannot be bad. The spice level, however, was weak and thai should not be bland. Great that Charleston has a hopping thai restaurant in downtown, but I will not be seen here diluting my memory of good food.

Overrated
by John (2 reviews)
May 18, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I've been to many Thai restaurants over the course of my life and I have to say this is probably the single most overrated restaurant in Charleston:
Food- Standard run of the mill Thai cuisine with no deviations what-so-ever. The majority of the items on the menu are quick to prepare Thai staples, it lacks any of the exotic offerings that require more attention for preparation. Completely by the book which would be fine were it not for the overinflated prices. With so little prep work and presentation work on these dishes, you should be paying 1/2 what you pay here. 3/10
Presentation- It looks edible but it lacks the flair often found in even the smalled Thai dives. 3/10
Service- Takes a while to be seated as it's overinflated rating has made it a very popular restaurant to dine at. This would be fine but they do not accept reservations so you are relegated to waiting in line no matter what. The wait staff, however, is very accommodating and attentive. 6/10
Atmosphere- Lacking in charm and personality, it's dreadfully dark but not in a romantic way. It's made all the worse by the loud drunks that are regular customers here. If you dine here in the evening, you have to shout to be heard, I spent the entire night cusping my hand to my ears to hear my dining partner speak. 1/10
Overall- 3/10
Summary: I wouldn't eat here again to save my life, there are at least 3 better Thai offerings in town(West Ashley, James Island and North Charleston) and by better, I mean waaaay better. This is the worst Thai eatery I've eaten at in my entire life, I would highly recommend anyone interested in well prepared thai food in a less rowdy enviroment skip this place.

Downtown Thai
by Andrew (9 reviews)
March 24, 2010 - Likes it
After hearing all the hype, I've been waiting to try this place for a long time....
1.) Setup and Service - 3.5 out of 5
We went on a Friday evening, around 6ish, well before the big crowd. Parking is tight, usually nothing out in front so expect to walk 3-4 blocks from where you're lucky enough to squeeze in. Beat the dinner crowd so we didn't have to wait, but as we were leaving (around 730) people standing around by the entrance began multiplying. Relatively small setup too and they do NOT take reservations, so if you don't want a wait go during the week or eat early. Service overall was great, they had plenty of staff and all were more than helpful (but not too helpful) to ensure we had what we needed.
2.) Food - 3.5 out of 5
The menu is relatively simple, but once you factor in that you can add chicken, beef, or shrimp or any combo it's got plenty of choices. She got the green curry chicken, I got the Pad Thai, and we had the chicken coconut soup for an app. Soup flavoring was great, but the other two were just average...nothing really out of sight. I almost got the Crispy Red Curry Duck, and looking back wish I had. Overall was pretty good, but honestly I've had Thai food just as good for not as much. Not much seating either, so unless you get there early (they don't take reservations) expect a good wait on a weekend.
3.) Overall - 3.5 out of 5
I have to say, not as impressive as I've heard. The food was good, but not great...the setup was okay, but not perfect...and the pricing was not too steep, but just enough to go "we should have ordered water." Still a good choice if you're downtown and in the mood for Thai but don't go too far out of your way.

One newspaper review describes this as a place to be seen...I wish I knew that before I thought this was a place to eat!
I was worried as soon as I walked in and did not notice an Asian person. But you know a true chef can put a tasty original dish on the table and the menu. 1st I had the mojito. It was horrible. Too much mint, no sugar. I asked the waitress for another drink or bring me some sugar. She said "management was funny about that kind of thing." and brought me sugar. More on that later. The shrimp app was god, but no better than what I could get down the street from my home...cheaper. It was much less greasy, though. The entree was fine. But it wasn't Thai or original. I've lived and dined in many places but this dish wasn't Thai. It was almost the exact recipe for sweet and sour chicken at a West Coast chain called the Mustard Seed. They bill themselves as Asian cuisine.
So back to that $8.99 mojito. Since it wasn't replaced, I didn't leave a tip. I think a $9 tip is great on a $31 check. Perhaps if management sees people not tipping because of substandard, overpriced drinks they will rethink their policy. I encourage you to follow my example or criticize it.
To summarize, it was a decent meal. Upscale atmosphere, adequate service and overpriced food do make a great restaurant. I make more authentic Thai at home and will stay there for Thai in the future.
