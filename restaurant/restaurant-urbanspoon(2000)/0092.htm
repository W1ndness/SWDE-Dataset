Tater Tots!!
by Jessica Lemmo (85 reviews)
October 19, 2008 - Likes it
I was impressed but how much the menu had to offer!  I had a hard time making up my mind what I wanted.  I got stuck between the mussels, a few different sandwiches or pasta dishes.  My friend recommends a certain sandwich so I followed her lead.
We both ordered The Thankful Turkey Sandwich ($7.95) - stuffing, roasted turkey and cranberry on a roll.  It is served with lettuce, tomato and chips.  However, we paid an extra $2 for tater tots with 3 chesses ($1.50 with no cheeses).  The size of the sandwich was very generous and I wasn’t sure how I was going to get my mouth around it.  The flavors were good but it had a down fall- It was dry.  Think of Thanksgiving Dinner with no gravy- it needed the gravy or something to help with the dryness!  They need to work on that one- Flavor is WONDERFUL but it’s dry.
Based upon the other people she knew there a lot of the other food was good (Shrimp Bisque, Sheppard’s pie, etc).  Now I was to full but I heard the Krispy Cream Bread Pudding is to die for!  I wish I had known about it before I finished off my sandwich so I would have saved room!
The service was average- we did have to chase down our check however.  If I lived in this area it would be a nice local place I would find myself at a lot for the atmosphere and food.  So if you down in the Historic Fells Point area and want to try something that not on the main drag head over here!

+ 1 more photos from Jason Black Great food, OK service
by Jason Black (25 reviews)
May 29, 2010 - Likes it
Overall a good experience. We had the crab tots and whatever they called pigs in blankets, and both were very tasty. Caesar salads seem to come with all entrees - a bit above average, but nothing special.
For entree, I had the rosemary tenderloin, which was cooked perfectly, not too dry, and was tasty, but didn't taste much of the rosemary, though a sprig was served on top. It was served with garlic mashed potatoes and snap peas. The potatoes were tasty, but not that garlicky - some might appreciate that, but I love garlic. The peas looked overcooked, but still had some snap. I also ordered a side of mac and cheese, which was delicious, with a nice crispy parmesan crust.
For dessert, we had the bread pudding a la mode - DELICIOUS! But it would be hard for it not to be, since in place of bread, they use Krispy Kreme donuts. Seriously the best bread pudding I've ever had. The pudding itself was warm to the last bite, and had a nice flavor of cinnamon.
The service was decent, but a little slow. Also, our server made two minor mistakes in what we ordered.
Overall, I'd recommend this place. Eight thumbs up, on Homer Simpson's scale of seven to nine.

Krisy Kreme Bread Pudding, to DIE
by SimplyOlivia (7 reviews)
August 25, 2008 - Likes it
Hon and I have had a few tasty dinners here, but the star of this bar is the dessert. More on that in a minute.
Food-wise, they serve good, basic pub food ... tasty burgers, a good flatiron steak sandwich, and a nightly blue plate special that is a pretty decent value (under $7 for a full comfort food dinner). They seem to push their "tots" quite a bit, so I gave in and got an order once. It was a big let-down. The tots I got were just the run-of-the-mill tater-tots that mom used to stick in the oven on busy nights. I was expecting something a little more interesting, more flavorful. I kept trying them to make sure I wasn't missing something, but half way through I realized I didn't like them as a kid and I don't like them now. They were just boring little clumps of potato, and if a restaurant is going to promote their "nice tots" they way Ale Mary's does, they need to do something to support them (pardon the pun).
Tots aside, the service is always friendly, helpful and attentive. They have a huge selection of beers and if you're not sure what you want to drink, the wait staff can make some reliable recommendations.
Though the food and drink have mostly been enjoyable, the most memorable part of my experience with Ale Mary's (and their claim to fame) is the Krispy Kreme bread pudding. It is an experience to be savored and honored. No exaggeration! Hon and I ordered this extraordinary dessert on our first visit and decided to share it. When the dish came, topped with vanilla ice cream, we slowly ate it in silence, as if we were in awe of the richness and flavor. In fact we were. Other than the first time I had creme brulee, I have never had a dessert experience like that before. I've gone back to Ale Mary's a few times since and haven't order the KKBP because I want to save it for special occasions. It's that good!
