mobile review
by Hanah Bae (1 review)
December 24, 2009
I guess we came at a bad time, but we had to wait an hour to grab a table for three (without reservations) the waitress barely spent more than eight minutes with us altogether, the pasta was overcooked, and crabcakes too oily, the soup too oily. This is the type of restaurant where you have to choose your foods wisely. The alad is definitely bit for health-nuts because they are loaded with sauce and cheese. I do recommend the chicken flatbread. The portions are huge, so you're not wasting your money. Do choose foods wisely, though. They have a nice course menu. Do make reservations. The interior decor is a bit two-sided. On one side, it has a nice vintage feel with the black and white photos and the brown color scheme. On the other hand, the lights are modern, and the table lcloth is elred and white checkered. Maybe if they added cream-colored. Curtains across the room it would feel good.
