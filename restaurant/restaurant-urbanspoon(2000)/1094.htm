Chicago Fire
by Doug (505 reviews)
March 24, 2010 - Likes it
Chicago fire, located in the small city (big city if you're from the area) of Folsom (yes, that Folsom) almost seems like a place you might find in a big, chic city.
With hour long waits, and a tiny bar and not much room to wait, perhaps you can enjoy the rest of the town outdoors, while growing even more hungry. Being a New Yorker I may be a bit more impatient but, at least the bartender kept me occupied while waiting.
The thin crust pizza, while hot and flavorful, crispy and great... could be had at any number of spots where I'm from... maybe even the freezer section of a grocery store... okay, maybe not quite, but I certainly wouldn't rave about it. It was just okay... at these wait times I wouldn't rush back. This isn't to say I'd never return, it was good... not great, and not the same as New York... or even Chicago for that matter. What it is, close to being is an UNO Chicago Grill chain, with fewer options.
The stuffed Pizza isn't any better; it's worse.
The service was slow and not the most attentive either.
I have a positive rating although my outlook kind of negative for Chicago Fire.
