+ 1 more photos from Barney Mixed Feelings... worth another try!
by Barney (485 reviews)
May 24, 2009 - Likes it
Stopped in for breakfast on a cloudy Sunday morning and was early enough to beat the crowd.
I really like the breakfast menu, lots of variety and a nice mix of old favorites and surprises. I went with the peanut butter French toast (Challah bread filled with peanut butter, dipped in batter, encrusted with bran flakes and topped with caramelized bananas) that was truly a PILE of food (see the photo). Unfortunately, I thought it fell a little short. It appeared that they were trying too hard to make this humongous. The result was a nice French toast crust on the outside but a cold and uncooked interior. I think it would have worked much better if it was a little less "thick" and could actually warm/cook all the way through... something about a ton of bread and cold peanut butter just missed the mark I was looking for from the fantastic description. By the way, in addition to down-sizing the portion, I think it'd be better to add a few more caramelized banana slices because the reality as served was bananas as garnish... not as a real part of the flavor combination as described.
Finally, with a half full house, I thought the service was tepid at best. A shining example of this is that it appeared to me that only my server was allowed to refill my coffee... couldn't get a refill from the server handling the next table over. What's up with that?
With all that said, I will go back. I saw some fantastic dishes at tables around me (such as the cowboy Benedict) and the breakfast sandwiches looked like a very good option for a smaller breakfast. Good weather and a seat outside would have made up for a lot of the otherwise disappointing aspects....

+ 1 more photos from Chris A breakfast place worth checking out
by Chris (76 reviews)
January 23, 2010 - Likes it
Having lived in Atlanta, I've done a rather poor job on hitting the 'hot AM spots.' I live walking distance to West Egg, never been. I hadnt been to Highland Bakery. Have been to Flying Biscuit and would rather have Waffle House (but then again, I love WH! :)
I had never been to Highland Bakery until the wait at (I think it was called) Anns Blue Bird was an UNKNOWN length, and due to fire code, we had to wait outside in the freezing cold. While waiting, I opted for Highland Bakery.
The wait was none when we got there on a Saturday morning. I was surprised by this as it was 9am. So, we sat down and our service was pretty good. They did a great job with my halfcafhalfdecaf coffee and keeping my water glass full.
The food took a little bit of time to get out, but it was almost a full house so maybe thats why. You also must know, Highland Bakery isnt a quick stop shop for breakfast (unless you grab a pastry or something), its a social house (oh, thats another I've never been to, Social House off Howell Mill).
I had the Burrito de Huevos and it delivered pretty well. We also had an order of the Sweet Potato pancakes for the table. The pancakes I did not really care for.. I had some incredible ones in Beverly Hills that maybe set the bar too high for the competition.
All in all, its a quaint location that is def worth grabbing breakfast with your gf after a good ... ahem ... nights sleep. The coffee is drinkable, the food is passable (and lots looks great), and the pastries look incredible (I have only had the 'small' cinnamon roll, which is pretty darn big, and good!)
Def check out Highland Bakery -- tell them Chris from Urbanspoon sent you. Theyll have no idea who I am or what youre talking about, but maybe with 'urbanspoon' in there and the fact you just namedropped will get you something cool! :)

cupcakes great - resturant horrible experience
by jessica (1 review)
October 19, 2009 - Doesn't like it
We went to the Highland Bakery this weekend and had a miserable experience. The wait was ridiculously long (2hrs) and the food didn't live up to the expectation. I would only go again and hit the bakery, their peanut butter-chocolate cupcake was worth the $3.95
the BAD
1st - We waited for almost 2hrs for a table. One would think that host staff might have knowledge of basic math - 2+2 = 4, so if you push together two 2 people tables you magically have room to seat 4 -- instead this staff sat literally 20 two-top tables before our group of 4. We weren't the only "large" group with this problem. There were several frustrated customers watching couple there less than 10min get seated while we wait a ridiculous amount of time. (oh yes, and each time we asked for an update on how much longer we would wait we were told 20min.) It was almost comical.
2nd - the food (not including the pastries) wasn't that great. We were hopeful that our 2hr wait would have been worth the anticipation. Sadly it was not. The shrimp and grits was horribly over seasoned - a combination of salt and jalapeno (or some other hot seasoning). I love spicy food but this was a little much. The poached eggs were over cooked to the point where the yolks were a solid mass of completely cooked egg. And the tofu scramble included some mystery liquid all over the plate.
the GOOD
1st - our waitress was very nice. Even suggesting other restaurants to our vegan/no gluten friend.
2nd - the cupcakes were wonderful. I wish we would have just grabbed a cup of coffee and a dozen of their mini cupcakes and make it a picnic.
