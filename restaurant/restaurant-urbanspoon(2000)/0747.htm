Know your Yin from Yang
by there is no spoon (84 reviews)
August 03, 2008 - Really likes it
If you ever wanted to dine in the presence of an 18' high stone Buddha floating atop a stoned pool of illuminated carp, then Tao is for you. Your breath will be taken away by the austere solemnity of this friendly faux temple appearance with airy 3-story brick open walls and modern chrome and glass balcony, giant asian calligraphs on the ceiling, and many other blends of luxury and zen privation. The varied menu offers much to explore, this our first visit we took the very pleasant waiter's suggestions and did right by him. The lobster pot was a fun melee of seafood and flavor, albeit a little trickier to eat so I'd not recommend on a first date if you're a neat freak. Sake menu was fun too, opted for one of the flights for tasting 3 healthy shots consumed one each course. The salad was ok, nice dressing but not enough on top it was on the bottom, lobster dumplings good, stir fried greens too sharp for my liking but ate them as best I good for my wont of verdancy. Desserts were a lot of fun, and it would seem this would be a good enough course to visit on its own. A requisite visit to the restroom would challenge you to remember yin is female, yang male, which I confess would be helpful if they had more clues than bumping into women at the wrong door.

Amazing! 5 out of 5 Stars!
by Alessandro Acquista (6 reviews)
January 17, 2009 - Likes it
Well, last night me and two other friends decided to go to this restaurant Tao (located on 58th street between Park. Ave and Madison Ave.). They serve Modern-Asian cuisine which is traditional Asian food, just updated and with a twist. The restaurant itself could be confused for a night club. The bar is always packed and good luck getting in without a reservation because even with one you need to wait a minimum of 15 minutes to be seated. In the restaurant area, you can sit in the lower area over looking the massive Buddha statue located at the front of the restaurant, or the upper area which is usually for smaller groups of two or three. Besides the decore and club atmosphere being out of this world, the food is actually amazing. Everything has so much flavor, and the sushi is like butter and tastes really fresh! Next time you're in Manhattan, be sure to check out Tao!

Pricy and absurdly highfalutin
by Barbara (9 reviews)
March 16, 2009 - Doesn't like it
Maybe I'm an Asian cuisine snob, but I'm Asian and that can't be helped. To use strong language, this is an abomination on Asian cuisine. The ambiance is nice, but it's horrendously ostentatious and tries way too hard. It's an Anglicized and consumerist facsimile of what Asia is. "Let's place a thirty-foot Buddha in the restaurant. Oh, and a koi pond seems appropriate - that's Asian, right?" Good God, my yellow skin is crawling. The food isn't bad, but the price you pay for it is not worth what you get (e.g. petite portions of chicken satay with peanut sauce served in square dishes). What you're really paying for is the "experience," which is close to the definition of yuppie Manhattanite. Do I sound bitter? It's because I am.
