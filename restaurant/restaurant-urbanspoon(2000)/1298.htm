A DC tradition
by Greggndc (75 reviews)
January 22, 2010 - Likes it
You have to love Ben's Chili Bowl. It is the law in DC! It is one of the last authentic spots left in DC where you can get good- if greasy - food at good prices and the location on U street makes it a primo spot for the hipsters and posers that frequent the clubs in that area. I actually dislike chili, but love Ben's version. It is neither to spicy nor too bland (and NO beans!!). It is perfect on top of a dog or burger (get the half smoke) and while I wouldn't eat it, I saw several orders of chili cheese fries coming across the counter. I prefer the fries plain but they are barely salted. A little Old Bay's would make them incredible. Two words to the hipster crowd: Be polite. On my last visit a young woman kept complaining loudly to no one in particular…"How long does it take to get an order of cheese fries" I have seen this behavior there in the evenings before and it irks those of us who have been going to Ben's for years. The girl was snarky and sullen to the waitress and didn't leave a tip. It was sort of crowded and the fries were being cooked. Whining doesn't make them cook faster - fortunately the cute lad and his girlfriend with her made up for rudeness by being overly polite to the waitress (and left a tip!). Good form!

Smiple, cheap, and good
by Michael (90 reviews)
May 10, 2010 - Likes it
A chili dog isn't something I could or should eat every day. But every now and again, it just sounds amazing. And at Ben's, they make a pretty damn good one. I like the sausages they use, they're bigger than a hot dog and are spicier. Also a nice touch is that delicate, slightly crispy bit of burned casing from the griddle. As for the chili? It actually packs a kick and I was surprised. I found myself really wanting a cold beer to wash it down, but sadly, they didn't have any. I guess that's what most people do before they get to Ben's!
If I could make one change it would maybe be for a sturdier bun that had been toasted, so as to stop it from getting soggy so soon. But I'm nitpicking, if I stopped in after a night of drinking, I doubt I would care. Ben's is just a simple, good thing. I wish there were more of them.

A standard met and my pride smashed!
by Mark Martens (2 reviews)
November 07, 2009 - Likes it
There is a certain quality that an exceptional chili dog has...I found it years ago at a roadside stand in Hollister, CA and have been looking for it ever since. I finally found it again at Ben's Chili bowl. Their Half smoke with chili is a treat that had me craving more at my next meal.
I make a nice chili...and have taken great pride in it. When i tried their bowl of chili (different stuff than on the dogs), I was again impressed! There is a nice heat (not too much for mild tastes either) and layers of flavor that make it addictive! Mine is a pale shadow of this excellence.
Lastly there is the vibe! Take the classic elements of the quintessential diner and stir in a hearty helping of urban hip and you have Ben's...the yelling of info, the grooving behind the counter (by the whole staff at points), the presence of their matriarch Virginia Ali. She stopped by with her purse on her shoulder and decided to stay a bit and the whole restaurant felt her presence, from wiping tables to chatting up guests, to discussing food quality with her team. What a treat to see her in action.
This is a place known for having celebrity guests, but beyond that it is a must eat for any serious dogger or for that matter anyone who loves good simple food served up with a bit of flair!
