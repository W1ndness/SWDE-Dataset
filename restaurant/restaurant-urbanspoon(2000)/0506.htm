Nice Upscale Mexican
by Frederick (217 reviews)
April 08, 2009 - Likes it
We went here by accident one rainy night because every other restaurant at the Town Center had a 3 hour wait. I immediately liked the vibe and the atmosphere which is quite upscale for Mexican fare. They make a big show of preparing their expensive guacamole at your table but the portions are generous so the price is worth it unless you're counting your pennies. The huge menu had so many choices I ended up ordering the Appetizer mixed platter for the table, and this is a meal in itself - everything was so delicous especially the skewers.
Another good dish for the perennially indecisive is their mixed grill - which has a fantastic array of freshly grilled meats like beef, chicken and quail.
For the traditionalists, I recommend the chile relleno which is probably the best I've ever had and I've eaten some really good ones in Jacksonville!

My favorite Mexican place
by JaxFoodieFreak (35 reviews)
May 11, 2010 - Likes it
Cantina Laredo located in the St. Johns Town Center offers upscale Mexican food while Chicago Pizza boosts great Chicago pizza, hot dogs, and bar food in 3 locations in Jacksonville (I went to the Landing location). I ventured to both places and enjoyed both, but sometimes it is a matter of what you order, especially when you want to dine well on a budget.
At Cantina, I fell in love with their "top shelf guacamole" for $9.49 made fresh in front of you with your choice of red onions, tomatoes, and other mixings; it does serve 2-4 people so bring your appetite! Naturally, I split the guacamole appetizer with my foodie friend, Cathy, who long ago, was my partner in crime on our college graduation trip to Cancun where we ate wonderful Mexican food for an entire week. That memory, especially the quality of authentic Mexican food where we avoided tourist traps, still stays with us. I thoroughly enjoyed my chicken flautas ($7.99) that contained chicken, monterey jack cheese and peppers served over a bed or rice and a small salad and some creamy sauce. Cathy ordered and enjoyed her enchilada veracruz stuffed with chicken, spinach and monterey jack cheese topped with tomatillo sauce, vinaigrette marinated vegetables, and cotija cheese ($9.29). Of all Mexican joints, this one is my favorite, but honestly, there really is not much competition here in Jax when it comes to Mexican food.

Home Run
by SteveS (15 reviews)
March 03, 2010 - Likes it
Today, I finally tried Cantina Laredo; they boast being upscale authentic Mexican cuisine. Well, I'm not so sure about how "authentic" it is; I never saw a field greens salad with cranberrys in Mexico! But nevertheless, aside from this detail, I found the food quite good. Yes, the motif is chic modern with lots of clean straight lines everywhere. Definitely upscale decor and furniture. We were presented with an alternative "side" menu titled TRES, which is esentially a 3-course meal, where you pick 1 appetizer from a choice of 3, 1 entree from a choice of 3, and of course 1 dessert from a choice of, well only 2! ha. All for only $15! More on this later. First, we were greeted by our server who brought us the prerequisite tortilla chips and 2 kinds of salsa. The red one was nice, better of the 2, and had a nice hint of lime. I also noticed some cilantro in there as well. I hate runny salsa, but this one had a nice consistency with lots of chuncks. It actually stayed on the chip and didn't drip right off. THumbs up. The brownish colored salsa was just okay for my palette--I can't formulate into words the taste of that one. I don't know what the ingredients were. My coworkers highly recommended the " fresh" guacamole. Yes, as expected the server gave a presentation of making it on a huge tray table-side. I'm not much of a guacamole eater, but this one was really delicious--nuggets of soft fresh avocado & diced vine-ripe tomatoes. He added some chili, which gave it a nice slightly spicy after taste. I'm not sure that $9 for a bowl of any kind of dip is worth it. You can get order a large pizza for that price right? I know, it's also about the experience and the presentation. To me, even though it's excellent, I would consider the guacamole to be an ocassional treat. Back to the menu...I ordered the Spring Field Greens Salad with cranberry dressing. As you would expect it was just that--very light and refreshing. They topped it with fetta cheese crumbs and dried cranberries. For the entree, I hit a home run with the grilled swordfish fillet. It was cooked to perfection--light and flakey. Since it was simply seasoned with just a little lemon and black peppercorn, the natural flavor of the fish was allowed to come through, which is slightly creamy. This is exactly how I like my fish cooked. It was complimented nicely with a cup of the standard Mexican yellow rice and a serving of steamed string beans, onions, and carrot disks. Again these were not over seasoned and mild in taste. They were still firm and not overcooked, so you get the full benefit of all the available vitamins & minerals. To top it off, the entire plate was sprinkled with cilantro flakes for added flavor and an appetizing appearance. For dessert, I chose the Crepe. The presentation of the crepe was in a ping pong paddle shaped plate! Yes, the porcelin curled up and formed a handle on one side. The crepe was accompanied by a scoop of vanilla ice cream and then carmel was drizzled all over. The creme was warm and stuffed with sliced green apples. I found it pleasant and not too rich. Not the best I've had, but not the worst. It was good. Overall, for $15, I was satisfied with my meal and consider it a good value. Remember, you're in an upscale restaurant in the St. Johns Town Center. I want to come back and try the more "traditional" Mexican items on the menu. On a 5 star scale, I give it a 4 overall, considering food, taste, value, portions, service, atmosphere, cleanliness. For a few more bucks, skip Chili's and eat at Cantina Laredo. Cheers!
