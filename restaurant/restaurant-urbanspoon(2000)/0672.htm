Worth a second look
by Jimmy (19 reviews)
July 20, 2010 - Likes it
Went by Comet Cafe on a Sunday morning during the brunching hour. Place was packed but our party of 6 was actually seated quickly.
I started out my meal with a cafe au lait. It was good and the serving size was quite large. It came in a pint glass, which was interesting.
For my meal, I ordered the biscuits and gravy and upgraded with a side of hashbrowns. The gravy was delicious...subtle hints of sausage in every bite and an ideal consistency - not runny but also not too thick or viscous. The biscuit was a bit acrid (too much baking soda!!!!) but it was redeemed by the simple fact that I could tell that the biscuits were made in house. The eggs were perfectly cooked over hard and tasted great...gotta love local, cage free eggs! The hashbrowns were perfectly browned although others that I ordered with seemed to get servings that were a little too browned or could have been cooked a little longer.
All in all a satisfying meal. Next time I'm in the area, I definitely plan on returning.

Good for vegans who want comfort food!
by Bekka (1 review)
May 21, 2010 - Likes it
As a vegan, I just had to try Comet Cafe as I heard so many rave reviews about their vegan "comfort food" options. And let me specify, if you're a vegan that is looking to eat HEALTHY...Comet really isn't the place for that! But it is worth the indulgence, trust me!
The first time I visited Comet Cafe, I had the vegan salisbury steak which is still my favorite dish there. Served up with a side of vegan mashed potatoes and mushroom gravy, it hits all the right spots on my taste buds!
I have actually tried a number of vegan dishes and desserts from Comet, and I have never been disappointed.
The meatloaf made vegan is comparable to the salisbury steak... it's delicious and very hearty!
The chili is to die for. I mean really. It's some of the best chili I've ever had, and even my carnivore Father liked it!
The seasoned fries are ALWAYS the way to go when you get fries with your dish...trust me! They are phenomenal.
The vegan gyro is good... it's my boyfriend's favorite!
The marinated tofu sandwich is HUGE... but is probably my least favorite vegan option on the menu. Don't get me wrong it is still VERY good and I would still recommend it, but out of everything I have eaten there it is at the bottom on my list.
Last time I visited I had the leghorn sandwich made vegan, and I instantly fell in love with it. Such an interesting combination of flavors, and again, a huuuge portion sure to fill up even the hungriest of fools!
But the REALLY awesome part about Comet Cafe is its vegan desserts. I get something for dessert everytime I go there and it has never ceased to cause me to melt right in my booth! I've had red velvet cupcakes, chocolate cupcakes, blueberry pies, you name it and they all have been absolutely heavenly. People that I know that are not vegan, don't believe me when I share take home desserts with them and tell them that it's vegan!
I should also mention that I once had a pumpkin curry soup here as well and it was AWESOME!!!
The atmosphere of Comet is cute... it makes me feel like I'm eating at a diner in NYC. However, Comet gets REALLY busy and they don't have much space for you to sit/stand while you wait for a table when it gets packed.
Sometimes the food takes a bit to serve up, but it is worth the wait!
I will continue to visit Comet Cafe again and again!
