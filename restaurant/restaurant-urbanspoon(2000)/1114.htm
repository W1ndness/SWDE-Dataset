Great Beer Selection!
by Newt (112 reviews)
October 07, 2009 - Likes it
In a town not known for having a great selection of alcoholic beverages - this place does. And the food was delicious. It's Austrian, which I have to admit I'm not an expert in, so we started slow with the cheese and meat plate. Everything was amazing - the pickles that they bring out were so good we had the waitress write down the name of them. I continued with the Goulash, which I loved and many people tried the Chicken Roladen, which looked fantastic. I would absolutely recommend this place - and also suggest trying the Polygamy Porter (a dark ale) just because you're in Salt Lake City.

Much love for Beerhive
by ChristianZ (3 reviews)
July 01, 2010 - Likes it
After a grueling conference day with my mean boss (Mary) heh heh, she redeemed herself by taking me to the Beerhive. Oh, yea, I love the name in the Mormon-laws Beehive state, but was most impressed with the menu. Two big pages of bottled beers, everything you could ever hope for. At least 200 different brews of loveliness. I sampled the Deadhorse Ale and the Rooster seasonal. Favorite thing: the ice rail running around the bar where you can put your glass down so your brew stays as cool as the moment it was drawn from the tap or poured from the bottle. Bonus: historic photos of the Beerhive's block adorn the walls.

Terrific addition to downtown
by Food Good (13 reviews)
October 02, 2009 - Likes it
This place has a really good feel to it with a high ceiling and long, narrow main seating area. The beer selection is one of the best in the city and, unlike some other beer bars, they had every beer we ordered. There is a lager-drinker's dream along the bar: a frosted strip of metal to keep your light beer cold. The bartenders were knowledgeable and attentive. I think they outsource their food from an adjacent establishment but we were quite happy with the pork sandwich and potato salad. This is my favorite new place in SLC in a long time.

a little "too" popular?
by mg (6 reviews)
March 01, 2010 - Likes it
Love this place, love the beer selection (try the LDS stout on nitro tap, amazing), haven't tried the food but I love Vienna Bistro so I'm sure I wouldn't change my mind on that.
The only thing that keeps me away is the crowd on bar nights. The people are great, but the building is very long and narrow with the bar stretching from front to back with relatively little room between it and the booths that line the opposite wall, so it gets very packed very easily. Navigating through the sea of people is next to impossible, and pretty dangerous when you're holding a beer. Finding a seat at the bar or a table is just as difficult if you show up any time after 10:30, especially when they close the patio in the winter. I personally think the owners should repurpose the basement and open it up to seating and/or a second bar so everybody wouldn't crowd the front half of the building. I imagine visiting this place for dinner can be a great experience, but the layout doesn't work well with the amount of people who show up on weekend nights.
