Nandos! Amazing!
by Simon (1 review)
March 15, 2009
For someone who does not want to spend a lot of money while dining out, Nando's Peri Peri is the place to go. Succulent chicken breasts, marinated beautifully with the Afro-Portuguese Peri Peri sauces that give Nando's its distinct and exceptional flavor, while accompanied with side orders such as french fries, homestyle mashed potatoes, cole slaw, corn on the cob, and more, makes a meal at Nando's a truly wonderful dining experience.
There is not a restaurant in D.C. that can imitate its flavors and sauces. My girlfriend also enjoys the fresh Greek salads and veggie burgers that make for a very filling meal. Don't forget to try their unique and tasty spicy rice or their olive dish appetizer. I've also tried the steak sandwich, and have not been disappointed at all. I've been to Nando's almost 10 times now within the past year, and I always come back!!! Can't wait for more Nando's to open up!

Great food, great atmosphere, lackluster service
by Herb Caudill (1 review)
August 16, 2008
The way the restaurant works is kind of unusual: You are greeted & seated by a hostess, but then you go up to the cash register to place your order. If you buy a bottle of wine they hand it to you along with glasses; then you sit down and they bring you the food. They should be clearer about how it works - for instance we didn't realize we had to get our own napkins and silverware. The service was a little disappointing - not particularly prompt or friendly. They really need to straighten that aspect of the place out.
I loved everything else about Nando's. The décor is really very cool, kind of a chic colonial-portuguese-African thing, with very cool art on the wall. There's a swanky hand-washing trough right out in the middle of the restaurant, which I thought was a nice touch.
And the food is really, really delicious - all of the taste and character of third-world street food in a pleasant setting.
