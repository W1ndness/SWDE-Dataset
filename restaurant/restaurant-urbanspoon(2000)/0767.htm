Good But Not Great
by Peanutgallery (60 reviews)
December 29, 2009 - Doesn't like it
We ate at this restaurant after the local review with the new Pleasurehouse opening. You'll find this location as part of a mini-strip mall but remains easily accessible from Pleasurehouse Rd. As a start up operation, you may find the interior decor lacking with a mix of diner tables and chairs mixed with pub height dining tables. On this very cold night, the fireplace looked welcoming except that is was non-operational. As for the anticipated experience with the food, I had ordered the Crab Soup and Scampi Francese. My wife had the Prosciutto & Fresh Mozzarella and Fettuccine Con Pollo Alfredo. I wish I could tell you about the Crab Soup... but it never came; the Server forgot to place the order. The Scampi Francese was well prepared with a nice light lemon sauce and large tempura battered shrimp. Had Penne on the side which was absolutely wonderful in a beautiful light oil. Very flavorful. As for the other meal, the Prosciutto & Fresh Mozzarella was brilliant. Absolutely fresh and well portioned. Our hesitation came with the Fettuccine Con Pollo Alfredo. Very heavy, even in presentation to the point where the sauce seemed dry. The mix of tomatoes, chicken and sausage should have worked well but there was zero flavor to any of the items... they all seemed the same to the palate. In a tight economy, there was not enough in the experience for us to return or recommend to others.

Best Italian I've had since Italy
by Robin (1 review)
April 02, 2010
I never like going to Italian restaurants because it always makes me pine for the summer I spent in Italy. It may sound presumptuous, but the store bought mozzarella, heavy sauces, overcooked noodles, and lack of fresh basil just makes me grumpy. We decided to check out Mannino's due to the good reviews we read online during restaurant week.
We were confused by the address; it's just in a little strip mall (both locations.) But when we went inside the Princess Anne location, we noticed a cozy dining room and long bar with muted colored walls and bright works of art. The original works of art lend a nice touch and the long "wine rack" looks industrial and almost homemade as well.
We had a great server that gave us all the information we could possibly need about the menu items. I was a little bit put off when I asked for some of his favorite and most popular dishes; he replied with "They are all fantastic so I can't pick any standouts. They are all phenomenal!" I was hoping he would give us more information than that and thankfully, he did! He suggested the Pollo Giovanni and the Cheese Tortellini, which we both ordered. We also had the prosciutto and homemade mozzarella as an appetizer.
The appetizer came out quickly, before the bread, and we absolutely devoured it! The mozzarella is so good. SO GOOD. I cannot explain how good this mozzarella is. Go get it. Right now. Fresh basil and prosciutto are nice accompaniments, but I could eat that cheese all day! The bread is light, fluffy AND crusty. It's really wonderful bread! However, they don't leave the oil and balsamic on the table so we ran out before our last couple bites.
When our entrees arrived, we were overwhelmed/pleased with the portions! My husband's pasta was beautifully made; a good balance of pasta, bacon, and peas and the sauce was impossibly thick and delicious! I don't know how the chefs create a sauce that rich. It's insane.
My chicken dish stole the show. It was very tender and flavorful and the sauce/veggies with it were amazing. The artichokes were the best I've ever had! Like little balls of flavor!! The dish has a lot of red peppers, but they are roasted just right (just the perfect amount of char and tenderness.) Every bite gave me a new taste and sensation! Amazing!
We ended up taking half our meals home with us!
This place is not to be missed!

It's the BEST!
by terrement (3 reviews)
January 25, 2010 - Likes it
We were introduced to Mannino's by some friends - we'd lived in VB for many years, and I don't think I've ever seen an ad or heard of this place - well hidden so tourists won't find it.
The reason for no ads became immediately apparent. Word of mouth brings them all the business they can handle. It is the best restaurant Italian food my wife and I have found. Highly recommend the Calimari starter, Veal or Chicken Parmigian, and the Manicotti as main dishes, and any of their wonderful desserts.
The place gets full at dinner times, and although not loud, it is not a place for quiet conversation - much like I'd expect a neighborhood restaurant to be in NYC, Philly, Chicago or other big city with an Italian district.
The service has always been friendly, attentive without being over-bearing, and we have enjoyed every visit. We chose it as the spot for a reunion get-together with 5 couples, introducing them all to it for the first time. Everyone was pleased and surprised, and they too are now regulars.
The newer facility on Pleasure House Road has the same menu, and the same quality service, but as noted elsewhere, the ambiance is a little different - which i ascribe to it just having opened. I fully expect it will be just as nice in the future as is the original.
Bottom line - for Italian food in the Tidewater area this is most definately a place to try.
