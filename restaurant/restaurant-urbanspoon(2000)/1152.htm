San Diego Stone Brewing Company~Crafting all of the elements
by HerbalGourmet (7 reviews)
April 27, 2010
Following the effervescent aroma of Beer from one Brewery led me on an extended journey to try another one Saturday evening. The Stone Brewing Company, located in Escondido is the largest Craft Brewery in the region. Part of their patented philosophy is to use in-season, locally and organically grown produce... best served with their seven year-round Beers. What attracted me originally to the otherwise goth-skateboard inspired entry was the event calendar which promised a mix of Fusion Food and "Big" Beers. Examples include a Beer & Chocolate pairing and the ne peut pas résister a Fresh Dinner-'Field to Plate' event. There was no signage on the front of the Bistro and you enter in to what appears to be a industrial warehouse with a store front. Your attention immediately shifts to the Brewery's signature logos: Gargoyles & Arrogant Bastard Ale. Tours and Beer were brewing as I snaked around the crowd in the nearly 12,000 sq. feet of space. The landscape could easily be interpreted as indoor/outdoor with an industrial appeal. Their philosophy might fit very well with Feng Shui~ elements that neutralize, enhance, or weaken the Chi in a physical environment: wood, metal, stone and water combined to create a compelling environment. Tall bamboo stalks border large boulders surrounded by a serendipitous school of Koi. The view to the left was ominous...mile high shiny stainless steel vessels and to the right a beautifully landscaped eco-friendly Beer garden. I ventured upstairs to the 'Crows Nest' where one gets a birds-eye view of the culinary landscape below. By using fresh, organic seasonal foods, the menus steep prices tip the scale of many Breweries. The menu is broken down with A la Carte choices and I could see patrons sipping Handcrafted Beers while eating the Soft & Stinky Cheese plate $15. For starters we tried the Wild Boar Baby Back Ribs seasoned in a dry rub mix, braised and slathered with Red Imperial Ale Five Chili Agave glaze and grilled to perfection $12. As a side note I ordered the Soup du Jour, a Cheese Garlic cup $5 with a side of French bread $2. For my main entree I had the Spicy Beef & Broccoli Stir-Fry , a veritable cornucopia of Sirloin stir-fried with Broccoli, Onions, and a spicy Hoison sitting beside a bed of Brown Rice $19. After slugging down a few Arrogant Bastards, my partner in crime ordered the Chicken Schnitzel, a breaded and seasoned Jidori Chicken breast pounded flat and pan-fried, served with Garlic mashed Potatoes, Vegetables and a Lemon-Caper cream sauce $23. The dessert options are just as interesting but to satisfy a late night craving we opted for a to-go order of the Wild Mushrooms over Penne. A blend of five Mushrooms stir-fried with Leeks and finished with a cream sauce over Penne Pasta and shaved Parmesan Cheese $18. There is also a childrens menu and all items include juice or milk and two cookies priced for $7. The Brewery's extensive drink menu also includes Wine, Sake and hot and cold beverages. Stone Brewing Company 1999 Citracado Parkway, Escondido, CA. ~ 760-294-7866
