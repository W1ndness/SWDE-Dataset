+ 6 more photos from Andrew D My New Beloved OC Haven!
by Andrew D (249 reviews)
June 22, 2010 - Really likes it
Who knew one day something as cool as Haven would reside in Old Towne Orange? Really??!! Well, at last, Old Towne Orange suddenly has one of the edgiest gastropubs around. And wow, I can't get enough!
The beer list is extensive. I ultimately decided on a Sierra Nevada Pale Ale, as I couldn't handle the heavier stuff with my dinner. I really enjoyed the lighter flavor.
I also started off with some house marinated olives. I wanted to start my real right, with some tart and richness.
And from there, it was onto mac & cheese... WHOA! Black truffles, gruyere, fontina, and parmesan all mixed with some elbow macaroni and topped with toasted buttery bread crumbs. It was so rich, so buttery, so tangy, so creamy, and so delicious! Best mac & cheese ever? Perhaps so.
And for dessert? Oh, nothing less than "The Irish Car Bomb"! It had Guinness chocolate stout cake, Guinness ice cream, Irish Cream chocolate sauce, and Jameson caramel... Basically a whole lot of chocolate brought together with a whole lot of beer! And oddly enough, it all worked out quite well with the burly, slightly bitter beer flavors balancing out the sweeter chocolate and caramel.
And all in all, Haven was quite the pleasant surprise. Who knew a funky gastropub like this could serve funky food that's so delicious? And do it all in Orange County? Consider me amazed.

great experience
by Johnny Automatic (238 reviews)
November 03, 2009 - Likes it
Had a fine evening of drinks and food with friends here. The service was attentive and quick. The beer choices were great and the option of a sampler of 4 different brews was much appreciated. Started with the house made potato chips. They were tasty, fresh and hot. They were not soggy or over cooked at all like one reviewer said. Had the pork belly, hanger steak and mac n cheese for entrees and all were very good. Only thing that was a disappointment was the sudden disappearance of the Irish car bomb dessert. We tried the new candied bacon ice cream served over a brownie. I love bacon, i've had it in chocolate and other dessert treatments, but sadly the treatment here is out of balance. Maybe if the bacon was more finely chopped and cooked til crisp. As it is you get cold pieces of bacon fat in ice cream - not good.
But this was the only sour note in an otherwise very pleasant dining experience.

Great Place
by Gazelles (7 reviews)
January 11, 2010 - Likes it
I've eaten here several times since it opened and I've been impressed every time. When I went there last time I had a Chorizo flatbread (their flatbreads are similar to a small pizza) for my main course and it was very good. I have yet to eat there and have someone I'm with say they didn't like the food- it doesn't seem you can really go wrong, but I have had people say they didn't feel like there were a lot of choices. For me personally, I don't really notice the menu to be limited, but I've heard it said both on this site and in my own experiences for what that's worth.
Haven's also got a formidable beer selection with something for everyone's taste. Whether you're into IPAs, Belgian-style beers or Hefeweizens they've got something on tap that should be up your alley and a more expanded selection of bottled brews as well. They have tasting flights available too if you just can't decide.
Overall ambiance in the restaurant is great, really well presented, and the location near the Orange Circle couldn't be much better. If you're in the area and looking for some good eats and drinks, this is a good place to end up.

mobile review
by Kitty (1 review)
October 11, 2009 - Likes it
We ate here last night, and let me say WOW! The first thing that impressed me was the decor and layout, cozy but not crowded and dim but not dark. Well done. Next was the beer selection, I actually had to order some water while I perused the beer list, and my wife had to do the same for the wines. Copious choices, all of which were excellent. I ended up picking the beer flight, since there were several I wanted to try. I had 3 immediately, but when I seemed stuck fir number 4 our server gave me a suggestion that was tailored to my tastes (she noticed that I had chosen mostly dark beer) and that was excellent! Just the beginning of the great service all night. For an app, we ordered the house made potato chips, which were fantastic. Well seasoned and the garlic and herb aioli was a perfect complement. My entree was the Haven burger while my wife chose the pulled pork sandwich. Both were superb, and came with some of their delicious French fries (sorry pommes frites :), but I'd like to say something about the ketchup that came with them. I. Strongly. Dislike. Ketchup. However, this ketchup was absolutely delicious! A hint of cinnamon and cayenne gave it a wonderfully complex character that was absolutely awesome! The burger was "oh my god" incredible. I've had some good burgers before (including the ridiculous Daniel Boulud foie gras and braised short rib monster....yummy!) and I can safely say that this burger ranks up there. I would easily pay $20 for this burger! I can definitely understand a previous comment about the bun being soggy, but I view it as a good thing! It's so fresh, soft and spongy that it does just that! Soaks up the juice from the burger like a sponge! We also shared the mac and cheese, which is simply amazing, though I think it could have used a touch more pepper in the sauce. For dessert the Irish car bomb, which was one of the most interesting takes on a brownie sundae I've seen and quite delicious. Overall I would give this place a 9.3 out of 10 and will definitely be back.
