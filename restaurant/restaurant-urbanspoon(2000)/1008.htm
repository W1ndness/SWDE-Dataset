mobile review
by johnnjill (100 reviews)
January 15, 2010 - Likes it
Sat at the sushi bar on a very busy Friday night (tonight) -- the atmosphere is lively, young and fun. Large common tables surround the hibatchi BBQ grills, like an old Benihana. The chefs do knife tricks and squirt alcohol fuel onto to the grills to raise a flame and the inevitable shriek and cheer that follows. There is a raucousness to the dining room that extends into the bar. The lighting is dim, accented with blue neon and tall vertical, rice paneled fluorescent bulbs that the highlight the grey stone walls and the archways between the bar and the hibatchi grilling room. The lights combine well for effect as they reflect off the black granite counter tops of the bar and the sushi bar. It's obvious that the liquor flows pretty freely here, but everyone seems to be having fun. The food...in particular, the sushi, is darn good to boot. Best I've had in Providence yet. I enjoyed the Paradise Dragon roll, even though it's a bit of everything--white tuna, eel, avocado and tempura crumbs combined into one heafty roll with three different sauces over the top. The "simpler" Tiger roll with a lobster salad center, instead of crab, was served with a spicy tomato salsa, which imho, it could have done without. I also enjoyed several pieces of nigri including Saba, Uni, White Tuna, Uni and Chu Toro. The plates are presented very well. And I had absolutely no trouble with the service like some of the other reviewers -- it was timely but not overbearing, helpful and polite. Perhaps it took them the first six months to work out the kinks?? At the end of my meal, the manager, David,stopped by to ask me how everything was. I said, "It was super. I like my sushi and I'll be back to sample more very soon."

Asian Grill and Sushi Nouveau at its finest!
by Voracious gourmet (10 reviews)
December 05, 2009 - Likes it
This is absolutely the newest 'atmosphere' restaurant in Greater Providence. Sublime decor greets you after the entrance waterfall!
However, unlike so many places that are all appearance and no substance, Ichigo Ichie delivers the goods. My group came for the Hibachi table shows--complete with flying daggers, and squirts of sake for catching the flying tidbit flung at you. Some great showmanship even for folks that have been to Hibachi joints bunches of times (Watch out, Oki's!) It's always hard to hide subpar ingredients when its cooked right in front of you, so I was very impressed with how clean and fresh everything appeared. Nevertheless, there is just so much anyone can do with steak, chicken, and shrimp, with a mound of fried rice (nice touch frying on the grill!).
This place really shines with its sushi interpretations. And interpretation is the correct word, because while they have an array of maki, hand rolls, and sashimi for a purist like me, this place really shines in the creative, New York-style alternative rolls. These can be rolled in crispy flakes and deep fried (blasphemy for a purist), sauced with a spicy, SPICY, mayo, or lots of other non-classic treatments. while my group went for the grill, I was seated with them and ordered sushi. I tried these special rolls, and I am hooked! As an additional nice touch, they provided me the same green salad and miso soup my other party members got with their dinners at no extra charge.
This is the East Bay find of the year. Just don't go on weekend evenings after 6. The wait is interminable--bordering on an hour!
