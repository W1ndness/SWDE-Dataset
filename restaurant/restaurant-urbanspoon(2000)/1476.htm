Best ATL Steak!
by Barney (486 reviews)
September 22, 2008 - Really likes it
There are a handful of great steak houses in Atlanta, but McKendrick's continues to be my favorite.
Dropped in last night for a lite supper, so I skipped my favorite house salad (w/ Tomato,Hearts Of Palm, Maytag Blue cheese, Cucumber, Pepper), and went for the 12 oz NY Strip, medium.  As always, this baby had a great char/crust on the outside and was cooked absolutely perfectly (and so were the other 2 steaks at the table).  In truth, I've never had to send a steak back here.
The table shared the creamed corn (always a winner) and went with the garlic spinach.  This might be the first time I've had the spinach here, but I thought it was perfectly balanced with a nice amount of incredibly thinly shaved garlic and had a nice punch, but not so much as to be overpowering.
For Desert, the table had coffee and shared a piece of the chocolate torte with whipped cream and strawberries.
While this place usually has a very energetic noise level, Sunday night was quiet and still very enjoyable.

Best steak ever!
by Michael (3 reviews)
February 01, 2010 - Likes it
I have eaten several times at Mckendricks and I consider it the top steak house in the southeast. Appetizers are amazing. The chef had fresh Nantucket bay scallops which he tempura fried and served with roasted jalapeno tartar sauce. Sweet, tender scallops with a spicy sauce, love it. I hear he gets his tuna from Hawaii and uses it for the seared tuna, very fresh. Crab Cakes have no filling all Jumbo lumb crab meat, domestic not that imported crab most restaurants use, light and fluffy and lots of Old Bay. Oysters are bluepoint, chilled and served on ice. My favorite salad, baby iceburg with maytag blue cheese dressing and topped with smoked bacon strips, they were warm and crisp that night. Caeser dressing, don't know what he does to the dressing but it was great. Seafood is a must here, Mero sea bass was my favorite with my wife and the way the cook the fish on a wood grill is the best. You order the seafood and it comes with a choice of sauce, and they concentrate on the freshness of the fish rather than put all kinds of junk on it. Scottish salmon is also good. Steaks are all 100% black angus and my ribeye had more flavor and more tender than most steak houses. 16 oz dry aged New York strip is the best around, seasoned charrred and cooked just right. Sides are always good and the can be shared. Mac and cheese, not on the menu, is topped with Maine lobster or truffle oil and it is the best in town, They use four cheses and I don't know what else but it is amazingly good, creamy and rich. Must have cream corn or try the Maine lobster mashed potatoes (also not on the menu). Service was excellent, servers been there long so they know the menu and can take great care of you. I know I will be back and I crave the steaks and Mac and cheese!! World Class!!
