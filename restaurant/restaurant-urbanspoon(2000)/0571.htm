Good, but not quite great
by Andrew D (249 reviews)
July 19, 2009 - Likes it
Why is it that of all the eateries at The Wynn (and Encore, for that matter!), the buffet doesn't quite meet the standard of excellence I'm used to experiencing at this corner of The Strip?
Now don't get me wrong, the food isn't lousy. Far from it, you'll find some delicious grub no matter the meal. I guess I just expect more consistency for the price. This may be the most expensive buffet in Vegas, after all! And for the price, I just don't think a bland omelet or dull Chinese fried rice is acceptable.
So if you want to try something at The Wynn but can't make a choice on a restaurant (or forgot to make reservations at one), go try the buffet and dig for the culinary gems in the rough. But if you want consistently great food, it doesn't cost that much more to just get a table at Stratta or Society Cafe.

Overpriced, snooty attitude and mediocre food
by Pete (30 reviews)
February 12, 2009 - Doesn't like it
Long time Vegas residents, we finally made our way to the Wynn buffet for a quick lunch. What a disappointment !
I remember thinking "What's the big deal ?" when I first set foot inside the Wynn after it opened, and the theme continues with the buffet.
Greeted by a overweight woman in her late forties in a way too tight pink outfit with the snootiest attitude I've seen in a long time. I was ready to leave right then, but the food is supposed to be great, right ?
A Manhattan Clamchowder I had was good, as was the smoked salmon. Everything else was terrible. Really.
Starting with the salad arranged in shot glasses, with the dressing on top. Too bad that when you're through the top inch of your salad shooter, all that's left is lettuce with no dressing. Am I supposed to dump it on my plate and mix it or what ?
All chicken dishes I tried were overcooked and dried out (I think I tried three different dishes), and the other food I tried, including the deserts, didn't impress me much, either.
The service at the table was pretty slow, too. Dirty dishes were cleaned up quickly, but drink refills took forever.
All in all, it would have been disappointing for half the price, and I will never set foot inside the Wynn buffet again. I should have followed my instincts and left when Miss Piggy at the entrance gave me an attitude.
Paris, Bellagio or even Mandalay Bay have been consistenly satisfactory, and I would chose any of these over the Wynn anytime.
