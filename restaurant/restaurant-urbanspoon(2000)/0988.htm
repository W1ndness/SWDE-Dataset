Don't Forget Cash
by Nightingale (2 reviews)
June 16, 2010 - Likes it
There's only two con's that I can list for this establishment. The first being that they only accept cash - which is not so much a con as it is something to be aware of. There is an ATM on the premises for the uninformed or the unprepared. The second is that it is so small - which is part of the charm, but can mean that you'll be waiting for a seat at this local favorite.
The food is excellent and outshines the service whenever I visit. It's not that anyone is rude or unhelpful, only that the food is THAT good and they are always THAT busy. They may not refill your drink as often as you'd like, but their menu will keep you returning no matter your qualms.
If you want to avoid the cons while still reaping all the benefits, order to go. You won't be disappointed.

On the phone we asked for five pieces of flatbread (not remembering that their bread is large, about the size of a medium pizza). The person on the phone (owner/manager?) said that pita came complementary with the food and that she would give us extra. When I went to pick up the food, there was only the one piece of pita. I asked for more. I told her I would pay for it. Name the price. Sue proceeded to tell me that they would not sell pita to me. Their ovens are constrained during the rush. I understood this logic, but it was 8ish and their dining room was clearing out, not filling up. Did not appear to be a single table without food. Why did she tell us that she would give us extra? Just a strange example of being too rigid and not erring on the side of sensibility and good customer service instead. Food is decent here, but on principle we will never go back to these pita-teasers! Mezza up on Woodstock is so friendly, with great food. More deserving of our patronage.

Good food for an amazing price
by ak_jorge (2 reviews)
January 12, 2009 - Likes it
Nicholas is not drop-to-the-floor amazing, but it is one of the better Mediterranean eateries in Portland. On a recent trip I had the meat mezza platter, and everything was very tasty.
The tabouli was my favorite, though I could have done with a little less salt from the kitchen. Definitely fresh as can be, though. The falafel was quite good, too, if not fresh from the fryer. Generous helpings of tzatziki and a great hummus made everything better. My only complaint is that the beef skewer was very undercooked -- I mean raw. I took it home and reheated it in the oven for a snack later, though, and it was delightful.
And all for $10!
