This IS Typical Mexican
by Brent Byers (97 reviews)
April 25, 2010 - Likes it
Rosa Mexicano could easily be in Mexico City. The décor is typical and the food is wonderful! It is not Tex-Mex, it is Norteno, it is not California. It is Mexican.
The bar is fantastic and the bartenders are very knowledgeable (ask for Tequila "a la bandera").
My wife and I had lunch at Rosa Mexicano on April 24. We started with Queso Fundido, cheese melted together with cilantro, chili, onion and chorizo. It was served with fantastic, fresh corn tortillas.
My wife had one of her all time favorites, Cochinita Pibil. Slow roasted pork marinated in sour orange juice, vinegar and annatto. It is served with pickled onion and corn tortillas.
I had one of my favorites as well, Alambre a la Mexicana. Marinated tenderloin, chorizo, jalapeno, onion grilled together. This presentation was a little unusual, more like a kebab, but the meat was perfectly cooked and the chorizo was outstanding.
As well as the entrees, refried black beans with queso blanco and rice with fresh mint and cilantro were served family style.
They also served outstanding, pungent tomatillo salsa and a mildly spicy chipotle chili en adobo salsa.
The service was outstanding, friendly without being obtrusive.
Unlike most of the Mexican joints in my adopted home hometown, San Antonio, Texas, you will not find cheese enchiladas covered in chili con carne here. You won't find tacos al pastor stuffed with roasted shoulder meat, shredded cabbage and avocado like you might in Monterrey. But you will find food, atmosphere and décor more representative of Mexico City.
This is typical, excellent, Mexican.

Disappointing.
by Krystal (41 reviews)
November 28, 2009 - Doesn't like it
The only saving grace at Rosa Mexicano is the freshly made guacamole. It is delicious and you know it's fresh because they make it at your table. The remainder of the menu is hit or miss. I truly feel that Rosa Mexicano is the type of restaurant that tries too hard to be cutting edge and trendy but fails in the process. As an avid fan of Mexican food, I love traditionally prepared foods but also like to try new and innovatice creations. Unfortunately, the creations at Rosa Mexicano fall short.
