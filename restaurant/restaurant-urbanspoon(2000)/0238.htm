Beautiful, but....
by Chris (62 reviews)
November 06, 2008 - Doesn't like it
Via Vite is the most stunning restaurant downtown. Rising from Fountain Square, every table has an awesome view of the city. And the interior matches the ambitious exterior.
But man oh man did we have an average meal there. Our table tried several pizzas and the crust was soggy in the middle and the fillings were nothing special (Dewey's is far superior). The salads were quite good, especially the goat cheese-based one on the menu. But entrees (a pasta and a fish) just didn't deliver. Both forms are a challenge – really good pasta is really rare, and fish is easy to mess up.
The service was indifferent.
I hope this restaurant gets its legs because it has such potential. We'll go back to try it again, I'm sure.

foodie2shoes
by foodie2shoes (3 reviews)
February 16, 2010
Well... for Valentine's Day, my man and I decided to go out to dinner. I left it up to him, which, sometimes can be a mistake. So when he said Via Vite, my heart sank a little. I've been there a couple of times and I left..well.. a little pissed.
I'm sorry folks-if it offends you- but Italian dining is a little overrated in my opionon. I guess it's because it was easy and fast to cook when I was younger. BUT , as a foodie, understand that everything has it's place. With that being said, I came back a little apprehensive.
WELL...the food was great! Thank you!!! Let's talk..
Okay: course 1: I had the roasted beet and asparagus salad while my significant other had the ceasar. I have to admit that the beets weren't as tender as I would of like them, but the grilled asparagus made up for that along with the whipped goat cheese. The beets at first were hard and tough, yet seemed to get sweeter with every bite. I LOVED IT by the end of the dish!
NEXT: This was the one course that blew me away . It was the Cioppino. It was amazing. Let me tell you... if you've ever had cioppino, it's not very traditional. There was no crab, mussels, clams, or anything of the sort. HOWEVER, it was a very flavorful seafood soup that had a creamy texture, and an amazing bite of tomato and spiciness at the end that blew me away. I wish I had ordered this for my entree...it really was that good.
OKAY...I'm getting full now. I ordered the farfalle pasta with smoked salmon and asparagus but subsituted it with rosemary-parpadelle pasta. Deliscious! So rich and creamy, the smokiness of the salmon added that extra touch. My date had the bolonese, which at first I though"BORING", but when I tasted it- it was the single most delicious hamburger helper I ever tasted. And NO, I'm not mad about the price. It was actually very good!( I kept sneaking tastes)
FINALLY: Dessert. I opted with the tiramisu. I was creamy, fresh and homemade. I found some odd plastic part, or rind, or something in it, and they sent me home with another. I did'nt need it, but I appreciate the gesture.
Over all, I gotta say I'm glad I came back. This place was actually very good, and I enjoyed the entire night. YUM! I can't wait to come back and sit on the patio!!!

Recommend | Not helpful
