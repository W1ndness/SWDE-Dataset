+ 1 more photos from Closet Gourmet Happy wanderer!
by Closet Gourmet (80 reviews)
August 15, 2009 - Likes it
After being wholeheartedly disappointed by the Inland Seafood Festival I was in need of a real seafood fix so I wandered up the steps to Mitchell's. On principal I typically steer clear of even high end chain restaurants preferring to support locally/family owned establishments. However, I have heard regularly that Mitchell's is a good choice for discriminating seafood lovers and I was not disappointed twice in the same evening.
As a single this evening I sat down at the nicely appointed bar, perused the impressive menu (which is printed daily), and ordered a cup of lobster bisque and an order or mussels - both favorites of mine. The bisque was creamy with nice chunks of lobster and a subtle hint of sherry. While I prefer more than just a hint of sherry, the bisque was delicious (even on a 90 plus degree day) and was served with a half loaf of warm & mild sourdough bread.
A generous portion of mussels arrived shortly thereafter and looked fantastic as they were placed in front of me. In fact three patrons sitting around me commented and asked about the dish. They tasted as good as they looked. Topped with croutons and romano cheese and sauteed in white wine with fresh garlic, parsley, and tomato, it was all I could do to not spoon out the remaining broth as I finished these little jewels.
On a very busy evening the service was stellar as well. As impressed as I was I'm thinking a second trip to Mitchell's is in my future wanderings. Enjoy!

from fresh fish to the 7-layer carrot cake
by Jo Se (10 reviews)
January 21, 2010 - Likes it
You expect fresh fish to be prepared perfectly at a seafood restaurant. That's not always the case, but has proven to hold true at Mitchell's. A few years ago, a former fisherman recommended this as Cincinnati's best bet for amazing seafood. In fact, only Primavista seems to be able to give them a run for their money on fresh salmon prep... but you'll pay $10 more too. Mitchell's is a great value in that regard. Certainly, this dining option is a better bet for people who think paying $15 to $20 at Red Lobster (or similar chains) makes any sense. Since the great seafood is anticipated, guests and myself can't stop raving about the amazing carrot cake. Yum. I leave Michell's Fish Market on the Levee impressed- from fresh fish to the 7-layer carrot cake.
