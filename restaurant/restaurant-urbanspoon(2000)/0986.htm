"It was the best of times, it was the worst of times"
by Bob's Stomach (173 reviews)
December 20, 2009 - Likes it
We took our son and daughter-in-law out for dinner tonight to celebrate Michael's birthday. Michael chose a Peruvian restaurant in the Pearl--happily, something different for us Seattlites.
The worst of times: We ordered drinks and many were in martini glasses. As the were served we could see that this was a disaster in the making. When my wife's was delivered it landed in her lap!!! My wife fumed in silence through the first half of her meal.
The best of times: We asked our waiter to bring us a half dozen small plates of his choosing. Some were OK and a couple (i.e. the avacado crab salad) were excellent. Eventually the manager stopped by to talk to my wife and offer to take care of her dry cleaning bill. In addition she was comped her drink (duh!) and we got a 'taste from the chef' of four pieces of who knows what that was truly delicious.
In summation: The waiter's choice of dishes was pretty ho-hum with a couple of exceptions. If the entire meal was as good as the 'taste from the chef' we would have been walking on air. Thanks to the great company I had a wonderful time--and the ambiance was great. The service was OK at best and since you are served by a team it took a few people to even make it to OK--the manager bailed them all out. I think this is a restaurant with potential and overall was OK--but as you can see, nothing to really get too excited about. That said, they could easily move to the next level.

Exceptional Vegan Friendly Restaurant ever.
by Rika (1 review)
May 19, 2010
Please do check out my photos at Andina restaurant. For vegans/vegetarians, feel free to check them out because I'm telling you, they are amazing stuff.
http://rikaphotography.com/2010/05/18/andina-restaurant/
Having dined there three times since March 2010, it was already a favorite restaurant in the Pearl District, especially for a vegan and a vegetarian. Service was spectacular and very responsive. The cocktails were very unique with their names and the ingredients were freshly blended into a delicious one. Always start with the cocktail containing haberno pepper because it is among the most unique cocktails you will ever drink once you leave this restaurant. As a vegan, it is never disappointing to request to have these dishes made for vegans because their individual menus was designated for a vegan and vegetarian, separating from the meat menus. Appetizers were to die for, especially when they were pressed into colorful cakes of potatoes infused with lime juice and a touch of beautifully sliced avocados. Colourful potatoes/beets and medley of tropical-coloured vegetables were used alot in many of the veg-friendly appetizers especially the main courses. There were only three main courses that can be made for vegans/vegetarians. The curry-like squash sauce with basil rice tasted ah-mazing especially the risotto cake with fresh wild mushrooms/golden beets.
