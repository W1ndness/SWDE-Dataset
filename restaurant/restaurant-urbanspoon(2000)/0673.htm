Good but needs work
by Jon (2 reviews)
June 03, 2009 - Likes it
My first impression upon entering Honeypie was that it contradicts itself. The space felt open, but also stifled and somewhat cluttered with pillars, walls, and dividers. The designer was obviously attempting something along the lines of a 50's diner, and splashes of chrome, an oversized clock, and checkered floor tiles added to this; but on the other hand, the church pew benches and solid wood tables were reminiscent of a typical hipster coffeehouse. These two contradictions really did not detract from the atmosphere, and actually served to keep my eyes interested in my surroundings while I was there.
Perhaps the most perplexing addition to this design is three large deer heads over the bar, and stuffed ducks mounted on the walls, their ratty feathers collecting dust, their mouths frozen in a permanent grimace. I was lucky enough to have full view of all of these corpses throughout my breakfast there. This begs the question: Why would a restaurant that obliges vegans and vegetarians alike make such a show of taxidermy? I have an answer, but I don't completely believe it: The owner is out of touch with his customers. And so, as a final note on the design portion of this review, I will ask the owner to kindly remove the heads and the ducks. It's not because they're offensive - although some people will absolutely be offended. They're gross and completely unappetizing.
Service was relaxed, leisurely, but also prompt and super friendly. I felt neither rushed nor ignored, and any server who can pull off this balance is golden in my book. Granted, it was a relatively slow Wednesday morning, but I have no doubt that the service would be fine under most conditions.
I ordered an egg scramble with feta, tomatoes, and green onions, with toast and home fries. My wife also got a scramble. These were $7.00 each, so I was expecting a good amount of food, and was not disappointed. The scrambles were tasty, the ingredients were fresh, and the presentation was fine. The home fries seemed slightly old, as if cooked early in the day and reheated. But they were fine. I do have one complaint about the toast, and this goes for Comet Cafe as well (and I'm gonna say it loud): SERVE EGGS WITH TWO PIECES OF TOAST. IT COSTS PENNIES, BUT IT MAKES CUSTOMERS LIKE ME MORE LIKELY TO SHELL OUT $7.00 FOR A BREAKFAST THAT PROBABLY COSTS ABOUT A DOLLAR IN INGREDIENTS. Sigh. But when all was said and done, I was full after eating, and satisfied. My wife was happy too. I should also mention that the coffee is fantastic. This is not thin, bland diner coffee. It's like a coffee you'd expect from a good coffee house. Worth the $2.25.
I'm going to include a grade in this review, only because I feel that Honeypie can and will improve in the areas that need it. So, currently it's at C+. Get rid of the dead animals, and it's at a B. Throw in another piece of toast, and freshen up those home fries, and it's in A- territory. I'll be back for dinner, and probably for another breakfast. Hopefully they'll hammer out some details. If they do, I'm sure people will be more likely to return for seconds and thirds.

Very GOOD!
by Mark Rowlands (1 review)
June 12, 2009
It's getting easier to find vegetarian food that isn't generic and cheaply made. Honeypie has a new approach to your cafe diner that caters to vegans and vegetarians. Their menu is diverse and inexpensive. Portions are very good and their fries are freshly cut. They also have 2 pasties every day, 1 vegetarian and 1 meat. Being a beer snob, their beer selection is amazing, ranging from your budget priced beers to your fancy smancy beer prices. The best thing is that it's not smokey in there which is a huge plus because I have a 3 year and a newborn. Also, the staff/crowd doesn't seem to mind a little noise coming from our table! The wait staff is very friendly and fun. If you're a fan of The Palomino and Beans N Barley then you will definitely love this place.

Dissapointment at Honeypie
by Christina Lynn Anderson (2 reviews)
May 08, 2010
I was definitely disappointed when I went to Honeypie because I heard so much good things about the restaurant. I decided to get the burger on the money, which had 7 oz. of meat. All along I was not thinking that the burger was going to be stacked with one patty on top of the other. I was imaging a flat hamburger patty with a lot of toppings on kind of like Kopps. The burger was a big mess, which got grease all over my hands and was really hard to split with my Dad. We cut the big gigantic burger in half to find out that the meat was medium rare. They never even asked me if I like it this way, but were kind enough to make me a whole new sandwich because I told them about it. I was pretty impressed by this to say the least. The flavoring on the burger was bland with no seasoning at all. I really could have made this particular burger at home and fried up sunny-side eggs on top of it! I probably would not go back to this restaurant and if by chance I did go back it would be for the pork-slaw sandwich with bbq, since it might be more manageable to eat.
