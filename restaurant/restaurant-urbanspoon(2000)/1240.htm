by JC (1 review)
February 01, 2010 - Doesn't like it
Went there for dinner last night..big mistake. The first red flag should have been the atmosphere..there wasn't any. Brightly lit room with no ambiance. We had to wait for a table so we ordered two cosmopolitans. We each received a drink that was half the size that you would have received at any other restaurant. I mentioned this to the bartender; she said that her mixing container was small and that's all she could do. Bad answer. The food was also disappointing..the soup was basically just a broth with no flavor. The chef had no personality and seemed bored and rushed to complete the dinner. In most of these places, part of what you are paying for is to be entertained and it certainly did not happen here. The quality of the ingredients such as the chicken, beef and shrimp was definitely on the inferior side..cheap cuts of meat that were not very tasty. I feel that my bland dish (chicken) was $15.95 was way overpriced and I don't see how they can justify charging this amount. Lastly, we both had intestinal problems after the meal and the next day. We definitely will not be back.

50/50
by Kguy (25 reviews)
July 10, 2009 - Likes it
Ok - so if you make me choose... yes I like it... could it be better, have I had better... most definitely. It is in a run down shopping center with a run down interior be-fitting of your neighborhood ma and pa Chinese buffet. The prices just don't fit the location and décor. The chef's are very good and the food is of decent quality. The waiting can be atrocious and since the size of the waiting area is small, which means when you are seated there are often people staring you down waiting for their turn. This makes for a pretty stressful and un-romantic meal; I don't recommend this as a quiet date spot. Like all Japanese Steak Houses if you come with less than 8 people expect to sit with some strangers.
Recommendation to the owner - move your location to a location more befitting of your establishment or convince the shopping center owner to let you put in an outdoor patio at the front, put in a canopy, some seating, some Japanese scenery and move your waiting area outside. This frees up more table space inside and will help establish a better interior ambience.
As silly as it seems, when I get the craving I visit the Tokyo Steakhouse Steak House on Boyette Road now, while the chefs aren't quite as good, the ambience is much better.
