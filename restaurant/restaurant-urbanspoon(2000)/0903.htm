Don't Be Afraid To Try It
by Dr. Danny (106 reviews)
July 18, 2009 - Really likes it
Boma is a buffet at the Animal Kindom Lodge - it has a large variety of food including "safer" food and authentic African food. I think it's a shame when people don't go here because they are afraid to try the food - even the exotic food is pretty neutral in my opinion. This is not the typical buffet - there are lots of different things to try, the stations usually don't have lines, and Boma stocks the 50+ item buffet at a great pace.
The restaurant is always packed, now that Disney has expanded their dining program, it's nearly impossible for a local to get in. I still try every time I make a reservation in that area when friends are in town, but I have not been able to get in for months.

One of the Best Buffets in WDW
by UCF Knights Fan (69 reviews)
January 02, 2010 - Likes it
Boma is a place I take every out of town guest when they come and visit me and each time we have a very pleasant dining experience. I would not really say it is African cuisine however. Jiko is African cuisine, but I think Boma uses more African infused flavors, if anything is African about it at all. The buffet is extensive and the servers are always amiable, pleasant, and efficient. I find the items on the buffet to be deliciously prepared, especially for a buffet of this size.
The one thing that frustrates me to no end about this place is the following: I will call Disney dining to make a reservation and they do not give you many time choices, making it seem as though the restaurant is fully booked or near capacity. We usually have to select a time that is not very convenient for us. However, everytime we show up, it is not as busy as the agents on the phone make it appear to be. I never understood this.
I continue to go back to Boma because it is a unique dining experience with a very cool atmosphere. Disney's Animal Kingdom Lodge is breathtaking and the food is pretty good too!

I did like it but I was not satisfied with the experience as a whole.
by Heather (4 reviews)
August 09, 2010 - Doesn't like it
The food was good. Several things were really delicious. However I was not satisfied with the experience as a whole. First, being a higher tier restaurant at Disney I was expecting a quiet, nice dinner. It was a zoo (no pun intended)! There were tons and tons of people. My husband and I were at a two person table which seemed wedged up against another two person table. I felt like we were dining with them. It was so uncomfortable we timed our trips to the food with them so we didn't have to be in their faces when we left our dining area.
The food was a far cry from anything African. If you call adding a little cinnamon and curry to the food African then this is the place for you. Again the food tasted very good, but not African at all. Would have been nice to know too where they supposed some of these entree's originated from. There were no country/place labels with the names of the food.
And lastly the price. Very huge expense, for something not at all what it was cracked up to be. I didn't think the buffet was expansive, quite small if you minus the area with fresh fruits and salads (normal food). The dining room was very LOUD. A regular buffet area....and when all was said and done you paid through the nose for this.
Save your money and go for two nights out on I-Drive someplace and enjoy twice the night's out at half the price.

LOVE IT!
by Luis (17 reviews)
March 24, 2010 - Really likes it
Heck yeah! This place is one of my favorite restaurants in Orlando! Everything on their buffet is delicious and super fresh. It's a bit of a drive for us (35-40 mins) so we try to make it for special occasions.
The place is beautiful and the staff goes out of their way to please you and make sure you are taken care of. It's a Disney restaurant after all.
My favorite items were the BBQ ribs, the salmon, the roasted chicken, but above all their butternut squash soup! This soup is to die for, and it should actually be considered a dessert of how sweet it is! The soup is not on their every day buffet menu, but if you ask your waiter they will bring you some... this made me feel special ;)
It is a bit pricey $28 pp, but to us it is well worth it since we know that we will be treated right and the food is just out of this world! Some of the items in the menu are a bit exotic, but hey why not try something different once in a while?
Parking is free, just tell the gate officer that you are dinning at the restaurant and they will let you in (since the restaurant is inside of the Animal Kingdom Resort)
OK, enough... give this place a try! I recommend it!
