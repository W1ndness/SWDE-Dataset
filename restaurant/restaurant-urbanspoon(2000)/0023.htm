Mostly great most of the time.
by Paxson (16 reviews)
July 15, 2010
Most of the food at Kinley's is great, and their wine and beer selection is solid and reasonably priced. The calamari and bacon-wrapped dates are fantastic, as is the halibut and blandly-titled but very flavorful "vegetarian entree." I have a friend who lusts after the lobster ravioli, although it's a little too rich for me. The only real miss, I think, is the crab cakes: whatever dark arts the chefs at Kinleys are dabbling in, they've somehow created a dish with almost no flavor. Kinleys has good, friendly service and reasonable prices, especially if you can catch half-price appetizers before 6.

mobile review
by Mandy (1 review)
August 21, 2009 - Likes it
I've had several fabulous dinners at kinleys and have always been impressed with the food and service, but recently I learned that they are open for lunch as well. I decided to stop in on my lunch hour today and was just as impressed with my experience. The lunch menu consisted of sandwiches, salads and some entrees as well and I was surprised to see that the prices were no more than you would pay for any mediocre microwaved meal at any chain restaurant. I decided to try the coconut crusted rockfish and my coworker had the Pot roast sandwich. I was very impressed with the portions, as neither one of us could finish ours and ended up taking a couple boxes with us. The rockfish was to die for, the sauce was amazing, and my friends sandwich looked delcious. The fries were the best I've had in awhile, cooked perfectly and very crispy. Service was fantastic as usual, and all in all a wonderful lunch, very casual, and not what I expected. Our bill came to less than $30, and we were in and out with plenty of time to get back to work. Highly recommended for all of those who are saying that dinner is too pricey. I'll definitely be back!

Loved it.
by Sharon J (1 review)
August 14, 2009 - Likes it
I stopped in for a quick drink and wound up staying to eat. The 1/2 price appetizers were delicious: the crab cake was moist, delicious and focused on the crab instead of breading; the duck spring roll was delicious and the coconut sauce was sublime; and the meatballs were a sophisticated turn on the dish. The wine and beer list is to die for and the bar staff was extremely knowledgeable on each and every one. The music was pleasant but not overwhelming and the atmosphere was a chic mix of eclectic and modern. The bartender recommended dessert was amazing: a fusion of savory and sweet with chocolate polenta, candied jalapenos and chipotle ale sauce, sheer genius. Finally, the presentation on each and every dish was beautiful. Well it is true that the portions are not the "super-size" ones you find in some other restaurants, the flavor and thought that goes into each dish makes it a pleasure to indulge the senses in each and every bite. Personally, I'll take that over a serving bowl of mediocre pasta any day.

best dinner this year
by zed (1 review)
March 06, 2009 - Really likes it
this restaurant makes me feel like im not in a provincial town. the music was hip, the wine was a superb and didnt break the bank, and the chef worked for charlie trotter, so the food had imagination and execution. most of anchorages best restaurants are in strip malls built in the 80s, with decor and menus to match, but kinleys is uber kewl. i can understand why some of anchorages yuppies would eschew this place, all the better for me. i loved the brazillian hard wood floors, the rock wall, the gyro ceiling fans, and the fact i did not see one ponytail! the staff was model beautiful, but pleasant and competent, all i saw was smiles for miles, and i left with one too. high lights were the bacon dates, calimari, almond halibut, new zealand pinot gris, and the down to earth wine education i received when i asked the sommelier a question. ive dined at kinleys for both lunch and dinner on several occasions, and can say there is no one in anchorage with as much to offer the fun loving sophisticates as kinley's.
