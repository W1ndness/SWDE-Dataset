Simple food, good service, but underwhelming
by bionici (13 reviews)
March 28, 2009 - Doesn't like it
First, let me state that I grew up around Italian families, lived in Nashville, Philadelphia, Baltimore, and near NYC. And, have eaten in some really great family, neighborhood Italian restaurants in most of those areas. Having said that, I can only compare City House to the dining experiences I've had in the houses in other cities.
The food was OK, the service was good, but the overall experience was "underwhelming". I normally do not eat pizza at a so-called Italian restaurant. There are so many other great regional & traditional dishes that can be had. But frankly, the menu did not seem truly Italian from a southern, northern, or other regional standpoint. Some items on the menu had Italian overtones, but it mostly seemed like a fusion of some traditional American dishes & Italian. Guess that's OK if you are into fusion. In addition, with the exception of the Painted Hills Tri Tips, everything seemed overpriced. Nothing on the menu shouted out for us to try it.
My party tried the cauliflower & greens salad, and it was OK, but better salads can be had at some of the chain Italian restaurants. Saying that is somewhat painful because I don't try to frequent chain restaurants. Marrying a little cauliflower & a minor amount of salad greens with a little salad dressing may be a good idea, but for some reason the chef just didn't come through on that. In addition, an $8.00 salad that was really a "salad wannabe" was disappointing. I'm not looking for the never ending "all you can eat" salad bowl, just a little more substance.
The "House Made Belly Ham" pizza was mostly crust with a minimal amount of good tasting cheese placed near to the center of the crust with a very minor amount of belly ham spread out on the crust. There could not have been much more than a couple of tablespoons of coarsely chopped ham on the pizza. I'm not looking for the Pizza Hut Super Supreme. But if the menu is going to represent the pizza as made with belly ham, at least add more than a minimal amount of ham and cheese to the pizza. I do have to say that the crust was some of the best I've ever had.
I probably should have tried the fish stew. I'm always looking for a great cioppino. But the ingredients represented on the menu (catfish, clams, mussels) were again underwhelming. At least add a couple of shrimp. A true fish stew would contain whatever was available that day at seaside. In addition, the price was $23. It's not that the price was too much. It was too much for the ingredients shown on the menu. I'd pay more than $23 for a truly great cioppino. Maybe I've got to go to San Francisco or Baltimore to get that.
The overall view was that City House is an overblown southern pizza restaurant since that is what most of the people around us were ordering. I truly wish that we could have had a better experience, since we had been told that City House was a great Italian restaurant.
I once read a review of a golf course which stated, "the last time I played the course was the last time I played the course". The same feeling was shared among our party.

Horrible Experience...never again
by Josh (1 review)
July 02, 2010 - Doesn't like it
Pros: The old, industrial setting is pretty cool, I'll admit.
Cons: Just about everything else.
Food: Decent, but not worth the money and way to salty. My girlfriend could have cooked it better for about 1/3 the price. That said, I understand that going out to eat, the purpose is to have the food cooked for you, served to you and treated like a patron and you pay extra for that service. However, if I am paying that much for only decent food, I better be getting impeccable service...
Service: Absolutely horrendous. Waited 45 minutes before the server even took our drink order. After that it took almost 30 minutes to get our drinks. If we weren't there with a group, we would have walked out. The server did not know anything about what the bar served. I asked if they had a certain type of whiskey and he had no idea, offered to get me a whiskey list which I considered, then just ordered a Jack Daniels neat because I realized it would be another 30 minutes before he actually got the whiskey list to me. I was served a Jack on the rocks which I had to correct. After all of this I was so frustrated that I just wanted to eat and leave.
Our server even got the splitting of the checks wrong. I was overcharged for a horrible experience. Expensive lesson but one well worth learning...
Needless to say, I'll never be visiting there again. City House really needs to step it up. I don't care how local, organic or amazing your food is, if your service is that bad, something needs to be addressed, especially if your food is not even that good.
I've had better service at a Taco Bell...

Favorite Nashville Restaurant
by tipnring (6 reviews)
July 06, 2010 - Likes it
The employees take the time to explain to you what some of the dishes are and what is in them.. The ingredients are fresh which is what we liked best. There is a big open kitchen which is run with team work. If you sit at the chef's bar the chef actually asks how you liked the food. Some of the best dishes were the Belly Ham Pizza, Kale Salad, Springer Mountain half chicken and the House Made Sausage with tomato ragu. They have an almond crusted pound cake with lemon syrup and lemon gelato that was great. The prices are good and you get an adequate serving of food. I was here for 2 months on business and frequently visited this establishment. The staff is friendly and the service is great.
