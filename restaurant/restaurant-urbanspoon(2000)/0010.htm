Close to fine dining
by Brenda and Michael (7 reviews)
January 10, 2010 - Likes it
This is one of the few almost-fine-dining-restaurants in Albuquerque. Two things hold this place back from being true fine dining: too many tables and too large of portions. However, as Ambrosia (an awesome true fine dining restaurant) didn't make it in Albuquerque because people expect giant portions like at the Outback or some other trashy chain, we can understand why Brasserie la Provence has large portions.
Aside from complaining about Albuquerque's lack of true fine dining restaurants, let's talk about how wonderful this restaurant is. Coming in, the decor is very quaint and warm. Our waiter handled things correctly, he was not pushy, he did not rush us but did not neglect us. He understood the time for a proper dining experience, as opposed to a shove-it-in-your-face eating experience. He also made wonderful suggestions on the menu and was very helpful in regards to our pescatarian diet, making sure there was not chicken or beef base in our soups and entree sauces. The food is very reasonably priced for the quality, dishes ranging from 10 to 20 dollars! We couldn't believe it either! Excellent wine list of French imports. We had the mussels for an appetizer, and the sauce was to die for. The salade nicoise was amazing, we ordered the tuna rare and it was exquisite. We also had the spinach quiche, which was amazing and came with a side side of field greens and a fabulous dressing. The finale was the chocolate mousse cake which was unbelievable. This is a must-try Albuquerque restaurant for people with taste. If you are on a budget, you can also have a wonderful meal sans wine for a reasonable price.

This is actually a brasserie!
by ChelseaGirl (6 reviews)
October 08, 2009 - Likes it
I was amazed to find that this restaurant was actually a brasserie like the kind you might find in France or Québec: high quality food at reasonable prices, bustling atmosphere, and down home French cuisine from southern France. I was immediately greeted by the Maitre d'hotel. He engaged me in conversation while taking me to a table, pulled out my chair for me, took my purse and placed it at an empty chair, and handed me a menu. Very hospitable. The waiter was helpful, but not intrusive. A few slices of fresh baguette to the table right away. Perfect and crusty on the outside, served with cold butter:) I ordered Soupe a l'onignon and a croque madame with a side salad. The soup was great, possibly a little too heavy on the sherry, but great nonetheless. I was given the appropriate amount of time to finish my soup before the entrée was brought out. Always a plus. The croque madame was exquisite. Perfectly runny egg on top. Smooth béchamel. Gruyère and ham used were of highest quality. The ham wasn't too thin. The bread was grilled perfectly-stayed crisp and firm though it was smothered in béchamel and egg yolk. OK yum! This was by far the best croque madame I've had in the States. Come to think of it, the best one I've ever had. Not gloopy or too heavy/rich. I got the salad, although you could get it with pommes frites-couldn't imagine that-would have been too rich. The salad that came with the meal was huge. Perfectly dressed with a red wine vinaigrette. Very fragrant greens, too. In short, I was impressed, and that's difficult to do. The portions were large, not just for a French restaurant, and this was at lunch. However, I believe that the lunch and dinner menu was the same. Next time, I will try the steak au poivre (ordered by someone sitting next to me-it looked beautiful and she couldn't stop making and "mmmm...." noise:) and then I'll have my boyfriend order a croque madame so I can have a few bites...Go here, you won't be let down.
