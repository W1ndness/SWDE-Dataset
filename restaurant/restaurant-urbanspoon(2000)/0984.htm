C'est manifique
by A. Waltz (25 reviews)
September 24, 2009 - Likes it
I'm not a huge fan of pancakes, but crepes are right up my alley. This place, and its sister restaurant on Craig Street, are one of the most satisfying ways to eat breakfast, lunch or dinner The dining area is small, but if you're willing to wait in line for a table during their busier hours, your patience will be well rewarded with inexpensive and excellent sweet or savory crepes. They offer a wide variety of fresh ingredients (Creme fraiche? Yes, please), and each savory crepe dish comes with a small mixed green salad with a sweet, tangy vinagarette. And with coffee, it's a mere $7. Genuis.
Some people would hate on this place because it's not "true" French cuisine, like the apparently unorthodox addition of the salad . But this is'nt France, and you snobby franophiles can go stuff it. You're so wrapped up in this whole ridiculous concept of Frenchness that it's made you unable to enjoy anything. Besides, I've been to Paris, and the crepes at this place are just as good as the ones I had there (it was the first thing I ate when I arrived at my motel room, at a tiny bistro just a block down). You want some real French food? Go fry an egg in butter and dump a glass of wine on it. There you go! Bon appetit, as they say.

Chocolate and crepes go great together
by Louis (15 reviews)
December 19, 2009 - Likes it
[Originally written March 18, 2006] Crepes Parisiennes to me has always been the place that I pass by and think to myself "I'll go there someday", and I do that twice on my way to and from Pamela's around the corner.
It is underneath a chocolate place, and going down the stairs you see a line of tables for two (or four if you combine them) on the right side. The flow is you go to the counter at the back to place your order, then find a table where the staff brings your food out to you. There are two categories of crepes. Savory and sweet. The savory ones read like meals, while the sweet ones read like dessert. My friend had the ham and cheese, I ordered a smoked salmon.
The crepes are a bit heavier than expected, although you still won't mistaken them for pancakes. The fillings went well with the sauces chosen (you choose among four, although for my salmon, there was a recommended sauce.)
The real highlight was when we had their hot chocolate. It was wonderful. Hot chocolate with dark chocolate added to it and whipped creme. It completely blew away the recent high quality chocolate offering from Starbucks, or a Frangos. Very rich, just the right amount of sweetness. Ahhhh.
And, for the record, even though this is technically well after March 14, I got credit for properly acknowledging White Day.

Great Crepes, Has Some Quirks
by Ellie (7 reviews)
January 09, 2010 - Really likes it
Fondly referred as "the crepe place" between my friends and I, this little hole in the wall serves some of the best crepes I've ever had. I've tried both the dessert crepes as well as the breakfast crepes and both have been very satisfying. Note that the breakfast crepes come with a little "salad" of leafy greens, mandarin oranges and dressing. Dessert crepes mainly just have whipped cream on top. I can't speak much for the coffee as I don't drink it, but my friends seem to enjoy it.
That being said, this place is not without its quirks. Service can be hit or miss. I've waited anywhere from 5 minutes to 30 minutes to place my order. Seating is crowded, and sometimes it is best to place an order for a friend while they save a table. Don't expect the place to open on time - I've waited in line for the doors to open until 10:15 some Sunday mornings. Also be warned - this is a cash only place, and things can end up being more than what the menu originally states.
Overall - a must go, but with an open mind and a relaxed disposition :)
