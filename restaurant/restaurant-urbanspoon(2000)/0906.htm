Really LIkes It!
by UCF Knights Fan (69 reviews)
December 15, 2009 - Likes it
I will probably have to say this is my most favorite restaurant in central Florida. The restaurant sits in a historic building in Church Street Station and the architecture is amazing. The food, service, and entertainment in the Flamenco Bar are equally amazing. I have been here numerous times and each time I leave completely satisfied. You can order as little or as much as you want, you choose. You must try the sangria, patatas bravas, and ensalada de la casa. I have to at least get these every time I eat here because they are so delicious. The banderillas mixtas is also delicious. They have a huge selection of tapas....some very unique items and some standard items. I went here for my birthday dinner last year and all of my guests loved the food, drinks, ambiance, and service. I would highly highly recommend this place. I am so glad it is here.

Terrible service, mediochre food
by Latenightnosh (1 review)
April 07, 2010 - Doesn't like it
We went to Ceviche for Tapas Tuesday. We were the only ones there waiting to be seated for a good ten minutes while the hostess chatted on the phone and didn't even glace at us. Got seated in a hallway so dark that we couldn't see the menu-but it took another 12-15 minutes before a waiter would stop by to re-seat us by a nearby table with a light. The same waiter waited ANOTHER 15 minutes to stop by and ask us what we wanted to drink. We had to ask for the tapas tuesday menus-and he took another five minutes to bring us ONE. I could see him as we were waiting; he was laughing and chatting with the bartenders, not waiting tables. He brought us the worst Sangrias I've ever tasted; a mixture of cheap red wine and some kind of thick syrup that seemed to be made of high fructose corn syrup. It took 15-20 minutes for our tapas to arrive; the salmon was good, but the chicken was leathery, and the cod came in a form that my friend described as "corn dogs without the dogs"; they were pretty disgusting. The grilled asparagus was served ice cold, and like most items on the menu there was no indication of how it would be served. If you want great tapas with good service go to Taste in College park.

Good but not great
by Nancy Theisen (1 review)
November 22, 2008 - Likes it
The atmosphere was fun and enjoyable.  We also liked the music.  The service was just okay.  We had to ask to for our waters refilled.  We ordered fried calamari to start.  It was lightly breaded and tender.  We liked it.  Then we ordered one of the ceviche's.  It was tasty, however, the shrimp was not fresh (it tasted like it had been frozen) and it was overcooked and hard.  The fish in the dish was absolutely delicious. Tender and fresh.  We also ordered babagonoush, this item was absolutely tasteless and we would not recommend it.  We also had the hummas and this was better, although we have had better.  The bread that comes with it was so hard that you could have broken your teeth on it.  It was also tasteless.  The peppers stuffed with veal and sausage were very tasty and we enjoyed this dish.  We will probably go again, with the understanding that it is hit or miss.  The prices were reasonable.  We had a couple of cocktails and they removed the awful babaganoush from our bill and it came to $48.
