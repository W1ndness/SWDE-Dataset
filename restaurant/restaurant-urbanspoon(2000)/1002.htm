Message from Decarli

Enjoy sophisticated Italian-inspired entrees created with fresh seasonal ingredients in an elegant yet rustic atmosphere at thi

  • simple, warm, inviting
  • fun cocktails, great wine
  • great for large parties

decarli is an Italian-inspired restaurant newly opened by husband and wife team, Paul and Jana DeCarli. With a menu that changes weekly decarli is able to feature fresh seasonal ingredients from the Northwest, created in an open kitchen that captures your attention when you walk in the door.

A simple design plan showcases the natural elements of the 1924 building-exposed brick, original fir floors, and soaring open beam ceilings all accented by drop filament bulbs and candlelit tables. The 'must-sees' are an enormous brass chandelier that hangs over the family table and the innovative countertop at the bar.
decarli is Beaverton's own piece of the Pearl, great for special occasions, yet comfortable enough for casual dining.
Check out our website to find out what new additions we have made to our menu!
Now open for Sunday Brunch and Sunday Dinner!!!

Love, love LOVE decarli
by ldybth (2 reviews)
July 01, 2010 - Really likes it
the bartender knows me by name, as does the hostess and half the staff. Is it because I come here too often? Perhaps....but it has more to do with the neighborhood friendliness of this suburban haven.
Thank you, Paul and Jana for bringing some class to Beaverton with decarli. The food is always excellent, and the experience is always second to none.
This is my favorite place to bring out-of-town company to. But I especially love bringing west sider's who've never heard of decarli here.....they are always floored by the ambiance and quality food.
Best bets....the polenta fries appetizer, the mussels with pernod cream, the tagliatelle, and the short ribs on spaetzle.....never had a bad meal here, and I've eaten here a LOT!!

Pretentious, Boring and Overpriced
by Rita (18 reviews)
May 24, 2010 - Doesn't like it
We went there last night. The food was very boring. My pasta pieces were stuck together, I bet that a 9-year old can manage to do better cooking orecchiette. My entree with shrimp and chorizo was greasy and one-dimensional. The attitude of their staff is very pretentious, give me a break this is Beaverton for crying out loud, what is the pretention about! My partner ordered the penne and asked the waiter to omit the ricotta and use a tomato based sauce instead of the cream but leaving the rest of the ingredients as is. What she got was a bowl of penne topped with Marinara, that is no other ingredients. The price for penne topped with marinara was $17, do they think they are in downtown NYC?
Bottom line, won't go there again.

I've been to Decarli at least 4 times, sometimes for dinner as well as for happy hour. The menu changes frequently, but some of my favorite dishes include their short ribs with spaetzle ( order it if it's on the menu), their crispy, thin pizza's, the ravioli and gnocchi.I like the hip, yet comfortable space with high ceilings and brick walls. If I lived closer I'd go more often, but when I'm craving good Italian food with a modern twist, I head to DeCarli !
