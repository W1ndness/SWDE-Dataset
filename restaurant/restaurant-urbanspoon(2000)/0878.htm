Cheap and good
by 2RG (54 reviews)
April 22, 2010 - Likes it
The Alvarados in Bellevue closed over a year ago, and I miss it. Really good food at really cheap prices. There also used to be one in La Vista, but it closed several years back. This is the only location still going that I know of.
My favourites are the carnitas and pollo asado. Love the rice and beans as well. I've tried pretty much everything on the menu, and, except for the green chili, liked everything. Great late night, after-bar food.

Fantastic Food for Criminally Cheap
by Aaron Andersen (15 reviews)
August 06, 2010 - Likes it
I finally just made it over there tonight after wanting to for a while, and the food did not disappoint! It was great from the "ground" up. That is, even one of the most basic things to start a dish with, the ground beef, was delicious! The portions are more than generous, for nearly Taco Bell prices; but oh, is it so much more delicious and authentic! Our cheese wasn't fully melted on anything tonight, but even with that downfall, I came away really liking this place. The staff was also friendly, helpful, and accommodating. And there was a free salsa bar, so order some chips! Surprisingly, this little, local, delicious place both is open 24/7, and has a drive through window. I will be back.

Good 24 hour Mexican but...
by Tony Nguyen (1 review)
June 29, 2009 - Likes it
I love going to this place after bar hours in the Old Market. I love the tacos and churros. There is also a variety of basic Mexican food. The place is open 24/7 with a drive-thru.
Portions are good and you can get a lot of food for under $10. The main negative with this place is the lack of cleanliness in the store. The food there is good enough for me to ignore this issue, but you may be a little turned off by the upkeep.
This place doesn't fall into the luxury Mexican dining found elsewhere, but it's a good place for "drunk food". Alvarados is essentially a bona fide fast food Mexican place that is a huge step up from the Americanized Taco Bell drivel, but not someplace to go on a date or family dinner.

Great food, great value
by fireball (1 review)
November 05, 2009 - Likes it
I am from Omaha, but had never eaten here before. I read some good reviews and my dad and I decided to give it a try. The place is not much for atmosphere, being in an old building that was built for a popular "Mexican" fast food chain, but it more than makes up for it in the food. I had the adobada plate- a huge mound of seasoned meat on top of beans, rice, guacamole, and pico de gallo with some shredded iceberg lettuce and a big flour tortilla on the side. My dad had an enchilada/taco plate with chicken for his choice of meat. We both cleaned our plates when really we could have gotten two meals out it! I'll go back for sure. My dad? I think he had a problem with the tired feel of the dining area.
