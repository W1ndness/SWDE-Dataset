+ 1 more photos from Jane Doe Eats - Seattle Seared Salmon Surprise
by Jane Doe Eats - Seattle (179 reviews)
May 10, 2009 - Likes it
Most everything about this place was spot on from prices, quality and portions, to seating and art.
My curry duck noodles arrived with what seemed to be half a duck breast or more. I couldn't believe I was eating this well for this price, in a city I expected to suck every dollar from my pocket, bank account and 401k.
The seared marinated salmon was f-ing amazing. The skin was crisp and tasty while the fish flesh was still soft, warm and tender inside. After 2+ years in Seattle I never expected to get better salmon anywhere else, but this dish knocked my salmon socks off.
Only unpleasantries included the stifling heat, dining room acoustics that busted our ears like a rock concert and the waitron who went MIA a few times with too many tables to manage. She also tried to talk us out of a pitcher full of frozen lychee martinis, cautioning that it would 'separate' as the temp dropped. My guess was it was labor intensive for the bartender so he was pushing the floor to move the sangria, which was prob ready to pour from a 50 gallon drum. We were glad we insisted on our preferred elixir but the sweetness got overwhelming at the bottom. Fresh coconut milk instead of coconut cream could fix that, and a few more mint leaves would have improved the complexity. I loved how my leaf froze into a mint chip in the frosty beverage.

Republic Restaurant at Union Square
by Antonio (1 review)
December 11, 2009
I had dinner at Republic on Dec 10 and believed the food to be mediocre. The atmosphere is fun (communal tables) and lively and the restaurant is very large, especially for NYC. The entree portions were large, however, the food was just mediocre. The restaurant was quite busy and perhaps the kitchen did not perform at its best. The appetizer of fried tofu was rather heavy from too much breading but the dipping sauce was a nice complement. The fried calamari were also accompanied by a great sauce, yet the calamari also suffered from excessive breading. The entree (curry noodles with duck) lacked a home-made texture and flavor. The various components of the dish (curry sauce, duck, noodles and other flavors) did not seem to combine to a whole that was better than taste of individual elements. I think Republic is a fun atmosphere and lively urban feel, however, other options will mostly likely deliver the authentic type of food featured on the menu.
