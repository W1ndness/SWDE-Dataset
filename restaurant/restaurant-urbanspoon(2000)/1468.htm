Great Breakfast.
by Barney (486 reviews)
December 22, 2008 - Likes it
I wanted to add a comment after a great breakfast here this morning, maybe the best one I've had around this side of town for a while.
Coffee was strong and hot.  For a little lighter breakfast, I went with the vegetable omelette (spinach, mushrooms, red and green bell peppers, sun dried tomatoes, and artichokes), the grits, and a biscuit.
The Omelette was very tasty!  The sundried tomatoes and artichokes did a great job of adding a little punch and avoiding a bland omelette.  Could have been a little better if it was cooked a tiny bit less, but it's rare to find a place that doesn't cook the eggs completely done.  Unusual for me, but I'll have to also compliment the grits!  Thick and coarse ground, they were neither soupy or tasteless.  Instead, they had a nice subtle flavor and hearty texture without being unrecognizable behind cream and cheese.  The biscuit was good, but pretty average.
Finally, the service and space were very nice.

A bit of a surprise
by Barney (486 reviews)
June 01, 2008 - Likes it
Given the location, I was surprised to find how nice and comfortable it was on the inside.  Folks were very pleasant and helpful as I tried to narrow down my choices for breakfast from "5" to "1".  I finally picked the cinnamon apple pancakes and a side of sausage.  I often times avoid pancakes because they can be just too heavy.  These were not.  There were a half dozen 4-5 inch pancakes that were fluffy and light and had big slices of apple throughout with a great cinnamon flavor and a blob if whipped butter on top that melted in as I watched.  With a strong cup of coffee and my side of sausage, I'd say that was a very successful breakfast.  Looks like they're almost done with the deck out front.  While the view might not be anything to write home about, it'll probably be a really great option for a leisurely breakfast outside.

Bread pudding heaven!
by Mary E. (13 reviews)
December 01, 2008 - Likes it
When I saw there was a savory bread pudding with shrimp, I had to try it. Honestly, it was so amazing I almost ordered a second helping to go! The New Orleans bread pudding is a jewel of a plate- moist, savory, with a tasty sauce and plump shrimp. I can honestly say it was the best breakfast I have ever had... in my life! The grits were rough cut and perfectly cooked- they made standard grit bowls seem so bland in comparison. The Whole Farm was such a neat idea- stack waffles, sausage, and bacon into a handheld sandwich and you have a winner- especially when each of the individual elements is good on its own. They don't sell sodas as a nod to healthy living and the food was so good I didn't even mind the caffeine headache from no Diet Coke! Social House is a new favorite, for sure, and I owe a big thanks to Urbanspoon for introducing me to this place.

Total Dissappointment
by marissa (12 reviews)
September 06, 2009
I'm a proud foodie. I truly enjoy trying new spots and uniquely combined cuisine. So with that said I was totally dissappointed with the food as well as the experience at the Social House. My girlfriend and I preserved our appetites (sitting through Sunday morning worship with grumbling stomachs) so that we could partake in a larger sample of the menu. We arrived to find a few small groups waiting to be seated, we sign in and find a seat, after waiting 20 mins my girlfriend asked the host (referred to by the waitstaff as Magoo) what our remaining wait time would be? He rolled his eyes, sighs in disgust and says "We don't do wait times here..." (his behavior warranted leaving but we were too hungry) An on looking waitress (Tammy) saw our frustration and made him aware of an open table, so we were seated . The restaraunt is quaint (almost too quaint) and is cutely decorated. I ordered the bread pudding and my girlfriend the salmon hash, with a side of sweet potato pancakes and grits. Thank goodness Tammy was our waitress she was pleasant and knowledgeable about the menu. The salmon hash was ok (needed a little seasoning and the fish wasn't fully cooked) as well were the grits. They were out of the pancakes, the bread pudding was odd and lacked cohesiveness. The shrimp helped to make sense of the dish but there were only 4 of them. ( 4 decent bites of food). As we tried to enjoy the food, we felt like we were in a sauna. There is no circulation throughout the restaraunt making the air stale and luke warm. The waitress was attentive she recognized that our entire experience was a flop so she adjusted our bill. What a let down...
