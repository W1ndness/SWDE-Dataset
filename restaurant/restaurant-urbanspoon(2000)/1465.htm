Message from Pappadeaux Seafood Kitchen

Freshly prepared Louisiana style seafood at its best

  • Signature Louisiana Style Dishes
  • Unique Cajun Style Cooking
  • Varied Specialty Drink Menu

Pappadeaux Seafood Kitchen offers the best Louisiana style Seafood dishes with a deep Cajun influence for discerning Seafood lovers. Only the freshest ingredients are used in recipes that have been mastered over the years and are unique to our restaurants. Diners keep coming back for favorites like; Texas Gulf Coast Oysters, Blackened Mahi, Seared Sea Bass, Seafood Gumbo, Live Main Lobster and Alaskan King Crab. The fun and festive ambiance welcomes patrons upon entering the restaurant and complements dishes typical to Louisiana. Signature Louisiana dishes include: Crawfish Ettouffee, Shrimp Creole, Mixed Seafood Grill and Blackened Opelousas Filet. Start with one of our specialty drinks and mouthwatering appetizers and you?re off to an unforgettable meal. For superbly cooked Louisiana style Seafood, stop by Pappadeaux Seafood Kitchen today.

Decent Food - High Cost
by D T (20 reviews)
January 26, 2010 - Likes it
I should have known that the dinner bill was going to be large when I was hit up at the bar for over $15 for a pint of domestic draft beer and a glass of Kendell Jackson Chardonnay (1 glass costing as much as a full bottle at the beverage warehouse).
For the first time in my life I saw a 5 pound lobster.... when it arrived by surprise on my companions plate! It was quite a shock, especially since the order was for a 'Maine Lobster'. Turns out it was the only lobster left in the tank and rather than asking if we'd like to switch to a different entree were brought the HUGE lobster - although I was only charged 1/2 price for it - $49.99. I would think that a waiter would have brains enough to ask if I wanted to pay $50 for a sea cockroach....
That being said, most of the lobster came home for a sunday afternoon meal (for two).
The crawfish etouffee was excellent, as was the lobster bisque. $4 for burned garlic bread was a disapointment but they specialze in seafood, not bread.
The desserts are huge (as is the price) so keep it in mind as you order.
Total tab for the lobster, crawfish, bread, 1 dessert, 2 bar drinks and 2 non -alcoholic drinks with tip: $125
