The Best Sushi in Richmond
by RVA Gastronome (44 reviews)
March 26, 2010 - Really likes it
I cannot believe how many times I drove past this place over the last few years and didn't go in to try it. It's one of those places I kept meaning to go to, but I just kept forgetting it was there. How stupid of me.
Finally, about 2 months ago, I walked into this treasure. I sat at the sushi bar and delighted in some of the best sushi I've had on the east coast. What's more, it's the only place I've found in Central Virginia that has fresh wasabi!!!!! Yes, FRESH. Meaning ground on a sharkskin grater from the root itself, NOT reconstituted powder. Yes, you pay for it, but you can definitely tell the difference.
The service is always excellent and they tend to be able to get their hands on stuff that's somewhat out of the ordinary for the area.
For real sushi fans, Umi is the alpha and omega in Central Virginia.

Beautiful decor, substandard food
by MuadDave (24 reviews)
May 09, 2010 - Doesn't like it
My wife and I tried Umi Sushi Bistro for my birthday dinner, and we'll likely chose another sushi spot the next time I have a hankering.
The decor and ambiance were wonderful; the place was tastefully and artistically appointed, but the food was only mediocre.
I had the sushi deluxe combo sampler which comes with assorted sushi and a spicy tuna roll. It did not come with miso soup or a salad as is typical in other sushi restaurants. I also ordered an eel/cucumber roll a la carte. The eel roll was very tasty, although a little on the puny side. The texture of the tuna in the spicy tuna roll was exceedingly mushy - distastefully so. I realize that the tuna was not a single monolithic piece since it was chopped and mixed with the spicy sauce, but there's a lot of ground between chopped-yet-firm and mushy.
The sushi itself was somewhat disappointing. The fish was sliced very long and thin, which made it difficult to eat and I think was an attempt to make it look larger than it was. The rice was similarly shaped and equally small in size, which made the sushi pieces resemble pale Slim-Jims riding rice instead of a generous portion of fish on an equivalent portion of rice.
The texture of the sushi was also a bit soft, as if it wasn't the freshest or perhaps not as cold as it should've been when cut.
My wife had the chicken teriyaki, which was average.
The bright spot of the evening was the dessert - we had the banana tempura with ice cream. It was wonderful - very lightly battered banana slices cut on the bias, drizzled with honey, and served with a very nice (possibly French) vanilla ice cream and whipped cream in the center. The flavors were sublime and worked well together.
Despite the excellent dessert, the quality of the entrees did not justify the final bill, which was significantly more than average.

You me at umi?
by Edward (16 reviews)
November 02, 2009 - Really likes it
The specialty rolls are worth the trip if you "roll" that way. Special shout out to my "Spicy Girl" roll. At $17 a pop it is a bit much, but in my little mind, worth it. For the semi-cost conscious, go with the Godzilla roll. Otherwise, the lunch boxes are a good deal and I recommend anyone try the place for lunch first if they just want to check it out. I believe Umi could be a real force in Short Pump, but they have a lot of competition in this sushi saturated area. I really really wish they could have some "happy hour half price rolls" or some creative marketing to get people in the door, but they aren't asking me.
june 2010 update -- sampled some blackened tuna and seared white tuna yesterday... AMAZING!!!
