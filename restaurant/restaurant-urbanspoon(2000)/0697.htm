Message from True Thai

Cedar-Riverside restaurant is famous for serving up authentic and delicious Thai cuisine - Newly Expanded!

  • Lunch buffet
  • Spiced to your preference
  • Dine-in and takeout

'One of the top 50 restaurant in the Twin Cities' The crispy catfish salad and the laab... the best I have eaten on either side of the Pacific Ocean.--Minneapolis-St. Paul Magazine

True Thai offers authentic Thai dishes filled with flavor and spiced according to your taste and preference. They feature food such as satay, curries, salads and noodle dishes like the classic pad thai. They are one block off of I-94 (Riverside Exit) on the Southwest corner of 27th and Franklin Avenues. They are close to both downtown Minneapolis and downtown St. Paul, the University of Minnesota, and is easily accessible to the entire metropolitan area.

Change of Heart
by BizzNessMPLS (127 reviews)
March 09, 2009 - Really likes it
Initially, I did not care for True Thai. I found nothing special about my pad thai, and the stir fry that my wife had also was mediocre. A couple of weeks ago, we were hungry for Thai, so we reviewed a few menus, and decided to give True Thai another shot as it is pretty close to our house. That turned out to be a really good idea. We started with the egg rolls and wonton wrapped shrimp. Both were good, not the best egg roll in the world as the menu boasts, but good. I ordered the Crispy Thai Potatoes with Beef listed on their new items menu. The potatoes were sliced paper thin and fried twice to give a real nice crispy texture. The sauce was described as sweet and sassy, which it certainly was, but I could have used a little more sass as I like my food spicy. The pad thai that my wife ordered seemed to have been improved because I had no complaints, maybe a little short on the chicken, but not terrible. I would say that the Thai Potatoes were the star had it not been for the Sriracha Fried Rice. At first bite, you get the heat of the Sriracha then the flavors or the fried rice. It is packed full of fresh vegetables. Definitely have a drink near by as the heat tends to build. Not so much that you cannot enjoy your food, but you can feel it. We enjoyed this meal so much that we were back a week later for take out, this time opting for the Original Thai Spring Rolls instead of the egg rolls. Either way, you cannot lose.

Great food
by Mark Gisleson (1 review)
August 17, 2009 - Likes it
Great Thai food but unlike most Thai restaurants they also have a limited but very good beer and wine selection. I'd stick with the beer for all but the seafood dishes. Extraordinary curries, very good spring rolls, and good Pad Thai. Other recommendations would include basil-seared salmon, laab, Tom Yum, lemon chicken soup, and, for kids, the sweet and sour (the owner donated the recipe to the St Paul Schools because it gets kids to eat their vegetables). Anna, the owner, is very personable and has a blog you can get to from the restaurant website. A few TC Thai restaurants may be as good, but none are better.
