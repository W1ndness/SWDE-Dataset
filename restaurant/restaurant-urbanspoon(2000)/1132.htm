Don't waste your money if you want GOOD SUSHI!
by MsJ (1 review)
July 04, 2010
Hello. I went with a party of 7 two weeks ago.
I am Japanese. I went with 6 of my friends who are Korean, white, Japanese and Latin.
We got there at 5:15pm to avoid the crowd.
We ordered 6 Steak Terriyaki, 1 Shrimp Tempura Udon and almost all of their rolls on their menu except for 1.
We were ALL disappointed. The food was not good at all. I cant say horrible because it was eatable but it is nothing special or good. First of all Steak teriyaki tasted like korean bulgogi except they put this teriyaki sauce on the top, same kind found at costco store. The meat was in small thin slices. It was salty. They marinated the meat korean style then put this costco bought teriyaki sauce on top to make it taste like Japanese. Very disappointing. This is NOT a steak teriyaki. It's not even a steak. It was just shredded beef...believe me not from a steak. One person ordered Shrimp tempura Udon. All Japanese restaurants gives you 3 shrimps in their Udon. There was only ONE and few other vegies. Did we not say we wanted SHRIMP TEMPURA UDON?
The broth was not made like it was suppose to from Dashi. From the taste of it it was made with soy sauce and sugar. I have never tasted or ever went to a restaurant where a UDON soup tasted so sweet and soy saucey...LOL The sushi.
OH MY GOSH.....HORRIBLE HORRIBLE. This we couldn't finish.....because it wasn't good.
I think this place is the WORST compare to all of the other Japanese restaurants in San Antonio.
The sushi rice was not made right.
The rice was too sticky, falling apart and sticking to the plate. There was MOSTLY rice in a sushi roll...Therefore it was BIG. Perhaps thats why people like it there. But people, why do you want to pay good money on ONLY rice? Honestly, that was what I saw and ONLY tasted. RICE. On a rainbow roll...the fish was very thin and dry.
The service was OK. Not horrible just ok.....
Oh another thing....if you want to go there to get sushi and want Low sodium soy sauce. Good luck.
They put normal soy sauce in their low sodium soy sauce bottles. How do I know this? I tried the low sodium....it was so salty. So I asked for another bottle. It was the same. So we all tried the red normal bottle and the Green "so called" low sodium bottle. They tasted EXACTLY the same.
I can tell they were not Japanese. They were Korean and had Korean music on...which is fine...but if a Korean wants to make Japanese food don't you think they should make like the way its suppose to?
I will never go there or will I ever recommend them.
It was SALTY, Not made correctly, fish was not fresh, and the worst bland rolls I have ever eaten in San Antonio.

hm...undecided
by rdsoxChick (3 reviews)
January 30, 2010 - Likes it
My husband and I went to Godai about a week ago. We arrived and the place was pretty packed, though we mustve showed up at the right time because we didnt have to wait for a table. Though those after us certainly had quite the wait time. Our server was very friendly and we decided on an appetizer called crab spring rolls. Essentially they were spicy crab meat and rice, fried in an egg roll type wrap. They were delicious. But we could have held off because they serve you miso soup and a small asian type salad. Both of which were very tasty. We ordered a few rolls (not knowing how big they are) and off the top of my head cant remember what they were called. I know we ended up getting the heart attack roll, which we didnt order but got for free because the chef accidentally added it to our plate. The rolls were pretty good. The only down side to the rolls, in my opinion, is that there was a lot of rice. It makes them big but you kind of lose the taste of the fish and yummy sauces that accompany some of them. But all in all was very satisfied with our dining experience.
