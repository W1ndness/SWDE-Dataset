Great food, terrible experience
by cacchio21 (5 reviews)
May 02, 2010 - Doesn't like it
Magnolia lived up to my expectations - excellent food with a terrible attitude. We were seated half an hour after our reservation, even though there were at least half a dozen empty tables. When I asked why we were having to stand in the bar (there are about 4 places to sit at the bar, all of which were taken by walk-in diners), we were told that they had to wait for the kitchen to catch up before we could be seated. Sure enough, we were seated at one of the tables that had been empty from the moment we got there - we just had to wait 30 minutes to sit. We were never offered a drink or any apology. The general attitude there is that they are the best and guests should be honored to eat there. The food is very good, without question. Sadly, their complete disregard for customer service ensures that we will not return. There are way too many restaurants in Durham that offer excellent food with friendly service to waste my time and money being miserable.

Best meal I've had in a while
by Katie (19 reviews)
December 29, 2009 - Really likes it
Was in Research Triangle for a three-day weekend and had to try this place (heard it was highly rated by several food magazines). There seem to be a lot of negative reviews, yet my experience was quite lovely. Walked in without reservations early on Saturday evening and was seated immediately. Our server was attentive and friendly and gave great recommendations (especially the wine!). Started with the pork belly - it was wonderfully crispy and practically melted in my mouth. I had the venison with pomegranate reduction and black pepper spaetzle - the meat was perfectly cooked and had great flavor. My sister's pork chop was also excellent. Finished with lemon souffle cake, which was nice and light considering the heavy meal we just had! No complaints - it's expensive but not over-the-top, especially for the quality of the food. I would definitely recommend this place for anyone who wants a special night out.

Biggest Letdown...
by Russ Jones (14 reviews)
January 06, 2009 - Doesn't like it
It has been a very long time since my wife and I went to Magnolia Grill so, unfortunately, I cannot be very detailed. We first went to Magnolia Grill upon the recommendations of several sources, none of which or whom were from Durham. I cannot remember if it came from guides or magazines or where, exactly, but not from the personal recommendations of trusted foodies.
We were excited though. Having recently been to Il Palio and discovering a truly fantastic restaurant in our midst, we had come to believe that Magnolia Grill was going to be something incredible as well.
Normally I give the caveat here that I have only been to this restaurant once so I am not sure I can make a good assessment. However, I feel pretty strong about this one.
1. Service. Most likely because my wife and I were younger at the time (early 20s), we were treated second-hand. I suppose it is uncommon for college kids to drop as much money on a nice dinner as we do from time to time, but regardless we deserve the attention of our wait-staff.
2. Food. Possibly tied to the service, the food was both not prepared to our specifications and was unquestionably bland and flavorless. I rarely fault a restaurant for going after eclectic flavors - I always fault a restaurant for being afraid of taking risks.
3. Price. Once again, this is relative. I would happily drop a couple hundred on an amazing dinner - but I would have had a much better meal at any number of far cheaper restaurants in the area.
Hopefully other people have had different experiences and maybe, when I have run out of other options, I will return.
