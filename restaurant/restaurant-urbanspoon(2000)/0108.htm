by CRog (176 reviews)
August 08, 2009 - Likes it
Atmosphere, atmosphere, atmosphere--this place is beautiful, and the food is above average. I'm always disappointed. That they won't stuff a pablano pepper with cicken for me, like other more accomodating Mexican or Tex-Mex places in town. It would seem that a chicken rellano would be easy, but obviously difficult to do. However the other entered are good, better than most other places and the decor is worth the visit--someone spent mega pesos here-- it's really impressive. Also don't overlook the black salsa I don't know what's in it but the flavor is superior to regular tomato salsa. The lunch menu is great there are plenty of reasonable choices on that menu. Try the roasted quail off the pollo section of the menu--you don't see that everywhere and it's a treat. There are always more native Spanish speakers than gringos so I take that as a good sign. The service is good--very enjoyable upscale Mexican for the south part of Hoover--good deal.

Great atmosphere, good food
by Unforced Error (6 reviews)
August 30, 2009
I recently revisited Iguana Grill and was pleasantly surprised to see that it had expanded, with the wall mural now even more detailed and interesting.
This has to be one of the most beautiful restaurants in Birmingham. The light fixtures are stunning!
As for the food, I adore the chipotle salsa. However, the dishes themselves are somewhat bland, although better than average. I usually get the fajitas, which are fine but unspectacular and devoid of guacamole. We saw a multi-person menu item delivered to another table called "molcajete" or "stone dish", with sirloin steak, chorizo and pork chops with green onions, grilled cactus and jalapenos served in a stone bowl. It looked amazing and we will try it next time.
All in all, the atmosphere is just superb. Enjoy the chipotle salsa and season your food and you'll have a very nice dining experience.
