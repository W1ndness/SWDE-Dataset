Burgergasm R' Us!
by Christopher Pineau (8 reviews)
December 15, 2009 - Likes it
I had the pleasure of experiencing this place this past weekend when some friends of mine and I were en route to an all day gaming event that would require some serious sustenance to keep us going. So after reading about this place on Texas Burger Guy's very amusing site, we made the pilgrimage to this little literal shack tucked away in the corner--not the easiest place to get to, but this was a minor issue.
On the outside it was literally what the name described, a little run-down shack covered with all manner of Texas & Houston-specific memorabilia. Inside was more of the same; a few dark wood tables indicated that take out is the place's greatest strength due to the lack of space--it is tiny, did I mention? This is a very minor issue, again.
The very pretty young woman who took our orders at the counter was also very friendly and sociable in an unaffected, down to earth manner. This bode well and shows that this really is a family-owned business, the kind of business where you get actual friendly service not dictated by an oppressive company mandate that demands obnoxious faux happiness from its employees. She handled everything perfectly, too. My friends purchased a double beef cheeseburger and a chili dog on one's part, and a double buffalo cheeseburger with no bun on the the other's part (he's doing the low/no carb diet thing), and I bought a buffalo double cheeseburger for myself along with their homemade jalapeno potato salad.
WHOA.
The title of this review was coined by one of the other guys at the gaming tournament I was at whilst watching me scarfing this burger. "Scarfing" is not accurate, though; I was lovingly savoring each bite and taking my time. These are proper burgers! The inner layer of wax paper beneath the foil they were wrapped in was saturated with grease and meat juice, and sticky with cheese, and the patties themselves were cooked well-done--as pinche Houston health codes dictate--but still surprisingly moist and tasty. The wheat bun I got it with was not a dry prefab one, either, I could tell it was homemade as well, it had flavor that you don't always get in a bun. The condiments were applied in the absolute perfect amount to keep the burger together and not gush everywhere on every bite. Every bit brought home the very simple fact that there is a reason Bubba's has been around for some time in Houston, and the flavor, texture, and overall quality of this burger showed me why. The jalapeno potato salad was rather good, as well, with just enough jalapeno for an appreciable burn. My friend fell over raving over his burger and the chili dog in particular earned high praise from him as well, and he is an even more demanding foodie than I am.
In short, this little place is well worth the hunt to find it. Reasonably priced, too; I got out for less than $10 with both burger and potato salad, and I was immensely pleased in general. While certain fast food establishments will do in a pinch, this is now my burger mecca until further notice. Seek and ye shall find, burger mavens!
