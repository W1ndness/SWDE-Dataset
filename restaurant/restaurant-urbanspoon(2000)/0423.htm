Gluttons Beware
by PickledPinkish (21 reviews)
June 20, 2010 - Likes it
Buffets, Buffets. Like one nights stands, so tempting and exciting during the action, they leave you feeling cheap and dirty in the end.
If you love sushi and have less discriminating taste, by all means this is an experience you must have. It's a great place to try out different types of fish if you've never tried it before and don't want to pay $3 per slice of sashimi. (From here, I learned that I love mackerel and shy away from basic albacore tuna.) However, the fish isn't too fresh, the rolls aren't very innovative, and the kitchen dishes are not worth stomach space.
It is a very fun place to go with a group of like-minded, fish fanatics. So, maybe just once, skip lunch, go for dinner and pig out.

For the serious sushi lover!
by Henry (1 review)
January 12, 2010 - Likes it
Small. Crowded. Frequent long lines. No fancy decor either, just a serious sushi haven for serious sushi lovers. Eat what you want from their extensive menu (they'll even make items to order) but eat all you request. If you don't like sushi, there are other foods to choose from. The presentation is beautiful. The sushi is wonderful. There's something here for everyone, so you shouldn't go here... no, you should save a table for me, because I've become a serious fan!
