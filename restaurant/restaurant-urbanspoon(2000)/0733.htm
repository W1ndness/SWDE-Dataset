New Orleans Brunch
by Stinky (196 reviews)
October 17, 2009 - Likes it
If I am in town on a Sunday, we will go here, (or a couple of other choice places) for Brunch. But this is my favorite. Sitting in the Courtyard with a Mimosa and an Eggs Benedict from the hot bar and a little jazz playing in the corner is my idea of a perfect morning in New Orleans. Prices are a little high, but you get an amazing amount of food & drink, if you can eat it all, but it's worth it. Service is hoppin', but friendly . If you're in the quarter of a Sunday morning, give this a try for a taste of New Orleans.

A New Orleans Gem
by Chef Dare (167 reviews)
February 21, 2009 - Likes it
The Court of Two Sisters benefits from the most beautiful location in the city. Built around an interior courtyard, the large French windows look out on a vision of creole beauty. The menu is decidedly creole as well, with occasional twists and novel approaches. Everything is delicious and the service is always high end yet friendly. The prices are a tad bit steep, but then this is the kind of place where you should not come with an eye on your wallet. It is an experience and there is no better place in New Orleans for a romantic dinner that will leave you with lasting memories of the crescent city. Just a warning, however. Avoid the over-hyped "Jazz Brunch". The food is EXTREMELY average and your money would be better spent elsewhere! Even the excellent jazz can't save this brunch!
