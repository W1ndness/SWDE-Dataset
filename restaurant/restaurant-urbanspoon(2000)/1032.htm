Good Brat Experience
by BLKuenn (4 reviews)
June 04, 2009 - Likes it
German restaurants in NC are pretty scarce and I was pleased to hear about the Bavarian Brathaus. So, while in town on business I made a point to sample the wares.
I did think the prices were a little high, but the food quality and ambience were excellent (and, afterall, this is Cary).
The soup of the day (goulash) was more of a stew than a soup (not complaining). Loads of beef that was as tender as any pot roast I've ever had. The broth was complex and the portion was ample. The garlic bread was rye and that was a good compliment.
I then moved on to the two brat plate which comes with kraut and mashed potatoes. I had the traditional and the Fire. Good sized brats and the meat is very lean. Don't expect any Johnsonville filler here. The Fire wasn't as spice as I would have liked, but it was very good. The house mustard/horseradish sauce is nice and tangy (and very aromatic, don't overdo it).
I read on here where someone blasted the wait staff. Must have been a bad night. My waitress was excellent and I was well taken care of.
I will be back.

+ 1 more photos from Bernd Munzenmayer A Keeper!
by Bernd Munzenmayer (1 review)
August 01, 2009 - Really likes it
We had been looking for a German restaurant in the area since we moved here a year ago. Having lived in Germany and my wife a wonderful German cook, we thought we'd give it a try. The restaurant is located on the end of a shopping plaza around the corner from the Cary Hwy. Outdoor seating is under a large copula. Cooler days warrant a second visit. Staff was friendly and sat us at a center table. The room has no windows but is well lit with various German pottery, steins and decorations and a larger fire place in the center. The 3 in our party decided to share the appetizer but try different meals. We ordered the potato puffers/pancakes. They were served with apple sauce and sour cream. A perfect slight crispy crust with just the right amount of flavoring and slightly puffy inside. My wife make her's much thinner but really enjoyed them. The entrees were the Jaeger schnitzel, Rahm schnitzel and Sauer Braten. The Jaeger schnitzel was a nicely breaded, thin piece of veal that was perfectly cooked and the sauce was good but a little milder then I'm used to. The sides included spatzel (home made noodles) I liked them; my wife thought they had too much spice. The red cabbage was unexpected creamy, flavorful but a little too tart for me. Waiter said they used to be sweeter but some customers complained they were not tart enough. Go figure! My wife had the Sauerbraten with bread dumplings. Sauce was perfect and the meat was tender. The dumplings were also tasty. My mother had the Rahm (cream) schnitzel with spatzel and red cabbage. She enjoyed the spatzel but the same verdict on the red cabbage. I found the cream sauce better than the Jaeger sauce and would try that next time. My wife and I had the strudel with vanilla ice cream. Philo dough was thin and tasty but apple pieces were a little large. Mom had the Black Forest cake and enjoyed it. The portions served are well sized and flavorful. Service was a little slow but may have had more to do with preparation in the kitchen than the waiter. Our meals were fixed price but not too expensive. As it says over the door as we left, auf viedersehn and we will see the Bavarian Brathaus again.
