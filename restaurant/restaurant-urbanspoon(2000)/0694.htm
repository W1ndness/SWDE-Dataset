Simple delicious pizzas
by Dan and Roxanne (106 reviews)
August 07, 2009 -
It's all about Naples here. The pizzas are simple and delicious. They are approved by the Naples pizza police. Use imported ingredients from Naples to make the approved Neopolitan pizza. Fairly priced and the good quality beer and wine are reasonable too. Wood burning oven 800 degrees for 90 seconds and done. Good salads.

High Quality
by BizzNessMPLS (127 reviews)
April 15, 2009 - Really likes it
We have been to Punch countless times and have never been disappointed. This last time we ordered the Toscano (Sausage, roasted red pepper, basil, cracked red pepper), which is our standard. We almost always order this one because it is amazing. For the second pizza, we wanted to try something new, so it was the Palermo (Spiced salami, sun dried tomato, saracene olive, oregano). This was another amazing choice. The salami was sliced paper thin and was deliciously salty. We typically do not like olives, but they added a really nice brininess. Instead of the standard mozzarella, we opted for the Buffalo mozzarella. It was creamy and had a nice chewy pull to it. Definitely worth the extra cost.

Sub Par Pizza?
by Fitgirl (11 reviews)
July 20, 2008 - Doesn't like it
While the smell was wonderful when I walked in to Punch, the question was would the pizza lead up to my expectations? I had heard this was the best pizza in the twin cities, and being an avid pizza maker myself I wondered if it was as delicious as my own creations. So I oredered up a Margherita pizza. When my Pizza arrived, the crust was quite a bit more burned than I would consider just a flavor addition from the oven. It was more like charred. Upon tasting the pizza it overwhelmed the other flavors. The toppings on the pizza had made it soggy, and they all seemed to slide off as I picked up my pieces. The staff at the restaurant were very courteous and also quick on bringing the order, but the sub par pizza (which was not as good as my home made stuff) made me not want to set foot in the door again. Was it an off day for the pizza maker? Who knows, but I am not going to find out any time soon.

Pizza Perfection
by Brad (3 reviews)
August 31, 2009 - Really likes it
This is my favorite, by a long shot, Twin Cities pizza place. In fact, it is my favorite thin crust pizzas amongst any that I have eaten in places such as Chicago, New York, and Italy. The crust is fantastic, and when fired in the super-hot oven, it leaves a perfect pebbled surface of flavor-enhancing charring. The simpler, the better, so my favorite is the Margarita with the perfect sauce, cheese, and basil. Or, go for the authentic D.O.C. version with buffalo mozzarella and imported tomatoes. Speedy service too, if you can get in.
