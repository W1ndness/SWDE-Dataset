Loved it!!
by marabeth (1 review)
May 23, 2010 - Likes it
My husband, 14 month old daughter and I went on a Sunday afternoon and had our choice of seats. The french-pressed stumptown coffee was fantastic. Even though there are no high chairs or booster seats it is a very family friendly place as the staff does not look at you like you're from Mars for bringing in however many children of whatever age you want! I don't understand why so many people have a problem with this, what is so awful about holding your child in your lap?? Maybe if my husband and I had triplets this would not have worked out but I doubt we'd be eating out very often in that case. We had the biscuits and gravy and coffee cake and all were exceptional specimens. Another problem people have with this restaurant seem to be the potatoes. Maybe we lucked out but I thought they were very good. They tasted like potato, gasp! They weren't your regular IHOP or Denny's hash brown swimming in whirl (those are good too) but were more like larger than normal home fries. We didn't even have to salt and pepper the biscuits and gravy like we normally do at other places. The waitress was very knowledgeable and was quick to refill our coffees and waters. The portion size and price were all very reasonable considering they don't just open cheap cans of stuff and put labor into each part of your dish. I will definitely go back again and again!

Just like home
by Rosebud (8 reviews)
April 03, 2009 - Likes it
I'll say first that the pancakes had a 'different' taste, but I think it was because of the spiralina flour, which I have not tried before. They were still very good, cooked to a nice golden brown. The eggs, scrambled, were done nicely with just the right about of herbs. I don't recall what my guest had, but the food was plentiful and greatly enjoyed. We found the music to be a bit loud. The place feels like you stepped off of the street into a kitchen. They have decorated with mostly recycled products. Doors act as the counter front, and various other recycled items are displayed throughout. Also there were only two employees there at the time service was quick, and the food was hot on the plate. What I saw of the lunch menu indicated a nice and unique variety. Cream of asparagus soup sounds worth trying. Easy to find, reasonable parking available. I'll be enjoying breakfast or lunch there again. Perhaps you'll give it a try as well.

Breafast was breakfast, but the Scone was to die for.
by Julrosec (1 review)
November 16, 2008
We went in on a Sunday morning and were there 10-15 minutes before the crowds started coming in, so we had our choice of seats. The staff was friendly and helpful. However, they did not have soy milk in the store (I thought this was a requirement of arty, trendy breakfast places in Portland, especially in SE), there was only one vegetarian entrée on the menu and it was infused with cheese.
The dishes we did order were good, but what really shone was the scone that came with them. I determined that while Arleta is not going to be on my breakfast outing rotation, they are going to be on my Bakery rotation -  Great baked goods. My scone was very fresh - moist on the inside with a good, light, crisp crust.
