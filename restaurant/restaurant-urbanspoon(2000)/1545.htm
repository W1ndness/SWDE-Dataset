Won't Go Back
by Kenzor (1 review)
May 22, 2010
Went to Luna Nueva on Thursday night with my daughter and her girl-friend. When we arrived around 8:00pm, there was one other table in the house, a two-top that shortly paid their check. We had the place to ourselves.
Looking around we noted the open insulation in the roof and a half-hearted attempt at decor with a fountain in the center of the room that is sadly, executed poorly. The salsa (red) is flavorless or (verde) overly sweet, same for the queso. Our waitress was inexperienced, but she was new so I give her a pass.
We ordered steak and chicken fajitas and a steak quesadilla--remember, we are the ONLY TABLE In the HOUSE--we drank our margaritas, ate the tasteless salsa and queso (we were straving!), and waited.. and waited... and waited. Finally, we heard a bell. Our heads turned toward the sound instintively, "is that our food?" Our waitress was hanging out behind the bar watching the manager/bartender make a drink. We waited. My daughter got up and politely inquired about our food; the waitress went to retrieve it.
The steak and chicken faijates were average, the quesadilla greasy, and the rice stale. Worst of all, the sour cream--which we PAID EXTRA FOR--was runny! All in all, a waste of my money.
Maybe they make their money on the after dinner crowd who come to listen to music (we didn't stay for the music, which was getting started as we left); if so, they should give up the kitchen. Good mexican food is plentiful in this city; Ponchos on Buford Highway and Nueva Laredo on Chattahoochee Avenue to name two.

Very Good, and Very Authentic, Mexican Food
by DrEnter (1 review)
July 06, 2009 - Likes it
Having only stumbled in on a quest for Mexican food which walking around Little-5, I wasn't expecting much. The first thing I noticed was the very authentic Mexico City ambiance, with the not too cool but not too hot interior, the indoor fountain, and the utilitarian decor. I was NOT expecting the food to be the most authentic Mexican I've had in Georgia, but it was.
This is not Americanized Mexican food! This is not Tex-Mex! Simple dishes that depend heavily on slow-cooked, well seasoned meats. Tacos made with cooked tortillas and no cheese. Thin but flavorful salsas. This is what Mexican food is.
Don't get me wrong, there is a lot to like in a good Tex-Mex meal, but if you think of places like Pappasitos when you think of Mexican food, don't bother. If what you want is delicious and authentic Mexican fare, try this place out. Do yourself a favor and try the steak or the lamb tacos, they are a nice place to start that should be approachable and recognizable and give you an idea of some of the differences you'll see in the rest of the menu.
I HIGHLY recommend it.
