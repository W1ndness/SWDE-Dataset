Fantastic
by y2Craig (26 reviews)
January 31, 2009 - Likes it
Great food at great prices. I have been back to this place numerous times and make an attempt to try something different each time.
Food is made promptly and generally served within ten to fifteen minutes. They never rush you out, so you generally enjoy the food and take time to talk and relax.
You get the basic tortillas and salsa to snack on before the meal. Although they have steak on the menu, I would stick with the various burritos and other Mexican food. Meals explode with flavor. They have the red and green habanero sauce if you like your dish to be more spicier. Not needed unless you enjoy turning the hot level to 11.
I will return here before going to a chain where you are just another number.
(4 spoons)
EDIT!!! (August 8, 2010):
The last time we ate here was in late December after the Trans Siberian Orchestra. One thing to note where they seat you, request a seat in the main eating area. If you get seated in one of the booths directly in front of where you walk in they tend to forget about you over there. We placed an order that took about 30 minutes or so to arrive! Another couple there I think waited a little longer and may have received our dish. They were a little disgruntled and walked out. We went through a couple baskets of nachos and drinks before our food was served. It was tasted great, as usual, but being forgotten or not getting the food that long is a major league fumble. Do not get those booths if possible and especially if you cannot be seen by their staff. Hopefully they are more alert to poor service for two different customers within a hour and corrected it.

More Authentic than most, and tasty to boot.
by Nora (12 reviews)
May 26, 2010 - Likes it
Let me start off by confirming that yes, their margaritas are vats of alcohol laden frosty goodness that are both cheap and delicious. Have one, or two for an appetizer and you seriously will neither remember nor care about what you ordered to eat. Just careful of the brain freeze, gets me every time.
The chips are fresh and crispy, as is all of the food. Not exactly authentic Mexican, but as far North as we are, the restaurant needs to cater to the cheese loving, lettuce stuffed stereotype of Mexican food Americans have.
The Tortilla Soup is the BEST on the menu and probably the BEST I have ever had. Fresh pulled chicken, sliced avocado, cilantro . . . amazing.
The Tacos, burritos, etc are also really very good, not a fan of the rice and beans though. The genius of Mexican food is that its only a select few ingredients recombined together and renamed. Point is, drinks are cheap, food is fresh, you will not leave hungry or dissatisfied.

Good food
by orion (1 review)
July 07, 2010
The menu is the same as Cinco de mayo on Union-which is my favorite mexican restaurant in the area. The owner/manager must have worked there and 'took' it.
The food, service and prices are great. Don't let the constantly empty looking parking lot fool you.
If I were to offer them advice: Re-design the parking lot to put the cars in front of the building to make it look more populated. An empty looking parking lot conveys an unsuccessful or a poor restaurant. Cars in front of the building will also cover up the dingy looking building and the fact that it still looks like a Denny's. Your interior is wonderful, but the way.
Have your staff park in front to make it look like there are many customers inside. Have people you know leave their cars there as well. Marketing!

Great Homemade Mexican Food
by Charles (4 reviews)
July 25, 2009 - Likes it
My wife and I go to El Palenque every six weeks or so, and have been well satisfied each time. The food is authentic and plentiful, the price is more than fair, the menu is varied, and the service is very good. Now, it is true some of the wait staff are somewhat brusque, but they are busy and in some cases a language barrier may exist; but we have found most wait staff are reasonably friendly, and all are efficient. This place is what Grandma Mora's -used- to be.
