Are you kidding me?
by Sarah (1 review)
July 14, 2010
I'm not sure what I'm more frustrated at... the size of the "8 piece roll", or the "your order will ready in 20 minutes". I called in my order at 12:07 which I can imagine is a very busy time, I was told the order would be ready in 20 minutes. I showed up early having underestimated the drive time from the Park... how great!! nearby sushi!!! Upon arriving at roughly 12:20, there was question about my order of maguro vs. hamachi. REALLY??? Aren't you the sushi restaurant?? Shouldn't the person taking the order know that they are different fish??? I was told "it would a few minutes" and I paid. $ 33.00 for 2 orders of sashimi, and 4 rolls. After waiting 22 minutes, I told the front desk that I had to leave because I had a meeting. I had assumed quite erroneously that a sushi restaurant might be able to create 4 rolls, and 2 orders for sashimi in 40 minutes. After waiting another 6 minutes, my order was finally ready, I was late for my meeting and management didn't even apologize. The himachi had a wierd texture, the soy sauce was even more wierd and the "8 piece rolls" should have been 4 piece rolls. I know that being a California transplant makes me a sushi snob but the truth is I was more bothered by long it took and that no one called to clarify my order since they had my cell phone. I am not likely to return and certainly won't refer anyone to Akan Sushi.

Could it get any better?
by Aaron Bradshaw (1 review)
June 11, 2009 - Likes it
Akashi has half priced sushi all the time plus a friendly staff who now remembers my name and drink order. We went for Valentines a few months back and they even served a free dessert (along with the half priced sushi!).
I have tried both the sushi and entrees and both are delicious and are reasonably priced. Be aware that it can be crowded at lunch but I have always been able to call ahead with a large group for an early or later lunch. At dinner it is quieter though. Everybody that I have taken to this restaurant has begged me to go back everytime we go out.

Best sushi in the Triangle
by MA Cook (1 review)
November 21, 2009 - Likes it
After trying a number of sushi restaurants, including popular ones southern Durham, we stumbled onto Akashi and have been loyal customers ever since. The sushi is amazing. We've tried a number rolls on the menu and have yet to stumble on a bad one. And we've also come to love the Hibachi items, the steak has become a personal favorite. The staff is friendly and very helpful. It's a great place to go to sit and talk with friends. We highly recommend it.
