+ 2 more photos from Barney Fantastic Bakery and Stromboli!
by Barney (485 reviews)
March 23, 2009 - Likes it
Okay, may be a little odd combination, but this place has an impressive bakery and great pizza items (disclaimer, I had the stromboli... which has all the pizza piece parts, but I can't technically comment on the pizza).
First the bakery. Lots and lots of really impressive cakes, tarts, cookies, etc. I sampled a number of them (must have been a half a dozen sample plates out), all very good, with the exception of the cannoli, which was absolutely fantastic!
As far as the pizza side of the menu, I'd heard rave reviews about the Italian Stromboli, so of course I had to get it. The small is not cheap ($10 or so), but probably will serve a couple of folks as advertised. Great slightly charred tasty crust done just right. Inside was a molten mix of cheeses and what seemed like high quality Italian deli meets (I can say that because, even through the cheese, I could taste the meat and taste a fair amount of flavor... not just salt). This came with a little cup of marinara that would have probably been a respectable sauce all on its own... nice amount of herbs and a very fresh tomato flavor without any sweetness. I would have to agree with others here, this is one very respectable stromboli and better than most around this town.
The one disclaimer is that this is really a carryout place. While they have some concrete benches out front, I'm not sure they really work for anything other than grabbing a quick slice.

Great Place for Pizza and Dessert
by Joe (1 review)
October 13, 2009 - Likes it
Around 6 or 7 months ago I discovered Alpine Bakery and Pizzeria and I have been addicted ever since. I have slowly made my way through most of the cakes, cookies, pastries, and the pizza side of the menu (a large feat in that amount of time given the expansive number of items).
The bakery side is a large array of some of the best dessert options available. The cakes, especially the red velvet, coconut creme, German chocolate, and carrot, are simply delicious, filling, and well worth the price. Alpine also holds a large selection of cookies and pastries, all of which are extraordinary.
The pizza side is blissfully simple, yet delicious. I have worked my way through the pizza menu, everything from the regular with homemade sauce, the thick crust Sicilian, and my personal favorite, the white pizza (which recently added a mixture of Ricotta, Romano, garlic, and fresh parsley). The lasagna is flawless and decadent, with high layers of fresh meats and cheeses, and complimented well by Alpine's Ceasar salad. The antipasto is perfect for a get-together, also incorporating fresh meats and cheeses.
Other reviewers have complained of the customer service but I have yet to receive bad service. I have noticed the bakery has employed a few new members, but once again, I have yet to receive bad service. The pizza guys are knowledgeable and helpful on their product and continue to serve great pizza, calzones, stromblolis, etc. with great service. Overall, Alpine can not be missed.
