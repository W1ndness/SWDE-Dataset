Not the best...
by Anita (6 reviews)
December 27, 2008 - Doesn't like it
Sebastian's seems to have a loyal following. I have to admit as a younger westsider, I enjoyed my fair share of Sebastian's. The last time I went, it was only mediocre, especially the tsatziki sauce-- thin and lacking in any cucumbers and/or seasoning. The last two times I've been there, the staff has said derogatory things about customers to those remaining as they have left the store I felt those comments were offensive and racist. The first time I was sure I heard it wrong; the second time there was no question about it. A lot of people may think they have good food, but they don't value all their paying customers equally... I'll never go back-- even if it was the best gyro sandwich in Cincinnati.

My favorite sandwich spot - Sebastian's
by FrankP (3 reviews)
January 20, 2009 - Likes it
I've been going to Sebastian's for years now… and they are the only ones that know how to make yummy gyros and authentic tzatziki (I probably spelled that wrong). I disagree with the post below about it being "thin." So many other imitation gyro places load their sauce with sour cream (not a very "Greek" thing to do). This place has the greatest gyros in town, and they've been around the longest too. I recommend it. - Frank
