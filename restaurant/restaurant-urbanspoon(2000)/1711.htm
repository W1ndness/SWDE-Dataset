Shhhhh stop telling everybody
by Fred Murtz (41 reviews)
October 04, 2009 - Really likes it
This place has amazing soul food! If you've ever eaten at Gladys Nights place, you know what to expect. The chicken and waffles is a must try. The waffle is malty and sweet, the chicken is huge crispy and delicious. There is a wait if you come at peak dinner hour, so be warned. I've been here 5-6 times and have tried the other dishes, from friends plates, but can't pass ordering the chicken and waffles everytime! The sides are amazing, the cornbread is like my mommas with tons of pieces of corn and sweet. I am so stuffed, that I have never made it to the dessert menu which looks awesome. Next time, I'm doing dessert for carry-out. The interior reminds you of an ante-bellum house in Georgia, yet the location is in a strip mall.... Go figure! Stop telling everybody, it's crowded enough already!

GREAT GREAT FOOD AND PLACE
by foodiegirl (4 reviews)
May 20, 2010 - Really likes it
I have read many reviews on this place and tried it out last night. It is located in one of the newer strip malls in Owings Mills but parking was plentifula s it is at the end. The ding room is small with booths all around and a view of the lot . The server (Devron) was helpful, professional, polite, gracious and attentive. I was pleased. the runner from the kitchen that brought the food out was very funny and informative as well--didnt get her name. NOW the FOOD !!! I had waffles with fried catfish, spinach and sweet potato fries which also came with a small caesar salad. My drink was lemonade iced tea mix with brown sugar and the "bread" was cornbread with corn baked into the batter. The food was DIVINE !!! I have never in my life tasted catfish fried so perfectly. The portions were huge . I had enough food left to fill 2 to go containers. I am eating some for lunch now. Everythign was delicious. My friends had the cheeseburger meatloaf and fried tilapia with 2 sides and salad. And one had chicken soup with rice as an appetizer. We found everything perfectly spiced and fresh . One of the reviews noted that their veggies were overcooked. I will note that in "SOUL FOOD" or "SOUTHERN" style food --this is how vegetables are cooked. Until they are dead ! LOL. Part of this style of cooking involves slow stewing vegetables until they are wilted and then adding flavor. This is the case. To an untrained pallet -you will be confused. The sides were perfectly cooked and seasoned for soul food. No crisp vegetables here. The catfish was crispy and had no soggy pieces(which catfish tends to have as it is moist) it was perfect and a very large fillet that filled half of my square plate. The sweet potato fries were seasoned with butter and cinnamon/brown sugar and came out crisp and hot and on their own plate. They were absolutely unlike any sweet potato fries I have ever tasted and it was clever to spice them that way as it is a twist on stewed or baked sweet potatoes which is a soul food/southern staple. Very unique to make them fries and sweet to boot. The spinach was very buttery with minced garlic and tasted stewed or very sauteed but good. the waffles came out in small rounded triangles next to the catfish. I liked seperate -other places serve the meat on top of the waffles but it was more sophisticated on the plate on the side. All in all I will go back as it was a wonderful experience. The only negative for me was the parking lot view and the very small dining room. The ambiance was pleasant though and reminded me of a small restaturant in the NYC theatre district. Very cozy and open with soft soul music classics playing. It is set up as a small fine dinign establishment and it is surprising to step outside and look around at the strip mall again. the prices for a full dinner range from $14-$27. The dinners come with the meat, 2 sides, and a small salad . The portions are so huge that I found the prices more than adequate. The drinks were about $4 each but with unlimited refills was worth the cost. The cornbread on the table is free and delicious and unlimited. Desserts were about $6 but we were all so overstuffed that 2 of us passed and the 3rd had 2 scoops of ice cream which was well, ice cream. I believe with the review I read, Baltimore(city (to an extent)and suburbs)are accustomed to carry-out food and casual dining as there is a plethora of Famous Dave's , TGIFridays, RedRobin, etc. in the area. This cozy place is an example of soul food presented in a fine dining atmosphere-despite the location. I mean the waiters had white shirts , black slacks and dress shoes and were groomed ! I kept expecting to look up and see a piano player somewhere. So, tha said , the prices reflect well . I encourage people to try this for themselves and not make judgments based on those who may not be used to what they are trying to do here. Also, didnt seem kid friendly--is more of an adult date spot or small quiet celebration. So leave the kids at home and enjoy a date night or girls or guys night out !!! There was one baby there with a couple last night but it was a tiny baby and only squealed once--wasn't of food eating age. Good job Granny's. See youa again soon

I was a little aprehensive about visiting here because I heard mixed reviews about Granny's but I'm glad I went for my self. They had a small, but classy, dining room. I'm assuming they fill up the booths before they use the tables which I prefer. The seating was very fast, then again it was Saturday afternoon, a few hours before the dinner rush. I've heard the get pretty busy. Anyway, the wings were fairly mild even though we asked for medium but that was ok because they were HUGE! Not to be considered wings at all. Oh, I forgot, before the appetizer we were serve a small piece of cornbread, tasted more like cake to me, along with some cold butter that melted from the heat.... So good. I ordered breakfast for the main course since it was still early in the day and it was really good. I got the buttermilk pancakes with bacon, "breakfast potatos", and apple wedges. The apple wedges were kinda gross and runny but everything else was delicious. So overall the service and food were good. Don't knock it till you tried it. And be ready to take some food home, they never let you leave hungry!
