Good but not worth a special trip
by David (8 reviews)
January 25, 2009 - Likes it
These are good fries, don't get me wrong - this is why I rated this as liking it.  I love that you can walk in and see the freshly cut potatoes sitting right next to the fryer.  The choice of sauces is interesting (maybe I should have gone beyond the traditional European mayo).  However, as good as these fries are, they are nothing like what I've had in Europe, and they're nothing like Belgian pommes frites I've had elsewhere.  These are the size of steak fries.  Also, I found a lot of them somewhat soggy, despite being twice fried.  The smaller ones in the bunch were perfectly crispy, though.  In hindsight, I think I should have tried one of the myriad special sauces instead of the plain mayo.  This is a neat place, the fries are indeed good, but you can find better elsewhere (try Five Guys, now in NYC), and I don't feel it's worthy of a special trip to this "destination restaurant," unless you happen to be in the area and are in need of a snack.
