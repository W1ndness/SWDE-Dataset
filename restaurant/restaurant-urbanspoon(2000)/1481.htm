Bland, loud and overpriced
by Wes (152 reviews)
June 27, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I hate to leave negative reviews of places, but this one was so bad it truly deserves it. I had such high expectations going in, only to be disappointed in every way. For starters, they are not solo diner friendly. If you do go by yourself, expect a $25-30 meal at a minimum. I left the first time I went by, but decided to try again last night with a friend, which was a mistake. Having dined there now, the ambiance really reminds me of a low quality restaurant, like a Pizza Hut, etc. The acoustics were so bad that my friend and I almost had to scream at each other because it was so loud. Our server was incredibly dim and inattentive. It took forever to get our salad, and then when we asked for more bread, he came back by a few minutes later empty handed and asked if we needed anything else. The small Caesar salad was gigantic, but the taste was not: I have had better Caesar salad from a salad bar. We also ordered the Quattro al Forno, which had 4 different pasta items in shells, none of which was that remarkable. My friend and I normally enjoy talking for a few minutes after a meal, but we were both so frazzled from all the noise, we left right away. There are other family style Italian places in Atlanta that are far superior to this one, and don't make you take a 10 mile tour of the restaurant before you sit down. Will not be returning.

Really depends
by fishsticks64 (20 reviews)
May 01, 2009
I went to this restaurant on a Friday night with seven other middle aged ladies. (Oh gosh - did I call myself middle aged and a lady in the same sentence? It's all downhill.)
I didn't pick it. I never would have picked a chain restaurant so that will give you some idea about my taste, in general.
If I'm in the mood, I actually enjoy family style restaurants as you get to try several items (if you're part of a large enough group.) But then, the food has to be GOOD!
The place was sufficiently full and we were seated in a very appropriate booth with high walls so the noise factor was minimized (and a few of the women I was with were LOUD anyway so that probably helped all around.)
The waitress was a young girl who seemed to have memorized a script (which she relayed to us in a sing song inflected voice, after the typical, "Have you ever been to Buca di Beppo before?") She was relatively ineffective (more to come.) The staff here needs additional training and some more professionalism. The manager also needs to buy some more silverware. We were twice without forks and when we requested them, they came out in the dishwasher rack and were dried next to us. TACKY!
We ordered a small mixed antipasto, a large caprese salad, large pasta sampler, a small chicken/lemon/capers dish and small stuffed chicken/prosciutto dish. The antipasto was fine, the caprese was missing the balsamic (so the somewhat unripe tomatoes were sitting on a bed of (olive - one can only hope) oil with a basil leaf and slice of so-so mozzarella on top. Eeeh.
The lemon chicken and stuffed chicken were both fine but the small dishes were purported to feed 2 - 3 people and I thought the portions really more for 1 - 2. We even had to order another stuffed chicken dish (and one of the women in our party ordered and was only eating her own salad because of some dietary restrictions so we were only feeding seven with the shared dishes.)
The stuffed pasta dish was typical although a few in our party argued over whether the stuffing was sausage or chicken. Those flavors should be discernable, don't you think?
Cheap chianti served tableside (cheap in quality but not in price.)
Desserts were large and fine. Again - nothing to rave about.
Coffee, served with dessert was the weakest I've ever had and we complained so then they made a new pot. What they then served was some of the worst tasting coffee ever -- as if they hadn't cleaned the machine in a very long time and were using a very low quality coffee.
For the quality and quantity of what was provided, I thought it somewhat overpriced.
Finally, back to the wait staff. We had a waitress who took our orders and then there were runners who delivered the food. The waitress was, for lack of a better word, pretty much 'absent'. We were there for a birthday dinner and it turns out that they actually provide a small birthday cake with candles and the staff sings. If you're lucky. In our case, we'd finished dessert before the waitress came over with a three armed candleabra and said, "Oh - they were supposed to bring this but the dessert came out too fast." We sang (just the girls in our party - no staff), she took the candleabra and left. She came back five minutes later with a to go box/bag saying, "Oh, and we provide a complimentary cake but your dessert came out too fast so I boxed this." ARE YOU KIDDING? They couldn't even get the timing on a small cake right.
This is not authentic Italian from any region. This is Americanized Italian made for the masses. It's not dining here; it's EATING!! Big time. Keep that in mind.

Good, but not great
by D T (20 reviews)
January 27, 2010 - Likes it
If you are into the famly sharing a large place of pasta thing, this is the place for you. It's by no means "true Italian" where you'll find wine bottles hanging from the ceiling and singing late into the night. Keep in mid that it's close to the mall so there is almost always a wait.
You'll find the menu hanging on the wall with an entertaining collection of paintings, photos and nicknacks, tighly packed tables, and an average wait staff. Your initial waiter will most likely disappear and return only to collect their tip.
The portions are large, but so are the prices. Some basic math skills will tell you that the meals are marked up about 300% (pasta about 800%).
I've found the best thing to do is go off-menu --- try to get veal marsala if the chef will make it for you.
I most likely will go back, but I have other preferred restaurants that are less crowded, have better food, and cost much less. I give it a 6.5 / 10.
