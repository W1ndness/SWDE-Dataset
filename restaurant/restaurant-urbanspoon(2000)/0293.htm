Good, but does it meet the hype?
by Nick (24 reviews)
March 22, 2010 - Likes it
For years I've heard people talk about Joe T. Garcia's. About what an awesome place it was for great food and atmosphere. We met some friends during the dinner rush on a Saturday night. Here was my take on the place:
PARKING: The parking is a little strange. We were able to get a close spot very quickly, so I have no complaints here. Though, I can understand how it can be confusing. There are two lots to choose from, and if I didn't have a friend in the car with me who had been there before, it could have been frustrating.
LINES: Like previous reviews stated, there are two lines. One for the patio and one for inside dining. We wanted to eat inside and had no trouble finding the right door (it was the first one we came to). Instead of putting your name on a list and waiting like most other restaurants, you stand in line and get seated when you reach the front. I don't think we were in line for more than 5 minutes, so I was pleasantly surprised and very happy considering this was a Saturday night and they appeared to be packed.
DRINKS: I was told to get the margaritas, as they were very good. They were right. I loved them. Our large group had several pitchers of both frozen and on the rocks. I am normally a frozen margarita guy, but the ones on the rocks were much better. Get those. They are also very strong, so monitor your drinking. What normally would be an alright amount can prove to be a bit too much here. You might not find out until you try to stand up to leave.
FOOD: They don't have a ton of options and there are no menus. We had the complimentary chips and hot sauce (good), Queso (good), Nachos (good), and Fajitas (great). We were warned that the fajita portion was huge, so my wife and I shared it. I am a somewhat large man and eat large portions, so I was surprised to find that it was actually enough. All-in-all I was very happy with the food. Tasty and filling.
DESSERT: We ordered sopapillas and they were your typical sopapillas. A perfect end to the tex-mex meal.
ATMOSPHERE: I was told before going that you go to Joe T. Garcia's for three things: Margaritas, Fajitas, and atmosphere. Since we ate inside (it was cold and snowing), I don't think we got the full experience, but inside it was great. Singers came by with their guitars and joked with us a couple of times. The place was busting at the seams with people, yet it didn't feel crowded. And the service didn't suffer as a result of the number of people. I guess they're used to it.
PAYMENT: THEY ONLY TAKE CASH!!!!!!! I repeat, THEY ONLY TAKE CASH!!!! There are a few ATMs in the restaurant, but if you plan to use credit, you are out of luck. We were unaware of this when we got there, but were only using our debit card, so we were fortunate that we could just get an ATM.
CONCLUSION: Did it meet the hype that everyone made out about it? Not really. If I'm in the area and we are looking for a place to eat, it will be on the list of places to go, but I'm not going to go out of my way to go there. The food was good, but there are too many options near me with good food to go that far for it.
So, if you're in the area and are looking for good Tex-mex, go try it out. Otherwise, check out the options near you and go there. You're not missing this great, unbelievable, experience.
