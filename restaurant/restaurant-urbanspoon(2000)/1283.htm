Remarkably good gourmet pizza... and mindblowing mac and cheese.
by Joe Bob Food Fan (14 reviews)
June 13, 2010 - Likes it
I will admit that I don't go to Andolini's often. It's a GREAT place, but I live in Tulsa and it's a bit of a drive to Owasso.
Thankfully, I have friends in Owasso, who give me a good excuse to go to Andolini's. (On the other hand, maybe Andolini's gives me a good excuse to visit my friends.)
As many others have already noted, Andolini's has FANTASTIC pizza. It's gourmet pizza, with a lot of loving care that goes into crafting it. It is my understanding that the owners are obsessive (in a good way) about quality control, and that comes through in the food itself. I have yet to be disappointed by anything there.
While they have plenty of amazing pizza options, I recommend the pistachio pesto pizza. They use a pistachio pesto (pistachios, olive oil, garlic, and basil) which they blend from scratch. Then, they use this as a basis for a wonderful cheese pizza. It is outstanding.
However, just because it's a pizza place, please don't feel that your options are limited to pizza. In fact, let me tell you about one of the best things they have on the menu: The baked gourmet macaroni and cheese. It's got mozzarella, provolone, parmesan, and cream, all mixed up with penne pasta and baked up in a decent-sized bowl. At just under ten bucks (at the time of this writing), it may seem a little pricy... but you need to splurge and try it. It is almost an addictive indulgence, and while I love their pizza, it's the mac and cheese that really blows me away.
Much like Mario's (my favourite pizza place inside the bounds of Tulsa), Andolini's has its roots in New York. The restaurant is "elegant" but not stuffy. It's very clean and has a great atmosphere, and it doesn't look too "corporate" like a chain restaurant. The music is not too loud and, as a longtime music aficionado, I have often found myself noting special "gems" in the song selections that I hadn't heard in a while. (It's my understanding that the songs in the background setlists are hand-picked by at least one of the owners.)
Even the decorations inside the men's room (sorry, I have to mention this) have an eclectic charm, with a picture of one of the locations from "The Godfather" that one of the owners visited, just down the wall from a picture of Frank Sinatra, Dean Martin, and Sammy Davis, Jr., all laughing and having a good time.
I have yet to have bad service at Andolini's. The worst thing I could say about the wait staff is that sometimes the servers really come across like they're "just doing their job" in a friendly manner, but without really showing a lot of exuberance or personality. Many of the servers are fairly young, and this may or may not have something to do with it. On the plus side, however, the staff have always had a very clean appearance, and they do a solid, professional job.
If you love good Italian food and you live anywhere in the Tulsa area, then you owe it to yourself to hop onto Highway 169 and make your way up to Andolini's. If you are an Owasso resident who loves Italian dining, and you haven't yet been to Andolinis, then that's just inexcusable. :)
By the way... did I mention that the garlic knots are awesome? I didn't? Well, then... now I have.

My Choice For the Perfect Pizza...
by R L Harding Jr (1 review)
May 16, 2009 - Likes it
Pizza, to me, is just pizza pretty much from one pizzeria to another until you have had a chance to dine at Andolini's and try their classic "Chicken & Smoked Mozzarella (with mushrooms"". Personally - my taste in food is pretty much centered around down-home meat and potatos menus. When it comes to pizza, finding a pizzeria that can offer you a pizza with the perfectly balanced combination of crust, sauce and toppings is a challenge. Now that I have happened upon Andolini's "Chicken & Smoked Mozzarella (with mushrooms)" pizza, my life-long quest for the "perfect" pizza has come to a "taste-pleasing" conclusion. I can only imagine that the rest of Andolini's menu is just as "taste-satisfyingly-perfect" also. To add one more feather to your cap - my wife agrees with me on this as well...
In conclusion, whenever we happen to find ourselves in Owasso at lunch or dinner time, you can bet that Andolini's is our first choice for eating pleasure. The staff is enjoyably pleasant and the restaurant most comfortable and inviting...
My cover (cap) is off to Jimmy and Mikey for putting into practice lessons learned from their Dad...
To the "Colonel".., "Semper Fi..!"
RLH
