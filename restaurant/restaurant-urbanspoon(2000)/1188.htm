It may be cliché...
by NMherron (72 reviews)
December 27, 2008 - Really likes it
5 Stars -
This is one of the best steakhouses I have ever been to.  I have been here about a half dozen times now when I come for business in the area.
Service is very good to great.  The food is excellent to superb.  I think the atmosphere is kind of kitschy too for a steakhouse.  The 40 oz. porterhouse steak I had on my last trip was insanely good.  I nearly ate the whole thing.
It is definitely pricey, but I cannot recommend this place enough.  My only caveat, is they don't take reservations.  So prepare to wait.  But it's worth it.

King of the Steakhouse Hill
by FoodZen (10 reviews)
September 04, 2008 - Likes it
Jacks is great, that has been established.  But how good?  I have eaten at Jaks well over 25 times.  I travel a lot for my work, and find myself eating all over the country.  Its business travel, so its expense account, and we inevitebly end up at steakhouses.  Im a fan of Zagat and Gayot, so we usually make our way down those lists.  What we find ourselves saying at the end of the meal is usally, "this was great, but it wasnt as good as Jaks."
Now there are a few point exceptions, such as the best filet Ive ever had was a Canlis, the best ribeye at Charlie Palmer DC, best prime rib at Lowreys Hollywood, best Fred Flintstone Porterhouse at Gibsons Chicago, best high maintenance full-of-theirselves NY steak at Peter Lugers, etc. But overall, none of these deliver the quality, livliness, consistancy, friendliness and value that Jaks does.  And Jaks seems to do it at 1/3 of the price, even on the suprisingly deep wine list.  
Yeah, the wait sucks, but its a good excuse to order an extra cocktail and meet new people at the bar.  If you can't handle the wait, you can always go down the street to Iris Grill. They are very good too and take reservations.
