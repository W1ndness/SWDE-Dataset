mobile review
by Jes Reviewin' (1 review)
July 29, 2009 - Likes it
I've been hearing about the 'Five Guys Burgers' hype now for close to a year, but tonight was my first time at any of these fast food chain locations. While the red-and-white tile decor evokes any other burger joint trying to evoke a 50's diner - the food itself is anything but standard fast food quality. The menu essentially just consists of burgers, fries, hotdogs, and fountain beverages, but the 'Five Guys' philosopy is quality, not quantity. All food is made to order and with fresh ingredients - you watch the cooks prepare your food in front of your eyes in the open kitchen. I ordered a cheeseburger with Cajun fries and a fountain drink. The wait was about ten minutes for my order - but worth the wait! The Cajun fries were just doused with a dry rub and somewhat of a let down on flavor and lack of spiciness, but no big deal. The burger itself - and this is why you go here - was cooked expertly. My burger was so moist and juicy and fresh - my only regret is that I didn't order a second one. At $9 and change for a cheeseburger, fries, and a drink this meal was a little more expensive then I was expecting, but I guess that is the price you pay for freshness and quality. 'Five Guys' isn't the be-all-end-all some make it out to be, but for what it is, it beat McDonalds hands down. 3 stars out of 5.
