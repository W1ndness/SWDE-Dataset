First time here
by Mike (15 reviews)
November 17, 2009 - Likes it
We don't ordinarily frequent the more up-scale franchises, but we received a gift card, so we went for lunch. Despite what other reviewers had said, and despite the sizable business lunch crowd, at 12 Noon exactly we had no wait at all. Our server saw us discussing the menu and asked if this was our first visit. On being told it was, he went to some lengths to answer questions and explain things. We went straight to the main course, ordering Mongolian Beef with the intention of splitting it. But the server gets a discretionary budget as a marketing tool, so he brought us the chicken lettuce wraps, too -- and my wife fell in love with them on the spot. (She even ordered another to-go serving at the end of the meal.) Even though it was a bit different from the canonical version, the Mongolian Beef was excellent. For afters, we split one of the little "shotglass" desserts -- which are just right for a sweet punctuation to the meal. (Reviewers who complain about the lack of size of these things apparently didn't notice the huge, million-calorie desserts on display up front.)
Reviewers who complain that Chang's isn't "real Chinese" and recommend you go to the nearest Chinatown (ignoring the fact that what you would get in Beijing and what you get at the Golden Turtle in San Francisco are also very different), need to realize that these guys have attempted to re-invent Chinese-American, or maybe "Nouvelle Chinese." Frankly, the prices are a little high for us to become regulars, but we'll be going back for some special occasions.
