Pastis is a classic: get the steak-frites!
by Lola (243 reviews)
February 02, 2009 - Really likes it
Pastis, sister restaurant to Balthazar, is a classic French bistro and serves up one of the best steak-frites in town. IMHO, it is also slightly less touristy than Balthazar although it gets a fair amount of publicity.
Most definitely, the skinny frites served with mayonaise here are better than Balthazar's. Other good choices are the salade nicoise, French onion soup, and fish and chips.
Another thing to keep in mind is that it's generally easier to get a table at Pastis than at Balthazar. Tthe the service is better too though the space is somewhat cramped. You'll be elbow-to-elbow with your fellow diners who will hear every word of your conversation.
On a scale of 1-5, I'm wavering between 4-4.5. For now, I guess I'll go with 4 because although the comparisons between Pastis and Balthazar are inevitable, the two restaurants are different. Balthazar is probably better for people-watching, while Pastis has better food and service. Personally, I prefer Pastis for lunch or dinner and Balthazar for brunch.

by primo (16 reviews)

I've been hitting Pastis since it opened it's doors down the street from Florent which was the mainstay prior. While many disagree with me, I've always enjoyed the Keith McNally dining experience, close proximity to tables as to overhear really interesting conversations, the crowd itself, some chill and some super uptight. The food is typical bistro fare but it's good. I love the oysters and frites, anything with shrimp, the bar steak is a nice way to start your night and wash it down with a carafe of Red. The hustle and bustle and good lighting and smart service is always a pleasure. I also love brunch and live goodies from the Balthazar Bakery. Late night drinks and snacks are a good time to hit as well. Keep on keeping on.

This place is my haven.
by stintless (8 reviews)
April 12, 2009 - Really likes it
After having just moved back to the States after a year of living in France and other Francophone countries, I was extremely pleased to discover this gem sitting right on my doorstep.
The food is exactly as it should be. It's authentic and creative at the same time and I always leave in the most pleasant frame of mind.
The atmosphere is great-- it feels as though someone took a traditional French Bistro and then stretched it on all planes; you get the feel of one without the cramps that a hole in the wall induces.
The service is mind-bogglingly professional and courteous. I go often and have my favorite servers, but they're all good. Good service goes a long way in my world and this is some of the best I've encountered anywhere.
This place is perfection itself.
