Crazy Feta...
by Trip Klaus (109 reviews)
September 19, 2009 - Likes it
I have really been enjoying this place lately. First of all yes it's small and can be noisy at night when it resembles a DC club. If that's not for you go for lunch or early when the staff is calm the volume low and the food great. Second, I love me some small plates, tapas, mezze whatever I understand their point and love the experience of multiple tastes usually with multiple friends. However, I feel that just having one of my favorites for lunch with the pita, olive and harrisa plate is plenty at a great price.
That being said, I can see where some Greek purists could be unhappy, but I love the chef's take on some of the classics. I enjoyed the harrisa (extra spicy) and the spicy hummus so much I rushed to Whole Foods to buy some for home. And the crazy feta with jalapeno don't get me started.
For me meat and potatoes are the order of the day: the disco fries (veal ragu and feta over fries like a Greek poutine), the braised beef (the best Cincinnati chili cheese fries ever) and the lamb chops (again with fries). My suggestion ask for the fries to be crisped a little extra because they can become soggy under the above. All the above items are rib sticking and well worth the money. I also like the saffron risotto and the white bean ragout (just for you veggies).
The best time to visit is Tuesday for half-price wine night when you can guzzle the appropriate Boutari Naousia till your heart's content. I can't wait to visit the DC branch where I hear they are going to start offering some type of Greek Pintxos during happy hour.

GREAT FOOD!
by Angela Pan (97 reviews)
August 08, 2009 - Likes it
I'm screaming because it's so darn loud in here! I went with a friend and we had a good time because the food is unbelievably delicious. We had the mussel, the cheese that catches on fire (Souvlaki?), and baby lamb but will be back for the scallop!
The rice that came with the mussel was delicious. It was moist and creamy without a heavy aftertaste.
We tried the cheese with 151 that they light on fire in front of you and weren't impressed. Mainly because they screwed up on ours but oh well. The cheese was crispy on the outside (breaded?) and seasoned with a wedge of lemon. It was good but really salty. I would recommended trying another one of the infamous greek cheeses.
The baby lamb was excellent. My friend thought it wasn't seasoned enough but I thought it was the second-best lamb I've ever had. The meat is so tender it falls off the bone. What I loved about it is that it was seasoned lightly so you can taste the flavor of the lamb without it being too dramatic.
We had red wine (Angelino) to complement as recommended by our extremely knowledgeable waitress and it was perfect.
It was a good thing that I came with a friend that was really generous with her food. Because let me just tell you, the food was so good that I did not want to share! The portions are really small but we came prepared. Both of us were not feeling very hungry but have always wanted to try the place out.
To indulge more about the environment, let me just say the place was a lot smaller than I thought. There is no waiting area and trust me, you will be waiting if you come when it's extremely busy. It is a really narrow space so you're walking down with little arm room between the bar and more doubles lined up to your left. So you'll be walking around many people standing and shouting at the bar, staying out of the way of waitstaff, and freezing your butt off at the same time while waiting and being seated in the main dining room. The decorations are pub style. Ask to be seated in the back because if you are stuck in the corridor prepared to have a billion people that are waiting to be seated staring at you eat since there is no place to sit. Or, even better, you can ask to be seated outside if there is room and the noise level goes down 5 octaves. It was so loud in here when I went that it's almost like being in a casino. Once your ears adjust your first dish has arrived and it ends up being a really enjoyable experience. Everything we had was delicious and very unique so your tastebuds really have fun! We will definitely be back again!

So far, so good
by stumpworks (1 review)
August 12, 2009
The good:
1. Lamb chops - twice superb.
2. Mussels risoto - excellent
3. Menu - a lengthy list of tempting selections.
4. Concept - great food in modest portions at reasonable prices.
5. Service - a capable and friendly staff.
The improveable:
1. Crowded and noisy - always a challenge for the popular.
2. Service - as a result a tendency to hurry you up.
3. The setting - legitimately Cava needs to move to something more spacious. The outdoor option is elbow to elbow with bumpers in the parking lot. Not pleasant in any weather.
Great place with excellent food and a competent crew. Difficult to criticize anything.
