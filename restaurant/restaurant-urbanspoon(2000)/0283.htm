A gem in Columbus
by jLocke1 (1 review)
July 29, 2010
I am an Indian food connoisseur and, having lived for a couple of years in Columbus, have been disappointed by what is available in the area. So I was glad to try this new place when it opened up. I was very impressed by Aab, primarily for the food, but also for the overall dining experience. The food itself is on par with any Indian food I've had in several big cities such as Pittsburgh and NYC. The Chicken Tikka Masala is remarkable, creamy and delicious with excellent ingredients and freshness, and the other dish I ordered, a Biryani, had great flavor and a nice mixture of ingredients (nuts, various vegetables). The drink menu is innovative and elaborate. The Indian desserts available are interesting and the one I had, a carrot pudding, was served warm and was very sweet, with some subtle flavors.
The interior of the restaurant is quaint and the patio, where I sat, is very ambient and opens out into "downtown" Grandview. My waiter was polite and knowledgable about the menu. I'm surprised by a couple of negative reviews I've read on this site -- though I suppose that can happen with any restaurant. Anyway, top recommendations!

best chicken tikka masala!
by beckylynn13 (13 reviews)
May 12, 2010 - Likes it
When I try new Indian restaurants, I usually use two menu items to determine whether or not I'll return - saag paneer and chicken tikka masala. Tonight I chose the chicken tikka, and it was excellent. It's so creamy - definitely not health food - and it's honestly the best I've had in I don't know how long. My husband got the lamb roganjosh, which was more creamy than I've ever had it before, but was also really good. The lamb was so tender, I didn't need my knife to cut it. Our server was super friendly, and the wait staff seemed to on their game. I saw someone else's review about poor service, but I urge you to try again, because I think they worked out the kinks, as the server next to us was also really attentive. The cocktails are a bit overpriced, but my husband really enjoyed his alcoholic lassi with kahlua. We'll definitely be back so I can try the saag :-)
