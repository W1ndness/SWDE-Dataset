mobile review
by Tim Gaffigan (15 reviews)
September 17, 2009 - Likes it
I've been to every other steakhouse in Houston Vic&Anthony's, Smith& Wollensky, Sulivan's, Morton's, The Striphouse, Shula's, Del Frisco's, Brenner's, you name them it and I've been there. Hands down Pappas Brothers Steakhouse takes the cake. Our waiter Mark Whitehead did such an elegant / fantastic job of being tentative to our every need. He knew what we needed before we did. We started with the chef's pick three of their best appetizers. Shrimp, crab cakes, and scallops. It was paired with an exceptional white wine that brought flavors your tongue will never forget. Then I shifted to the Turtle Soup. Look out Brennan's. They've got you beat on this dish. Which in my opinion everyone always compares Turtle Soup to what they've tasted at Brennan's. For the main course our group had the Filet Duo, 2lb Lobster, Filet, NY Strip, and I had the 22 oz Ribeye. Oink, oink. I got it prepared medium. Everyone's steak was grilled to perfection. Mark paired our red meat lovers with a beautifully full and explosive bottle of red called, "Rocket Science". WOW. My mouth is watering from just writing this review. My steak wasn't just a normal ribeye that you get at any of the high class steakhouses in Houston. It was truly dry aged beef. The quality of the meat at PBS is like none other I've ever experienced. Not to brag, but I've dined in NYC, LA, and Chicago at all the expensive steakhouses and this steak blew my taste buds out of this world. A must have. I don't know how, but I had to try the "Gooey Pecan Pie" and it was worth every sinful bite. I'm already planning my next event to visit Mark at PBS. It was DEEE-LISH!!!!
