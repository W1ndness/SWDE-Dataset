New perspective...
by Food for Thought (76 reviews)
December 03, 2009 - Likes it
Lindey's has always been good even if by reputation but tonight got a new view...
Took big client and about 10 other people and service was super! Great suggestions and actually honest ones from our waiter which was perfect. Dinner was super with the majority going with seafood. Lobster bisque was fantastic and had 7 scallop dinners at the table of which 1 was mine. Great job as it exceeded expectations. Dessert, dont ask... another great dish in summary but the highlight of the evening was the bartender Tony of which started a bit cold but sealed the deal with great friendships made and wonderful invitations back. We will be back and already booked 15 for next week!

by Morgan (2 reviews)
August 08, 2010 - Doesn't like it
Lindys is normally our favorite spot out for a nice dinner on the patio in the summer. The food is not the best (save for the tuna tartar appetizer) but the patio scene always makes up for it. Unfortunately, we had horrible service on our most recent trip. Our server was Kari S. We had empty drinks all night. After 1 1/2 hours we finally received our soups but I was not given a spoon. The server did not return to check on us so I did not get a spoon until after my dinner arrived. Needless to say the soup was cold and I was no longer interested. For the price you pay to eat at Lindeys I expect to have decent, if not impeccable service. This experience changed my view of our favorite restaurant.

Special Occasions Only
by SassyA94 (12 reviews)
October 18, 2009 - Likes it
I would highly recommend Lindey's for a special occasion. I went for Valentine's Day and they offered a special menu for the holiday that offered you a choice of a couple different appetizers, salads, entrees, and deserts all developed especially for that day. They also have a nice wine list. When dining there on a normal night I don't think there is much special about it. The ambiance and over-all upscale appeal is there but as for the actual food it's nothing to write home about. It's a nice treat every once and a while but for the price I'd go else where.

Lindey's German Village
by Mike (4 reviews)
January 25, 2010 - Likes it
For over 25 years, the bustling Lindey's has attracted the town's movers and shakers thanks to stellar steaks, seafood and pasta, all dished up in a sparkling bistro setting. In addition to the white-tablecloth service, guests can expect spiffed-up steakhouse fare, like jumbo lump crab cakes, grilled swordfish and matchless rack of lamb. In summer, Lindey's delightful courtyard is in high demand.
