A Journey Worth Taking
by Jessica (3 reviews)
January 28, 2009 - Really likes it
Journey is a wonderfully feng shui buffet of Asian staples. The atmosphere is elegant, yet casual and the staff are very professional. From basic Chinese staples, like sweet and sour chicken to freshly prepared sushi, this place has a selection for every palate. Chefs diligently work before your eyes to keep the food in ready supply. Buffet lines include a fruit bar, fresh shrimp, oysters, and crawfish, salad bar, Chinese food, grilled flavors (such as prime rib, roast beef, salmon, halibut), American cuisine (pizza, garlic bread, mashed potatoes), dessert, and an extensive sushi bar with skilled sushi chefs working hard to keep their selection plentiful. The fondu tower with fresh fruit and made to order crepes top off their vast array. This place is great for a speical date or a family lunch.

THE FOOD WAS GOOD BUT...
by KathyCalculates (10 reviews)
February 20, 2010
We dined at The Journey on a Saturday evening and since they close after lunch and re-open at 5 we arrived about 15 minutes early to already find a line outside in the cold. We had tried to get a reservation since we were a party of 7 and celebrating a birthday but we were told that they only reserved tables for parties of 8 or more. We stood in the cold as several people cut the line to move ahead of us...a little irritating. We were led to two booths and asked if there wasn't a table available where we could all sit together and were told that all the tables were taken by people with reservations. Irritating!
We had a very nice waiter who attended us fairly well...just one time when the plates piled up a little longer than was comfortable.
Since there were a lot of people at the buffet at the same time immediately after opening, it was unclear where we should begin so we ended up in a huge line in front of some hot foods only to discover that it did not progress to the remainder of the buffet stations and had to make our way to another line near the sushi. Because of the crowded conditions and the length of the lines, there were several "discussions" around me when people broke into a line to grab an item and then move on.
We were impressed with the freshness and quality of the sushi rolls. They were being made right in front of us. This is the first buffet where we found both cold and hot crab legs in abundance and they were both of very good quality...sweet and firm meat.
The length of the lines and the difficulty of getting in and out of the booths kept us from going back for more so we probably missed several stations.
The lobby was full on our way out and lined up down the hallway making it very difficult for us to exit. I would be very concerned if there were an emergency if there could be an orderly and timely exit.
So, although we enjoyed the food, we probably won't return due to the problems we encountered on this visit.

Just came back from eating there and thinking of taking my daughter for her birthday in July since she loves fish and especially sushi.
If I had read some of the reviews prior to going tonight, most likely would have never gone. Thank god I didn't. For people stating can get better food and quality at another restaurant is wrong when you compare all that you get. I am one of those people that buffets love, meaning I don't eat till I throw up and due to this I am skinny and eat till full, not bloated. To compare this to others is absolutely ridiculous to say that by paying $19 per person is too much. Yeal you can go to the country buffet for $7 to $10 per person (don't know since has been most likely over 10 years ago I went) but what do you get? Prime rib and mashed potatoes plus plenty of salad and ice cream.
Now to put things in prospective. When you go to a restaurant and get sushi as an appectitzer, you pay between $8 to $12 for this. You want crab legs, well pay another $10 to $20 per person depending on restaurant. Now that gets you the standard salad, rolls plus side dish, but you are limited to what they offer.
Want desert, then pay an addtl $4 to $8 per person. Well I love crab legs, forget lobster, never understood what all the fuss was about. They have hot/cold. My husbands all time favorite and hard to find is oysters rockefelter and they have that. The cost to pay in a regular restaurant costs at least $12 to buy. I never had a tasting for sushi till tonight and they had about 12 to choose from so I got about 6 to try and liked them all. They had fresh shrimp, cooked coconut shrimp, spicy shrimp and others that I did not try. But buy shrimp as an appetizer or as a main course, then add above the same figures.
They had all kinds of the typical asian food which I did not get, but loved the fresh fruit for desert plus dipping in the chocolate.
We came around 7;30 pm on a Tuesday night and had no problem getting a table and great service. But since we usually eat late, may not have a problem and if on a weekend on the "standard dinner hour" most likely will need to call for reservations or to get a larger table if we have family per reviews.
But based on getting just what we want per person and if we were to go to another restaurant, we would have spent at least double what we spent tonight. I would put this as a high end buffet restaurant and priced accordingly and correct.
To compare this to any other buffet where starch is "king" to fill you up; ie; potatoes/rolls/macaroni;
desert is ice cream or pudding and maybe a cake;
salads galore to fill you which include macaroni, potatoe also fillers is great if you have a large family and have to pay for children who may not enjoy sushi, crab legs, shrimp etc. So this could be pricey for children, but you would have to consider the age to consider since so many restaurants today may give small children free meals when their parents buy a full meal, which is great but unfortunately many times they "short" the server a tip for the "free meals" which they serve for 2 or 4.
So for a buffet and considering the per price cost to buy at another restaurant, you come at way ahead and save money.
And the wait, we did not experience this, but how many times when you go to a restaurant and have to wait between 30 minutes to an hour? If you never have to wait, then think about it, is there a problem especially if dinner time or weekends?
We will definitely be back.
