Disappointing food and service
by Harrison Cheung (30 reviews)
January 31, 2010 - Doesn't like it
Cheesecake Factory has usually been a premium family friendly chain in other cities, but here in Austin, it's more glitz than food. Its beautifully decorated location in the Arboretum doesn't make up for a nightmare parking lot, bad service and poorly executed food.
Let's start with the parking. The Arboretum has recently suffered a drop in traffic thanks to the Domain, but the compact car spots across the Cheesecake Factory are still up to the challenge of every SUV driver.
The reception desk seemed to be staffed by high schoolers who weren't 100% sure how their table allocation system worked.
Once you get your seat, you check out a large menu that hasn't changed much over the years and from location to location.
Whatever I've ordered has been subpar. Shrimp and Angelhair pasta? Pasta was overcooked and clumped together. The few shrimp were rubbery. Slider appetizer? Arrived AFTER our main course with grease-soaked hamburger buns. A grilled chicken dish was over peppered on a mound of cold mashed potatoes. If you can get your waiter's attention, you might be able to ask for a basket of bread. That will be the last basket you see for the evening since the waiters disappear and a different set of waitstaff actually bring the food to your table.
Desserts continue to be CF's strong point. There's a giant variety of cheesecakes and they all seemed to be wonderfully fresh and creamy.
This is a menu that was popular in the 90s, so IMHO, Cheesecake Factory is overdue for a major menu overhaul. For this kind of money, I'd much rather go to NXNW down the road. With the demise of TGIFs, the Arboretum needs a new family friendly restaurant. All that's left is the mediorce Serrano's and the overpriced Japanese Kenobi's.

Should have good cheesecake, right?
by bwbollom (37 reviews)
August 20, 2009 - Doesn't like it
The menu is outrageously thick...takes time just to wade through it all and find what you might want to eat. Once you decide and the food arrives, it's average at best and overpriced.
Also, if the name of your joint is "Cheesecake Factory" you should actually have good cheesecake, right? Well, they have a lot of them and I've tried 3 different ones on 2 visits and they all were blah and left a chemically, waxy aftertaste in my mouth. Not good. You can get much better cheesecake (one of my favorite desserts BTW) down the street at Manny Hattan's where they have it shipped in from the NYC Carnegie Deli.
I don't care if it's a chain or a local establishment, as long as it's good. I won't be going back to Cheesecake Factory for dinner or cheesecake. My money is better spent elsewhere.

mobile review
by I like turtles (4 reviews)
March 02, 2009 - Likes it
I don't think CF being a coporate restaurant is enough of a reason to write a bad review. I think their food is awesome. My boyfriend and I always start off with the Avocado Egg Rolls. They come served with a sweet chimichurri sauce that is just delicious. Their Cajun Ribeye is phenomenal! Marinated for 36 hours; it is a huge, juicy, tender piece of meat. Expensive, but amazingly worth it. I've never had anything on CF's menu that I haven't liked. Their strawberry infused martini is quite possibly the best drink I've ever had, and I've downed quite a few cocktails in my day. The taste of fresh strawberries and kettle one in a martini glass with a sugared rim is a drink well worth sipping and enjoying. For about $10-$12 this drink should not be missed. I won't even mention the carrot cake cheesecake because anything with 1500+ calories a slice is bound to be sinfully delicious. Overall, I've never been dissapointed. Food, drinks, and service have always been great, and to me that is what makes a restaurant good. Not whether or not it is a corporate joint.
