Pete's Mexican Restaurant and Cantina
Thursday, August 12, 2010
Why I don't want the restaurant to be called Pete's Halfbreed
My father and my mother started a little taco and tamale stand out of the back of an old world war II ambulance back in 67. Through hard work and vision they built that little stand into local restaurants that set the standard for New Mexican cuisine. I would love to tell you the rest of the story(it's pretty amazing), but I can't, ( I'll explain later)..IN 87 I lost Pete Sr. to a massive heart attack that came without warning at the stroke of midnight, on new years eve, at the new years eve party, at his restaurant. I was with my father when he died, but then, much of the community was with us that night. The following year my mother re-married, but. the joy was short lived. My big brother Danny was in Studio City California chasing his dream to be a rock drummer, and attending classes at P.I.T.(the percussion institute of technology). My brother fell down a flight of stairs, banged his knee, got an infection, and died a couple weeks later. I guess the stress of losing my dad and my brother was just too much for my mother to take, because, shortly after the death of my brother, she had a stroke that left her unable to walk or speak. She was placed in a nursing home and died six months later. Even before her death, tensions were high. It seemed that it was just my big brother Donny and I, against the world..(keep in mind we are still trying to run a restaurant and manage probates and death taxes, and it seemed that everyone involved had their own lawyers). I guess the stress of losing the best parents anyone could ask for, and his identical twin, (our brother Danny) was just too much for brother Donny, because a year later, my brother Donny was diagnosed with terminal liver disease. Our wife's were now running the restaurant, while Brother Donny and I tried to stay alive another day..... I lost him too. By this time the four probates were overlapping, and it seem the family attorney was more interested in pushing me out of the restaurant than keeping me in. My parents were great business people and had all their wills in order, however, I was finally pushed out by my mother's second husband and replaced as conservator of the estate, by my attorneys' stepson..(conflict of interest). I petitioned the court and regained control of the corporation, just in time to incur the entire tax liability on my shoulders. I.R.S. closed me down, no wills were to be found, and the once mighty Pete's Mexican Restaurant and Cantina Home of the original Half Breed, was no more.
.The restaurant was sold for a song. And I was completely broke and on the verge of a divorce. Because the the sale of the restaurant and all other propertys of Pete, Priscilla, Danny, and Donny was so poorly managed, the proceeds didn't satisfy the tax burden and me and my wife were left owing the I.R.S.in excess of 300k.(a debt we still pay on till this day). As bad as all that was, I was relived it was over. No more having to break the news( a dozen times a day), to every customer who asked for one of my deceased family members.they .(the conversations went something like this,:'how's your brother Dan?" Oh, he died.......I am so sorry to hear that. He was so cool. It must be very hard for Don, losing his Identical twin and all.... How's he doing?... he's dead too.... Wow....I guess it's just you and your mom dad....Yea...about that......... You get the idea. I would have nightmares that my Brother Donny was sad.about the deaths, and I'd wake up in a cold sweat and try to tell myself that it was just a nightmare. It didn't work, because in the reality that I woke up to, brother Don was dead too. It took many years to heal. When I started writing this, I mentioned that I could not tell the story of my Father and Mothers' achievements, and I said I would explain why. The reason I can't tell the story is, because 72 hours ago I saw the sign the new owners of the restaurant put up. It was this big sign that says Pete's Mexican restaurant and Cantina Home of the Half breed. Wow! How can they do this? I was crushed. I found out the name of the new owners and scheduled an appointment to talk about their plans to open the restaurant as Pete's. . They told me that it was a business decision, and they already checked the legality of it, . Everybody started calling and emailing me from as far away as Ohio and Washington to congratulate me on re-opening the restaurant. I don't mind talking about my father and mother. I just don't want the story to end up being used by people that I don't know, to make money off the death of my family. Why not just call it Little Anita's-that's what it is. Aren't you proud of the name you built? Why fool the public by using mine and my fathers' name? Use your own name! I don't want anything from you, but to let me get on with my life without having to relive the past, and explain myself. to everyone .PLEASE CHANGE THE NAME, MY FAMILY HAS BEEN THROUGH ENOUGH! Open your restaurant and prosper, I hope you do well for yourselves and for your families. I respect you and yours, respect me and mine. Change the name and I'll go away, don't change the name And I ain't going nowhere. Take the high ground and do this because it's right. please.
