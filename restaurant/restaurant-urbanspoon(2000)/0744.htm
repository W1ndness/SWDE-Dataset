Skip this joint
by Bianchi (48 reviews)
September 05, 2009 - Doesn't like it
My quest first started here at John's Pizzeria near Time Square. Walk through the doors and the aroma of sweet basil pleasantly greets your nostrils. You'll get seated rather quickly due to their bright and spacious dining area, with a sweeping staircase to the second floor to boot. There are also 4 large brick ovens, which means you'll get your pizza fast too. The only downside to all this space is the limited number of waiters/waitresses. They're juggling so many tables that you hardly get 'waited' on. You don't get any bread and butter (or olive oil) on the house either. However, once the pizza reaches your table you won't have to ask for your oregano, red pepper, and parmesan shakers, they're already there!
Choose your slice and watch as the cheese stretches onto your plate without breaking. Upon my first bite I realized something, hey, where's the sauce? Yes, their crust may be thin and perfectly charred, but their sauce or lack of it, lacks any real flavor. Did someone say big Costco can of sauce in the back? Who knows? What you do notice is the cheese. Yes, everyone loves a good mozzarella, but although John's mozzarella is good, it isn't great. In fact, it's quite salty and a little on the rubbery side. Perhaps that's why it can stretch for so long without breaking. The imbalance of sauce and cheese leaves this pizza a little on the oily side. John's pizza is what I would call, a standard cheese. Of course that is not to say that it doesn't taste good, no, it just isn't the best.

"HOW to DO John's - an Education"
by nycvagabond (1 review)
February 23, 2010 - Likes it
1. This is considered, among true NYers and those who know what NY pizza should taste like, to be some of the best pizza in the city; if you prefer your Domino's or or gourmet (Two Boots), or Chicago-style pie, this is your issue, not John's.
2. This is NOT an expensive restaurant for NYC - especially for Times Sq. If you've checked out the prices at TGI Friday's just a few blocks away, you'd realize this. Yes, if you load a thin-crust-style pizza down with five toppings, it gets pricey, but that's unnecessary. Stick to 1-3 toppings and actually TASTE the pie: cheese, sauce, dough.
3. This is John's PIZZERIA, not PASTA or PANINIRIA. Yeah, there are other options, but they're known for their pizza, so don't complain unless you've really had the John's experience...the pizza.
4. Order a pitcher of soda, juice or beer if you're going to want more than one drink. You don't get free refills anywhere in TImes Sq. Split a salad, a pitcher and a pie and you've had yourself a bargain. The fried calamari is also excellent if you don't mind spending a little more.
5. The wait's long because this is a tourist hot spot and it's right in the middle of the theatre district. Get to John's for a late lunch (after 2pm) and any time before 6pm to avoid a long wait.
6. Some of the servers are amazing. Some aren't. This isn't fine dining. The restaurant is huge and these guys are racing during a rush. Customary tip in NYC is 18-20% for good service. If the waiter doesn't plaster on a fake smile and tell you their name like you're at Denny's, this doesn't mean they're not nice or not giving you good service; it means you're not at Denny's. Low tips are of course, the customer's right for poor service, i.e. mistakes that are the server's fault directly, blatantly surly attitude, or never checking in on you. If there's a problem with your food, speak up and let the server or a manager know so it can get fixed. They will fix it.
Take it from a true New Yorker, this is a unique and affordable experience if you know what to expect.
