Maroo
by Fresno Magazine (20 reviews)
February 10, 2009
Drinking sake is nothing new to me. Drinking it on its own -- not from the bottom of a beer glass -- is. This concept of seeking out sake came partially from watching too many reruns of Ninja Warrior, indulging in the original Iron Chef and a desire to instill a bit of culture into the column. Finding the finest sake in Fresno was a simple task; I chose to follow the food, and assumed that if the rolls rock, the sake should knock me out! I braved this new cultural experience with the assistance of the knowledgeable and friendly staff at MaRoo. MaRoo offers a full menu of premium sake, cold sake and hot sake, a full wine list, as well as a vast list of imported and domestic beers, but I went for the sake experience.  
To a novice, sake can seem a bit daunting. With an ancient history and a complex fermentation process, sake is much more than a simple drink. One could possibly spend an entire lifetime dedicated to the art of sake, but I was content to brave the lineup of 11 premium sakes on the menu at MaRoo. The premium sake list covers the spectrum of tastes and each sake is filled with a world of distinct flavors and treats for your palate.
Of the 11 -- yes 11 sake varieties that I sampled, the sake I most enjoyed was Shirataki, aptly described as “Pure Flavor”. Shirataki has a wonderfully sweet and fruity quality without coming off too strong or heavy. The fruit flavor and the dry body of the sake combine to create a light and clean texture which makes it go down smooth -- as if it were simply gliding its way down your throat. Shirataki is so well balanced that the moment you think you might actually be drinking air, the superb acidic aftertaste kicks in and reawakens your senses!
Wakatake, affectionately referred to as “Demon Slayer”, and  Namahage, or “Monster Sake”, are two I would suggest for anyone who wants to try something with more power! Wakatake is bold, with a strong, full flavor which is initially masked within its silky texture. This sake starts off soft and quickly builds into an immense, rich taste full of fruit and acid, trailed by a delightfully bitter aftertaste. Namahage is similar in that it is bold and full of flavor. However, where the Wakatake is silky, the Namahage is dry and filled with earthy undertones.
I like to believe that I completed my Sake Challenge with honor, but drinking sake in appreciation for the process and taste, not part of theenticing “Sake Bomb” takes true strength! Fortunately, MaRoo provides a comfortable and relaxed atmosphere and a bar staff well-versed in the tradition, history and custom of sake, so I left feeling like a champion!

Maroo
by Fresno Magazine (20 reviews)
February 10, 2009
If you’re a few months behind on your new restaurant visits, don’t feel bad, the Lunch Ladies were behind a bit, too. MaRoo Japanese and Korean Cuisine Restaurant has been open in the Pavilion West Shopping Center at Palm and Bullard since December of last year and it was our first time to try it.
The Korean word "Maroo" means a living/dining room with wooden floors where family and friends gather to eat and enjoy each other's company. I can’t think of why a wooden floor would be mentioned or important, but eating and enjoying one another’s company is something the Lunch Ladies are good at!
One thing that makes me proudest about being a Lunch Lady is that our group is really a fundraising endeavor. Lunch Ladies invitations are won through raffle drawings or silent auctions at local charity events. Joining us today was Divonna Swan, who won an invitation for two at the Fresno Covenants Foundation fundraiser in February. She brought her daughter, Kim Ahl.
Nicole Caruso won a Lunch Ladies invitation for herself and a guest at Junior League of Fresno’s Breakfast at Tiffany’s event. Her guest was Christina Linneman. Before lunch was over these ladies all said they were going to bid again to make a repeat visit with the Lunch Ladies. We’ll be looking forward to it.
MaRoo has an open and airy feel to it. The room is spacious and there is outdoor dining as well. The colors of fire red, sunset and camel make the interior warm and relaxing. Instead of greeting us with a wet towel to wash our hands, our server presented us with dry compressed towel in a bowl which they then ceremonially poured warm water over from a silver tea pot. This makes the towel “grow” into a suitable washcloth. It was a unique touch.
Nicole was quick to notice the big screen T.V. playing a slideshow of the restaurant and guests who have visited there. We didn’t have time to watch much of the show, but we all wondered if we would see anyone we knew. Kim was hoping they would take a picture of us and add it to the slide show. Before we left, they did take our picture.
We knew that our chefs today were preparing a list of dishes for us to try. Our server, Joshua, greeted us and took our drink orders. The service at MaRoo was exemplary. Joshua served our large group flawlessly with our every need anticipated. There was no waiting and we were soon served our first appetizer, followed by… a lot more appetizers!
To start off, we sampled miso soup and then crispy onion sashimi, which was fried onion strings served with seared Ahi tuna. This was Divonna’s favorite. Next was the Maroo roll, with king crab and avocado covered with langoustine (also called Norway lobster or Dublin Bay prawn), and fruit cocktail. We also tried the Iwahiro roll, which is made with crab, spicy tuna and avocado wrapped in cucumber. This dish is unique because it was actually named after a customer who is a regular visitor. He asked for the dish so often they added it to the menu. We all thought we’d like to have a dish named after us as well!
The next dish was Christina’s favorite. Crab mari is a dish made with Dungeness crab and leek wrapped in spinach crepes and a mustard sauce. It was also a favorite of Pam and Penny. Penny thought it would be a perfect luncheon entrée. Nicole agreed and picked this dish as one of her favorites, too. Nicole’s other favorite and a favorite of most of the group was a huge sashimi plate that had one of the most impressive presentations I’ve ever seen.
This sashimi dish was served on ice molded into the shape of a large bowl with one side cut out. It resembled an upside down igloo. A decorative fish was on top of the ice with all the sashimi fanned out around it. If you go to MaRoo, you must try this dish. Not only was the presentation fabulous, the sashimi melted in my mouth. Kim said it was “out of this world.”
Our entrees were equally intriguing. Eel gui is broiled eel in a sweet soy sauce served on large, sizzling river stones. Penny said it was tuna-like with a pleasant flavor in a slightly sweet sauce. The overall favorite was bul go gi, which was sizzled and sliced beef rib eye. Kim said it was mouth-watering. This was my favorite as well.
For vegetarians, entrée items include, dol sot bi bim bap, which is a popular Korean dish. The word literally means "mixed rice" or "mixed meal". It was served in a sizzling, hot stone bowl. If you’d rather include chicken or beef, they will prepare it that way as well.
Other entrees included simmered blackened cod in a spicy sweet garlic soy sauce and kal bi gim, which is beef short ribs simmered in soy sauce with Japanese mushrooms, pumpkin and Chinese broccoli. We also tried potato prawn, which were deep fried prawns wrapped in mashed potatoes with mango sauce.
For desserts, MaRoo borrowed from the French. We were served crème brulee, and mille-feuille, which is a French pastry layered with phyllo dough over cherry mousse, strawberry and vanilla ice cream. Next, we sampled a tropical igloo with shaved coconut ice topped with sweet beans, jellied fruit and vanilla ice cream, which had a bit of a tart flavor. Our last dessert was a chocolate mousse. I can honestly say I was too full to try that one.
Needless to say, that was my last meal of the day. There’s no way anyone could have left with any room for anything else other than smiles and conversation with new friends. No one in the group had any suggestions for improvement for this restaurant and most of the comments noted the special attention to detail and how each dish had its own amazing presentation.
Fresno has a wide variety of Asian restaurants, but this one is not to be overlooked. The food is excellent and the service is sublime. It’s a great way to try some not so familiar dishes along with some that you can get in other Asian restaurants. I think MaRoo will see us back again soon.
