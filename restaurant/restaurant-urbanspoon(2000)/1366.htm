My Worst Meal Ever in New Mexico
by Larry McGoldrick (128 reviews)
August 13, 2009 - Doesn't like it
Sad to say that a trusted friend's bad impressions were confirmed recently. We were quite fond of the last incarnation on that property (The Gastropub). The current place might be repairable if the new owners:
1. Hire a decent chef.
2. Get a decent menu.
3. Teach the waitstaff how to do their jobs.
My wife and I decided to try the place after a particularly trying day. The old place was comforting and always got us out of the dumps. We were somewhat apprehensive after reading some negative comments, but old memories die hard.
We sat outside and waited maybe ten minutes for attention. The young waiter appeared and went directly to a table near us that had two rather nice looking young ladies seated (after we arrived). When finally asked if we wanted something from the bar (no, thanks -- we don't drink), gave us menus, and disappeared. He finally came back to take our food order.
The menu listed two dishes that I use as benchmarks when visiting a place for the first time: lamb shanks and bread pudding. I never met a lamb shank that I didn't like. Until now. Advertised as having a red chile-coffee sauce, they came out with the sauce burned and the burned flavor permeated the bed of lentil/carrot/onion mixture, and the lentils were quite hard. I decided against trying the bread pudding.
My wife ordered fish with red chile sauce. A complete disaster. The fish appeared to be a nondescript white fish, and was cut with one end 3/4" thick and the other about 2-1/2" thick. The laws of thermodynamics and heat transfer tell us that this shape cannot be cooked uniformly, and sure enough, one end was dry and the other was soft and mushy. You know, raw. Raw white fish sucks. And there was not enough food on her plate. Not that it mattered. She just wanted to beat a hasty retreat.
All in all, a disaster. Too ambitious with no attention to the details. They will never compete successfully with the Indigo Crow (which does lamb shanks really well). Pity, because the place is beautiful. Maybe in several months they will find their niche in Corrales after they overcome their arrogance.
I could say more bad things, but why bother. You get the idea.

Ambitions Amok. The Result is Woeful....
by Al I. Mentary (2 reviews)
December 29, 2009 - Doesn't like it
Casa Vieja is housed in a lovely, historic dwelling in the charming village of Corrales, New Mexico. These are the first and last kind words I can muster up in this review.
The menu is quite intriguing -- mouth watering -- but the taste and preparation of the dishes does not rise to its promise. I have had the Coffee Red Chile Braised Lamb Shanks on two visits. The first time was a complete fiasco. That was just a few weeks after they had opened, and I chalked that up to startup jitters. I did not pen a review then, preferring to give them time to define and adapt to their mission. Months later, readers gave it high marks in the Alibi's annual poll, so I returned, hoping that they would have their act together.
They don't.
The lamb shanks were just as poorly prepared, and this time they were too stringy and dry. The sauce was a flawed and laughable attempt at gustatorial inventiveness. It failed. The service was just as bad as it was on my first visit. It's as if I was invisible. This is inexcusable anyplace, but especially in a restaurant with Casa Vieja's pretensions toward greatness. I think that the Alibi readers got it wrong.
Corraleños deserve much better. They can have a grand experience at the Indigo Crow, superb New Mexican food at Perea's Tijuana Bar (among the very best in the state), and delightful, inventive fare at Hannah & Nate's, all a stone's throw from Casa Vieja. I would love to see Casa Vieja rise to meet its potential. Sadly, they're not even close yet. Perhaps if they scaled back their ambitions and found what they can do best, and paid attention to service (dreadful), they would have a better chance of succeeding.

by NMNative (1 review)

We live in Corrales and have a great affection for Casa Vieja - one that has held through its serial reinventions over the past couple of decades. We appreciate the creativity and the courage it takes to operate a restaurant in this economy and this review is intended not to keep people from eating at Casa Vieja, but to have their dining experiences be better than ours was. We had a party of four on a moderately busy Saturday evening. The waitress was obnoxious when she could be found. The enchiladas were OK, but for $18, with Perea's real deal across the road at half the price, they should have been wonderful. We're giving Casa Vieja some time to work out the wrinkles before we go back. If it's like this experience, it will be our last time.

Great new restaurant for the Village of Corrales
by Jim Tritten (3 reviews)
March 17, 2010 - Really likes it
Josh and Kate Gerwin are a delightful young couple who have opened up an exciting new restaurant in a very old and historic building in our little Village of Corrales, New Mexico. My family and I have now eaten there over a half dozen times and have enjoyed our experiences immensely. We have and will continue to come back.
Chef Josh loves to cook and it shows with the meticulous attention to detail in his preparations. The best seat in the house is at the Chef's bar, in the kitchen. I just returned from there this evening where I thoroughly enjoyed the dishes that Josh prepared while simultaneously handling a packed house on their first anniversary and St. Patrick's Day.
After watching Josh at a local cooking class, I have opted to no longer order off the menu and instead just ask him to prepare whatever he thinks I might enjoy. That has included Sunday brunches, lunches, and a number of dinners. I have never been disappointed, nor have my family nor have some very discriminating world-traveling friends that have accompanied us.
Food served at Casa Vieja is slowly and individually prepared to the highest specifications. We have watched Josh make tortilla chips just prior to serving them. Tonight's buttermilk biscuits were out of this world. In my opinion, the lamb is the best thing on the menu and the best I have ever had anywhere - including in New Zealand. Bottom line, I have never had anything other than a wonderful experience at Casa Vieja under the Gerwins and I do not expect to otherwise.
The Gerwins are supportive of our Village agriculture and go out of their way to purchase locally-produced raw materials. They also support Village artists who have lent their craft to make the Casa Vieja not only a restaurant with wonderful tastes and smells but with a unique local ambience make our Village proud. If we are lucky, these folks will be with us for a long time.
Good luck to the Gerwins and I look forward to another year of wonderful experiences from a very talented fellow Correlaño.

Alibi Readers got it right
by rawkkandy (1 review)
October 28, 2009 - Likes it
After reading the weekly Alibi's yearly best of Burque edition my friends and I were drawn to the village of Corrales to try what the readers voted as Best New Restaurant and Best Chef. We were skeptical about this small town providing a dining experience to live up to such praise. Our skepticism was quickly and completely demolished.
Our service was prompt, knowledgeable and professional. But the food and drink were the stars of the show.
We started in the bar for a round of cocktails from a bartender who we found out was also award winning. His array of fresh fruits and herbs was something you don't see very often, or at all really, in New Mexico. Drinks were delicious and innovative and we enjoyed watching the care, effort and craftsmanship involved with each one.
Dinner easily lived up to the hype. Duck Ballotine was a rare treat. Coffee and red chile braised Lamb Shank was the best I have ever had. Quinoa Risotto delivered as well. All together one of the best dining experiences i have had in New Mexico. Thank you Casa Vieja. We will be back.
