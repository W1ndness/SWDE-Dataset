First rate
by A Tevington (707 reviews)
June 18, 2009 - Likes it
Rococo reminds me of restaurants in the Philadelphia suburbs, where eating Italian out is a cultural thing. Bare tables are a no-no, poor service is an affront to your mother's memory, and bright lighting is saved for Phillies' night games. Here light is romantically low. Tables are covered with a bright red cloth laid over a sparking white one. Otherwise, red and black are the predominant decorating colors. Meals are heavy on beef, fish and pasta. Each is very well prepared. The proper salad and soup is as important as the entrée. Soups especially are wonderful. While soups change from day to day, the mushroom soup is always available and well worth trying. They'll serve it hot or chilled. The staff are friendly and know the dishes. The owner knows his wine, too. The exterior of this place needs serious professional help, but inside all is first-rate.

....Sadly Disappointed....
by Kayla (112 reviews)
February 12, 2010 - Likes it
Last Friday my husband and I tried Rococo's for the first time. The exterior is deceptivly drab. Upon entering the small restaurant, it smells tasty, and is dressed fun and funky. The ambiance was promising and the band was good but a little loud for the small space.
The hostess seated us in a corner in the left side of the restaurant which was full of a huge, obnoxious, "Happy Birthday" singing group of jersey shore wannabes..... Not the best foot to start out on, but hey: anyone can go to a restaurant. We sat for 10 minutes before the waitress even ackowledged us. Chris ordered a beer and I ordered a water for then and a martini menu.
.... Another 10 minutes passed before he got his beer and I got to order my drink. My drink didn't come until Chris was halfway through his second beer. We ordered an appetizer and it was brought over before I got my drink.
We ordered the crabcakes and they were wonderful. They are meaty and flavorful. The sauce is great and really compliments the perfectly cooked cakes. If it hadn't been for the crabcakes we would have left... Ultimately, I wish we would have.
Chris ordered the Shrimp Scampi and I ordered the Duck.... The shrimp were... boring and in my opinion, inappropriately cooked for the dish, and the sauce was good, but too brothy. I'd rather make it at home.
My Duck.... was... just awful. I hate sending a dish back but I did. Again, it didn't taste properly seasoned (just bitter and bland) and the sauce it was served with was not good. I want to say that it tasted like watery A1... not for duck at all! I almost didn't want to order another dish but the waitress insisted and I ordered the Salmon.. with was supposed to be one of the better menu items........... the manager came out and talked to us also.
Then the salmon came. It smelled good and looked good. First bite was alright but a little too sriracha-y. I like heat- actually I love heat, but when you can no longer taste any other aspect of the dish or your wine.... not good. The waitress realized this and although I protested, told the manager who came out once more. I found out that the chef was actually in the kitchen, and even though I had sent back the duck, did not prepare the salmon.
The manager bought us dessert... I chose a chocolate cayenne creme brulee that was good.
Rococo's has a great wine list and good drinks. They have Coop beers which is great, but I can't say I want to go back. Maybe Rococo's just isn't my style?*** SEE FOLLOW UP

Be Warned of Bad Management
by H.Levy (1 review)
February 28, 2010 - Doesn't like it
It takes a lot to disappoint me, even with being a "foodie", as friends would call me. And for a long time, I've always recommended Rococo as the best place to have crab cakes and good beer. The atmosphere has always lacked, with fake red roses and ivy teeming from any possible corner, but those crab cakes have always made up for it....until tonight. My fiance, as done on a couple of prior evenings, utilized a coupon from restaurant.com, which can be a great tool try different places or even to shave a few dollars in this ailing economy. By no means do we use it on a normal basis, but we planned to on this particular evening (a Sunday, mind you). The place had a total of two tables, including ours, and we even joked about Gordan Ramsey having a field day with the decor and some of the food choices. Still, we enjoyed our crab cakes, entree that we split, and creme brulee. The ticket came and the coupon was rejected. Later, we totaled the tab and realized we were less than about $.50 shy of the $50 minimum minus alcohol. That was fine and dandy. What wasn't fine was the manager (apparently, the only manager the restaurant has on a normal basis from what our server said) coming over and insinuating that we used a calculator to make sure we ordered enough food. She said the coupons aren't for people to just spend the minimum to get the discount. Hmm, really? I can't think of any person who orders food, presumably good food, with a calculator in mind. We certainly don't. We order what we like and want and apply a discount if we can. We couldn't. Big deal. Insulting customers never wins me over, regardless of food quality. I won't even go into details about the service, but if the management has anything to do with it I can't blame the poor girl.
End result? We won't be back. We even told the manager, and she had a "I care" attitude. At least the server cared enough to give us the owner's card. As regulars at Rococo's (pardon, I meant former regulars), I feel cheated knowing how much business I sent their way. And, I can honestly say I've never written a poor review of a place, but I don't wish our experience on anyone, friend or foe. Please be warned, if you only care about places for food, you might be golden. But, if you were like us and something horrible happened to shed light on how truly terrible their staff is, it's not worth it. It looks like after all these years, the inside of Rococo's is finally catching up with the outside. And it looks like crap.
****
3/1/2010 update on review. I must say, I'm impressed. Bruce, chef and owner, sent an e-mail through this website with his phone number. I was reluctant to call, but I'm glad I did. He apologized for the service, saying it's not what they're about, and offered to make amends (which I won't go into details). I look forward to taking him up on his offer and having a different review to post. So, a word to those reading: if you've had a bad experience at a place you once loved, don't let it slide. Do something and let the owner do something to change things around. It's their livelihood and they have families to support too. I'm glad I called back...we'll see what the path has to offer...

Amazing Food, Outstanding Service!
by TGM0103 (1 review)
March 22, 2010 - Likes it
3/21/10: Sunday evening, about 8:00PM I met a group of friends for dinner at the Roc, Rococo Restaurant, and I was so impressed with our visit that I deemed it necessary to finally take the time to make an account on this site and review the place!
I have been to the Roc twice before and the food has proved to be great. What made this visit outstanding is that the service was beyond what you can expect from the fine OKC restaurants we frequent!
We ordered stuffed mushrooms and crab cakes for appetizers. Both dishes were awesome, and we'd highly recommend them. The bread; Lavash and a white traditional bread, was absolutely delicious, and while the butter was served cold and hard, the waiter quickly warmed some up for us at request! Our server never skipped a beat, I was never without water or drink and I have to admit, I am part camel!
Then entrees came with perfect timing, they were well prepared and presented. At the table there was a variety of the fish special, an Alfredo dish, some pastas, and an outstanding Meatballs with red sauce and pasta dish. Everything was outstanding, especially the meatballs dish!
Everything except the meat on the special wasn't quite right, my friend said it wasn't bad, it just wasn't to the level of the rest of the meal. One quick mention of it to the staff and they swung into action. Our waiter quickly remedied the problem. A tall blonde female manager who looked a bit like Uma Thurman came from the back and was so great. She apologized sincerely, she made a quick conversation with the group to insure that we were not only satisfied but going to visit her again soon and she took care of the dish accordingly. We were thrilled with the outcome!
The moral is, in any restaurant in any city of the world you can get some amazing and possibly not so amazing cuisine, but the staff makes the difference between an outstanding dinner experience and being gravely disappointed. Last night the staff at Rococo and the management of the establishment proved that they are truly in pursuit of being the top fine dining destination in OKC. Thanks to them all, we will be back soon!
