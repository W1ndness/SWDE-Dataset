Message from Blind Onion Pizza Pub

  • Mico Madness 3 to 6 Daily
  • Go Hudsons Bay
  • Fun Atmosphere

Vancouver's 'Best Kept Secret' Pizza joint since 1997. Hand Rolled Crust, free garlic everyday, 10 micro brews on tap, anchovie Tuesdays, micro madness daily 3-6 pm, 100% whole milk mozzarella, fresh ingredients, (no frozen Italian sausage), made and cooked the traditional way in classic pizza ovens. It?s a family friendly atmosphere with TV's on sports, history and news, assorted board games to play and at mill plain a pool and foosball table. The place is cozy, it's open seating and the best customers in Vancouver (OUR'S), will most times share a table with you if you missed getting your own. If not don't worry, have a beer or glass of good wine and relax, one will open up soon. Don't forget to leave us your picture of Fred for the Wall of Fame. See you soon. Ps: the secret is so well kept we had to open second place. Shhh!

Good Pizza, Not so Good Price
by Chris (4 reviews)
June 28, 2010 - Doesn't like it
After living 3 blocks from this pizza place for almost a year now, I thought I'd give it a shot last night. After all, people must go here for it to stay in business right?
I ordered a large half Hawaiian (no onions) and half Pepperoni and Sausage, nothing else, and this set me back $24 (with tax). Yes, $24 for a pizza, and that was for carry out none the less. Unfortunately, I think $24 for a Pizza is a little bit much.
The pizza it's self was good, well, the pepperoni and sausage side that is. The Hawaiian side had WAY too much pineapple. Literally, no joke, each slice of the Hawaiian was absolutely COVERED in pineapple to the point you had to squint to see the Canadian Bacon. Sure, Hawaiian pizza has pineapple on it, but isn't this a bit excessive? I should have asked for some Pizza with my pineapple.
All in all though, The Blind Onion has Good pizza (just ask for light Pineapple, if your not used to having an entire one on the pizza), but the price is the deal breaker for me. It's just not worth it to spend $24 on a comparable pizza I can get from Dominos down the street for $8 (with tax and a tip).
Hopefully this will change soon!
