Exceptional Italian Food
by Paul (21 reviews)
June 09, 2010 - Really likes it
This is one of many little gems tucked away on Magnolia Avenue. I was lucky enough to spend 4 months studying in Italy and eating here always brings me back. The food is mostly quite different from what we think of as Italian food here in America, but delicious all the same. The menu changes on a regular basis, but you can always expect a great selection of handmade pasta and superbly prepared entrees. The grilled calamari with parsley pesto is the best I've had, even in Italy. The service can be extremely slow when they get busy, so make sure you bring a bottle or two of a nice Italian wine. The food is absolutely worth any service problems that might pop up.

Tough call
by OurHandsFull (25 reviews)
June 04, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I've gone back and forth on "likes it" or "doesn't like it" for this one. If there was an ambivalent choice, I'd pick that. The bottom line is I don't like it enough to ever go back, so that must mean I don't like it.
The food wasn't bad. When spending $75+ on dinner for two, though, I expect a certain level of deliciousness that this restaurant did not provide. The entrees were satisfactory. The desserts were satisfactory. I'd just much rather spend $75 at one of the many other local places that provide delicious food, not OK food. Just two doors down, Lilly's Cafe is a much better experience all around.
I had no problems with the service. We were seated pretty quickly by a very cheerful and friendly waitress. I can't imagine anyone faulting her service. When she noticed an uneaten entree at the table next to us, she brought the gentleman a complementary new dish without him even asking. I noticed the owner later sent that table two complementary glasses of wine. I call that great service. This is not a rush you in and out chain, though. Expect a slower, relaxed pace.
Speaking of chains, I neither expected nor wanted American-Italian food served in chain restaurants. I have never been fortunate enough to visit Italy, but this is authentic? Lasagna served with rare (actually raw) ground beef is authentic Italian? (I thought that was illegal) Salad that is more corn than lettuce, served on cheap plastic plates is authentic? Serving the priciest menu item on a small wooden cutting board so that the overly rare tenderloin's juices run off the flat surface, through the many holes in the patio tables and into the patrons' laps is authentic Italian? More than half their seats are outside, so they really need dishes that don't spill into laps.
I didn't hate it. I would just rather go elsewhere.

Fort Worth is a very conservative town but you can still find a few small independent restaurants with unique food competing with the corporate chains. I recommend this restaurant, all it lacks is a waitstaff as professional as the chef to compete with the truly great restaurants in the DFW metroplex.

Poor service dominates the experience
by Josh K (1 review)
May 15, 2009 - Doesn't like it
My Americanized palate for Italian has certainly been tainted by the Olive Gardens and Macaroni Grills around here. True northern Italian is more delicate and subtle compared to the cream & cheese bombs served up by the chains. The pasta in the involtini was paper thin, but the overall dish was bold in flavor. The beef tenderloin was cooked a little more well than I like, but the shaved parmesan and truffle oil added so much flavor to what was a simple dish. For dessert the pear-apple torte was obnoxiously good.
There were some low points with the food. The grilled polenta cakes that came with the steak were almost charred and seemed to be soaked with oil. My wife also ordered the Dolce Nutella and it was burnt to a crisp around the edges and inedible. I would have given feedback, but it didn't seem like my opinion would matter. We were all but ignored by the staff.
The biggest drawback to a place that only seats 21 people is poor service. I think you might get quicker service in the Social Security Administration office or the DMV. It was that slow. We were the first to be seated right when they opened and our orders were taken right away. The antipasto, salads, and entrees came out at a stead pace, but we had to wait a long time to get refills of water. We waited almost 30 minutes to get the dessert menu and another 20 minutes for the check which is totally unacceptable given just 6 tables. I have read other reviews stating that the owner treats patrons like family, but I guess we were "those" relatives that everybody has but nobody likes. She did walk around and have some wine with what seemed to be regulars, but not so much as a hello from her. And yes, this was in a 6 table restaurant. My wife and I want to go back and try others, but right now I don't think we can afford to give another 3 hours of our lives to this place.

Best Food in Fort Worth
by Bakman (1 review)
December 11, 2008 - Likes it
Donatella Trotter and her husband Gaylen, a prominent attorney in Fort Worth, would host 3-4 dinner parties a week at their home.  At the urging of their friends she decided to open a restaurant.  Her husband bought her a small (and I do mean small) location at the 1400 block of Magnolia in Fort Worth.  Little did they know of the success that awaited them.  
Nonna Tata, named after Donatella's grandmother, has food so delictible that people wait upwards of 2 hrs. for seating (I know...I've done it before!).  They serve authentic Northern Itallian quisine (you won't find this at Maccaroni Grill).  Everything is cooked fresh and is mouth watering.  Be sure to save room for dessert they're literally some of the best I've ever had.  It's BYOB so bring your favorite bottle of wine (no cork fee) and they accept cash and checks only.  
Put this place at the top of your list.  It's an absolute must!
