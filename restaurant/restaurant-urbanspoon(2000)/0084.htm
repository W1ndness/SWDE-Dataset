Perfect summer eats at historic lunch counter
by bobcatarts (10 reviews)
June 25, 2010 - Likes it
Had a "doubledog with everything," and friendly service too, even after 59 years.
The dogs (you get 2 on a double) were steaming hot and perfectly cooked. The onions were cool, fresh and crisp, and the chili was nice and meaty without being greasy or too salty. Just the right spice. The sub roll was warm and fresh.
Their chocolate shakes are heavenly, and hit the spot on a hot and swampy day.
This is a PERFECT spot for summer eats, with lots of shady picnic tables all around. Or take your meal to nearby Dixon Observation Park and watch the planes come in for landings at BWI Airport.

Not what it use to be...
by John (1 review)
July 14, 2009 - Doesn't like it
Yes, we know, all things change, but unfortunately for Ann's it was for the worse. I remember my parents taking me there in the early 60's and it was a special treat...people would wait in line for over and hour to get served...but then it was worth it.
As of lately, Ann's has obviously changed hands (the name is still the same) and along with that the new owners obviously lost sight of what an icon Ann's was. The number one draw for Ann's was the chilli used for the foot longs...that was changed...then the rolls and then the dogs...sad.
Given any Friday, Saturday or Sunday, lunch or dinner the line would be out the door...but, no more. The ol'timers that headed to Ann's for that special foot long or Daricreme aren't biting anymore...again, a sad thing. I went a month ago to give Ann's one last chance...mann, was that a disappointment.
To the owners of Ann's, you really screwed up messing with something that wasn't broken.
