Good, but overpraised
by Matthew K (76 reviews)
January 02, 2010 - Likes it
The atmosphere is quite nice and intimate. The restaurant is situated in an old house with about one third of the dining in an enclosed patio that affords a corner view of the local area.
The wine list is small but seems well thought out. The beer selection is respectable.
FWIW, Sage's is practically vegan, though they only claim to be vegetarian.
The entree selection is limited: five cooked options (+ one special) and two raw options. The special of the night, polenta and sliced portabello served over squash with red onion and nuts on the side, was good and the sort of dish that is just fussy enough to prepare that one is happy to pay for it. But most of the other entrees seemed like things I could whip up a home in a reasonable amount of time, e.g. a pesto dish or a stir fry. Sage's Cafe has been voted best vegetarian for eight years by City Weekly readers. Given that the entrees that do not seem particularly special, my guess is that Sage's wins by default due to a lack of competition.

Hit and Miss
by Slim pickens (1 review)
February 22, 2010 - Likes it
With the understanding there is virtually little competition in Salt Lake City for Vegan and Organic food, Sage's can be good if you know what to choose and depending on the chef "on" while you are there. You have to also be outspoken as they, at times, seem to not know how to service the client. You have to be observant and outspoken.
I have been to Pizza night many nights where I was starving and had to repeatedly ask the wait people for more pizza if they even had it. There were a few nights where they actually had a good supply and were servicing appropriately.
Take out portions are at times considerably less than on site, depending on the chef "on".
Raw night, the last Fri of the month, is at times grossly over rated and overpriced for the seemingly last minute menu.
There are excellent menu selections especially for Saturday breakfast and off the regular menu.
The wait staff seems to be a work in progress.
