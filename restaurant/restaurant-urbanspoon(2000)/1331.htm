It would be a fantastic place if they only knew how to make pizza
by Forked Tongue (7 reviews)
November 14, 2009 - Doesn't like it
I've eaten here three times. I really wanted to like it. I love the atmosphere ... it's so New Yorky and adds to the continued good vibe that is Albuquerque. If only they knew how to make pizza.
I can't expect a restaurant to cater to my particular tastes nor to not think outside of the (pizza) box. Wood fired, pizzaioli in jaunty chef's caps, good aromas ... this place has everything going for it ... except they make a terrible pie, by my definition and standards.
The first time I excitedly ate there, I kept saying, "Yes." Yes." "Finally." until I sunk my teeth into this gooey mess. I ordered the finocchio pie ... I love a good Italian sausage. The sauce and toppings were good ... tasty, and well made. It's the crust that's gooey. It is not firm nor crisp as many have called it ... It is soggy. It droops. When it droops the toppings slide off. I called the waitress over to inquire if this is 'normal', as I held up the gooey, detumescent, toppings-sliding, formerly "a slice' mess. She said, "Oh my ... It's not supposed to be that way." I said, "... but it is ..." She walked off, end of conversation. I gave it one star out of a possible ten.
Believing that this could have been a one off, a few months later, I went back. Ordered the same thing and had the same experience. (Including a different wait person who said, "Oh my ... it's not supposed to be that way", who then walked off). Zero stars.
Is three the charm? A client of mine mentioned that she had a great pie at Farina's ... I told her of my misfortune to have eaten there twice. She insisted we go again. We did. She told the waiter of my prior bad experiences and he apologized (very good) and insisted that things would be different. They were and they weren't. Not only was the crust unable to stand up on it's own (and therefore not a 'crispy' crust) but in order to make it 'crispy' the pizzaiolo burnt the bottom of the crust so bad that it was entirely inedible. I showed the waiter the drooping mass of burnt pizza and he said, "Oh my." My companion was shocked at the mess in front of her. I asked the waiter if he would give the balance of uneaten pie to the maker of same. He took it over to the counter, but they were busy making gooey, non-crispy pies and the burnt pie was not going to be tasted while I was there.
We paid and left.
Three times. Three strikes. I believe that the problem is in the dough formulation. I believe that they know there is a problem but do not know how to fix it. Or they do not care. I hope that is not the case. -200 stars
For 'cred' sake:
Worked at three pizza places in Chicago (long time ago)
Been making pizzas for thirty years.
Spent one month in Rome studying Pizza.

Grumpy Waitstaff, Soggy Pizza Save your $$ for someplace else
by LG (1 review)
July 23, 2010 - Doesn't like it
After hearing several people recommend Farina we decided to give it a try on a rare date night without the kids. We were very disappointed. The restaurant is wonderfully decorated and has a cozy atmosphere until you realize that if it is just two of you dining you will be sitting across from each other with the table next to you just a few inches away. The unsmiling host let us know that in no certain terms was the patio available and no we could not sit at the table by the window unless we had at least four people.
The equally stoic waiter barely said more than twenty words to us our entire meal . He rudely dropped our ticket on the table as he was walking by without saying a word when dinner was over. Looking around as we were eating I noticed that most of the wait staff looked to be having a bad night as there was very little smiling going on. The only exception being the young man working behind the bar who brought us our salad with a very chipper disposition. We could only imagine how they would have treated us if we had gone out as a family and had our kids with us!
My favorite pizza is the margherita and I order it whenever I can. Farina's version was soggy, greasy and had about 10 small pieces of basil. My other half said his pizza was ok but only ate two slices.
If you are looking for good pizza, get back in your car and drive East on central to Brickyard just across from UNM. The deep dish pizza is yummy and the staff is friendly
Going the other way? Take Central to Rio Grande, North on Rio Grande to Old Town Pizza across the street from Walgreens.
