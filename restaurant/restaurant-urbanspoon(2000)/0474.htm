authentic
by Larry C. (18 reviews)
December 25, 2009 - Likes it
I am fairly knowledgeable about indian cuisine, and in fact have prepared several recipes as instructed by a Bangladeshi, so I feel quite comfortable reporting about this restaurant. Bottom line, "It's a go, Houston". We arrived a little after 6pm and already nearly all the booths were taken. My wife & I ordered a wine and Kingfisher beer while we perused the menu with the manager. The variety of the selections made it difficult to choose. We ended up ordering a spicy shrimp dish, a boneless chicken tandoori, dahl (the mixed beans selection), garlic naan, and two condiments (mango chutney and spicy pickles). Total for all was ~$52, plus tax. Based on the quantity of food, we came home with some of the chicken and shrimp. The chicken was so tender and flavorful that we didn't need the sauce on the side that we ordered. The shrimp was flavorful and spicy, and made our noses run, which we tempered with our drinks. The manager was extremely helpful in working through the menu for us (my wife doesn't eat meat), and all in all we had a very enjoyable and delicious meal. One negative was the garlic naan, as it was flavored with powder and not real garlic. One neutral was the dahl, as next time I'd order the pure, yellow lentils as opposed to the mixed we did order (not that it was bad, just my preference based on what I'm used to). All in all, I would return.

Terrible experience (food and waiter)
by mamadpatti (1 review)
April 06, 2010 - Doesn't like it
My fiancee and I went to Shiva in March 2010. We saw positive reviews online and decided to try it out since we were near Rice. We were greeted by the owner/head waiter (guy with a goatee and shaved head) who was also our server. He was annoyed that we didn't know what we wanted in the first 5 minutes of getting there, then gave us over 25 minutes to decide. We asked him about one of the appetizers, specifically, if it would be too much for two people to eat. He responded that his 10 year old daughter can finish it (specifically, two pieces of tandoori chicken, one chicken kabobs, one lamb kabob) but he cannot (he is about 6 feet tall and 200 lbs) so he cannot tell us if we would be able to eat it because he does not know what our appetite is like. He gave no idea of how much food came with any of the dishes. Never try the Tamater Soup unless you want tikka masala gravy in a cup at half off price because that's what it was. The Tandoori chicken was OILY which was odd and definetely the worst Tandoori chicken I have ever had at any Indian restaurant in Houston ,Chicago, Detroit, Phoenix, Milwaukee, Florida, New York and London. The main courses that we ordered (chicken tikka masala and saag paneer) looked the part, but had minimal flavor and no spice. The only good thing was the papad (which is free), the lassi, and the warm towel at the end of the meal. We will never go back to this restaurant.
