Message from POPS

South End restaurant enjoys a high-profile location while serving affordable, upscale comfort fare.

  • Outdoor, Heated Patio
  • Weekday Brunch
  • Private Events Available

With the elegant yet casual feel of high a scale restaurant without the pomp and price, Boston's Pops Restaurant proves you can have an affordable meal without sacrificing taste or quality.

Located in the heart of South End, this Boston eatery boasts a diverse menu that uses the freshest, organic foods, meats and ingredients as well as a full wine menu to accompany your meal.
Whether you are coming for weekend brunch with friends on the heated outdoor patio, enjoying lunch with co-workers or having dinner with family before enjoying a show at one of the South End theatres - Pops Restaurant offers an unforgettable dining experience.
Can't stop in for a bite to eat at this South End restaurant? Then enjoy some takeout instead. Pops Restaurant is also available to cater special events and private parties. Find friendly, professional service and great food at Pops Restaurant.

Cozy Space, Gracious Service
by Frederick (217 reviews)
November 12, 2009 - Likes it
We went here for a pre-theatre dinner so were unable to linger over our meal, but the staff was gracious and understanding and helped us get out the door on time for our show. We both ordered from the prix-fixe menu, I had the butternut squash ravioli with poached egg which was delicious but was just a single ravioli! Fortunately my main course, the Kobe beef meatloaf on a bed of whipped potatoes, was more substantial. My dining companion had the monkfish and didn't leave a molecule on her plate (and refused to share) so it must have been pretty good. Since we could not fit the included dessert into our timetable, the staff suggested that we come back after the show and they would serve us then, which we did -- a rich dark chocolate tart with fresh whipped cream. The atmosphere was warm and cozy, mostly local gay couples from the neighborhood plus a few clued-in visitors.
UPDATE: 4/16/2010 We were here again for another pre-theatre prix-fixe. There is a swanky new bar which dominates the main dining room so we sat in the back in the 'greenhouse' area. The price of the fixed menu has gone up to $35 but the portions are much larger and the choices are more adventurous. I had a duo of lobster croquettes bursting with fresh lobster meat, followed by the butternut squash ravioli - five gigantic pillows stuffed with buttery-sweet squash. Dessert was a pair of house made doughnuts filled with chocolate ganache alongside a signature Popsicle and cruet of creme anglaise. Service was flawless as always -- our young waitress made sure that we could get out in time for the show across the street at the BCA.

Pops is a fantastic restaurant
by Debby (1 review)
April 11, 2008 - Likes it
Our drinks were excellent as they carry only top shelf.  Even the bread was good--excellent texture and something slightly hot on the surface, like hot oil but very subtle. We shared a crabcake appetizer--perfect, not too much filler, excellent poblano horseradish sauce with thinly sliced pickles.  
I had crispy confit of duck with cherries and whipped sweet potato and my friend had lambchops with butter beans.  Both entrees were flawlessly cooked.  Many interesting desserts.  Ours was a fabulous skybar concoction.  I will gladly go there again and again.
