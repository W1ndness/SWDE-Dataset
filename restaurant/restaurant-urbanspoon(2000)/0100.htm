Platonic Bliss
by Captain C (11 reviews)
June 22, 2009 - Likes it
Baton Rouge is enjoying, recently, some fine new gourmet ethnic food outlets. One of the newest is Latte e Miele, which translates to "Milk & Honey" in Italian. Those are two of the primary ingredients in their wonderful array of gelatos, sorbets, frozen yoghurts, coffee drinks & other frozen creations. BTW, they also feature classical music in the background. A visit to this emporium of truly good taste is well worth the drive out Highland Rd., near the intersection with I-10...unless, of course, one lives at the Country Club of LA or other outlying residential developments. The State Police & Blue Bayou are nearby, too. The owner's family stems from Rome, Italy, where a tradition of similar ice cream shops abounds on the piazzas of the Etternal City. Needless to say, one can travel to Italy...or make a much shorter voyage to this delightfully tasty shop in Baton Rouge. The milk and creams are from Kleinpeter Dairy and honey is obtained from local beekeepers. All of the flavourful fruits are selected from area produce markets or local growers. The espresso coffee, as well, is absolutely the best I have tasted in recent years. Be sure to ask for a twist of lemon peel, an Italian traditional style. One will surely forget the thirty-one flavour stores and other purveyors of ice cream products, once Latte e Miele becomes a favoured destination. There is simply no comparison with anyone outside of Italy, itself. Taste perfection for yourself, now available on Highland Rd. Currently open very conveniently from 10 am to 10 pm. Molto bene!
