+ 3 more photos from Barney Great Atmosphere, Great Food.
by Barney (485 reviews)
May 03, 2009 - Really likes it
We went last night and had a great meal, in great surroundings, with great service.
I think her appetizer (Roasted Beet & Avocado Salad w/ thomasville goat cheese, pistachios) was the winner. Huge salad that she said was fantastic. My Oysters were pretty much the standard, with the addition of a cucumber mignonette, and were fresh and tasty.
I think, on the other hand, my main course (Crispy "Duck & Dumplings" - duck leg confit, duck meatballs, ricotta dumplings, wilted spinach & arugula, sour cherry) was the clear winner. Perfectly done duck leg and thigh that melted off the bone and had a great mild flavor, with very strongly flavored duck meatballs balanced out by the creamy ricotta dumplings and just the slightest hint of the sweet/sour cherries here and there. Of course, my wife may argue that it was better because she certainly enjoyed her grilled salmon with a smokey tomato pan sauce.
Because of the large serving sizes for app and main, we did not NEED desert, but that didn't stop us. We had the Meyer lemon donuts w/ espresso ice cream which were steaming hot made-to-order and tasty, but nothing magic for us. The winner here was the rum "sopped" coconut cake with creme fraiche. This was big, and thick, and moist with an absolute huge coconut punch fantastically complimented with the taste of the rum. Probably didn't get half way through these huge deserts, but only because we were so full!
What can I san say, this has quickly become a new favorite for us. Not a huge place, it has a very casual filmy elegance and great service. As with last night, with a full house it can get medium loud, but not so loud that you can't easily have a conversation with other folks at the table.
One of these days, I'll have to try music and oysters upstairs at the bar...

+ 3 more photos from Barney Sunday Supper!
by Barney (485 reviews)
February 16, 2009 - Really likes it
Finally got the chance to try JCT's special Sunday Supper menu and really really enjoyed it.... what a deal.
Started off with a plate of deviled eggs to the table.  Done well, but really not anything unusual.  Next was a VERY nice salad with a lemon vinaigrette that came to the table with fresh biscuits (apple butter and caramel butter).  
Everyone at the table gets to order their own main and then order 3 vegetables for the table.  I had the “Slow and Low Cider Braised Short Ribs” that were very tasty.  Nice deep dark flavor balanced very well by the cider flavor and the baked apple slices.  The Veal Meatloaf and the Fried Chicken also got very good marks.  For vegetables, we went with the Sweet Corn (house cured bacon, bleu), the mashed potatoes (Vidalia Onions), and the Collard Greens.  All the veggies were as good as they sound, with the surprise being the slightly sweet maple flavor of the collards.
Finished with the petite chocolate truffle cake for each of us.
Walking out, we all agreed that we enjoyed absolutely everything and that it was a great deal at $24 for everything except drinks.  Sure, it's a small menu, but $24 in ATL sometimes doesn't even get you the meal... and here you get high quality app/salad/main/veggies/desert... as I said, great deal.

I love this place
by Food Jerk (15 reviews)
June 08, 2009 - Really likes it
Tucked back off Howell Mill on the tracks is JCT Kitchen and Bar. The dinner club came here May 29th and I wish we never left. We all met up at the roof top bar and listened to the live music before we were seated. The roof top is actually a really nice area with outdoor couches and tables for large and small groups. As usual we ordered three apps and drinks to start with: the Deviled Eggs with thinly sliced country ham on top, Truffle-Parmesan Fries (FRICKIN SWEET!), and the Farmstead Cheese platter, to be fair it was very tasty but dwarfed in size comparison to the other two apps. All in all the appetizers were really good. When I go back I think I will get five orders of the deviled eggs (that's because they are just that good). Dinner consisted of the Benton's Bacon Wrapped Pork Tenderloin for myself and the Crispy "Duck & Dumplings" for my bride. Mine was AWESOME!!!!! But if you do not like smoky flavor do not order it. But if you cannot get enough of the rich, smoky, pork on pork action I suggest you order it and not let anyone else touch it. Duck is definitely for duck lovers only. It was good for duck but I am not a fan and unfortunately my wife is not either. Luckily she traded plates and ate the Sweet Corn & Georgia Goat Cheese Filled Pasta which was alright. I tried the short ribs but they did not compare to the bacon wrapped goodness. When you go, do not valet. We did and they took forever and my car was actually parked in the handicapped spot in front of the place, so I gave the guy a 5 and unparked it myself.
