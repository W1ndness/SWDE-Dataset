Hallelujah !!! - There really IS a good seafood restaurant in Jacksonville
by John M (62 reviews)
March 21, 2010 - Likes it
On the East side of the Ditch where Roscoe Blvd morphs into Solana Road, this diminutive and quite affordable restaurant is a real treasure.
It's cheerfully noisy inside as it's very, very busy (reservations are a must). The service borders on excellent thanks to a strong restaurant management team.
On this Saturday night, we enjoyed an Albarino and Cabernet Sauvignon from an affordable and comprehensive wine list.
The Roast Oysters with Smoked Bacon and Parmesan, Conch Fritters with a Lemon Aioli and the Ceviche appetizers were quite delicious.
For entrees we chose the Blackened Mahi, the Baked Cod and the Low Country Boil. The portions were very generous, the fish cooked perfectly (the side of collards were smoky and cooked to perfect tenderness) and the deliciously constructed Boil was really more than enough for four.
We left enough room to sample the Key Lime Pie and the Salty and Sweet Chocolate Pie (think Snickers Bar), both of which were a perfect finale.
In one word... BRILLIANT !!!

Astounding food and service!
by Brian Smith (28 reviews)
August 13, 2009 - Likes it
Palm Valley Fish Camp is the definition of a great restaurant. The service was impeccable. The salmon entree was beautifully prepared and fresh. The bread was tasty. The crab appetizer was creative. The prices were very low, almost surprising. The view of the waterway out back was pleasant. The atmosphere was like a hopping New York place, in a good way. The owner is one of the most personable women I've met in the restaurant business, willing to talk all about her ideas and history. There's a reason you can't get a table here without a reservation. Beats the hell out of Marker 32, even.

Little known winner
by ThreePutt (16 reviews)
April 03, 2010 - Likes it
Just tried this restaurant after reading the positive reviews, and we were definitely sold. Pros include the fact that they take reservations, the food is excellent and they offer a nice mix of fresh seafood as well as fried fare. It is on the water and offers a comfortable atmosphere. The inside is a bit small, so there is a feeling that you are sitting on top of each other. They serve beer and wine only. The oysters and clams are HUGE. I had the trout and my wife had the pompano. They were both excellent, but the crown jewel of the night was the bread pudding. We definitely plan on going back. I only wish they offered outdoor seating.
