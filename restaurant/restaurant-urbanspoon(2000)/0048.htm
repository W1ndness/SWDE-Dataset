Wisteria is my new favorite restaurant!
by Bella (1 review)
July 14, 2008 - Likes it
I recently moved to Atlanta from Phoenix and was anxious to sample some traditional Southern fare. A friend suggested Wisteria, and I am so glad that  I decided to check it out. The restaurant has a cozy atmosphere that made me feel right at home. Since I'm new to things like grits and corn pudding, my server, Jeffrey, was kind enough to take me on a tour of the menu, offering suggestions and answering my many questions. My friends and I shared sea scallops over braised pork belly (YUM!) and the southern antipasto platter, which included lots of fun things like pimento cheese and pickled okra. I couldn't resist trying the fried chicken, and at first bite, I thought I had died and gone to heaven. My friend, Jacob, ordered the beef tenderloin and I was all too happy to help him finish the amazing mac and cheese that accompanied it. Jeffrey helped us select a couple of half-bottles of wine from the extensive and approachable wine list, and we topped the evening off with an awesome cherry cobbler and a glass of my favorite dessert wine. All together, dining at Wisteria was a perfect restaurant experience, and I am definitely looking forward to returning.

Family Friendly
by belinda (1 review)
July 10, 2008 - Likes it
I would like all family diners to know that Wisteria is a very nice, 'white tablecloth' restaurant that was friendly to us and our family last weekend.  I have noticed that a lot of nice restaurants seem annoyed with folks who bring their kids in however this has not been the case for us at Wisteria.  They have several kid friendly menu items including pasta and chicken fingers.  Being a father as well, the chef visited with us and our children and really made us feel comfortable on a very nice night out (with the kids in tow!).  Patricia was our server - she was very kind, professional and fun.  Add this place into your dinning rotation - we like Sundays and Mondays to avoid the big weekend crowds!
