Nahm is a hidden gem
by Animal (11 reviews)
July 16, 2010 - Really likes it
I visited Nahm for the second time last night. The first time was really, really good - so my girlfriend and I decided to give it another shot to see if the second time around would be as good as the first.
I'm happy to report that it was just as good. Upon arrival, we both were thinking that we weren't really all THAT hungry but we ordered an appetizer anyway - we selected the shrimp dumplings and when they arrived, the first bite had our mouths watering.
We ordered glasses of wine (Smoking Loon's Viognier, which was very reasonably priced and a perfect match for the seafood entrees we eventually settled on) and had to make the difficult choice of picking a dinner entree from a host of great choices.
I had the spicy salmon special with green beans and grilled shrimp. The sauce was very spicy and the combination of the perfectly cooked King salmon with the spicy sauce was sublime! My girlfriend ordered the basil sauce lobster which was served with a few sea scallops. It was incredibly delicious.
We ended the evening by ordering and sharing a dessert - the coconut cake with a small scoop of mango ice cream. The cake is so light it melts in your mouth.
The owner, Nahm, made a point of coming out introducing herself and asking pointed questions like "Is there anything we could do better?" and told us, "Let us know if you aren't happy with anything and we will take it back and give you something else right away."
As if that was going to happen... Everything was perfect. The atmosphere is beautiful but not pretentious or stuffy. The waitress (Pearl, I think) was friendly and attentive, but not hovering. We also noted that someone from the kitchen was keeping a discrete eye on us to make sure that our food arrived right on time when we were ready for it (as soon as the appetizers were done, our entrees appeared hot and fresh as if by magic. They got it all exactly right.
Can't say enough good things about Nahm. If you like Thai, you will love this place. Even if you aren't sure about Thai food - this place will spoil you.
Oh - if you don't know exactly where it is, you will have to keep a sharp eye out. It is set back from the road (Windward Parkway) behind Sushi Nami.
