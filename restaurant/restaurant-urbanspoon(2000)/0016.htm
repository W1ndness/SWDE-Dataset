Plenty of Charm Saddled with the Red Chile Alarm
by Drexler McStyles (205 reviews)
December 06, 2009 - Really likes it
I hadnt been to Cervantes in about a year. I remember loving the Old World Decor and that they have a pretty cool Bar. I also remember that the service in the front/main part of the restaurant not being so great. So we sat in the other dining area, near the bar. This area has crushed red velvet booths and walls, which are also adorned with bullfight paintings and other decadence. I love it, the place is oozing with a strong sense of history.
On their Signboard there was a warning that the Red Chile was VERY hot that day. This was good news for me but bad news for the wife.
I ordered their Carne Adovada plate, which comes with the standard Spanish Rice and Beans. My wife got the Combination Platter which is loaded up with a Taco, Cheese Enchilada, Carne Adovada and a Chile Relleno (and Rice & Beans). She was sure to get her Red Chile on the side.
There were a bunch of guys from the AirForce base a couple tables down, being crude and obnoxious, which kind of put a damper on our romantic night out. After running up a $200 tab they stiffed the waiter and left, which finally gave us a little peace and quiet. Anyhow, they were not joking about the Red Chile, it was HOT. My wife couldnt eat it at all, and I, who love some good spiciness, got a runny nose and went through more than the usual amount of beers and water trying to douse the fire inside my mouth. However, the food was really good. The Carne Adovada was nice and tender and I was given a large chunk of it. The Beans and Rice were everything they should be. The real surprise came on my wifes plate. If theres one type of Mexican food that I dont get very excited about it is (Beef) Tacos. To me there are 2 kinds; Good and Bad. However, the Taco I ate last night at Cervantes changed my thinking on that subject forever. It was the best Taco I ever tasted. The seasoning in the Beef was outstanding. We decided next time we went there one of us would order the Taco Plate. It was fabulous. My wife also thought that the Tamale was one of the best she's had in the Duke City.
I also want to say that our server was great. We wanted for nothing, and he kept a friendly professional attitude even after dealing with the military morons who were beyond demanding. Overall, we had a really nice meal, even with the crazy Red Chile. I look forward to going back again sometime soon (and get some of those Tacos).
UPDATE 1/14/10
Went back a couple nights ago and the Red Chile was retarded Hot again. Maybe Ive just gone on the wrong nights but they really need to do something about this. Most people would not be able to handle the Red.

Off-Beat New Mexican
by Chef Dare (167 reviews)
April 26, 2009 - Likes it
From the moment that you enter the dining room of Cervantes, you know that you are in for some sort of "experience"! The question is, "Will it be a good or bad experience"? The decor here is something akin to Spanish Gothic...lots of wrought iron and dark wood and dark colors. It is almost surreal. The service can be quirky. The best server that I have had here is an attractive older woman who reminds me of Flo from the old "Alice" tv series! The food too is just slightly different from other New Mexican restaurants. But everything is really quite good, too.

Love This Place
by Daytripper (6 reviews)
January 08, 2010 - Likes it
My wife and I can't go to Albuquerque without dinner at Cervantes. We had lived in ABQ in the 80's and learned to love it then. The chicken tacos were the best we had ever had back then and thought it was because they said they made the taco shells fresh. In later years the quality of the tacos went down a bit but the green chili enchiladas are to die for. Unique atmosphere but always a pleasure for us.
