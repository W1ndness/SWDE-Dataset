"Delectable and friendly"
by ou812 (2 reviews)
February 24, 2010 - Likes it
I found myself here on a quiet Tuesday night and I have to say this is a great place for a business traveler to eat alone. I sat at the bar and the service was fast and very friendly, and the few locals who were there were quick to strike up a conversation about their favorite menu items. I ordered the Lamb Chops - three of them, of reasonable dimensions, braised in a red-wine reduction -- carried with it spiced grace notes of molasses and cinnamon. Its accompanying side of savory sautéed spinach roasted root vegetables was very good. I was stuffed after all of that, but intrigued by the ravings of one regular I had to try the Bread Pudding for dessert. It was excellent, but I had to stop myself after only eating half of it as I feared I was going to put myself into a food coma! If I lived in Saint Louis, this would be one of my regular haunts! Great food! Great Service! I give these guys an A+. It did not disappoint.

Very Disapointed
by piasabird629 (1 review)
February 13, 2010 - Doesn't like it
Went to Osacr's on Friday Feb. 12th. to have a get together with local friends a relative in town from Texas and Tennessee. In total there were 10 of us. I will not speak for anyone other than myself and my wife,
We have been to Oscar's numerous times in the past and other than some service problems have always been pleased with the quality of the food. But on this evening thing were not up to past standards.
Our waiter John, made a point of informi us thatt they had a new chef who had come to them from Harvest Resturant..
We ordered an appitizer of their scallops, which were nicely pan seared and flavorable. While we waited for the appitizer bread (rolls) were served, warm and fresh. On the table was butter, olive oil and grated parmasian cheese. I was a little disapointed that fresh cracked pepper was not offerd to accompany the oilve oil for dipping. Anyway, the next course of a salad was served.
I did not order a salad but my wife had ordered the stawberry spinach salad. When serveres it had three very small pieces of strawberrys (not even full slices) and although it was supposed to have had bleu cheese crunbles, there were none. When we asked for additional bread, we were told that it was 'in the oven' and would just be a few minutes.
For dinner I had odered the lamb chops and my wife the sea bass. Although my wife didn't care for the bass, another member of our group who also had the bass thought it to be very good, so I'll just consider her dislike as a matter of personal taste. I returned my lamb chops to the kitchen because the vegitables server with it were cold. Most of thr table were fairly into their meals before I got my plate back from the kitchen. The vegatables were good but the chops were very fatty and only had about two eatable bites per each chop.
Back to the bread service. We requested bread at the time of salad service and finally got it when the entries were served. We were out of butter which was never replace, I was drinking coffee, I got one cup with the appitizer, another cup when others were eating their salads, and then went the entire meal without ever bering refilled or even aske about wanting another cup.
All in all the food was not of the quality to charge the $27.00 price, the service was very poor, and the kicker was that because of the size of our group there was a manditory 20% tip added to the bill.
A very dissapointing evening.
