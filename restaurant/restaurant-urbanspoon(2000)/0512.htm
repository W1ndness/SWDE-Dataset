Good Thai food - erratic service at Lunch
by John M (62 reviews)
March 04, 2010 - Likes it
You'll find this family run (Lek, Rudy and their sons) restaurant in a strip mall on the northside of Baymeadows just before the turnoff for Old Baymeadows.
The interior is chic industrial with SE Asian ornamentation. Service is generally efficient and quick but we've quit eating lunch there after two visits with very long waits for food (>35 minutes).
The menu is a comprehensive tour of Thai cuisine and the food is cooked in an exhibition kitchen. Everything I've ordered I've enjoyed.
The curries, pad thai and the fried snapper with ginger sauce top our list of favorites.
It is quite expensive.

Overpriced and Rude
by SpaceCadet (1 review)
June 28, 2009 - Doesn't like it
Everything with this restaurant seemed pretty routine until it was time for the check. The waitress delivered the check with the dessert and said the traditional "I'll take this when you're ready". As I began to enjoy my dessert, the waitress came back less than a minute later to pick up the check, but I had not gotten around to placing my debit card with the check since I was still eating. Again she said "Take your time" Not less than a minute after that a different waiter came to the table and said, "Can you pay? The waitress needs to leave" I then had to stop eating my dessert and attend to the check. I could not believe how rude this was! This restaurant needs to improve their customer service immensely if they want to charge $15-$25 per entree. My entree was also not prepared as ordered. I asked for medium spice and got no spice at all. The waitress then offered to bring me some chile spice from the kitchen which I then had to stir into my meal. Ridiculous!! Just go to Thai Palace instead. Their prices are much more affordable and the staff is attentive and pleasant.

Best Thai Restaurant in JAX
by John (1 review)
September 25, 2008 - Likes it
Lunch or dinner, this restaurant is the best in Jacksonville for Thai food. The family that owns it is all friendly and provides a fantastic dining experience. I have tried other places in the city but none compare to the personal involvement and recipes for food preparation. The ingredients are not just thrown in a wok to cook but Chef Soh introduces each ingredient at the appropriate time in the cooking process to bring out the flavors and desired crispness/consistency or temperature. The whole Red or Yellow Snapper is to die for. In fact, everything that I have eaten from the menu has been delicious. John
