Good pizza!
by Jackson (1 review)
July 07, 2010 - Likes it
I checked these guys out because the StamfordPizzaTour.com gave them a superlative award for "Best Delivery" -- and I was in the mood for pizza but didn't wanna go out. Tonight is my second time ordering and I definitely have to say I like it. I've been reviewing place on Yelp for years, but I'm new to Urban Spoon.
Here are my thoughts on their pie:
Crust: Thin and crispy, but not dry. Arrives slightly less crispy than I expect it would be in the joint.
Sauce: Fairly sweet tomato sauce. Quite good with a salty topping like sausage.
Cheese: Stringy, good flavor, but not gourmet or anything.
Toppings: Admittedly I've only tried the sausage, which incidentally comes as crumbles instead of little slices. I used to really like the slices of sausage from other places, but Ridgeway is actually beginning to sway me towards crumbles, because it spreads out the flavor, and prevents the little grease buckets from forming in the cup shaped sausage slices.
Price: Great delivery price.
Overall: Not too shabby. It's quickly becoming my favorite fast/cheap delivery pie in Stamford, when I don't want to drive to Amore or Sorrento's, or sit down at Remo's.

love the crust, sauce, ehhh...
by recovering doormat (8 reviews)
April 18, 2009 - Doesn't like it
After hearing my teen son rave about Ridgeway Pizza I decided to try it myself.
I ordered a plain cheese pie.
The crust was as described by others, nice and thin but not burnt at the edges, and most importantly not burned on the bottom (hear that, Planet Pizza?). The cheese was good, too, not dried out and not too gooey.
But I'm a tomato sauce fanatic, and I have to say it is just okay. I still like Sorrento's and John the Baker's better.
Still, not a bad pie. And better than the Planet Pizza cardboard disk I got last weekend.
