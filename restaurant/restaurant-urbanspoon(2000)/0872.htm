Going Green at Blue Planet
by Captain Napkin (284 reviews)
July 07, 2010 - Likes it
You can eat at Blue Planet and feel good about yourself. The food is tasty, and the restaurant takes pride in both their food as well as their dedication to recycling and cutting our carbon footprint. Service is casual: you order at the counter and they bring you your food. Lots of sandwiches, wraps, salads and similar fare. This place is also vegan friendly. Eat indoors or on their large patio. This is a nice place to go when you don't feel like dressing up yet you want something better than fast food.

by 2RG (54 reviews)
June 21, 2010 - Likes it
Wraps are tasty. So are the salads and sandwiches. Wife loved the mac and cheese (white cheddar with chicken). The baked fries and onion rings are good. Great iced tea, which is a rarity at most restaurants Portions are fair for the price. Lunch/dinner for two shouldn't run more than $20 unless you splurge. The outdoor dining is nice, and the staff friendly. Perfect place to grab a healthy, light, tasty meal.

Pricey and Gross Gluten-Free
by Jessica Buike (3 reviews)
August 09, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I've been there twice now, because I always believe in giving a place a second chance. The first time, I got a gluten-free hamburger.
The bun looked like something you'd find on a sidewalk left by a dog (if you know what I mean), and tasted really gritty and disgusting. I ended up eating the burger bunless, and it was only ok for the price that they charged.
The second time, I had their gluten-free pizza. I ended up just eating the toppings off the pizza, as the crust wasn't great. Once again, I felt like I was ripped off when it came to price for how little I ended up eating.
I also brought a vegan friend with me the second time, and she didn't like her vegan meal for the price either, so it's not just their gluten free foods that need improvement.
Overall, I would only eat there if I had to, because their prices are too high for such bad-tasting foods.

Tasty and healthy
by Dave Reid (5 reviews)
August 03, 2009 - Likes it
Moved into the new Broadmoor Aksarben Village apartments across the street and their menu looked good so we thought we'd give it a try.
I had a BLT with chipotle mayo. For a simple sandwich, it was very well portioned and was darn tasty, especially with the mayo. The fries that came with it tasted crisp and not the least bit oily. The different flavored ketchups are very interesting. My favorite had to be the garlic ketchup. My fiancée had the Hawaiian Ahi salad with a ginger sesame dressing, which she loved every bite of.
As far as service goes, you get your own drinks, but the employee who took our order was patient and helpful as it was our first time there. Our order was prompt and correct, so I can't complain!
Overall, it seemed like a very good place and we'll occasionally be back if we need something that we can walk to.
