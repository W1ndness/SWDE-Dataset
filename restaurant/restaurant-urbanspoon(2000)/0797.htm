Not worth it. Bland
by Simon (137 reviews)
October 14, 2009 - Doesn't like it
Not worth it. I have been here three times. Every time was bad.
You would think that a place that has a coal oven would deliver delicious meats and food. Unfortunately, not the case.
I paid over 20 dollars each time for ribs and chicken that was honestly... tasteless. And the portions were minuscule, except for the sides.
If I wanted to rely on bbq sauce, I would cook up a 3 dollar piece of meat, and slather a bunch of sauce on it. The meat and smoke make the flavor, not the sauce.
They did give us enough soggy fries, and so-so rice to feed an army though. But you know what that costs them... nada.
I don't want to feed my family on so-so rice, and otherwise bad food.
The place "looks" very nice and "smells" nice, but is WAY overpriced, and doesn't deliver.
Trust me on this, find a different Churrasqueira.
They can do better, I hope they change their process.
Until then, pass on it.

Ironbound In South Plainfield
by Big Foodie (11 reviews)
February 13, 2010 - Likes it
I'd bet if you surveyed 100 people: Where's the best Portuguese Food in NJ? South Plainfiled isn't the first answer that comes to mind.
But I would seriously urge anyone reading this to change their thinking.
Recently rennovated, Paulo's features a warm mediterranean ambience. A dining area that can accomodate a romantic dinner for two as easily as a party of 20 or more without a hitch. A beautiful stone patio for dining Al-Fresco in the warmer months, and a nice bar area for a pre-dinner cocktail, and a must stop spot for an after dinner drink.
The menu at Paulo's has all the best Portugouse fair. From wood fired Churasquieria style Chicken, Ribs and Steaks to Urban fav's like Paella, Garlic Shrimp and Tasty Chorizo.
Whatever your choice, the Overly-Generous portions will satisfy even the heartiest eaters.
Family run, the Owner Paulo greets you with a charming smile. He is a good hearted soul, who always makes you feel like part of his family. Beware gents, on nights with live music he may get your lovely bride up to dance.
Personal Fav's are the Pork and Clams Alentejana, Beef Ribs, Shrimp and Chorizo in Garlic Sauce. And their Rice... well, my wife has made me drive in many a snowstorm to get take out from Paulo's just because she had a craving for their rice.
As someone who knows their way around the backstreets of the Iornbound (not just Ferry Street) I can honestly say that Paulo's is a foodie's paradise.
Get Here, Get Fed, and Be Happy.

A SCAM - Do Not Go Here
by shoerepair509 (1 review)
September 11, 2009
If I could give this place a negative star rating, I would. Paulo's had advertised a $9 Rodizio weekday lunch special, I even called to confirm the price, date and time before coming. My friends and I LOVE rodizio, we normally go to Iberia's in Newark (Highly Recommended), but decided to try Paulo's due to this advertised special. My friends and I are in graduate school and normally do not go out to eat that often for no occasion, but made this an exception.
Let me first say that the food here could not hold a candle to Iberias. The stale garlic bread, taken out of a black garbage bag, seemed to be what was left over from prior customers. The meat either tasted bland or overly salted and was often times dangerously undercooked. The chocolate cake for dessert was advertised to be homemade, but tasted very much as if it were store bought. You could imagine my shock and embarrassment when we got the check and the $9 lunch special had some how turned itself into $87 (for 3 people) affair. When we asked about the bill the waiter told me we had eaten the regular price rodizio, not the lunch special portion. Not anywhere or at anytime was it said or written that portions were different for lunch and dinner pricing. When we then asked to speak to the owner (Paulo himself) he quickly escalated the confusion into a screaming match. A fellow business owner myself (my family owns a local business) I understand that he wants to make money, and told him such, but services can and should not be misrepresented. After a 10 minute argument, the owner finally agreed to knock off one of our $23 rodizios. That extra $20 that Paulo scammed out of me and my friends has just caused me to want to tell the world how terrible this place really is. Even if our bill was priced as advertised I still would not want to go back due to the poor food quality. The scam that Paulo's played on my friends and me, however, has caused me to write my first ever, positive or negative, restaurant review. Please, for your stomach and wallet's sake, do not go here.

Not what it used to be
by Chris S (4 reviews)
April 02, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I was probably one of the first hundred customers at Paulo's and remember the first time I drove by on my way to work what must have been 15 years ago. I immediately convinced a few coworkers to order takeout with me and a tradition was born. It used to be a 5 table + counter-top, 90% takeout, operation where you could feel the heat coming off of the rotisseries when you walked in the door. A pretty Latin woman cutting fries and chopping chicken, a "wise-ass" kid running the counter, and Paolo himself serving it all up.
Times have changed. Got a hankering for this the other night and decided to go over there for some takeout. Got the chicken and beef ribs combo and a "pig roast". It was all over cooked and salted like it was stowed in the Mayflower. Paulo's is not Iberia, if you want that, go to Newark, if you want "what it used to be", go to Grove BBQ.
