Great service and a darn fine oyster sampler
by Gail Emelko (17 reviews)
July 28, 2009 - Likes it
This was my 4th trip to Kings in Las Vegas for lunch, due to it being near where I stay in Henderson.
As usual, I chose to sit on the patio for a pleasant afternoon meal. I ordered a generously poured Grey Goose martini ($11.50) with freshly stuffed blue cheese olives, which went down way to smoothly on this particular afternoon.
I only wish the oyster sampler (9) wasn't $19.95 so I could eat my fill without breaking the bank. On this particular occasion, the wahoo was drier than usual and made me wish I had simply ordered another 9 pack of oysters. The northwest vegetables and mashed potatoes saved the lunch ($14.95).
As always, the staff friendly and extremely courteous.
They could teach quite a few restaurants the meaning of service professionals. I was fortunate not to experience any of the loud music issues reported by some other patrons.

Wow, customer service was out of this world!
by Lex (9 reviews)
March 11, 2009 - Likes it
Don't worry, my review of this place gets better at the end.
My wife and I enjoyed a dinner here. I had a fried seafood platter. I would rate it 5 out of 10. The crab cakes were downright HORRIBLE. But then again, I'm from Maryland the real home of crab cakes. So I'm a little bias. My food sucked. The fries were cold, the crab cake was yuck. The piece of fish was so small I wasn't sure if it was fish or a shrimp. The shrimp was really good though.
My wife went with the Shark which was EXCELLENT.
Even if the food was so/so, the customer service was out of this world.
First off, the waiter was quick and quite attentive. Halfway through our meal I had put my head in my hand (which made me look annoyed I guess, but really I was just tired since I only had 3 hours of sleep) and I guess an assistant manager came by and asked me if I was ok, and if the waiter did something wrong. He said it in a joking but kind fashion. I assured him our service was great and that I was just tired. Talk about paying attention to your customers.
But the next part is unheard of....
Later I reached over my wife, spilled her mixed drink all over jacket and seat (what happens in vegas....right) and immediately the waiter comes over and says "let me grab you some seltzer water soaked towels to clean off your jacket". After apologizing at least 3 times (as if he spilled the drink).
He comes back with 3 rolled up small towels soaked in seltzer water. Ok...that was nice but then check this out....
The manager comes over and apologizes (as if he spilled the drink as well) and chats it up a bit while she cleans her jacket. At the end of our dinner he comes over and hands us an envelope. And apologizes again, it's an envelope with $20 (cash), and a gift card for free appetizer.
WOW! He says "that should cover your dry cleaning".
Now my wife is still confused (well we both are) we're not sure if he thought the waiter spilled it (even though I specifically told him I spilled it) or if he was truly just going above and beyond the call of duty. I like to think it was the latter. Now that's customer service, every company should take note of that.
I promised myself i would write review just because we were so impressed with the service (granted the food was OK, hit or miss).
