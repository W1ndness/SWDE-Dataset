mobile review
by Donald (22 reviews)
April 03, 2010 - Likes it
Though everybody is free to thie own opinions, to the three who complained I say this: first, to the one who complained about the prices. They're maybe $0.60 each. A meal is usually what, three donuts at the most? So $1.80 plus maybe a $1 coffee. I don't know, I don't drink coffee. So $2.80 for breakfast. You can't go to McDonald's and get a breakfast and drink there for $2.80. So why are you complaining? Second, to the person who complained about them being too greasy and having too much sugar. Really? That's your complaint? Do some homework. Donuts are fried and they have sugar on top. That is what a donut is. If that's not what you want, get a waffle out of your freezer and heat it up in your toaster. I can't believe you would write a bad review about a donut shop because their donuts have grease or because of all things they have a lot of sugar. Get real! Lastly, to the employee whose boss brings you day old donuts. First, they're free. Thank your boss and stopped being spoiled. Second, the only way your boss has day old heavenly donuts is if he/she brings to you on Thursday what his family couldn't finish on Wednesday. Thee Heavenly Donut does not have the kitchen size of the fast food donut shops. You can't blame them for cooking their donuts before their rush time. If you want hot donuts, tell your boss to order the donuts before he/she goes to get them. I have had to wait on my order because I've called ahead and told them I would be by for 8am and I arrived early. So I know from personal experience that they will cook your order fresh. I have lived all over Southeast Louisiana, and I find that Thee Heavenly Donut is as good of a donut shop as the best I've been to. Though my family has not been there but a handful of times, we always leave very pleased with food and service! I wish we would have found Thee Heavenly Donut on Highland sooner. We will continue to be customers.

who dat cakes are the best
by Paul (1 review)
January 21, 2010 - Likes it
We live near the Highland Road store and have watched the sign change from Christmas Wreath cakes, to King Cakes, and now the Who Dat cakes. Tucked away next to the Hancock Bank at the intersecion of Perkins and Highland (near Troop A) is the best little donut shop in town. Kara and her staff there are the friendliest people I have encountered in a bakery in a long time. I have been to another fancier place and not only been treated like an unwanted guest, but almost passed out at the prices. i have a serious problem when I have to wait for a server to either finish their cell phone call or their text. At least here when I walk in, they remember me and often start bagging up my donuts without even asking. Now I know that I can change my order, but it's nice that someone remembers you and your usual. I read on here that someone thought they had second day donuts and all I can say is someone is not being honest. Since there are no preservatives in their donuts (cause I asked the server), they don't last for days anyway. I have been there at 4:30 am when half the shelves are empty and they are preparing fresh donuts. I can see where you might get an hour old donut, but I have a hard time believing that they got day old donuts. Especially when you see them box them up right in front of you. I guess we are all allowed to have our own opinions, but this web page is to be truthful about what you say - cause some people actually read these reviews and it makes a difference. And i just want to say if you have never had a kolache, they are great and you owe it to yourself to try one. It's probably one of the items that disappears the fastest - I have called in to order in advance and they always have them ready when we pickup. I give them a high five with taste, variety, friendliness, cleanliness, and price. Kara and crew - keep it up! Good job!!

Best Bakery & Staff in BR
by Sheryl (3 reviews)
January 21, 2010 - Likes it
I have been in both locations, but I prefer the Highland Road store since it is more convenient for me. The donuts are always fresh - I mean it's like they can't hardly keep them on the shelf. So, all you people walking in for 3 dozen need to call ahead so people like me can get just a few! LOL The king cakes are absolutely fantastic! This past weekend we actually tried the "Who Dat" cake and WOW - almost as good as the game Sat night!!! It's refreshing to walk in and have the staff remember you and speak to you - almost like a family atmosphere. I think their product line is great and the prices are fair - they are less expensive than other shops in the area, and they are definately are fresher. You can almost watch as the donuts are glazed! doesn't get any fresher than that! I recommend you stop by and meet the staff and try a donut or apple fritter or maybe get a king cake - it will be an absolutely delicious experience!!
