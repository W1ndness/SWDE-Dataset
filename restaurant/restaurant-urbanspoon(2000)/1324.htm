Not quite In-N-Out Burger
by jburwen (5 reviews)
August 06, 2008 - Doesn't like it
As a native Californian, this review is surely quite biased. Elevation Burger is definitely attempting to be to the east coast what In-N-Out Burger is to the west. Unlike In-N-Out, though, it's not necessarily worth a long detour when you're driving through the area.
(Disclosure: I am a pescatarian. I eat the "grilled cheese" option when I go to an In-N-Out burger joint, and otherwise I only eat burgers made from fish or soy protein. Arguably, I am not an expert. Nonetheless, I believe my review can still inform your choice.)
Like In-N-Out, they go for the joy of simplicity: burgers are your only option. However, they go a step further in that there are a few veggie burgers to choose from--one "meat-like" and one "veggie-like." As a vegetarian, I was quite pleased. I was further pleased by the "Vertigo Burger" option, which allows you to stack up to 10 patties onto a single burger. (I also laughed at the "Half Guilt" burger, which stacks a veggie patty on top of a meat patty.)
I ordered a two-stacker of the vegetarian burgers, one of each kind of patty, but was informed that they were out of meat-like ones. Undaunted, I accepted a two-stacked veggie-like burger with melted cheddar, accompanied by fries and a chocolate shake.
The veggie burger was soft and squishy; biting into the burger caused it to squish out the sides. Not cool. Thank goodness they wrap their burgers in a tight envelope the way they do at In-N-Out.
The fries were tasty, but very very thin and quite oily. I was told that they fry their potatoes only in olive oil, which sounds like a great tasty idea. But I also know that olive oil isn't exactly a high-heat oil, and so that's some heart-clogging fries. On the other hand, they give you some thousand island dressing for dipping, so it's not like you had any pretense to health anyway.
The shake was solid--just that right mix of ice cream solid and milky liquid. I noticed that they also stocked 3-4 kinds of root beer, which made me ponder the root beer float possibilities should I ever return.
All told, though, it was pretty underwhelming. My meat-eating compadres seemed to enjoy their experience a bit more--understandably, since burgers are meant for meat. No one, however, seemed to think the food was particularly interesting, and Falls Church can be a pretty long way to go for a burger...
