A nice, middle-of-the-road Italian joint. I can see how people would make Carmine's their regular Saturday night date spot - it was good food at reasonable prices. We had the hot stuffed peppers, a couple of pasta dishes, and finished up with cannoli and gelato. We liked everything, including the cannoli which is head and shoulders above Chef's. We'll be back.

Worst Restaurant EPISODE of my life!
by Tony (4 reviews)
June 14, 2010 - Doesn't like it
My wife and I gave this place a try late Saturday night. As we were entering we specifically checked the hours on the door to make sure we weren't keeping anyone. When we walked in, we seated ourselves at the bar and were welcomed by a friendly bartender. Dont remember his name, but that's not important. We were quick to order two glasses of wine from the list and then inquired if the kitchen was still open, We were given a vaugue respose, something to the effect of , it shouldnt be a problem. I didnt really get it, but anyways .....It either is or it isnt. We weren't all that hungry we just wanted something to snack on with our wine. Anyways, we quicly placed an order basically just asking the bartender witch two appetizers he would recommend. Soon after the order was placed two young men came out of the kitchen swearing and making a scene in very foul terms about how unhappy they were that they just got this order. Now mind you, the sign on the door said they were still open and this was verified by the barender. So after the bartender shuffled these two back behind the doors the bartender returned and with this look of shame on his face almost couldnt address me. So with that I said to him, that I wasn't trying to upset anyone and just cancel the order not to worry about it. I could do without it. Basically he told me that if I didnt take the food it was going to make a bad thing even worse. I told him I didnt want to eat anything that someone was so upset about preparing for me. I didnt trust them. So he went into the kitchen and came back a minute later and said to me "It's too late" they had already started the order and that I was basically stuck with it. aA this time he made it clear to me that during this entire episode I was sitting right next to the owner, who had failed up until this point to even address the situation at all! so after I was forced to buy food that I didnt want after the staff had a 2 yr old fit over, The owner delivered one of the plates personally and could barley even put together 3 words to address what just took place. I was charged for everything, didnt recieve an appology, and was kind of made to feel as tho I was doing something wrong by even being there in the first place. What in the world are people thinking when they let their emloyees get away with this sort of thing and don't bother to address the situation at all? What a disgrace , the owner should be ashamed of himeself! I wouldn't spend another nickle in there , What a mess! I've never seen anything like that in my entire life. Besides the food was as poorly prepared and presented as anything Ive eaten in a long time. Sorry but a side of tarter sauce isnt a good dip for "Artichoke Fritters". And as far as the "shrimp cargo" . If your going to leave something hanging out in a freezer for 6 months try wrapping it so when the food makes it over tot the microwave and into my mouth Idont feel like im licking the freezer walls! Looks like the kitchen is as bad with food as they are with making people feel welcome! If that guy still works there today , shame on everyone involved! Even myself, for paying for that garbage!

Stick with the Pizza
by Randy (6 reviews)
September 19, 2009 - Doesn't like it
The pizzas are made in a brick oven pizza and are really terrific. Nice thin but hard crust, just the right amount of sauce and cheese.
However, the spaghetti and meatballs were bland, the salad was totally without flavor, and the dressings come from a bottle. Stick with the pizza and leave the other items for home cooking.
