Good but not brilliant
by godot (21 reviews)
June 20, 2010 - Likes it
The food was good, and I have no complaint about the service, but it's not something I'd write home about. Certainly it was a nice change from typical local fare. But I have to say it makes me wince when I read a restaurant review (like one I saw for this restaurant in a local paper) and everything is compare to Japanese food. That said, I take most local reviews with a grain of salt (which is not something to hold against a restaurant). The food was good and simple. The prices somewhat higher than noted. Entrees range to $22, and the $10 "entrees" are sandwiches. Appetizers cost from $3 for a cup of black bean soup to $15 for a sampler, or seafood. The salads were in the high range at $7-15 for a side salad. Dinner for the 4 of us was easily over $100, and we had the moderately priced chicken entrees and a shared apetizer.
This is a restaurant I'd return to to break the monotony, but I'm not likely to become a regular, and it is far from my personal favorite.

Recommend | Not helpful

Nice meal
by Pompiere (52 reviews)
May 03, 2010 - Likes it
Great meal, reasonably priced. Our only complaint was how loud it was when other diners slid the wrought iron chairs on the tiled floor. Like scratching a chalkboard in a small room. The Mojo grilled shrimp on the appetizer sampler were the best grilled shrimp I've eaten in 40 years, and I love shrimp. The Ropa Veija was also perfect. The black bean soup was uncharacteristically boring and the Mojito was overly sweet, but all in all a nice meal.

My favorite restaurant in Honolulu
by Alicia (2 reviews)
December 06, 2008 - Really likes it
My fear of reviewing a good restaurant is that it will become so busy that the next time I go, I won't be able to get in. The lighting, artwork, Caribbean-African music, and friendly wait staff is only part of what makes Soul de Cuba a memorable experience. The food is wonderful, and quite reasonable. Make sure you save room for the Flan! I've ordered flan all over the world and this version, with the pairing of platano maduro is, well, just spectacular. And yes, you'll probably need reservations. Enjoy.
