It was good but curious about all the RAVE reviews
by foodsnob (421 reviews)
June 25, 2010 - Likes it
Husband and I finally had our sushi craving again and headed out to Lenexa last night to check out all of these ravings about Edokko. Well, granted we've only had one visit but I can't see justifying all the gushing reviews. It is a very nice, attractive, and clean restaurant that is quite warm and inviting-even serene. Our service could have been better, we were seated at the sushi bar and had to ask for a salad as we had not been asked about anything beyond our drinks. Which brings me to the drinks-we ordered drinks before we ordered our salad and had to remind them to get them before we began our sushi and didn't have them with our salad-so that was at least a 15-20 min. wait. Our seaweed salad was 1/2 seaweed salad and 1/2 jullianed cucumber. The sushi was prepared nicely but room temperature and tasted good but I didn't feel it was as good as Shogun which isn't that far away, my husband thought it was as good as Shogun. In all we still like Jun's best. I would by all means go there again but if I was closer to Shogun I'd go there instead. Better yet if I was headed out to do sushi then I'd just pick Jun's. But, if you are in the neighborhood try it.

My Sushi Spot
by Chris Kelly (1 review)
June 11, 2009 - Really likes it
Since Edokko's grand opening I have enjoyed this restaurant around 20 times. All of those visits are due to the amazing service and quality which I receive every time I stop in.
Upon entering, the first thing you will likely notice is the appearance of this establishment. Framed art, ambient lighting, and mosaic sumo wrestlers are just the beginning. Top to bottom, it is simply immaculate.
Every visit I am seated promptly, often by the owner herself. After a brief and pleasant talk, I'll order something off either the kitchen or sushi menu. Either way, you can't go wrong. Even the noodles have presentation value with fresh cut citrus framing your entree. The sushi is fresh, and delicious displaying generous proportion.
Edokko's bar is fully stocked, and is home to imported ales and my personal favorites.. the sake based drinks.
It doesn't just end with the atmosphere and authentic foods, Edokko is host to special events and Meetup* groups. I enjoyed a sushi and sake pairing which was fantastic, both informative and satisfying.
Please, if you've never been.. and if you're trying to decide if you should. Just go. You will find new food, new friends and a new restaurant to relax in and forget your troubles.

Service is a 5/10, food is a 13/10
by Forrest Saucier (1 review)
January 20, 2010 - Likes it
I'll be honest, having gotten take-out and eaten in at this restaurant wait times can be annoying. But considering the epic taste of the food I could care less. I'd stand a day in the freezing cold wearing a speedo for most of their food.
The Seafood Udon is simply the best noodle-soup I've ever had. I'm a spoiled california native, and the seafood is still fresh enough for me and very well prepared. The portion is also massive, I challenge anyone to finish is after and appetizer or roll has already been enjoyed. BIG BOWL! =)
Magic Mushroom appetizers positively explode in your mouth.
And having sampled half a dozen sushi dishes from the maki menu, every roll is spot on.
Usually you can identify so-so sushi by textures being off, and all of these are incredibly well executed in texture.
And as a date destination, a friend and I got out for 50 bucks - two rolls, soup, appetizer, two mixed drinks. Not bad at all!
Conclusion: Your waitress may be hard to find, but the food is worth any amount of time and money.
