by ADM (1 review)

My husband and I love the pizza here. We go about once a week. The thin crust is amazing, the ingredients are fresh, and the place has character. I live nearby, and am severely opposed to the air quality problems and sustainability issues a coal oven will pose. I will certainly stop eating here if it occurs, and will spread the word to friends and neighbors as well. I just feel that everyone, including the local businesses in Portland, need to be good neighborly citizens.

It's all about the bread(crust)
by Garlic Joe (4 reviews)
September 10, 2009 - Likes it
I initially was addicted to Al Forno when I walked up to the Purple People Feeder on 4th the first day it opened (just outside my office building). I walked up and said "give me something good!". The Panino-now called Scarpetta- was some of the best food to cross my face. Perfect combo of good(-real?-) dough and quality ingredients. When I learned that they were selling the cart and opening the resteraunt, it was a sad day indeed. Skipping my white coller duties (no hippies in my family) I ventured to the new digs to see if the flavor perserviered... it did! I used to be a "Chicago" type that liked the 2 inches of toppings (Flying Pie) but learned that both pies can be good. The focus with Al Forno is clearly that they want to use the best ingredients available, local, organic, homegrown... good.
Crust is nicely charred on the bottom and chewy on the top. If you want a slice, you will miss out on the full goodness, as the only way to get this food is right out of the kiln. It's all about the bread.

Worst vegan food ever
by Nodrog (1 review)
April 15, 2009 - Doesn't like it
So, I called in advance and was reassured that they'd be happy to make a "vegan pizza". As Bella Faccia down the street, and Hot Lips not much farther away, do fantastic vegan pizza, I was optimistic. It's easy to be vegan in Portland, I thought -- look at all the pizza places that do vegan pizza! Plus the reviews of Al Forno Ferruza's food cart are undeniably stellar.
Imagine a too-thin pizza crust. Big dough bubbles, quite burned on the top. Small wet spots in the valleys between the bubbly peaks where there was a teaspoon, sometimes maybe a bit more, of admittedly tasty garlicky/oregano-y pesto. It was a surprisingly small, bubbly, burned, dry round thing with a very minimal and patchy amount of of pesto. About 80% of it was mostly dry, burnt crust, really. It was ridiculous. We sat around and joked about it.
Oh yeah, and we'd ordered "Spinach" on top. We got what looked like a small handful of "baby greens" (like you'd buy a bag of at the store) thrown on top before it went in the too-hot oven -- the remaining bits of greens were crispy, dried out, and pretty burnt. And I paid $20 for this?
It was by far the worst vegan food of any kind I've had in any restaurant in Portland. Ever. I have no doubt that the rave reviews of their normal cheese pizza are accurate. But they have no business saying they actually have vegan food when they haven't a clue how to do vegan pizza. Really.
We'll be returning to Bella Faccia for good vegan pizza.
