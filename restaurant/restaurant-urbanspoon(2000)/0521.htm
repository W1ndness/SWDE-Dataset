It's all good.
by 76mph (10 reviews)
February 17, 2010 - Likes it
Very happy to see this organization expanding while maintaining the quality and consistency of their product.
Everything on the menu is awesome. Although I have my favorites, I try to force myself to try something new each time because I'm confident that whatever I order will be good. They never disappoint.
The quality of the product is a direct result of the quality of the ingredients. Fresh crisp veggies and high quality meats are the cornerstone of their success. You can taste it in the sandwiches, homemade sauces, and exceptional sides.
My personal favorites are the Buffalo Burger (request a fried egg on top), the Classic, and the Mahi Mahi. All the sides are good, but I'm particularly partial to the onion rings.
AND THE BAR! Outstanding beer selection, creative cocktails (the Lone Horse Feather is the perfect spring afternoon drink), and Grown Up Milkshakes complete the experience.
Their are a few drawbacks that require mentioning. The dining room is smallish, so expect to wait to get a seat. The food also sometimes takes a while to get to you. These are to be expected in a restaurant that is committed to delivering a quality food, and I assure you your meal will be well worth the wait . . . just go in with that expectation.
And my one personal pet peeve that can hardly be held against the proprietors: In the warmer months, on weekends it turns into something of a "after soccer game hang out" for the children of Leawood. (I remember get orange slices after the game when I was kid). Although I have no idea why these people would bring their kids up with such heightened expectations regarding a burger experience, that certainly is their business, not mine. Just a word of warning that if you go on a Sunday afternoon in the summer, you may have to wade through 10 year-olds in all their sweaty, soccer glory. Personally, I'll wait till after their bedtime. The burgers are well worth the wait.

Good stuff!
by SparkleAggie (45 reviews)
August 12, 2009 - Likes it
Mookman and I had dinner at Blanc last night. As he mentioned below, we were expecting to wait for a table, but we got right in at 6:00. Shortly thereafter, though, they were on a wait.
I love the interior of both locations of Blanc. The crisp white of the furniture and walls, paired against vibrant orange accents really give it a hip, metropolitan feel. One of my favorite restaurants in Omaha, Bianco, has an almost identical motif, so much so, that I was left wondering if the owners may have visited the Omaha contemporary Italian restaurant for inspiration.
This time, we ordered a Caesar salad to split, and the mahi mahi "burger" and Au Poivre burger. The Caesar, for me, was nothing to write home about. My fiancee seemed to like it, though.
The fish sandwich was light and tasty, and came with a little slaw and tartar sauce on the bun. Pretty good.
When they named the Au Poivre burger, they weren't kidding around. The black pepper coating around the meat was extra heavy. I ended up trying to scrape most of it off, and even then it was too much for me. I'm not complaining, though, after all, if you order an Au Poivre burger, you're going to get an Au Poivre burger. It's Mookman's favorite.
I would reccomend their bison burger, and to drink, The Kansas City Bulldog. The bison burger comes with pepperjack cheese, peppadew pepper jam, butter lettuce, onion, tomato, and mayo. The meat has so much flavor, and the "accessories" really pull together this great burger. Plus, bison meat is leaner than beef, generally. I also highly reccomend the Kobe burger. Dee-licious!
The Kansas City Bulldog is awesome. A fresh take on the standard Colorado Bulldog, our city's namesake has vodka, kahlua, local Shatto rootbeer milk, and a splash of soda. What a creation! I love the integration of the Shatto milk. Order this.
We still love Blanc. We like the convenience and openness of the floorplan in Leawood, but I I like the feel of the Westport location a little better. Then again, I'm still coming to terms that we don't live in our beloved Midtown and Waldo areas anymore, so I might be a little biased. No matter which location you choose, you're going to get a quality burger in an upscale setting, and I'm pretty sure you'll come back for more.
