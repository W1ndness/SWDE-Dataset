Tops for dessert
by undorgre (34 reviews)
August 17, 2009 - Really likes it
If you are out to satisfy your sweet tooth then Bailey's Chocolate Bar is the place to stop. My sister and nephew accompanied on my first visit to Bailey's and we were not disappointed. I tried some of the Chocolate Brownie dessert and it was excellent... the center of the brownie was gooey and the portion was large (enough for 2 people). The crème brulee was average, but I've had better. For myself I ordered from their cheese menu and I was glad I took that route. I ordered the Drunken Goat cheese, which is a mild and creamy cheese that went great with cranberries. I'd recommend this to anyone. My other cheese choice was the Roquefort and it was as good of a bleu cheese as you'll find. The cheeses are served with plenty of house breads, fruits, and nuts.
My sister was impressed with the chocolate martinis and from the sips I took they seemed to be good if you're into that kind of thing. I had a glass of the Young's Double Chocolate Stout. I'd rate it as very good but it is a pretty heavy beer, which I enjoyed with the cheeses.
The service was good and the atmosphere at Bailey's is dark and candlelit, a good spot for a date. There is a bar at the front of this establishment that would be good if you just wanted to pop in for something quick.

Dark and Delicious
by Brett (19 reviews)
March 31, 2008 - Likes it
We had a magical experience at Bailey's Chocolate Bar. From the dark, romantic dining area to the knowledgeable and not too chummy staff, the place is dynamite. We began our evening with the adventurous drinks menu. We mostly drank different kinds of beer and some wine by the glass. The best of the night was a mustard ale that tasted a lot better than what it sounds. Other highlights were O'Fallon IPA on tap and a delicious "Black and Red" (stout and framboise). From there we ordered a cheese platter. All of our cheeses were great; my favorite was an aged cheddar that bit back a little. Others liked the "Drunken Goat," a medium-hard goat cheese from Spain. The focaccia bread that accompanies the cheese is superb as are the little spiced nuts and other goodies added to the plate for fun. And, of course, we ended the evening with delicious desserts. My pear bread pudding with chocolate was pretty good as were the banana and nutella crepes of a companion, but I felt that neither was outstanding-I feel pretty confident that either dessert could be easily replicated in a home kitchen. But the single-serving Irish Cream cheesecake my wife ordered was really good, the kind of thing you couldn't make for yourself. The flavor of the liqueur was intense, and the cheesecake was firm in the mouth. The Chocolate Bar is a unique place to go to celebrate a special event or for a romantic evening. I hope we can return soon.
