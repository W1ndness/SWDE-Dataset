Absolutely NOT Fabulous
by jean (1 review)
December 23, 2009 - Doesn't like it
If you have a sense of humor and money to burn, it is worth a visit to experience the appalling service. The food is mediocre and doesn't really warrant a review at all. But the service...
We arrived on a bitter cold Wednesday night to find a couple of four-tops filled in the dining room, and the bar crammed with loud-mouths who appeared to know the owners. The first server ignored us, as she was busy writing down the specials on her notepad. The second server shambled over a while later, and asked if we'd like to sit in the bar. We said we'd prefer the dining room, but since she shoved us to a table right outside the kitchen door, we reluctantly accepted a table in the bar area.
Throughout the evening a cast of characters came in through the back door (leading outside), blasting us with freezing cold air, carrying trays of food, unwashed heads of lettuce, a whole assortment of stuff, from somewhere outside into the kitchen. Our server asked if we needed plates for our appetizers, or could we just go with the bread plates (never mind the film of the house-made hot pepper oil - goes well enough with lamb on croutons, right?) The service went down from there, culminating with the assertion that my husband's credit card had been rejected, with a parade of servers weighing in on the matter (you just need to swipe it through seven or eight times; it's a comcast issue), and my husband going out into the freezing cold to the ATM machine (they offered to just write down our address and we could mail a check, but we never want to deal with them again). We walked by last night on our way to Turners, and "Ab Fab" was DEAD, and Turners was HOPPING. Well deserved. I would like to see them gone very soon, replaced by a down-to-earth, reasonably priced, unpretentious solidly good neighborhood restaurant.

Defending the bad press
by L (1 review)
February 16, 2010 - Likes it
My husband and I went to AbFab Saturday Feb 13 -- we were iffy because of the bad reviews here, but went anyway (we live in Melrose). We made a reservation, sat towards the back, and proceeded to order lots of food. The service, while not perfectly polished, was more than fine, very attentive, and I thought the pace was great -- no complaints at all there. My only gripe was that our 2-top was very small, we had the mussels (amazing) and the large dish barely fit in the middle of the table with our other app plates, glasses, wine, etc.
We then split a salad, very generous portion, again, timing was fine. I had the seared scallops with saffron risotto, and my hubby had steak -- both were to die for. My husband's steak was so big we took some home. We had dessert as well. The value was the most impressive part -- for mussels, salad, bottle of wine, two mains, and a dessert our bill was $112. That is very impressive and it was a lot of really great food.
Now, could this place survive if it were in the South End? Probably not, but for higher-end suburban dining, it was really great -- we will go back, often...hoping to try the brunch next. I think the biggest thing you notice is the difference in the service (mainly, and not to sound snarky, but your waitstaff should know how to pronounce the food names). We had a great night and think you all should check out this new local hotspot!
Also, to the people who had a to wear a coat while dining, we weren't bothered by that, just have a glass of wine and you'll warm right up!

Fabulous!
by stephanie (1 review)
December 31, 2009
I'm surprised by the reviews I'm reading - both the reviews themselves and also perhaps the expectations??
Had dinner with my mother at AB on a Wednesday evening, relatively early. We were pleased to see that we weren't the first patrons - usually a sign that the experience will be good. The food was fantastic and fresh. The menu is simple elegance. I could have ordered almost anything on it, and I happen to be one of those people who struggles to find a dish I like and even after I do I usually alter it somehow when ordering. Don't be fooled - the food is jam packed with flavor. The service was lovely. We had an opportunity to chat with one of the owners and learned about how the restaurant came to be.
Many of the reviews indicate poor service and while I don't agree - I'm also surprised at the examples provided. The restaurant is brand new. It is not a chain. And it wasn't opened by a family with six other joints. The two owners took what was previously a catering business and a very successful weekends only spot to a full time restaurant. Have some patience people! If you're expecting white glove service then I agree - go elsewhere. If you're expecting friendly, laid back service then this could be the place for you! For those of you who were unsatisfied with your experience, I'd suggest you give Absolutely Fabulous another shot. And I'd also recommend that in addition to taking to your thoughts to the internet, that you also let the owners know. I found them to be more than accomodating - open to concerns and questions.
Overall we had a lovely dinner and I plan to return in the near future!

Nice Try, but It's Spilling over the Edges
by Peter (1 review)
January 27, 2010
Went to join friends on a Monday nite. There were over 8 of us at the table, so was able to have a wide sample across their menu. After many years myself in the hospitality, restaurant, and catering business, here's the pro's and con's
Pro's:
Nicely decorated--warm; attempt to inject contemporary decor
Amazing food and recipes-chef clearly takes great pride in the food
Friendly bartendar
Established following of local patrons
Con's:
Layout of the space in the front room was terrible. People complained of being very cold if you were near the front door. Ask for a table in the back.
Waited a loonnnnnnnngggggg time for the dinners to hit the table--a common occurrence in the dining room--even after the kitchen was running our order, our table took over ½ hour span for the dishes to get from kitchen to the table. Polite folks who got their meals first at our table had a mighty cold dinner as their reward for their good manners. Think about bringing a book or a board game to pass the time, and if questioned, cheerfully let the staff know you were made aware of how slow the kichen is and wanted to make sure you were constructively using the time. They haven't gotten the message yet with the bad reviews I'm seeing out there.
Wait staff clearly knows about the gap--however, no time was taken to span that gap--filling water glasses, refreshing drink orders, schmoozing and glad handing the diners. Plenty of time to molly-coddle to disguise the huge flaw of bad kitchen timing. Our table went without water refills or refreshed drink orders (a hi gross profit item for food service). Sip your drink slowly or prepare to be parched.
Summary: Don't go if you are running a tight time schedule. Chef has great pride, but too much control., and needs to have staff trained to her exacting standards. They moved from a small spot to this larger location--Chef cannot continue to try to micro manage every dish--she's got to have staff in place to overcome the timing, because even her food is not going to surmount the constant dissapointment that will continue from the diners over the delays in getting dinner onto the tables.
