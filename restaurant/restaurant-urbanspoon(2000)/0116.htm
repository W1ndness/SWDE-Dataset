In a town filled with BBQ, Saw's will renew the faith
by Charlie (182 reviews)
October 13, 2009 - Really likes it
There are a lot of BBQ restaurants in Birmingham, and many that are just ok. Some are good at a few things, and slack on the rest of the menu. Not Saw's.
I've been eating BBQ all of my life, and Saw's tops it all. They don't have a crazy big menu like some of the local joints that have tried to go big-time (like Jim N Nicks, Golden Rule, etc). At the same time, their menu isn't as limited as other Alabama staples (like the original Dreamland was). Saw's serves something for everyone, but sticks to the basics, and does the basics very very well.
Like I've been telling all of my friends, maybe Saw's doesn't have the very best pork sandwich, or the very best white sauce, or the very best sides. However, everything they do have is so close to the very best... and the overall effect is that nothing, ever, is lacking. If you were to assign a ranking to all of their food items from 1-10 and average it out, it'd be almost a perfect 10 across the board... so unlike the big spots as well as those that do too little.
One thing that Saw's might do the very best in all of the BBQ land: the Baker BBQ potatoes. Bigger than a collegiate football, tastier than anywhere else.

Carolina Q Invades B'ham
by B.C. (33 reviews)
March 02, 2010 - Doesn't like it
There really should be a sign hanging over the door that reads "you ain't from around here, are you boy?". Don't get me wrong, the food is good if you're a fan of Carolina style pulled pork, it's just not my fave.
The pork was abundant and very tender and smelled smokey but tasted more of vinegar.. Great if you're in the Tar Heel state, but this is Alabama. The sauce was way too tame for my tastes. It's a simple vinegar and sugar type sauce with a few flakes of pepper for effect but certainly not for flavor. The turnip greens were cooked nicely and are worth a shot, but I'd like them far better if they weren't so sweet.
Saws is a cool little spot and it was slammed when we went at lunch. Clearly lots of folks dig the place, but I'll stick with Bob Sykes, Dreamland, Demetris and Full Moon.

Best BBQ in Birmingham
by Ryan (1 review)
June 12, 2010 - Really likes it
When Saw's opened up down the street from us in Edgewood, I was a little hesitant at first. The former BBQ joint at that location (Broadway BBQ) was ok, but not great, and so I thought that this would be more of the same. My wife and I ventured over about a month or so after they had been open to try it out. The results were outstanding!
The atmosphere is typical of many different BBQ places, with plenty of old car tags, Alabama football garb, and other odds and ends finding a home on the wall, but because it is such a small place it feels more homey. Service is friendly and speedy as you will almost always find the owner circling around tables and talking with customers.
I think that Saw's is easily the best BBQ in Birmingham and it is always my first recommendation when guests from out of town ask for a place to eat. All of their meats are tender and perfectly flavored. Their sides are homemade and delicious. And their BBQ stuffed Bakers are my favorite meal in town. Do yourself a favor and be sure to eat at Saw's soon! You won't regret it and I'm sure you will be back again and again.
