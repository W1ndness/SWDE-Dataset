Message from King Noodle

New Asian Restaurant Offering Excellent Vietnamese & Japanese Cuisine

  • Happy Hour & Sushi Bar
  • Vietnamese & Japanese Menu
  • Lunch & Dinner in Cedar Park

Newly opened in Cedar Park, King Noodle Vietnamese Cuisine & Sushi House offers excellent Vietnamese & Japanese entrees for lunch and dinner. You'll enjoy the high quality offerings such as any number of vermicelli or noodle-soup bowls, as well as several shrimp, beef and chicken entrees offered over rice. In addition, King Noodle also offers a top notch Japanese menu including beef, chicken, salmon & shrimp Teriyaki, as well as tempura. We offer a full sushi menu featuring all your favorite sushi rolls which can be enjoyed with a glass of wine or beer. King Noodle also offers several delicious vegetarian dishes. And don't forget dessert for your whole group or casual date! Join us for Sushi Happy Hour weekdays 3-5pm or feel free to place your orders to-go. Sometimes bad is BAD
by JD (2 reviews)
December 13, 2009 - Doesn't like it
I wish I had checked out the reviews from other sites before ordering from King Noodle. There is absolutely nothing here worth paying for or even writing about except to serve as a warning to others.
This restaurant offers a fairly complete list of Vietnamese, Chinese, and Japanese items so I picked something from each. All were abysmally bad and nowhere near worth the price paid. I suppose the fusion of too many styles and the complete vacancy of the restaurant during the prime time dinner hour should have been a red flag. But hope spring eternal I guess and I was caught up in the cuteness of the name, King Noodle. It sounded so cool in my head.
The Sushi was old and poorly prepared with no attempt at any kind of enjoyable presentation. The Sushi at your local HEB is a better deal at half the price. Nobody with me wanted to try the King Noodle Sushi based on it's appearance so I managed to swallow a few greasy pieces before dropping the rest of my $20 in the dustbin. I ordered two completely different Chinese dishes (one supposedly SPICY and one sweet) and none of us could tell the difference between them in appearance or flavor. No kidding. No attempt was made to disguise the fact that the cook just couldn't be bothered to make two different entrees. Nope. Just take the chicken from over here and plop it over there and it's done. The Miso soup was a sludge of too much tofu and seaweed. The only redeeming portion in my order was the rice.
I wasn't expecting massive portions in these orders but I was barely able to satisfy 3 people with the $50 I paid. That would have been OK if the food had been great. But this was a complete embarrassment. The quality of the entree's were actually worse than what you might find at a Panda Express (and I actually like Panda as far as fast food goes).
The service was professional as indicated by others here and the dining room is clean and well decorated.
Eating the food at King Noodle was too embarrassing to ever repeat in my lifetime. There are much better restaurants around town. Don't waste your time or your money at King Noodle.
