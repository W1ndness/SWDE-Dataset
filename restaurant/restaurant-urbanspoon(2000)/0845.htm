A Step Above the Normal "Pub"!
by Dr. Matt (130 reviews)
August 03, 2010 - Likes it
ReRUBlic has not disappointed! Especially the burgers and the massive on-tap beer selection!
The burgers are quite good! And affordable. The toasted pretzel bun is half the battle. The good angus meat is the other. Then there are some interesting burger selections, like the Republic Burger, that comes with some carmelized onion and apple wood smoked bacon relish, bleu and gruyere cheese, and topped with a fried egg. It is fun, and at $8, very affordable.
I find the sides to be somewhat lacking, but I would definitely steer away from the fries. They are a bit greasy and have too much sea salt. The mac and cheese is a better choice, but still a little unbalanced.
Also, dress warmly. Yes, I said WARMLY. I was naturally in my shorts and t-shirt, as it is blazing hot this summer, Big mistake. I almost froze to death, as I think the air-conditioning was set on about 65 and was blowing the whole time.
On a side note: Some of the reviews seem way harsh. This place is suffering from the "new restaurant" syndrome, like so many other good places in OKC. The waits are long, and if that is frustrating, I suggest going at an off time. Don't go at 6pm on Friday and then give me a bad review about long waits, when you signed up for a long wait!

Major Problems
by LeeAntoinette7 (5 reviews)
April 05, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I have been to Republic 3 times since opening, and the conclusion I've come to is this: this place is a poor representation of a GastroPub. What it wants to be, and the reality is so far from each other, I imagine they may never reach it. I realize that as far as food goes here in OKC, the standards have been set low for too long, so you may be satisfied with your overcooked scotch egg, your dry, tasteless pork belly...but this isn't how its done elsewhere. It may be the cool new spot for yuppies and wannabes alike, but those who know food, stay away. Service is lazy, management seems blind to this, and worst of all they don't seem to know how to treat guests. So far the food I've tried hasn't been impressive, except for the pub mustard, but hopefully you can't screw that up. The scotch egg was missing its partially gooey yolk that is a major component in this traditional pub dish, replacing it with a chalky sulfurus yolk. The pork belly was dry and just plain salty, there was no complexity in the flavor. I realize this is just the beginning, but insiders hint that unless a major staff overhaul is made, it will never improve. Service staff is too busy playing with their hair and looking bored to get the food out on time, and from what I saw of the kitchen on a lunch visit was that some of these people obviously don't have much experience in a kitchen. Frankly, 45 minutes for a burger is intensely too long on a lunch break that averages about 30 minutes. 45 minutes for a beer? definitely outrageous. And as someone stated earlier, the music is indeed tired, and cliche. I do hope they work the kinks out, its not hopeless. If the demographic they are trying to reach is satisfied, then I guess that is enough to make them successful.

Delicious! Keeps us coming back for more!
by I love good food (8 reviews)
July 22, 2010
Food you must try at Gastropub:
-Homemade Pub Pretzel (topped with thick sea salt, cooked to perfection, served with honey mustard that you could take it or leave it and with beer cheese fondue that is finger licking good)
-RePUBlic Burger (with caramelized onion-apple wood smoked bacon relish, bleu cheese, gruyere cheese, arugula and a fried egg on a toasted pretzel bun that melts in your mouth)
-Belle Isle Burger (with grilled onions, sauteed mushrooms and beer cheese fondue on a toasted pretzel bun that melts in your mouth)
*I recommend asking for your burger to be cooked to a medium temperature.
-Sea Salt Shoestrings (served pub style, seasoned just right, and so freaking yummy)
-Three Buck Salad (great as a side dish with dinner, go for the spicy vinaigrette made with Sriracha, even though the salad is only a cheap $3 it doesn't lack in the ingredients, having great lettuce base where iceberg is nowhere to be found and yummy veggies like bell peppers, top all that off with some crumbled cream cheese - yes PLEASE!)
Drinks - ALL delicious. I highly recommend the beer flights!
Price - REASONABLE! This was the biggest shock to me and my spouse - because we thought the place would be expensive... Whoever says rePUBlic is too expensive needs to reexamine things. The food is of good quality AND quantity and I think is VERY reasonably overall. The drinks are reasonably priced as well! RePUBlic's Well Vodka + Cranberry + Lime = yummy Cape Cod for less than $6. That's reasonable. The mixed drinks on the menu are also very tasty and well priced.
Service - FANTASTIC. No complaints here about the service. The servers are fast and friendly. The food comes to the table in a good amount of time - not too soon, not too late. The food is always hot and the presentation is always nice. We are almost always visited by a manager and asked about our dinner, drinks, and experience - great service rePUBlic!
Waiting to be seated - If you go during dinner hours, be prepared to wait a bit to be seated. We usually go around 6-630p for dinner, we usually wait about 20-30 minutes for a table. While we wait, we go to the bar and get a drink. Or we go outside and chill on the open seating patio. They take your cell phone # when you put your name down and they call you when your table is ready. No paging over a loud speaker at rePUBlic.
Overall - we LOVE rePUBlic and visit almost once per week, we highly recommend it!
PS - remember, it's a PUB - so it can be a bit loud at times.... but that's a part that makes the place so great - it feels like you're at home with a bunch of friends.
ENJOY@
