Dry vs. Wet....
by dfr (96 reviews)
September 13, 2009 - Likes it
The first time we went to the Rendezvous, we didn't like it. We expected wet sticky ribs and creamy cole slaw. Then we discovered regional differences in both bbq AND cole slaw (wet vs. dry, mayonnaise vs. vinegar) and returned with more open minds. We were also forewarned that the waitstaff TRIED to be brusque and abrasive as part of the "feel" of the place. When we returned with a different frame of mind and different expectations, we had a completely different experience. People will always have their preference between wet and dry ribs and between creamy or vinegar cole slaw. Some people will never enjoy a restaurant where the customer isn't "coddled". However, as a Memphis institution, this place is worth visiting to at least say you've been there. You may actually like the food!

Ribs
by Mike Walker (30 reviews)
March 15, 2009 - Likes it
Personally I have other places I like better, but I'm not going to put this place down. I've eaten here many times. The BBQ'ed nachos are fantastic and the sausage and cheese plate is excellent. I can make a meal out of baked beans alone.
Arguing ribs in Memphis is like arguing the individual merits of Heaven, Utopia, and Nirvana. Everybody has strong opinions.
Rendezvous ribs are cooked over charcoal, not smoked like most other places. This gives them a flavor different than most of the other BBQ restaurants in the city and metro area. I've seen many of my guests go in expecting smoky flavor, only to be surprised by what just landed on their tounge. Pretty soon, after a bone or two, they learn to appreciate what they are eating. A well cooked dry style rib.
Come on down, eat the ribs, and enjoy the atmosphere. I'll take the honesty of Rendezvous over any foo foo place anytime.

Didn't meet expectations
by squirrelhenge (6 reviews)
August 10, 2010 - Doesn't like it
More than a few people encouraged us to get barbecue at Rendezvous, and we were aware of its reputation. However, it definitely didn't live up to that rep.
Going in, you need to know that they serve their ribs dry-cooked with a rub, not wet-cooked with sauce, and you may prefer the latter. The full rack wasn't much bigger than some half-racks I've had at no-name joints, and the meat was dry and a little chewy, not tender at all. The flavor was average. Their vinegary mild sauce was very good, and the slaw was unusual and flavorful, but the beans were only about average.
Prior to dining, we waited more than half an hour in a loud and crowded bar where we almost couldn't hear the seating hostesses on the PA. Our server was polite and as efficient as he could be, handling several large tables full of hungry people in an incredibly busy restaurant. To his credit, he brought us each a pitcher of the soda we ordered, clearly knowing he wouldn't get by for refills very often.
Loved the decor -- hugely eclectic, with something interesting to look at everywhere you turned your head. But that couldn't compensate for the merely average food. Go to Central BBQ or the BBQ Shoppe instead.

Overrated...sure, but some decent food as well.
by Anthony (18 reviews)
November 17, 2009 - Doesn't like it
Rendezvous is overrated, perhaps the most overrated place in Memphis and very touristy. This doesn't mean they don't have some decent food though.
Rendezvous' location is easy to most downtown attractions. Parking can be had in many of the surrounding city garages and the restaurant is an easy walk. Expect large crowds of tourists and a long wait on weekends. Some locals go there, usually when friends come in town, but its tourists who keep it in business. Service is extremely poor but its part of their act/image.(honestly) Some think its part of the charm to have a curt and slow waiter who doesn't refill drinks, others are annoyed. I tend to be in the latter category.
The food itself is decent but in a city known for BBQ they certainly aren't #1. The one standout on the menu is the ribs. They do taste very good most nights but quality can vary and they can get dry and stringy. Portion size is mediocre and prices are a bit too high.
Should you visit? Well, as an attraction, maybe. If you are going purely for a dining or culinary experience no. If you want the best BBQ in Memphis look elsewhere. You will get a friendlier better tasting experience.
