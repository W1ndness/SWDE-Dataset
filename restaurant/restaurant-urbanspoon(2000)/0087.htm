Missed Migas
by Trip Klaus (109 reviews)
February 02, 2010
I've been waiting for a while to visit Red Star because from it's web site it appeared to have everything good about Baltimore. What I found was a beautifully restored space with high ceilings, brick walls and wood booths, again unfortunately the food didn't match the space. I visited for a late brunch on a Sunday (I would have prefered lunch but it wasn't available) however one thing on the brunch menu has been calling me for months. Migas. Not real Spanish migas but an interesting mix of eggs, chorizo, peppers, potatoes and chips served with tortillas for wrapping and a side of onion, corn and bean salsa for spreading.
First, Karma was clearly against me because I always comment about people complaining about waiting for food. "What do you expect on a Friday night at 8:00?" It was 1:00 and hardly anyone was eating. They were all enjoying the bottomless mimosas. After 45 minutes my scramble and my friend's burger arrived. I think both had been cooked earlier that morning and set under a lamp and forgotten. The chorizo was underwhelming and the potatoes tasted of maple syrup. I tasted my friends burger and it was overcooked and had also sat. The best thing was the side salsa. It actually gave my wraps some flavor.
The wine list was at least interesting for a bar and fairly priced, but the beer list was pedestrian especially in beer centric Fells Point.
I guess if you down enough pitchers of the mimosas it does't matter what the food tastes like.
I'll try again for lunch or dinner, but I don't have high hopes. What is it about these places in Fell's Point that do such a good job of designing the space and such a poor job of staffing the kitchen.

Fabulous Sunday Brunch
by SimplyOlivia (7 reviews)
August 25, 2008 - Likes it
My hon took me here for Sunday brunch on my birthday and the bottomless mimosas absolutely did me in!! I had two pint-sized mims (huge) and ended up spending most of my birthday conked out. I guess I'm not the drinker I once was, but the buzz was good fun.  My mimosa was delicious and hon's first Bloody Mary was just as good. His second one didn't measure up, but by then we were well on our way.
Other than drinks there is a lot to be said for the food.  I went a little carb-crazy and ordered (get a load of this) a mashed potato omelet.  No, really!  It was a mashed potato omelet and it was delicious.  Perfectly cooked eggs folded over mashed potatoes mixed with sour cream and chives.  The mix of ingredients was just right, it was moist, flavorful and not as heavy as you might expect. The omelet was served with a side of breakfast potatoes. Yes, yes, more potatoes.  Red Star's breakfast potatoes are real potatoes, not the oily shredded clumps that my hon detests (but I secretly love).  The dish also came with a few slices of orange, I guess to add some kind of healthy element to the dish.  
My hon's brunch was tasty but a bit disappointing.  It was crab hash with poached eggs.  The poached eggs were done perfectly, but rather than the lumps of crab that the menu touts, we only found shredded pieces and they weren't too abundant.  The table next to us complained about the same thing and the waitress brought out a bowl of crab for them.  I imagine they get that complaint often if that's how they serve it.
Since we were being excessively indulgent, we decided to order dessert after brunch and were disappointed to hear they don't serve dessert with brunch.  Alas, I'll have to go back another time to sample the creme brulee I saw on their dinner menu.
Overall, we had a great time and enjoyed the atmosphere and the experience.  The service was a tad spotty, but the lone waitress seemed to be working hard to keep up with Sunday crowd.
We'll definitely go back again, but next time we'll make sure the creme brulee is ready and the mimosas aren't so easily accessible.

What a great place!
by ferdmack (4 reviews)
May 04, 2009
Definitely worth checking out. This place is just a bit hidden, but you will be rewarded by (what was for us at least, due to late afternoon timing) a nice quiet atmosphere. I had their version of a Pittsburgh Primanti's-style sandwich, with fries and corned beef, and while it was definitely not the same as the real deal, it was absolutely great. K had the panini (which was not panini pressed at all) with chicken, rosemary, brie, and prosciutto- it was a nice combination of flavors, but sadly a little bland and dry (some spread of any sort would have helped), and not to mention that her portion was quite small. The sweet potato fries are equally as good as the garlic fries, try to bring a date and each get one or the other! Leinenkugels on draft rounded out a great lunch. It was a great spot for a quiet late lunch on a rainy afternoon (although driving the cobblestone streets around the area is a bit of a shock).
