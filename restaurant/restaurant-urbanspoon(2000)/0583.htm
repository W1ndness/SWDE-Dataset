+ 8 more photos from Andrew D Fabulous Grub & Wonderful Service
by Andrew D (249 reviews)
January 07, 2010 - Really likes it
My dad & my aunt just arrived earlier tonight, and oh my were they hungry! They wanted to eat plenty, and I wanted to try something new. So hey, why not go to Off The Strip!
The place was crowded when we arrived, much busier than so many restaurants I'm used to. But just as my dad and aunt were growing frustrated over the wait, a table magically opened up and our waiter promptly seated us.
As soon as we made our orders, the bread immediately arrived. The sliced mini baguette was soft, and the butter was good quality. I had also ordered some pesto garlic bread, which was also topped off with fresh tomatoes, mozzarella, and shredded parmasean. That garlic bread was probably the best I've ever had!
And speaking of best I've ever had, the eggplant parmigiana I had here may very well qualify. The marinara sauce was quite zesty and spicy (not hot, but savory). The eggplant was a little soggy from all the sauce, but otherwise the flavors were most definitely there. And the cheeses? The mozzarella and parmesean perfectly complemented everything else!
And finally, how could I leave without trying Chef Al's famous cheesecake? Oh yes, I now know why it's famous! It was so rich, so tasty, so perfectly (and not too) sweet, and just fruity enough (with strawberries and strawberry sauce) and chocolatey (sauce drizzled) enough to be oh so satisfying!
So all in all, Off The Strip is most definitely worth the trip. The food is fantastic, and the service is near impeccable. The friendly service enough is great reason to go back, and the cheesecake will certainly make sure you keep going back thereafter!

Favorite Vegas eatery
by Tuba Guy (1 review)
January 12, 2009
When we come in from out-of-town to visit friends in Vegas, we never miss an opportunity to stop by Off the Strip in Southern Highlands (sometimes a few nights in a row!). The vibe is casual bistro, with an open kitchen. It has that "hidden gem" feel, with prices that can't be beat relative to the food you're getting. The rigatoni bolognese with meatballs is about as good as it gets, as are the fried calamari and Beef Wellington. Chef Al seems to hit everything he aims for, and while the menu hovers in the American/Italian range, don't be surprised to find some French and Asian-influenced dishes as well. Be sure to leave room for the cheesecake!

Just GREAT Food!
by PC Wineguy (12 reviews)
February 28, 2009 - Likes it
OK, if you're looking for that "romantic" high end place, this is NOT that place. However, if you want GREAT food at very reasonable prices, then this IS the place for you. My wife and I were celebrating our 7th anniversary, and we were thinking of going to a high priced restaurant on the Strip. But a few places we were considering got mixed (and some very bad) reviews. So I looked around and found this place. The reviews were overwhelmingly good, and now that I've tried it, I can see why. The owners personally greet you and also wait on you. When I told them it was our anniversary, they gave us a desert on the house, AND 2 shots of Port! And they didn't charge us the normal $15.00 corkage either. We had 2 absolutely delicious appetizers: one was a Crabcake, better than most I had in Baltimore, and the other was a Pork dish, which was fantastically flavorful. Dinner comes with a salad, which were fresh. Then we had the Beef Wellington and Chicken Saltimbuco. Both were outstanding! Very hard to find a good Beef Wellington, and it was only $26! And the cut of fillet was about 10 to 12 ounces! The cheese cake was homemade and delicious! Cheesecake Factory take notes....
All in all, this place DOES make great food, and have very friendly service. Only "knock" is that the atmosphere is more like a Sports Bar than a Serious Restaurant. But then again, maybe that's why their prices are so good.
HIGHLY recommend this to all comers. And this comes from a serious "foodie."
