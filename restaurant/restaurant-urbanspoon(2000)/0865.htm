Quirky. Exasperating. Wonderful.
by Captain Napkin (284 reviews)
January 28, 2010 - Really likes it
Please check your hang ups and hurries at the door and prepare to "wing it" at this most "left of center" eatery, located between skid row and the Old Market. The atmosphere is fun, as the place is part art gallery, with an ever changing array of art work hanging in the dining room. The menu ranges from Cajun to German, but focuses on south of the Mason Dixon line. Everything is listed on a large chalk board. If you don't get their early they are more than likely to be out of your first choice, so pick something else and go with the flow. There are no bad choices.
Service may be friendly and efficient, or not. The food is always excellent, however, and at very reasonable prices. Oh, and please remember to BYOB as they don't sell alcohol but would love for you to enjoy your own beer or wine with your meal.
One more thing, you should call to make sure they (1) will have room for you and your party (the place is very small), and (2) be open. If the owner feels like it he may just not open that day. It is that quirky. The owner lives on premises, by the way.
In nice weather they have an excellent patio for dining.
Dixie Quicks is very popular with the younger set, as well as the LGBT community.
I really love this place, both because and in spite of itself.

A creative breakfast
by SparkleAggie (45 reviews)
July 28, 2009 - Likes it
I love this place for Sunday brunch. This past Sunday, I ordered their flap jacks with mixed berries and whipped cream. The pancakes were a great consistency, not too thin, not too thick. It was exactly what I wanted.
I think what sealed the deal for my affection for Dixie Quicks, is that they have Oatmeal with Ice Cream on the menu (which is hand written on a giant chalkboard on the wall). I ordered it a while back and wasn't disappointed. It is, as the name implies, a big bowl of oatmeal topped with a scoop of French Vanilla ice cream. The day I got it, I was craving sweet, so it was a toss up between that or the Sexual Chocolate pancakes, which I think were chocolate chip pancakes with nutella, if I remember corectly.
The restaurant is easy to pass by if you don't know where you're going, but when you find it, I'm sure you'll like it. Also, check out the attached art gallery!

My Favorite Omaha Restaurant
by Ann (12 reviews)
August 03, 2010 - Likes it
Dixie Quicks is the best restaurant in Omaha. The food is fabulous, and reasonably priced, and the place is quirky. Everything from whether they are open that day, to what is on the menu is up for grabs every day. The collard greens are the best I have had anywhere, the south included. There is always fun to be had, people at different tables engage in conversation, it is kind of like a family reunion, except you are meeting your relatives for the first time. What is not to like, remember to bring you wine or beer with you.

Bob R.
by pastorbobryan (1 review)
November 13, 2009
I saw Dixie Quicks on Food Network and was excited that it was located in Omaha. We made a reservation for lunch and were seated in a timely manner but as the care worn dining area sat maybe30 guests it was cramped at best. The menu was hand written on a chalk board, hard to read and as they gave us no explanation on arrival we wasted ten minutes waiting for menus. Instead we were gruffly pointed to the chalkboard with frustration that acted as though we should have known. Menu items that were touted on Food Network were not available. The service was very sub-standard and made you feel as though they were doing you a favor by taking your order. Follow up service was non-existant and we had to beg continually refills. The food was ok though portions were small and had no where near the pizazz as they did on Food Network. I took it that the owner who did the Diners, Drive-In's and Dives show cooking was not in the house and had not trained his staff to prepare meals the way in which he did for Guy Fieri. The desert seemed like an afterthought and overall I felt like they had put on a great show for television but departed claiming false advertisement.
