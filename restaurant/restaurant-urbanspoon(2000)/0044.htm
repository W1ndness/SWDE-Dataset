Message from Pure Taqueria

Once a filling station, Pure Taqueria adds gas to Alpharetta's casual dining scene.

  • Delicious Mexican Cuisine
  • Great Outdoor Patio
  • Catering Available

Hands down, PURE Taqueria is Atlanta's most exciting and satisfying Mexican dining experience. A far cry from the one-on-every-corner, assembly line taco and burrito joints, PURE uses only the freshest ingredients and authentic family recipes straight from Mexico City to create innovative and taste-bud-pleasing favorites like posole, ceviche, crispy fish tacos, tortas and more. From small plates to nibble as you sip a mind-blowing margarita or mohito, to full-on entrees that are a near-religious experience, PURE Taqueria offers a rare treat to Mexican food fans. Grab a table inside near the open kitchen and bar, or relax outside on the covered patio. Either way, you will enjoy the friendly crowd who gather to soak up PURE's warm, casually hip atmosphere of this authentic Mexican culinary paradise.

Tamales & Jarritos !!!
by Barney (485 reviews)
December 01, 2007 - Likes it
My wife and I stopped by Pure Taqueria today for lunch, based almost totally on the fact that it kept bubbling up to the top of Urbanspoon’s “Top 10 Restaurants” in Atlanta (using some detailed and arcane formula, I assume).
We started off with guacamole and chips.  Very good, but a huge bowl big enough for at least 4 people (this is a meal spoiler if you’re someone like me who hates to leave good guac behind).  We both had daily specials, she had the pork tamales and I had the grouper tacos (marinated then grilled, not deep fried… good stuff!) I was also very happy to have a Jarritos with my lunch (toronja, my favorite).
Without a doubt, this is a GREAT place if you like innovative/gourmet casual Mexican food and want great options from a full bar.  I’m sure that we’ll go back many times because I wanted to try everything on the menu.  With that said, even though it is very good, it is not the best restaurant in Atlanta
UPDATE: The formula for "best restaurants" has changed, but if you like tex or california mexican, this is still a "Must Try" if you're in that part of town.
