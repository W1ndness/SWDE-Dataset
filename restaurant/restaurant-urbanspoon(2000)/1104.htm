In-N-Who?
by aveneyer (9 reviews)
July 06, 2010 - Likes it
If we drive two miles south we reach In-N-Out, or two miles north we reach 5 Guys. Each and every time we have to choose on a burger joint, we choose 5 Guys. The meat they use is great, and most of the toppings were pretty fresh as well. My only comment, would be to keep the veggies a little cooler before serving them; You can watch the cooks make your burger, and the heat of the kitchen effects the tomatoes / mushrooms at times. The amount of fries you get is insane! One order is enough for two big eaters, three small eaters. Where that other restaurants shoe-string fries may cut it some some people, I prefer these fresh thick cut fries personally.
To the person stating they need fry sauce, it's there and on the counter... take as much as you need! You do need to put it into containers yourself, but I prefer that over places who don't give you enough for the amount of fries you get.

Great fries; want to like the burgers...
by Scot (2 reviews)
April 28, 2010 - Doesn't like it
It's difficult not to compare the food here to other places, but I'll try so as not to show my bias toward In-N-Out (darn; too late!). The fries are the star of the show here: Fresh and hot out of the fryer. The cajun seasoning (optional) is a nice break. 5 Guys idea of a regular fry is feed-bag size, and it's difficult to limit myself to a sane quantity (although a little bit o'cajun goes a long way.) The burgers here should be good, and are--on paper, at least: Hand-formed, fresh beef, with lots of toppings (including 'shrooms). Served nice and hot, but they leave me cold. The airy buns definitely don't do it for me, and the patties...well, they just don't do it, either. And, my stomach has told me after eating here a few times that the toppings just aren't as fresh as they should be. That's OK, now that I don't need to go out-of-state for a Double-Double!
