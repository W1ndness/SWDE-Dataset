+ 4 more photos from Lori It All Depends on How You Dine
by Lori (38 reviews)
March 22, 2009 - Really likes it
Our first dining experience at AB was off the menu, to celebrate our anniversary. We split the chateaubriand. We expected to be given a meager serving of vegtables since we were sharing. On the contrary! We each were served a full-sized portion.
The head chef at AB was invited to battle against one of the Iron Chef Americas. He won. The secret ingredient was ostrich. There is an ostrich satay on the appetizer menu. It is outstanding!
Since we were celebrating an event, a complimentary whole pound cake was given to us. We had to take it home with us--it was huge!
I love French onion soup, so I eagerly tried theirs. I found it to be mediocre. The broth was weakly flavored.
We then found out about dining at the Chef's Table. It's a four-course meal prepared for you while you sit in the kitchen. The food you are served is chef's discretion. We have done this twice and have been blown away both times by how awesome the food is.
What is even more amazing is the Chef's Table is just $75 per person (without alcohol). Considering other restaurants offer this at two or three times as much, this is an ultimate bargain. Plus, it is fun to watch all of the behind-the-scenes action in the kitchen. Everyone in the kitchen is friendly, and go out of their way to be kind.
We're thinking about trying one of the special event dinners. The website gives details and pricing.
I've uploaded pictures from our on the menu and Chef's Table dining experiences, and will let the pictures speak for themselves.

The Experience
by Valid Opinion D. (3 reviews)
February 02, 2010
The Angus Barn experience is like going to a play. There is a certain "show" that you will be a part of via the atmosphere and the staff. The atmosphere is fantastic. It's an old barn with vintage decorations. You have a feeling that you are in the old south and the charm and strange past simply ooze out of the walls. It's dark and relaxing. When I came here as I child, I thought it was so very special.
The produce and meat comes from the finest around town, but the food itself doesn't have the creativity and frills of a modern American eatery. Just like the atmosphere, it's a bit of a throwback and don't expect the trendiest food combinations to be on your plate. Enjoy it for what it is: Classic.
