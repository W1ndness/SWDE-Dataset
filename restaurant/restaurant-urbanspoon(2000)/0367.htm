BEST OF THE BEST OF THE BEST!!!
by Doc Loco (1 review)
April 17, 2010 - Likes it
In every town I visit around the country I ask for and visit Mediterranean restaurants looking for one as good as Anita's. I have never found a single one to even remotely compare. As a 30+ year vegetarian I have always been thrilled with the large selection of vegetarian items and the very inexpensive fresh juices. I started eating at Anita's in 1983 when they had the busiest booth at the food court in Farmington Hills, later taking over a sit down location adjacent and have been a big fan of their Troy location and now am thrilled to be able to walk to their new Ferndale location. I can't believe anyone could find anything less than fabulous about Anita's. Not only is the service impeccable, the food is incredible. Anita is the most wonderful person and treats you like family - we love her, and Jennifer and Joe - and Anna have carried on the tradition with great food and sincere concern for your pleasant dining experience. Anita's does not try to be a posh, snobby place. It is an invitation into a genuine Lebanese home kitchen with great people and the best food on earth. Whole wheat pita's would be nice, but really - the health value is negligible - all gluten products are less than good for you - get over it. Over the years I have been served by several dozen long and short term staff members and they have all been consistently well trained and very attentive and friendly. I suppose those pseudo-snobs who dis Anita's think a Big Mac is real food!
My favorites: The Best Baba Ganoush ever, the Best Vegetarian Grape Leaves anywhere (I put hummus on them instead of yohgurt), Super Falafel Sandwich (fried) and grilled and a number 16 juice.
I think the Ferndale location has a humble elegance while the Troy location just has a great family feel - you can even see pictures of Anita's house in Lebanon hanging on the wall.
Both locations are generally packed while other local restaurants are sitting on their thumbs. I miss the Masabat El Darweesh, but the Majadra Rocks (add raw chopped onions at no extra charge). The coffee in Ferndale is great. Simply GREAT people and GREAT food. Try it and Anita's will be your favorite too.
Authentic, friendly, fast service - they used to start our food when they saw us pull in the parking lot and would serve us as they sat us - I mean it! Generous helpings - I just can't say enough good about this family and their food. The staff in Ferndale is huge (in number) and every one of them is as adorable as the owners. I just don't believe anyone has ever encountered a rude waitperson there and if you run into a rude customer now and then - well remember - there is no Anita's in Birmingham, so we are bound to get a few snobs hanging around now and then, but in 26 years I've never personally seen a rude customer there either.
I think any negative reviews must come from other jealous restaurant owners. Anita's simply kicks ass!
Doc

I want to like it...
by pmtincdt (4 reviews)
February 04, 2010
Ferndale really needed a restaurant with fresh, healthy fare, and I wanted so much for Anita's to fill that void. I had been to the WB location a few times in the past and was repeatedly disappointed with the food. Very ho hum, and when the trendy new ferndale location opened, I thought the menu might match the new diggs, but it's just the same food in a fancier setting. I keep thinking it could be so much more than it is. Just very standard middle eastern fare, iceberg lettuce in some of the salads which is a personal pet peeve, and plenty of white pita bread. (We all know by now that white flour is bad stuff and there are many healthier alternatives.) I will say the baba is very good, and I haven't sampled the menu extensively, as I'm mostly vegetarian these days, so this is just one probably biased opinion, though I noticed a few other reviewers feel the same. I've had some really great middle eastern food - both very traditional and more creative, and I'm just not sure why they don't rev things up a bit at Anita's. They are nearly walking distance from our house, and we never go there. We're always amazed at how busy they are at peak hours.
