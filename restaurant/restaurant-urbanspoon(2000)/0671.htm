Great time and really, really good food. Our first and definately not last our trip to cafe centraal was full of pleasant surprises. I had some family and friends (all who have never been to the Cafe Centraal before) meet here to celebrate my wifes birthday. We were greeted by the hostess and was accomodating to us when we asked to have at Least 8 seats at the bar. Our barkeep also greeted us kindly and started to suggest us our cocktails or one of the many many beers in house. We ordered four apps which were all fantastic. Corquettes, frites cone, calamari and the Scotch Egg. Food was hot, presented great , flavorful and good sized portions for the $. Another great part of Centraal is that it is smoke free and a great place to people watch! Our group was really happy and we will all be back.

I purposely looked this place up and went out of my way to go there. They have an extensive selection of Belgian and Abbey beers (the sole reason I went there). The list isn't as long as the Bier Markt in Toronto but it had a few I hadn't tried before so I was very happy.
It was late and rainy so I didn't get a good feel for the neighbourhood but from what I could tell it seemed decent. Service was good. I highly recommend Cafe Centraal if you are in the neighbourhood or if you love Abbey beer.

Clone but good
by Tom (15 reviews)
April 04, 2009 - Likes it
Wow. Have always loved Cafe Hollander so when we dropped into Centraal we felt like we were sitting at Hollander. The Belgium-centric beer list, the frites and mussels. So stepping away from the deja vu I have to say it was a very good dining experience. The space is very open. The copious amount of windows allow for natural light to pour in.
The food was good. We had the chicken sandwich (with an upgrade to salmon) and a veggie burger. The veg burger was black bean based, spicy and very moist. The salmon sandwich was a bit ho-hum. Salmon was pretty uninspired but the toppings were a nice compliment.
The wine and beer list are good. The beer list focuses heavily on Belgiums. I would go back again but wouldn't make a habit of it.
