Fairly Good, Expensive
by Frederick (217 reviews)
July 31, 2009 - Likes it
I've been to several of these churrascarias around the country and this one is neither the worst nor the best of the lot. I was pleased with the quality of the meat, especially after reading some of the negative reviews, but will admit the service (the gauchos) can be a little slow sometimes. Our waitress, who was primarily responsible for the drinks (fantastic caipirinhas and glasses of a supple malbec) was just great, very warm and personable. The salad bar was decent enough, the standouts being the mashed potatoes and the feijoada. We had to ask several times for the roasted pineapple before it was finally brought and it was served in a simple side dish rather than carved tableside as at Tento. The bill for dinner for two was $121 before tip.
One thing that did surprise me, at least on the Friday night we were there, was how many little children were running around. This is NOT an appropriate place for the little ones and I've never seen so many tots in a churrascaria or rodizio before.

Hit and Miss...
by Christopher W. Wickersham (23 reviews)
April 26, 2009 - Doesn't like it
I've been to several other churrascarias in many cities. In the US, Miami probably has the best collection of them, and out of those Porcao is my favorite.
Espeto was a bit of a disappointment, compared to the others I've been to. This could be growing pains, or maybe not. Out of the cuts of meat presented on skewers, only one was really good, and that was their "signature" tenderloin. That one dish was pretty tasty.
Everything else, though, was pretty bleh. The chicken was overcooked and very dried out, as was the pork. The raw-materials quality, when it comes to the meats, is lacking and not what I was used to at other charrascarias.
For $39 per-person (not including beverages or sides) the meat quality should really be USDA Grade "Prime". If they're going to be pinching pennies, then USDA Grade "Choice" should be the minimum they should use. In reality, they are using either "Select" or "Standard" Grade meats, which just doesn't fit with a meal that runs easily $100+ per person. If there's one thing I could say to this place, it would be: "Quit skimping on Quality!". People can easily taste the difference.
The service was also waaaaaaaaaaay slow. Even though they must have a good 10 or 20 employees wandering around at any given time, they actively ignore/avoid you when they sense you're about to ask for something. It took 20 minutes to get our first round of drinks from the bar, and the subsequent ones weren't any faster in arriving.
We had to ask 2 different waiters 3 or 4 times for the hot buttered rolls you're supposed to get when you first sit down in a charrascaria, and they finally showed up only after we were completely finished eating, and were stone-cold to boot.
I also ordered a side of Platanos (plaintain fries), an item that is normally free at other churrascarias, and they showed up 30 minutes later, with a charge of $12. Dinner for 3 wound up being well over $300, and that is actually pretty "normal" for this category of restaurant. But knowing that price point, the quality and service need to match. This place has a bit of work to do...

An Upgrade
by Jaxman (9 reviews)
July 09, 2010
This upscale churrasaria sits at the far reaches of Avondale on the river. Fresh grilled meat served on swords is the order of the day. They do offer a wonderful salad bar, but honestly, it is only there for window dressing. They have some wonderful and tasty items, but MEAT is the star. And you can eat until your arteries are completely clogged. I have eaten a several of this type of restaurant in different parts of the country, and this is certainly not the best, but even more so, not the worst. There were hits and misses with some of the offerings. The lamb chops, sirloin, fillet, and hot pepper steak were outstanding, but the sausage, rib-eye and bacon wrapped chicken not so much. But such is the danger of such an intense relationship between fire and meat.
The service was attentive, and personable. One of the owners spent some time at our table talking, and the wait staff was great. We were kept in ample supply of caipherinias, the national drink of Brazil, along with wonderful desserts and strong Brazilian coffee.
It is a bit pricey, and for most, a special occasion destination, but well worth the price for the food and experience. Price for our party of five, including tip was just a tick under $400. Certainly in the upper stratosphere for Jacksonville, but not unheard of. You could easily do this amount of damage at Mortons, Matthews, Bistro Aix, or Ruth's-Chris, but not have as much fun.

Best in Jacksonville
by Leighton (13 reviews)
March 31, 2010 - Likes it
I don't know if that title says enough about how WONDERFUL this Espeto's is. This place is a secret that needs to be let out. Great food, great service. I should be in a food coma right now from all the good food, but I needed to write a review, so I'll begin.
If you have never been to a Brazilian Steak House, you are certainly missing an experience. There are some good ones all over the country, such as in San Diego, or Orlando, but Espeto's is a GREAT one. From the time you come in, you are greeted cordially. We were waiting on some others to show up so had a seat at the bar. The bartender's personality was awesome and eager to make the perfect drinks, WHICH HE DID! The decor is very upscale with a view of the river. The attire would call for business casual to semi-formal.
The food was great. Traditional selections, like a black bean stew and rice, to their own sweet potato salad, which was beyond belief. I had never tried heart of palm, but it was good. It is so tempting to get full from the hot and cold selections from the bar, only to be reminded of the 14 courses of meats you will be offered during your meal. I tried every one. And each was AWESOME! Personal favorites were the peppered steak, bacon wrapped filet mignon, and the ribeye.
The service was impeccable. While the price range of the meal is on the upper end, the quality of the food and experience is as well. A GREAT VALUE FOR YOUR $. And they are one of the few fine dining restaurants who honor military members with a discount.
Not only will I be back to Espeto, but I can't wait!
