On the fence...
by Wes (152 reviews)
August 02, 2010
I can't decide if I love this place or hate it. On one hand, they have really unique doughnuts and flavors you aren't likely to find elsewhere. On the other hand, their selection is so "snooty" compared to other doughnut shops, and the flavors match the snootiness. Some of the ones I tried were good, others were awkward and the dough was bitter. They almost come across like little pastries as opposed to real doughnuts. Either way, it's worth checking out. They serve high quality coffee, the staff is nice, the place is very clean, and I like the fact that they are around, even if I am not their most frequent customer.

Don't understand the hype
by calisno (2 reviews)
June 15, 2010 - Likes it
Don't get me wrong, this place was good, it's much better than a chain. However, it's pricey. I mean, I guess $5 for coffee and a donut isn't bad if you're alone, but if you're feeding a small herd of children or your family, it adds up.
The atmosphere is nice, though, and the staff is friendly and the donuts are unique. However, I really don't think this place is worth driving more than ten minutes to - the people who are driving 30+ miles one way are clearly nuts. Perhaps if there was a more reliable menu (e.g. "we always serve X donut on Tuesdays"), I would be more inclined to drag myself out here, because there are some I really want to try. But even if I check the menu on their website, there's no guarantee they'll have any when I get there at 11, and I'm really not going to drag myself out of bed earlier for donuts. I also agree with what was said about the filled donuts being a huge mess.

mobile review
by Red Melly (6 reviews)
April 03, 2010 - Likes it
I think the individuals have great ambition and lots of creativity, but, the final product needs some work. The donuts look wonderful and smell fabulous too but the flavor and texture of a few of their donuts need some work. I had a key-lime bismark this morning that looked like it was sent from the donut gods, but, when I took my first bite i was disappointed. The filling was too runny and made a mess all over my plate and the topping was too sweet and messy as well. I sampled a lemon glazed raised and raised coffee flavor frosted donut too..both of these were just okay. I had a caramel apple fritter a few months ago that was enjoyable. Their raised donuts are too dense. I think a properly made raised donut should be somewhat chewy with a light, fluffy airy texture (the old winchell's donut shops and a donut shop in Salt Lake city called Banbury Cross donuts are very good with raised donuts..I digress). I will return to this donut shop because I think they have ambition and lots of potential; having said that, I think they have some improvements to make to the taste and texture of some of their donuts.
