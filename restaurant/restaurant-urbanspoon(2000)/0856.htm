hands down, best bread in town
by Matt Lowery (2 reviews)
May 07, 2010
John McBryde and co. make the best (and in my opinion, only) artisan bread here in Oklahoma City. The baguettes are killer, and easily my favorite, but you aren't going to find a bad loaf of bread here.
Lunch is always tasty, and the takeout entrees are both delicious and priced right (usually no more than $8). It's sandwiches, soups, and a daily special (usually something a bit more substantial; think beef enchiladas).
But the bread is what always gets me, and this place is a bakery first and foremost. If you've been to Europe and have been weeping into your Wonder Bread ever since, Prairie Thunder will be the answer to your doughy prayers.

He got the stuffed baguette and I got the quiche of the day... his meal was much better. THe quiche was Ham & Cheese and though it wasn't bad it was a little bland and not as crispy as I would have liked. The crust was very, very light almost looking uncooked (but it wasn't). I am dying to go back and have a stuffed baguette to myself. The bread was INCREDIBLE. Cookies were too dry and crumbly but the carrot cake cupcake was moist and fabulous! I am thinking they should stick to breads...
