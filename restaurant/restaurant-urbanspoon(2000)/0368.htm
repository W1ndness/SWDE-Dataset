Awesome Brunch
by Shane Broyles (10 reviews)
March 18, 2010
Beverly Hills Grill is simply the best place in the Metro Area for Brunch.
The food is inventive, beautifully plated and most importantly, TASTY.
Crab-cakes Benedict, Corned Beef hash and Huevos Rancheros are all great, but my all time favorite is the Breakfast Carbonara. A mix of bacon, peas, scrambled eggs, oven dried tomatoes & pecorino romano tossed cavatelli pasta. To. Die. For.
If you think you have room , make sure to try the homemade sausage du jour.

Huge Disappointment!
by Michael R Valentine (12 reviews)
October 24, 2009 - Doesn't like it
I wanted to like this place. Hitting the door there was a 1 hour wait. We decided to wait at the bar for a table, and I asked the bartender for the house pinot noir. It was great, I thought we were off to a good start, it'll only get better. The wait never went down, so we decided to eat at the bar. The special was a salmon with spinach, and good mac-and-cheese. It arrived and I could not believe my eyes. It looked burnt and over-cooked. It was worse than that. If I were serving this at home, to guest, in the trash it would have went. Let's see here- greasy and over-baked?The spinach? Salty, yet somehow bland. I still can't figure out that one. Under normal circumstances I would have sent it back but was not in the mood to discuss it. I had to order a shot of Patron at $11 to kill the grease in my stomach from the salmon, which is easy to prepare. My wife ordered a lobster risotto, that was simply flavorless and not really creamy. Dessert was an apple cobbler that was also equally flavorless. Needless to say I am still bewildered by all the great reviews. A bad night for the chef? The tab came and it was not cheap. We could have literally had a better meal at a Big Boy. Do yourself a favor- save your cash, in this economy you'll get a much better value elsewhere. At least the bar tender's service was great, but I will not repeat.

Recommend | Not helpful
