Message from Mexico City Gourmet

Mexican Restaurant Heats up on Weekends with Live Music and Authentic, Affordable Flavors.

  • Lunch and Dinner
  • Authentic Mexican Cuisine
  • Serving the Decatur Area since 1982

Mexico City Gourmet has been serving up fresh, authentic Mexican faire to Decatur and Emory since 1982. Conveniently located in Decatur, Mexico City Gourmet offers tried and true traditional Mexican dishes sure to please the discerning diner. The salads, soups, and appetizers are standouts at Mexico City, and you won't want to miss the Quesadilla Especiale or the Guacamole Salad.

The Specialties of the House are numerous and varied, and most selections are available with savory steak, flavorful chicken or delicious, fresh seafood.The Georgia Burrito, Steak Taco Poblano and Carne Asada are customer favorites.
Stop by and you'll understand why everyone comes to Mexico City Gourmet.

A notch above "cheap mexican"
by Barney (486 reviews)
February 12, 2009 - Likes it
We go for lunch, so I can't comment on the margaritas, but I can comment on lunch specials.
Today had the special chicken burrito (chicken, onions, mushrooms, & poblano peppers), rice, and a small salad for six and a half bucks.  What a deal.  There was a bunch of tender white meat chicken in this medium-large sized burrito (and no, this is not one of those grotesque football size burritos).  The filling had a nice flavor with the poblanos very noticeable, but not spicy hot.  Rice got a solid "meh" and the little salad on the plate was refreshing and big enough for a half dozen bites.  Ultimately, with the addition of chip on the table, this is a great deal for the price!

DO NOT go there.
by Amanda Holloway (2 reviews)
May 06, 2010 - Doesn't like it
Who runs out of RICE at a Mexican restaurant? And that wouldn't have been a big deal, all the waiter had to say was "I'm really sorry, we ran out of rice, what can I substitute for you instead?" Rather, when we asked where the rice was, we got "Oh we ran out. Sorry." and he walked away. And we got to the restaurant at 9:00, were not served dinner until almost 11 pm. Unacceptable. Awful service, I will be warning everyone I know not to go there.

A good place gone to greed
by Charles Wommack (1 review)
January 25, 2009 - Doesn't like it
I have been a patron of this Mexican for more than 20 years. My most recent visit may be my last.
As one of a party of six men we were treated with disrespect by the waiter and were notified that water was no longer free and that only bottled water was served. When we ordered the bottled water it came to the table in open bottles which we suspect were filled from the tap in the kitchen.
Over the many years that we have been going to this establishment we have never been charged for salsa and chips. A substantial charge was added to our bill even though no prior notice was made that charges were going to be made for chips and salsa.
The food quality has also suffered a decline in the last few months.
A majority of the individuals in our party plan to vote with their feet and patronize other better managed places.
