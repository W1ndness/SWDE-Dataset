Message from Pappadeaux Seafood Kitchen

Freshly prepared Louisiana style seafood at its best

  • Signature Louisiana Style Dishes
  • Unique Cajun Style Cooking
  • Varied Specialty Drink Menu

Pappadeaux Seafood Kitchen offers the best Louisiana style Seafood dishes with a deep Cajun influence for discerning Seafood lovers. Only the freshest ingredients are used in recipes that have been mastered over the years and are unique to our restaurants. Diners keep coming back for favorites like; Texas Gulf Coast Oysters, Blackened Mahi, Seared Sea Bass, Seafood Gumbo, Live Main Lobster and Alaskan King Crab. The fun and festive ambiance welcomes patrons upon entering the restaurant and complements dishes typical to Louisiana. Signature Louisiana dishes include: Crawfish Ettouffee, Shrimp Creole, Mixed Seafood Grill and Blackened Opelousas Filet. Start with one of our specialty drinks and mouthwatering appetizers and you?re off to an unforgettable meal. For superbly cooked Louisiana style Seafood, stop by Pappadeaux Seafood Kitchen today.

Ragin' Cajun
by Icewolf (150 reviews)
July 14, 2010 - Likes it
I love coming here for the food. I mean I know it's a restaurant and that seems a silly thing to say, however sometimes you just have to point out the obvious.
Holy crap is the food good.
Bisques, gumbo, etouffee... creamy, flavorful, tons of ingredients and not just the castoffs you can sometimes find in soups/stews. Really big pieces of seafood.
Oysters on the half shell when it's the season so cheap at less than $4 a dozen.
Poboys, and platters, and deep fried nummy bits.. with big tender chunks of alligator.
I love the fish, blackened, grilled, stewed, fried. I've tried just about every variety here, catfish, mahi, halibut. And the scallops Lacombe, cooked perfectly. Seared, juicy, like pillowy flavor bombs from the sea.
And if by some measure you manage to save enough room for dessert, the Bread Pudding with bourbon creme anglaise? OH yes. Yes please, and thank you very much I'll do the extra 15 minutes on the treadmill tomorrow.
Go and enjoy yourself and say hi to the King of the Tank for me.
