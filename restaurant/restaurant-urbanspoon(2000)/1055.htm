Jumped the shark?
by jason s (23 reviews)
July 08, 2010 - Doesn't like it
The GOOD: Legendary Richmond pizza joint which everyone has been to.
The BAD: Not what it used to be? Currently turns out an undercooked, overly doughy pie with borderline cheese and non-existent sauce. The pizza can't decide whether it wants to be deep dish (ala UNO's) or medium crust. Screams "college pizzeria" which is not a good thing for anyone outside of college. Limited nearby parking. Pricey.
VERDICT: Hate to say it -- but I think Bottom's Up has stopped trying and coasts purely on reputation. Unfortunate.

Good Food Requires Time
by Michael. C (11 reviews)
January 29, 2010 - Likes it
Be aware, they cook your pizza when you order it. It also has a thick sourdough crust. If you never order apps, consider getting some. All of their apps are phenomenal, and you will have time.
Now that your pizza is here, you will notice that your "slice" is equivalent in mass to about 1/2 a medium pizza at any other place.
Your pizza, any way you get it, is the best in Richmond. Here's the best part, they now sell you their pizza for you to take home and cook. Good luck doing anything besides asking how much longer with that scent wafting from the oven.
