Great Food!
by JST (2 reviews)
May 09, 2010 - Likes it
I've been to many different Hibachi restaurants before, but this was my first time at Kobe.
The food was excellent and I highly recommend the sushi - very fresh and flavorful. The Hibachi was also excellent.
But the service didn't match the rest of the experience. Our waiter seemed nervous, scattered, and overwhelmed. Maybe it was his first day. I think the only reason he got our orders right was because we had to point to it on the menu. I could tell he was trying really hard but he was slow and we could hardly hear what he was saying.
I will probably go back to Kobe because the food is excellent and reasonably priced... and hopefully that poor waiter has more experience by then!

No Integrity
by AC (1 review)
April 24, 2010
I just recently dined at Kobe for dinner and had an odd suprise when I recieved my bill. I was not in the mood for the typical hibachi, so instead, I ordered sushi from the menu that my waitress handed me when I sat down. When I recieved my bill, however, I noticed that the three sushi rolls that I had ordered was somehow $9 more than the menu prices. When I asked my waitress, she informed me that they had mistakenly given me an old menu, but that they could not change the prices and I was told to pay the higher price. When she involved the manager, I was still told that even though they had mislead me with an old menu and old menu sushi prices, that I was to pay for their mistake by ultimately, paying the higher price. Again, after stating that it was their fault, not mine, that I had been mislead, the manager actually told me that she would "meet me in the middle" with the price. I was not only shocked, but insulted. Finally, after a few minutes of debate, the manager finally gave in, but not without being extremely rude and disrespectful. In conclusion, I will NEVER return to Kobe. FYI - their "old" menus are still on there page on this website

Good experience
by Brad O (4 reviews)
July 28, 2009 - Likes it
I went there with family and we had a good experience. The nigri sushi was very tasty and fresh. The slices of fish were ample and the rice ball was just the right size for the piece. The chef did a Philly roll nicely with not too much cream cheese. We sampled several rolls and I would say they were all good, however the flavors were more muted on the the deep fried rolls. I'd recommend just ordered the non-cooked stuff. The sushi chef was worked mostly quietly but did answer a few questions with a smile. The place is fairly big and the waitstaff attentive.
