Message from Corner Bistro

The last thing you need at 3am is a flame-broiled, half-pound burger. But you know you want one.

  • Full bar
  • Burgers & appetizers
  • Draft & bottled beers

Corner Bistro, the last of the bohemian bars in West Greenwich Village, has been serving customers since the earlier part of this century. The original ambience is pretty much unchanged, and we like to keep it that way.

An Amazing Burger
by Jackie (81 reviews)
July 19, 2010 - Really likes it
I don't know what they do to their burgers at Corner Bistro to make them taste the way they do, but I assume it has more to do with what they don't do. They don't try to use high end, low fat (some pronounce it "flavor") meat, they don't put a bunch of crap in the burger, and they don't put a bunch a crap on top of the burger. They simply use ground chuck, a whole lot of bacon and the result is an amazing burger in a great, historic, hole in the wall staple of West Village.

Mighty Tasty Burger
by Karen (18 reviews)
August 01, 2009 - Likes it
If the Corner Bistro was in my neighborhood... it would be my neighborhood bar. But something's lost when you have to travel to your "neighborhood bar". This only pertains to the ambiance of the place. Too many screechy, loud, short women in cocktail dresses who seemed to have dropped in on their way someplace else. All clustered by the front door as if ready to make a mad dash for a cab at any second. Annoying. And did I say loud?!?
Now for the food. The burger was excellent. So good that I forgot that I didn't ask for cheese until I was over half done. Now I wonder if cheese would have made it any better or not.
The shoestring fries were nondescript and probably out of a bag. But I didn't come for the fries.
The service was excellent. The server was attentive and friendly and my burger was served quickly.
I also ordered an Original Sin hard cider. It starts off strangely since it's fermented with champagne yeast. But about half way thru... not so bad. I just wish I could find someplace with really decent bar food and Woodpecker on tap!

Great any time!
by dro (5 reviews)
July 29, 2008 - Really likes it
I usually find myself here after a long night of drinking but I've gone here more and more to start off an evening. Nothing fancy, cash only and the beer may be slightly skunked (stick to the bottled beers) but the corner bistro burger with bacon, lettuce, tomato and cheese is fantasitc and rivals any burger in the city. If you're looking for a high end burger with exotic ingredients, you won't find it here. If you're looking for a classic, good old-fashioned burger that hits all the right spots while drinking a bottled beer, this is your joint. If you want more but can't fit in another burger, try the grilled cheese (same old classcially good experience as the burger).

Great Burgers, Great Price
by Ralph Monte (2 reviews)
October 19, 2008 - Likes it
Just to warn anyone going, it's CASH ONLY!!
Suffice it to say, this place is VERY POPULAR with the locals, and frankly speaking, I'd prefer it be kept a secret among locals and regulars, to the detriment of the tourist crowd.
The place is old, but since it's in the West Village, what can you expect. But, they do burgers how it was meant to be done - old-fashioned, slow-grilled, with condiments and veggies piled on top. Their biggest burger is priced at about $6 or $7, much lower than the fancy-schmancy upscale burgers of Midtown. And much better in taste for that matter. I don't need Kobe beef or caviar with my burger.
Just make sure to time when you go. It's busy, fast-paced, but relaxing and welcoming nonetheless.
