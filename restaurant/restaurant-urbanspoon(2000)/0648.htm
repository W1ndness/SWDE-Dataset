Great Caribbean
by Jesse C (37 reviews)
March 10, 2009 - Really likes it
This place is one of the favorites among my group of friends. The appetizers...coconut fried onion rings, tostones (banana chip nachos), cuban bread among others, are excellent. Dishes run the gamut from jerk chicken pasta to sandwiches to seafood specialities. Desserts and drinks are nicely prepared, if you have room when you're done. I recommend the Lemon Breeze (non-alc).

Good Fun, Good Food
by JRC (78 reviews)
February 05, 2009 - Really likes it
This is a franchise restaurant, so don't expect some quiet or trendy urban restaurant.  Its decor is like any other franchise restaurant, over the top, gaudy, but fitting for the type of restaurant this is.  This is not the place to go if you are wanting a quiet dinner, its loud, and does a fair amount of its business in liquor.  That being said, they have some rather tasty menu choices.  As an appetizer I would recommend the dip made with the goat cheese.  Its served with toasted bread and served in roasted red bell peppers.  The Chicken Santiago is an excellent dish on the more affordable side of the menu, and is served with an excellent sauce and comes with fried yucca which is very good.  For desert the cheesecake is excellent.  If you are looking for an intimate dinner look elsewhere, but for a fun night out with friends this is an excellent destination.  Its generally very crowded, so expect a wait.

Something Different
by Corey Malone (11 reviews)
October 28, 2009 - Likes it
In short:
On a 1-10 Rating I would rate this place a 9 On Service and 9 on food. I absolutely enjoyed the wait staff and atmosphere. They have a different variety of foods (Jerk Chicken Dishes, Pastas, Pizzas and Steaks) and some of the best fresh made drinks I have ever had.
Pro's:
Nice big place with an open bar to order drinks while you wait on your seat. They also have a small live band with outside seating and an open fire for cool days. The food has a unique taste and lost to choose from. I really like spicy foods so to me they have the best Habanero Wings I have ever had. If you cant handle the heat leave these alone.....lol...You get a full 2lb of fire in a bowl. They have wonderful coconut shrimp and the brick-house pizzas are wonderful. Its a very nice place to go with a group of people especially when you have a few drinks before eating on your mind. The fresh drinks they make are absolutely delicious.
Con's
Weekends are packed so be ready for a wait. There are not many places to sit while you wait unless you like to drink.

always amazing
by Aes (3 reviews)
February 15, 2010
I have to say I love Bahama Breeze, and I have since they opened. It's obviously not some chic restaurant, but it is what it is and it does that well. I always have to have an appetizer, usually the coconut battered onion rings that are to die for! They come with two really yummy sauces. Their coconut shrimp is also really good.
For my entree I like the jerk chicken pasta, but I LOVE the fish, specifically the almond-crusted tilapia. It is so like and flaky and come in this lemon butter sauce that just complements it perfect. I also get the mashed sweet potatoes and perfectly crispy green beans with it. My mouth is watering just thinking about it!
