How They Make Your Burger at Kelly's
by Drexler McStyles (206 reviews)
February 06, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I was having a conversation with a friend (who works at Kelly's and of course will remain nameless for all the obvious reasons) and I mentioned that I had a Burger from there a couple days earlier and wasnt very impressed with it. The individual was not surprised and then proceeded to explain how Kellys makes their burgers: Every morning they make a large batch of burgers. They actually do start off with fresh ground beef, they pack the meat and then shape it with a cutout. Then they cook the burgers to medium. Then they place all the burgers in a large tray full of beef bullion to soak until some time later someone actually orders one. At which time they throw it on the grill to reheat it. You pay $8 for this. Unless you want some of their disgusting processed plastic cheese at an additional cost. He says this is just one example of the pitiful way they prepare the food at Kelly's.
YUM!!
Somehow I dont think this is standard business practiced by most restaurants. Suffice to say after our conversation my wife and I agreed that we would never go to Kelly's again.

a love hate relationship
by Drexler McStyles (206 reviews)
September 17, 2009 - Doesn't like it
Im not quite sure what it is about Kelly's that bugs me. Its popularity perhaps, popularity based on the fact that its a good place to be seen, particularly on its parking lot sized outdoor patio. The food is OK. I usually get the French Dip w/ Fries and the size of the portions for both items vary per visit. Their Omelets are pretty damn good, though, once again, the amount of Potatoes you get varies wildly every time. The one thing that stands out and is a must have is their Creamy Green Chile Chicken Stew. It is phenomenal!
The service is a mixed bag, Ive been there for breakfast when there are only 4 other people in the entire place and it took 45 minutes to get my food. It seems like theres a lot of teenage drama behind the scenes with the waitstaff. Theres always some blockhead military guys in there that manipulate the waitresses time, and then tip 6% on their tab that were already discounted 50% because theyre in the military. Meanwhile Im still waiting for my silverware.
On duty police officers dining with nervous looking 18 year old girls who they let off with a warning if they agreed to meet them for lunch. Screaming kids throw crayons and whine until you want to beat them, etc...
Its just a bit too much when all I want is to eat my sandwich, y'know?
