Elegant, humble, warm, tasty, original, historic
by ray cannata (532 reviews)
March 05, 2010 - Really likes it
My wife, my friend and I started at the bar next door. We had the signature house drink -- the French 75, which is champagne based. Terrific (though pricey at $13). The bar tender was Chris Hannah, one of America's best.
Appetizers were truly stunning. The entrees very good but not as strong.
Oyster Bienville ($14) great; souffelle (pushy potato things with sauce -$9) was fantastic. Shrimp remoulade and stuffed mushrooms very good.
The entrees were good but not as strong as starters. Pork tenderloin and creole shrimp -- good.
Service was possibly the strongest I have ever had (and I have eaten at almost 500 places in NOLA). Fun, unassuming, humble, attentive.
The flaming cafe brulot's presentation was amazing. 10-20 steps. Taste was decent. Just $7 and came with refills.
The decor is elegant, historic, old school. The weird Mardi Gras musuem upstairs --- it is not even advertised. Coolest collection I have ever seen.
If you want French Quarter, old school elegance -- Arnauds is much stronger than Gallatoires or Brennans. 3/10

With prices like this it has to be exceptional
by Tom B (67 reviews)
April 30, 2010 - Likes it
I can't imagine how anyone could have a meal at Arnaud's for the reported $25. I was in New Orleans for a conference, and the hosting company hosted dinner here. Otherwise, I don't know that even I could have justified the expense (figure $75 per person, before liquor). However, all things considered, it was an exceptional dining experience.
The restaurant boasts an elegant dining room, and several smaller rooms that accommodate larger private parties. As there were about 40 of us, we were in one of these rooms. Service is impeccable. Waiters were solicitous, accommodating and friendly all at the same time. Water glasses and drinks were refilled before it was necessary.
I started with the Shrimp Arnaud. There's a reason why they refer to their remoulade as "famous." Order the Oysters Rockefeller. You will not be disappointed. The chicken and Andouille gumbo was fantastic, even if I don't understand the point of okra. For my entrée I had the filet mignon au poivre. It was exceptionally prepared with a delicious, creamy brandy sauce. And the soufflé potatoes made a wonderful accompaniment. Unfortunately, dessert was impossible after sampling all of this, but the cappuccino was strong and creamy and served as a tasty finish to this decadent meal.
