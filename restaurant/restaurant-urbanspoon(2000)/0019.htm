Not much for Chains
by Tanya Law (4 reviews)
May 15, 2010 - Likes it
We have avoided this place. Heard it was expensive and we just like supporting the local business owners. That said, it was recommended by a couple of people and a close friend of ours just loved it. That was good enough for us so here we go.
First off, it is fondue and you are going to cook your food. If you don't get this little point you need to stick to fast food. The waiter was super friendly, informative and patient with all of our silly questions. The table across from us was a bit confused by the whole process and had I been their waiter, I would have escorted them out the door but not this amazing guy. He walked them through the meal one step at at time and they were clearly pleased by the end.
He walked us through a few steps as well and we were on our way to a multi-course meal that we thoroughly enjoyed.
I highly recommend this to any one who wants a fun dinner with someone they love. A bottle of wine, 4 course meal and a great night for 150 bucks. A bit pricey but we were not unhappy with our choice.

There's Just Something About It
by Mark (4 reviews)
April 16, 2009 - Likes it
So, when I tell my friends and family that I've dined numerous times at The Melting Pot they always ask "Why would you pay so much for fondue?". I think the best way to answer that is not to say that it's outright delicious (which it is), but to say that there's something about service and atmosphere that adds to the experience.
Every time my wife and I have gone, the servers go above and beyond what I've ever seen at any restaurant. They are extremely friendly, very helpful, and very informed about everything on the menu including wine parings and such. And when they are preparing your fondues they will basically try to get to know you and tell you a bit about themselves making it more personable.
The atmosphere is something that you can tell the franchise has perfected. It's quite, relaxing, and just makes the experience that much more. There is even an area called "lovers lane" which is specifically for couples to dine in private. Even though there are multiple tables down this "lane" there are walls surrounding 3 sides of your table blocking the other tables and another just a couple of arms lengths away to block the rest of the restaurant so you feel like you're the only ones there.
As for the food, it's always top notch. I've tried the majority of the different items offered (cheese fondues, salads, main course fondues, desserts) and it's always been great.
With that said, while the price seems high for someone who hasn't been, once you've gone you'll know and you'll want to go back time and time again.

A Complete Experience
by bcovert (9 reviews)
December 18, 2009 - Likes it
I went here with my girlfriend and this place was so good that I had it on my mind for like two weeks. People complain about the price, but you're paying for excellent service and an overall experience with the food. To be honest it wasn't as expensive as I was expecting and the quality of the food was great.
Our first course was the classic swiss and gruyere cheese fondue, and our waiter was very friendly and kindly explained all of the ingredients as he made the fondue infront of us. This was perfect and with all of the dippers I think this was the highlight of the dinner.
Next came the salad with a delicious ginger vinaigrette.
Next was the main entree. Neither of us eat meat so we for the vegetable broth, with the vegetarian entree. I enjoyed cooking our own food because it allowed us to sit and talk and appreciate each others' company instead of just devouring whatever was put in front of us. The artichoke hearts, portobello mushrooms, asparagus were fresh and very tasty, but my favorites were the two types of ravioli, and the marinated tofu. My least favorite was the edamame.
We were then given champagne with a delicious chocolate fondue with cheese cake, marshmallows, rice crispy treats, strawberries and more for dipping, absolutely delicious and a nice close to the night.
My only real complaint was that the price of the wine was nearly $40 for bottle of wine that is $12 at the store. Quite a markup, but it wouldn't feel complete without some wine so I gave in.
I will definitely be back.
