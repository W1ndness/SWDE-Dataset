Pan-East Asian tapas
by KananRoad (1 review)
September 28, 2008 - Likes it
This is a pan-East Asian tapas restaurant, not a Japanese izakaya.  Its strong point is creativity rather than authenticity.  That doesn't make Sasa Matsu bad, just a different experience from what you would get in a Japanese izakaya.  I had the ginger green salad, saba shioyaki (grilled Norwegian mackerel), fried tofu with mushrooms, the pickles and peppers plate, and the poached pears.  The salad dressing came very close to what I've had in Japan, and the salad itself was tasty and inventive.  The saba shioyaki was nicely and creatively cooked, but contrary to expectation the "shio" (salt) was on the plate, not cooked into the fish.  The tofu was heaped high on a plate with the mushrooms.  Very tasty, if different from what you would expect at a restaurant in Japan.  The biggest surprise was the pickles and peppers plate.  In Japan, pickles are usually served in a tiny dish on the side (at no additional charge), not arranged to cover a medium-sided plate, and certainly not with fried peppers.  Be sure to share the pickles plate with a friend or two, because it's more than one person would normally eat!  And the poached pears were amazing.  Overall, I was slightly disappointed because I was hoping for a genuine Japanese izakaya experience--okonomiyaki, nori-wrapped cheese, whiskey sours, and other inexpensive delights rarely found in these parts, served at big noisy tables to enjoy with a herd of friends.  But, now that I have been initiated I can return to enjoy high-class East Asian tapas with one very special friend.  And I will be back.
