Finn's is Fantastic
by Doug Bruhnke (151 reviews)
May 17, 2009 - Really likes it
Finn's comes from a long line of success, and this clean little Scandinavian-inspired joy is the best yet. The Scandinavian breakfast served all day of fresh poached fish with eggs, hash browns and rye brown is wonderful on multiple levels. Today the fish was trout from a small farm in Idaho - and it melted in my mouth like a mouthful of Utah powder. Lunch is also available and also outstanding. Highly recommended.

Light and airy, a delight
by Matthew K (76 reviews)
December 29, 2009 - Likes it
Finn's is a refreshing change from the old school Salt Lake breakfast joints. The difference starts with the rounded white building that seems slightly out of place but in a good way. Once inside, the plentiful natural light, blond wood floor and tables, artwork, and general tidiness give the establishment a pleasant airy feel even in the dead of winter.
The food is also on the light not so greasy side. Try the Norwegian waffles, made with a hint of cardamom and topped with lingonberries.

A Swedish delight
by slcfoodie (49 reviews)
July 19, 2009 - Likes it
A Swedish diner with a great menu and even greater atmosphere. Waitresses in red checkered aprons, diner style interior, and patio seating in the warmer months. Try the French toast on cinnamon bread or the egg white omelet if you're wanting something healthy. They also have great fresh squeezed juices and smoothies. Trade the jam for lingonberries with your toast for a more Swedish experience.

Good breakfast, better atmosphere
by Food Good (13 reviews)
August 23, 2009 - Likes it
Finns keeps me coming back. The bright, open space, consistently good food, and easy pace make it an excellent place to meet for breakfast or brunch. There is also an outdoor seating area, better than average coffee, and a free apple (at the front) to replace the free mint from other establishments. The oatmeal, fresh fruit, eggs benedict, and salmon are all reliable choices. On the down side, Finns has a small menu and a couple of times their coffee has tasted old. Bring your newspaper and relax as they will not rush you out the door.
