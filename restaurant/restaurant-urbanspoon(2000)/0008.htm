Best red? Close...
by Larry McGoldrick (128 reviews)
September 02, 2009 - Really likes it
While my sister is in town for an extended stay, we decided to run down my restaurant wishlist. Yesterday was Duran's turn. I am always in search of the area's best red.
I ordered a Green Chile Tortilla Cheeseburger, but with a twist: hold the green and replace it with red. They fixed me exactly what I wanted: a large puddle of red with a tortilla neatly folded in half over a perfectly cooked beef patty. A beautiful sight, like poetry on a plate. Now you can't hold this burger in your hands (well, yes you can if you need to have red all over your hands), so knife and fork it is. This combination is heavenly. One of the nicest things you can do to a burger. Non traditional. Wish I had my camera.
The tortilla was exceptional. Was the red the best in Duke City like some claim? Close -- it is currently tied for second place with Marlene's. [The best red I have found so far. is made at Mary's and Tito's.]
My sister wasn't very hungry and settled for chips and salsa with a milkshake. The chips were ordinary, the milkshake very good, but the salsa was the best green salsa I have yet tasted. Fresh chules and piquant enough to test your pain threshold without murdering the taste. .
Rumor has it that the Carne Adovada here is among the very best. I plan to return and slowly eat my way through the menu, so I'll let you know. Yes, pardner, this place is that good.
Oh, you should grab a bottle of red before you leave. Five and a half bucks, and easily worth it.

Used To Be Great...Still Good
by Chef Dare (167 reviews)
March 11, 2009 - Likes it
This used to be one of the premier spots to introduce outsiders to New Mexican Green Chile Stew. Over the years, the place has changed just a bit and their stew, which is still very good, is not quite what it used to be. Service can also be a little shaky at times. But all in all...still an Albuquerque landmark and one that should be experienced by all visitors to town.
