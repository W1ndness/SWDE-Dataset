OK food, nice service, great concept
by Bill Courson (10 reviews)
July 31, 2009
Cuban Pete's serves passable Cuban/pan-Caribbean food (although to the serious Cubaphile not all that authentic) in a great environment with wonderful service. The restaurant's owner, the irrepressible Dominic, is not only a superlatively talented restaurateur but clearly cares about his guests and their enjoyment, and has spared no effort in bringing a little bit of Habana to Montclair.
A couple of high notes: his non-alcoholic sangrias and mojitos are incredible and he serves the best Cuban sandwich in the state. While there are not a lot of vegetarian/vegan dishes on the menu, what is there is wonderful, particularly the salads and the fabulous vegetarian paella.
Don't go to CP's expecting the kind of Cuban cuisine that you'd get at "David's" or "Las Islas Canarias" in Miami, but do go expecting a good time, and good food at a good price!

mobile review
by Wes Lomax (1 review)
January 12, 2010 - Likes it
Good food great price. First if all let me be clear you WILL have to wait to be seated by no fault of their own, they're popular deal with it. Its BYO so if u think there will be a wait bring a 6'er and get to it while you wait. That said, before you go or while you wait have someone get some rum or the appropriate bottle of wine to mix with the sangria or mojitos you can choose from. They come relatively low in alcohol ( about .5%) so you can spike the pitcher or each drink individually. Either way don't go without one of their signature drinks. The tapas menu is pretty awesome and most selections are under $4 so you can put together your owb sampling for less than a combo platter at a chain restaurant. Also, a lot of the tapas dishes are smaller versions of menu items so you can use that to decide on an entre. There is a lot to choose from and there is something for everyone. A lot of people tend to shy away from Latin food because of concerns over spice. While there is some spicy this cuisine is more savory than anything else. If you are looking for something entry- level try the ropa vieja or the suckling pig. Or to do something different go for anything with " crispy" meat or the seafood entres. Top it off with a dessert and coffee all of which are excellent and that dinner for 2 can come in at about $70 total for good food in a fun place!
