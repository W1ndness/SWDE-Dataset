Quite good!
by pugmama (44 reviews)
October 24, 2009 - Likes it
After reading about Drago's on urbanspoon, we drove to the Metairie location from Baton Rouge and arrived around 7 pm on a Friday night. It was incredibly busy and we were astonished that we were seated immediately as we expected to have to wait. The restaurant is much larger than it appears from the street and we were escorted to an elevator in the rear across from a kitchen.
We were seated on the 2nd floor which had its own host. We waited for a good 10 minutes with no acknowledgment from any server so my husband approached the host who apologized profusely and gave us a delicious appetizer (a warm, cheesy seafood dip with multicolored tortilla chips) to make up for the lapse.
Husband ordered the shrimp and corn soup and they accidentally put 2 orders in so the server gave me the other bowl. This soup was piping hot and full of the biggest shrimp I've ever seen in shrimp and corn soup.
After reading about the chargrilled oysters I had to try them and the flavor was quite good but I found the texture a bit off-putting. In a couple of them, I chewed some crunchy weird texture that left me unable to finish my half dozen.
My entree was the portabella and beef tips which was fabulous and my husband enjoyed his cajun surf and turf but found the steak portion a bit dry as he ordered it medium well. Husband tried the chocolate mousse but neither of us were able to eat much of it after the appetizers, soup, salad (comes with entree) and entrees which we'd eaten maybe half of.
If I had one complaint it would be that we were absolutely freezing throughout the time there. Since no one around us appeared to be uncomfortable, we just dealt with it.
My husband, who is not a foodie by any means was totally impressed. He said that he'd definitely return to Drago's any time he's in the New Orleans area.

Recommend | Not helpful
