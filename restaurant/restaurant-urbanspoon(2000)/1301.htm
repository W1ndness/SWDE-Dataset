2 Amys - tip #2 - get your pizza to go!
by Lola (243 reviews)
May 29, 2010 - Really likes it
I love 2 Amys pizza. It's authentic Neapolitan and the ingredients and toppings are fresh and as good as what you would typically find in a Naples pizzeria. However, and it's a big HOWEVER, I totally agree with other reviewers that the restaurant has been overrun by screaming, noisy kids, often unsupervised by their parents. For that reason, I rarely eat at the restaurant anymore; I just get my pizza to go.
It's a shame because I think grown-ups also deserve to have a nice meal out, and I wish 2 Amys management would think of a way to deal with the noise and ambiance situation.

Excellent Neapolitan-style Pizza!
by Lola (243 reviews)
October 04, 2009 - Really likes it
2 Amys is known for its excellent Neapolitan-style pizza and together with Pizzeria Paradiso, serves the best pizza in Washington, DC. People looking for New York style pizza should know this before trying it because you can't compare apples with oranges!
I recommend the margherita pizza (buffalo mozzerella, fresh tomatoes, & basil) and usually don't get toppings on my pie in order to savor the classic combination of flavors. For appetizers, I usually get the salt cod croquettes and the "suppli a telefono" (deep-fried rice balls), which are delicious and as good as any I've had in Italy. The selection of Italian wine is OK, but not the best IMHO. There are also a few kinds of beer on tap as well.
A warning: don't got to 2 Amys during peak dinner hours (6-9 pm on weekends) because it's kid-central and is very, very noisy! The best times to go are weekdays (lunchtime or late afternoons/early/evenings).

The only place to eat Pizza in DC
by No Doggie Bag (37 reviews)
April 30, 2010 - Likes it
As most transplants from NJ/NY will attest, DC is not a place you want to eat pizza. However, 2 Amys changed the paradigm. The pizza is great, thin crispy crust and a reasonable number of pizza options on the menu that don't overwhelm. One overlooked menu items: CALZONE! People, you can get authentic calzone with real ricotta and Moz goodness inside. if you like calzone you won't be disappointed. Also, if you want to have appetizers and sit at the bar, you won't be disappointed. This is the place to go have italian appetizers. They change often and always outshine the regular menu. Most important, do not go there starving or with children because you will have to wait. Also, it is a very loud restaurant so do not take people who are hard of hearing to this restaurant -- I guarantee, you will not have the energy to talk over the roar.

better then pizza hut!
by monica (14 reviews)
July 29, 2010
2 Amys is probably the best pizza I have had since I have been in DC. That said, its no street corner in Rome pizzeria.
Although 2 Amys isn't really located in a DC hot spot, its always swarming with moms and dads trying to stuff vegetables in their toddlers' mouths. The atmosphere really imitates the pizzeria down the street, with an open brick oven on the side of the restaurant, and a bar in the back. That said, don't do 2 Amys if your looking for atmosphere. This place is all about taste and price.
Keeping it simple, I ordered the basic "Santa Brigida" pizza, which was tomato, mozzarella, cherry tomatoes, and arugula. Although the bread and crust was soft and delicious, (having that distinctive taste of Indian roti according to my dad) but the rest of the pizza formed more of a paste-like gunk on the top of my pizza. Although it was delicious, it was kind of messy, and the glops of cheese really detracted from that unique taste of arugula (which is a bummer because arugula is my favorite green)
Aside from the pizza, we sprung for the potato and prosciutto croquettes. They were absolutely delicious. But considering the itty bitty portion was even to little for a runway model, I could only savor that half bite I got.
But I still give 2 Amys 4 stars, because I really am a fan. The taste was more authentic Italian then any of those other so called pizzerias in the area (dominoes anyone?) The crust really is to die for, and I have been back since then.

Pick a Peter Pastan pizza...
by Attila The Hungry One (2 reviews)
June 13, 2007
It's worth the struggle to find a parking spot (sneak into the Giant lot) and the long wait in line (if there's only two of you, push by and head for a bar stool at the back) for Peter Pastan's wood-fired-oven pizzas. You won't come any closer to the genuine article with his  Associazione Verace Pizza Napoletana-approved Pizza Margarita, Pizza Napolitana and Pizza Marinara. But try his own assemblies, like the palate-tickling Etna and other interesting combinations. It's worth tucking into one of the sides or starters, like the dish of garlic-spiked rapini, the beet salad, a plate of fine Italian charcuterie, or a rice ball stuffed with melting mozzarella. Take refuge from the families and their excitable infants round the corner at a bar-area table.
