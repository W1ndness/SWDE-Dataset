Delia's offers good food and service. We've eaten here five times, and it's consistently tasty. Delia's chef is pretty great with presentation. Last week, we brought a visiting relative and her boyfriend to Delia's. They are both chefs, and he even has his own restaurant. When the waitress set down his chicken dish on the table, they both said, "Wow!" The menu is pretty big, so I'll give you some highlights to help narrow it down: -- The hummus (appetizer) is simply incredible -- very lemony and fresh. -- The Pollo Francese is identified as a signature dish, but it really isn't the best thing they make. Don't get me wrong, it's very good, but we wouldn't order it again, because so many other dishes on the menu are even better. -- My husband really enjoys the Bisteca Toscana. It's big and beautifully done. -- I think the Grilled Bone-in Pork Chop is truly unique with it's hint of sweet apple in the savory sauce. -- The Vitelli e Granchio is another favorite. The sauce is so creamy and the lump crab meat is sweet. I don't know why they even bother with the pork medallions, since the rest of it so so good. The decor in the restaurant is also pretty nice, but there really isn't a lot to absorb the sound, so it can get kind of loud when there's a full dining room.

Delicious Food; Great Pre/Post-Movie Destination
by Nate (1 review)
May 15, 2009 - Really likes it
My wife and I love to catch dinner and a movie at the AMC Hoffman Center in Alexandria. Delia's opened recently and we decided to give it a shot on a recent rainy Monday evening. The interior of the restaurant was filled with dark, polished wood textures and low, intimate lighting.
I must admit that, as a designer, the first thing I noticed was the tastefully designed and well-organized menu. Our waiter was friendly and solicitous, eager to explain the different specialties of the place. The Parmesan and pepper mix that came with pita chips (instead of something like bread) was delicious.
I ordered their tricolore salmon: it came on a dark, triangular plate, and was beautiful. The salmon was grilled to a mouth-watering orange. It was flavorful and moist, the kind of dish that makes you close your eyes with each bite.
After dinner, the waiter gracefully upsold us to have some espressos. They came in elegant, small espresso cups with a small piece of sweet, delicious biscotti. This was the perfect way to end our meal. I left the restaurant satisfied and smiling.
I hope Delia's does well - if its other menu items are comparable in quality to the salmon and the customer service is as excellent as what we received, it deserves its success.

Really impressed with the pizza at Delia's, and their crust is the best I've had in many years. Paradiso, 2 Amy's, Matchbox, all have good pizza, but their crust doesn't have the flavor or crunchy outside - light and airy inside that Delia's does.
