overhyped
by Allison K (292 reviews)
August 03, 2009
Been to several Babe's locations and still not understanding the hype. I have had better fried chicken at home and other restaurants, truck stops even, and the biscuits are very "floury". They are smaller than you would expect. Green beans have no chance of flavor because they sit in a pool of oil. The mashed potatoes are creamy but I wouldn't be surprised to learn they are powdered. The chicken fried steak is a small highlight to the "menu", it has plenty of black pepper spice and the breading is crispy. The gravy is good and thick. If there isn't a wait then it is good to go try but I won't lose sleep over not going again. And you would think for some place with "homestyle" cooking that they would also have good tea but it actually is watery. I think Cotton Patch Cafe is much better if you want homestyle cooking.

Crackling Crispy Chicken
by Charles Pizzo (5 reviews)
March 25, 2009 - Really likes it
Crust on the fried chicken at the Frisco outpost of Babe's was crackling crisp, yet the meat itself was moist and flavorful. It far exceeded my expectations and experiences with Babe's in Garland and Burleson. One imagines that the meat is brined to stay so juicy. But one does not achieve such crisp, flavorful crust without a deft hand in the kitchen. Only a combination of well calibrated fryers, clean oil, timing, and the ability to speed it out of the kitchen quickly could produce such a great result. Crackling hot but not overcooked. Pretty darn close to perfect.
