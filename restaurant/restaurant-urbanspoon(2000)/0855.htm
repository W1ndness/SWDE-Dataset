Shameful
by kmartin3379 (1 review)
August 02, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I have been dining at Bellin's since the mid 1990's, and in the past both the service and food have been good to excellent. However, I dined there yesterday (August 1st) and had the worst service I have received anywhere, ever. We sat at the bar and the first (of 3) bartenders asked "what do you want to drink?" He did not greet us, nor offer any of the usual pleasantries, but simply asked what we wanted to drink. We were there for a little over an hour, and were "helped" by two other gentlemen (I'm assuming other bartenders). The second one brought us our food, but never asked how the food was nor if we needed anything. We finished our drinks and slid them towards the bartender (the third), in case he hadn't noticed they had been dry for close to 10 minutes. He never responded, so I asked if he was our new bartender. His reply: "Yeah, what do you want?"
We decided to pay our tab and take our business elsewhere, and as we were leaving we were ignored by the hostess, who was busy talking on her cellphone at the host station. Needless to say, we are very sad to discontinue eating at Bellini's as it (was) one of our favorites. I'm not sure what has happened here or why, but know that they failed to meet even the most basic standards of service.

What happened?
by LaDonna Sharp (8 reviews)
April 05, 2010
My boyfriend surprised me with lunch at Bellini's last week. I've not been in quite a while but my last experience was wonderful - attentive service, spectacular food and a fine dining atmosphere that is difficult to find in OKC. Upon entering, the place smelled like hair perm solution. I asked the server if there was a hair salon next door and he looked at me like I was crazy and shrugged his shoulders. We ordered the Tomato Caprese. The tomatoes were not ripe and it was served with spinach and no fresh basil as the menu states. What the heck? I ordered the Capelli Pescatora. The shrimp and scallops were plentiful. My boyfriend ordered the Pesto Tortellini. It was pretty good as well but, this is Bellini's not The Olive Garden so it should be more than just "pretty good." They were not busy since it was late afternoon but we had to wait for our wine, wait for our check, wait for our change. Once our server and my boyfriend figured out they knew each other, his professionalism went out the window. He chastised me when I asked if they had a white Cabernet, saying there is no such thing. Well indeed there is but I guess since he hasn't heard of it, it must not exist. I won't say I'll never go back because I do like the food , it's just not what it used to be. They have lowered themselves to the typical Okie standards.

Recommend | Not helpful
