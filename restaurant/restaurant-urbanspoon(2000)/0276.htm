Latin sushi!
by Closet Gourmet (80 reviews)
May 19, 2009 - Likes it
Try the ceviche - especially if your a fan of cilantro! Ceviche is raw seafood marinated in citrus juice. Certain shellfish is cooked but mild white fish is basically cooked by the acid in the citrus. This is not a dish you normally see on a menu let alone your neighborhood strip mall mexican joint. In fact the only other time I've seen this dish featured on a menu was at a tasting prepared by a well known chef. Another way to think of ceviche is a chunky white salsa. Aside from the seafood the ingredients are similar with cilantro and lime pronounced (in this recipe). Wonderful!
I have visited Cuco's several times now, tried a variety of dishes and every visit has been enjoyable. Don't let the modest strip mall appearance fool you, good food awaits you inside. The interior is best described as south of the border kitschy and feels a bit like a bazaar. There is even a small market with an assortment of mexican groceries. Enjoy!

Fried ice cream to die for
by klahdane (44 reviews)
May 15, 2009 - Likes it
My experience at Cuco's was great for the most part. My dish arrived steaming, bubbling hot and took about six or seven minutes to cool. Wonderful chiles rellenos, though--very cheesy. The salsa isn't the best in town and there are definitely better blends available.
Save room for the fried ice cream and share it with a friend. A heavenly fried tortilla dusted with cinnamon sugar rests under a large ball of ice cream rolled in sweet fried outter shell and drizzled with chocolate and caramel. I'm pretty sure this is heavily catered to the American desire for sweet chocolate desserts, so don't get too upset if this seems really authentic.

Best Tacos in Columbus
by Brenton (1 review)
March 03, 2010 - Likes it
I went to Cuco's on a steady Wednesday night. Service wasn't great, but that's status quo for a taco place. Inside it's nothing fancy; just a small place with a couple of tv's by the bar. But while they may lack in service and ambiance, it's more than made up for by terrific tacos at great prices. I had a chicken, a chorizo, and a fish taco. All three were excellent. Done simply with fresh onions and cilantro on a corn tortilla; these tacos are the most authentic I've had in Ohio. If you are looking for cheesy -sour creamy nachos with proper english service... go to Applebee's. If you want some great authentic taco's and strong margarita's: go to Cuco's.

Dissapointed!
by Nicole Pettigrew (4 reviews)
January 09, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I am no Columbus native but, I've eaten at MANY mexican restaurants before. I found this little place and read a bunch of reviews that were fairly positive so I thought I would give it a shot. We only to wait about 15mins for a table for 2 (which seemed great for a busy restaurant on a friday night). After being seated we waited about 7-10 mins for our chips an salsa to arrive. The salsa was WAY colder then need be and had no texture (seemed like pure tomato paste). Our server was friendly (at first) and we placed our drink order shortly after receiving the chips. Waited about another 10mins for 2 margaritas which would have been fine if our server had even bothered to notice we were out of chips. After the drinks arrived we placed our entree order, a pork chimichanga and the enchilada suisas. The drinks were very good and strong (we opted for the 1800 vs. house). The food seemed to take longer then need be to come out, we passed our time with conversation but, the restaurant s very close quarters so any serious subject matter is kind of left at the door. 15mins pass and still no food, by this time we are starting to get annoyed with the poor service. Finally after flagging our waiter the food is delivered a few moments after. Both dishes were very dry, lacking cheese, salsa verde and sour cream. I felt ripped off even for the low price I was paying. The meal was pretty flavorless and I only managed to get thru it due to intense hunger and a slight buzz from the drinks.
Here's where I become agitated: The server never ONCE came back to our table after initially bringing our entrees. I find this highly rude. Didn't even ask if everything was ok or if we needed another drink. Which we did, so I made eye contact and he came over and I ordered round 2. Another 10 mins goes by before we get round 2. I put more fork down and can't force anymore dry chicken in my mouth so I decide to relax and enjoy my drink and talk to my dinner date. Fairly hard when there's a subwoofer in your ear. And by that I mean this place is RIGHT next door to a strip club or regular club, either way if you are sat on the left side of the restaurant you will hear some very loud bass through the wall. Our server makes his way back and the first words out of his mouth were "how many boxes would you like?".
My agitation escalates, he still hasn't asked how my meal was, he didn't offer dessert or coffee, or even ask if we wanted another Margarita. I quickly snap that I'd like to see a dessert menu. He brings it (and 2 boxes) about 5 mins later. We order fried ice cream. The dessert took about 10 more mins to come out. It seemed like the corn flakes they used to roll the ice cream in were stale because it was very chewy. Flavor-wise it was a great dessert and had some very interesting well balanced tastes but it was hard for me to get past the staleness. We ask for the check. (This is my final straw) We were waiting for easily over 10 mins JUST TO GET OUR CHECK , when I look back and see our server TEXTING. I am fed up so I walk up to the hostess and tell her I would like my check for my table and asked my waiter about 12mins ago and now he is leaning against the wall texting. The check arrived very promptly after that and needless to say the large tip I usually leave stayed in my pocket.
If you like dry , flavorless mexican food and EXTREMELY poor service this might be a good place for you. Otherwise stick to El Vaquero.
Oh I will say again, the margaritas were GREAT and there's a back bar so if you like a good drink then I would recommend it in that sense just don't expect good service
