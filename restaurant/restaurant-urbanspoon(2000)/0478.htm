Excellent
by Lori (261 reviews)
February 20, 2009 - Likes it
Tonight we enjoyed our first dinner at Ibiza, and it will definitely not be our last. We were a party of 4 and enjoyed two fantastic bottles of wine, which were very reasonably priced. For appetizers, we had the off-menu razor clams, which were unique but good, and the beefsteak tomatoes with blue cheese, which was delicious. I ordered the braised lamb shank; the portion was generous and I was very happy with my choice. The other diners ordered the coq au vin, porkchop, and morrocan grilled shrimp. The first two dishes I tried and really enjoyed but the third I did not try since i'm not a fan of shrimp. For dessert, we had the tres leches with coconut sherbert, and it was absolutely decadent. Overall, we had a great experience and would recommend Ibiza to anyone and everyone wanting a nice dinner out on the town.

Nothing unique
by Dareon (118 reviews)
August 16, 2009 - Likes it
This place is great to go for an elegant dinner with a loved one or close friends. The food isn't anything new or complicated to make. The meals aren't particularly large so don't expect to go home full but, more like satisfied. If you go on a weekend evening don't expect great service because their aren't a lot of servers when it gets busy. It can be very loud and three chairs down you can't have a decent conversation with someone at your own table. They have a decent wine list and you can't go wrong with any of their seafood choices.

I just love sayin' "ee-beeth-uh"
by loren (13 reviews)
December 21, 2009 - Likes it
Great service. Every time. Their calamari is tasty. If you have an adventurous cohort at the table with you, split an order of the Mediterranean mussels - they're served with shallots and smoked in paprika (and perhaps some cumin or something else to give it a smoky zing. I recently tried the grilled pork chop for the first time... yum. If you make it past the tres leches on the dessert menu, you cannot go wrong with the "chocolate trifecta." Unless of course you don't enjoy chocolate, in that case, my opinion means nothing to you and you probably should move on to someone else tawkin' bout food on their iPhone. Oh, and the people watching at the "Lounge" next door is fun. Cheers!
