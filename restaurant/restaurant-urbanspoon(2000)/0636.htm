Yard Bird
by Mike Walker (30 reviews)
January 12, 2009 - Likes it
I can not believe Froemmer's used the word *dingy* to describe this place. It is a well used building, informal at best, but it is clean, well kept, and the home of the best fried chicken in the world, bar none.
If you know a better place, post it here. I'll put up $100 to bet you are wrong.
As always, I have no vested interest in this place.
Here's all you need to know about Gus's. The bird don't hit the fat, 'til the order comes back to the kitchen. Like I said about "The Flying Fish", I don't know how you define "Hot and Fresh" but the chicken here comes to you almost always still making noise. Go ahead and get a big 4-0 of beer (in my case a pitcher of unsweet tea) and wait it out. It will eventually cool down. When it does you'll understand why I sent you here.
The side dishes are average at best, but the name is "Gus's Famous Fried Chicken" not "Gus's Famous Side Dishes".
And for your own sake, GET THE CHESS PIE! I'm sorry, did I just shout? Seriously, GET THE CHESS PIE! It is the perfect ending to a perfect meal. The other desserts are good, but they lack the one key ingredient I need when I'm dining there:
Chess Pie.

Hyped, but hyped for a reason.
by Anthony (18 reviews)
November 17, 2009 - Likes it
Gus's and Redezvous are two of the most famous Memphis establishments. While they are both hyped and touristy Gus's actually lives up to its reputation.
Gus's is a tiny little shop that seems to always be busy. Expect a wait just about any prime eating time. You may also have to look around a bit for street parking as well. The décor can best be described as basic, but it's everything one would want or expect from a historic fried chicken place.
The wait staff is overtaxed during busy times but they are usually friendly. The kitchen cooks in huge batches so once seated your order usually comes out quickly. The chicken itself is classic southern fried. Its almost always cooked perfectly (there are occasional exceptions) Chicken is generally server with bread, beans, and slaw. The meal is best finished off with a slice of their very tasty coconut pie.
Expect to spend about $10 a person. For a faster experience go at odd hours or during the week.
