Cooked food is better than the sushi
by BRchick (26 reviews)
June 11, 2010 - Likes it
Not sure what the hype on the sushi is... I've definitely had much better ones at a much better price. However, I really enjoyed their cooked food. We ordered the Eggplant Miso to start, which was really tasty and a different way to taste eggplant. For our main meal I had the Misoyaki Butterfish (absolute delight), my husband ordered the Miso Teriyaki Chilean Sea Bass (tasty as well) and his nephew ordered a garlic steak (he really liked it). Not that impressed for having to pay $3.50 for a bowl of sushi rice (entrees were a la carte). We also ordered a couple of rolls which wasn't anything special. Service could use improvement.
I would say "I don't like" this restaurant if it wasn't for the cooked food.

Great setting, good sushi
by Maureen (11 reviews)
February 18, 2010 - Likes it
We ate here on a Saturday night and it is a good thing we stopped by earlier in the day to get a reservation or we would have been waiting up to an hour. The restaurant has great decor and open air to the outdoors, but is a bit loud. Service was prompt and friendly, although our order came out sporadically. The best two rolls we had were the Doraku roll and the Emperors roll. Prices were reasonable for the size and ingredients of the rolls.

Best Sushi, Pacific Rim Fusion in Waikiki
by plone (1 review)
February 19, 2009 - Likes it
My wife and I visited Doraku and was blown away by the food and the atmosphere. The prices were more than reasonable (about $15 - $25) and each dish was a taste explosion. We visited 3 or 4 sushi places while on Oahu and Doraku was by far out favorite. We even went back on our last night to try some different things lol. Seriously, if you're in Waikiki and like sushi and Pacific Rim fusion, try this place. It won't set you back much and you won't be disappointed.
