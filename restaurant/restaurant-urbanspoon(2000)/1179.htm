Bastille Day!
by Mick (32 reviews)
July 14, 2009 - Really likes it
What a wonderful way to celebrate the French holiday. I just got back from a leisurely lunch, where everything was fantastic. The dining room is small, charming, and welcoming. Allez Les Blues!
by Eternal M's Optimist (62 reviews)
October 02, 2009 - Really likes it
There's a good reason why Le Pichet is a favorite: it's simple, unfussy, and affordable. No dish exceeds the $25 range, and most are comfortably in the teens, like the perfectly cooked piece of halibut I ordered smothered in Dijon mustard in an incredibly tasty basil and spinach sauce, also accompanied by several pieces of gnocchi, which goes for $17.
The rabbit and pork pate is also memorable. The meats are bound together by gelatinous duck fat and arrive on a plate with caper berries and fruit jam. While I think the former is a bit of an unusual pairing with pate given the saltiness of the pate, the sweet jam is the perfect complement to the chunky and savory pate.
And then of course is the charm of the place. From its chalkboard tables, to its outdoor seating, it's truly a slice of Paris in Seattle.

by Nancy (49 reviews)
March 22, 2010 - Likes it
What I like about Le Pichet is that I could go there and eat excellent quality food, simply prepared but with superior technique. How can you marry simply preparation with superior technique? Visit, you will see. Slice the meats thinly. The butter is fresh, no, not just pulled out of the fridge, but freshly made (I don't believe they make it but it seems so). The salade verde was 3 dimensional...taste, texture, crunch. The wine I ordered didn't arrive even though I asked for it twice and on my third request, it became gratis (that'll do). It is a small cramped place, but quite civilized, to coin a innately European phrase.
Much appreciated and thoroughly worthy to be sought out, but don't tell anyone who won't luxuriate in its approach. No room for those folks.

Helga says "Nein" - cozy? ...or just awkwardly small?
by Helga Fromage (14 reviews)
June 12, 2010
I am sure that Le Pichet serves great good food -- it certainly receives favorable reviews from the professional food critics. My "no" to Le Pichet is simply that it is too small, crowded and uncomfortable. I have dropped in several times with friends to see what the wait for dinners was like -- it is always going to be a long wait. The place was packed -- which a good sign with respect to good food -- but all the while we were asking the hostess about the wait, we were crammed inside the front entrance, hovering directly over diners ... none of whom looked any too pleased that so much was going on right next to their table ... and the cold draft from the door was blowing in on them every single time the door opened. If I am going to pay to eat out, it has to provide at least the ambience equal to that of my home ... otherwise, what's the point? We always leave and go directly next door to the Virginia Inn. It has expanded and doubled in size since the 1990s and is relaxing -- it even has nice comfortable, Helga-size booths ... Helga does not like to feel rushed when dining out and Le Pichet seems like the kind of place where a diner feels rushed. My vote for someone who desires french bistro food in Seattle is Bastille Cafe & Bar in Ballard.

Great food, bad service
by Gregg (1 review)
May 17, 2009 - Doesn't like it
My wife and I went to Le Pichet for the first time last night. While I don't have any food allergies, my wife is very allergic to diary. I know what you're saying, why go to a French restaurant if you have a dairy allergy? Because every time we do, the chef will alter one of the dishes to make it without dairy (and because I can eat whatever I want). It never fails. Until last night. Not only did they refuse to do any modification to any dish (except leave the cheese off a salad), the waitress had to go back and forth to the kitchen 4 times asking if certain dishes had diary in them. And we were informed that she's been there for years. The wait staff should know EVERYTHING about the dishes they are serving. On the bright side, the food was wonderful. I had the Chicken-pork sausage and it was sublime. So, go for the food, stay away if you're picky or have any kind of food allergy.
