mobile review
by Bruno (32 reviews)
February 22, 2009 - Really likes it
Great Churrascaria! Superior customer service, beautiful ambience, and delicious meat! It is located downtown and this city really comes to life at night...a very safe & enjoyable environment. They provide complimentary valet parking and the dress code varies from casual to business. Everyone blended in very well; not to fancy or overly casual. The service was superior to any other Brazilian Steakhouse or any restaurant I've eaten at around here. Management was present and the place didn't miss a beat! The food quality was outstanding! The salad bar had a copious variety of foods. Try the chicken-salad it is a traditional Brazilian side dish! The fresh mozzerella, heart of palms, and rice and beans are yummy. Try the Farofa with some vinagrette. Everything was very fresh and always filled. The meats were on-point! You have to try the rack-of-lamb, picanha, filet mignon, sword fish, and alcatara meats! Some of these names are in Portuguese but almost every employee appeared to speak it so they'll understand (they understood me). Try the Brazilian beer Brahma you will love it! If you've never been to a Brazillian steakhouse know that your dining experience will be different from traditional American ones. The waitress will explain everything to you and not only will you have fun but you'll also get your money's worth! They will bring out and quickly replenish all your side-dishes and appetizers but do not get full on them...after all you're there for the meat right? The cheesebreads, fried yuca, and fried bananas are hard to resist though. This is definately on my top ten places to eat! Try it you will not be dissapointed! Before trying this one I drove by the other place "Brazas" I can't comment about their food but (not to sound snooty) the place appears to be in the badside of town; although I've heard lunch is cheaper there. Last TIP: If you register online with Chima you will receive a $25.00 off coupon. Hope this helps.

One of my favorites!
by Andi (37 reviews)
January 17, 2010 - Really likes it
I am not a big meat eater, but I absolutely love Chima! I'll even eat rare meat here, it's that good! For $40, you get a lot. It starts with a high-end salad bar (my favorites include the hearts of palm, artichokes, shrimp, calamari, and beef carpaccio). You also get an endless supply of meat (my favorites are the bacon-wrapped filet, boneless chicken, lamb chops, and the sausage). On top of that, they keep your table full with several really delicious sides (creamy mashed potatoes, Brazilian fried meatballs, fried polenta strips, Parmesan bread, fried bananas, and a turkey spread). The bread was my favorite - it almost tasted like a mini popover! But everything else was really good as well, and we tried them all. The drinks were good, too, although a bit pricey - most of the martinis run $9.50 a pop. Service is the absolute best -- extremely efficient, very friendly, and helpful. At one point, I was trying to get the attention of the gentleman carrying the ribs, and when another waitress saw me trying to get his attention, she quickly ran after him and asked him to visit our table. What great service! At the end of the meal, rather than having a drink, my husband and his friend tried the Brazilian mate tea (pronounced mah-tey), and that was an experience! If you like strong tea (and don't mind it being slightly bitter), I highly recommend you give mate a try at Chima! They serve the tea loose on top of a huge mug, and you slowly add hot water and let the tea steep inside the mug before taking a sip with the special straw. Chima is one of our top favorites in Charlotte and if you have not been, I highly recommend that you give them a try!
