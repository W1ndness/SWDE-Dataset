Olé again!
by Greggndc (75 reviews)
June 20, 2009 - Likes it
Jaleo was the first restaurant to open on 7th Street past Pennsylvania Avenue long before there was a Penn Quarter or Gallery Place. It was an instant success simply because office workers and the few local residents were starved for anyplace to sit down and get a great meal. Since then Jaleo has had its ups and downs but it has always been busy. My previous visit last November was a disappointment. Among the hot tapas I selected, the grilled shrimp were rubbery and swimming in oil, the quail was overcooked and under-seasoned, and the croquetas were mushy and salty. My favorite salad - manchego cheese and apple contain only a tiny bit of cheese and contained so much oil that it must have been the last scrapings from the bowl. Add to that hurried and forgetful service, dishes that we hadn't ordered added to the bill (one I can forgive but six?), and not very welcoming hostesses and I wrote them off. During a recent visit from my New Mexico family my sister insisted on going. Resigned to my fate I went along. Is the old Jaleo back? We were told it would be twenty-thirty minutes for a table and during prime time and requiring a table for seven that seemed possible. We scrounged seats in the bar and had a drink. Thirty minutes turned into and hour and twenty. A large table that had paid their bill and finished their drinks sat and held it for almost forty minutes. The extra wait was not the fault of the hosts but perhaps they could have speeded them along. Regardless, to us they were most apologetic and attentive. Perhaps it was hunger but I think not. All of the dishes mentioned above arrived and were incredible. The shrimp almost floating in the oil and perfectly grilled, the quail succulent and savory, and the croquetas so crisp outside but hot and melting inside that we order four more plates. The apple and manchego salad was lighter and the addition of fennel added another dimension to an old friend. Other dishes - lamb chops, pork sausage, and octopus were equally delicious and perfectly prepared. Our waiter - the same one who served us in November - was definitely on his game that night and was up to the challenge of a large party with hungry (and gastronomically adventurous) children and adults who know food and wine. The bill arrived - and despite our constant orderings of more patatas bravas and sodas fro the kids - it was perfectly in order. It is a welcome relief to know that Jaleo is back on top of its game and that this Penn Quarter grande dame is no longer showing signs of age. Brava!

Very enjoyable evening
by tom-doran (50 reviews)
December 13, 2009 - Likes it
I had gone to Jaleo's a few years ago and really enjoyed it, I went again last night and remembered why this tapas bar was so good. They have a huge menu selection with classic Spanish tapas along with some modern twists. My wife, my sister, her husband, and I went there and split about 9 entrees, each of which was excellently prepared. The octopus in citrus was delectable, and it was hard to choose which was better between the rabbit and the quail. Probably the best dish was the duck, recommended by our waiter.
Jaleo also has delicious sangria. The sangria is definitely a little overpriced though, $30 for a "pitcher," which really is only enough for 4 people to have a single glass. Not that its not good, but there are other places in DC where the sangria is better and cheaper.
Try the after-dinner Indian sherry, very good.
Only really bad thing - rude hostess. Random, right?
