Opening Day Dinner
by Krystal (41 reviews)
November 24, 2009 - Likes it
My partner and I went to B Spot Burgers on opening night. As huge Michael Symon fans, we were waiting on edge for his new burger joint to open...and we weren't disappointed. Fans of Michael Symon will be pleased and patrons who don't even know who Michael Symon is will be blown away by the unusual menu. It was promised that B Spot would serve "meat on meat" and they do.
To start the evening I ordered a St. Louis Framboise, Lambic (delicious!) and my boyfriend opted for the "Vanilla Bean Apple Pie Bacon" milkshake. And yes, you read that correctly. Vanilla Bean. AND apple pie. AND bacon. In ONE milkshake. Before you even mutter the word "ew" you need to try it. True to Michael Symon's style, it is bold and delicious. It took incredible self control for us to not order a second one. My boyfriend looked like he died and went to heaven after the first sip.
For dinner we ordered the Lola Fries and B Spot Onion Rings to share. The Lola fries were as delicious as always (my favorite french fries on the planet). In response to his first bite of the onion rings my boyfriend said, "I have no idea what he did to these but, wow." Crispy, well-flavored, and not overly greasy. Perfect.
Our "entrees" were practically perfection in a basket. I played it safe and ordered the Bacon Cheeseburger. Perfectly cooked to the medium temperature I requested, the burger was juicy and flavorful. Delicious, probably the best best bacon cheeseburger I have ever had, but still not mind-blowing. But that's my own fault for "playing it safe."
My boyfriend, on the other hand, ordered the Red Hot which is a burger with pulled pork, pickled tomato, hot peppers, pepperjack cheese, and siracha mayo. Mind-blowing doesn't even begin to convey the deliciousness that was contained in that sandwich. Again, the burger was cooked to perfection (this time medium-rare) and the toppings combined marvelously together. While the burger had a nice kick to it, the heat was not overpowering. This was, hands-down, the best hamburger I have ever tasted.
For people who aren't big meat eaters, there are other options on the menu but I highly encourage that you try a Michael Symon burger, just once. I hardly ever eat beef but I trust Michael Symon and his chefs to do it well. And they succeed every time.

Burgers were done to perfection, and the pickle bar was very good.
Good selection of draft beer and we thought the service was very good.
We ate at the bar because the tables were going to take over 40 minutes to get seated.
We had onion rings and wings for apps and were very pleased with them.
