Sapa Bar and Grill
by Scott McClarin (3 reviews)
July 21, 2009
Theres Another New Sushi place in SLC! It's not a chain, its owned by several entrepenurial utah locals. The market for SLC sushi places is becomming congested, but if you do it right you may find your nitch! the Sapa menu works well even for people who are squeemish about raw fish, with deep fried and battered sushi rolls which are as delicious as the normal sushi. the fish is flown fresh daily from japan, which may put a damper on your concerns for the environment, but to get sushi this good in UTAH, it is the only way possible to have fresh fish. Fresh fish equates to quality sushi, and for 6-8 dollars for standard rolls the price is acceptalbe, even the Saki starts at 10 dollars for a large container, which will serve 6 which is quite good. you can also go for broke and order specialty items if you have a limitless budget! There you have it, SAPA seems to have it all, even a nice bar and patio area for evening dining so go check it out and see what a quality contemporary sushi dining experience is like.

Customer Convenience

July 28, 2009 - Doesn't like it
Well I have to tell you, I was not impressed with the place. Working downtown I have the opportunity to visit many of the dining establishments. When at first you walk in you immediately impressed with the wood and earthy tones, fabric on the walls, a well designed bar as well as the nice outdoor seating. When seated the lunch menu is a bit tricky to read and not very informative. After some clearification and a few too many trips to the kitchen your server finally takes down your order. If you are planning a business meeting or a friendly meeting be prepared to pick up everyone's tab. And as it was put to me by the manager "It is for the customer's convenience".

Updated review
by Rick Branham (7 reviews)
August 13, 2009 - Likes it
The last two times we've been to Sapa have been disappointing ... while the Sushi remains excellent, the fusion dishes have been mediocre at best. I hope the owners of Sapa will pay attention to the food and not just ride the coattails of a pretty atmosphere.
========================================
Salt Lake finally got a great Asian restaurant with not only fantastic food, but a hip, inviting atmosphere. Last weekend about 12 of us went for a birthday dinner. The courtyard was wonderful--beautiful layout with fountains, vietnamese structures, etc.
The service was spot-on, and the food was very well-prepared. I recommend the Massaman steak; it was braised, fork-tender, and swimming in a bath of velvety-peanutty massaman curry. My mouth is watering just thinking about this dish!
Good job, guys, and good luck with the restaurant. We'll definitely be back!

A wonderful place to dine and relax!
by Rebecca Parr (1 review)
August 10, 2009 - Likes it
The Sapa Grill provides all of the things I look for in summer dining; comfortable outdoor seating, good service, and great food. This place is so good that I went there for dinner two days in a row. Although many of the entrees sound familiar, they are uniquely and freshly prepared by Mai, the Sapa Grill's chef. I intend to go back until I have tried every single one. In the meantime, I suggest the beef teriyaki, the massaman beef, and the curried salmon. Also, don't forget to try the jalapeno bomb! Sapa Grill provides a serene outdoor dining experience. The tables are placed apart from one another so that diners do not have to share one another's dinner conversations and the quietly bubbling fountain sooths. A great place to go when you don't want to feel rushed.

Repeat Customer
by Rebecca Page (1 review)
November 27, 2009 - Likes it
I have dined at Sapa Grill numerous time with many different friends and we have never had a bad meal or bad service. The Hot Summer Soup and Shrimp and Crab Crispy Rolls are light but flavorful. I have had the Short Ribs, Massaman Beef, Braised Pork (good, but not my favorite), Miso Sea Bass, Teriyaki Tenderloin and the Teriyaki Salmon. All delicious. The service has always been timely and friendly and we are always greeted warmly. The prices are fair. Lunch for two: around $30 for, soup, entree, drinks, tax and tip. We have ALWAYS received SEPARATE CHECKS and have PAID SEPARATELY. The portions are ample (I usually need a carry-out container, but I do not eat enormous portions). The outdoor patio is delightful in the summer, but the traffic noise from State Street does intrude somewhat. I would recommend giving it a try.
