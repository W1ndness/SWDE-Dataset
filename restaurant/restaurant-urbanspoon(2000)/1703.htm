Getting better and better
by Rachel (1 review)
November 03, 2009 - Likes it
After a number of undesirable incarnations since the glory days of Polo Grill, the new restaurant at The Colonnade, Alizee shows a lot of promise. I have dined there on two occasions; once in the bar from the lite fare menu and once in the main dining room.
The bar is an exceptionally nice place to have a cocktail, with a good mix of people and a celebratory energy.
The dining room is grand. There were lot of 4-tops of relaxed enthusiastic patrons enjoying themselves. The food was good - there is some room for improvement here, but the braised short ribs and the mushroom risotto I sampled were delicious. The wait-staff was incredibly nice. Not all of them are completely familiar with the restaurants offerings, but they make up for this with a genuine good nature. While we dined, the chef came out and said hello to each table.
Keep up the good work Alizee, and consider adding some more regional MD fare to your menu. Because for some of us, the ghost of Polo Grill will never leave - but a homage to that regional menu would probably go over well.

COME GET A TASTE, the food is amazing!!!
by greg (1 review)
June 01, 2009
I've been to many times and the food has always been excellent! I love sushi and it is probably my favorite food alizee but I was getting tired of trying the same old stuff everyday. Alizee has this sushi which they call Fushi which is making my mouth water right now. It is so refreshing to have something innovative when it comes to sushi. They get very creative with all their food and make rolls like the lobster banana roll which is excellent and the maryland crab roll with watermelon which is the best sushi roll I have ever had. (Im not a big roll guy I prefer sushi and sashimi but this roll was out of this world.) The soft shell crabs were probably in my top 3 best tasting ever, Alizee risotto (which changes every day) is always incredible, and the rich mans breakfast or poor mans breakfast i forgot the name was like a taste explosion and definitely got me hooked. Its a small dish of french toast, froi gras and quail egg with crispy bacon on top. If you go to one new restaurant let it be this one and I promise you wont regret it.
