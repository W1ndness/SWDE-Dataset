Unexpectedly Delicious
by Jill Schmidt (7 reviews)
March 30, 2010 - Likes it
We randomly found this restaurant on our GPS when the restuarant we were planning to go to was closed. This was a VERY happy accident, it turned out. Fresh and tasty guacamole and salsa and perfectly salted chips combined with a cheese dip we completely finished off in 5 minutes. We took the "when in Rome..." approach and ordered the Flying Burrito for him, while I ordered the Flying Enchiladas. The meats were perfectly seasoned, the cheese was melted just right, the sauce was not overpowering, and the taste I had of my fiancee's burrito was just as good. My son even ate every last bite of his bean and cheese burrito from the kids meal, which was a mAssive amount for an 8 year old, but especially one who is such a picky eater! That really speaks the most about this place, I think. Margaritas were good, but with all the food i probably should have abstained. Either that or stopped halfway through eating for a to go box! ;)Service was courteous and fast, despite there only being two members of the waitstaff on for a surprisingly busy after lunch surge. I would HIGHLY RECOMMEND anyone from in town or anyone from out of town to go here! We live in San Antonio, and this place puts some of our Mexican restuarants to shame!

I've tried with Juans, I really have. I love Mexican food. I love TexMex food. I love salsa and chips and guac and queso and margaritas, but I just don't like Juan's. I've heard complaints about the service, and while I probably wouldn't think that highly of myself if I served burritos all day, its usually fine to good. My issues are with the food. The guacamole and salsa taste store bought, while the fish tacos are drenched with watery slaw that disintegrates the tortilla and the chicken enchiladas are dry. The pork tacos were okay. I've been to Juan's dozens of times and I'm sure I'll go back again, but I'll probably never be happy with it.
