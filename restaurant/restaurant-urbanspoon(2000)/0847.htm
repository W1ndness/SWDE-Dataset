Comfy but Cool?
by Andrew (63 reviews)
August 01, 2010 - Likes it
I say it's comfy because they have carpet on the floors and the atmosphere with the curtains, old-modern outdoor diner setup makes it feel weathered and worn. On the flip-side, with the large bar area with its curving lines and cool blue lights it is easy to call it stylish and cool. The food was good. I got a chicken quesadilla with mushrooms with pica de gallo. The ingredients were fresh, smartly cooked, and the cheese was at a good consistency. My gf got salmon with broccoli. The salmon portion was what I would call "diet-size" and was good but not great. It was cooked with a nice crispiness on the outside but they were not able to control it enough to keep the inner meat soft and moist.

Pricey but good
by Rick Cain (23 reviews)
August 04, 2010 - Likes it
A good place to take a date out on. Trendy interior, fully stocked bar, and an IKEA sort of austerity. The prices don't have the .99 on the end and are rounded up. Its not cheap so you will probably spend $15 a person at the very least, but everything I've had was pretty good. The only weak thing was the flatbread pizza, which could have used a bit more of a kick spice-wise.
I always get the sweet potato fries. The fruit and cheese plate is definitely for more than one, and the scallops are darn good.
As it always seems to be, some things are a little heavy on the salt, so they either a chef that smokes, or its just that I'm just a supertaster and am pretty sensitive to salt in foods. I'm going to go back and try some of their bigger dishes. Keep lots of cash handy.

Nothing like it in Norman
by gostoso (4 reviews)
April 22, 2010 - Likes it
For anyone who's given less than thrilled reviews here, I say, try again, because I think you're missing out on something great.
I choose to go to Blu because it's got qualities no other place in Norman has been able to pull off. The indoor feeling is modern, sleek and fun (not full of itself), they have a spacious patio for when the weather's so nice you would ONLY eat outside (YOU know.), and their food and drinks range comfortably from the college price and portion to the extravagant treat-yourself/date night/foodie arena. So if you're a college kid on a budget & you need $2 well drinks/mimosas and steamed veggie and chicken quesadilla with homemade salsa (huge and cheap, seriously) you can still eat and hangout in style at Blu. And if you're in town celebrating with an impossible mother in law (=me, last week. yikes.) Blu's brunch and bloody mary bar have more style and panache than she's got undercutting comebacks. In fact, she was so impressed, we came back for one of their wine dinners, which, if you EVER have to chance to go to, I guarantee you, will be the most impressive meal you ever have in Norman. Hands down. Another simple thing that sets it apart (which I appreciate) is an extensive wine list, and an unusually knowledgeable staff. Blu truly is a one of a kind, locally-owned treasure!

Okay for drinks, food is bleh
by Sara Hoo (6 reviews)
August 02, 2009 - Doesn't like it
I used to really like Blu. Their drinks are pretty good, although their mojitos have more of a dirty taste, and I'm pretty sure they're using yucky mint, or something.
Their food, on the other hand, is just a bizarre attempt at trendy, semi-fine dining. Everything you order either comes with a spoonful of some weird, corn and black bean relish, or a styled mouthful of cold mashed potatoes. Their salmon is alright, the pork-tenderloin comes in some awful, near unedible sauce, their pizzas are always doughy, and everything is generally disappointing. Get a quesadilla if you're going to eat here, because at least you'll be expecting what you're getting.
Instead of trying to be trendy and mildly innovative, they should concentrate on freshness and quality, simpleness of flavors, and once they've got that mastered, get back to their tiny portions and trendy plating.

Accomodates My Diet When I Feel Like Sticking To It
by failedvegan (3 reviews)
April 27, 2010 - Likes it
I love to go out to eat with friends. It's always a fun way to catch up and a great excuse to spend a little extra money on myself. That being said, it can sometimes be a pain to find a restaurant that will have veggie options for me, while still serving "regular" food that the rest of my friends can enjoy.
I have never been disappointed by my options at Blu. All my friends and I love to split the (vegan) hummus with pita bread. I am not limited to just one entree. I love the black bean soup (which I get without sour cream), the veggie sandwich (which I can order without the cheese) or if I'm only able to stick to a vegetarian diet, I love the veggie quesadilla or the spinach salad with bleu cheese crumbles.
The staff is always super helpful making sure any special instructions I have get followed. I went to my first wine dinner in March and can't wait for the next one. Again, even though I don't eat meat and there is a set menu, the staff did everything they could to accommodate my dietary wishes without making me awkward or like I was causing any trouble at all. While everyone else was devouring their steaks, I had a delicious sandwich with a portabello mushroom cap, roasted red pepper and mozzarella cheese instead.
I will definitely be back and I recommend Blu to everyone I know.
