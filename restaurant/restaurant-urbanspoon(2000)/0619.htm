Good but uncomfortable.
by Aaron Anderson (5 reviews)
November 26, 2009
The food was typical Indian fare, I don't exactly understand why LEO votes it the "best of" every year. The restaurant itself is somewhat uncomfortable to be in. Two experiences that make me feel that way:
1. When my girlfriend and I watched from our table, straight into the kitchen, a worker grabbing a rat off of the ground and discarding it. The squeaks of the rat were louder than Bardstown Road traffic at 5:00 p.m.
2. The head waiter was too close for comfort to my female companions, enough so that we left early.
