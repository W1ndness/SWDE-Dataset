No plans to return
by Bobby Gregory (25 reviews)
January 16, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I didn't like this place. I have only been there once, for lunch. At first I was going to order a gyro, but the menu indicated they were either "beef" or "chicken." I like my gyro meat to be made of lamb, or a lamb-beef blend. I ended up ordering a cheeseburger but the creepy little weirdo waiter gave me the business. "You sure you don't want the lamb-burger? You don't want to try something different" He then proceeded to tell me "the burger made with lamb is fresh, but the cheeseburger is made with a frozen patty." I told him I would just go with a "beef" gyro and fries. Jackass. I was really let down by the portion size and quality of the gyro, which ended up being a wrap, and fries. They sprinkled some green herbs on top of the fries in an effort to be fancy. In my mind,, there is no need to get all "fancy" with fries, be them French or Freedom. Back to the cheeseburger, why do they even have it on the menu if they don't want people ordering it? At the end of our lunch, the old timer in the back did bring out a tray of complimentary baklava, which was some of the best I have ever had. Unfortunately, the baklava didn't make up for the db waiter or weak gyro.

Great experience start to finish!
by Nate Bryhan (3 reviews)
May 05, 2010 - Likes it
I've lived in this neighborhood for too long to not have tried Mandaloun before! My date and I were welcomed enthusiastically and shown exceptional customer service throughout dinner. For a starter, we shared the Baba Ghanouj, which was excellent, and Fattoush, which had a dressing that was out of this world! For dinner, I ordered the mixed grill (skewers of lamb, beef and chicken) and had a very difficult time choosing which was my favorite meat of the three, all were perfectly cooked and very flavorful. My date ordered the Chicken Shawarma, and while I didn't have plate envy, the couple bites I tried told me it was a dish I wouldn't hesitate to order in the future. To finish our meal, we shared Baklava that was excellent - just the right size for a good taste. Overall, we were very pleased and will not hesitate to go back soon. From the exceptional service, to the generous portions, to the outstanding food, I absolutely recommend Mandaloun.

Best Authentic Lebanese Restaurant in NE Florida
by IAO (2 reviews)
March 25, 2010 - Likes it
Having traveled around the world and coming from Middle Eastern heritage, this is hands down the BEST Mediterranean restaurant in North Florida. The food is authentic Lebanese cuisine, served just like you'd get in Beirut. The owner, Pierre, is incredibly hospitable, all of their dishes are fresh, and there is even belly dancing on the weekend. If you like Arabic food, this is definitely the place to go - best Middle Eastern food in Jacksonville. I recommend the grape leaves (with meat), the either raw or cooked kibbe, the house hummas and kababs. The spicy fish is also impressive.
