by Carol Gundlach (9 reviews)
August 04, 2010 - Likes it
For you fellow Southerners, Comfort is a slightly upscale meat-and-three, with alcohol. The food was good basic Southern fare--roast chicken, fried okra, cheese grits, veggies, cornbread, meatloaf (you get the idea)--with a few extra flourishes. Good indeed. But, to tell the truth, I can get a great meat-and-three (without wine, sadly) around the corner from my office for $8 instead of $30. Was it good? Yes. Was it worth the price? Probably not in what is, after all, a Southern city. But it was packed, not a table to be found without a wait, so what do I know?

Best Dinner in Richmond
by Sean Holihan (3 reviews)
March 03, 2009 - Really likes it
I'm in Richmond pretty often and I haven't had a chance to go to Comfort till this past month. I have been to the Comfort in Portsmouth and was sad to see it go since I had a great meal there last year.
Well, four of us went to Comfort on a Thursday night last month and were treated to an amazing meal. Because we are all pretty unoriginal, two of us got the Meatloaf (because it's the best meatloaf in existence) and two of us asked for the Salmon. While the entrées were great, what really made the meal were the sides. Each of us ordered three sides per dinner giving us the entire side menu with the exception of roasted beets.
What a feast. Sweet corn, mac and cheese, lima beans, creamed spinach, cheddar cheese grits, mashed potatoes, braised greens, green beans, squash casserole, sweet potato puree, fried okra and scalloped potatoes were passed around like we were at a picnic. And all the while, the service was great-- our waiter was accommodating, attentive and just a nice guy.
If you want to treat yourself to something special, try Comfort. You won't regret it.
