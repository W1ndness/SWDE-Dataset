Post-Modern Olde Town
by Trip Klaus (109 reviews)
June 09, 2009 - Likes it
Olde Town Ellicott City has a great new addition, Pure Wine Cafe. This small 35 seat wine bar is a mix of modern lighting and design with exposed brick and stone. Due to construction constraints the the kitchen is limited to a special high speed convection oven rather than normal fryers and cooktops which does restrict the menu. No problem. The small plate meals while few are very inventive.The flatbread sandwiches are flavorful and come with side salads or a surprsingly cooked potato salad. The chef is experimenting with the oven and the menu will certainly grow, but all the items are well chosen and perfectly suited for the wine.
The wines are from small producers and not many of the names are well known. There are not hundreds of choices but enough to remain interesting, and carryout is also available.
Next time you're in old town give this new place a try before it becomes imposible to get a seat when people realize this may turn out to be the best new place in EC.

Wine Cafe that lacks wine knowledge
by Michael Alto (1 review)
August 13, 2010
I have been to Pure several times and each time it gets worse. The service is horrible. If you are looking for a fun dynamic wine bar, dont go. Chesapeake Wine Company in Canton is by far a better choice. The bartendars know nothing about wine, the last time I was in the bartendar did not know what sangiovese was and she was very young, unprofessional and not experienced. The pours are weak and the food is average. Pure is trying to be something its not or does not know how to be. The bartendar was cutting her dress with a wine key last time I was in. If your in Ellicot City I would head to a beer bar, like The Judges Bench, great selection of beers. Ellicot City cant handle a real wine bar yet, better of going down town.

Great Find in Ellicott City
by paulamnicholson (1 review)
August 30, 2009 - Likes it
We had some great happy hour Sangria at Pure Wine yesterday. At two dollars per glass, it is a deal extraordinaire. We sipped a few glasses, enjoyed several $2 tapas plates and ordered the delicious hummus plate. The place filled quickly during the time we were there, lots of friendly and outgoing folks, felt like we were at a friend's for cocktails. It doesn't get any better than that.
We met two of the owners. Both enthusiastic and pleasant guys who made our time there even more fun!
