A Monthly River Market Lunch Stop
by man eats world (56 reviews)
April 29, 2010 - Likes it
I like this place for lunch or an early supper with friends, I go here maybe once per month. If you go for lunch, get here by 11:30 or you may not make it out in an hour (this place is popular with downtown workers). Of the items that I've tried on their menu, I like the French Dip and the City Fried Chicken the best. The French Dip is served with Harry's signature fries, which are cut very thin and are fairly crispy (we used to call these shoe string potatoes). I like them quite a lot. The roast beef is well seasoned and very tender. I like the au jus, but if you are sensitive to salt or you do not like onions you would be advised to order something else, the au jus is very salty (which I like) and has a strong onion flavor. I am usually satisfied after eating this for lunch, cost: $8.95. For an early supper, I usually order the City Fried Chicken (Chicken Fried Chicken) which is served with garlic cheese grits and seasoned green beans for $10.95. The chicken has always been moist and flavorful. My favorite part of this meal is the garlic cheese grits. I know, they sound awful but they are baked into a square and have a small piece of bacon on top, the garlic and bacon flavors the grits nicely. I also like the seasoned green beans which are cooked with bacon and onion and taste like my grandmother's. Check this place out with friends for lunch, they don't serve your typical pub food out of a box, this stuff tastes house made. For those of us that enjoy eating al fresco they have a large outside seating area, at least half of which is covered.
