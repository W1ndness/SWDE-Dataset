Not sure what the fuss is about.
by wjaqueline (2 reviews)
August 25, 2008 - Doesn't like it
Some friends and I headed down to Royal Oak to catch the midnight madness at the Art Theater there, trying to kill some time, we decided to stop into the Town Tavern.
Talk about a place that takes itself WAY to seriously. It's 11pm on a Friday night, there are 6 of us, of course, no room at the bar, where they want us to sit if we're just getting drinks. Well the Host had abandoned us, and we assume they are pretty laid back with the seating at this hour and grabbed a table in the very empty rear area of the restaurant.
Right away, they give us the vibe that we're not welcome if the bar is full and we just want drinks. It's 11pm, are all the tables going to be filled with people wanting dinner? We ask if there is a better place to sit, and they again point at the FILLED BAR, then put napkins down.
The drinks are over priced, my lemonade was well over $3, and my husbands coffee nearly the same, and neither one of us got refills in the 50 minutes we were there, not even offered one. No one in the party was offered another drink until it was obvious we wanted to leave with our payment methods in the open.
The setting is nice, the music isn't too loud, and there isn't tacky shit on the wall, plus there was sports on the tv. It lours the average party in, then make them feel like they should have never shown up. They make the employees where the tacky black tie and white shirt, which doesn't really match the sports on tv, and otherwise 'unique' persona of the place.
It could be a super fun place if the staff could loosen those ties and chill out. Lower the prices a bit on the VERY mediocre drinks, but keep the decor.
Based on the first experience, I wont be returning. I'll be looking for a place that actually welcomes customers, no matter what they are ordering.
