FUN!
by Chef Dare (167 reviews)
February 21, 2009 - Likes it
Tucano's is NOT a nice, quiet, little restaurant! It is loud! Very loud! And everything is bigger than life! If you are looking for the perfect romantic date restaurant...this is NOT it! It is an experience, though, and can be a great place to go for a fun and energizing meal with a group of friends! Be prepared to come with an appetite as waiters deliver meat to the table, fresh from the grill, and cut the meat directly onto your plate. The grilled pork, chicken and beef with a variety of seasonings are delicious, but my favorite from the grill is the pineapple! The grilling process caramelizes the sugars of the fruit and the smokiness adds a distinctive yet subtle savory quality to the pineapple. Delicious! But don't forget the salad bar/buffet! Vegetables, meats, seafood and soups are all here to add to the total experience. Just make sure to save room on your plate for a little bit of everything! All in all, the food is good, but fairly simple and straight- forward, and a little bit pricey... but still worth a visit!

A definite hunger buster
by His Joeness (24 reviews)
November 13, 2009 - Likes it
Tucanos is a Churrascaria located at the gates to Albuquerque's vibrant Downtown. It is conveniently located within walking distance of several bars/clubs, a coffee cafe, a Century movie theater and an assortment of shopping establishments.
The food stops only when you quit. You are given a wooden cylinder that signals whether you want more (green), need help (yellow) or are defeated (red).
The salad bar is incredibly well kept with crisp vegetables, sweet fruits and quail eggs.
But it is the meat of the matter that drives people to return. Tucanos servers continually prowl the restaurant with skewered meats in search of empty plates and hungry eyes. You need only say yes and sirloin, mango fish, asada, chicken hearts, roasted pinepple and more will be yours to sample and enjoy.
Reasonably priced for all that you get. The view is street level in downtown.
Caution. The hosts and servers occassionally break out in carnival-like song and performance to entertain those celbrating their birthdays.
I recommend the fried bananas that come in the breads basket, the sirloin, the roasted pineapple and whatever happens to be your poison of the day.
Excellent customer service and wonderfully accomodating hosts.
