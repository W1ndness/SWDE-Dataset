mobile review
by J (1 review)
May 03, 2010 - Really likes it
Do you know how lucky we are to have a bakery like this in Anchorage? I've been to the best bakeries in San Francisco and Paris, and Fire Island takes the cake. (Really, I didn't plan that one. lol.) And who cares if the prices seem high -- at least I don't have to bake these things at home anymore! You just can't put a price on quality -- not to mention all the love and hard work they put into the food. Plus, the prices help in not overindulging. It's all about the food, the experience, and supporting local artisans. As for recommendations, it just depends on preference. Everything is good. I like that they have spelt bread & muffins, vollkornbrot, onion rolls, and special holiday treats. I also love their carrot & chocolate cakes, scones, and ESPECIALLY the hot croissants in the AM -- whole wheat or white, chocolate or ham & cheese. Oh, I almost forgot -- the peanut butter ganache sandwich cookies are so good, you really don't ever want to try one -- unless you want an addiction. The only problem I have is that my clothes are now too tight, and that they're not open on Mondays & Tuesdays. But maybe both of those things will change in the future. One last note: They do have one gluten-free menu item -- coconut macaroons (as far as I know).
