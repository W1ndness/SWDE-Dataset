Creation Cafe Falls Short
by Hoosier4goodfood (1 review)
September 26, 2008 - Doesn't like it
Who wouldn't want Creation Cafe to succeed? The Canal has long awaited a place to enjoy good food while gazing at the Indy skyline & canal. My husband and I gave the Cafe a try this evening and ultimately left wishing we had stayed home and cooked for ourselves.
The service: okay. Our waiter was kind and fairly attentive, although he couldn't remember what we ordered and it didn't help that he wasn't writing it down. Our mixed green salads w/ Maple & Ginger dressing were good, but we barely managed to eat two bites before our entrees were brought out.
My entree was incorrect, so I waited another 15 mins. for the correct order. My husband ate his dinner before it chilled as I eagerly awaited a Salmon dish with a vegetable medley. No worries, so I thought.
One would think this would be a light, healthy meal. Well, not at Creation Cafe. Would you like some salmon and vegetables with your butter sauce? My plate was literally a soup bowl of butter sauce! It took two napkins to soak the butter off of the salmon. The spinach and vegetables were ruined, so I just ate the salmon.
After spending $60 for two entrees and two glasses of wine for a 'casual' dining experience, I doubt I will spend more on their version of 'fine' dining when they take over the previous Buggs Temple restaurant. I will just stick with Elements, thank you.

Ruined our evening
by fluffqueen01 (1 review)
April 17, 2009
We were a small group of 6. (3 couples) who had plannined to meet for dinner at Creation Cafe and then walk the canal as it was a gorgeous evening. We arrived at 6:40. At 7:00, a waitress asked us if anyone had talked to us yet...no. She took drink orders. Took 10 minutes to get a glass of wine, one iced tea and 4 waters.
She took our orders, but when we asked about a certain kind of soup as a special, she told us that she didn't know as she hadn't looked at it.
At 7:45, we have had no one refill our drinks (I got up and did found a pitcher and refilled the waters for our table), no sign of the waitress or any food.
After one of the husbands located the manager and asked why we had been there over an hour with no food (and we ordered 3 salads, one burger, one chicken sandwich and one fish sandwich), things finally were brought to us.
Salads were good, burger was ok (however my medium well burger was very pink inside), fish sandwich ok, but fries with it were ice cold, chicken was rated flavorless.
When we went to pay, they did take 15% off the bill, however they added in a mandatory tip. We just wanted to get out of there as it was 8:30 so we didn't argue it, although the service was so bad, I would not have left a dime.
Needless to say, it was dark, canal walk was out, so we went somewhere else for desert, since no one had eaten much of their dinner.
The good? Decor and view. That is it.

Fun!
by Katie (43 reviews)
May 19, 2010 - Likes it
Having lived near by, I watched Bugg's Temple come to fruition over the past few years, and have always wanted to check it out. The location is a little awkward for me, especially working downtown, surrounded by many other delicious lunch options.
My friend took me to Creation Cafe for lunch this week and it exceeded my expectations. I glanced at the menu before I left to aid in my decision making, but it didn't really help because many options sounded delicious. My friend didn't make my decision much easier because she assured me that the items I had been considering were all very good.
I decided on the Caprese BLT and loved it. It's hard to go wrong with a BLT, but cheese does not usually do it for me. This sandwich is the genius combination of two favorites, TMB (Tomato, Basil, Mozzarella) and the BLT, and it works! The homemade potato chips that accompanied the sandwich were excellent also.
Our server was nice, our food came fast. No complaints in the service department. But what sets Creation Cafe apart from other restaurants is the atmosphere. It was an incredibly unique, cozy and fun environment. The well decorated walls provided lots of viewing entertainment for a girl with a short attention span. It was so relaxing and inviting that I could hang out there for hours.
I will definitely be back to Creation Cafe, but hopefully when I have the opportunity to walk there on the canal and not running to the door in a downpour.

great for lunch - not for dinner
by anne-e-mcgrath (1 review)
September 22, 2009
I went with family and my brand new fiance on a Friday night to celebrate, yes you guessed it, our engagement. I had lunch at Creation Cafe months earlier and loved it.
Dinner was a total chaotic mess. We actually had a waiter who when we asked if he could take our empty glasses from the table said, "eventually I can but not right now." What he ended up doing was going to the next table to pick up his tip.
The food was very mediocre which disappointed me because the Cuban sandwich I had for lunch was outstanding. One of our orders didn't make it to the table until 15 minutes after everyone elses food. Fries were cold. Beverage service was very slow.

They need to create a beverage service area that is waiter friendly because after reading other posts for this restaurant, it doesn't appear to be so.
I love the atmosphere, though. And I can handle happy hour drinks and some apps but more then likely, I would not take a group to dine there.
