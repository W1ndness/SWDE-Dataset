This is the best Chinese restaurant in town, and I have tried them all....
by Richard Nickel (1 review)
August 02, 2010
I have been eating regularly at the Orient for almost twenty five years. It has the best luncheon value anywhere and you should try it. For $5.50 or so you get a great meal that includes a spring roll and wonton soup plus the entree of your choice. Now try and beat that anywhere. If you can have a good sub from Subway for $5.00 you have to admit that a real meal from the Orient is a much better value. And I love the $5 subs from Subway. I am a cashew chicken slightly spicy fan and love the way they prepare it at the Orient. Several years ago I thought that the Schechuan House restaurant on Seminary Avenue in Luthersville might take the "best Chinese restaurant" title away from the Orient, but in the long run my family and I have all returned to the Orient. Great food and the best service for a very reasonable price.

Awesome Lunch Steal!
by Kathy (9 reviews)
June 13, 2010 - Likes it
The food has always been consistent and solid, and the lunch specials can't be beat for value. The sushi has always been good.
Our favorites include Szechuan string beans and Sha-Cha chicken (a slightly curried spicy brown sauce).
The hot and sour soup is thick and sour, and the heat can vary a little day-to-day. Most of the brown sauces are dark, and can occasionally be a bit salty. The white sauces (like Moo Goo Gai Pan) are bright and gingery!

My Stand-by for a Chinese dinner out
by MovingForward (16 reviews)
January 07, 2010 - Likes it
Thank heaven for The Orient. Nice restaurant, always a table. No waiting. Decent service. Open on Christmas. Yes, the decor is stuck in the 1980s, but it's not grimy. The food is decent. The mooshu pork is one of their best dishes. I prefer this to The Orient in Perry Hall, because it's more polished and Perry Hall doesn't serve liquor. (I live in Towson.)
