Not Mexican, but Perfect for the Neighborhood
by ghost (76 reviews)
October 31, 2009
Hacienda Colorado specializes in what they call "Mountain Mex," and it is exactly that: Mexican dishes given a very local twist. Not to imply that this translation of Mexican food is bad, but you should not go here expecting authentic Mexican dishes.
That said, the food is freshly prepared and served with a variety of tasty sauces. Just stay away from the queso because it is just nasty processed cheese. Otherwise, the Poblano de Pollo and Camarones Baja are real crowd pleasers. The fish tacos and shrimp enchiladas are also quite tasty even though all of their seafood comes in frozen.
The tequila list is long, and the margs are plentiful. Don't expect your servers to be terribly knowledgeable about alcoholic beverages though. You kind of have to know what you want before you go in. The fountain drinks are huge, so you don't have to worry about asking for too many refills.
The service consists of a bunch of teenagers and young adults, who seem a little better trained than the usual chain employees. However, service can be spotty, but the management staff will bend over backwards to make you happy.
Finally, this restaurant is truly a family restaurant. If you don't like being around families with small children, you should seek out another restaurant. On the other hand, if you want a place to take the extended family on a Sunday evening, this restaurant is just right for you. Expect a wait on the weekends, and call ahead if you can.
I don't love this place, but the atmosphere and food are mild enough and approachable enough for local families to really enjoy.
