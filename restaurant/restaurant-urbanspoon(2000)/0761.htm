Gushing
by TalesFromTheCrip (40 reviews)
June 09, 2009 - Really likes it
I usually reserve judgment about a restaurant until after eating there. Not so with superstar Thomas Keller's eponymous Per Se in New York. As a result of their e-mail barrage confirming and reconfirming our reservation, a tedious telephone message explaining a dress code so pedestrian that only drunken rugby players direct from a scrimmage are likely to violate it, and a steep penalty for those who dare not show for dinner, I'd had enough Per Se arrogance.
But, when the time for our reservation came, we were there and our earlier peevishness was gone before the amuse bouche arrived.
A meal at Per Se is neither simple, nor complex. It may have thousands of components, but they're designed, performed and executed to relaxing, satisfying, unpretentious and outrageously delicious perfection - a magic carpet of culinary care. As well deserved as Nicholson's Oscars, so are the Toques, Diamonds and Stars earned by Keller's tribute to taste and spiritual nourishment in Manhattan - Per Se.
Though their draconian cancellation policies put me off, now, after one amazing dinner, they make perfect sense. It would be unthinkable to allow even a single chair go empty; frankly to deprive a diner of such gastronomic works of art should be criminal. A no show is the guest's loss, and the penalty is Per Se's way of saying, "don't make the mistake of missing this." The metaphor continues as the front of house staff are more likened to curators, than hosts or servers. Just as time allows only a limited number of fortunates to file by a Michelangelo or Da Vinci, so Per Se's courteous gatekeepers are trusted with the difficult, but surely rewarding, job of maximizing the numbers permitted to feast.
From the unexpected automatic glass side panels that provide "open sesame" ingress to the culinary magic kingdom, to the breathtaking views of Central Park, they are all upstaged, without fuss, stuffiness or presumption by dish after dish of edible masterpieces - prepared and presented with precision and finesse. The incredible made to look easy.

Per-Fection
by Miranda (22 reviews)
October 26, 2008 - Likes it
First of all, eating here is about more than just the food (though the food is truly spectacular). Every aspect of the experience is special and perfectly executed with what seems an effortless graciousness - the setting, the service, and the tone of the place are all spot-on.
Per Se is a super-fancy, high-end place that still manages to exude warmth and a sense of welcome that would seem more characteristic of a tiny, county inn or bistro. I was made to feel like a treasured guest while at Per Se, which is exactly what you want anywhere, but especially somewhere that charges $275 a person for dinner. And, of course, it all too often seems that the more you pay for a dinner, the snootier and less welcoming the restaurant tends to be. Per Se is not snooty, not at all. For me, this was perhaps the biggest and most welcome surprise of the evening.
I should say that I'm a huge Thomas Keller fan. I think his approach to cooking is brilliant - the application of precise, albeit time-consuming, processes of food preparation result in impeccable results (ultra-refined sauces, intensely flavored garnishes, ridiculously tender meat, and gorgeous, artistic presentations). So, naturally, I expected the food to be among the best I have tasted - and it was fabulous.
Not every single dish was perfect. An in all honestly, I think I've probably had the occasional dish or two elsewhere (here I'm thinking of Vetri in Philadelphia, Crush in Seattle, or Jerome's in the south of France) that transcended some of those at Per Se individually in terms of flavor combinations or originality, but never have I experienced such precision and refinement throughout - such minute attention to every detail of the food and how that food was presented, displayed, and consumed. And this was true of every course we were served and of every other aspect of the restaurant too (all the way from the fold of the napkins on the table to the genuine pleasure the servers took in presenting and describing our food and answering our questions). It was truly impeccable.
The highlight of the evening turned out not to be just the amazing meal (perfectly paced, perfectly portioned), but the fact that Thomas Keller himself was present in the kitchen and that, after dinner, we were taken on a tour of the kitchen, and that he stopped what he was doing to chat with us for a moment and pose for a photo or two. He was gracious (I'll admit, I was surprised), perhaps recognizing real people who genuinely loved his food and appreciated his contribution American cuisine. It elevated the evening beyond our expectations (which were pretty high) and provided the perfect example of how welcomed we were made to feel throughout the entire night.
Save your pennies in a jar - and when you've filled 50 or so jars, go to Per Se. You won't be sorry.

Preferred over French Laundry
by D. Vower (30 reviews)
April 14, 2010 - Really likes it
Can a chef stretch his artistry too far, with the whole suffering? Yes.
My experiences at Per Se have been superb: they rank among the best of my life. One in particular involved a vertical tasting of Chateau Lafleur dating to the '47 vintage, all bottles direct from the Chateau. Dreams are made of this stuff! Chef Keller has gone to great lengths to ensure that Per Se lives up to NYC standards. And those dining at the establishment have fortunately benefitted; I will certainly rave about my experiences there. But alas, the child that put him on the map, the French Laundry, has suffered from neglect. For a scientifically perfect meal rendered and presented flawlessly, Per Se is the restaurant in NYC. Nevertheless, I feel somewhat saddened that "the other place" has suffered as a result. For me, this is a mixed blessing.

The most beautiful culinary experience I have ever had
by mimi (6 reviews)
January 29, 2010 - Likes it
My mom and I went to Per Se on a whim...I've been dreaming of it ever since. We both did the vegetarian tasting menu and it was truly perfect. From the presentation and plating of the dishes to the construct of each dish and the progression of flavors as you moved through the tasting it was nothing but total bliss. Around the 7th course my mom and I were telling them we couldn't eat anymore and the food kept coming-- and we keep indulging in it.
Aside from the food, the service was impeccable- wow, this is the definition of the finest service. To illustrate my point, there was one particular dish that was just too salty for both my mom and me-- our server could tell we didn't love it since it was mostly still there. The server had the head maitre d' and general manager come over to apologize profusely in concert with him. We let them know it was quite alright and there was no problem. After the desert that came with our menu, they brought us a second round of raspberry beignets- a dessert from sister resaturant, French Laundry- to make up for the plate we didn't like.
To top off the meal and once again apologize, they gave us each a box full of their chocolates to take with us.
Worth every penny, spectacular experience that you will never forget.
