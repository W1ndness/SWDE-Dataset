Eat at Thurman's, at least Once.
by Ambrosiac (4 reviews)
June 20, 2010
Thurman Cafe, German Village's crowning jewel delivers burgers that are unparalleled in taste and quality. The dive bar ambiance is textbook and a cool spot to bring out-of-towners. Any carnivore is sure to find something that fits, from their juicy burgers served medium well to hot and crispy fried jumbo chicken wings served whole. For others, their thin-crust pizza that will satisfy those with the most discriminating palate. Thurman's always gives you what you want, the food is so mouth watering because they take their food seriously.
With that said, Food is given an A but their service does falter due to overwhelming patrons at lunch and dinnertime. Their waiting lobby is just as big as the dining area itself, and tactically stocked with pinball and a Galaga/Ms. Pacman combo arcade machine.
From my own experience, I have waited in the purgatory-esque waiting from 6 pm to 9 pm on a weds. night. just to sit down and order. Even then, the food does take patience but by then anything will taste good. But rest assured, the food is good. Bring friends, grab a beer and be prepared to wait during busy hours. Consider yourselves informed. If you're a group of 3 or less, I'd consider sitting the bar.
Pros- delicious,hot food unrivaled in taste and quality by any non-chain burger establishment. Juicy burgers, Jumbo whole wings, and pizza shop quality crust will have you returning.
Cons- The service does bog due to overwhelming patrons. You will wait a long time to be served at busy hours. Not the cleanest restaurant.

The Thurman-ator... yum.
by James (9 reviews)
April 03, 2009 - Likes it
The Thurman-ator is the best burger I have ever had. From bottom to top, it has: bun, mayo, lettuce, tomato, pickle, hot pepper, 12 oz burger, bacon, cheddar, another 12 oz burger, sauteed onion, mushrooms, green pepper, ham, american cheese, provolone cheese, top bun. And if that is not enough, it is served with fries.
This is definitely a burger you eat with a knife and fork.
The next time I go to Columbus, I will surely return. I wouldn't get a Thurman-ator again, since that was a little too much food. But I had to order it once. :) I can eat a lot, and was very hungry by the time it came. I was about three big bites from finishing. I could have done it, but I would have been in pain. As it was, I didn't need to eat much else for the rest of the day. I might just go with a Thurman Burger next time, which is similar but only one patty.
Be prepared for a wait, even if you go at an off-peak time. I tried to go on a Saturday evening, and the lineup was crazy. I am guessing about 3 hours, but that is just my guess based on the number of people on the waiting list. I figured they had to turnover the entire place about 3 or 4 times before we would get in. We returned on Sunday. We got there at 11:30am, which I figured would be quite early. We still had to wait until after 12:00 to get our seat. Our order took about 50 minutes to get to us. The wait was very much forgiven by about the second bite.
If you are reading this considering whether you should go, just do it. It is worth the wait. Just plan for it.
