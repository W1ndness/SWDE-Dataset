Surin
by David Aldridge (124 reviews)
June 10, 2010 - Likes it
We went for dinner on a Tuesday night and it wasn't crowded and they had half price appetizers until 7pm. I thought the surin baskets were amazing and I liked the first order so much that we ordered another plate of them. The spicy chicken soup was also very good and there is plenty of it to share. We had the chicken stir fry with vegetables over rice and everything tasted delicious. I loved the flavors and all the spices in the sauces and I thought the service was very good too. Prices weren't bad - especially when it's half-price appetizers. And as for parking, I'd recommend finding a spot behind the restaurant or on the street right beside it.

So I'll be back--when in Atlanta and want Thai--try Surin, it's really great.

Fan-Thai-stic in VH
by Michael Erickson (26 reviews)
December 05, 2008 - Likes it
Of course, you must start with the coconut chicken soup which is absolutely delicious and comes to your table in a big flaming bowl.
It's a little loud and crowded, but luckily we were able to get a table in the corner, so it was quite nice.  I ordered the sweet and sour chicken (I know, I know, Michael E. can't you be a bit more adventurous than that?!?!) and enjoyed it very much.  
There's a bit of a language barrier with some of the waiters, but I think it adds to the experience.  We all figured out what we wanted after pointing to the menu.
This place has been around for a long time and I imagine it will keep going strong for many years.  Great place to take out-of-town friends, too.
