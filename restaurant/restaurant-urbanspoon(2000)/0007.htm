Finally, a Great Coffee House in Corrales...
by Larry McGoldrick (128 reviews)
November 12, 2009 - Really likes it
Oasis Coffee & Tea is a new full-service coffee house that is just opening in Corrales. We have been watching the building next to the firehouse for signs of life, and yesterday were rewarded with their soft opening. Grand opening will be very soon.
As we entered, we were met by Chris Norman, one of the owners (brother Paul and sister Melissa are others). Chris filled us in on the development of Oasis and gave us a tour. The site, not quite finished, is very beautiful with decorations, statuary, and fountains from Penelope's Garden, a lovely decorators shop just down the road (near Nate & Hannah's). Oasis and Penelope's are joint family ventures.
Jane and I had delightful coffees (roasted by a local Corrales roaster), and sat to chat with Chris. He told us about the menu that will be available for the official opening. Pastries, tarts, sandwiches, and even some pizzas will be served.
Paul fixed us a "Corrales Hot Dog," which is a hot dog sliced in half with a white cheese and green chiles grilled between two slices of delicious bread. Not like your grandma's wieners. Wonderful, and if this is representative of their fare, they have a real winner in this place.
On a later visit, I had a double latte accompanied by a Cubano sandwich. The Cubano was just right -- pork, ham, swiss cheese, pickle, and mustard on very good bread, and the whole thing grilled to a nutty brown. Better than any Cubano I've had in Miami (haven't been to Cuba yet). Probably the best in Duke City.
Corrales needs a place like this. There will eventually be live music (soft, I hope -- I suggested string quartets). Oasis is a short walk from my house, and I expect to meet many old and new friends there.
More later. See you there. Look for the Cowboy Hat.
*****************Note added December 22 *************
I stopped in Oasis last Sunday with Jane at around 4:00 pm as the place was scheduled to close. It was still crowded, so we stayed and chatted with Chris and Paul for quite a while. They are happy with the response from the community. The interior has been finished, and many lovely fountains are running. Paul told me that Penelope's Garden, which was near Nate and Hannah's, has now been closed and the garden business is now an integral part of Oasis. [Nate and Hannah have turned the former space into a consignment shop, according to Paul.]
Some of my dear friends have been to Oasis, and enthusiastically give it thumbs up. Two thumbs up.

The Oasis In The High Desert
by Victoria (1 review)
March 25, 2010 - Likes it
I love this place! It is beautiful, relaxing and friendly, not to mention the good food and a fine assortment of great drinks and pastries. The environment lends itself to a really nice experience wether you are hungry, just want to take your time and visit with a friend, make new friends or hold a meeting. This is the place. Gentle chimes, water features, subdued decor, and a variety of seating arraignments. You can join the throng or just cuddle in a corner and read, write, just sit and think, or count your blessings. There is even outside seating, and a nice view of our mountain. The caring staff will always welcome you and care about your comfort. The above reasons are why I love this Oasis in the middle of Corrales New Mexico.

Great Light Snacks and Coffee Drinks!
by Michael K Corrales (2 reviews)
March 23, 2010 - Really likes it
I particularly enjoy the tuna melt, and, of course the excellent coffee drinks. The espresso is superb! Coffees are locally roasted, and can be purchased packaged for your enjoyment at home (from Fat Boy Coffee Roasters in the east mountains, and from Corrales Coffee Roasters). The atmosphere is relaxing, with a variety of fountains and water features, and the owners encourage small groups to make use of the cafe for events. All in all, one of the most enjoyable places to chill out over a good cuppa in the Albuquerque area.
