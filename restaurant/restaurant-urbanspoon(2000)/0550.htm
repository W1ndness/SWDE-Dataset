Excellent!!
by Mr. Bill (122 reviews)
September 06, 2009 - Likes it
This has to be one of the smartest contemporary restaurants in Knoxville! The interior design deserves as many compliments as the food. The new room with the copper walls is stunning! It just glows with the distinctive lighting throughout the restaurant.
I couldn't begin without the Duck Wellington…outstanding flaky pastry and finished with a whisky fig preserve and chive crème fraiche. Our Caesar Salad with very crisp romaine and croutons was topped with a nice dressing. The main feature of the evening was Honey Ginger Pork Loin. Whenever I see duck or pork loin, that will be the direction I head. It was presented with apple chutney and apricot polenta that was a nice diversion to the ordinary polentas served in this area. We also enjoyed a Sundried Tomato Salmon that was served over whole grain mustard/thyme mashed potatoes. A beautiful saffron wine butter sauce accompanied this dish.
Of course, desserts looked very tempting, but we had a very good coffee to finish. The wine and beer selection is very good…but no martinis here.
As I overheard, Seasons Café delivers high-end food that is easy on the wallet.

Favorite Spot!
by Heather (1 review)
June 09, 2010 - Likes it
First off, I've been frequenting this establishment since it first opened. The thing I love about Seasons Cafe is the gourmet menu which offers a variety of dining choices while still falling into a moderate price range. Some of my favorites: Pumpkin Bisque soup (seasonal), spinich and berry salad, crabcake sandwich (which is heavy on the crab and light on everything else-yum!) and the new york strip. Still, these tend to only be fall-back items in the event they are sold out of the nightly special. Usually the special includes a seafood dish which is sure to please. My favorites include anything Chef Little conjures up with "sea bass" in the title. If you call and he's got this as a special you better hurry in, because it won't last long and those who try it are never disappointed.
In addition to the fine food, I am extremely fond of their extensive and varied wine list. I have found the staff to be extremely helpful in choosing just the right wine to compliment my meal. A new concept that has recently been a success is adding liquor drinks including some daring and bold martini choices created by my favorite bartender, Sarah.
In my opinion, you can't go wrong spending your time enjoying the fine cuisine at an establishment such as Seasons Cafe. Enjoy!

If you love Season's for dinner - Webster's is for you at lunch
by farragutfirst (1 review)
December 17, 2008 - Likes it
As a long time Farragut citizen living in Fox Den, I was thrilled the first time I experienced Seasons Café in the Renaissance-Farragut development on Kingston Pike. Not only was this cozy locally owned cafe close to my home, I had some of the best food I had ever eaten! Several years later, and many many trips back to Seasons Café, I am still just as enthralled by the atmosphere, the staff, and most of all the FOOD. I have traveled all over the world and the food at Seasons is constantly as good or better than some of the most expensive places on the planet. The best of all is that their pricing is extremely moderate for the level of quality and service that you receive. Although, that is not why I logged on…The guys who own Seasons opened a little deli next door called Webster's. They have been open for over a year, yet, I never went there because of my love for Seasons. However, yesterday I made it a point to go try Webster's. AND I WAS AMAZED. These guys have done it again. It is hard to explain, but it is a deli type of place with a twist. The sandwiches I had, called the HC Anderson, was out of this world. If you have not tried Webster's, then you got to give it a shot - Nothing in Knoxville beats Season's for dinner, however, Webster's for lunch now gets my vote!

Disappointed...
by Emelia (3 reviews)
October 12, 2009
We were excited to try Season's after hearing a lot of great reviews from friends. But there were more than a few problems with our food. First off we ordered the baked brie appetizer in puff pastry. Yuck! This is a very easy dish to make tasty. But it was nothing like I had imagined. Slices of brie sat on top of a very dry piece off puff pastry with a plate covered, no drenched in what can only be described as sickly sweet caramel sauce. There were also tempura apple slices and a huge amount of very pretty blackberries, raspberries and blueberries. However this should have been a dessert. There was almost nothing savory about it with the cloyingly sweet sauce. I honestly pretty much lost my appetite after two bites. The harvest salad was fairly good, but once again too sweet. My friend had the fig and prosciutto flat bread which sounds good in theory I pictured sliced figs with a delicate amount of cambazola sprinkled on top, baked and then covered with thinly sliced prosciutto. But what he got was a piece of flat bread(just like what is served before dinner) covered with a sort of fig paste, topped with thick slices of dried out prosciutto and so much cambazola that it made your mouth pucker. There were some other problems with our food but those were the two worst offenders. I think the chef has a serious sweet tooth and I am sure there are a lot of people who will like that, but it just isn't our style. On the plus side the service was good. They also have really great wine specials. I found the atmosphere pleasant but not outstanding.
