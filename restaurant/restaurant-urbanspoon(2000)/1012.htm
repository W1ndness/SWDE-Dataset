Always Perfect
by Frederick (217 reviews)
August 01, 2010 - Really likes it
I've never had a bad experience here - and I've been going to Chez Pascal since their earlier incarnation years ago in Narragansett. The transformation wrought on this old La France space was stunning and I was so glad to see the last of that dated Lucretia Borgia decor. There are two recent experiences that stand out as exceptional at this restaurant -- their annual heirloom tomato dinner, featuring seven courses showcasing the bounty of Rhode Island's fabulous crop of real tomatoes, including a dessert of grilled baby yellows with bacon ice cream. And the other experience was at Christmas when I needed a custom prix-fixe dinner for twelve people. The service was so smooth and gracious and they skillfully kept my grandparents from ever seeing a menu and thus avoiding a two hour discussion on the price of asparagus.
One good tip - start your evening at their intimate bar and get to know the bartender. They craft some killer martinis, the real ones, served up in ice cold glasses about the size of a bird bath.

Hello from Tucson!
by foodswinger (12 reviews)
January 17, 2009 - Really likes it
To Kristin, Matthew and staff of Chez Pascal:
All I can possibly think to say to you is wow! Thank you for such a memorable experience this last week. It was amazing.
For those reading, let me give you a feel for our experience.... first, upon entering the restaurant, I immediately felt welcome, Kristin (owner) was at the front, very inviting - we were early for a reservation for 7, so we went to the bar. Our bartender was very helpful, we chose 2 cocktails, a pear + ginger sorbet drink with Goose Pear, and I had the "Chez Lounge" (which I highly reccomend) it had Lemon sorbet, citrus vodka - it was so smooth. We were taken to out table, and the fun really began. Here is how we ordered: Bottle of great rose - not sure of producer with: charcuterie plate, duck prosciutto salad, special of the night: fish soup (outstanding) - My aunt had taken a look at the menu a few weeks prior, and fell in love with the scallop appetizer - unfortunately, it wasn't on the menu, but the chef was kind enough to create an appetizer from some scallops he happened to have in the kitchen - it was great. My brother also had a special request - the beef shortribs were too good to miss, but he didn't want them for an entree, so the chef was kind enough to make an appetizer using them - could have been one of the best dishes all night!!!! The warm cabbage salad with bleu cheese custard - THIS WAS AMAZING. We could not believe how harmoneously this dish worked together - it had a great bite of vinegar, the custard was creamy and rich... wow.
Bottle of Nicolas Potel burgundy - incredible. The only small complaint was that the menu said 2005, and we were served 2006 - but I love this producer, and the wine was KILLER. Really smooth, lots of character. For dinner we had a really tough deciding. But we shared: Duck entree (breast and confit leg), tasting of Pork (will come back to this in a second), cassoulet, chicken, and veggie dish. All were honestly superb - the tasting of pork was stupendous. It is so cool that Matt gets in a whole oinker, breaks it down, and presents different cuts and different preparations on the same dish. Flavors were irresistable. Owning a restaurant myself in Tucson, for the first time recently, I was completely blown away! Mark, our server was great, there when we needed him, and away when we didn't. He helped guide us through what were his favorite items (food and wine), he has been there since they opened, it is great to see a team -member "owning" the guests experience. Also some of the art in the place were his creations, check out the 3 pigs...
I will stop here - but believe me... Chez Pascal will see us again!!! Thank you all again. Brian

Decent food. Horrid service.
by Juan (1 review)
July 24, 2010 - Doesn't like it
After doing a healthy amount of research on where to eat in Providence during my stay there, I decided on Chez Pascal. While i found the food to be, for the most part very good, I was completely put off by the service.
During our entire meal, our waiter was very gruff and short with us, at times talking down to us. Additionally, we were rushed through every course, and the check was laid down on the table before we were halfway through dessert. Now it's possible that it was a reflection of his preconceptions of our age, or perhaps that after Restaurant Week he was tired and frustrated, or any number of other preconceptions, but we will not be back to visit, regardless of the decent food and menu.

Very Good French Restaurant
by Dave (12 reviews)
September 03, 2009 - Likes it
On a recent visit, we had the charcuterie appetizer, which I think is an absolute must have if you like this sort of thing. Yes, it's pricey, but it is superb! The food at Chez Pascal is some of the finest prepared in the state, and it won't cost you an arm and a leg like some of the more pretentious places in Providence and Newport.
If there was one thing that I'd like to see improve at CP, it's the service. There was nothing terrible about it, but the food and the dining experience there demand a higher level of expertise than there is currently. Still, go for the food - you will not be disappointed.
