Message from Serendipity 3

  • Best Desserts in NYC
  • Brunch, Lunch, & Dinner Menu
  • Created Frrrozen Hot Chocolate

A cozy palace where dream-sized desserts and ice cream parlor whimsy lull you into a fantasy of another day and age. Here you can buy a Tiffany lamp or teddy bear, dine on a hot dog or caviar, and sit at a table that Marilyn Monroe, Bette Davis, Andy Warhol,or Jackie Kennedy once sat at. Since 1954, the landmark Serendipity 3, has served delicious comfort food in a unique and entertaining setting. Whimsical dishes such as 'Young Chicken Sandwiches', 'Curried Chicken Almondine Casserole', 'Bi-Sensual Burger',& 'Madame Butterfly Pasta' has made ordering almost as fun as eating! The oxy-moronic Frrrozen Hot Chocolate is one chilly bowl of magnificence that renders the place a must visit. Lovers have proposed over it, & diets have been broken over it. From the 'Can't Say No Sundae' to 'YuDuFunDu', the desserts at Serendipity will satisfy the sweet tooth of all.

+ 2 more photos from Barney Whimsical tourist spot
by Barney (485 reviews)
December 07, 2008 - Likes it
Guess I should have known this place was a bit of a tourist destination. I called yesterday and they told me it'd be a 3 hour wait (yes, they have limited reservations, but none available for the next couple days). During my call, I got the advice "show up 15-20 minutes before we open tomorrow and you should get in the first wave." We showed up at 10:45 and were seated by 11:15. The place is tiny and very very very tight (capacity 150 or so). We were lucky enough to be upstairs, a little less traffic and less claustrophobic, but you're pretty much jammed in with chairs very close and the staff trying to navigate.
Service was very nice, but not the most speedy. My wife had The Haight-Ashbury (sliced turkey, tomato, avocado and alfalfa sprouts with melted muenster on raisin pumpernickel with Russian dressing) which was a relatively large sandwich, nicely hot, and very tasty. I had the Ftatateeta's Toast (a "french toasted" cream cheese sandwich with strawberries and apricot jam). Boy was this tasty! Not too large and not overly sweet. A really great breakfast option that was also very tasty and something I enjoyed a lot. Finally we shared a "frozen hot chocolate" along with every other person in the place. They're right, it's frozen (enough that you have to be careful of the brain freeze) and tastes pretty much like hot chocolate. Note, see pictures of all this attached to the resto page.
All in all, a lot of fun if you're not in a hurry, but probably more of a one-time tourist stop than someplace I'd hit again.

Serendipity 3 - Sweet Delight in the Apple
by Ms.MunchMunch (17 reviews)
August 02, 2010
The whimsical and twinkle in the eye Serendipity is now a tourist attraction....but hey, it's what attracted me!
My preference is to go at night, and to be seated on the second floor. The first floor has got the looks, but the second floor has got the WHAM! BAM! THANK YOU MA'AM (AND MISTER)! feel of the place.
I had this great server and so I asked him what his recommendation is. And he answers,
"Well, my absolute favorite is the Seafood Pesto Alfredo."
So I ordered the pesto alfredo. And I was definitely not sorry. I like it cheesy, so I sprinkled it with parmesan queso and it was perfecto! Thank you Mister Server!
And on for the sweet, sweet finale: Frozen Hot Chocolate.
You know, if they just coined the term - Chocolate Shake, it would not be the same. Really.
But "Frozen Hot Chocolate" hints that it will give you the same sensation as a Hot Chocolate would, the night before Christmas, the night all children ("from 1 to 92") get super psyched and tingly about. Just with a chill thrill.
If it was just a "Chocolate Shake", then no. But BECAUSE the name is "Frozen Hot Chocolate," I love it so.
Needless to say, but I'll so say it anyway, it is HIGHLY recommended to get a reservation! It's simple, ladies and gents! Call them a week, a few days before, to place your reservation and you're set. Sans reservation will place you (at least) an hour-ish wait.
