Message from Toulouse Petit

Toulouse Petit offers one of the most compelling dining and lounge experiences in the country . . . in the most texturally rich, detailed and hand crafted environment in the region.

Our diverse menu is centered around the cuisine of France and New Orleans.
Our extensive Dinner Menu features more than 85 items, 50 of which are priced between $6 and $15 for unmatched value.
Toulouse also serves the best Happy Hour menu in the nation.  50 compelling menu items are priced less than $5. Our premium cocktail menu is priced at $6.95, and fifteen excellent wines by the glass are priced $7 or less.  Happy Hour is served from 10 pm to 1 am and 4 pm - 5:30 daily.
We also our best in city Breakfast and Lunch Menu from 9 am to 2:30 pm daily.  More than 60 breakfast and lunch items are priced less than $10.
From exquisitely prepared cuisine, our sincere service, our energized and inviting environment, and lowest possible pricing Toulouse is committed to providing maximum  value.

Can Wait to Come Back...
by Bob's Stomach (173 reviews)
January 10, 2010 - Really likes it
I had heard about the $5 per item Happy Hour breakfast deal through January at this new New Orleans/French restaurant. I didn't know that it was weekdays only but we decided to give it a try anyway--and that turned out to be my first really good decision of the day. We ordered (and split) a fruit plate, beignets--both recommended by our server--and an avocado and Roma tomatoes Eggs Benedict. I never thought there'd be anything about a fruit plate that would cause me to rave about: wrong! The fruit was hyper-flavorful and combined with the condensed milk-cream is a knockout. When they call this The City's Best Fruit Plate they are not exaggerating.
As for the beignets, the delicious pastries combined with the chicory anglaise was incredible. To top it all off our eggs benedict were as tasty as everything that preceded them--however, they did arrive a bit cold (but it didn't stop us from devouring them). The potatoes were remarkable--which is of itself remarkable as I view potatoes as 'filler' and rarely eat them, no less comment on their preparation.
I found it interesting reading other reviews commenting on poor service. Our server, Maya, was a delight: prompt, attentive, personable, and when we mentioned that the eggs had been somewhat cold, she comp'ed us for the eggs. Lastly, the decor in Toulouse is unusual and visually compelling. From the marquetry tabletops, ironwork artistry in the lighting fixtures and railings, to the overall design and wall treatments, our local artisans did well.
I expect that this will become another regular breakfast haunt for us.

- We were seated next to the window on the far southeast corner of the restaurant where there was a bright street light switching on and off every few minutes. We were thinking a little bit of drapery on the high windows would be nice to add to the drama of the decor and solve this ambience killer.
- The appetizers and desserts we shared were okay. The escargot was sparse and chewy, and the fried spicy alligator was reminded me of popcorn chicken. We shared beignets and bananas foster for dessert. The beignets were night as light and fluffy as we would have liked.
- Lastly, the service had points of opportunity in terms of timing. Our bread came out essentially with the appetizers. The sauce that my friend ordered on the side with her entree was missing so it was awhile before the waitress checked in for us to ask her about it. Then it took them awhile to come out with it. Not to be too nit-picky, but there were a couple of times when our water was being refilled that the water was poured all over the table and was not noticed by the pourer.
Overall:
- Again, the experience was good, but for the amount of the bill in the end I would have liked to have had fewer not so good points either in the food or service. I may come back for another visit and think it is worth going at least once, but would say it's not a spectacular restaurant that I would return to again and again.
