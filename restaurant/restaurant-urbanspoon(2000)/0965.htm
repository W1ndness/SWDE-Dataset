Fresh Food, Friendly Staff
by Amber (24 reviews)
July 20, 2009 - Really likes it
I'm a bit behind in writing this review, but you shouldn't delay in getting over to Latitude 8!! The restaurant is located in the heart of downtown Chandler, just south of Chandler Blvd and Arizona Ave. We had the Spicy Thai Lettuce wraps as an appetizer and the Tom Yum Soup as a starter. The owner poured our wine, a delicious Australian Shiraz that complemented everything we ordered. My date ordered a Thai Iced Tea, one of his favorites, and was not disappointed. For our main courses we ordered the Steak Pad Kee Mao and the Wok Seared Ginger Chicken and Prawns. The Steak was cooked to medium rare perfection, and the Prawns were huge. Every dish tasted like the herbs and vegetables had been picked fresh from a local garden. Unfortunately I was too stuffed to order dessert, but am looking forward to the Fried Plantains on my next trip!

Good but not Great- Pricey
by SC (15 reviews)
May 13, 2010 - Likes it
UPDATE to this review:
Shortly after this review was written I received an email from the owner addressing every detail of this review. This shows Latitude 8's stride as a local business to reach out to their customers and community. The email was kind and informative.
------------------------------------------------------------ ---------------
We found Latitude 8 to have a modern hip atmosphere with good lighting. We were greeted at the door and sat immediately.
We liked what we had for the most part. We voted "like" but will not be in a hurry to return. Definitely over priced. They are trying to attract a certain clientele but will need to work on a few things before it is really worth it.
All dishes were well presented and were a good portion size.
The waitress was unengaged and was only at our table to take the initial order and then to drop and collect the bill. We were there for about an hour and we were never approached again to ask how our meal was or if we needed more to drink. This nearly swayed my vote. We dine out for a unique meal, social aspect and atmosphere. This made the cool surroundings seem unfriendly. There was a larger table near us and all of the working crew seemed to be very attentive to this table but obviously forgetting they had other quests.
We had stuffed chicken wings for an appetizer. Odd texture to them with the minced chicken. Bland without the sauce on the plate to dip them in. All in all they were good.
For entrees we had Panang Curry Chicken and Pad Thai. Neither dish had much spice to it, quite bland. There weren't any traditional sauces given to spice to your liking. Every Thai restaurant I have been to will present you with different sauces. The chicken in both dishes was DRY. It was evident that the chicken was cooked too quickly on a very hot grill- all the juice was gone.
We found posted on the menu that refills for your soda and iced tea were not free. Kinda crazy when you are paying a lot for your meal.
The wine list is not that good for a pricey restaurant. We also thought that they could support local wine makers.
Support local businesses. We always make a strong effort to do so and hope that others will too. This way they can afford to improve and remain in business.
