Reasonable Food, Bad Attitude
by Nathaniel (2 reviews)
December 11, 2008 - Doesn't like it
I have been going to Rosa's since 1992 because of it's friendly family atmosphere and it's amazing Sonoran Mexican food. Every time my family and I went there we were greeted as if we were old friends.
All that slowly changed with Rosa's increasing popularity. When they expanded, the restaurant was launched from a friendly (and occasionally very busy) little family joint, to a place of indifferent and sloppy service. The quality of the food, while still good, has gone down hill, and the service has degraded from friendly and fun to mistake riddled and hostile.
Every time I've been in the past few years, the discrepancies on the bill have increased, and the over all experience has gone down. You can order extra guacamole, but it might not find it's way to your table until after your meal. And when you ask your server for the 2nd, 3rd and maybe even 4th time for it, you will be treated as if you are somehow to blame for it's absence.
The last (and I do mean the very LAST) time I went there, the service was it's (now) regular lousy quality, the plates came out randomly, the salsa had to be requested 4 times to 2 different servers to get, and I was made to feel bad for wanting extra salsa in the process. When the bill finally came out, it was totally illegible and we had to get a menu to sort it out. Turns out they had added things we never ordered and I was treated poorly as I tried to resolve the issue. I informed the woman I was dissatisfied with my experience there, but instead of an apology she retorted that I didn't have to come here anymore if I didn't like it.
As a long time patron and a person who has always tried to give them the benefit of the doubt when things didn't go smoothly, I was hurt.
In closing, the salsa is still great and while the food quality has slipped, it is still good. But if you want a pleasant dining experience where you are made to feel as if you are a welcomed guest in an original Tucson establishment while you enjoy quality Mexican food, I recommend you make a reservation for Cafe Poca Cosa and stay far away from Rosa's.
