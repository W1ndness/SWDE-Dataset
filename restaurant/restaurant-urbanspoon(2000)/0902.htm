Great Time and Great Food!
by UCF Knights Fan (69 reviews)
July 13, 2010 - Likes it
I have been to 'Ohana in Disney's Polynesian Resort on two separate occasions and I enjoy this place immensely.
The decor is inspired by the South Seas - wood carvings, stone tikis, tropical greenery, and Polynesian motifs can be seen all throughout the restaurant. The meats are cooked to perfection over an expansive 18-foot oak fire pit that is visible from the dining room.
Everything is served all you can eat family style. You start off with coconut bread, mixed green salad with honey lime dressing, stir-fried vegetables, pork fried dumplings, lo mein noodles, and corriander sesame chicken wings. They also bring you three different types of dipping sauces for your meats (peanut, chimichurri, and sweet and sour). The servers bring skewers of marinated sirloin steak, chicken, barbecue pork loin, and shrimp to your table on a continual basis. The table also gets a huge dessert to share - bread pudding a la mode with warm banana foster sauce. You must try 'Ohana's signature alcoholic drink - Lapu Lapu! It is served in a large fresh pineapple and is worth the $13.50 they charge for it.
I enjoy all the food I have tasted here. The meats are flavorful and charred to perfection, and the pork fried dumplings and lo mein noodles are especially delicious! The servers are always very friendly and accommodating.
Some tips: Time your dinner so you can watch the fireworks over Cinderella Castle at the Magic Kingdom - we did and it was a nice treat (8:20pm reservation for the 10:00pm firework show). The restaurant always seems to fill up very quickly with reservations, so if you want to eat here plan accordingly and make your reservation way in advance.
They give you a lei and have cute events around the restaurant for the kids - maraca congo line type of dance and a coconut race.
Although it is pricey, I would recommend trying 'Ohana out. It is a unique, fun, enjoyable Polynesian experience for all!
