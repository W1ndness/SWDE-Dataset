Simply exceptional.
by bubs (17 reviews)
May 21, 2010 - Really likes it
This has long been one of, if not, my favorite restaurant in Pittsburgh, and my meal there last night was an easy reminder as to why.
Started with a cocktail of the premium and hard-to-find Boyd & Blair vodka, then moved onto to a delicious plate of Italian meats & cheeses for an appetizer. For dinner, some really outstanding house made gnocchi in a light lamb bolognese sauce with an excellent glass of Sangiovese. Dessert was a slice of Marscapone-Limoncello cheesecake, that might be the best Ive ever had -- light and almost fluffy, it had a perfect bit of lemon taste. A well-prepared espresso helped it go down. After dinner, I went back to the bar for a Frenet-Branco cocktail, also something of a rarity.
Service was excellent from the waitstaff to the bartenders. The atmosphere is sophisticated yet very down-to-earth. A fantastic place to spend a special evening. Just love this place.

Bravo!
by Bobo15203 (7 reviews)
May 17, 2010 - Likes it
Yes it's very small. Yes it's tucked away on side streets of parked cars in the South Side...Yes, you should still eat here.
I love Dish and thats why I find myself at this establishment at least once a month. If not for dinner at least for a drink or late night bite. The great thing about Dish is that it knows what it is...no identity crisis here whatsoever.
If you make a reservation, your seat is almost always ready. If they are behind, a drink at the charming front bar makes everything ok. They have amazing martini's and a creative drink menu that might make you skip dinner! I have never waited for longer than 15 minutes when I had a reservation, because they don't overbook the space. Girasole and some other establishments could learn a thing or two from Dish.
The service is ALWAYS friendly, accomodating and unpretentious. As for the food, that too is always perfect. This place defines neighboorhood, family owned, authentic nuovo Italian. Bravo Dish!

Ups and Downs
by C.D.S. (8 reviews)
November 10, 2009 - Likes it
My girlfriend and I went to Dish for our anniversary. We enjoyed a few of their specialty cocktails, which were nicely balanced. Their wine selection is also extensive enough to satisfy all but the uber snob.
The scallop appetizer w/ white truffle oil was delicious and perfectly prepared, as was the carpaccio with parmesan chucks and lemon oil. The apps were a bit on the small side, but in my opinion that's great for two people.
We both had the same entree: Thick-cut pork chop stuffed with smoked mozzarella and prosciutto, served with polenta and julienned brussel sprouts.
This was the only part of the meal that disappointed. While the chop tasted great (the stuffing plus an au jus were delicious) but it was tough. And I mean, "sawing at it with my knife" tough.
The polenta was great and played the part of mashed potatoes well. The brussel sprouts were tasty too, and the julienning made for a unique twist.
For dessert, we had tiramisu, which I usually find a bit heavy due to the mascarpone. On the contrary, theirs is very light and delicate.
Overall, I would recommend it and I would go back. But next time, I think I would get fish/shellfish or a pasta dish for my entree.
