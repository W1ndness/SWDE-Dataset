Terrible Service
by Ligea (17 reviews)
August 01, 2010 - Doesn't like it
On a cold, wet night this past winter, my hubby and I were in the mood for the fabulous food at the Kitchen. We walked up to the restaurant and opened the door. The wait staff were all gathered in the back corner of the restaurant and ignored us. Finally after 5 minutes I guess one of them decided we weren't going to leave so she approached us and said "we don't open for 5 more minutes." 5 Minutes!? She couldn't sit us at a table and get us some water and just let us wait the 5 minutes inside? No, she expected us to wait outside. Really. I could not in a million years make this up. Needless to say, we haven't been back. There are too many great restaurants in Boulder to bother going to one where you are treated so rudely.

This would be my last meal on earth
by emily (8 reviews)
July 10, 2010 - Likes it
I love The Kitchen. I even love The Kitchen when it isn't great. I never leave disappointed. There is something completely satisfying about the place. It has dignity without pretension; everything about it exudes a love and respect for food. And, oh yeh, it's delicious. On the brunch menu, my favorite is the polenta with mushroom ragu. On the dinner menu, anything they do in the spring with peas or fava beans is fantastic. I have been transported by their scallops, their house-made pastas, and the pot au chocolat. And the sommolier is wonderful -- don't be afraid to ask, he loves wine and wants to share his expertise.

Good food but prices have skyrocketed!
by Kim Knickerbocker (1 review)
September 15, 2009 - Likes it
As someone that eats out multiple times per week, I was a die hard fan when the Kitchen first opened. But in the last year or so the prices have begun to far exceed the quality of the food. Not sure if it's just due to rent increases on Pearl, but it's no longer a "standby" for me, which is unfortunate. Not only that but the last time I was there the host tried to seat us next to another couple (that we didn't know) at a four top table. We actually had to say, "Uh, no, we don't really want to have an expensive dinner sitting with strangers" before we were moved to our own table. I'm all for community, but not for meals at nice places. Pretty disappointed with what the Kitchen has become.
