Best Pizza, but don't be in a hurry ;-)
by Kozmo (23 reviews)
October 06, 2008 - Likes it
They have the best Pizza in town! The place is dark inside and has not been remodeled in decades. if you grew up in the 70's like I did, this will take you back to the little old pizza places like my father used to take us too. it's dark inside and don't be in a hurry. a pizza can take about an hour to go. so go with some friends for some conversation and a beer while you wait. but the wait it wroth it. Some people think them unfriendly and rude but I kinda like that attitude, at least from an old skool pizza joint like this.

A Bad Taste.
by prufrock (5 reviews)
April 23, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I know that the Twin Cities holds Red's Savoy in high regard, but I think a lot of that comes from it having been here for so long. It is definitely a holdover from the 60s, if not earlier, and I am almost always on board with that kind of respect for longevity. I held off going there for decades--literally-- because my Minnesotan friends kept raving about how great the "thin crust" is. (I grew up in Chicago, and cheese and tomato sauce on a cracker was never a big deal to us. Not to be too snarky.)
So I finally hit the place last week, and it was interesting to park where Mr. Coleman Sr. had so much fun, and inside the vibe was kind of similar to a tavern in Cicero (in the 60s). We ordered a simple pizza, which is what you do in a place like this, and it was good enough, but why people get so excited about a thin crust is beyond me. But to each his own. It was served on a plastic cafeteria tray, which while convenient for all concerned still made me cringe a little. Service was OK considering the amount of traffic in there, and what was needed. The pizza itself was.... OK. But no better than Carbones or any other.
What sealed the deal for us was our last experience as we left. On the way out, we saw a poster ridiculing the President in a teabagger sort of way, in the entryway. Dyspepsia ensued. I'm all for everyone in the Nation having the freedom to have and express their beliefs freely, but I just wish I had known what I was going into beforehand, and I could have avoided the heartburn that I got from Savoy's Obama-hatin'.
