April 02, 2010 - Likes it
Holy FREE sangria buffet! Book a reservation for a Sunday, and make no post-meal plans beyond an extended siesta. Brunch is tasty, with a playful taco cart roaming the room, but dinner is where the chef shines (hands down, my favorite paella in the city). For both meals, enjoy a dangerous spread of 8 complimentary sangrias. Start at one end of the table and stagger your way down before passing out.

+ 2 more photos from Jeff mobile review
by Jeff (14 reviews)
February 26, 2009 - Really likes it
While eating at 'Columbia' in Tampa Bay may give you a slightly more authentic Cuban meal, that's probably not right for NYC. Calle Ocho does a great job of mixing traditional tastes with an urban feel. You never forget that you're in a hip, city restaurant (I saw a '30 Rock' TV star the night I as there) but that's probably what most of their customers want to feel like. I hate the term 'don't fill up on bread' because I feel that a restaurant should start you off with their best. 'The bread' should never be a chore, and Calle Ocho starts you off the right way with a bean-spread for their dense rolls. The match is perfect and that alone was better than entire meals I've had at other restaurants. Everything else I tried was flavored distinctly, but not so much as to mask the taste of the ingredients themselves. All 7 people in my party were very pleased with their meals.

Recommend | Not helpful
