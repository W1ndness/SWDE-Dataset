Very creative pizzas, admirable reuse of an old building, and smoke-free!
by Bryan (9 reviews)
April 30, 2009 - Likes it
Transfer makes one thing clear when you first pick up the menu: these pizzas are not -- nor do they attempt to be -- beholden to any traditions of Sicily or southern Italy. If you know that going in, you're sure to have a great taste experience. The combinations of ingredients are innovative, and you can always concoct your own. The building used to be the Transfer Drug Store (according to the preserved stained-glass windows), and I give the owner(s) props for injecting some needed commerce in a pretty dreary part of Milwaukee. I'm sure they could have easily opened up in a trendier location in Bay View or the East Side, but they've decided to do their part in revitalizing a decrepit corner of the South Side. This is one place no one should ever drive to, since several buses converge at the Milwaukee County Transit System (MCTS) garage across the street. They've also been running a special on Wednesdays, where you get a discount if you ride your bike there or show a transfer from an MCTS bus! Also, it's smoke-free, which is always welcome here in the ash tray of the Midwest.
