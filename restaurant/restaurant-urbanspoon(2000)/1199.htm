The Vine on Grand
by cristina s (8 reviews)
February 28, 2010 - Likes it
No you cannot see the Mediterranean shore from the Cafe' but you might as well pretend that you are in a balmy coastal town surrounded by white-washed houses and blooming vines! The food is authentic (to a fault! I don't know too many Americans who can stomach the yoghurt salad or the super-sweet fruit sodas!)... I used to buy at the deli's when the place was only a grocerie store and was delighted that it turned into a cafe'. The best hummus in town - no doubt about it (not overly garilcked as many seem to do!). The baba-ganoush is respectable. The grape leaves are very delicate and moist. The schwarma to die for! The felafel and kibbi are very tasty.
I miss the savory lentil salad they used to sell at the deli... (hint, hint...).
People are very friendly and the ambiance is intimate, and the prices are quite reasonable for the amount of food they serve.
All in all, a great little find!!!!

The Best Falafel in STL!
by Nowell Gata (1 review)
October 13, 2009
Read the title. It's only a glimmer of what you can find at this place. Yes, a falafel sandwich will run you just under $4. And, currently, it's served deli-style so it's a great place for an a-la-carte lunch, but the other offerings are amazing.
At any given time you can take home a good-sized portion of babaganoush, hummus, or (my favorite) tabbouleh along with some fresh pita. Yay garlic!
There are also some specialty items on the menu, like a fried chicken and Lebanese rice, stuffed peppers/zucchini/squash or another tasty treat: dolmas, which are stuffed grape leaves (vegetarian or meaty).
There's something for everyone here. And I can't wait until their cafe finally opens. According to the wonderful staff, the restaurant will have a falafel bar, a bakery (bread and sweets) and in the summer months, The Vine will offer hookah on their patio.
