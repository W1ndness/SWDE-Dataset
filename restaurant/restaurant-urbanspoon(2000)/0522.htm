If your Grama was a gourmet chef this would be like eating at Grama's!
by foodsnob (421 reviews)
January 11, 2010 - Really likes it
First, I tried several times and could not handle the wait at the old location so this was my first experience and it's at the new restaurant. Very homey eclectic diner. The fried potatoes are fried, damnit-and they are fried dark brown like they should be not Johnson County Beige!! But, this is not just diner food-it is more. A BLT with eggs and cheese and arugula and pesto mayo-AND not just any pesto mayo, this was more fresh pesto than mayo. Seriously one of the best sandwiches I've had in a long time-in fact-now I'm craving another one! I had a chocolate mint brownie also that I took with me and ate later-also outstanding. I can't wait to go back with my husband and I promise it won't be long. The only draw back is that they are busy and don't rush you so the wait may take awhile and the wait staff isn't a corporate trained staff so they are a little rough around the edges but they seem to all mean well and care about their clientele-they're nice people dog gone it. I'd say this is sort of like if Woodswether Cafe was married to Happy Gillis Cafe-Succotash would be their child.

+ 2 more photos from The Food Carpenter In A World Of Mundane Breakfast, Succotash Stands As An Exception
by The Food Carpenter (157 reviews)
February 09, 2010 - Likes it
Breakfast might be the most difficult meal to draw the masses in with, largely due to the difficulty of differentiating one's menu enough from the competitors' to justify travel time above and beyond that of going to the nearest local eatery. And that is what makes Succotash so special. Their menu is what I would consider 1/2 classic and 1/2 unique (and not the type of unique you would associate with abstract art that completely escapes you, but rather the type that hits you every time you see a new invention that's so obvious, you're mad for the next week that YOU didn't think of it sooner). Portions are large, the food is prepared with an obvious passion, and for some strange reason service has improved since the recent move to 2601 Holmes. So, if you find yourself feeling a little frisky in the morning (for food that is), and willing to go the distance, try Succotash and have yourself a "cake" & a smile (which is actually on the menu).

Lil bit country, Lil bit gourmet
by Meghamium (53 reviews)
August 13, 2010 - Likes it
First things first...a wildly popular breakfast place is going to be crazy busy on a Saturday. What do you expect? So either wake up earlier, go on another day, or suck it up!
Onto the place...we tried Succotash for the first time on a Friday morning. The place was pretty empty. Our waitress (who was waiting on 4 other tables) was very responsive and always there before we needed her. My coffee never got below a half cup before being refilled. The decor is interesting...vintage light fixtures and decorating elements with modern art make it a little hipster-deco. IMHO, the booths were fabulous (but quite low)...wish I could've taken a post-breakfast nap there. My only complaint about the entire place was the unappetizing painting above our booth of a woman eating/dripping out of her mouth what looks to be blood soup. Oh, and the buzzing fly that joined us at the end of our meal...but it was only one fly so I can deal.
Now onto the food! Hubby ordered the colossal blueberry pancakes. They were good, but I prefer my pancakes thicker and less chewy. Fresh OJ was very good. I had the smoked salmon benedict. It was delicious!! I loved the dill hollaindaise, smoked salmon, and capers, but my favorite part was the beer bread. It was one of the best breads I have ever had. Chewy, kind of sweet, and super delicious! The potatoes were wonderful too. They were crispy fried and soft at the same time. Mine came unseasoned so I had to add some S&P but that's fine with me. I didn't know what to eat first so by the time I got to my potatoes they were cold :(. Next time, I'll eat them first.

Worth a Second Chance
by j (1 review)
June 11, 2010
After reading many strong reviews, I made my first visit to Succotash. The wait was 30 minutes, as it seems to be quite a hot spot--in a sketchy part of town. Folks from all walks of life frequent the joint--Johnson County transplants, church goers, weirdos, freaks, cyclists, bikers, tattooed people, straights and gays. Everyone is welcome.
The fresh juice menu is interesting, and I regret not partaking. I will on the next visit. I ordered the traditional breafast...scrambled, wheat toast, potatoes, and, yes, BACON.
The potatoes are the best breafast potatoes I have had...even better than mine. It will force me to improve mine. Although, I made the most wonderful roasted, rosemary new potatoes two weeks back...cripsy on the outside; creamy on the inside.
My eggs were undercooked. I prefer my eggs on the dry side. I am not complaining that they were not dry; they were NOT fully cooked. The whites were a bit...well, clear...as in NOT COOKED. I scrambled my scrambled eggs to pretend that they were done, dowsed them in Cholula and closed my eyes with each bite. The toast was of quality bread...great bread...served with real butter...a truck load of butter. I scraped off over a tablespoon of butter and still had plenty. The coffee was decent. However, the waiter who thought he was trendy and cute, and who smelled as if he had not showered in a week, ignored the fact that my coffee cup was long empty.
The hostess was quite put together, the decor eclectic, and the ever-important bathroom was adequate--nothing gross in there--just felt it was in need of a good bleach scrubbin'.
Sooo, then...
The seating is below standard, as the bench seats along the walls are too cushiony and too low. This needs major attention.
I am willing to try Succotash again...and ask that the eggs be cooked on the dry side. I will order the fresh ginger pear juice next time.
Oh, and by the way, the flies are free.
