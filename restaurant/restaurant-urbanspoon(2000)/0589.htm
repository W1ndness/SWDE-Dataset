A Classic Dive To Love
by lexfoodie (10 reviews)
November 07, 2009 - Likes it
I'm not gonna lie, I love Tolly-Ho. I'm completely biased about this institution since being taken there as a child by my dad, now deceased, who worked for UK. I'll never forget my first time there, indulging in their cheese fries at lunch, it was tasty and I only wanted more.
I spent a lot of time their during my high school years, given it was one of the only "safe" places for people under 21 to go to at night in Lexington. At that time the place was rather kooky and scary, with dark corners and a few unseemly individuals that were regulars, smoking their cigarette butts and playing their video games in the corner next to the bathroom.
Over the years the place has gone through a make-over to say the least. Dark corners are now well lit, the bathroom is tiled-over, clean, and open, and the vibe seems more chill and boisterous (especially when the Ho Virgins are announced) than seedy. It feels like the ruckus has been either cleaned up or run off but what's left is still a solid restaurant and simply good, artery clogging food that can be bought 24/7. There's even a general store inside now called "The Ho Mart" where you can purchase t-shirts, cigarettes, aspirin, ect.
I just ate there about an hour ago (11-7) and it was still as good as I remember. I ordered the Ham and Cheese Omelet with mushrooms added along with a side of raisin toast and a pop. My friend ordered the Tolly-Ho Burger (just cheese and ketchup, he's picky, ha.) with a side of Bacon Cheese Fries and water. He really loved his food and I was surprised how fresh the omelet and its ingredients tasted, definitely no canned mushrooms here. The omelet was put together well and my toast was pretty good too. I stole some of those previously mentioned fries off my buddy's plate and they were quite tasty too. Forget Waffle House, stop in at the Ho anytime you need a good greasing over, it's worth it.
