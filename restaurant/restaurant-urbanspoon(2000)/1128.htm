Can You Hear Me Now?
by Benjamin (129 reviews)
January 02, 2010 - Doesn't like it
Damn, this place is loud on a weekend. Whoever designed the interior of this place needs to be hung up, beaten with an old tortilla and slopped with Rosario's roasted salsa!
Other than noise, they serve a mean bowl of soup and Robby, a longtime server at Rosarios, is one of the best waiters I've ever had. Just incredible (and funny). Ask for him by name if you're wanting the best experience.
I do think at times that Rosarios gets a little full of itself, knowing well that they're still horribly popular after all these years and proudly showing it. Their hostess staff can sometimes be a little too fancy for their own good and the bartenders sometime seem to stand around not doing much of anything during peak busy times.
Mostly, I recommend Rosarios TO GO. Unless you're able to sit and eat there mid-week, mid-afternoon, it's better food at home.

Solid food
by Joseph Coppola (44 reviews)
May 25, 2010 - Likes it
Rosario's provides an entertaining dining experience. As soon as you enter the restaurant especially at night, you are overwhelmed with all the happenings such as the salsa dancing, live band, the bar, or all the patrons at the restaurant. I've been several times. If you don't mind paying $15 for the grande patron margarita, get it. It tastes amazing and you will feel it. The shrimp nachos would make a perfect pairing as it has ample shrimp with great flavoring. The chie Relleno de Pescado was outstanding. My favorite was the sauce that accompanied it. I do enjoy the fish tacos here too although they are not the best in the city. The fajitas are equally tasty. There is a tendency for the flautas to have a burnt look to them which certainly doesn't taste good on the outside. The rice isn't as moist as I like but the beans are good. I love love the chips and salsa. Best in the city!
