Always something to eat.
by gourmetloveaffair (6 reviews)
June 12, 2008 - Really likes it
Situated on the border of Scottsdale and Phoenix, one can dress semi-casual or dressy and still feel in place.
The menu is varied and I can always find something to fit my mood. The menu starts with dessert… it is the White Chocolate grill you see… a laugh when eating there for the first time.
My favorite starter is the Tomato-Gin soup, they pour the gin in at the end after cooking for delicious flavor and smoothness, you actually have to be 21 to order it.
Another favorite I always get is the Filet Mignon Cobb Salad. It comes out on a huge plate with colorful rows of marinated mushrooms, eggs, smoked bacon, seasoned filet, Maytag blue cheese, halved cherry tomatoes - all served on fresh baby greens. It so huge I can hardly ever finish it. The fillet is cooked to medium rare (and that is hard to do in such tiny chunks) it melts in you mouth along with the rest of the ingredients.
My husbands favorite is the Prime Rib French-Dip which comes delicately spread with mayo or horseradish on a crusty bun that stands up well to the jus. Another delicious choice is the Maytag Blue Cheese Burger.
Dessert is the best. Their Apple Pie, served ddep dis style in an individual ramekin is superb, and easily feeds two. My favorite is the White Chocolate Brownie, made with Callebaut chocolate, it comes out warm (with the chips inside still melted) slathered with fudge and topped with a delicious scoop of cool French Vanilla ice cream. The contrasts of white and dark chocolate and warm and cool are amazing in the mouth.
I would easily go out of my way to eat here… if you ever happen to be in the Phoenix, Vegas, or Chicago areas, look it up and get ready for the best “American” food you’ve ever had.
