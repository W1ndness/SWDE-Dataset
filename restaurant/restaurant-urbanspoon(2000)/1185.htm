The Best Duck EVER!....
by Fatkid (57 reviews)
May 27, 2010 - Likes it
We went to Canlis during a little getaway weekend to Seattle and the restaurant was recommended by the concierge at the hotel. It was stupendous. It over looks the bay and sits up high on a cliff, so the views are great.
We ordered a 55 day dry aged duck which was roasted and then crisped, Peking-style, then glazed with an orange sauce. It was a play on a couple of classic preparations and it was the best duck I have ever eaten. They bring out the whole duck to your table and then take it back to the kitchen to break down. I found myself wondering where the legs went after I finished the breast that came out on my plate. It was absolutely wonderful.
I would also recommend the potatos au-gratin as they were very nice.

June 14, 2009 - Likes it
Canlis provides a unique experience for me and my wife. I love to go there for any occasion but especially Valentine's Day. No, it is not inexpensive. Yes, the food is excellent, and the service is at the top of the totem pole. I don't mind paying what they charge for that level of service and atmosphere. We have talked to the owners while dining several times, when they just wandered over to our table and started talking to us. We got the history of this place and it is fabulous how it has stayed under the same family ownership for so many years, and for at least two generations. The interior design is classy, what I feel harkens back to the deco style of architecture and design of the 50's and 60's. I love it. A Once in a Lifetime Meal
by Bob and Becky (1 review)
July 25, 2009
Becky and I celebrated 30 years of marriage at Canlis recently. She chose the restaurant after doing tons of research, including using Urban Spoon.
The setting, although tough to find, was amazing. We had a window seat looking out on a weekly sailboat "dance" out on Union Lake. They kindly held our table when we got caught in Seattle traffic.
The service, from the beginning, was spectacular. The wait staff was attentive but personable and not at all stuffy.
The prawn appetizer was excellent and we appreciated the suggestion of sopping up the buttery sauce with the delicious bread.
The salad choices were excellent with unique flavors and presentation.
We had the New York steak and the lamb chops and both were excellent. The arrival of our entrees was pretty slow, but our waiter kept us informed.
We shared a creme brulee which featured mango flavors. It was delicious. We were disappointed that the special Kona coffee was sold out already, but the drip coffee was excellent.
Yes, Canlis is expensive. But we felt the food and the experience (did I mention the wonderful pianist?) together was well worth it for our special event.
