Smooookey.....
by Barney (485 reviews)
August 29, 2008 - Likes it
Had an excuse to be in this part of town for lunch today and stopped in to check out all these great reviews for myself.
Typical little bbq shack/resto atmosphere, with the addition of some very nice folks behind the counter.  A little bench seating inside, and a little more covered seating outside.
Went a little out of my way to try lots of stuff: quarter rack of ribs and pulled pork combo with Brunswick stew and collard greens.  It appears that they had a number of specials not on the menu, such as some of their own smoked sausage, smoked bologna, brisket, and smoked salmon dip with the special sides being mac-n-cheese and collard greens.
The ribs were big and meaty pork spare ribs with a small smoke ring and a nice thin outside crust without a lot of sauce applied. They were above average.  The pulled pork showed some sign of a smoke ring, and a little bit of outside crust throughout, but was probably closer to the average side of bbq around town...just not too much flavor.  The stew had a nice spice level and was only unusual in the tomato-ey sweetness.... but still above average.  The collard greens were pretty straight forward without anything unusual.  Finally, pickle slices, a couple slices of white bread, and a little cup of sauce rounded out the meal.  The sauce, by the way, is a lightly spicy red sauce with a medium vinegar "direction".
As bbq goes around town, they are surely above average. As far as bbq dollar value goes, I put them pretty much in the center.

These guys are so good in so many ways.
by Steve Bryant (2 reviews)
January 02, 2009 - Likes it
I discovered Big Shanty back in the spring of 08 and became an instant 3 meal a week customer. Their more expected line up of killer ribs, bad ass brisket, pulled pork and choice chicken are everything a real-deal southern barbeque aficionado could hope for and their Brunswick Stew is the icing on the cake.
But Chic and the ladies offer so much more. Their smoked salmon, if served in Buckhead by a hip waiter with attitude could easily be sold for several times the few bucks it'll cost you on a Big Shanty platter. Chic's own home made sausage will haunt you, and I don't even usually eat sausage. The mix of sides is full of nice surprises and their home made balsamic dressing makes their salads worth the trip on their own.
OK, I'm almost done but I have to tell you about their catered meats. For Christmas I took home a smoked turkey, some sausage appetizers and a smoked beef tenderloin. The turkey, so moist and so smokey, the sausage always awesome, but the beef tenderloin was insane. Perfectly medium, perfectly smoked and totally off the charts. I had just eaten at Chops for my anniversary a few nights earlier and my $350 filet mignon meal paled in comparison to Big Shanty's foiled-wrapped masterpiece using the same cut of meat. (For a tiny fraction of the price, of course.)
The bottom line? These guys are good. Exceptional food, super clean (I know that's weird for a BBQ joint), a quaint and authentic BBQ vibe, and good people to boot. Go, eat, enjoy!

Finally good BBQ in Atlanta
by dkjatl (4 reviews)
August 05, 2010 - Likes it
I've eaten BBQ in every state from Virginia southward and west to Texas. I love the almost endless regional variations in cooking styles and sauces. But in Georgia, good BBQ seems to be the domain of the southern two-thirds of the state. Metro Atlanta is almost entirely devoid of good BBQ.
Big Shanty is a delightful exception. It's chopped pork is excellent, with just the right amount of hickory and oak smoke and spices. The meat is moist without being wet and it is mixed with a good proportion of outer and inner meat. The sauce, however, leave much to be admired. It's more like bottled sauce one can buy at the supermarket. Why would they put such care into the pork and then serve it with anything less than a superlative sauce? And Texas Pete hot sauce on the tables? Tsk. Tsk. The beef brisket - a rarity east of Texas and even more rare to find cooked properly - is also excellent. It's tender with just the right amount of crust on it. Now if they'd only learn that it should be mesquite smoked... The sides are so-so, but then they are in most BBQ restaurants. Big Shanty focuses on the BBQ, which is what most people go for anyway.
Is Big Shanty the Best BBQ in Georgia? Most assuredly not. But it is the best I've found north of I-20 so far.
