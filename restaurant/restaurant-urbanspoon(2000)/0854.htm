Excellent food, but beware of the service.
by rianacooper (12 reviews)
June 08, 2010 - Likes it
I have previously written a glowing review for this cafe, but I have since eaten here twice, and feel like I need to comment on my latest experiences. My husband and I ate here several months ago, and that experience was less than stellar. First off, we were waited on by this man who I felt had a very snobby attitude and gave me the distinct impression that he did not enjoy his job at all. He was extremely slow and inattentive to his customers. I felt like he ignored us for the majority of our visit there. At this particular visit, I ordered the softshell crab po'boy, which this waiter had called a "good choice". I was picturing the filling of my sandwich to be like tunafish or egg salad (ie. the meat cut up and mixed with the dressing), however, when I got the sandwhich I was less than pleased to see that the soft shell crab was indeed intact ( I guess some people would be totally ok with this). While the bread and sauce tasted good, I just could not get over the texture of the crab, so I could barely eat my sandwhich (I guess I should stay away from softshell crab). Despite of the poor service and my dislike of my previous meal here, my husband and I brought my parents here several weekends later. This time around we were lucky enough to have the tall slender lady as our waitress (and she is excellent). My dad ended up ordering that crab sandwhich and devoured it mercilessly. I was also pleased with my meal this time around and the cake is still divine. So overall, the food here is pretty tasty, but beware of the service. (Note: I've had a chicken sandwhich here before and thought it was great).

Delicious!
by rianacooper (12 reviews)
March 27, 2010 - Likes it
I have really been wanting to try this place for a long time, but unfortuntely their hours make it difficult (they are only open from M-Sat from 11AM-2:30PM). Well, I finally got to eat there today and I was very impressed by how delicious their food is. I ordered the Calfiornia chicken sandwich and my fiance ordered the Spi-cee chicken sandwich,both of which were very yummy. The sandwiches are made with freshly baked bread and come with homemade potato chips. To top it if we shared a slice of the Ribbon Cake topped off with whipped cream and fresh strawberries. Words can't describe how truly divine this cake tasted! I will definitely come back here again the next time I get a chance. If you've never been there, you should beware that the atmosphere is kind of an eclectic girly-girl type with colorful beaded chandeliers hanging everywhere. I wish they had longer hours for their cafe, maybe even being open for breakfast. I wasn't too impressed by their little gift shop/bakery, which is in a separate building from their cafe.
