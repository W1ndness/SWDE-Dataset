by Troy (211 reviews)
February 14, 2010 - Likes it
If you are looking for a fine dining experience, that is not what you will find at Los Chiludos. What you will find, is a "Mom & Pop" type of Mexican fast food restaurant in a remodeled classic building that feels comfortable, not stuffy, similar to what you might find at a Chipotle or Qdoba, but with a bigger menu and more authentic choices. Yes, the food may be served on disposable plates, cups and silverware, but that has nothing to do with the flavor and quality. Admittedly, this isn't my most favorite place for Mexican food (I don't like eating with plastic silverware and drinking from Styrofoam cups either), but I will say that what I've eaten here on the few occasions I've visited, the food was good. By the way, if you want quality chips, salsa and guacamole, go ahead and buy them, you won't be disappointed.

Mexican just as I remembered
by fu-dee (1 review)
March 18, 2010 - Likes it
I grew up in a small PA town that had one little Mexican restaurant in the middle of nowhere. BUT, it was owned by a family of Mexicans, AND it was operated right out of their home. You walked into the family's house and their living/dining room was converted into the restaurant's dining area. It was...uh...unique. But the food was unquestionably authentic and scrumptious. Not at all like you would find at your Americanized Mexican chains. The only other place I've had Mexican as delicious as that family-owned diner was when I was in TX.
Pittsburgh, on the other hand, is an unlikely place to find really good, authentic Mexican food, but then you find a small gem like Los Chiludos. LC reminds me of the very same deal as that hometown restaurant.
It's an old fire station (?) converted into a small restaurant. There's only a few parking spaces and the dining area is limited in size. There's nothing fancy about this industrial, warehouse-like atmosphere (and I'm not talking 'hip' industrial, like so many restaurant chains are mimicking these days). But, the food...Ahh..the food!...is unquestionably authentic and delicious, and there's a reason. The owner - who was seen doing all of the cooking in the couple of times I've visited - grew up in Mexico and these are his grandma's tried and tested recipes. This makes sense, since I literally wanted to lick the plate when I was finished with my Ahi Tuna Tacos, with mango salsa. (I am still thinking about those tacos and it's been several days later).
It's also good to note that as of this writing, the restaurant is closed on Sundays. I highly recommend this place.
