Fine Dining and Entertainment
by Jennifer (23 reviews)
January 11, 2009 - Likes it
The coolest time to go to Vicente's is on a Friday at about 8pm. Everything looks just like a high quality restaurant. Stick around for a few hours, and the room seamlessly transitions into a hot Cuban club, complete with live music and dancing. Every time I've been here the place is packed. Food is very traditional and delicious, as if someone's loving Grandma is running the kitchen. Sangrias are reasonable and hit the spot!

Delicious!
by denverguy81 (1 review)
January 06, 2010
Buzzaa is incorrect about a few things in their review. The mojitos are not $15. They are $8. They are also pretty damn good, too! Made from scratch, no premade mix.
Vicentes does, in fact, have black beans and rice... It's just called moro rice and isn't listed on the menu.
Prices are reasonable for the quality of food and the amount you receive. Entrees during dinner range around $17-$21, the max being $26 for the filet mignon. If this is a little too high for you, lunch prices range from $8-$12.
The service is friendly and personable. I know many people who are regulars of the restaurant not only because of the food but also because of the waitstaff.
