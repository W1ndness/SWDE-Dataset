Message from Fenouil

Contemporary American cuisine influenced by European techniques in an elegant setting make Fenouil Portland's premiere dining d

  • Happy Hour,7 nights a week 4-6pm
  • Monthly Winemaker Dinners
  • Lunch Opening May 24th, 11:30-2pm

Fenouil's 'living' menu changes with the seasons, from wild Chinook salmon in the spring to Nantucket Bay scallops in the winter. Executive Chef Jake Martin strives to use the best ingredients and let the flavors compliment and enhance one another. Complete your meal with continuously changing, seasonal dessert selections.

With Portland's city lights as a backdrop, Fenouil's dining room and bar mix a high-energy vibe with European romance for a seductive and urban decor. Vaulted tile ceilings and limestone-clad columns blend seamlessly with roll-up garage doors, expansive windows, cozy banquettes, two fireplaces and an open kitchen.
Locals mingle in a more casual setting in the bar which features European and local wines and cocktails. The Bistrot menu includes Tete de Cochon, Rabbit Vol au Vent, and artisanal cheeses and charcuterie.

by Lou (2 reviews)
June 01, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I took my wife and in-laws to Fenouil for dinner to celebrate Mother's day. The food was OK. I was disappointed to find out that they had hired a new head chef (according to the maitre d') and they no longer served the fantastic Paella that they used to have on the menu. They don't do it off menu either. So I ordered the closest thing on the current menu (Coquilles Poêlèe) which was nothing to write home about. There were three (yes, only 3) seared scallops for $29 and some undercooked squid and white beans. It was good but not nearly enough food for $29. My father in law ordered the soft shell crab special which too was undersized. Further the massive plate on which it was served made the crab look tiny.
Needless to say, I won't be going back. Our total bill for four people was $210 and two of us left still hungry. There are other restaurants in Portland with much better value for dollar. For example, the Tabla three course meal with wine pairings can be had for roughly the same money and no one will be hungry afterward.
