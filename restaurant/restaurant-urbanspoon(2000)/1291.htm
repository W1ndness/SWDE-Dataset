Romantic, lovely... and for us, unfortunately, once a year.
by Jean France Erickson (10 reviews)
November 01, 2009 - Really likes it
Polo Grill has been our restaurant of choice for anniversary dinner several years in a row. They serve a multi-course meal in an elegant setting - low light and candles, leather booths, thick real tablecloths/napkins and nice heavy flatware. High quality, attentive service. Extensive wine list... going way beyond the cliche wines to include some more unique ones like Malbecs and Temperanillos. Sometimes the chef sends out an "amuse-bouche" - a little taste of something to "amuse our mouths" while we wait. This is a special treat. We LOVE their steak-- don't know what they do to it, but it tastes wonderful, every time. I could go on and on. We usually end the meal with a creme brulee... which, to their credit, they don't mess up with strange additions like other restaurants. The meal for two -- wine, entrees, appetizers and desserts, including tip, will run $175-$250, depending on wine choice.
