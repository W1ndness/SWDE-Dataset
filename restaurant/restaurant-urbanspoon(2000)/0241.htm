Sub-par staff, mediocre food...
by Pam (1 review)
June 14, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I was DYING, literally DYING to try Melt since it first opened, but never got the opportunity...and now that I finally did, I don't see what all the hub bub is. First of all, I visited the East Side establishment on Cedar and South Taylor, in Cleveland Heights, Ohio. The appearance was clean and well kept, however the kitschy decor of "light up Christmas ornaments" --seriously!-- was very redundant. I was literally staring at a superfluous amount of light up Easter bunny lawn decorations for the majority of my dining experience. I think the decor is very tacky and cheesy, and would have expected something more "hip" or "trendy" based on the popularity of Melt. The entree I ordered off the menu under the subheading "Gourmet Grilled Cheese" was the "Smoky Russian", which the menu states "smoked turkey, fresh napa vodka kraut, smoked Gouda, Russian dressing". All grilled cheese entrees include "sweet slaw" and "hand-cut fries". When my order (finally) arrived it contained all that was mentioned on the menu, however the "sweet slaw" looked like garbage- nothing but shredded carrots and cabbage out of the bag, with an overabundance of black pepper liberally sprinkled on it. There was no oil, or mayonnaise that one would usually find in "slaw" to hold it all together. Very disappointing in my opinion..now the sandwich, which sounded very appealing on the menu, but did not look like any "grilled cheese" I ever had. When I envision grilled cheese, I conjure up images of gooey, gloopy cheese dripping down the sides, as shown in the menu. My sandwich was literally light golden brown, and the bread had a slightly yellowed color from the butter that was not browned. My sandwich was below par and appeared hastily concocted in my opinion, as the "napa vodka kraut" tasted like bland cabbage, and in my opinion, they were very stingy with this topping. The russian dressing was the only thing that probably made this dish semi-palatable, which I used to dunk this sandwich with to make it more desirable. Now some of you will say if you want the "gooey, gloopy cheese" to order The Kindergarten off the menu, but I have made a better grilled cheese with various ingredients and it came out 10 times better!
As most of the reviews mention, the wait here is ridiculous- 20 minutes to be seated, over and hour for food to arrive...plus, I ordered a cup of soup which the waitress seemed to have forgotten to bring to me (but she did not forget to add it on my bill). The wait staff is not very nice, and the waitress was unapologetic when we asked her what the hold up was (supposedly, a call in order for 25 sandwiches....one thing I did not understand, was at the food counter there or 4 or 5 employees standing there waiting for the dishes to come up off the grill...is this really necessary? Couldn't you possibly utilize these people to assist with cooking or prepping? My overall opinion of this establishment: I really wanted to like this restaurant, I heard so many reviews and though this type of place would be "right up my alley". But I cannot tell a lie, and in my opinion I would have given less of a tip had the gratuity not been included (I had a party of 6). I may try this restaurant in a few years, as hopefully the "hype" will die down. At this point, I give it 2 out of 5 stars. Hands down. The fact the restaurant uses locally produced dairy and vegetables gives it an extra point in my opinion.

Melt Bar & Grilled
by Carl Ricco (2 reviews)
January 31, 2010
So much hype for this little place! It's fantastic though and definately lives up to blogs.
I always end up going on a Friday or Saturday evening, probably the worst time to visit a hip eatery in Lakewood. You put your name in at the crowded Host/Hostess table, and proceed to wait and wait and wait.. Oh and wait some more. Here's a helpful hint. Exit Melt towards Detroit, hang a right, and walk a block or two down to Pheonix Coffee and have a mocha.
You might be lucky and grab a bar seat though, and when that happens the night/day/morning gets magical. Grab a beer from their immense selection, and peruse the menu. The appetizers are good but you won't really need to eat them - since you'll be stuffed after trying to eat the entree plate. Check out the grilled cheese! You'll see the delicious Parmageddon (Perogies, Bread, Cheese) The Big Popper (Jalapenos, Bread, Cheese - then beer battered, then deep fried), and my personal favorite, the Chorizo and Potato(Chorizo, Potato, Bread,, and Cheese!).
The wait staff are great fun! Never had a bad experience at Melt, except for the wait - but it's not so horrible.
