A little gem on Dale Mabry.....
by Gulf Coast Guy (820 reviews)
December 13, 2009 - Likes it
So, we went to Vizcaya last night, hidden on Dale Mabry to the north of Linebaugh. An unassuming building from the outside blending in with it's surroundings.
Once inside, it is a transformation into a little out of the way eatery down some lane in a small town in coastal Spain. The hustle and bustle is just enough to allow you to soak it in but not too much din to disturb a nice evening. One could even call it romantic although this depends on when you dine and the mix of people in the place at the time.
This trip noticed at least two ro three tables gabbing away on their cell phones at their tables. Shame shame. Take it outside folks if you are that important.
Back to the meal however.
We ordered four plates this evening along with a salad which seemed to be a big deal since they only come with entrees it seems. However, our waiter told me he could get me one as a side.
Cold cuts plate came with one cheese and three meats, Salmon and Scallop Ceviche was nicely presented and had popcorn garnish and corn nuts mixed in for some crunch. Jumbo shrimp with lump crab was tasty in a tomato based sauce worthy of more bread sopping, although there were no noticeable lumps in the crabmeat portion. Our fave was the Merluzita Rellana, a fish cake with a nice savory, homecooked, comfort food taste. All I can say is order it and be prepared to dip your bread which is served in a basket with butter pats. (Need to serve with olive oil IMO)
Also, ordered a half pitcher of the red sangria and as usual wished we ordered a whole one. Goes down smooth and easy and has the right blend of fruit and wine taste. First glass is poured over fruit juices in the glass.
We were pleasingly full after our meal and although the salad never showed up it was not needed. Not on the check and no explanation offered or asked for as to why it was not served.
Service was a bit slack on this evening with little check back after we got all our plates, May have cost them one more plate and maybe another half pitcher of sangria.
Comparing this to Ceviche, our other fave Tapas stop I would say this is a bit more laid back and less variety. and of course the place is much more intimate due to it's size.
A couple of the tables here are really too close to the ones next to it and if you sat in them you could eat off your neighbors plates with no problem. ;0)
This trip on the 4 seashell scale........
Food 3.25
Service 2.25
Atmosphere 3.0
Overall 2.75
Next trip I want to try the entrees and maybe go with the Paella.

Great Experience - Reserved Elegance
by Kathy Spaulding (1 review)
January 27, 2009
Our daughter gave my wife and I a Christmas gift certificate for "Vizcaya Restaurant and Tapas". We invited a friend from upstate New York and made a reservation for 7:00 PM Monday evening ... no problem. Our situation changed and we modified our reservation to 8:00 PM the same evening. Again, no problem. We arrived, were promptly seated and our attentive server explained the "tapas tradition" and gently guided us through various dining and selection options. We asked our server for a Spanish wine recommendation. His guidance complemented our tapas selections and was within our expected price range,(approx. $30/bottle). We ordered (4) cold tapas assortments, followed by (4) additional hot tapas selections. Everything was equal to, or above, our expectations. The service was spectacular, attentive but non-invasive. The food was well prepared and presented in a timely/dining manner. We will return to this friendly, efficient, and delightful, tapas restaurant.

Well done Chef!
by Kris (1 review)
May 16, 2010 - Likes it
I attended the Tampa Bay Food and Wine Festival in St Pete Beach this past weekend and I watched a few of the cooking demonstrations performed on stage. Some were better than others but the chef that really got my attention was Chef Felix. I've heard very good things about his Restaurant in the past but never had the opportunity to try it or meet him. His presentation was on olive oil and as simple as is seems I learned a great deal. For example, he explaine the differences between the types of olive oil and where and when to usem effectively in recipes. He went on to explain all the different grades of olive oil from the different presses and processes in obtaining it. He also made a demonstration on cooking Paella . The paella pan was as big as a boat! At least 6 feet across. It tasted Amazing !! Again it was great to listen to him , the detail and the passion he transmits with his knowledge on food and that European accent !
So the next day me and my husband decided to drive to Tampa to pay him a visit to his restaurant. Everything about this place tells you about Felix. The place it's intimate, about 60 seats, very cozy and colorful. Very attentive staff from the moment you walk in .The menu is very extensive - ceviches,seafood,quail, chorizo ,sweetbreads etc etc, an infinite selection of ingredients and tapas. They also have a menu of entree choices that changes seasonally . The feeling is of that carefully prepared food from scratch with great creativity. We love chef driven restaurants and this is a real gem. We also had his Chilean sea bass, I do not want to bore you : it was just the best I had ever tried, we are foodies that eat around the world!!. Bart the manager , very knowledgeable about food and wine , made great accommodations and recommendations. All his service staff was well synchronized and very courteous ! and of course Chef Felix came to say hi !
I wish Tampa will have more chef driven restaurants like this one !
