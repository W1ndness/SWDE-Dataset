Message from Cafe Fiore Restaurant

Traditional rustic Italian cooking with a southern flair

  • Martini Lounge
  • Outdoor Patio
  • Jazz and Live Music

Located in the heart of downtown Ventura, Cafe Fiore Restaurant and Martini Lounge is an understated elegant spot for traditional rustic Italian cooking with a southern flair. Comfortable loose pillow boothes, overstuffed couches and a warm fireplace in our Martini Lounge is a great spot to sip fun, flavorful martinis and listen to the sounds of hip Jazz trios. Listen to the blue Pacific while eating a peaceful meal on our outdoor patio. Casual lounge chairs decorate our patio's relaxing evening out from simple appetizers to a complete dinner.

Great Restaurant Turned Bad
by Jamie (1 review)
April 05, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I used to love Cafe Fiore. The atmosphere is so beautiful, the food was delicious, and the service was top notch, however on my most recent experience I would have to say that the latter two have gone downhill significantly. The food was sub par and terribly overpriced and the service was so slow and quite unattentive in general.
The wine list by the glass was somewhat lacking, especially for an Italian restaurant. They didn't even offer a blend by the glass. So I ordered a wine on the server (Suzanne's) recommendation and I checked the menu to see the price, and when we got our bill she charged me $2.50 more per glass. When I brought it to her attention she brought me a completely different menu to show me what I ordered and not only was it a different menu, the way they had it labeled was different. It isn't even about the money, it is just the principle of it.
The food was just okay honestly. Not at all worth the price. I've paid less than half that amount for Penne Arrabiata in the past and it was heavenly. This was just noodles in sauce really. Might as well have been called ragu. If it weren't for the pound of parmesan cheese I had them add to the sauce it would have been terrible.
Our server, Suzanne, was so unfriendly and as a former server who takes care of her own, I was really disappointed at the lack of attention and willingness to listen. She lost more than the slight difference on the wine in her tip, needless to say, and I am always a 20% + tipper. The atmosphere is the only redeeming quality of this establishment, so if ever I am in the mood for an overpriced glass of wine in a beautiful environment, I may go back, but other than that, I'll pass.

Recommend | Not helpful


+ 2 more photos from S. Phillips Love it!
by S. Phillips (2 reviews)
December 28, 2008 - Really likes it
A warm & stylish decor that's always comfortable to be in.  Most every seat in the dining room is a big plush/comfy booth.  But I think the biggest reason you see this place jam packed and enduring so strongly can be boiled down to how it serves every different type of diner, with $10 ranged Pastas & Pizzas to $20-$25 entrees & specials.  My top 3 dream dinners here are: Braised Lamb Shank with Gorgonzola Gnocchi; New York steak drizzled in balsamic glaze with Butternut Squash mashed pots & Gorgonzola sauce; & the Seared Ahi Tuna rolled in sesames, topped with balsamic glaze & over Grilled veggies.  Love the Pear/Gorgonzola/Candied-Walnut Salad or the Burata Salad.  Love the Bread Pudding Dessert.  And of course the fresh Mixed Berry Mojito is the most amazing cocktail ever!  Not a lot of places do good mojitos, but this is a can't miss mojito.

Absolutely Brilliant!!!!!
by Athena (2 reviews)
September 13, 2009 - Likes it
This is by far my favorite restaurant and I am very picky. I want things just so and they are happy to provide.
The staff is amazing. Completely attentive and quick to service. They are accommodating and make it a point to see to your comfort and needs. They don't raise the bar when it comes to their customer service, they are the bar, their capability to handle a tremendous number of people and have a genuine hospitable attitude toward every customer they accommodate.
The General Manager is a sweet and kind woman who is dedicated to assuring a perfect experience for every customer.
The owners are thorough with the menu design and the atmosphere of the restaurant. They have kept the feel of comfort and elegance.
The sous chefs and other support staff are very talented, turning out food that is not only beautiful but delicious as well. They are creative all on their own, sometimes creating the soups or salads that are a delight. They are a big part of why the restaurant functions on the level that it does.
Ken the Executive Head Chef is marvelous at creating new and innovative dishes to that continually evolve and dance in diversity. His culinary skills are so widely ranged that he is conformable creating a barbecue sauce for barbecue pork sliders, (which are amazing), to a stunning New York steak with a truffle risotto fried arugala and grana padano.
My only suggestion is to not go on Tuesday and Wednesday, one those two days there is fully different crew in the kitchen that has a different style of cooking that does not reflect the same essence I have come to know from Cafe Fiore.
I have never tried the martinis, not my thing but I understand that they are lovely.Overall this is a wonderful restaurant that is a can't miss. Have a great dinner.
