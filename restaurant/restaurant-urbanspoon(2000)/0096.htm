Simple and Excellent.
by Dining Baton Rouge (5 reviews)
March 20, 2010 - Likes it
Definitely better than Acme for cheaper and better quality seafood with an also casual atmosphere. Different from Mike Anderson's and Lagniappe which are also great.
Out of all oyster bars, this one is my favorite. Definitely the best oysters in town. Smaller gulf oysters from Houma for the raw, and giant Wine Island oysters for the chargrilled. Chargrilled has always been perfectly cooked with just the right amount of their cheese/seasoning blend.
From here I also recommend the Andouille encrusted fresh fish. Fresh fish ponchartrain. The barbecued drum (PERFECTLY spiced) and the white chocolate bread pudding.
it's a fun time and beer is very fresh on draft.

Boring, Boring, Boring
by Bluegoose72 (9 reviews)
May 11, 2009 - Doesn't like it
I have eaten at Parrain's several times and without fail, I am underwhelmed, bored and generally dismayed that I've spent money to have such an unforgettable meal and dining experience.
My last visit to Parrain's was with someone who lives out of the state and who had heard good things about it. Being an accommodating host, I drove them right over, despite my misgivings.
When our food arrived, everything was topped in the same pinkish-brown sauce with varying degrees of crawfish/shrimp/crab spattered on top.
I've never had anything TERRIBLE there, but I've never once had anything worth the $$ they charge for mediocre food, a strange, disjointed dining space, and spotty service.
