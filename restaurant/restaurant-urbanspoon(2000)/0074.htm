High Hopes; Mostly Shattered
by jjandb1234 (2 reviews)
May 22, 2008
Based on the cute exterior of the restaurant, I had high hopes for a new local spot on Harford Road. But Clementine disappointed on several levels.
First, I was not greeted at the door and there was no indication that I should seat myself. After looking like a fool for a few minutes, I went to the back and asked if I should seat myself. I found a spot near the wall and was pleased that so many kids and families were eating at the place. This means the owners aren't uptight and can handle any age.
The second disappointment involved portion size. I was still hungry after my sandwich and had to order a soup (which arrived with no crackers or bread and the bowl wasn't even filled to the top!). The bread for the sandwich was mediocre (think Safeway bagged rolls) and I wasn't served the "multigrain" bread as was noted on the menu for my particular sandwich. They ran out, but didn't bother to tell me until I asked. A nice woman switched the kaiser white bread to wheat for me. Maybe there is some hope for the staff crew.
The third disappointment was the blaring music and mostly cold service from my primary waitress. Considering that the restaurant just opened, I thought she would be eager to go over the restaurant's specials or explain some of the dishes.
If you have children, I'd recommend this place. I'd also search for the menu for something more hearty or don't go if you are hungry. Unless the owners increase portions, express a friendlier demeanor, and communicate better with their customers, Clementine won't last.
I'll have to stick with my trusty Giovanna's up the road.

Great Food - Very Family Friendly
by Maryland Foodies (2 reviews)
July 28, 2010 - Likes it
Really creative menu. Definitely "fusion" conceived. Tuesday nights is "Taco Tuesdays" where the taco platter is just $10 per person for creative platters such as Chile & Lime spiced Beef, or Orange & Cumin spiced chicken with amazing black beans and a hot sauce which is out of this world. The mexican longnecks are also only $1 and the margaritas are homemade and the real-deal. The kids area is great if you have young ones in tow. It's not supervised for infants / toddlers, but it's perfect for the 4-12 crowd. They still allow BYOB, but they now have a full ABC license with a mixed drink menu that could rival some of the best mixologist around. The have aperitifs and digestifs in-house that we have only read about before and never actually seen. It can be a little bit of a drive if you're south of B-more and the neighborhood may look questionable to some, but the food is absolutely worth the effort. It's easy to see that these guys will be around for a long time to come if they keep to what they're doing now!

Our Favorite Lunch Place
by Meg (3 reviews)
March 13, 2009 - Likes it
Clementine is locally owned and is a convenient 3 blocks away from our house. It's also en route to the library. These are fringe benefits though; Clementine would be our favorite place to eat lunch even if we had to drive far out to the suburbs to get there.
The atmosphere is delightful. I love a place that can be kid-friendly without being a zoo, and Clementine strikes a perfect note. My nearly 3 yr. old bounces from our table to the small but well-stocked kids' corner throughout the meal and never gets restless. The kids' corner has an easel with crayons and paper, numerous books, some large plastic dinosaurs, and other miscellaneous fun stuff to keep the small fry occupied. The seating is very comfortable and attractive, and there is just enough and not too much art to look at while you wait. I especially like the huge neighborhood map on one wall.
The service has always been friendly, attentive, and quick. Many of the staff greet my kids by name, and they always put the mac 'n' cheese on first thing -- a very sensible idea, and one I greatly appreciate. They were friendly about bringing multiple plates so that 3 children could share 2 orders, a courtesy we no longer require since all 3 are now members of the clean plate club. Drink refills are pretty quick too -- I love this!
Now for the food. The aforementioned macaroni and cheese is the best. I can't say it any plainer than that. It's rich, but not overwhelmingly so, and gussied up just the tiniest bit with tomato, scallions, and perhaps something else I can't put my finger on. The kids love it and so do I. The Moroccan Carrot Salad is quite tasty, and I usually order it with my sandwich. The potato salad is also quite good, and I sometimes go for that. The salmon cakes also are the best of their kind I've ever had and I don't bother making them myself anymore. I think the chicken sandwich is probably my favorite though; the tomato jam, avocado, and blue cheese really make it special, and the proportions are perfect. I've had various soups and specials, as well as the charcuterie, and can vouch for them as well. I've never had anything that wasn't at least very good, and lots of what I've had has been spectacular. Oh, yeah, don't miss the Basil Lime Elixer! So refreshing!
