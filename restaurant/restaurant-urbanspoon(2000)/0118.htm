Fantastic pizza!
by Charlie (179 reviews)
January 12, 2010 - Likes it
Delicious, crispy, European-stye crust, combined with bright, straight-out-of-the-ground produce and a unique vision for the BHM pizza scene make Za Za Trattoria a new favorite. Two slices of pie + salad = $7 at lunchtime. Can't beat the quality for the money, and the quantity was just enough to leave me satisfied but not enough to make be over-full.
I had a slice of the David and a slice of the Sausage, both top notch. The David was very interesting and surprisingly good. I'm generally weary of any pizza base other than tomato or barbeque sauce, but the sweet-potato puree topped with spinach and bacon worked out more than nicely. The Sausage was excellent, too, and I loved the big chunks of fresh tomatoes baked in. Even though pizza isn't the most healthy option of all, with all the quality veggies and such I really felt like I was eating fairly well. Nice salad dressing, too!

imagine you're in Rome
by carolinagurl (5 reviews)
October 07, 2009 - Really likes it
Based on the reviews on this site, my husband and I went to Za Za's for lunch in September. Though we have been in Bhm area for 13 years, we have only been in Northside once or twice. We went in and ordered 3 slices of pizza and the pasta of the day to share. This restaurant has indoor and outdoor seating and is the perfect place for a Fall day. They usually offer 3 types of pizza per day, one pasta, and two desserts. The cashier said they will make you whatever pasta and sauce you want if they have the ingredients.
The pizza was authentic Italian style, made on a long rectangle baking sheet, NOT ROUND, and cut into generous squares. The pasta of the day was chicken and broccoli in alfredo, not one of my favs but had wonderful flavor. I went in expecting a different pasta with arugula and pinenuts but then I realized this menu changes weekly so call ahead if you have seen a menu posted on this site and have your tastebuds set on it.
Watching actual cabs, and people bustling in and out of all the area restaurants and businesses gave us the feeling of sitting in a smaller town outside of Rome so Za Za was a great mind escape. For some reason, it also had a San Francisco feel (possibly the homeless people).
The service was perfect and the men running the place are very enthusiastic. Like the previous reviewer, this is the only place that has real Italian tasting and looking espresso and the price is right. (Continental is hit and miss on the chocolate tasting froth.) We had the chocolate tort for dessert and there are no words.
My husband and I have often fantasized about opening our own restaurant. If we ever did, it would be exactly like Za Za, offering one pasta of the day, one panino, and some pizza. I hope Za Za resists serving chicken fingers like everyone else in this town and keeps offering creative pasta and sauce combinations to continue expanding the taste bud octaves of Birmingham.
