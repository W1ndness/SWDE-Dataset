A real bar
by skippyjon (8 reviews)
July 28, 2010 - Likes it
DH let in on the secret of Dressels by the Jesuits years ago. We met up there before a concert in June. The gentleman manning the bar was WONDERFUL. He knew all about the beer, the menu, and was definitely very well read and educated about all of the authors depicted in the portraiture on the walls. He suggested I try a hard cider for the hot day and it was PERFECT. Pasty was also good. Chips a given there with malt vinegar.

A Mean Guinness Pour and Chips.
by primo (16 reviews)
November 25, 2009 - Likes it
Dressels is a thinking man's pub. I see the various academics chatting fireside. It's a superb winter pub and the food is above the norm of pub fare. Excellent chips and rarebit. Nice vege burger (off the menu) along with a wide range of salads and sandies (me hubby likes the French dip). Pull a seat up at the bar and wait for your beer (you will wait a while, service takes second seat here) but the warm glow of the place and the poet's photos which dot the walls project a nice, chill vibe.

Where's the sandwich?
by Foodfan (2 reviews)
January 18, 2009
I am a big Dressels fan. Love the chips. Love the rarebit. Love the drinks. But recently I ordered a "gourmet" grilled cheese and was left wondering where the rest was? It was the most pathetic sandwich on white bread with two tiny pieces of cheese and one slice of tomato. This was presented on a plate with absolutely nothing else. And...brace yourself...was $8!! Are you kidding me?
