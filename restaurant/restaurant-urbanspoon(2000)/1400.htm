Fools Gold
by southbound (1 review)
June 30, 2010 - Doesn't like it
If you are mining for something good then choose another vein. After ordering food for delivery, it came in the time frame that they indicated but came incomplete. After opening my dinner, I realized that they totally left out a couple of items. The ribs tasted as if they had been warmed over from the day before. They were tough at best. The only bright spot was the side of barbeque sauce that came with it. I had heard about how good the korn fritters were so I wanted to give them a try...wished that I hadn't. They were basically fried dough (tough) with kernels of what appeared to be old corn in them. As others have mentioned, they are overpriced! I am certain that I could have the same meal (and better no less) at Friday's or Chili's for at least $10-$15 less. So not worth it at all, especially if you want barbeque.

Indifferent is being polite.
by eschweik (1 review)
August 29, 2009
Indifferent is the nicest thing I can say about our experience in this over (over!) priced tourist trap. Our server ignored us and had to be flagged down every time, except bringing the bill which she was extremely efficient at.
The salmon was very dry and terribly disappointing, the barbecue was tasteless (though the sauce provided at the table was acceptable). The chicken-fried steak was mediocre (grey and salty), though the white gravy and basket-cut chips were good.
The biggest deal, though, was the prices. Completely out of control, even for Alaska tourist traps. Make sure you google their menu before you go so you know what you're in for. Better yet, go to any other Anchorage restaurant instead.
