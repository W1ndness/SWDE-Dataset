An Osaka Clone
by S M (49 reviews)
February 26, 2010
Take the Osaka concept, copy it, and this is what you get. It's exactly what you expect for this type of restaurant where you sit with a group at the grill. I've been several times, and the food has been consistent and fairly good (not as good as a steakhouse, but that's not why you typically go to a place like this). The chefs at this place, Osaka and Tokyo Garden are all good. The service/management experience is a little better at Osaka and Tokyo Garden. I've found if you have a problem or a change, admittedly which occurs infrequently, Shogun is less willing to deal with it. For example, we had a large party seated, and one person came late (the appetizers had been started). This caused great consternation with the owner (she happened to be working that night). For a couple or just a few people, pick any of these places and it should be fine. For a large group, I'd pick one of the others besides Shogun.

Love it!
by Jen (10 reviews)
March 19, 2010 - Likes it
I love eating here! Steak & Shrimp...yum! I love their clear soup as well and all the vegetables (especially the zucchini) that come with your meal. The show from the chef's are fantastic. I recommend Toshi, Hasan & Paul...they have all been there a really long time. I used to work there too. :-) The new location is so much better than the one on Harvard that I worked at. I love their special sauces. I have ate at similar types of places in Colorado where I live now and no one comes close to the sauces and ginger salad dressing that Shogun's makes...delicious! Everytime I go back to Oklahoma, I have to eat there! The only thing I wish they would change is charging for refills on drinks. I believe only iced tea has free refills. Be aware that in this type of restaurant there is communal seating so you might have other people at your table which is not necessarily a bad thing. The Chef's put on a show so they need to have full tables. If you don't want to sit with other people, I suggest you have at least 7 to 8 people in your party.

mobile review
by Kris Roberson (3 reviews)
February 08, 2010
Easinable food. As typical the prices are high, no surprise there. Soup and salad on par with any other Japanese Steakhouse. I enjoyed the salad dressing with its nice savory Gonger tang. The Chef seemed very inexperienced but since all of them have to star somewhere it can be forgiven. I had the Shogun Special (Lonster tail and Fillet Mignon) with a 6 piece shrimp side. Lots of oil began a lackluster show. The opening veggies were adequately cooked, again nothing to "write home about". I asked for Shirachi sauce (red pepper hot sauce) pr othe hot sauce. The wait staff hadn't a clue of wha I was speaking. Finally a manager arrived to say that they had Cayanne pepper on a small shaker but that was all. The shrimp was vey flavorfull an perfectly cooked. A suprise fir a land-locked city. Lobster was fishy and bland, really quite disappointing. The filet was tender and properly cooked to order. Sherbet and green tea rounded out an OK meal. Having frequented similar places all over the country I'd say this one was mediocre overall.
