Excellent
by Aaron (46 reviews)
May 22, 2010 - Really likes it
My girlfriend and I stumbled upon this place while driving around looking for a place to eat while in the area. I'm glad that I noticed it. It's not visible from the highway and the exterior is inconspicuous.
Every gyro/Greek restaurant I've been to in the Cincinnati area has been a dive with mediocre food. This is not the case with Greek Isles. The place looked immaculate, the decor was very pleasant, and the the food was fantastic.
I had the chicken souvlaki, my girlfriend had the gyro, and we both shared a greek salad and baklava. The chicken souvlaki was absolutely delicious, and I took a bite of my girlfriend's gyro, and that was just as good. I've never actually enjoyed gyros before. I was pleasantly surprised by what I had here. The baklava was also perfectly done.
Overall it was less than $25, which is pretty stellar considering how good the food is. I'd consider it well worth a bit of a drive. I hope business is doing well, because I plan on coming back!

Coming back? Try the rack of lamb!
by getinmybelly (2 reviews)
January 30, 2010 - Likes it
Just got home from another trip to the Greek Isles. I have been going with my family since shortly after GI opened for business, and I keep coming back.
Why? Because this restaurant has the best Greek food imaginable, and the greatest atmosphere one can pray for. The authentic (yes, I said authentic) fare reminds this reviewer of being invited to a lovingly prepared family meal where the family actually likes one another.
Constantine, the owner, does much to commend this restaurant. Despite an ever growing clientel, he knows all of his customers, and will sit and discuss the food with you. If you are truly lucky, he will let you try something new from the menu.
I have had most of the menu, and while I had not had much Greek food before, I know his will be a touchstone for the rest of my life. Try anything there, but my favorites are the Gyro, Mousaka, Kalamata Olives, Pastitsio, and if you are truly hungry, the Greek Isle Sampler!
Tonight we went for gyros (his are the best, everyone else may go home. This includes best in Pita, best in Hummus, best in gyro meat, etc.) After our gyros I noticed a sign indicating the newest addition, "Rack of Lamb". When Constantine made his way to our table, I expressed regret that I had not seen the sign before, suggesting he name such a dish "Thermoplyae", in keeping with some other dishes named "Colossus" or "Titan".
He said "Wait a minute", and returned, bringing back an entire serving of the Rack of Lamb. He stayed to watch our reaction. The marinated lamb is sublime, free of the usual lamb scent, perfectly cooked and served atop a hill of special rice.
I shook my head, "To heck with 'Thermopylae', call it 'Olympus!"
If you favor great food with a family atmosphere and a host never to be equaled, add this to your list. For the food in question, I paraphrase Leonidas, "Come and get it!"
