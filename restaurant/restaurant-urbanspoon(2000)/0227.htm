8/10
by Aaron (46 reviews)
June 05, 2010 - Really likes it
This is the only place I've been to in Cincinnati so far that rivals Nada - which is fitting, since they apparently are a part of the same company.
I went the other night on my birthday. I was treated to free Prosecco before dinner and Muscato after dinner. Not something I was expecting, but a very pleasant surprise.
As for the food - it was absolutely delicious. We got the proscuitto wrapped asparagus and tuna for appetizers. I'm going to have to say that while these were both very good, they didn't necessarily warrant the price. However, I was given advance warning by my waiter with regards to portion size. "Everything on the left side of the menu is very small in portion" - he wasn't kidding!
For entrees, we got the seabass and filet mignon. My seabass was done well, and was very enjoyable - not blow your mind good, but still well executed. The filet is what you want to get though. The steak was perfectly done, and the sauces and sides that came with it were exquisite.
For dessert we got the sampler - 3 different items on there. All good, nothing spectacular.
Overall, Boca holds up to its reputation of being on the forefront of the artisan food scene in Cincinnati. I think the price kind of outpaces the food (was $160 for 2 people, including wine), but it's still definitely worth trying if you've got the money to do so.
