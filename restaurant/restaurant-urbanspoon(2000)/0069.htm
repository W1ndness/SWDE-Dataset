Big Po-Boy's
by iEatAtTheBar (374 reviews)
April 10, 2008 - Likes it
I ate here for lunch and had Red Beans and Rice for an appetizer, then Po-Boy lunch special.
The Red Beans and Rice were good, but not as spicy as I would expect. I got the cup, which was served in a coffee cup, and even with me trying to mix it up (not easy with a big spoon in a small cup) the bottom still had just rice and no sauce or beans.  Some of the rice was still hard and un-cooked.
The Gumbo was good, but lacking any real kick. It also tasted 'burnt' as if the bottom of the pot burned and they stirred it up into the Gumbo. It had more of a burnt taste than anything.
I would go back to try again and would be hopeful that it would be better, but I feel that most all of their dishes are not a spicy as  most would expect their Cajun to be.

Bring tip money
by Austin Blues (55 reviews)
April 09, 2009 - Really likes it
An interesting place to eat some great Gumbo and etouffee. Suze and I went out tonight for a quick gumbo. She ordered, what has become her usual, the Maw Maw Sausage and Chicken Gumbo. I went for the Craw Fish Etouffee. For an appetizer, Suze ordered the Boudin. When it arrived, she exclaimed, "Now that puts a smile on my face!" Frankly to me, it looked like Johnny Holmes had left something behind on the set. The Bodin was a large portion of yummy, ricey goodness, with a wonderful herb taste throughout. Quite filling. Our dinner arrived, as did another Albita Amber, and the silence began. I really like the roux of the Maw Maw gumbo. A down and dirty, dark roux with nice bits of chicken/sausage. The Etouffee was a large portion, with plenty of Craw Fish and in a blonde roux. I would of liked the spice heat to be more, but afterall it is an Etouffee. Those are the pro's. A couple of little cons..... I understand that musicians need money, so do a lot of folks, but I don't understand why they are not paid to begin with? Perhaps a cover charge? It is always a bit awkward to me when one of the cute little waitress's comes by the table to ask if you want to tip the band, while holding out the tip jar to you. They have the same little smile that the kiddo's do during Halloween when they come by your house, with their little bags open, begging for you to give them some teeth rotting candy.. little bastards... But, I digress...I would prefer not to be pan-handled during dinner..... The restaurant is a tight squeeze, I mean you probably couldn't swing a dead gerbil without hitting your neighbor..... and finally it is a little pricey.. $50 tonight for a gumbo, etouffee, boudin, 2 glasses of wine, 2 Albita Ambers.. $5 tip to band. The seafood Gumbo I had before was great, but comes in at $13.99.. Despite these items, the place is pretty neat, and the food is terrific. We will be back.
