+ 3 more photos from catapillo Amazingly Delicious!
by catapillo (9 reviews)
November 09, 2008 - Really likes it
This is the best restaurant for Thai cuisine and sushi in the Cincinnati area! Far better than Teak and Lemongrass, Sweet Basil offers a friendly atmosphere with high-quality, freshly-made Thai dishes that are absolutely delicious. Pad See Ew, Pad Kee Mao, Pepper Garlic, and Duck Noodle Soup are among my favorites. The sushi bar is excellent, and extremely fresh. The owner is the sushi chef, and will offer up great conversation while you sit at the sushi bar. There are some very tasty rolls on the menu. My favorites include New York (tuna, spicy mayo, avocado, and tempura flakes), Mona Lisa (smoked salmon, avocado, crab, spicy sauce, kiwi, and sweet sauce), and the Crazy Horse (smoked salmon, cream cheese and avocado, all tempura-fried and served warm).
This restaurant is highly recommended! My husband and I go at least once a week - look for us at the sushi bar!

Sooooo Delicious!
by Bridget (6 reviews)
August 13, 2009 - Likes it
When my husband and I walked into the restaurant, we were pleased with the warm decor, dominated by dark wood tones. We sat at a comfortable booth and looked over a menu that offered a wide selection. As a vegetarian, I was happy that I could order most menu items with tofu. We began our meal with the peanut sauce salad, lettuce topped with tofu, crispy noodles and peanut sauce. "Crispy" is the key word in the description of this salad. With the greens being very fresh, they provided crunchiness. The crispy noodles lived up to their name, and were a delightful change from the typical shredded cheese topping. Even the tofu had a crispy coating. The peanut sauce was "out of this world", adding complexity to this salad of few ingredients. Since my husband loved the sauce, he ordered the Ramayana Garden for his main course - a dish of steamed, mixed vegetables, choice of meat and peanut sauce on the side. I ordered the yellow curry with tofu. What a colorful meal! The vegetables were vibrantly bright - cooked just right. We spent more money than we usually do for lunch, but it was definitely worth it! Our server was friendly and attentive; however, she was overly anxious to take our plates, bowls and utensils. This situation is not particular to Sweet Basil, however, as my husband and I have experienced this in other restaurants that we have visited. That's too bad, because when a meal is delicious like the one we enjoyed at Sweet Basil, our goal is to savor the flavors, and relax! Overall, our experience was a pleasant one. I will take this opportunity to thank the people at Sweet Basil for providing healthy food and vegetarian options. Best wishes and much success to you!

Terrific Thai, Splendid Sushi
by Christian (3 reviews)
March 18, 2010 - Likes it
I have eaten at many Thai restaurants in the area, but for some reason...I just keep coming back to this one. There are really so many qualities that go into a good Thai and Sushi eatery, and this place puts a check on so many that I can't resist a return visit.
Most importantly, the thai food! At Sweet Basil, you are gettin' the real deal, love, spice, and all. I have ordered in and taken out many of their dishes and I have to say I was rarely disappointed! The Classic Thai Spicy is exceptional, the Cashew Nuts is outrageous, and I can't even talk about the Green Curry it is soo good. You can tell that these cooks have had some practice, and they don't skimp either.
Next, the sushi. Honestly, I'm not the biggest sushi eater in the world, but I do appreciate quality, and this place has it. I've been there with some friends of mine who eat a lot of sushi and I ate with them, but let me just say they didn't bother to wait for me to go back. They were pretty enthused about the chef's ability with the knife (they watched him slice and dice), the quality of the fish they were served, and the overall beautiful knife work that went into elaborate decorations surrounding their entree.
Lastly, and for me, definitely not least: the service. I often have the same server when I go there, but either way they are always attentive and whether they look busy or not, I know that they are putting effort into their jobs. The Cook, the chef, the servers, and the owner (whom I've personally met) are all really great and you can tell they really put work into making their small business thrive in every possible way.
I may eat at other Thai and Sushi places from time to time, but my heart will always be at Sweet Basil, and that you can be sure of!
