Strictly New Mexican Chili Dog
by Chef Dare (167 reviews)
January 23, 2010 - Likes it
The Dog House is an Albuquerque landmark restaurant that serves up a phenomenal foot-long Chili Cheese Dog! Be forewarned though, the chili served here has very little in common with any type of Eastern "Coney" sauce! This stuff is New Mexican style chili sauce and has the appropriate kick to let you know that it is proud of that fact! Most people eat in their cars here, but if you do decide to venture inside to the extremely tiny dining room, don't be put off by the "retro,route 66,greasy diner" appearance. It just adds to the whole atmosphere of this place. Not fine dining by any stretch of the imagination, but if you are looking for an earthier, local dining experience, then this might be the place.

El Gringo Curmudgeon
by Bob of Los Ranchos (3 reviews)
July 29, 2010
I've eaten probably 450 feet of DH chili cheese dogs con onions over the past 40ish years and am down to needing only two napkins!! I'd be surprized if the NM red chili that is a DH dog, has changed its "hand made" status over the years. Lest you've only been once, you don't realize that a few owners have probably produced the most W-2 forms than any other business in ABQ by giving a hands-up to many youngsters over the years and therefore a glitch may come along once in a while, e.g. a soggy bun. Actually, their secret to a good chili dog holder is "toasting" the buns on the grill which maybe a gal missed. (Eh? didja notice the pains they go to to maximize the dog's flavor? Yes! they take the time n trouble to split the dog before grilling!!! Lastly, like choosing a proper wine when dining elsewhere, I recommend the Orange Drink (even if they were to serve margaritas or Dos Eqis) to truly enhance the Foot-long, chili-cheese dog con cebolla experience. Muy Sabrosa! The colas only detract.

Try It!
by Lily Mas (29 reviews)
December 25, 2009 - Likes it
This is an incredible place which has been in ABQ for a long time. If you want to see it "live", go around 11 or 12:00, lunch time. It can be a madhouse but that's part of its charm. Just keep an eye out for your order - some people will reach in for yours if they are in a hurry. The chile dog (I don't like cheese on it) is incredible and I've never had one like it anywhere. If you don't want to go in, just park and waitresses will come to you. Cheap, crazy, fun and the best chile dog I've eaten! Very highly recommended.

Great retro atmosphere; truly awful dogs
by Peter Piper (1 review)
March 25, 2010 - Doesn't like it
There is a local joke about this place saying that if you lose a bet you'll have to eat a dog at the Dog House.
Unfortunately the current owners merely run this place as a greasy spoon lunch joint. The kind of hot dogs they serve are absolutely the cheapest (chicken-pork-beef-and-beef-by-products) junk from the sysco truck. The so-called chili for the chili dogs is simply chile-con-carne from the can with added red pepper. Extra spice attempting to cover up for the fact that it's straight out of the can. I ate here once and could barely finish the hot dog.
I went back a few months later thinking that perhaps I made a mistake the first time. It was just as bad the second time. I think many who write reviews confuse food with atmosphere and rate a place well simply because it has uniqueness and orginality. This place is indeed a surviving relic of the heydey of route 66. It's unfortunate they serve such bad food.

Chili
by Brooke Nicole Jorgensen (5 reviews)
June 27, 2010 - Likes it
Today was the first time I had been to The Dog House. They have a selection of hamburgers and hot dogs. My mom and I walked into The Dog House and the atmosphere was small and cozy. They have a bar that seats five and a few tables to sit at. So we went down and picked ourselves a nice little table. It was time to order. I started off with a strawberry shake. This shake was delicious! The chocolate shake was great too! Then we both ordered a chili cheese dog with onions. The chili was red chili, which was really different and tasted great. If you're into hot chili, you will really like this chili dog. I would reccomend this for a date or a family outing. It is a really informal environment.
