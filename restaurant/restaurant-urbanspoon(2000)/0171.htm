Truly Worth It!
by heather (1 review)
November 30, 2009 - Really likes it
Eating at Hyman's is a religious experience. Everyone is genuinely kind, the waitstaff are very friendly and professional, and the most important part: the food is nothing less than the best seafood in the south. I had a fried combo of shrimp, scallops, and a crab cake. All of it was excellent, and unlike some of the other reviews I have read I did not find my food to be too greasy but rather perfectly fried. (Order your food broiled if you don't like oil). This place is very kid-friendly, the food is moderately priced (about 16-17 dollars for a large plate of food, and kids' stuff between 4-6 dollars, adult platters easily shared as the portions are quite large), and the atmosphere and employees really make it a dining experience to remember. Do yourself a favor and just go!

Hyman's was recommended by my best friend. We tried it on our first trip to Charleston. It was delicious and worth it. It's placed in a two-story setting spanning three store fronts in historic downtown Charleston. I introduced my boyfriend to shrimp and grits for the first time, which he enjoyed. They were delicious. I don't eat a lot of seafood, bu I tried the crab dip and swordfish. Both were excellent. The dip was mouth-watering and had a hint of spice though I couldn't place it. The boyfriend thought it was horseradish. Either way, delicious. The swordfish was a meatier fish than I expected, cooked with Cajun seasoning. Scrumptious and filling! The server was great giving recommendations and answering all our questions. Owner even came by to chat with us and was delightful! The ambiance was very beachy with wood paneling. The staff was friendly and everyone was delightful! I highly recommend it as a seafood stop when in Charleston.

Love it
by restaurantguy (3 reviews)
November 03, 2009 - Likes it
Tourist trap? What does that mean? That they lure tourists there and force them them to write great reviews and then keep coming back year after year?
You get the same quality of food as some downtown places (just down the alley for instance) at nearly 40% off. Atmosphere is kitchy but fun. Staff is really attentive and friendly. I have eaten here over ten times and never had a bad meal and always greeted by manager or owner. Fried green tomatoes are the best. Crispy flounder is literally the same as the place on market and you can save $10. I have tried the additional following and loved each: buffalo shrimp, hush puppies, cajun salmon, caribbean mahi, tuna, coleslaw, okra gumbo, chicken salad sandwich, turkey sandwich (this is the real deal!), fried scallops and more! Even the shrimp & grits, though a bit salty for my tastes, is a quality meal. Trust me, you won't go worng with this place... and you won't go home hungry!
