The definative hamburger
by R. E. P. (279 reviews)
May 30, 2009 - Likes it
Make no mistake about it, Five Guys has a very good hamburger. They make each to your specifications one at a time. Their website claims they can make over 250,000 different burgers using various combinations of their toppings. We went Saturday night to see what people were talking about and found first a hard time finding a parking place, second a long line waiting to order, and third a packed dining area with people waiting for tables. But we hung in there, ordered burgers, found a table outside, got our drinks, waited about 15 minutes for our number to be called out, and we were finally were rewarded with our order. I will have to admit the burgers were worth the wait. All four of us got just what we had ordered, no mistakes on anybody's toppings, every burger prepared perfectly, hot and juicy.

Great burgers
by Michael Ward (30 reviews)
March 20, 2010
Very good burgers. Not the best in town because they can be a tad dry, but probably the best for the price. And they have lots a great toppings.
The french fries are even better than the burgers. The hot dogs are good too.
On the down side, the tables are packed too close together in part because so much usuable floor space is cluttered with sacks of potatoes and boxes of peanuts, and I get sick of listening to double Q everytime I'm in there since radio is mostly commercials anymore.
There's also very littler variaty and no kids meal. However, they do sell a grilled cheese sandwich so if you've got a little one you might want to make note of that because it is easy to miss on the menu.
Edit: I'm downgrading my review of Five Guys because in the short time they've been open they have messed up my order twice. Combined with all the other issues, this is not a restaurant I see myself going back to unless I'm with other people who want to go. A restaurant needs more than an good burger to be a good restaurant.
