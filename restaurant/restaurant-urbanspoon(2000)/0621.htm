Worth the money.
by CowTown ChowHound (235 reviews)
June 02, 2009 - Likes it
While on business in Louisville I had the chance to enjoy dinner here shortly after my having visited Ruth's Chris. I can honestly say that the whole experience was better here. The staff were friendly and while I'm sure the several different Bourbons I went through while enjoying the oysters I had prior to my steak may have put me into a better mood I do think what I remember was real:)
The oysters on the half shell were great. they were fresh and as good as any I've had at Catch in Calgary. The steak was dry aged USDA prime and not the wet aged that Ruth's Chris served two days earlier. It was cooked to not quite what I wanted but was tasty and tender.
A great place for for the true steak lover.

Tendency to Burn
by Pizza Lover (17 reviews)
June 09, 2010 - Likes it
Jeff Ruby's is not bad in a pinch. Great bar. Sides are excellent. Service is top notch ... but they burn my steak every single time. Four visits, four orders for a steak cooked medium, four burnt steaks. You could understand if I ordered well done or possibly even medium well (but no excuse here, not at a restaurant in this class) ... but burning a medium order? Simply sloppy. I'll take Ruth's Chris over Ruby's hands down.
Still, a good place for a date night or to enjoy the bar.

Don't go!
by dollyla (1 review)
June 17, 2010 - Doesn't like it
All I can say is "Don't go!" I was visiting a friend in Kentucky and she chose Jeff Ruby's for our highlight meal. Our waiter spoke so fast we could not understand him. He also spent the entire time looking over our heads. Twice we asked him to slow down. He still spoke too quickly for us. We split the Kentucky Bibb Bleu Salad - which was okay. We also split the Cowboy bone in ribeye ($49) . Our waiter assured us that "anything was possible" - just not doable. We wanted the steak split and cooked medium rare and medium. Both halves were overcooked and dry. We got the Wok-Charred French Beans which I really liked. For desert we each ordered the Crème brûlée. It came out with the topping burned with no crunchy top (how is this possible)? We sent it back and the second time it was worse. We asked our waiter to take it back and take it off our ticket. Final consensus: snooty service and highly over-rated, over-priced, and over-cooked food!

Everything I expected..and MORE
by OldChef (5 reviews)
November 17, 2009 - Likes it
Having lived in Louisville for several years, this was one of those "have to go" places on my list. Finally the perfect reason for palate pampering session presented itself. The foie gras appetizer was little bit heaven on a plate! The steak and sides were perfect in every respect. Service was superb. It surpassed my expectations, not an easy thing after building up three years of anticipation!
I can only say the old adage of "you get what you pay for" aptly describes Jeff Ruby's. There is no sacrifice in quality, service or atmosphere. The product received was definitely worth the cost.
