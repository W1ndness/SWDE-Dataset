Cincinnati Classic #1
by Closet Gourmet (80 reviews)
June 11, 2009 - Really likes it
Whenever family or friends come into town for the first time there are several stops on my tour of the city. Fountain Square, Sawyer Point/Serp Wall, Mt. Adams, Eden Park/Krohn out to Ault Park via the parkway and back. At the end of the loop we always stop at Arnold's and weather permitting sit in the courtyard. Arnold's embodies classic Cincinnati, it's german heritage while being oddly hip at the same time. Ask to sit next to the bath tub if you can't sit outside.
Arnold's is a (real) beer drinker's paradise. Arnold's "old" menu which includes pasta dishes (big fan) and sandwiches is always consistent. Unfortunately if you catch the kitchen on a busy or bad night it can be hit or miss (which seems to be a common problem with many established restaurants). Especially if you try some of the newer offerings don't hesitate to send it back or order something different if not up to your liking. Service is very casual but always friendly and helpful. Ask for a recommendation and enjoy!

I didn't have high expectations but
by critic (1 review)
March 02, 2010
Having read other reviews, I didn't have high expectations but they still managed to disapoint me.
I had their signature Pasta Androsky. I came almost instantly - not a good sign. The pasta was well cooked and firm. The wine sauce was not really a sauce - it was a limp mix of wine and water.
The topping of Roasted red peppers, wild mushrooms, artichokes, red onion, sun dried tomatoes and fresh spinach was poor - there was hardly any of it. The dusting of romano was just that - except microspic dusting would have been a better way to describe it and unfotunately, I did not have my microscope with me.
Ms Androsky says that the customers should talk to her so that she can rectify it. How can she rectify tired and unimaginative cuisine. I got the impression that the waiting staff were a little embarassed to serve it.
Again, the best that can be said of the desert - peach cobbler - was that it was OK.
OK is OK but not for that price tag. OK should cost $7 for and not $14.50.
To top it all, I was given the wrong bill.
I will not return to Arnolds even if Ms Androsky rectifies the faults on my request. I don't go out to have a complaint. I go out to have a carefree evening.
