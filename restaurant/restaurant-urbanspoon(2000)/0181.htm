Needs Improvement
by The perfect eat (3 reviews)

The pastries and croissants are great, the soups and sandwiches are delicious, and the coffee is usually serviceable, but it could be better. They have plenty of space to sit down with friends or co-workers, and nobody will ever make you feel pressured to turn over a table.
Unfortunately, Amelie's needs to do a little better to impress me. Most of the staff is pretty surly, they've started defaulting to paper cups for coffee, your (justifiably) expensive pastries will be shoved in a plastic clamshell, and there's never enough counter people for the long lines that sometimes form.
Step it up Amelie's, I know you can.

Hit and miss
by Christopher Baier (3 reviews)
August 18, 2009
I was very excited when I heard about Amelie's-hoping Charlotte finally got a good, European-style bakery.
So I went there yesterday for lunch with mi amigo. Was mesmerized by the display case of tarts, eclairs, croissants, pains au chocolate, and a few cakes. The employee at the counter told us they also had lunch available, so we decided to stay for lunch. The sandwiches-a roast beef sandwich and a Croque-monsieur-were good. The CM actually was awesome. But 2 sandwiches and 2 sodas cost around $17, if I remember correctly.
We got some sweets to go-a Napoleon and some French macaroons. I should have known by seeing the dark brown color of the pastry that the Napoleon was over baked, but I went ahead and got it. And tasting it I knew that the pastry was definitely way overcooked and the filling could not overcome that over-baked flavor. I don't know what authentic French macaroons taste like (I only recently made some myself at home a few weeks ago), but I was not impressed with these cookes.
Today I suddenly had a pastry craving and decided to go back for some pain au chocolate and maybe something else. I got the pain au chocolate (which I am saving for breakfast), a chocolate cream puff and a cream cheese puff. The person who was on oven duty for the cream puff must have been the same person who baked the Napoleon pastry, because it too was over-baked. The filling also squeezed out of a hole in the bottom when I bit into it, but that may have been user error on my part.
The cream cheese puff had perhaps a heaping teaspoon of cream cheese filling in it (ok, maybe two, but definitely not enough), so was mostly baked pastry dough and a streusel-like topping.
My Amelie days may be over for now, but I may visit again in a few months. I definitely am not in a hurry to return.
Oh, forgot to mention, they have free wi-fi.

24/7 Multi-Culture
by Altroy (2 reviews)
October 04, 2009 - Likes it
Amélie's is by far one of the most cutting-edge Multi-cultural establishments in the city of Charlotte. Not only is it open 24 hours a day, seven days a week and serves a variety of coffee and Teas, but it offered my favorite mineral water Perrier:-)
Amélie's offers a great selection of Fresh Bread to include another favorite flavor, potato and onion. Of course, they do offer a Classic Baguette, but you can also get a Multigrain or a sweeter Cinnamon and Raisin.
Their Pastry selection is excellent as well, which I've most recently tried their Tea cakes, which I believe came in lemon, raspberry and almond flavors. This was my choice opposed to having the Huge Neapolitan Pastry that I enjoyed on my previous visit, which I didn't have enough room in my tummy for this time.
The soup and sandwich selections are quite tasty as well. Especially their special seeded roast beef with horse radish and melted mozzarella..mmm good:-) or adding one of their freshly made soups, which are served with a baguette.
The atmosphere is comfortable and eclectic with Antique Furniture and a variety of Art decor. The Multi-Cultural patrons seem to be an asset in the NoDa area and significantly adds to the Life-Style in Charlotte. There is quite more to experience there than what I've mentioned, but I think you can get a pretty good idea of the place. Check it out!
