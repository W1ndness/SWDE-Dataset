Message from Hearth Pizza Tavern

Wood Fire Pizza, Bar Scene and Outdoor Patio in Sandy Springs

  • Huge Outdoor Patio
  • 1/2 Price Pizza Happy Hour
  • Thursday $5 Martini Specials

Hearth Pizza Tavern in Sandy Springs is a place like no other. This pizza eatery offers all fresh oven fired pizza pies with a wide selection of beers, wines and spirits. All of the food at Hearth Pizza Tavern is made fresh, in their own kitchen, right down to homemade dough made with organic flour, cheeses and a variety of pizza toppings.

The signature pizzas are the freshest in Atlanta. For seafood lovers, be sure to try the seafood sliders and fresh mussels. As an appetizer, don't miss our parmesan truffle fries. Hearth Pizza Tavern has one of the best bar scenes in Sandy Springs and an outdoor patio that calls for a table of friends and cold beers.
Whether you are dining al fresco or inside, you are sure to enjoy their quality pizzas and top shelf food with a Tavern feel. Cheers, and enjoy what they like to call Hearth Fired Happiness!

One of our "go to" places
by fishsticks64 (20 reviews)
February 23, 2009 - Likes it
I have two children - a five year old and a two year old. We regularly go to Hearth for pizza, the fabulous meatballs with ricotta cheese/bread for dipping and the salads. We don't deviate all that much from those items - although we did try the calamari once - and it was very good.
The staff is always super efficient and friendly - even bringing lemonade for my children before I order them - watered down as I would request otherwise. They are friendly and very accommodating in terms of my children so, at least early in the evening, this is definitely a family-friendly restaurant (and there are always lots of families there when we're there.)
There is a deal on pizza for dinner before 6, I believe - half price and I think this is a nice touch. As a native-NY'er, I definitely have a pre-disposition about pizza. The pizza at Hearth is wood-burning and hand "shaped". The personal pizzas are somewhat rectangular in shape and not cut like a standard pie (in other words, they're cut in half and then in three or four - generally resulting in 6 or 8 square pieces). This is not my preference but again, the pizza is open "hearth"/wood burning in style and taste. It is very good, nevertheless.
The aesthtic is wonderful at Hearth (and I can't wait until we can use the outdoor area - although it overlooks the parking lot, they've done a great job with the surrounding fence, the tables, the heat posts, etc.). They've used dark wood (for the tables and booths) but the feel is not dark nor cavernous. The bar is impressive.
I wish they had some pasta offerings, but I understand this is not the "model" for the restaurant (which is rumored to be the flagship - with more to come??
Definitely recommend.

I pride myself on being a connoisseur of pizza. That simply means I sample the local pizza where ever I go and that I am judgmental about the quality of the base pie. The right balance between cheese sauce and dough. Combined with proper firing can produce, on some occasions, the perfect blend of taste and texture. Hearth on Roswell Rd. has found the touch.
