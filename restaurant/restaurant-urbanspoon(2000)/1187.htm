Message from Kokoras Greek Grill

Authentic Greek Food

  • Authentic Greek Food
  • Open for Lunch or Dinner
  • Free Baklava with Lunch

Kokoras Greek grill is reminiscent of a small Greek tavern. Authentic Greek dishes are prepared and served by owners Spiros and Demetra Rouvas. Kokoras combines a pleasant atmosphere with delightful service. Kokoras Greek grill has come to West Seattle. Eat, drink and enjoy your experience you won't leave Kokoras Greek grill hungry!

No revelation, but good sit-down Greek
by banderstone (157 reviews)
October 27, 2007 - Likes it
I usually don't write reviews for places so long after I go to them.  I swore I did so already, but it seems not.  Caveat emptor:  It's been months.
Kokoras is a very strong place.  It's not much of a hole-in-the -wall.  If you're looking for a meaty, saucy, savage place of deliciousness, this isn't the place (I recommend Byblos Deli in Bellevue).  But if you're looking for a mannered, pretty plate in a casual-but-sophisticated atmosphere, Kokoras is excellent.
This is nothing fans of Mediterranean haven't seen before, although it's not all staple food.  But in a part of Seattle that is seriously lacking in quality restaurants, this is a nice find.  This is probably the best restaurant in West Seattle Junction, and the best Greek I've been familiarized with south of Interstate 90.
Recommended.

A great weekend lunch spot
by Holly Reilly (3 reviews)
July 03, 2010 - Likes it
Our family of four (two adults, a 90 year old, and a 4 year old) went in for lunch, and because grandma hadn't had Greek at all before, we decided to grab the lunch-menu sampler platter for 3-4. I've decided that it's more appropriate for 5-6 people!
The platter was piled to overflowing with souvlaki beef, lamb, chicken, and gyro meats, as well as feta cheese, tomatoes, red onion, and cucumber. The platter of pita wedges and tzatziki came right after and really hit the spot. Because we were assuming that there would be less food, and because we wanted to give grandma a full compliment of choices, we ordered the falafel platter as well. Greek fries and falafel, as well as a large portion of hummus. Both platters also came with a first course of salad, which is loaded with feta cheese and olives, and a great dressing (which I bought a bottle of, to take home).
Grandma wasn't impressed (but she's 90 and really prefers "american" fare), but my husband, daughter, and myself stuffed ourselves to the gills, and still walked out with enough leftovers to have a full dinner that night.

Warning: Kokoras will Ruin You for All Other Greek Food in Seattle!!
by athena (1 review)
January 20, 2009
Let me just preface this by saying simply that I love Greek food. I've been coming to Spiros' & Demetra's restaurants since before I could drive (I'm now 31). While the food was always amazing & something I dragged every last person I knew to experience, I didn't fully appreciate it until 1) I started trying other Greek places in Seattle and literally across the nation and 2)went to Greece & Italy a couple years back. The intense flavors of Kokoras are something that only comes from homemade, whole food based, quality driven & legit source. After years of eating the stuff over & over again like some addicted fiend, I wasn't surprised at all to learn that all sauces, dips and dressings are made from scratch by the hands of Spiros & his son, Staci, all meats are grilled to order, and all menu items have a whole food base. Everything is fresh w/quality at highest priority, served with a smile & oozing with Legit Greek deliciousness. I must confess, I still eat Greek elsewhere if I'm not in the area and craving it, but thats happening less & less as it just leaves me wanting Kokoras! NOTES - There is sometimes a wait as its super tiny, but its worth it everytime - just put your name on the list and go hang at the pub across the st, they'll call you when your table is available - Try the specials, their ALWAYS great! - This is the only place in Seattle to get good, fresh lamb - The lamb dolmas are to die for as is the calamari which is the best I've ever had - Take a menu to go...trust me, you'll need it!!

A fair restaurant
by Lee (2 reviews)
February 18, 2009 - Doesn't like it
Our family of 4 went to the restaurant - 2 adults and 2 teens. The waiter was polite and welcoming, but did not fall into the trap of hovering and interrupting.
The menu is quite limited in terms of choice of main dishes, but there are sandwiches, soups and salads available. My son ordered a gyro sandwich, which was so generous it was impossible to pick up until more than half was eaten. He did enjoy the gyro very much. As well, we had the chicken souvlaki platter and the lamb platter. My chicken platter had too much cheese sprinkled on top, but the meat was tender and juicy. While it was not bad, it was not delicious either. My husband had the same opinion of the lamb, and he was disappointed to see many raisins in the rice pilaf. As a result, he felt it was too sweet. I found the salad dressing too sweet as well, but obviously this is a matter of taste. My teenager ate every bit of the cheeseburger, and enjoyed it.
If you like sweet salad dressing and/or rice, you will enjoy both here. The calamari was probably the most tender I've tasted in a long time. We all thoroughly enjoyed that appetizer.
This is a quaint little restaurant, but the food just wasn't enough to bring my husband and me back. Our dinner for 4, with one beer and tip included, amounted to $80. Contrary to other reviewers opinions, we didn't find $20/person to be overpriced (this included a beer).
