Message from Gourmet Burger Grill

Burger restaurant serves up high quality beef with a bevy of toppings available.

  • Certified Angus beef burgers
  • Extensive gourmet burger menu
  • Free Home-style potato chips

Gourmet Burger Grill in San Antonio was voted 'Best New Restaurant Less than One Year Old,' by the 2007 Express News Readers' Poll.

Gourmet Burger Grill uses a new concept that offers Kobe beef, lamb, seafood and veggie options, accompanied by freshly baked rolls and a myriad of gourmet toppings. We focus on quality, value and selection. Certified Angus Beef burgers, chicken and turkey burgers, salads, soups, sides and specialty burgers made of lamb, Maryland-style crab cakes, Ahi tuna, Portobello mushrooms, Kobe beef and vegetarian patties are all made to order. Choose from a variety of freshly baked buns.
Burger prices range from $6.95 to $12.95. Gourmet Burger Grill uses no trans-fats and all patties can be wrapped in lettuce for a low-carb alternative. Sauces, dressings are made from scratch. Visit our website for our menu. Take-out and catering are available.

GBG = Good, but expensive
by Michael (29 reviews)
July 12, 2010 - Likes it
Pricey, but I'll be back!
Burger prices range from $6.95 to $12.95. You can it with a great variety of innards: Certified Angus Beef, chicken and turkey, lamb, crab cakes, tuna, Portobello mushrooms, Kobe beef and vegetarian. Great buns which include Sourdough and whole-grain wheat rolls that are baked each day. Toppings range from a selection of over 10 cheeses, over 20 sauces and items ranging basil pesto aioli and chipotle mayo to apple-smoked bacon, mango avocado salsa and fried eggs.
The burger is truly gourmet with you adding the individual touch when you order.
Two downfalls:
(1) No draft beer
(2) The price is on the high side.
Note: A kids' menu is also available, with choices priced at about $5--I could get a McD Kids Meal for less than this and the kid would be just as happy (maybe happier).

Best burgers anywhere
by Jimmie (44 reviews)
February 22, 2009 - Really likes it
GBG is the one restaurant I've found that outdoes my own homemade burgers. The wide variety of gourmet burgers, the homemade chips, the sweet potato fries, and a number of non-burger choices should make anyone happy.
The service is always friendly, if sometimes overworked. And the owner makes a special effort to get to know his customers. (My teenage daughter was thrilled when she took some friends in there and he not only recognized her but treated her as a special customer.)
The daily specials are definitely worth trying. My personal favorite is the Surf-n-Turf burger (Kobe beef topped with a lobster sauce) but the Champions burger and the Lynchburg burger are excellent as well.
I wish I could tell you what the salads taste like, but I'm a beef kind of guy. I can tell you that the salads are presented beautifully.
Soups are an amazing surpise at a burger joint - they are the best soups I've had anywhere. Lobster or shrimp bisgue, brocolli and cheese, mushroom, etc. -- all are wonderful.
If you have room left, try the deserts. The cheesecake is one of the best in San Antonio and their specialty deserts for different occassions (raspberry-chocolate sliders for Valentine's) are worth coming for by themselves.
The only drawback to the GBG is how far away it is - we try to go every week, but it's a good 30 minute drive each way for us.

Loved everything except the bland veggie patty
by M.S. (1 review)
March 26, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I have been a vegetarian since 2002 so I like to know where I can get a great veggie burger. I also don't mind paying a little extra for good quality food. I was very excited to eat at GBG based on the rave online reviews and a personal recommendation made by a colleague.
Imagine my surprise when I received a bland (completely unseasoned), obviously store-bought (pre-processed), dry, 'veggie' patty in the veggie burger. I was only able to take a few bites, and (other than not eating meat) I am *not* a picky eater. I didn't see any vegetables mixed into the soy patty so it should not be called a 'veggie' patty. I am very sorry to sound like a whiner, but a bland, dry patty is not 'gourmet' quality. GBG should try to offer a black bean burger which is definitely a 'step up' from a plain soy patty. For example, the Morningstar black bean burgers in the grocery store freezer section are more flavorful than the GBG veggie patty I was served. Chili's and Red Robin restaurants offer fairly good black bean burgers (as long as it's not over cooked, a common problem).
To GBG's credit, there were pieces of fresh mango, red peppers, and fresh spinach layered on top of the patty, sandwiched between a fresh whole wheat bun. All of that looked wonderful! But the dry, bland patty ruined the whole ensemble.
The onion rings were very good, obviously homemade, and I really liked the french fries. The unsweetened tea was very refreshing.
The seats were comfortable, and I enjoyed the music playing overhead. The staff was very friendly.
Overall, I loved everything except the veggie patty, which, to be honest, I hated.
I decided to leave a review because (1) I wanted to warn other vegetarians that the veggie patty may be sub-par, and (2) I would love for GBG to re-think the veggie patty that they offer in their restaurants. Vegetarians can be loyal customers. I always want to see local businesses do well, and I would love for the owner to upgrade the plain soy vegetarian patty. Then I would gladly re-try a new GBG veggie burger.
