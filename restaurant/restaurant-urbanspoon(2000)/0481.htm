Mama Carolla's
by Mariposa (59 reviews)
January 20, 2010 - Likes it
I'm so glad I finally tried this place. The decor is very pretty and romantic, and it's definitely a great date spot. We started with the bruschetta, which was topped with huge chunks of tomato and piles of fresh shredded mozzarella, but I was even more impressed with the shrimp cocktail. It was REALLY spicy... not quite St. Elmo's level, but almost. I had the chicken rigatoni, and I can still taste that crispy pancetta and spicy white wine sauce - highly recommended. Of course, I couldn't leave an Italian restaurant without ordering the tiramisu, and I'm glad I did. It wasn't the best I've had in Indy, but it was still quite good. All of the portions were large and came with soup or salad, and the super crunchy bread was hot and yummy. I was shocked to see the bill - it was extremely affordable, even including several glasses of wine. I will definitely return and am pleased to support a local restaurant instead of the dreaded Olive Garden.

I'm Neutral.
by Emily J. Auth (25 reviews)
April 20, 2010
I took a rather large group to Mama Carolla's on a busy night, yet our waiter was excellent, attentive and professional.
Despite initial positives, I am reluctant to recommend Mama Carolla's to any serious foodie. I was expecting fresh, made-in-house pasta and was sourly disappointed. Also, there was a significant disparity in portion size. These two reasons should not stop the curious from trying it out, but you should be aware before you go.
We had a rather large party so I was fortunate enough to sample a variety of the fare; my Veal Medallions, my husband's Chicken Involtine, my friend's Chicken Picatta, my mom's Beef Lasgna, my dad's Pork Chop.
The waiter warned me that the creamy gorgonzola sauce for my medallions would be very heavy, and he was not kidding. The dish was very large and I was able to eat only half of it, and at $19 it was what I was expecting. I wasn't terribly impressed with the medallions themselves, and unfortunately the sauce was slightly bland. I was left regretting my choice but was glad I was able to sample from everyone else.
The Chicken Picatta was very good and was the right portion size for $15. The sauteed herbs and capers were a perfect mix with the light, lemony sauce. My husband's Chicken Involtine, also for $15 was excellent. However, it was very VERY small, we couldn't get enough of it. There were 3 minuscule pieces of yummy prosciutto, spinach and cheese stuffed chicken breasts and a tiny nest of angel hair pasta as an afterthought on the plate. The herbed champagne tomato cream sauce was amazing, and it was regrettable that the portion itself was so small.
The Lasagna was very good, as was the amusingly named "Wow, Whata Pork Chop" ordered by my dad. It seemed an odd choice but he was very enthusiastic about it. I very much enjoyed the slightly spicy sauce that had a slow, sweet start and a fiery finish.
After the meal we were anxious to try the Tiramisu. It was a great size and a good finish but didn't quite have enough zest to it for my personal taste. The recipe could stand to be tweaked a tad, but it was lovely otherwise.
One serious high point was the bread. We could have eaten several loaves of it, and we happily used it to soak up the remainder of that excellent creamy herbed tomato sauce from the Involtine.
We enjoyed our time at Mama Carolla's, as the atmosphere was very nice and we were in good company. However, I doubt I will be back. With the offerings of restaurants in Indianapolis that serve real, quality fresh pasta and authentic fare, it is just too hard to compete.

Foodies Beware: When In Indianapolis, Stick To Steak
by JLo (1 review)
April 28, 2010 - Doesn't like it
In Indiana on business, and looking for a diversion from red meat, I took a chance on this highly rated Indianapolis restaurant. Big mistake.
First came my appetizer: shrimp cocktail. The cocktail sauce, so saturated with horseradish, was downright nuclear. The color was closer to white than red, and the slightest dab brought tears. That left the shrimp to be eaten naked: they weren't up for the task, over-steamed to the point of tasting boiled. Ugh.
Next came the beef lasagna; a risky order, I know. And I received just what I feared: a bowl of salty mush. No layered texture, no layered flavor, just salty, microwaved mush. I might as well have been biting into a Big Mac.
Can I say anything positive about the place? My Myers and Coke did in fact arrive with Myers rum. And the bread was freshly baked and delicious.
On my departure, I was surprised to see that the restaurant had gotten crowded. And that brought to mind a pretentious truth: this is what passes for Italian food in the Midwest. If you're in town from either coast, just stick to steak.

Mama's A Solid Staple in Broadripple
by Shep (6 reviews)
January 31, 2009 - Likes it
When returning to Central Indiana, EVERYONE we met in the Broadripple-Meridian Kessler area recommended Mama Carolla's.  That is a pretty big build up and Mama's didn't disappoint.
I would say this is a frequent haunt of ours, even after the arrival of our little dude (now 18mos old).
On a recent visit, I had been a bad mommy and pushed dinner into the realm of near melt down.  We were seated at 7pm and little guy was starving.  Fearing that we may be lynched as that annoying family that should just keep their poor toddler at home, I told the server that I would need a kids size penne pasta with Alfredo stat.  God bless this poor woman (it is safe to say she does not have kids) she was a little flustered at our not following the usual drinks-apps-entree routine.  She returned from the kitchen and said that they did not offer Alfredo in a kids portion and I (in a near panic) said "THEN JUST BRING AN ADULT PORTION--I DON"T CARE WHAT THE COST IS!!!"  She jumped on it and quickly delivered a piping hot bowl of creamy deliciousness.  Little man shoveled THE ENTIRE PORTION down his gullet (like I said Mom of the Year had let him run down to empty) and then started in on some of the artichoke appetizers we were sharing.
The rest of the dinner was incredible and my son's hair and skin were definitely treated to an Alfredo mask treatment.  GOOD TIMES.
The only nit-picky gripe I have (and this is true for a lot of places) is that it gets a little loud when it is crowded even though the dining room is really broken up into lots of little rooms.
Bottom line-go to this place, support independents.  This city needs them.
