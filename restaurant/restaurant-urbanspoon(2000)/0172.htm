Worst Brunch in Charleston!
by frannybnanny (16 reviews)
September 15, 2009 - Doesn't like it
We decided on Oak for brunch, since it is considered one of the best restaurants in Charleston and wanted to change it up a bit. Boy were we disappointed! When we arrived they had just opened and the hostess had us sit at the bar to wait, while they prepared the tables. The bartender came over to ask if we wanted drinks, and since we were about to be seated we opted not to. The bartender gave us ATTITUDE, even though bar was empty! After being seated by our hostess (who was wearing flip flops), we were barely greeted by our waitress, who obviously did NOT want to be there. I ordered the eggs benedict, thinking it was a safe bet for brunch. 2 of my guests ordered the french toast and a side of hash browns, and my husband ordered the crab benedict. My eggs were the size of quail eggs, served on the thinnest english muffins I have ever seen and both were grossly overcooked not to mention the lack of hollandaise. The potatoes that came with my eggs were BURNT and unseasoned, it seems like they gave me the crumbs that remained from the bottom of the fryer. The french toast, which boasts the Chef's name, tasted nothing more than bread covered in cinammon. The only redeeming quality of my husband's eggs benedict were the crab cakes as his eggs were also grossly overcooked and were lacking hollandaise. Over all, this was the worst brunch experience I've had, and at one of the top rated restaurants nonetheless. If you're looking for a good place to brunch, skip the pretentious Oak and hit up Joseph's down the road, Fat Hen, or even Triangle!

First Class Service & Outstanding Food
by sylcart (1 review)
July 03, 2009 - Really likes it
We walked by at 12noon and a nice lady named Michelle greeted us and invited us to dinner that night. She had a great personality and made us feel welcome. They were setting the restaurant up for dinner but the whole place was filled with incredible aromas. We made a reservation and went back to the hotel. When we arrived later for dinner, Michelle greeted us again and led us to our table. Our waiter Jon was very attentive without being overbearing and also made us feel welcome. This is why we love Charleston so much -- we have never been treated like a tourist. I was trying to eat healthy by ordering the Sesame Tuna with avocado slices- which was delicious- but we couldn't resist sharing the Lobster Macaroni & Cheese. It was decadent and out of this world!! My husband got the Bacon wrapped Filet Mignon and said it literally melted in his mouth it was so tender. There was a really good Balsamic BBQ sauce that I wish I had the recipe for - it had the perfect tangy BBQ flavor. As we were enjoying dinner, Michael,the manager stopped by our table to make sure everything was good and to see if we needed anything. We didn't have room for dessert but we will definitely be back for more the next time we are in Charleston. I highly recommend Oak Steakhouse to anyone visiting Charleston and looking for a great meal with great service!! Oh and the free valet parking was a nice touch too.
