We're Regulars at Little Saigon
by College Park Omnivore (162 reviews)
November 26, 2008 - Really likes it
We regularly eat at Little Saigon. The prices are fantastic and this is the best place for delicious soup. Their Pho Ga - chicken noodle soup vietnamese style - is the perfect soup for anytime. It's a light clear broth with slices of chicken, rice noodles, onions, roasted garlic and cilantro. It's a huge portion, but don't fret - take the rest home and it is delicious left overs.
They have many soup variations to choose from - some with seafood, some with beef, some with chicken and either rice or egg noodles. They also have many non-soup dishes and those are fantastic as well.
Their Spring and Summer rolls are a must. One is fried and one is not (I always forget which is which) but order one of each and you'll fall in love. The fried rolls come with fish sauce - a sweet sauce that my 6 year old neice loves (read-ANYONE will like this sauce). The one that's not fried is served with peanut sauce which is quite sweet. Add hot sauce to it to add spice and tone down the sweetness (read below for descriptions of the on-table sauces).
There are three sauces on the table - mix and match them with any dish. One is a full red sauce - it's HOT. One is a red sauce with seeds - also hot but tasty (goes very well with the peanut sauce mentioned above). The last is a thick sweet plum sauce - very tasty.
Another must, is the Vietnamese Iced Coffee. This drink will get you going and keep you going for the entire day. It's very strong and also sweet - DELICIOUS!
If you like tea, their tea is great (and CHEAP). The also have fresh "limeade" which is awesome.
We rarely order dessert - we're usually too full. But go with the more traditional vietnamese desserts - they're fresher.
You will LOVE this place! :)

What am I missing?
by wokanni (3 reviews)
November 16, 2009 - Doesn't like it
This is the best Vietnamese Orlando has to offer? We were looking for a good meal and were sorely dissapointed. On the plus side: it is clean, the waitstaff during dinner was attentive (not a usual feature of a busy ethnic eatery), the price was good, and the service was brisk. The negatives: the Bun Bo Hue (specialized soup from the central portion of Vietnam) was completely lackluster and probably the worst I have ever had from a restaurant - I could have gotton more flavor from a bowl of crayons with hot water dumped over it. Even the wrong condiments were served with the dish. The pho tai bo vien was similar - it was missing the usual anise/cinnamon/clove flavor that usually accents the aroma of the dish; the broth was watered down and bland with no real beef flavor whatsoever. Even the dish that many of the other diners have touted as their highlight - the "summer rolls" (goi cuon) were small, with maybe a quarter-shrimp for each roll, and had the additive of crispy fried onions - far overpowering the delicate herbs found within. We ordered these twice just to make sure it was not a mistake, and it came the same way. The fried egg rolls (cha gio) were bland and soggy. The bubble teas were nearly inedible, even for the small children accompanying us.
The best dish we ordered was the rice and sauteed pork. It had good flavor and was well-cooked.
Based on our experience here, if you are into very mediocre Vietnamese food or have little to no experience with this cuisine, it probably comes across as OK. If you are looking for the depth and flavor true Vietnamese cuisine has to offer, this is not the place for it.
