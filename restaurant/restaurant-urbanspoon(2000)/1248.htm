Cajun Cafe Laissez les Bon Temps Roulez
by frick (4 reviews)
December 02, 2008 - Really likes it
Cajun Cafe has been one of the best kept secrets in Pinellas Park. Don't let the proximity to the flea market or the number of Harleys in the parking lot scare you. The Thibodeaux family from Thibodeaux, Louisianna (seriously) bring a slice of the bayou to our Long Bayou - Google Map it, Long Bayou! So, now that I have described the inherent ambiance, can I please describe Gator Piquante (pronounced PEE-can). Flavorful and spicy without the toiletpaper-in-the-fridge heat. I prefer a dash of Tabasco with the jalapeno on the side. The beer selection is world class and be sure to save room for the bread pudding with just the right amount of rum flavor. The place unfortunatly is no longer kid friendly. They are no longer amicable towards feeding the catfish that hang out at their docks. Lighten up Frances...Do they realize that there is no law against feeding fish? Have they been to any tourist restaurant in the Keys? At the end of the day , you have to decide between good Cajun food and anal Cajun managers...if you don't bring kids, it's a no brainer, but if you have kids, have fun, but watch out for the Nazi regime by the water.
