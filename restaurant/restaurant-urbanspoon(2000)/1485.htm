I'll definitely be back...
by Spin H. (1 review)
July 21, 2010 - Likes it
I decided to visit Sazon for the first time today (I work right by it but have never tried it) since it was my birthday and I wanted to treat myself.
A treat I certainly got. I was quickly greeted by a gentleman (manager? owner?) and sat at a small table. I was immediately greeted by a friendly server and given a basket of bread with a spicy green salsa-esque sauce. After looking through the menu, I decided on the lunch portion of the Masas de Cerdo (pork chunks, fried and served with a citrus sauce and sauteed onion). The portion size was pretty good, with the main course, yellow rice, a pot of beans, and a few slices of fried plantain. The food was very tasty and the items on the plate provided nice counterpoints to one another.
When I was finished I decided to go for broke and order a slice of Tres Leches cake in honor of the day of my birth. When I mentioned it, the server prepped my cake and then gave it to me on the house... I wasn't expecting that at all, and was happily surprised. The cake was really good, and the price was quite reasonable. I'll definitely be coming back, and bringing friends!

Don't waste your money
by BonnieT (3 reviews)
August 11, 2010 - Doesn't like it
We have eaten at PLENTY of Cuban restaurants and this one is not good. The Ceviche which is 'cooked' in lime juice was under cooked. The fish was raw (meaning I could literally see what parts of the fish were cooked in lime and which parts were raw) and the other mixed seafood was tough. The flavor of the ceviche was not good either. Both of our entrees were just ok. I'm sure people who dont regularly eat Cuban food probably can't tell the difference but this food really didnt have much flavor. The sangria was not bad, however Sangria usually has cut up fruit, they put fruit cocktail (out of the can) in the sangria! I'm interested to see how long this place will survive in a city that has tons of great restaurants.
