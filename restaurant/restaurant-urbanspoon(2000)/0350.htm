Every burrito starts with the tortilla
by slakingfool (33 reviews)
July 31, 2010 - Likes it
Compare a burrito from Bandit Burrito to one made by El Rey in West Des Moines. The tortillas at Bandit Burrito are heated in a press for a short period, while the tortillas at El Rey go on the grill and literally cook until dry on the outside. I enjoy the fillings at both Bandit and El Rey, but the tortilla at Bandit is on the gummy side while El Rey's has a drier bite that gives way as you bite and doesn't stick to your teeth like Bandit's.
That said, the vegetarian chorizo and the wild rice at Bandit rocks.

mobile review
by Ron (1 review)
December 07, 2009 - Doesn't like it
Honest review. I don't doubt that this place may be good when the food is fresh, but how often is that? They don't get very busy, so the food has time to get terrible. I had nachos. My chips were stale and bitter, rice was overcooked and smelled burned. The fajita veggies were halfway black and crunchy. The corn salsa must have been bad because it smelled like ethanol, the cheese sauce was like a paste and tasted like brocolli, and the chicken was dry and rubbery like it had been left overnight and reheated that morning. I ate at 11am. Being a restaurant that dies most of it's business at lunch time, it seems they have a long way to go to catch up to it's competition. Also, the person with me got a vegan burrito. The owner made both of our meals after arranging beef on the previous customer's burrito. He didn't change his gloves before moving on to the vegan burrito. He also reached into the cheese bin with beef on his gloves. I won't recommend this shop to anyone unless you're looking for mediocracy.

Great Real Food
by Matthew (1 review)
March 22, 2010 - Likes it
I probably would have moved on when looking at these reviews except I saw the other two most recent reviews and couldn't let them go unanswered. The variety of food far surpasses any other burrito shop. The authenticity of the food is beyond compare. The beef is unmatched anywhere. Beware: Queso that doesn't come from a can, may have cheese in it. Delicious. Free Veggies and sour cream and cheese with the base burrito: It is heaven.
When anyone is positive towards Panchero's It becomes hard to believe anything else they say. Of all the chains, they are by far the most bland, have the most limited choices and charge the most for items like sour cream and veggies which come free at Bandit. Chipotle is good, Qdoba better, Bandit better than both, and Panchero's...come on...did you really eat the food or did you just chew and swallow?
