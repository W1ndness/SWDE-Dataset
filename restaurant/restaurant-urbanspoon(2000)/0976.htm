Best Pizza in the 'Burgh.
by ERiC AiXeLsyD (109 reviews)
August 21, 2008 - Really likes it
You can't miss all the pizza shops in all the little neighborhoods around town... they all have their fans... they all claim to be the best. Well, in my book... Aiello's is the best. Usually all I get here is the normal pizza and a little jug of Turner's iced tea. Toppings could be pepperoni, green peppers, any combo of the above. The sauce is sweet, and can't be touched. It's worth trying to find a damn place to park over there.
I've had the white pizza, and the meatball sub... both can't touch the normal pie with its sweet tomato sauce that graces the crust that somehow manages to be crispy & doughy all at once.
They call my father-in-law "Anchovy Bobbie" because that's all he ever gets on his. My mother-in-law swears by their salad dressing (which you can buy by the bottle if so inclined).
I got yelled at by Joe once because I was 15 min. later than expected when I called for a pick up because of traffic from Oakland. Ha ha ha.
If you're in Squirrel Hill, this is the place to get Pizza. If you're not in Squirrel Hill... You need to drive to here to get Pizza.
(Exceptions can be made... maybe... for Beto's or Larry's & Carol's.)

Best Pizza outside of NYC
by Kurt Johnson (12 reviews)
August 15, 2009 - Likes it
So my wife and i just moved here from NYC. Out first project was to make a list of all the restaurants in town, and find the best that could replace out NYC haunts. Aiello's takes the prize. Service...

Best Pizza in Pittsburgh
by Jennifer Stanley (17 reviews)
June 21, 2010 - Really likes it
I live in Squirrel Hill, I can't understand how Mineo's even competes with Aiello's. Aiello's pizza tastes like your Italian grandparents made it for you. Mineo's is just greasy and covered with tons of cheese. I've tried several varieties of pizza at Aiello's, but I have to say, the white pizza blew me away. The best part was that I actually didn't have to pick the tomatoes off, they were delicious! Their bread sticks are also excellent. I'm dying to try to their other food (e.g. chicken parm), but I just can't get over the pizza.

Huge variety, good pizza
by Cassie (30 reviews)
May 27, 2010 - Likes it
Every time I go in here planning on getting a couple plain slices, the huge menu makes me crave something more exciting. I've heard their pizza compared as a "healthy version of Mineo's" which I kind of agree with. There is less grease and cheese and more sauce, but it's still not "healthy" by any means. The pizza is good, but I think some days it is better than others. The veggie rolls are good too, but greasy enough that I can barely finish a whole one. I've tried the pierogie pizza before which I thought was a bit overpriced ($12 for a small) and left me slightly disappointed. It was original but didn't have as much flavor as I would have liked. I decided to stay away from the gourmet pizzas, even though I'm sure the others are good, and stick to their good ol' plain slices when I stop by. Those never leave my disappointed.
