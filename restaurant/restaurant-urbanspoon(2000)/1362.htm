Mediocrity Rules at this Overrated Rip-Off
by Drexler McStyles (206 reviews)
September 06, 2009 - Doesn't like it
Went here on Saint Patricks Day. Only because its right down the street from my house. I'd been here once before and had a Burger for $8+ that was gross. While the Beef was lean (too lean, a decent burger should be made from 85/15 beef. Anything leaner is no good for a burger) it was obviously a frozen, processed, machine formed patty.
This time I went with my wife. We each ordered a beer and she ordered the Chicken Sandwich ($7.50). I had the Fish and Chips ($8.25). It was not yet busy but it took ten minutes to get our beers, when we did get them my wifes was room temperature and the girl did not bring the lemon she had asked for. I ordered a Guinness ($5) which was brought to me in a 10 ounce paper coffee cup.
The food shows up and I cannot believe how small my pieces of Fish are. They reminded me of something you would get out of a HungryMan Dinner from Wal-Mart. 3 tiny, grotesque pieces. I'm not sure what kind of Fish it was supposed to be but it tasted very fishy, so probably nothing decent. Half my Fries were undercooked. My wife said the Chicken Sandwich tasted fine but it was very small. $35 plus tip for a bunch of garbage.
The popularity of this place bewilders me. An Irish bar in name only, it has no real personality or character other than the sterilized/polished atmosphere they are obviously aiming for. If matching patio umbrellas are more important to you than good food then you might like O'Niell's. Otherwise steer FAR clear of this overly hyped place.

Nice Place
by Chris Iller (48 reviews)
January 08, 2010 - Likes it
I've been to this Pub a couple of times. First time was just for some drinks with friends. But I went for the food on my last visit. Did lunch there.
I had the Philly Chicken Sandwich. It was good. The place has a nice atmosphere, and looks slightly upscale. Nice & Clean. Everybody else in my party seemed to enjoy their lunch too (they were the ones that recommended it as a lunch stop). Some of the food had a Celtic theme. But it was pretty standard stuff.
One of the few places in the Knob Hill area (although it's on the outskirts) that have ample parking.
I say, give them a shot.
