mobile review
by Captain Napkin (284 reviews)
April 12, 2010 - Likes it
We stopped here for a late Sunday brunch. The weather was perfect, so there was a line waiting to sit outside. We settled for a table by the window... Great for people watching in this pedestrian friendly neighborhood. My French toast was extra thick cut, and needed to be carved before eating. Nevertheless it was tasty, and the sausage links that came with were good, with a mild flavor. The "hashbrowns" were actually home fries, and were also enjoyable. My friend raved about his hamburger, saying it was the best place in the area to get a good burger. The atmosphere is neighborhood cafe, the wait staff all younger, attired in black, and competent enough. Our meal was paced slow enough to allow for ample conversation time, which is what brunch time dining is all about. I will say that the inside noise level is rather high. Prices are fair. All in all a nice place to enjoy a leisurely meal.

Missed Opportunity
by Trip Klaus (109 reviews)
September 03, 2009
I was tempted here to the Del Ray neighborhood by the idea that Evening Star was one of the few places with "retail" wine pricing in the DC area. Thanks to the wine shop next door one can choose a bottle at retail price and have it opened in Evening Star for only $10 more. Anyone who is tired of paying 3 times the retail price for wine in a restaurant can only revel in the opportunity.
Unfortunately, the menu and restaurant don't match the idea. Evening Star is a perfectly fine neighborhood local with wooden booths and a friendly staff. The menu is comfort, but only comfort. I was talked into the fried chicken sandwich with jalapeno mac and cheese. Not my norm but highly recommended. The bacon infused greens were just wet enough to turn the biscuit into a mush and the mac and cheese (can we please end this trend) had only a hint of pepper.
I'm not ready to write the place off, however I don't recommend a special trip. If you are in Del Ray it's a nice tavern to stop in, but not fine dining.

Del Ray Fave
by Greggndc (75 reviews)
May 08, 2009 - Likes it
On a rainy Sunday afternoon, two of my friends and I braved the crowds at Evening Star for brunch. The wait for a table - 15 minutes, was about right… although DC types forget that bars in Virginia are still pretty smoky (cough). A hunky waiter seated us at a booth and we were presented with fruit bread - (Banana? Apple? Whatever it was, we found it delicious!) and a nice Sunday beverage - a bloody Mary for me - spicy but not hot. I opted for an omelet - well seasoned and nicely cooked with sweet-salty ham and cheddar. The egg salad wrap a companion tried had my eyebrows raised. Really? As a wrap? He said that the egg salad was very nicely prepared but messy to eat. The salad greens and grilled chicken the third guest selected lookd nice but didn't excite me. Pretty but dull. This is a great neighborhood place with a loyal following. The many meals I have enjoyed here have never disappointed and on some vists had me singing the Cafe's praises. The vibe is enervating, even in the smoky bar. The service is well intentioned and fortunately rarely rushes guests to turn the table. A reliable and favored spot in Del Ray.

Just "ok".
by Quik (4 reviews)
March 15, 2010 - Doesn't like it
On its face, Evening Star Cafe looked like a nice place to have dinner, but it was quite loud and the food was just "ok". Part of the reason why we went here was because I heard about the chef's interest in creating healthy meals for kids. Well, out of all the dishes the kid's meal was the mediocre at best.
I had the with roasted rockfish with roasted caramel sauce that I thought was interesting and quite tasty. Overall, I thought my dish was very good, the fish was well seasoned and cooked perfectly. I also liked presentation of the dish.
My fiance had the grilled beef tenderloin with the garlic mashed potatoes. The beef looked great, but it lacked flavor.
His nephew had a kid's lasagna that was on the garlicky side. I had a bite and it tasted like it sat in a baking pan for hours. I've made lasagna before and I think it always tastes better the day after its been baked but this one needed to be scrapped altogether.
Overall, I thought the quality didn't reflect the prices of this restaurant. I really expected better.
