Great For Scenesters, Bad For Sushi
by x y (2 reviews)
May 10, 2010 - Doesn't like it
It may be a great night-spot, but Sticky Rice is a bad restaurant. It belongs to a particular class of establishments that make a pretentious claim to ethnic authenticity by overwhelming their customers with chic atmosphere and persuasively worded menu, but utterly fail to deliver on the promise created by both.
The only way this formula works is if you can manage to get your customers drunk by the time the food comes. This is actually something that Sticky Rice does well. Most of the twenty-something patrons who have kept this place in business against all culinary odds are there to hang out with their friends, have a few drinks, and check dinner off the list.
If you are looking for a hip spot for late night tater tots and California rolls, Sticky Rice is perfect. If you are looking for good sushi, this isn't it.

by Rob (1 review)

The tater tots are fabulous and the sushi (vegetarian) is alright, but Sticky Rice is mismanaged beyond belief. The hipster wannabe hostesses, if you can call them that, epitomize ineptitude and self absorption. Bad quality
by Krystal (41 reviews)
November 29, 2009 - Doesn't like it
I've been to Sticky Rice twice. The first time I would have given it three stars. The second time, I would have given it one. So I settled for a rating of two.
The first time I went I ordered the "Asian Garden" salad. It was good quality and a decent portion size. They didn't skimp on the shrimp, either, wich is a definite plus. It wasn't the best salad I ever had by any means, but it was solid in quality.
The second trip was a complete disaster. I once again ordered the "Asian Garden" salad and it was TERRIBLE. The vegetables were all wilted and the shrimp was flavorless. I complained to the waitress who offered to remove the salad from our bill. I decided to order the "Starter Combo" sushi as a replacement. Also terrible! I should have known- we were sitting at the sushi bar and the fish in the case didn't look too terribly appetizing but I figured I'd try it anyway (bad idea). For starters, they were out of two types of fish, so I had to replace them with shrimp. When the sushi arrived my skepticism grew even more...it just didn't look fresh. Still, I dove in. Like I said, bad idea. It was actually repulsive to eat. After one bite, I gave up.
If you're looking for sushi in Richmond, find yourself an authentic place. This place is only good for the tater tots.
