Better than average
by dondk (32 reviews)
April 02, 2010 - Likes it
Visited this establishment a few days ago, very clean, nicely laid out, with a decent variety.
I thought the quality was better than average, and the selection was varied. My wife was disappointed the only fried rice they offered has shrimp (she is not a seafood fanatic), but they had enough of non seafood to keep her pleased.
A note on the table tells you that if you take too much and end up wasting it, they can charge you an additional $8. While I like the concept, the wife was less than enthusiastic on trying things, fearing if she did not like it she would be on the tab for the additional charge. Everything I tried I thought was pretty good.
Sushi, yes not the largest selection compared to other buffets, but nevertheless more than enough that no one should have an issue on finding something they really like.
Now the things I did not like about here. On the carving station there was duck (surprisingly) but not much was left and after 45 minutes, the paltry amount was still there. The flounder and a shrimp dish that did not move looked like it had been sitting there for hours (not appetizing). The items that moved they replenished quickly, the items that didn't just sat. This took away from the buffet, not enough for me not to come back, but tells me they don't monitor the stations as closely as they should.

Good asian buffet
by pittfoodie (21 reviews)
August 05, 2010 - Likes it
This place is great to get stuffed with a lot of different types of Asian food. The quality is decent, but definitely not top notch. However, given the price ($17 per person, and that includes your non-alcoholic drinks and dessert), I think it is very reasonable. Staff are attentive and courteous. There is also a section of food for those not liking Asian food - fried chicken, pizza, etc, a hibachi type area where you choose your meats and veggies to be stir fried, and sushi. The sushi/sashimi is decent - and some I can tell were frozen, but still good. Hey, it is a buffet after all, and it will be tons cheaper if you fill up on sushi here. There are also typical chinese dishes like lo mein and such, and a few more exotic things like kim-chi and chicken feet. Overall, a fun and clean buffet, in a very nice atmosphere.

Better than expected, but be choosy--
by VLiu (1 review)
September 13, 2009
Background:
I'm a first generation Chinese who loves seafood and most asian cuisine. I'm also a student, so I'm also looking for the most efficient use of my dollar.
Review:
I tried mostly everything except the fried dishes, and for the most part I was pleasantly surprised. The seafood's texture, both cooked and raw items (i.e. raw clam, crawfish, crab legs, sashimi) appeared fresh,\.
However, in some items (i.e. some crawfish and the steamed clams) there was a VERY UNNERVING scent and taste that mimicked naphthalene (mothballs). If you tried some of the seafood without adding cocktail sauce, lemon, or wasabi, you might also taste this.
The hot dishes were hits or misses, but I was very surprised by the deliciousness of some of the savory and sweet dumplings.
The service is attentive, but seems very depressed.
It's a great bargain, but given the chemical smell and taste to some of the seafood, I'm not sure I'll return.
