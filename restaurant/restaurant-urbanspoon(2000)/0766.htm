dammit!
by 23451bum (34 reviews)
May 18, 2009 - Doesn't like it
I SO wanted Kin's Wok to be great. Most of the take out Chinese places in Hampton Roads are in a race to the bottom. It seems the winning formula is use the price of the order as the numerator, and the weight in pounds as the denominator -- lowest number wins! The result is undistinguished food, and LOTS of rice.
So, Sunday evening, and the DW says, how about Chinese take out? I quickly think of all of the "Best of ..." banners outside Kin's and think they are perhaps the secret to the missing link -- like maybe one place earns their spurs by consistently delivering good food? Hope springs eternal, huh? Like people say about second marriages -- the triumph of hope over experience. So, thanks to Mdawg, looked at the online menu, ordered two shrimp eggrolls, cashew chicken for DW, "spicy" shredded beef, and white rice. Pick it up (wow, is this heavy!), and head home.
Egg rolls -- should have been advertised as shredded cabbage egg rolls, since a shrimp wouldn't have been found dead in there. Yeech.
"Spicy shredded beef" -- who are they fooling? Yes, the beef was shredded, and it was very tender. The veggies were excellent -- lighly stir fried green bell pepper, carrot shreds, and bamboo shoots. Problem is, the sauce tasted like beef broth -- couple of tablespoons of my emergency supply of chili sauce with garlic got it "spicy". But why is this a paint by numbers exercise?
Cashew chicken -- good, short on the chicken, long on the vegetables. And again, no flavor.
Every place can have a bad night -- went back for the lunch kung pao chicken, Same song, third verse. Plenty of tasteless food.
I mean, come on. Kin's advertises Schezuan and Hunan dishes. Those are genres built on bold, distinctive flavors. That concept is lost here. They have been at it a long time, and probably know their market better than I do. There is obviously a market for the price/pound formula. It just won't be with my dollars (NB -- in a ridiculous move for 2009, Kin's doesn't take credit cards, so yes, it is DOLLARS!).

Best fast food Chinese in Norfolk.
by Jim Gitman (50 reviews)
February 16, 2010 - Likes it
Kin's Wok has become indispensible for local vegetarians, but anyone who enjoys Chinese prepared in the fast food syle will enjoy this restaurant. There is a large dining room, but most of their business seems to be takeout. Service is somewhat slow, but very friendly. The main drawing point is that the menu offers more variety than usual, and each dish is actually a distinct preparation. I particularly enjoy their orange flavor tofu and kung pao vegetables. Unlike many Chinese takeout places, you can actually differentiate between (for example) szechuan and hunan sauces.
Beware, though: they don't accept credit cards.

it's chinese fast food!
by Jen Otis (2 reviews)
July 05, 2010 - Likes it
This place is not some high-end fancy-schmancy place so complaints against eggroll wrappers not being handmade is ridiculous. Go find some high-end dim-sum place somewhere else. Even though it's been professed it's not as good as it used to be, it's still better than the other restaurants serving similar fare. My favorite is the house special lo-mein. The noodles are the perfect diameter and color and the sauce is not too sweet. There was a really good portion of meats and vegetables mixed in. Yes, it was a little greasy but lo-mein is a PAN FRIED noodle. I happen to like the lo-mein a little greasy, it's delicious. I would have been dissapointed otherwise. As for the cash only rule, I think it's great they can get away from being under the thumbs of credit card companies and I don't personally feel so inconvenienced by this. There are a number of chinese places in this area and I only crave the lo-mein from here. I will glady go to one of the hundreds of ATMs in Ghent and get cash to pay for the greasy, flavorful deliciousness.
