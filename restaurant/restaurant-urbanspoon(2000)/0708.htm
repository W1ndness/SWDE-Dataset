July 03, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I did not enjoy my experience at Barrio as I thought I would. The patio seating was nice, but crowded in a tightly-confined space, and noisy. The service was slow, but helpful. The "gringo" taco that I had couldn't have been a more standard hard-shell taco, but costed $4. I could have eaten about 3 more, but you can do the math there. The mobile truck they park at large events & festivals was terrible. They had inconsistent pricing between the order sheet they had the customer and the signage, and when asked about it, the individual in the truck was very rude. Those "Gringo" tacos were very different than the 1 I had in the restaurant: much smaller, far fewer & cheaper quality ingredients. I am consistently unhappy with my Barrio experiences, and will not be revisiting without some major changes taking place.

A fun night out
by Carrie (4 reviews)
August 23, 2009 - Likes it
The two of us went to Barrio to celebrate my birthday. We had a leisurely dinner enjoying several of the small tapas plates. The seafood ceviche, potato sopas, and guacamole were especially good. A lot of the dishes had a fresh, lively taste and it was fun to order a bit of lots of things to try. We ordered too much, though--I think 4 or 5 plates between us would have been plenty.
And the drinks were great, too. I had a fabulous Old Cuban mojito and we both enjoyed some aged tequila as a bit of a digestif. You're not going to find this variety of Reposados and Añejos, so I'd encourage anyone to try one.
I feel like a bit of an old fogey saying this, but when we first arrived I thought it was a bit dark inside and the music was a bit too loud, but accepted that as the character of place--it is a bar, after all--and enjoyed it for what it was. Not a quiet, romantic place, but a fun, boisterous place for a night out.
