by Grant W. (1 review)
January 02, 2009 - Really likes it
Let's face it. The greater Tulsa area is awash in Mexican restaurants, with varying degrees of "authenticity." Odds are you live no more than a few blocks from one of these family-owned places. When Mr. Mambo opened up in Broken Arrow at the site of the old Pepper's Grill at 101st and Elm a month ago, I'll admit I was skeptical.
Upon entering the place, I wasn't immediately struck by anything. It doesn't have the pompous grandiosity of Los Cabos, for one. Everything about it screams family-owned. The decorations on the walls are somewhat sparse, but everything about the inside is a little muted. This isn't a bad thing at all. I've been to far too many Mexican restaurants that were a little too much like a family museum. In the low-key and stylish tones of blue and Southwest patterns, it keeps your focus on your company and on the food.
What does pride taste like? If you take the exact same meal prepared by two different chefs, but one had more love for his craft, would you taste the difference in the end? My money says yes. In Mr. Mambo's cuisine, we see a refreshing return to the basics. My benchmark for any Mexican fare is the fajitas, as it's the perfect portion size for dinner for two. At Mambo's you're presented with homemade authentic tortillas that are a lot thinner than one's you'd find other places. Believe me when I tell you this is a good thing. The smaller and thinner homemade tortilla allows the simply prepared and seasoned fresh ingredients to shine. So many other fajitas are like a huge tortilla and meat sandwich, there is really no adequate written way to describe the perfect balance of flavors brought on by the consumption of a Mr. Mambo's fajita. This is Mexican food the way it should be prepared.
Their approach to traditionalism doesn't end on the plate either. Whoever is tending bar back there has a real reverence for the history and tradition of Latin American spirits and mixed drinks. Their cocktails in large part eschew the current trend of overly complicated sensationalized chain-restaurant drinks and offer an invigorating return to simple perfection. Absolutely fantastic selection of classically prepared drinks that are all reasonably priced. If Ernest Hemingway was alive in Green Country today, he'd be swapping fish stories over a Mojito at Mr. Mambo's.
I've been on a long search for the perfect Mexican restaurant in the area, and it's been a long and exhausting search. I've found some places that are are very good but came up short in one way or another. With its exceptionally friendly service, fantastic selection of mixed drinks, and extremely well-balanced food preparation, Mr. Mambo's has effectively ended my search. What we have here in Broken Arrow at 101st and Elm is nothing less than the greatest Mexican/Latin restaurant in the Green Country area in years. It really is that good.
