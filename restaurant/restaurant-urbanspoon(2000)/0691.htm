Great Burgers in a Tiny Space
by BizzNessMPLS (127 reviews)
January 22, 2009 - Really likes it
We started with a couple of apps, the cheese curds and the beer battered green beans. We really wanted to try the Spam Bites, but they were all out, so that was disappointing. The alternative were the cheese curds which were so-so, nothing like the State Fair. They were greasy, which one would expect, but they did not seem completely cooked. The beer battered green beans were tasty and crispy. I would get them again. On to the burger, I ordered the Breakfast Blucy, which happens to be one of the greatest burgers of all time. It is your standard Jucy, but with more cheese, topped with bacon and a fried egg. I had never had a fried egg on top of my burger, but it works. The combination of the egg and bacon is classic but add some cheese and a burger between a hamburger bun, and you are transported to another dimension of hamburger bliss. Also, there were no complaints with the Bacon Blucy, the Classic, or the Cajun Blucy. All were delicious. You should know that the place is tiny (no more than 10 tables), and it gets busy, 30 minute wait on a weeknight. No complaints though, trust me, no complaints.

Move over Nook, you are now #2 in my book.
by RFGuy55 (42 reviews)
April 20, 2009 - Likes it
I was a faithful Nook eater for many years, but Blue Door Pub is a bit better. The BDP burgers are bigger and juicier for about the same price. The BDP also offers deep-fried green beans and tator tots instead of fries. They have a great micro-brew selection as well. We were there at lunch on a Sunday, and thankfully we got in early enough to get a booth. We ordered the Spam Bites with the chile-lime dip, and they were great! Unfortunately, they were having kitchen issues and the Spam Bites came out with our food instead of beforehand as an app. The owner simply removed them from our bill without us asking. A simple apology was sufficient, and we had already gotten that from our waitress who was very attentive. It was very nice for him to do that! The burgers all came out hot and they were delicious. Overall, it was a really nice meal, just make sure you get there before the rush!

Find a table if you can!
by Soofaye (15 reviews)
January 21, 2010 - Likes it
I tried the Blue Door for the first time Wednesday afternoon after being told by multiple people that its usually hard to find a table. It is a small restaurant with about 10 tables and several bar seats. I did get a table for me and my sis at 4:00 but the place was crowded by the time I left at 5:30. Burgers are served "Juicy" style with the cheese stuffed on the inside. I had the Juicy Blucy which is stuffed with blue cheese and a little garlic, it also came with pickles. Yes, the burger was juicy and delicious and the basket of tater tots was the cherry on top. Lastly, you can't beat the price, $6.50 for a Blucy and no burger is over $9. If you're a fan of Juicys try Blue Door.

I found a spot at the bar and ordered a beer, followed by the Jiffy Burger with tots. The staff at this place is so laid back and cool I felt like I was hanging with my buddies at the pub. I would recommend this place to any brave food fan that isn't afraid to wait, bump elbows with other diners and get great service and great food. Bravo, Blue Door. Did not live up to hype.
by Matt (3 reviews)
January 29, 2010 - Doesn't like it
The place is TINY!
Maybe about 10 tables and a few bar spots.
It took two different tries just to get food, and we even had to get it to go.
People tend to sit and sip on their drinks for hours clogging the pub for service.
If you do wait for 30-90 minutes for a table, I hope you don't mind 10 people standing or hovering over your table while you eat.
The food was okay. Nothing spectacular.
We had the spam bites which were probably the highlight, although it was a tiny cube of spam surrounded by a ton of cream cheese.
The burgers tried were the Cajun Blucy, Bangkok, and standard Blucy.
The bangkok was decent and very sweet. Flavor was okay, the savior of this dish was the curry sauce.
The Cajun had jalapenos mixed in but was far from spicy. It was very bland and I ended up dipping that also in the curry.
The Standard Blucy was also a bit bland, minus the blu cheese which salvaged any hope for mediocrity.
Overall, not impressed, long wait, crowed, sub-par food, gave us belly aches.
