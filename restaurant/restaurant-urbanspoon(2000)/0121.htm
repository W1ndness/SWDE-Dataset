Fantastic lunch!
by Charlie (182 reviews)
February 04, 2010 - Likes it
Came here for the first time today and thoroughly enjoyed my experience.
The atmosphere is a nice mix of stylish, comfort, and warmth. Service was great (order and pay at the counter, but they are very attentive throughout your meal).
To start I had a cup of the creamed squash and rutabaga soup. It had the perfect consistency and, while playing more to the squash side of the palate, contained hints of the rutabaga. Real treat, as it is a true rarity (as far as turnips go) to find rutabaga on BHM menus.
For the main portion of my lunch I went with the Angello sandwich. Atop wonderful Italian flatbread, this rather large creation was good, quality eats. First of all, ingredients were all top quality and out-of-the-garden fresh.Plenty of lamb, roasted to perfection, and no shortage of the goat cheese, roasted tomato, arugula, or fresh oregano--giving the whole thing an abundance of flavor. Every last bite was truly delicious. If you love goat cheese, and the way it works off of tomato and meats, you'll know exactly what I'm talking about.
Can't wait to try dinner!

7/10
by Nicolja (25 reviews)
January 31, 2009 - Likes it
One of my favorite places to eat in Birmingham. This is about as authentic Italian pizza as you will find in the states. The pasta dishes and entree's are all really good. In general, the food will meet or exceed your expectations. The areas where Bettola could improve are in service and wine selection. They offer Italian wines, not a bad thing, but the list of wines is pretty small. I was told by the waiter that their reds are much better than the whites they have available. Well, I'm a white wine drinker so I was a little disappointed. If you get tired of eating at Fon Fon or Bottega Café, I would give Bettola a try for mid-priced food.

Italian trattoria with a metropolitan feel
by Psych Eater (33 reviews)
September 07, 2008 - Likes it
Others may go for the pasta or antipasti but I go for the Neapolitan style pizzas.  Bettola's thin, chewy and delicious crusts are the ideal vehicle for their New York pizzas made of fresh ingredients.  Pizzas like the pancetta e cipolle and the classic margherita are so good I considered applying for a job just to learn how to make them. Additionally, their menu features authentic Italian trattoria fair, such as their mix and match Antipasti, pastas, eggplant, and grilled sandwiches.  At first, service was not their forte, but based on my last few visits this seems to have been remedied. Buon apetito!

Frank Stitt Approved!!!
by Bham Food Bully (19 reviews)
April 25, 2010 - Likes it
I was flying back home to Birmingham and noticed South West Airlines had a featured article about Birmingham. In the article South West asked Frank Stitt about his favorite Italian/pizza place in Birmingham and he said "Bettola." Curiosity got the better of me so I decided to go and see what Frank was talking about. Now after visiting Bettola 3 times I sincerely think it is the most authentic Italian restaurant in Birmingham. I say this because there is a wood fired clay oven in the restaurant which produces authentic Neopolitan style pizzas. Even though the pizza is great I really enjoy the Antipasti platter that you and your dining partner can customize from a selection of delicious cheeses-meats and vegetables. You have to love a restaurant that liberally uses Prosciutto on it's Pizzas-Pastas & Antipasti platter! Keep in mind this is a special place to go because of the expense. You get more than what you pay for at Bettola because of the extremely high quality. When you go I recommend sitting up at the bar which is in front of the chefs. You get a great dinner and show.
