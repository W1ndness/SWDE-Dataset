Message from Olives (Bellagio)

Visit our website for more information and online reservations!

  • Modern Mediterranean
  • Casual dining
  • Reserve your table online

The new Jeffrey Beers design offers an inviting milieu of natural hues, golden tones, dark woods, and distressed leather combined with a sexy, sleek appeal.

Todd English continues his highly-praised Mediterranean menu of flatbread pizzas, housemade pastas, rotisserie dishes and flame-licked seafood and meats. Olives is sure to be your premium pick for acclaimed Mediterranean cuisine.
Included in the design is the chef's trademark farm table with a view into the open kitchen and, of course, the stunning view of Lake Bellagio and the dancing fountains from the dining room and patio.

Beautiful setting, great food!
by Melanie Bruck Coffee (7 reviews)
June 29, 2010 - Likes it
I have to say - the crew at Olives really hooked us up! I had made reservations on-line for 6:00 on a Saturday and mentioned that it was our 10th wedding anniversary. We arrived about 15 minutes early for our reserved time - and my husband asked if we could get a table on the patio overlooking the fountains. The hostess wished us Happy Anniversary asked if we were willing to wait 15 minutes for one - which we agreed to. So worth the wait!
They sat us at one of the best tables in the house - right against the Bellagio Lake. We ordered wine, the mushroom flatbread for an appetizer and our main entrees. Although the pacing of the meal was nice and relaxed - our server was a little high strung. He would ask you a question and then rush off before you could really answer, which was strange. Maybe they were just busy.
The food was really great. The mushroom flatbread with ricotta and truffle oil was super tasty and the crust just nice and crispy on the bottom straight from the woodfire oven (although kind of big for the two of us to split before our entrees). I think the entrees were coming out a little slow, so our waiter offered us a special small taster that the kitchen had prepared (especially for locals don'cha know) - but then our entrees made an appearance instead! Weird.
Rich had the Duck Two Ways - which looked amazing and tasted even better. He was even nice enough to give me a bite of each! The confit ravioli were in an astounding maple sauce - so good! My entree was just ok really - nicely cooked fish, but not the density or presence of flavors the duck had. Next time (and there will be a next time for sure) I'll order the duck.
I had a great cup of coffee to end the meal - and they brought out a small plate of chocolate truffles (since we never did get that complimentary amuse). Our waiter even picked up the charge for the coffee! All in all a great experience - but the waitstaff needs to take a deep breath and RELAX a little.

+ 1 more photos from Chante Galliano Todd English's Olives
by Chante Galliano (14 reviews)
July 10, 2009
I am concerned about reading so many negative reviews. I will agree on two things, one, the view is spectacular, and second, the service isn't great. We sat for a while in the hot 109 degree sun with no water and our bread didn't come until after our order was put in. But the food was absolutely delicious. We started with two types of tapenade and an assortment of bread. Then moved onto the Grilled Squid and Octopus, which is laid on top of a vinaigrette with chickpeas, tomatoes, toasted garlic, and parsley. I detected some fish sauce and perhaps a white wine in the vinaigrette, which I thought alone was delicious. We sampled a mushroom risotto, which was the best I have had in a long time. Finally came the Fried Soft Shell Crab Po'Boy with an avocado tomato relish, corn salsa, cherry pepper aioli, and plentiful skinny fries. My sandwich was delightful, flavorful and a thousand times better than the one I had at Toby Keith's I Love This Bar.

Worth the Price of Admission
by Athenaeus' Banquet (1 review)
December 28, 2008
On a recent trip to Las Vegas a group of us decided to try Todd English's Las Vegas outpost - Olives. In all honesty the restaurant was chosen for its balcony overlooking the Bellagio fountains more than for Todd English's reputation, which is solid, or the menu which looked interesting if not all together inspired.
The flatbread appetizers were excellent and the winelist solid. Where dinner fell apart was with the entrees. They can be divided into two categories: expensive red meats and less expensive poultry and seafood. Those who selected the meats were indifferent about the quality of their meats and their various preparations. Those who selected something a bit lighter were even less impressed as the protein was almost all overcooked and chewey with sauces that were not sufficient to recessutate them.
Dessert was good but by no means memorable. Were it not for the location I would give Olives a clear pass, but taking into account the patio and the service which was solid I might recommend it for lunch where expectations and prices tend toward the more modest.
