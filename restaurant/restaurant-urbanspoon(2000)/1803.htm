User reviews of Costa's Mediterranean Cafe

Share your opinion with the world. Add your review below.
mobile review
by CRog (176 reviews)
August 29, 2009 - Likes it
Enjoyed the sausage and peppers--the servings were huge and the pasta was good. Folks with me enjoyed the pesto chicken, the parmesean, primavera, and the lamb chops. All were pleased. I think the prices are alittle high, but the huge portions and leftovers are worth the cost. Salads are ample, and the place was busy--the staff lacked attentiveness, but eventually took care if the table. Not fine dining, but a nice Italian meal-- next time I'll try the moussaka it looked tasty.

Recommend | Not helpful

mobile review
by Corey Scogin (1 review)
July 28, 2010 - Doesn't like it
Decent food, overpriced, moderate service, awkward experience. Upon arrival, I noticed the host was poorly dressed wearing what appeared to be short blue swim shorts while the staff was in all black (slacks mind you). Our party of three was seated at a table for six directly next to a table for four. We asked to move and did then were told by our server that she would have to find the server for our table. The bread was warm and good but keep in mind that they charge for extra bread. The meal was decent but a bit overpriced. The service was reasonable.

Recommend | Not helpful

mobile review
by sujag91 (17 reviews)
July 12, 2010 - Likes it
Great family place for a relaxing Italian meal. Excellent hot, bread with olive oil and spices and they will keep bringing you more. But don't get full on bread alone. Their marinara sauce provides a good base for their dishes. But Their cheesy pasta bake with a alfredo sauce had no taste. Stick with the classic dishes with a tomato base and you can't go wrong. They don't have the best bar in town as most of their drinks taste watered down, even the wine. But you can definitely get a good family meal at good prices and be satisfied at the end of the night. You'll be back.

Recommend | Not helpful

Liked It!
by mom3290 (6 reviews)
October 11, 2009
This is a favored holiday place for our family. The portions are huge and everyone likes everything! They serve so much food, you can definitely share. This makes this restaurant a great deal. Favorites are the baked ziti, lasagna, and veal. Tip: add the table seasoning to your olive oil for the warm bread, which they bring out for free. (Also a favorite.) We were only disappointed when they started charging for extra bread.
