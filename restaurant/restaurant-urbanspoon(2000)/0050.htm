+ 1 more photos from Barney Consistently Great
by Barney (485 reviews)
April 10, 2010 - Likes it
I've continued to visit weekly (twice last week) and have yet to have a bad meal. The pulled pork has always been moist and full of smoke. The pork ribs are moist and very smokey with a great crust, with just the tinyest bit of fight left getting them off the bone (perfect! can't stand it when you pick up the rib bone and no meat comes with it). One of the surprises is the pulled chicken. Mostly dark meat, big moist chunks of chicken with a lot of smoke (the pulled chicken sandwich is one of my favorites). All the sides are great with favorites being the stew (a little spicy), the collards, the spicy/piggy baked beans, and the mac-n-cheese. I even think the slaw is pretty good (not something I say often). Only average side I've had is the potato salad. They now are starting to have specials, usually a couple of vegetables and deserts.
This is turning into one of our favorite places in this part of town. The addition of some limited outside seating has made this place that much better in this great spring weather.
(Certainly is interesting reading other reviews... when it's come to customer service they've always been very nice... on occasions that they're very busy they tend to spend less time with chitchat which seams reasonable, but I'm not sure I'm expecting to be fawned over at a que joint, but that apparently is not true for everybody)

+ 1 more photos from Barney Fantastic
by Barney (485 reviews)
November 24, 2009 - Likes it
I gotta admit that I've been watching and waiting for them to open given all impressive things said about the folks behind the que here. Not even a full week under their belt and this is some of the best beef bbq I've ever had in Atlanta.
Went in for lunch and picked those things I've already heard so much about: the beef rib and the brisket. Wow. Amazing how they can get so much smoke, color and crust on that huge rib and it still be moist. Very Nice. Brisket was also nicely smoked and still a little moist, not too much fat (have spent enough time in TX to know that's not an easy combination). For sides, the mac-n-cheese had a fantastic crusty cheesy shell and was nicely flavored and thankfully not runny... also above average in my book. Finally, the collards were medium seasoned (high on the vinegar scale, which I like) and not overcooked.
For sauces, a thin red sauce that was pretty mild (not too sweet, not too spicy) and a vinegar based that was pretty tasty but could have used a little punch of spice.
Yes, I liked this place and will be going back.

So So BBQ, rude service
by TJ (1 review)
February 17, 2010 - Doesn't like it
My hubby and I decided to try this place to see what all the fuss was about. I had actually been really excited to go as the reviews were raving, plus we are big foodies and very loyal to Fox Bros when it comes to BBQ.
So we go in and the 1st thing I noticed is that the sandwiches are a la carte. For 6.50 you only get the meat on two pieces of texas toast. The sides were an additional $2. I went for the brisket sandwich and mac and cheese. The portions were kinda small but tasted good. Good brisket, dry at times but the mac was great, only wish I had more of it.
For $11 hubby ordered the combo meats plate which was the exact same portion as my brisket sandwich with one extra side, very disappointing. His food tasted good, especially the greens. But again, small portions, especially for a $25 lunch.
Perhaps most dismaying was the lack of customer service we experienced. We observed the owner speaking to and greeting most other patrons, except us. Dont wanna play the race card but it came to mind. Even as we walked out and left a tip, he failed to acknowledge us with a "thansk for coming" even though he was standing nearby. Needless to say this put a very bad taste in my mouth to an otherwise decent lunch.
I wont be back.

The Perfect BBQ Restaurant
by David (1 review)
November 25, 2009 - Likes it
Being a member of a local BBQ club, I visit many BBQ restaurants. I have to admit, this without a doubt is the BEST BBQ in and around the Atlanta area. This is a MUST GO TO BBQ restaurant. I felt very welcomed here, and that is hard to find anywhere.
The Mac and Cheese is no doubt the best that I have ever tasted and I have tasted a lot of different styles.
I honestly cannot wait to go back. I cannot believe that Community BBQ actually have Beef ribs, no one sells them. I am allergic to Pork products, so I was very excited to see these on the menu. I cannot wait to order them next time.
I had a Beef brisket sandwich when I ate. The Beef was so tender and moist, for once I found a place that didn't load the meat down with a heavy smoke taste. I would not have changed anything if I could, it was that good!
The portions: I did everything that I could to finish my sandwich, I could have shared the meal with someone else.
Sauce: I had a choice between two sauces, one vinegar based and the other more like a Tomato based. Both were VERY good. My favorite was the Tomato based sauce. VERY TASTY sauce and added a very nice taste to the meat.
Overall score: UNREAL.. I would have to rate this place 9.9 out of 10. I would defiantly recommend this restaurant to everyone.
Sincerely,
David S.
