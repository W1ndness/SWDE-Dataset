Mixed feelings
by pointedview (7 reviews)
October 03, 2008
The food was good, but a lot of little mistakes kept the restaurant from reaching its full potential.
The space was very noisy, our server didn't seem to care in the slightest that we were there, and the sushi menu completely lacked prices! Additionally, they had no combo dinners (the sushi menu didn't even list soup, despite the fact that they offered it for hibachi combos), and they served tepid water with tea bags for the tea. The maki were looser than they should have been, and they don't have their sushi rice right. At first I thought this was just because the kitchen was backed up and rushed (which I don't quite understand, because the majority of their business was for the hibachi tables), but after receiving my disappointing order of nigiri (served well after my entree, instead of being an appetizer like I requested), I wondered if the sushi chef was new: he or she completely overpowered the delicate ika with large daubs of wasabi. I like wasabi, but it's potent stuff that's meant to be used sparingly, and it ruined the squid.
However, despite the numerous problems, I have to give them points for trying to do Midtown-level sushi out in the 'burbs. The fish was fresh. The sushi menu is about as anti-traditional as you can get. Anything goes: one roll, called The Orthodontist, included American cheese! I had the Siberian Tiger, which incorporated spicy salmon, pine nuts, and soy bean pseudo seaweed as a wrap. It was excellent. My husband had the Dynamite roll, and we split the Empress roll, a combination of spicy yellowtail, spicy tuna, eel, and avocado.
These issues could be easily remedied: get the chef familiar with the fish he or she is preparing and teach him/her how to roll it properly; reprint the menus with actual prices (what a concept!), coach the wait staff a bit, hang some attractive cloth banners from the ceiling to help buffer the noise, and so on. As it is, though, I'll keep searching for the best sushi on the north side of town.
