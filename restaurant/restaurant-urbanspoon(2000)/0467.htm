Message from Shade

Inventive and informal gourmet fare in a sophisticated Heights setting.

  • Catering
  • Shade Club
  • Wine list

Shade is an exciting new full-service restaurant owned by Heights residents, Claire Smith and Russell Murrell, who along with Chef Jeb Stuart are committed to meeting the demand for quality dining in our community. We are excited to bring you a selection of sensibly crafted dishes in an atmosphere of attentive and hospitable service. Shade serves lunch and dinner every day of the week except Mondays and brunch on Saturday and Sunday. The seasonal menu is creative, fresh and affordable. The decor is contemporary, yet inviting, with a fabulous dining room and cozy outdoor seating. Our goal at Shade is to provide a comfortable establishment where customers consider themselves 'regulars'. With your support, we hope that Shade will become a new dining destination for friends and neighbors as well as an attraction for new visitors to the Heights.

Hopping in The Heights
by Greggndc (74 reviews)
May 18, 2010 - Likes it
On a recent trip to Houston, I was introduced to Shade, a lovely and lively spot in the now very hip Heights. When I used to live in Houston, there were really no great food options in the Height, due to liquor restrictions, I would guess. That has changed and Shade seems to be one of the culinary leaders. One of the things I really liked was the daily soup trio. One can order a bowl of soup - try three small servings of the soups-of-the-day. The Texas quail is a perfect starter - elegantly composed and balanced with the light gaminess of the bird offset by the tang and creaminess of the blue cheese. What a great idea. I often get duck when I am out (and one of my companions did, so I got to taste a little bit of the confit in wine sauce... I love polenta, but thought this accompaniement was a little wan) but had the salmon that night. It was the Dijon mustard sauce that lured meinto this selection and I was not disappointed. The salmon was tender inside but had a crispy, almost caramelized shell on it that was divine. I was able to wrest a forkful of beef tenderloin (beautifully cooked) from a companion and got a snippet of his blue cheese-mashed potatoes. I would have never thought of that combo -but do now. I zeroed in on my personal favorite for dessert, a decadent coconut cream pie, and this was the best slice since I was a little kid. I didn't sample anyone else's dessert - just was happy with my own. The room is elegant but minimal and surprisingly quiet considering that it was crowded and had a wait for tables. The service was good and fairly attentive. If this is an indication of what the food scene in the Heights has now become, Houstonians are very lucky.
