+ 1 more photos from Gulf Coast Guy Another trip, another nice time
by Gulf Coast Guy (820 reviews)
January 28, 2010 - Really likes it
Business diner last night that turned into a nice chat and nosh fest. That is the beauty of a place like Ceviche. Turns what could be a more mundane event into a celebration.
Started with a pitcher of the red sangria. Always great. Are you listening Columbia?
Nice hot bread came out with dipping oil and herbs. Suggestion: Bring out olive oil only too.
Ordered a ceviche, salad, paella(chicken and pork) and one of my faves, the Tabla Mixta, which is two platters with assorted cheese and meats with fruits and such to accompany. All these were fine and this is my first taste of the paella. It was fab. Much richer than I was thinking.
Grabbed some albonedegas, filet and shrooms, eggplant something and a scallop dish. All these were great EXCEPT the eggplany selection. The ggplant was wrappedaround something inside but was really really tough. Could not even cut it.
Also, this trip we ordered desserts and they shine here. Breadpudding with dried fruits and ice cream. flan, which was divine and silky, creme brulee in a chocolate shell and a chocolate cake with raspberry.
Bread pudding was great and a lot. COuld not eat it all even sharing. Suggest a table of four order two desserts max to share.
Finished mine up with Cafe Con Leche. Service was great and three hours later we finaly left.
On my four seashell scale:
Food 3.25
Atmosphere 3.50
Service 3.50
Drinks 3.50
Overall I am at 3.50
A fave.

The Best Tapas in Town
by TMV (40 reviews)
August 12, 2010 - Likes it
After trying several tapas restaurants, Ceviche is, hands down, the best in atmosphere, food, wine, and entertainment. I happened in there on a Friday night when bandaleros (or Gypsy-King style musicians) were playing in the back bar area. It was loud out on the patio, however, so my date and I took to a small cozy nook in the back of the restaurant. We were greeted with delightful service, our server was informative on the wines and helpful in the food recommendations, and the intimacy of the atmosphere made for a perfect romantic dinner. After trying several tapas - a few to note were the patatas bravas, the mussels in white wine butter, and the ox tail - and having an inexpensive but fabulous bottle of jumilla wine, we strolled to the back bar to listen in (and dance) to the singers. All in all, the price of the meals and drinks were comparable to restaurants with only half the quality of food and service. I will continue to go back often.

Fun nght out
by TampaCook (4 reviews)
December 17, 2008 - Likes it
Tampa has a distinct shortage of grown-up bars. Ceviche isn't just a decent tapas restaurant; it also offers one of the few places in town where a group of adults can go have a relaxed, fun evening with good food, good drinks, and (usually) good music. Other reviewers are right: the service is not fast. But that's not really the point of the place. If you want fast service, go down the street to Ciccio's. Ceviche is great for a long night out with a table full of friends.
Ceviche has an enormous menu of tapas (dozens of items), almost all of them are very good and several are excellent. Our favorite way to eat there is to order in rounds. Let everyone at the table choose one item to start. The tapas items come out one by one, as they're ready, not all together. You nosh, and talk, and drink wine or sangria, and nosh some more. Order another round of food. Repeat. Oh, and the prices are incredibly reasonable too -- I'm usually shocked by how little the bill is at the end of the night.
By the way, the reviewer that commented on Ceviche's unusual location at the bottom of an apartment building is way out of date. Ceviche moved more than a year ago to a new location just down the street (the old St. Barts), and now has much more space, a bar with seating, and a big patio around a pool.
