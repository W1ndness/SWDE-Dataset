Finally (And So Worth It)
by Kitchen Geeking (133 reviews)
May 07, 2010 - Likes it
Got here last weekend finally and was as impressed as expected based on the friend-reviews I'd received over the past few months. Fantastic pizza with execellent topping options and quality. Personal preference issue, if the crust was even 1/8" thicker it would hold up to being a hand-eaten slice. As is, it's a fork n' knifer to get this delicious stuff in yer belly.
The Margherita was classic and fresh with huge basil flavor. The spicy sausage with red sauce and mozz was spot on. The white pizza with dollops of ricotta and serious garlic was pungent-tastic.
Surprise menu treats are the risottos (OMG carrot/curry...friggin' GOOOOOOD) and if I can tear myself away from a pie the next time I go there are sandwiches on the menu that whisper sweet (savory) nothings (everything plus mayo) to me.

Fresh ingredients make heavenly pizza
by bobcatarts (10 reviews)
July 31, 2010 - Likes it
In the Charles Village neighborhood of Baltimore is a pizza shop and bar that worth its weight in gold.
Framed by a perfect summer sunset, their patio was bustling while a live band provided entertainment within. If you like bars, head inside, but the patio is simply perfect.
The service was a little harried, but very friendly and apologetic for any perceived delays.
The Hawaiian pizza was as beautifully made as it was tasty.
Square crust was thin and crispy, neatly charred around the edges from their specialty oven. Fresh bright green cilantro complemented the shredded rings of golden fresh pineapple, perfectly cooked ham and bacon, and mild cheeses on an exquisitely tangy tomato sauce. Even though I'm not personally a fan of thin crust, this was an absolutely perfect pizza and I could have it again and again.
Dishes other customers had looked delicious and beautifully presented, showing a skilled kitchen that belies the hipster-neighborhood aesthetic.
I can't wait to come back and explore the rest of their menu!

Incredible!
by Miss Spaghetti (1 review)
June 30, 2010 - Likes it
I've never been as pleased with a restaurant's food as I was with Joe Squared's food. Albeit the atmosphere and overall design of the interior wasn't great, but sitting outside and enjoying my meal was very pleasant.
Everything about my meal was absolutely perfect. The crab cake sandwich that I ordered was phenomenal-- the crab cake was seasoned just right and cooked to perfection, and the proportion of crab cake to lettuce/tomato/sauce was just right (I usually pick stuff out of my sandwich or have to reassemble it). Even the *bread* of the sandwich was chosen well and toasted just the way I liked it! To make things better, the side salad I got (instead of fries) had a delicious honey mustard-esque dressing lightly drizzled over it, rather than drowning the salad. To wrap things up, I was wholly satisfied at the end of my meal-- the portions were perfect and filled me up, but not to the point where I'd feel overfull, the meal itself was the most perfect meal I'd ever gotten at a restaurant, and it was all reasonably priced. I highly recommend that you go to Joe Squared! : )

good pizza to go
by johndiggity (18 reviews)
February 03, 2010 - Likes it
joe squared makes an interesting pizza. it's square-ish, yet with thin crust fired in a coal burning oven. if you want traditional pizza, go elsewhere, as the sourdough crust alone might be enough to scare you off.
the pizzas are offered in a seemingly endless variety of toppings and combinations. expect choice paralysis to set in pretty quickly as you look over the menu. the flag pizza is a personal favorite.
as good as the pizza can be, the ambience is really what kills this place. rickety seating outside can be fine on summer days, but you are still sitting right on the corner of north ave and howard.
the inside looks like what is used to be, a neglected, run down, chinese restaurant. there is also a pretty strong smell of mildew as you enter, which i imagine emanates from the original carpets.
joe squared is great for the eternally up-and-coming station north area (or whatever they are calling it now), yet, much like the neighborhood, it is still not ready for prime-time dining. thankfully, they deliver and carryout is available.

A diamond in the rough.
by VandeLinde (2 reviews)
January 23, 2009 - Really likes it
I can only register two complaints with Joe Squared. The first is that it does not have the best location. The second is that the menu is a little pedestrian considering the quality of the ingredients and the quality of the kitchen staff.
The food here is exceptional, if you are looking for pizza or subs there is nowhere better in Baltimore. They also have a selection of salads (the beef/red pepper salad is my favorite), risottos and specialty wings. All of it good. On most nights Joe Squared also boasts a variety of live music, making it a great place for a date.
Finally, I wholeheartedly support the bar's selection of rums and imported beers.
