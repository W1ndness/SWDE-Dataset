Message from Zaftigs Delicatessen

  • Cheddar-apple omelets
  • Catering
  • Art decorations

Since 1997, Zaftigs Delicatessen has been offering great meals in a lively atmosphere. Have banana-stuffed French toast, potato pancakes, matzo ball soup or a Reuben sandwich while appreciating jazz and the arts indoors.

Our complete menu is available for takeout and most menu items can be prepared to meet your dietary needs.
We serve breakfast all day, lunch and dinner.
We were voted one of the Ten Best Delis in the World by Reader's Digest in 2006.

BINGO!
by Mr. Foodie (14 reviews)
April 14, 2009 - Likes it
So I find myself (with family) in Boston over Passover and in dire need for a deli sandwich. Coincidently I pick up an issue of 'Where' magazine and read an article about a deli located in the Brookline district of Boston named Zaftigs. (For those of you who grew up with Yiddish spoken at the table you know the word Zaftig means plump, full etc ... Being an Zaftig person myself, to me this meant 'Heaven')
I ordered the Brisket layered between Potato Pancakes also known as 'The Lupo'. It was an excellent choice, the brisket was moist and tender and melted in your mouth. The potato pancakes were sized like hockey pucks but were outstanding. Full potato taste with a nice contrast of crunchy shell. They also served 1/2 sour pickles which to me is as important as the meal itself. My biggest disappoinment was that I couldn't try the desserts because of the Jewish holiday restriction.
The service was first class and the place was spotless.
I even had to get one of their funny t-shirts to remind me of the great experience I had there.
Too me Zaftigs is a MUST go while in Boston.
PS - I have my fingers crossed that my daughter decides to go to school in Boston so I can come back to Zaftigs often.

quick dinner
by Maureen (2 reviews)
February 17, 2010 - Likes it
The atmosphere is fun and relaxed-very casual. The food is delicious, but a bit on the pricey side.
Instead of the usual bread and butter to start, our waitress gave us homemade bagel chips and an herb cream cheese. I ordered a corned beef Reuben (yum) and my bf got the BBQ grilled chicken sandwich. My sandwich was a little on the dry side; I usually like a bit more Russian dressing on my Reubens but the meat was so flavorful and cut so thin that I couldn't stop eating it long enough to ask for more dressing.
The meal was just fine but I couldn't help but feel like I was eating at a diner-only more expensive.
Next time I'll throw down the extra $2 and get an entree.
