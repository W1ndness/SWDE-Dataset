Fun place for food and drink
by hornguy (2 reviews)
July 14, 2010 - Likes it
Stopped by for dinner last night and to get caught up on some work. Had the falafel bake and an iced tea.
The iced tea was amazing - great stuff with some nice floral undertones. Forget what kind it was - their current selections are listed on the board.
The falafel bake was a little hit or miss. The falafel patty itself was a bit underseasoned, but the concept of the sandwich is a good one. I particularly enjoyed the tortilla that serves as the outer layer - it was kind of flaky, almost like phyllo. The hummus was easily the best thing on the plate and is definitely worth trying - it shows up in three or four difference places on the menu. The potato chips on the side were just kind of there, but they did give me something with which to eat the leftover hummus. Did I mention how outstanding the hummus is?
Either way, Kavarna's a great place to hang out, meet friends, catch up on some reading, and have some decent food and drink downtown. And as a veggie-centric establishment, it's a welcome part of the area's culinary scene.

Vegan much?
by JHerm (7 reviews)
March 29, 2009 - Really likes it
I'm not a vegan by any means, but this coffee house offers much more than one would think when hearing 'Vegan coffee house'. Yam fries, Pita bread and hummus, cheesy artichoke wrap...good stuff and all for under $20.

One of the best
by keith (5 reviews)
October 13, 2009 - Likes it
Of any place to eat, this has got to be in the top five for me. #1 is definitely the Urban Frog though, which is also on Broadway. I always check to see if the Frog is open before going to Kavarna, but I do like them both as they are fairly similar and have good tasty food for the vegetarian, health-conscious types (such as myself). Though I suppose they also cater to those with a sweet tooth or people addicted to icky coffee.
