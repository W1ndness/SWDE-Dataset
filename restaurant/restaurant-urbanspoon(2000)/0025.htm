Good food & great wine.
by Ruth Quinlan (5 reviews)
July 06, 2010
Think about Crush for lunch. Crush Wine Bistro and Cellar is located across the street from Nordstroms at 6th and D.
Crush is about wine so I don't always think of it for lunch during the work week. I revisited Crush this week and found the menu has evolved over time and I think they have really found the right mix.
Sitting at the bar with a few co-workers turned out to be a nice chance to chat with other patrons and owner Robert de Lucia.
The polenta was a crowd favorite. One woman at the counter admitted to having a hard time picking anything else once she tried the polenta.
My co-worker declared the polenta her new favorite dish of all downtown! I got herb grilled chicken and was too focused on the juicy delicious meat to offer a taste to anyone while my co-workers were sharing bites of their delicious entrees.
For me the surprise of the day was the potato salad. The seafood chowder sounded marvelous and was according to those who tried it, but I can't have dairy and so I opted for potato salad.
I got a plate of the most beautiful combination of flavors to ever be called potato salad. This I had to share and there was plenty to go around. We all agreed it was an amazing potato salad.
A restaurant that provides food worth talking about and returning to try again AND serves memorable wine. Now that is worth a visit.
More good news the Crush Cellar has been in the works for a while now and it looks like this new wine store will finally be opening in March! Soon you will be able to buy the wine you discover you love just upstairs from the restaurant you love.
To look over the menu, which is as good as it sounds, or to find out more about becoming a wine club member visit www.crushak.com.

Favorite hang out spot with the girls!
by Jennifer Lanier (6 reviews)
May 23, 2010 - Likes it
Crush is a great place to eat dinner and spend the rest of your evening casually sipping wine and ending the night with a latte and desert! I love this place and all of the waitstaff. They all take such great care in teaching you the knowledge base of the wines if you care to know and if you go there a few times even remember you by name. The owners are phenomenal and jump in to help their staff at any needed minute and they all seem to work as a great team/family, this is reflected in the superb customer service received and one of the many reasons myself and my friends continue to frequent Crush. The food is outstanding! The menu is ever changing as to never get boring and there are always great specials on the weekends. The menu items themselves seem to have a twist or just be flat out different than those of the standard downtown eateries which is enticing and also pair nicely with the outstanding vast variety of wines Crush carries - which also are changed out regularly as to not bore ones pallet. Over all I must say this is one of my favorite places to be if I am not at home!
Great ambiance, great food, great beverages, great people, great vibe-What more could one want!

by blackpete (1 review)

having read the reviews on this place, i was ambivalent to try it out with my wife, because it sounded like it could either be superficial and striving or serious but disarming . on first blush, it was well appointed and even somewhat chic, but, sadly, after standing around feeling like an idiot for a few minutes--even denny's greets you when you walk in the door--and not knowing whether to seat ourselves, belly up, or take a seat in the lounge?/waiting area?, we opted for taking off. i'd love to laud the fare or praise the celler, but, alas never felt welcome enough to try it. sorry crush, but there should be someone to welcome your guests, whether it's a host, server, bartender or whatever. no one likes to be ignored. you only get one chance to make a first impression and you blew this one.
