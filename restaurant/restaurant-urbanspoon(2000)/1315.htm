On a roll...
by Greggndc (75 reviews)
January 01, 2009 - Likes it
UPDATE 4/4: On my recent v isits I always get the fried risotto balls...crisp spheres of rice covering a morsel of mozzarella that oozes from the center as you bite. Highly recommended. Today I tried one of their pasta salads. All of them feature arugula and pastina (a pellet-sized pasta like cous-cous) dressed in olive oil. A great option to their tasty sandwiches....
I came a bit late to the frenzy that was part of the opening of Taylor Gourmet. Hoagie?? I could go to A. Litteri's in the warehouse district for one of those. When I finally succumbed I was impressed. My choice of the Italian sausage sandwich with sharp provolone and grilled onions was a good one. The sausage was well-flavored and perfectly grilled. The provolone, too sharp on its own, was a perfect compliment. I skipped the peppers that were part of the mix, but the onions that came with were almost caramelized and added a sweet component to the mix with the exceptional roll - crusty and flavorful. The bread is what sets Taylor apart from the competition. While waiting, I checked out the Italian imports for sale. Pasta, olive oil, vinegar - what one would expect and prices that were high but not unreasonably so (Litteri's has you beat here and as a better selection but also the room to offer better variety). What impressed most was how the guy taking orders patiently explained the process to guests and about Taylor's policies. Local old timers were used to ordering at a more traditional deli. He struck the right note between being helpful to newbies and keeping the orders flowing through a busy lunch rush.

ATTN: Hoagies Arrive in DC!
by No Doggie Bag (37 reviews)
April 30, 2010 - Likes it
Finally, people from the NJ, NY, Philly area have a place to go have a sandwich. I ask myself, why has it taken this long? The food is fresh, it is authentic and the owners have the requisite love and respect for the submarine sandwich. You can pull up a stool, a beer and a bag of chips and enjoy a quick gourmet lunch. My only criticism is that there are too many choices of sandwich.
