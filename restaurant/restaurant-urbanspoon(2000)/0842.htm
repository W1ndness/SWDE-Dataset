Good. Not great.
by Rufus (166 reviews)
May 15, 2010 - Likes it
I'm always on the lookout for a good pizza and when a place draws this sort of positive mojo on the 'spoon I gotta check it out. It's some solid pie.
Rodney is generous with the toppings and they seem to be of high quality. My main issue is with the crust. I can't put my finger on it but something just isn't quite right and that's a shame because, in my humble opinion, it's the only thing keeping this from being first rate pizza.
The crust is a thin style crust but not Hut thin. Sort of a thin/hand tossed hybrid. It's a bit chewy but pretty dry. Dry but not crispy... almost like it is stale (I know this isn't possible buy I'm grasping for words in trying to describe it).
Sauce is just right. Cheese is plentiful and the meat toppings all over the pie and quite tasty.
The place appears fairly new; a pair of "rooms" divided by a "fireplace". The staff seems friendly for kids that clearly wished they were someplace else on a Saturday night. Must be live music on the weekend as I noticed a keyboard on a riser in the corner.
A final note... the menu needs updating because that large pepperoni doesn't cost $14.50 anymore. It's $17 now and that may be pushing it.

Type A and Type B
by Dave Sause (5 reviews)
March 03, 2010 - Likes it
Whenever I want someones opinion on a restaurant, I ask them what some of their favorite ones are in the area. If they mention A,B, or C (which I have been anticipating going out of business and can't understand why they're crowded) then I know I have a type A person. If they mention D, E, or F (which may be dives, have no atmosphere, but give you a ton of great food for a few bucks, and may even be on the spicy side) then I know I have a type B person.
The type A's would just as soon eat cardboard with a few pieces of artfully sculpted 'prosciutto' aesthetical arranged and they believe they are gormets. The type B's may be gourmandes, but their places pile on the pepperoni and add two kinds of olives for good measure. If you haven't guessed by now, I'm a type B, and Rodney's is my kind of place. Read through the reviews here and if you agree with the negative ones, then find one of those guys to tell you where to eat (you have no taste anyway, so why bother?) If the positive ones seem more your speed, that you gotta try Rodney's, and prepare to become a lifelong friend and customer. By the way, someone mentioned Rodney's acting like he'd been doing this forever, ask me the story sometime, he has been, and the last place he worked used to be on my list, but isn't since he left. Get the picture?
