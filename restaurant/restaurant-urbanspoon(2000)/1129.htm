This is SA's newest hotspot eatery, but it's showing some signs of strain from the get-go.
The dining room is sparse, bordering on cold. The much talked-of 'wine glass' centerpiece is severely too small for the room and looks somewhat unprofessional with it's LED, Christmas string lighting.
Tables are far too close for this kind of price. When I can hear what the Mayor is saying, in private, a table away, that's cause for concern. I'm not a fan.
Service was new and untrained. That will likely change.
The food was inventive, but on my first visit, oversalted. I've now come to find out that their meat curing process takes place in the loft space above the eatery, leaving me to wonder how sanitary the conditions are. Until further notice, I wouldn't suggest eating ANY of their in-house cured meats.
I look forward to seeing this restaurant grow (and it will), but for now cannot rave about it as so many have. Sure, it's a see-and-be-seen sort of place, but that's about it for now. All that glitters is definitely not gold.

Delightful & Refreshing
by KS (11 reviews)
May 02, 2010 - Really likes it
We were greeted by two kind hostesses today at Il Sogno. As there was a brief wait to be seated, the hostesses gave us the wine list and served us a glass in the entrance while we waited. I found this to be a nice touch and a warm embrace; afterall, this is in the spirit of Italian hospitality. Everyone in the waiting room was in a happy mood due to this small gesture, and it was fun to watch the chefs at work in the kitchen through the glass windows.
We were seated in the naturally-lit dining room, and were promptly served glasses of chilled water and a choice of lime, lemon or orange slices. Again, this small gesture was a testament to the pride and passion of those at work in the restaurant. Our server greeted us and took the time to make us feel comfortable and welcome. He recommended a nice bottle of wine which I enjoyed thoroughly. He then took the time to walk us to the antipasti bar, which is a sight to behold, and then proceeded to explain each item on the bar succinctly and with enthusiasm.
We ordered 3 beautiful and delicious items for antipasti (roasted beet w/ orange, pea puree with ricotta and mint, tuna with marinated peppers) plus we enjoyed the lardo focaccia which was served quickly and very fresh. We could hear Luca in the kitchen calmly calling our orders to his team of craftsmen, which was another reassuring and impressive element of the meal.
We enjoyed our meal very much, and this was also served suprisingly swiftly. We shared the scallop and monkfish skewers which we shared. I had the osso bucco with parmesan risotto which was, I have to say one of the best I have ever eaten, and my fellow diner enjoyed his lobster ravioli very much.
The experience was, to echo the title of this review, nothing if not delightful and refreshing. It is rare to receive such kind and attentive service (our water and wine glasses were never less than half full). The food was crafted to perfection, and I could not have asked for better execution of each element of each dish. The price is no higher than any other high-end restaurant in San Antonio, but the service and pace of the meal at Il Sogno makes the entire experience priceless. My only regret is that I was too full to order dessert...but I will remedy that regret soon during my next visit.

A bit disappointed but hopeful for future visits
by celestialbebe (2 reviews)
August 17, 2009
I had lunch at the much-anticipated Il Sogno at the Pearl Brewery on Saturday after the farmers market was over. Maybe it was all the hype about Andrew Weissman's super-vigilance, attention to detail and authenticity of the food, or maybe they're only pulling out the stops for dinner but I was kinda under-whelmed.
The lunch menu is pretty spare and I didn't think it was very imaginative or interesting. Only a couple menu items seemed like originals that I wouldn't find at every other Italian place in SA. To get a taste of a few different items, I had the antipasti sampler (5 items for $12). The cheeses are fantastic, the cold cuts were hit and miss, roasted peppers with anchovy sauce was great (and I'm not even a fish-lover), but the absolute star of the meal was an eggplant dip that was lemony and wonderful. I would go back just for that! Surprisingly, they did not serve bread with the antipasti - only a couple token crackers. We had to ask for more in order to have something to put the cheese and cold cuts on. Did not get to try the octopus/potato salad, margherita pizza or the panini with speck on this trip but they all looked/smelled great and will try on the next visit.
For dessert we split a nutella tart, and although I'm a huge nutella fan this too was less than thrilling. Honestly, Tre does a better job with its antipasti and its nutella dessert. We topped off the meal with a cappucino and espresso, which I must say was the absolute best coffee I have had in months and certainly the best I've had anywhere in San Antonio. Two people sharing one antipasti sampler, one dessert, two coffees - $30 plus tip. Not bad at all, if it had felt a little more unique (maybe Tre has spoiled me). Also, keep in mind that it's very loud when you sit near the open kitchen... our waiter had to repeat himself several times for us to hear him, but it really is fun watching the chefs at work.
