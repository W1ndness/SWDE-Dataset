Athens: Nashville's ONLY Authentic Down-Home, Greek Farm-Style Cuisine
by Rick Culpepper (1 review)
January 12, 2010 - Likes it
If you want Greek food prepared the way it was preapred by a Greek mother in her own kitchen, then you need to make a bee-line for Athens. As an added bonus, Athens has been voted Best Breakfast in Nashville by readers of the Nashville Scene. AND, Athens is open and serving meals around the clock from Thursday morning until Sunday evening every week.
Owners Adel (who is also the Head Chef) and Mohammed have created a quaint restaurant that serves incredible food. The pastichio is incredible. The lamb chops, lamb fricasee, lamb stew -- really anything with lamb -- are all simply stupendous. Oh, and you just have to try Dina's potatoes... OHMIGOD!
We discovered Athens in the first few months after they opened about 3-1/2 years ago. Adel and Mohammed made us feel at home from the first moment we stepped in the door. The food was incredible and so were the people.
Since that day, we have been loyal customers -- and ardent evangelists -- of Athens. The only downside? 3-1/2 years ago, I could always get a table as soon as I walked in the door. These days, if time is a consideration, I call ahead for a reservation.
I could go on an on about Athens, the food, the people, but I'll spare you.
In short, if you want a great meal of Greek or American cuisine at breakfast, lunch, dinner or even late night snack on the weekends, check out Athens. You'll be glad you did.
