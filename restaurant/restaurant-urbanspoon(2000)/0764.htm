Nestled next to the rail in Ghent!
by MJ (3 reviews)
January 18, 2009 - Likes it
I love the ambiance of Pasha a mix of urban chic with a splash of Turkish cultural artifacts. The train adds to the coziness. Do not miss the sampler tray if you have not had the pleasure of this type of cuisine. I love the red lentil speed, hummus and the muhammarah. The bread is homemade with a rich thick texture. Not a lentil fan, try the soup anyway. You may change your mind. The lamb with Jasmine rice is very rich and a small portion will satisfy even a hearty appetite. The bread pudding was perfect for me since I do not like raisins, chocolate cake is good, and the rice pudding looks wonderful. There are plenty of choices so try Pasha Mezze for something new.

Outstanding
by ptownguy (6 reviews)
September 21, 2009 - Really likes it
I have now had two meals at Pasha Mezze and can happily recommend it. I had lunch one day - the red lentil soup and a borek (phyllo dough baked with spinach and feta). Both were very good. I returned for brunch with two companions and we were each really thrilled with our dishes. The omelette was spectacular - choose your own ingredients. The Turkish style French toast was as tasty as it was beautiful and the sweet-freak who ordered it had to get a box to take some home, it was so rich. Noi syrup needed. I had the Turkish b'fast with a hard boiled egg, cukes and tomato slices with olive oil, a spread made of feta and walnuts, some olives, a potato cake, there may have been more. Fabulous eating. The place has a nice feel to it and the service was a perfect balance of enough attention and privacy. I will be back to work my way through the menu. There is something there for anyone, even those not accustomed to or wanting Turkish/Medeterrainian food.
