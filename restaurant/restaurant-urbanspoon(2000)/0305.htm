Great sushi for Killer Price
by nsticka (26 reviews)
June 21, 2009 - Likes it
It had been a while since we visited the N. Arlington location but were craving sushi and we decided to try the new locations. We made reservations in advance and we were seated immediately upon arrival. Within minutes our server brought us their little noodle bowl and hot towel wipes and napkins. We orders our apps; I had the spicy conk and octopus salad and my wife had the seared tuna. Although I liked my dish as it was very flavorful with the onions and red peppers and asparagus, I would of preferred more conk and octopus.
My wifes seared tuna was perfect; wish I could of had one for myself as well.
The rolls I ordered was the Latino roll and Spider roll, both very good. The Latino is not listed on the menu as there seems to no rolls listed as spicy and thats what I wanted. My wife had the Dragon and Sexy Roll and we ordered the Pad Thai for my son, our server took the initiative and ordered the lunch portion for us and that worked out.
It was a great experience with great service and great sushi but it was over $100 for 21/2 people which included one martini and one beer. That is a little pricey to us and we can get great sushi in Mansfield w/o the hassles of traffic.
But if you are in the area and craving sushi, we would recommend this place.

Wonderful Overall Experience
by Joe (1 review)
September 01, 2009
After moving to Arlington a few years ago, I was less than inspired to try to find decent sushi among the stadiums and theme parks. My girlfriend dragged me to the Piranha in north Arlington and I was impressed. We have been back many times, and just recently tried the Highlands location. After a 20 minute wait (Friday night)our service there was excellent- the drinks were out quickly, and the server was knowledgable about the dishes I like that were not on the menu. The fish was fresh, and the sauces were consistent with those found at the other location. Everyone around us appeared to be having a great time, also. Tables were bussed quicky, and despite being seated at a corner table we had a clear view of the chefs working. Try this restaurant, and ask about the Latino Roll if you like some heat. Try the drink called the Naughty Asian. Also, the house Sake goes well with just about everything.
