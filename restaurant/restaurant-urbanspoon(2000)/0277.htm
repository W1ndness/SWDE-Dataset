Try before you buy!
by Barbara A. Fisher (1 review)
August 04, 2010 - Likes it
Good lord!
I cannot believe that people are complaining about buying an unusual flavor like Thai chili ice cream, which is described as coconut, peanut butter and chili, and then not liking it! Well, duh--try a taste of it before you buy it!
And by the way, it isn't repulsive--Thai chili, now renamed Bangkok Peanut is one of my favorite flavors. It is complex, sweet, but not overly so, with a delicious chili zing.
Don't listen to reviewers who didn't have sense enough to try an unusual flavor before buying it--they are in the minority when it comes to Jeni's--most people who try the ice cream love it. You don't get a national reputation like Jeni's has by serving universally "repulsive" ice cream...

Very expensive...
by Kevin (17 reviews)
July 02, 2009 - Doesn't like it
I recently visited Jeni's this past weekend and decided to try something unusual - the Thai Chili ice cream. My wife and I both got a small scoop in a paper container. Each scoop was $4.00 or $4.50, from what I recall.
Unfortunately, the flavor was quite repulsive. I took one bite and was quite turned off by the chili/red pepper flavoring. I literally took one bite of the ice cream and threw the rest of it away. I believe that my wife made it through three or four bites before she threw her's away too. She was hesistant to do so because we had spent $9.00 on two very small scoops of ice cream.
Unfortunately, we made the false assumption that if this ice cream was sold there, then it must be at least somewhat palatable (and I'm really not picky). Additionally, we were fooled into thinking that if it's that expensive, then it has to be good as well, right? Wrong. Shoudl I have gotten a more mellow, normal flavor, like vanilla? Probably. But then again, I could've walked down the block to Dairy Queen and have gotten a vanilla scoop for a fraction of what she charges for her boutique ice cream.
$9 down the drain. I won't be going back.

Recommend | Not helpful

Eclectic tastes, unique experience
by Christopher (1 review)
September 25, 2009 - Likes it
If you're willing to brave the line at the Short North location, or going to top off your Stauf's espresso with some Jeni's next door in Grandview you're going to be satisfied regardless of what your tastes are.
I go for the trio everytime, and it's always something different. In response to some other comments on here, it's true, everyone is not going to love every flavor, and certainly not just because it's expensive. Flavors like "goat cheese with dried cherries", or "cucumber melon with cheyanne" will test most boundaries of what ice cream is supposed to taste like, but Jeni's will give you infinite spoonfuls of any flavor to taste just to make sure that what you finally order is something you're going to love.
I couldn't image getting two orders of one flavor on your first visit without trying anything else...it is no wonder why you had a bad experience (to reviewer above/below). So go, be adventurous, try everything and leave satisfied.
