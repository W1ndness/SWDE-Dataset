OK, But Not Worth the Crowds
by Steve M (140 reviews)
April 26, 2010
Yeah, this place is certainly very good, but not the holy grail of NYC pizzerias as many make it out to be. Yes they cook it to perfection, the crust is just right, the sauce is tasty and they add just enough cheese, but do you really want to stand in line all day to find that out? No. Neither do I.
Every time I've come here It's always been at 2 PM- 4 PM on a weekday as to avoid the crowds of tourists, but lately, even that hasn't seemed to work. Even then, you'll have to wait on line for a good 15 minutes.
I hate to steer locals away from this great place, but save your time and try one of the John's, Patsy's or Totonno's franchises around town. They're every bit as good, if not better and you won't have to wait in line or share close quarters with the bus full of tourists from rural Indiana either.

Good Pizza, Horrific Noise, Traffic, etc.
by Jane Doe Eats - Seattle (179 reviews)
May 24, 2009 - Doesn't like it
The Don't Like it Vote aggregates our perception of the pizza, the establishment's environment and other features or characteristics that comprised our dining experience. The 45 min wait was not a factor.
Yes, the pizza is good, albeit the cheese is only fresh mozzarella so don't expect the pie to be covered wing-to-wing with greasy, gooey part-skim mozzarella like most places. We consumed a large half mushroom, half pepperoni and felt pretty amazing afterward. The flavor of the fresh mushrooms popped, the tiny discs of pepperoni were spicy and the crust was laudable, albeit tougher and drier than I expected.
The problems we had with this place included the limited menu selection, i.e. no salad , no draft beer and only one local beer, a decibel level that make us feel like we were at Chucky Cheese, tables so tightly crowded together that we had elbows within 12 inches of our plates, and one bathroom for 200 people, which seems to be the NYC standard because native New Yorker's have camel sized bladders and only poop at home.
We'll never go again but recommend taking it to go if you want to enjoy the pizza without the crowd, noise and other limitations.

There's mozzarella, then there's Grimalidi's mozzarella . . .
by Eternal M's Optimist (62 reviews)
May 27, 2009 - Likes it
I'm a sucker for a good slice of pizza, and this is definitely one of the better pizzas I've ever had, primarily because of the cheese. The mozzarella here strikes a perfect balance of chewiness, saltiness, and thickness, and there is just the right amount of the stuff on this coal-fired pie. If you want more, you can order extra mozzarella for only $2 more. And with several mounds of the stuff visible from your seat in the open kitchen, Grimaldi's knows how to flirt with all your senses.
Also don't be deterred by the looks of the line. They turn over tables pretty quickly, and what seems like a substantial wait is much shorter than expected. Plus you can just walk towards the pier and get some Brooklyn Ice Cream Factory while you wait.
Make sure to bring your greenbacks . . . like many places in NYC, this is a cash-only establishment.

Tourist trap
by Bianchi (48 reviews)
September 05, 2009 - Doesn't like it
If I had to describe Grimaldi's Pizzeria in one word it would be, "overrated". Yes, this famous Brooklyn pizzeria establishment is known for their pizza, but after having gone there myself, I must ask why. Driving up, I was surprised to see that the line was literally out the door and longer than anything I have ever seen at Di Fara. Naturally, I got excited. While ordering inside, I saw their coal-fired brick oven which produces the 850F heat to impart the crust with the right amount of crispiness and smokiness. When I held the pie in my hands I must say, the smell was simply intoxicating. My hand trembled slightly as I held my first slice.
"Wow", I thought to myself, "I might actually taste something that surpasses Di Fara's works of art." I took a deep breath, closed my eyes and took a bite.
The first thing you'll notice is the cheese. No, not because it tastes good, but because it is rubbery, yes rubbery. It's tough and chewy texture lead me to believe that I was eating grade B mozzarella cheese. Before you have time to complain, you'll notice the crust; not crispy, not doughy and hardly smoky but chewy (and no, not the Starwars' character). I don't know about you, but I want to eat my pizza and enjoy it, I do not want to give my jaw a workout. Grimaldi's only saving grace (as far as taste goes) was their sauce, which reminded me of Patsy's pizza sauce: sweet, but not sugary sweet, rather its tomato sweet. It also was not overcooked nor over seasoned, but just right.
Grimaldi's Pizzeria is said to be the best in all of New York City. I am now glad to know this NOT to be true. What makes a great pizza? Well, according to Grimaldi's it takes three things: fresh, quality ingredients, know-how, and a great oven. Sadly, I am not sure they have anyone of those qualities. Thankfully, the price for a large cheese is $15.20, but they only take cash, so be prepared.

There is better......
by theBKdiner.com (8 reviews)
April 01, 2009
This is one of those infamous places in the New York history books that will never disappear in our life-time. The pizza they serve is of a tradition called brick-oven baked and it is like no other. There is a resurgence of this baking process and a number of place that serve this type in and around the 5 boroughs. I have been to most of them.
Grimaldi's is housed in a tiny building just under the Brooklyn Bridge on the Brooklyn side. There is often a line outside, even in inclimate weather. Some are locals and alot are tourists. I often have to give directions to those asking. I am not a fan of Grimaldi's. I work in DUMBO during the day and many times the office will order their pizza. I have not flipped for it like others do. Main reason: the sauce. Why people think that you can open up a can of tomatoes, slop on dough, sprinkle some garlic powder, oregano, cheese and whatever else, bake it in a brick oven and think it will be great is beyond me. The sauce that is cooked and stirred for hours on the stove is the only way it should be. Now - they may argue they do that but I beg to differ, then they should cook it longer.
Grimaldi's boasts that their brick oven is coal-fired - only adding to the smoking flavor of the crust. This does not cover up the lack of flavor in their sauce and adds pollution as far as I am concerned. Want good pizza - brick oven and all that - go to Arturo's on Houston in the City. Excellent!
