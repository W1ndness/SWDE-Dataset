Completely and totally UNimpressed
by FireFly (34 reviews)
June 19, 2010 - Doesn't like it
I've been looking forward to trying this place for awhile, due to its high ratings in places like Urbanspoon, "best of" contests, and other restaurant review sites.
I finally did try it and was completely and utterly disappointed. I do not at all understand the high ratings. I was expecting the "dive"-y, hole-in-the-wall atmosphere. I'm totally cool with that, when I'm in the mood for it. I was NOT expecting a fairly pricey menu (for what the place is) of bland, poorly done Indian food. I'm left wondering if maybe many of the ratings are done by friends/family of the owners...that's how little sense this restaurant's extremely high score makes to me.
The food was a universal disappointment. I got to try different appetizers, dishes, rice, and naan, and there was NOTHING that did not disappoint. I can make much better naan and Indian rice myself.
I have found a favorite Indian restaurant in Omaha (Flavors, downtown...yum!) but I had been hoping that this would be an inexpensive almost-as-good nearby alternative for when I am shorter on cash. It is NOT inexpensive and is nowhere NEAR as good. Unless I'm invited with someone else, I doubt I'll be trying Mother India again. I have been to 3 other Indian restaurants in Omaha so far that are ALL much better.

Tasty, spicy Indian food - worth the extra $$
by angeldandruff (2 reviews)
April 09, 2010
Mother India is an extremely small restaurant/store/building with a very small menu and a hardly any room for the 7 - 8 tables that they have stuck inside. Some serious Tetris playing there. There is also an option to eat in the patio, but that is only if you prefer eating adjacent to a parking lot and looking at passing vehicles while you eat. The clever option is to order food to go. The menu, as mentioned before, is very short, but what they lack in quantity, they make up for it in quality. As an Indian, I can certify that this is THE best place to eat Indian food in Omaha. Granted, there are not many Indian restaurants here, but, hey, beats eating at The Dhaba.
The food can be made spicy according to your taste, and by spicy, I mean heat induced due to various spices, and not by using chilli/cayenne powder, or blending jalapenos into the food. The Tandoori chicken was soft and succulent, and the lamb vindaloo tasted great. And if you find the spice hitting you, quench it with a delicious Mango Lassi. The staff are courteous and friendly, and will suggest good dishes based on what your cravings are.
It is a little pricey, but if you are ordering to satisfy that occasional craving for good spicy food, Mother India is the place to go. Be advised, it gets crowded pretty quickly, and so, be sure to get here at around 11:15AM for lunch, if you intend to sit down and eat here.
