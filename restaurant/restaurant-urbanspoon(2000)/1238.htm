+ 8 more photos from Gulf Coast Guy Not wowed...
by Gulf Coast Guy (820 reviews)
August 03, 2010 - Doesn't like it
Maybe it was the build up, maybe it was the place, maybe it was a combo of the two BUT we found this one to be a HUGE disappointment.
Atmosphere is borderline Village Inn Pancake House boring first of all.
Menu is diverse and so is the quality of the food we ordered and were served. We definitely gave them every opp to please us with a nice variety of food sampled on this occasion.
Chips and Salsa were a decent enough start but many places have decent chips and salsa. One major factor not in their favor is not having Margaritas of the true Mexican eatery, WITH Mas Tequila. This is almost a disqualifier alone. Duck salad was a hit, served medium rare on the bird. (Not really Mexican though). Enchilada Trio was a hit and miss platter with the one winner being the duck. Seafood and Chicken versions were ho hummers. Guac was awful and could have been store bought. Asada App was deemed good but is it enough?
Sangria was good but does not make up for not having Margs IMO.
Overall, we do not think we need to visit again, although we might sometime when in the area.
On my four shell scale:
Atmosphere 2.50
Service 2.75
Food 2.50
Drink 2.00(No Margs hurts)
Overall 2.50
Is The Cantina better than this? We will let you know when we try it soon.

what happened?
by Maria Pia (19 reviews)
May 11, 2009 - Doesn't like it
I remember when this restaurant first came on the scene. We kept it to ourselves in order NOT to encourage those that couldn't appreciate it to stay away so it wouldn't get "dumbed down". THAT is exactly what happened.
The food was something that has not been done in the Tampa bay area. A high end Mexican cuisine that never cheaped out on ingredients.
What happened? I have been back 3 times in the past year to be disappointed every time. The lack of quality of product is beyond obvious if you were part of the early years. Every time we hope they just had a bad day to be disappointed again. The care is just not there. The ingredients are thrown on the plate creating that classic low brow Mexican soup that is all too familiar in America. And the sex appeal of an evening out in an exotic ambiance is gone. Now it's all about turnover in order to squeeze in people that "don't want it too spicy" wearing Teva's and shorts too short for a table with no cloths...Sorry Mesa. I'm out!

Always great
by zzzzdoc (4 reviews)
September 17, 2009 - Likes it
This is one of our weekly go-to restaurants. Food is always great - no surprises, no off days. Often the specials are really worth getting. They have had some real winners there. I really miss the scallops with plantains, though.
The duck enchiladas, Shrimp and Grouper al mojo, tuna, and espresso steak are routinely wonderful. And the red sangria is a legend in town. The caesar salad and pastor salad are large and flavorful.
Wait staff is very efficient, which is great in a very busy restaurant. They remember your favorites and always give you a warm welcome.
There appears to be very little turnover in staff here, which I'm sure is a great testament to the ownership.
Easily on our short list of the best restaurants in Tampa Bay.
