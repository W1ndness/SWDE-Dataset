+ 1 more photos from Nancy Wow, but Pah-lease...
by Nancy (49 reviews)
January 25, 2010 - Likes it
So, just so you know, I have drunk the Koolaid. I loved the food. My kids loved the food, we will be back. It was lovely, the whipped cream on the hot chocolate, the herbs stuck to the bacon, the 3 quarters of lemon for the Big German, the TASTE of the pancake, just great. I liked that there wasn't 12 different additions to the pancake, just fresh fruit or no. (if the pancake is good, you don't need chocolate chips...seriously, am I wrong?).
That said, the place is too friggin' loud, some noise absorbtion (anywhere) would help. How can I enjoy my breakfast if I have to ask my kids 3 times what they just said? And, with a full restaurant, we sat in front of the only empty table for 4 for 25 minutes waiting for (another) table to open. Halfway through our meal we found out why...friends of one of the waitresses came in (she had held that table open for 45 min). More than a bit annoying when I know that we could've come and gone in that time (and, honestly, I had to listen to my 9 year old whine he was starving, why couldn't he sit there?). Yeah, it isn't my restaurant but seriously, on a Saturday morning? Again, kudos on the vittles!

pear-havarti heaven
by Dashery (4 reviews)
July 18, 2010
One dish on the menu keeps me coming back, despite the long wait and somewhat noisy interior: the pear havarti pie. This custardy, sweet deliciousness is a heavenly mix of sweet pears and chewy, mildly tangy cheese. Topped with creme fraiche, it's a happygasm on a plate.
The Scotch egg is also a tasty, rather oddball item, even if it is a vegan's nightmare.
