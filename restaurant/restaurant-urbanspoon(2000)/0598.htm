Nice place...
by Tina (3 reviews)
June 27, 2010 - Likes it
This place is tiny, like postage stamp tiny, but their food is delicious, and the service was perfect, only complaints I had was my plate was 70 % rice, enough rice to feed 4 people, with my 3 kabobs on it, and their yummy hummus, and garlic sauce for dipping etc. I would have much preferred less rice, and instead some sort of veggie or something else. Also there is not much elbow room if they get busy, but the prices are reasonable, and the food is good so, I don't mind too much the tight space. This is a nice place for a quite luncheon with friends.

*Sigh*...will the disappointment ever end?
by Mr. Eats (11 reviews)
July 11, 2010 - Doesn't like it
This tiny place gets an 'A' for effort in regards to Atmosphere. The food was entirely a different matter. Oasis was more like a 'Mirage'...the sights and smells here had me excited about the entree that I had ordered, but when the food came the disappointment on my face must have been obvious because the waiter couldn't help but join me in a mutual grimace. The shishkabob's seemed to disappear on a heaping plate of rice! If that wasnt enough, they were so dry it was like chewing on Beef Jerky!
And heres another place that seems to have great a staff, but again Im not paying for the chitchat. I really just want some good food!

We love it!
by Miss Gayle (12 reviews)
June 21, 2009 - Likes it
We have been eating the lunch buffet on a regular basis for a couple of years now, and have taken visitors out to dinner there numerous times. We have NEVER had a bad meal or a bad experience. There are plenty of vegetarian dishes, meat dishes, shiskabobs, pita-wiches, fatoush and other salads, and, of course, Falafels! The clove (lebanese) tea is good, as is the mint tea - and you can order turkish coffee. Everything's good, and I'm pretty sure we've tried it all. Enjoy!

Quite a find!
by the smibbit (18 reviews)
July 02, 2009 - Really likes it
So, who'da thunk that in Lexington Kentucky there'd be a fantastic down-home Middle-Eastern restaurant where the fool is spicy, the fried kibbe perfectly executed, the falafel not dry and crumbly, the baba ghanouj pleasantly smoky, and the waraq 'inab (stuffed grape leaves) flavorful and tender. Not one but two (!) tables of customers speaking Arabic. Music to my ears. Will be back whenever I'm in town.
