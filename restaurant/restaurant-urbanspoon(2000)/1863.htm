Victory at Victoria's
by Tri (3 reviews)
March 01, 2010 - Likes it
All day, I had craved for seafood.
It's kind of craving that has me thinking about the ghost-town feeling of Galveston, before the reckless battering of passing hurricanes, and the blinking neon signs that run on life-sized plasters of blue shelled crab and along a fringe palm trees. So, I texted Jason, informed him of my plight, and we agreed to go to Brookline. First we stopped by Coolidge Corner, taking a direct route on the 66 bus into Allston, walked for some time past the old theater, gave a nod in the Paris creperie, and took the long walk to Commonwealth Avenue in a dark street as I told riddles that kept us stirred for some time (something about two sisters and a mysterious stranger.)
Softshell Crab, Victoria's Seafood
We decided to try Victoria's Seafood, a Chinese restaurant establishment that I was highly recommended by Phuong, one of my co-workers this past summer. To put it simply, Victoria's was the best Chinese food I've ever had in my life, needless to say in Boston. I loved everything about the place. You walk into the a seemingly dingy exterior (aged red edifices and hazey, obfuscated windows, that would otherwise have you dismissing the entire shop to begin with had you not known about the place). The tables are packed with people - families are wrestled into tight corners and eating around large round tables with lazy susans, sometimes two or three seats more crowded than in any western restaurant, wielding chopsticks at a menacing rate, piling on rice in mouths like a bulldozer. The restaurant is loud; chatter is boisterous and the clamoring of dishware rings through the space. Still, it's authentic; and the patrons are mostly Chinese.
As Jason and I walked to our two-seater in the back room, we noticed that every table had plates and plates of seafood. We knew what we had to get.
Twin Lobster (Ginger and Scallion), Victoria's Seafood
One look at the menu and we were mesmerized not only by the selection, but the fabulously low prices. I would highly recommend turning to the back of the menu and taking a quick look at the Chef's Special (which apparently changes every month, adjusted to reflect current seasonal prices of the seafood.)
Feeling rather ravenous, we ordered:
* Softshell Crab, salted over diced cabbage and chili peppers ($10)
* Twin lobster, marinated with ginger slices and scallion ($17)
* Steamed tilapia over a soy-ginger suace ($12)
* Steamed rice ($2)
The food came out quick. The service was efficient. Not perfect, but perfectly fine for a Chinese restaurant. Waitors refilled our waters and brought the food out without any fuss. We weren't pestered at all to be rung up for the bill, which made for a pleasant eat.
Steadm tilapia, Victoria's Seafood
On a culinary level, the food was phenomenal. It's so rare to find good seafood without busting up your wallet. So I'm so happy I had a chance to satisfy my craving for lobster for so cheap.
The softshell crab was crunchy and crisp, perfectly salted and you could taste the griddy taste of the ocean with every bite. The lobster, exquisitely seasoned and tender-soft. Fresh, obviously, from the tanks of glass displays of live lobster and crabs as one walks past to the back room. (I highly recommend getting the ginger and scallion flavored version of the twin lobsters.) The steamed tilapia was moderately-sized but the soy sauce in which it bathed made the meat so impeccably savory and delightful. Even Jason, who was initially frightful of the sight of translucent fish fins poking out and glossy fish eyes staring back at home, enjoyed a hearty meal. The trio of seafood complimented steamed white rice and a side plate of chili and soy sauce perfectly.
Lobster tank, Victoria's Seafood
For the adventurous diners among us, after you're done with dinner, you could even go over to the tanks of live fish and crustacean and witness how the cooks net the creatures and haul them into the kitchen for preparation. So, if you're ever in Brookline or in dire need of cheap seafood, give Victoria's a try. It may end up impressing you. I know I'll certainly come back.
