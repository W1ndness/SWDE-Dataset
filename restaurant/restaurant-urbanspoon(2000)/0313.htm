awesome brunch bistro
by Kerry Tucker (6 reviews)
June 27, 2010 - Likes it
Well, I guess my thoughts about this place are in the name. It' is totally worth the 30 minute drive from denver to just eat brunch. Mussels are fantastic. just the right amount of cream, thyme and butter. And their burger is cooked perfectly, topped with caramalized onion and gruyere. It is to die for! Literally. All that juicy goodness is worth your arteries tempting fate!
The interior is everything a true brasserie should be. Quaint and comfortable with plenty of greenery and ornate decorations that combine to create a warm and welcoming atmosphere. I could sit there all day and be happy.
Service can sometimes be a little off, but that's to be expected in a college town. Other times it can be simply great. The servers all around seem to have good menu knowledge, there are just some who lack a certain finesse.
Either way, I definitely recommend this place. I absolutely love, love, love it!

Some food with your salt?
by Deb (3 reviews)
October 13, 2009 - Doesn't like it
I made the mistake of thinking this would be a great place for the special occasion of our one-year anniversary. While it is a good place, it was far from the top quality French cuisine and service experience I was expecting. The server forgot to bring bread to our table, and claimed after the meal that it was by request only. They were out of many of the better wines by the glass, and the server would not check with management to see if they would make a substitution for a lesser bottle. Finally, both my husbands steak and my vegetables had so much salt they were not edible - and this is coming from someone who likes her food on the saltier side.
Overall, it seemed like a place where college students take a date to pretend to be fancy, but not a real top quality dining experience.
