Nothing but Disappointed...
by Kevin (2 reviews)
May 23, 2010 - Doesn't like it
We stopped in on a week night to check out this new addition to Cahaba Village's lineup of quality restaurants. The facility was certainly well decorated, with modern designs and plenty of seating inside (and outside). We were greeted by a rather quirky cashier, who took our order, managed to get it all sorted out, and gave us our number. After filling our cups with our beverage of choice (they had about six different varieties of brewed tea and a broad selection of Coca-Cola products from their fountain machine), we strolled along towards our booth being followed by one of their associates with only a portion of our meal. We were left thinking how our order could have been filled so quickly, but proceeded to take our seat and receive the salad. We quickly found that the salad had either been made 5 hours earlier, or just pre-made, because although it is a salad, portions of the salad that should have been hot were droopy and disgusting and completely drowned in flavorless, water-dressing. The salad also had wilted, soggy 'fried onions' on it, and had chicken that had been micro-diced and had the texture of a canned mixture. The asian dumplings arrived and were delicious in flavor but very cold on the inside, as if they were steamed long enough to thaw them, but not cook them. Next came the breadsticks, which were actually great, but the sauce that came with them was cold, flavorless, and super acidic. Finally, the remainder of the order was filled (none of the order came all together, it came out one at a time, about 5 mins apart). The beef stroganoff was very hot when it came, but was something that I would believe came from a microwave tray. The noodles were a sad, rehydrated mess with salty, add-water sauce. The only thing 'real' about the dish, were the mushrooms that were sliced into it. The dish was 100% unoriginal in every way, and could probably be found at your local supermarket. The experience was super disappointing, as I was expecting the same fresh approach that is offered at Maki Fresh and Newks next door. This restaurant is a complete botch of cuisine-- I will NEVER be visiting again, and I suggest dodging this place at all costs.

Huge Portions
by Noel Bagwell (20 reviews)
June 25, 2010 - Likes it
When you first come to Nothing But Noodles, the menu seems overwhelming. Who knew there would be this many choices? My advice is: don't come alone. Come with someone with whom you don't mind sharing a dish; find something you both think you'll like, and share it. The portions are (too) big, unless you're trying to gain wait for high school sports (wrestling, football, etc.) or something.
Also, don't sit near people with children at this place. No offense to parents or kids intended, but this restaurant needs some serious noise-reduction (maybe some decorative noise-cancelling foam pads on the walls / ceiling) or something. This restaurant has the acoustic ambiance of a steel drum.
The noodles are, I have been told, made fresh in the restaurant daily. I believe it. They're delicious! Also, I like that, similar to places like Pei Wei Asian Diner, this place has a soda fountain and serves Coca-Cola products! That might just be one of my little idiosyncrasies, but I prefer Coke to Pepsi, and I like being able to refresh my beverage myself, rather than having to wait for a server.
All-in-all, I recommend this place, but either split a dish with someone or be prepared to take your left-overs home.
