  • We are regular customers of Indochine. We love everything on their menu. The chef always has Specials and they are so good. Now, when we come to Indochine (since we come so often), we tend to order from their Daily Special Boards. The items tend to be more expensive than the regular menu items but they are so worth it. The curries have been getting spicier and we were told since they expanded to this big location, the customers have been asking for spicier curries (not too much broth but more paste and more vegetables). We love it! I read the first review and it did sound to me like someone who is a competitor of Indochine who is not doing well enough to describe the food at Indochine that does not even sound like coming from the Indochine we know. Indochine cannot be this popular if the food is what the first reviewer said. Try them for yourself. You will love it.

  • Order: Beef Cubes, Green Curries, Ginger Jumbo Prawns, Rice-Crusted Seabass, Salmon Rolls

  • The first reviewer reinforced my belief that mean people are everywhere and they hate to see great people succeed.
    We have been going to Indochine for 3+ years and Yume and Jeff (owners) are the most amazing hosts you will ever meet. The food is fantastic and the only thing that comes from a food vendor frozen is the ice cream.

Indochine about two months ago moved into a bigger and much more elegant looking place on Mainstreet in Parker Station. The food is terrific and the service has always been spectacular.
    As for the guy in the first review not coming back, I think I can speak for the people in Parker and the many loyal clients of Indochine... we would still welcome you back but we are sorry you are so jealous of such good people... :)

  • I was reading the previous review and felt compelled to do my own review (I have done my reiews of Indochine several times already). I have been at Indochine 7 times since they moved to the new place and love it every time. The place is busy all the times! My best friend is Vietnamese - that was how I was introduced to Indochine. Their Vietnamese Noodle Bowl is amazing with the best eggrolls around. Not sure what the other reviewer talked about eggrolls out of frozen food vendor. Beef cubes is the best around - and I ate at Slanted Door in SF many times (Slanted Door is known for their shaken beef a.k.a beef cubes). Indochine's food does not make me tired after eating it since it does not have MSG and mostly are gluten-free. Sound to me the other reviewer might be a competitor of Indochine and trying to be vicious. Well, Indochine is extremely popular and it has huge following among the foodies (I am included!). The place is classy and feel high-end which unlike many Asian joins I have found here in Colorado. It is un-pretentious and yet sophisticated - perfect for Indochine's type of clientele. It is a gem.

  • Order: Beef Salad, Beef Cubes, Vietnamese Noodle Bowls, Green Curry Tofu, Mango and Sticky Rice
