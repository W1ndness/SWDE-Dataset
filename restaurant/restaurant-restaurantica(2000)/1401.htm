  • A regular’s guide to the Tom Cat.
    Table 1 – aka the millionaires club. If you are retired and racist this is the table for you. If not, steer clear from these guys. D-
    The hook. A cool place to sit. Mostly made up of folks who like to have a couple of drinks, watch a little TV and bs a little. A-
    Other side of the Well. Mostly made up of gamblers. Folks here like to play the Keno type game on the TV. Also, there is one nut always sitting here who for some reason likes to blurt out the word “Toledo” for no reason at all. C+
    RFI Table. This table is usually reserved for the same group of guys who work together. If you sit here, you might be asked to move by a certain time. No grade
    Back area. Probably the best place to sit in the whole bar. Mostly made up of pool players and sports fans who are genuinely nice. A+

    From opening time to a little before the end of happy hour, this bar is 98% regulars. It might seem cliquish at first, but the majority of the folks are friendly enough to newcomers as long as they are not jerks. If you are the type to come into a bar and start fights, save that for after 9 o’clock or so, because if you start crap with a regular, so will not only have the other regulars all over you but you will more than likely get 86’d.
    Morning times are mostly filled with retired folks. The hook fills up towards 1 or so, and the pool players are there between 2 and 6. Best deals: Hebrew National Hot Dog, Cajun Burger, Chicken Quesadilla. Fridays at happy hour are free spicy wings.

  • Order: Appetizers, entrees, drinks
