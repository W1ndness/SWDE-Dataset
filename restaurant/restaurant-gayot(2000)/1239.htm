Situated on the shores of Old Tampa Bay, Oystercatchers is about as upscale as a fish house can get, having undergone a $4 million renovation. The 35-acre setting in a nature preserve offers some of the area's best views of the bay, and the food is on target in terms of preparation and freshness. Chef Kenny Hunsberger has overhauled the menu, most remarkably with the weekend brunch, an array of luxurious raw bar items, carving stations and traditional breakfast fare. One popular selection, when available, is the Kookaburra wings---chicken wings with a "secret" sauce. At dinner, fish is the main attraction, the Gulf's finned bounty offered in a variety of preparations and with sophisticated sides (braised bok choy with sesame oil or caramelized fennel). The bar attracts a hip professional crowd with its stylish list of house cocktails and thoughtful wines-by-the-glass program. If mosquitoes are at bay, dine outside on the outdoor patio or deck, and try to plan your visit around sunset when the pinks, purples and oranges of the Florida sun melt into the Bay's shimmering water.

Overpriced, underdelivered...don't make this mistake please
by sail6382 on 09/02/2008
 
So disappointed. Went for dinner on Friday night. It a very posh venue with beautiful views, but that is where my positives end. We would have done better by going to McDonalds. In fact, one of the sauces tasted like Big Mac Sauce that you would take from the packets and squeeze into a delightfully pretty bowl for show. My Swordfish was way overcooked and all sauces were brought on the side for tasting. This meant that the fish itself had no flavor and you had to work to make it taste good. When I am paying that much for a meal I expect it to be prepared for me. Another in the party ordered the Filet which tasted, no joke, like licking a campfire. The smoke flavor was so overpowering that you could not taste the meat. Had we been alone, we would have complained but with the company it was inappropriate. Honestly, I have had better fish and steak at places like Applebees. The crab cakes were edible but as an appetizer we were told was sufficient for four people, much too small. Also, we were told that the sides would be served family style, but they arrived on the plate so we did not share them. So incredibly dissapointed and it sucks that it was $150.00 for two people and a meal we hated.
