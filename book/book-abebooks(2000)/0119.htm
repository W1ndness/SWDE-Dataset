Editorial Reviews:
Synopsis:

Follows the sensational 1925 murder trial of African-American doctor Ossian Sweet, who was accused of murdering a white person during a mob attack on his home, in a tale that includes a history of the Sweet family and a portrait of his attorney, Clarence Darrow.


Review:

"[B]y far the most cogent and thorough account yet of the trial [of Ossian Sweet] and its aftermath. One of its virtues is the way Boyle vividly recreates the energy and menace of Detroit in 1925."

New York Times Book Review, 09/12/2004
