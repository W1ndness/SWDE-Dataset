Editorial Reviews:
Synopsis:

Seductions of Rice With a depth of passion and experience, and an ability to embrace and convey richness of place and taste, the authors of the groundbreaking Flatbreads and Flavors embarked on a far-reaching excursion to find the world's most essential and satisfying food. Along the way, they experienced firsthand dozens of varieties of rice, offering unimaginable subtleties of taste, as well as a staggering array of foods to accompany them, all providing a simple way to get flavor and variety on the table. Seductions of Rice is the glorious result: two hundred easy-to-prepare dishes from the world's great rice cuisines, illuminated by stories, insights, and more than two hundred photographs of people, places, and wonderful food. Cherished dishes--Chinese stir-frys, Spanish paellas, Japanese sushi, Indian thorans, Thai salads, Turkish pilafs, Italian risottos--are shared not just as recipes, but as time-honored traditions. Seductions of Rice will change the way we eat, the way we prepare and appreciate our food. It's as easy as putting a pot of rice on to cook!


Review:

"Naomi Duguid and Jeffrey Alford...write fascinating and beautiful books, filled with Jeffrey's stories...and Naomi's lush, gorgeous photographs.THE SEDUCTIONS OF RICE abounds with comfort foods, perhaps because rice lends itself to them..."

Corby Kummer, Atlantic Unbound, 04/27/1999

Review:

"...SEDUCTIONS OF RICE is the book to end all books on rice. If authors Duguid and Alford haven't included it, it probably isn't worth knowing....Think of something--anything, I dare you--and THE SEDUCTIONS OF RICE has it covered....[A] triumph of a cookbook: one whose sheer weight and volume can't help but inspire admiration....The recipes--and there are many--are clear and concise and the directions are easy to follow. As well, each recipe is prefaced with an interesting story...."

Linda Richards, January Magazine, October 1998
