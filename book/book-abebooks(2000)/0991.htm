Synopsis:

From 1954 to 1981, Maeve Brennan contributed to The New Yorkers "Talk of the Town" department under the pen name "the long-winded lady". Her unforgettable, sketches -- prose snapshots of life in the streets, diners, and cheap hotels just off Times Square -- are a timeless, bittersweet tribute to what she calls the "most ambitious, most comical ... the saddest and coldest and most human of cities".
