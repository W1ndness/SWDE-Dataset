Editorial Reviews:
Review:

"Among many other things, [Shackleton] was an opportunist out for the main chance, and a pictorial record of his expedition was crucial to its financial success. That's why he and his photographer, Frank Hurley, went back to the sinking Endurance and lugged many photographic plates across the ice and sea....They are as stunning as ever, and surely Shackleton and Hurley would be delighted that the images they fought so tenaciously to save have endured."

Robert R. Harris, New York Times Book Review, 12/20/2001
