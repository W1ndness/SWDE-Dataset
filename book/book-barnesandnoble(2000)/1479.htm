      Synopsis

      Since 1993, "Garage Logic ®" listeners have been fleshing out the mythical town, populating it with characters and filling in the nooks and crannies that have made it such a special place. Now, in Garage Logic ®: A Companion Guide to Life in the Radio Town, the streets come alive. There really is a fabulous Spoon Lake, a downtown, a Knack Hardware and Lounge, and a golf course called Creature Path. Listeners, who had such a strong hand in creating the town, now can read the stories and listen to residents of Garage Logic ® tell in their own words why the seat of Gumption County is so blessed by common sense and good times in "The Opener," "Last Drop Days," the Fourth of July fireworks, and a shocking discovery about garage wood that stunned the whole town.

      Biography

      Columnist and radio show host, Joe Soucheray, is the author of several books, including Waterline: Of Fathers, Sons, and Boats, Once There Was a Ballpark: The Season of the Met, 1956-1981; Souch! Sports writing of Joe Soucheray of the Minneapolis Tribune; Modern, Caring, Sensitive Male: A Curmudgeon Columnist Looks at Life; and several books in the series, [Creative Education Sports Superstars.] He was a sports columnist for the Minneapolis Tribune before he joined the St. Paul Pioneer Press. Soucherary has been the host of Garage Logic ® since 1993, where he serves as mayor and fireworks commissioner. The show is broadcast from ESPN 1500 in St. Paul and heard on 22 affiliates. Not entirely surrounded by Euphorians, but close, Soucheray and his wife Jennifer live in St. Paul, Minnesota.

      Soucheray & Keillor: a couple of Midwesterners who make a living by ridiculing Midwesternersby Anonymous

      Reader Rating:
      See Detailed Ratings

      June 14, 2010: It's highly amusing that Joe Soucheray has a book about an imaginary community in Minnesota. Joe is an am talk-radio host and and newspaper columnist who regularly ridicules liberals, yet he has essentially ripped off (or maybe parodied) Garrison Keillor's imaginary community in Minnesota: Lake Wobegon. Soucheray is probably the best-known conservative media person from Minnesota. Keillor, on the other hand, is the state's best-known liberal. Keillor, who can be just as mean-spirited as Soucherary, is a bit more successful than Soucheray. Both men present images of false nostalgia about a bygone era when things were better than they are today. Both men make fun of Midwest provincialism but yet at the same time hold it up as a virtue. Basically, Keillor "got there first." Soucheray didn't invent his imaginary Midwest community until several years after Keillor invented his. I live in the Twin Cities Neither Keillor nor Soucheray are interesting enough that I'd bother to see either of them in a live performance. However, I do have to say that Keillor is obviously more talented than Soucheray.
