    Annotation

    On the 20th anniversary of its publication, this classic manifesto is updated with an important new preface by the author. Freire reflects on the impact his book has had, and on many of the issues it raises for readers in the 1990s. These include the fundamental question of liberation and inclusive language as it relates to Freire's own insights and approaches.

    January 07, 2005: This is by far the best book I read during grad school...even if I didn't have to read this for a class, I would have devoured Fierre's masterpiece! No other text has ever given me a greater understanding of the plight of those oppressed by the 'majority' or dominating populations. My whole understanding of the world and history was turned on its head. I recommend this book to anyone interested in social justice, affirmative action, civil rights, minority struggles...it applies not only to the community in Brazil, but all peoples facing social and economic oppression.

    October 25, 2002: This book is a guide on not only how to teach, but how to go about everyday life. Pedagogy of the Oppressed gives a detailed outline on how to free yourself as well as others around you. While Freire was relating this book to large amounts of people that are being oppressed it can, and does relate to everyday life. The book is hard to read though, but do not let this discourage you from reading it. Just buy a dictionary when you purchase this book. Then sit down and read.
