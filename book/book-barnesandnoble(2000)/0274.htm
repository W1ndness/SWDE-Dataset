    Synopsis

    It's the first day of school! Join the kids as they prepare for kindergarten, packing school supplies, posing for pictures, and the hardest part of all-saying goodbye to Mom and Dad. But maybe it won't be so hard once they discover just how much fun kindergarten really is!

    Annotation

    When a group of children prepares for and experiences the first day of kindergarten, the parents are the ones who have trouble adjusting.

    Children's Literature

    It's the eve of the first day of school in this adorable takeoff on the classic Clement C. Moore Christmas poem. The children are busily preparing for their first day of kindergarten, packing school backpacks and dreaming of school supplies. The highly anticipated day arrives and they pose for pictures and prepare to say goodbye to Mom and Dad. Miss Sunrise and her delightful classroom filled with books, toys and fun activities make saying good-bye easy, for the children at least. Teary-eyed parents need a bit more support—a final hug and a happy wave and the happy children are free to discover how much fun kindergarten really is. This colorful story provides a way to calm those first day jitters. This would be a great gift for preschool graduation. Parents and children will both love the surprise ending. 2001, Grosset & Dunlap, $3.49. Ages 3 to 5. Reviewer:Julie Eick Granchelli

    About the Author
    Natasha Wing is the author of children's books and magazine articles. She created the popular The Night Before series about holidays and big events in kids' lives. Several of the titles have appeared on the New York Times and USA Today best seller lists. Natasha lives in Northern California and loves to do school visits to get back in touch with her readers.
