Author/Artist Review

Author: Carrie Turansky
Located in: Lawrenceville, NJ
Submitted: October 26, 2005

    Tell us a little about yourself.  I am a full-time wife and mother as well a writer. My husband and I have been in ministry for over twenty-five years. I was born and raised in Oregon, but I have also lived in Hawaii, Belgium, Kenya - East Africa, and now live in New Jersey. I have five great kids who I homeschooled for many years. I am now enjoying my first grandson who is just about two. When I am not writing I enjoy gardening, reading, spending time with my family, and walking around the lake near our house. I also lead a women's Bible study and oversee women's ministry at my church.

    What was your motivation behind this project?  All four co-authors have passed their 25th wedding anniversary. Three of us are pastors' wives and have counseled many couples facing the challenges in those middle years. We wanted to write stories that highlighted the challenges of this season of life and give readers hope and encouragement to make their marriages stronger.

    What do you hope folks will gain from this project?  We hope these stories will inspire as well as entertain readers, especially those who are in the "middle years" of marriage. Each novella features a couple who is approaching their twenty-fifth wedding anniversary and is facing a difficult issue that could either destroy their marriage or make it stronger depending on their response.

    Who are your influences, sources of inspiration or favorite authors / artists?  I am blessed to be a member of American Christian Fiction Writers. This group includes well-known authors as well as aspiring authors. The daily encouragement I receive through our email loop motivates me to press on and grow as a writer and as a Christian. Some of my favorite Christian fiction authors are Debra Raney, Siri Mitchell, Jan Karon, Karen Kingsbury, Elizabeth White, Angela Hunt, Irene Hannon, Colleen Coble, Tricia Goyer, Laura Jensen Walker, Vickie McDonough, Tamela Hancock Murray, and new authors Bonnie Blythe and Marilyn Griffith.

    Anything else you'd like readers / listeners to know:  God is the author of marriage. We need to treasure our mates and look to the Lord for help, strength, and courage. Glean wisdom from His word, and you can face any difficulty in your marriage. Wedded Bliss is possible when your eyes are on the Lord and Christ-like love is your goal.
