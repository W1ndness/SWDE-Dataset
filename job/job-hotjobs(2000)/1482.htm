HR Business Partner: Broadcom

With our commitment to innovation, our value of leadership and our drive for excellence, Broadcom is a center for entrepreneurial challenge in communications technologies. Challenge Yourself!

Innovation
At Broadcom, you will be part of cutting-edge technology innovation. This is your opportunity to improve your skills and abilities by working with industry experienced leaders and a highly talented and accomplished workforce. We have an extensive and broad IP portfolio featuring over 11,000 U.S. and foreign patents and applications. This portfolio is recognized annually by IEEE as one of the top ranked portfolios among semiconductor companies.

Leadership
We understand the value of change and take calculated risks to maintain our lead in communications technologies. Our leadership originates from our engineering innovators with almost 75% of our workforce covering software and hardware engineering. We excel not only in the vision of our business leaders, but also in platforms to leverage market convergence. The result is long-term success and stability in the fiscal market.

Excellence
We stand behind our results-oriented culture. You will be an essential member of a small team of smart people focused on solving challenges that directly impact our business relationships. Learn what it means to be part of a team that delivers exciting technologies to the top customers worldwide. At Broadcom, you will have the opportunity to be an entrepreneur and see your talents make an immediate, measurable and lasting impact on our continued success.


Job Description
The Role

The HR Business Partner at Broadcom plays an instrumental role in the organization: coaching, mentoring, giving compensation guidance, organizational and management development, implementation of HR programs, conflict management, and varied hands-on activities.

The successful candidate will work with the Sales organization providing them with a broad view of the many business challenges and opportunities. This is a challenging role - it requires a multi-faceted approach to a broad range of groups, personalities, cultures, and challenges.

After integrating yourself into the business you will be relied upon heavily, and will be the go to person for a client group including employees, managers, directors, and vice presidents. After a few months, you will be leading key projects globally for your business units, using your consultative approach and keen eye for detail. After a year, you'll be a fully integrated Business Partner - able to pro-actively manage and seek out solutions to problems before they exist. Additionally you will have the opportunity to work on or lead corporate wide projects and initiatives, making a difference beyond the groups you directly support. You will be visible to your customers to the extent they will see you as part of their team. With this, you will understand the line between being an HR professional and a business partner and will manage expectations maturely, developing creative solutions to the challenges you face.

The Requirements

As a HR Professional, you'll already know the list of competencies required for this role is extensive and ever changing. The level of deep knowledge means the role requires a minimum of a Bachelors degree plus 8-10 years of related experience in an HR Generalist capacity. Ideal candidates will have experience of providing HR guidance to North America, Europe and Asia Pacific regions.

Experience in complex employee relations, federal and state laws, verbal and written skills, compensation analysis, and advanced Microsoft Office skills should be a given. What sets the candidate apart is experience in managing mergers and acquisitions, incredible proven business acumen, strong multi-country project management, and a great sense of humor.
