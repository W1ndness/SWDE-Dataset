Submission Manager: Image Solutions Inc.

The role of the Submission Manager is to plan, execute, and finalize projects according to strict deadlines and within budget. This includes acquiring resources and coordinating the efforts of team members, other in-house functional groups as well as coordinating efforts with other third-party contractors or consultants retained by the client in order to deliver projects according to plan. The Submission Manager will also define the project’s objectives and oversee quality control throughout its life cycle.

The Submission Manager will work closely with the other Submissions Managers to serve as a mentor for junior level staff.

The Submission Manager’s primary responsibilities will include the following:


REQUIREMENTS:

The successful candidate will have a combination of the following skills/experience:

  • Overall knowledge of industry standards related to eCTD, and other variations of electronic submissions and document publishing
  • Bachelor's degree, plus at least three years related experience
  • Experienced or have knowledge in regulatory electronic document management systems and/or system requirements for publishing system integration
  • Experienced with or have knowledge with leading industry tools including but not limited to MS Word, Authoring templates, Documentum, Livelink, eCTDXPress, CoreDossier, ISI Toolbox
  • Experience in producing electronic and/or paper submissions for various application types including but not limited to NDAs, BLAs, ANDAs, INDs, MAAs, NDS, etc. in various formats including CTD, eCTD, NeeS, eNDA, paper applications, etc.
  • Detail oriented with good organizational and communication skills
  • Hands-on publishing skills for submission and document level publishing
  • Ability to manage several tasks simultaneously and across multiple projects
  • Strong written, verbal & presentation skills
  • Essential experience at working both independently and in a team-oriented, collaborative environment within a global team
  • Strong inter-personal skills and professional relationships
  • Persuasive, encouraging, and motivating with an ability to elicit cooperation from a wide variety of sources, including upper management, clients, and other departments
  • Ability to travel 30% or as required
  • Overtime may be required in order to meet project deadlines.



ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:

Electronic Submission Capability Assessment

  • Act as subject matter expert to assist clients on the fundamentals of eCTD and lifecycle management
  • Produce recommendations for clients with respect to submission format, processes, and standards
  • Lead internal discussions and client meetings
  • Define project scope, goals and deliverables that support business goals in collaboration with senior management and stakeholders
  • Develop full-scale project plans and associated communications documents
  • Estimate the resources and participants needed to achieve project goals
  • Identify and manage project dependencies, critical path items and prepare for workload peaks and flexibility to accommodate critical milestones.
  • Assist in development of requirements, pre-sales activities, Request for Proposals (RFPs), compilation of vendor evaluation feedback
  • Provide critical assessment of electronic publishing processes and solutions
  • Maintain up to date knowledge of relevant regulatory guidances, requirements and specifications as well as industry best practices. Communicates new information to colleagues and ensures processes are kept current
  • Communicates effectively with external clients and internal associates to achieve project goals.



Overall Expectation of Capabilities

  • Provide guidance and assistance for clients including best practices for document types, attributes, and folder hierarchy
  • Indentify and communicate issues in timely manner. Look ahead for possible issues and take action to mitigate, manage and resolve.
  • Provide electronic submission publishing subject matter expertise including best practices for compilation, review, and approval
  • Provide document authoring assistance including MS Word templates, style guide, e-sub ready definition, and author, review, and approval processes in collaboration with other ISI service departments
  • Ability to participate in workload sharing and balancing efforts across GRO teams
  • Work with clients to create processes based on business requirements, available system functionality, and industry best practices
  • Assist in the development of Working Instruction documents, QC checklists and/or SOPs
  • Coordinate the start up activities such as identifying the initial user lists for hosted projects, coordinating applicable product support, and establishing document exchange methods
  • Support Regulatory-related training and workshops such as history of electronic submissions, eCTD, etc.
  • Effectively communicate project expectations to team members and stakeholders in a timely and clear fashion
  • Work directly with the project lead for issue resolutions and publishing/process questions
  • Be available for after hours support to team members and clients in different time zones
  • Delegate tasks and responsibilities to appropriate personnel
  • Identify and resolve issues and conflicts within the project team
  • Define project success criteria and disseminate them to involved parties throughout project life cycle
  • Contribute to hands-on publishing as needed
  • Conduct project close out meetings and create a recommendations report in order to identify successful and unsuccessful project elements
  • Perform quality assurance checks on finished products when appropriate
  • Other duties may be assigned

Project Financials

  • On a routine and timely basis, track project actuals (hours/costs, expenses) to budgets
  • Proactively manage changes in project scope, identify potential crises, and devise contingency plans
  • Lead development of “Change Orders” as necessary
