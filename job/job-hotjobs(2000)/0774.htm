Systems Administrator

Description - administer user rights, process flow of documents, manage interface with financial and receiving systems, and perform table maintenance to the extent permitted by the system(s) commensurate with the daily requirements of users.

Administrative support for Procurement Systems includes the following:

a) modifying and correcting errors created by users;

b) possessing expert working knowledge of the financial accounting system software momentum;

c) upgrading approval flow of orders to assist users with completing contract awards and modifications moving from one user to another;

d) entering vendors into the Procurement database;

e) Creating and monitoring user accounts.

System Administration for Integrated Logistics Management System (ILMS) includes in software functionality of CGI-AMS’s an understanding of Momentum Acquisitions and the following:

a) setting up and editing document prefixes;

b) updating changes to warrant types;

c) creating and updating users;

d) defining base PALT types;

e) provide de-obligation report and procedures

f) routing and rerouting work assignments, as requested;

g) possessing knowledge of the Department of State’s financial management system to incorporate new vendors into the ILMS data base that is integrated in real time with financial system;

h) tracking progress of workload assignments especially at end of the fiscal year;

i) facilitating individual reporting of FPDS-NG.

Administrative support duties (a-e) and (a-i) shall be completed within 4 hours of a user call and not more than one business day for complex actions.

Develop and maintain automated templates, checklists, milestone data, and forms to aid Momentum Acquisitions users in streamlining work processes. Tasks include:

a) developing and updating approval templates and associated routes;

b) developing and updating checklist templates

c) developing and updating clause templates

d) Adding and editing funding templates

e) developing and updating milestone templates

f) developing and updating predefined routing templates

Provide on-site and group training to users of Momentum Acquisitions contract management modules. Training should reduce user dependence on support and build individual understanding and expertise using the software for managing contracts. Possesses knowledge of Federal Procurement Regulations (FAR) and Department of State Acquisition Regulations (DOSAR) to effectively communicate and correlate contracting business process with enabling technology. The ability to update training material as needed.

Analyzes and recommends and/or assists technical team (which may include developing pro-active partnerships other contractors on the (ILMS program) with development system improvements and assist with implementation of approved process changes.

Provide various acquisition related reports using Microsoft Excel spreadsheet, system administrator must have the ability to analyze, validate the accuracy data being submitted to Department heads.

System Administrator must have the ability to enter data and provide reports using Universal Tracking Systems (UTT) or automatic workload tracking systems.

Mandatory requirement: Must be a U.S. Citizen and Must have a Security Clearance

Education/experience requirements: A bachelor’s degree or higher with major study in business administration, computer science, information systems management, mathematics, statistics, and provided knowledge equivalent to a major in the business field plus a minimum of 2-3 (two-three) years experience.
