Systems Engineering; Windows networking services; Windows server infrastructure



GENERAL PURPOSE:

The Systems Engineer contributes to the overall engineering, design, build, and operations support of all existing and new Windows server Infrastructure.

ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:

1. Manage all systems supported by the Infrastructure team by performing server builds, patch management, virtual machine management, virus scanning, etc.

2. Install, maintain and monitor equipment, which may include network and server infrastructure, end user computer infrastructure, application systems and database systems.

3. Recommend and implement changes to the overall system and network environments through following an institutionalized change management process.

4. Act as an escalation point on critical issues for various internal technology groups.

5. Respond to critical system issues on a 24x7x365 basis.

6. Keep management up to date about any issues and escalating in a timely manner.

7. Document processes, procedures, instructions, and training materials for users and IS colleagues.

8. Administer company security procedures and monitor compliance.

9. Implement project activities as requested. Demonstrate knowledge of IS project management standards and contribute, to varying degrees, to the creation of project plans and the analysis and monitoring of project risks.

10. Analyze and document business and IS process workflow, and make recommendations where improvements can be made.

11. Pursue expansion of knowledge of and skills in current and emerging information technology.

MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS

Education: Bachelor’s degree or equivalent combination of education and experience.

Necessary Knowledge, Skills and Abilities:

A. Required

  1. At least 3 years of experience supporting, maintaining, troubleshooting, and administering an enterprise environment.
  2. Experience with Windows Server 2003, including, but not limited to, Active Directory, Exchange 2007, IIS, SQL Server, MS Cluster and WSUS.
  3. Proficiency in Windows networking services, such as DNS, DHCP, etc.
  4. Experience with enterprise level backup/recovery tools (preferably Tivoli Storage Manager).
  5. Demonstrated understanding of TCP/IP networking.
  6. Basic knowledge of storage concepts, including SAN and NAS.
  7. Strong written and verbal communication skills.
  8. Ability to operate in a high pressure environment and successfully handle multiple priorities.

B. Preferred

  1. Demonstrated knowledge of audio equipment and tools.
  2. Experience deploying applications through Citrix Presentation Server.
  3. Demonstrated knowledge of Windows 2008 and Linux.
  4. Demonstrated knowledge of High Availability, Business Continuity, and Disaster Recovery concepts.
  5. Demonstrate knowledge of SharePoint.
  6. Familiarity with VMware ESX server.
  7. Demonstrated knowledge of Scripting.
  8. Project management skills.
  9. Industry certifications, such as MCSE, VCP, etc.
