IT BUSINESS ANALYST * (102151)


FACILITY INFORMATION

Catholic Healthcare West (CHW), headquartered in San Francisco, CA, is a system of 41 hospitals and medical centers in California, Arizona and Nevada. In the Sacramento area, it operates Mercy General Hospital, Mercy San Juan Medical Center, Mercy Hospital of Folsom and Methodist Hospital of Sacramento. Founded in 1986, CHW is one of the nation's largest not-for-profit hospital systems and the largest private health care system in California. CHW is committed to delivering compassionate, high-quality, affordable health care services with special attention to the poor and underserved. The CHW network of nearly 10,000 physicians and approximately 53,000 employees provides health care services to more than five million people annually. In 2008, CHW provided $967 million in charity care and unsponsored community benefit.

POSITION REQUIREMENTS

IT Business Analyst II

Reports To: IT Area Director


POSITION PURPOSE

The IT Business Analyst II works with key customers (both business and technology) and focuses on business analysis activities throughout the solution life cycle. Typical tasks include business requirements and process flow analysis, solution evaluation, technical specifications creation, and testing. The IT Business Analyst II has a strong understanding of technology and can apply it to a range of small to mid-size business operational solutions that enable the organization to achieve its goals.

MAJOR RESPONSIBILITIES
· Work directly with customers to understand business needs and priorities. Work primarily with business supervisors and managers.
· Elicit requirements using interviews, document analysis, requirements workshops, surveys, site visits, business process descriptions, use cases, scenarios, business analysis, task and workflow analysis. Facilitate workshops for groups of 5-10 people.
· Refine, validate, and prioritize business/functional requirements. Critically evaluate information gathered from multiple sources, reconcile conflicts, decompose high-level information into details, abstract up from low-level information to a general understanding, and distinguish user requests from the underlying true needs.
· For business needs requiring the evaluation of different buy vs build solutions, drive small to medium scale RFP efforts.
· Work with business, IT groups and vendors to estimate hardware, software and labor costs for project proposals. When necessary, create business case outlining all costs and benefits for solution.
· Drive focused discussions to ensure the appropriate level of detail business requirement questions have been asked as technical requirements are being developed. Create detailed system specifications.
· Serve as the conduit between the customer community (internal and external customers) and the software development and support teams. Analyze tradeoffs between usability and performance needs.
· Create IT system test plans to support the business need. Perform system testing and coordinate User Acceptance Testing activities, manage open issues and remediation testing, and provide progress reporting.
· Working with the Project Manager, determine scope and resolution of problems and ensure customer/partners have appropriate updates. Present options for problem resolution.
· Recognize scope limitations and raise potential scope issues to the Project Manager, while supporting the best technical solution design and approach.
· As part of a project team, work on mid to long-term, small to mid-size scale system integration, development, and/or maintenance projects.
· May be asked to perform project manager role, as well as business analyst role, on some projects.
· Follow and support the defined methodologies for Project Management.



QUALIFICATIONS

Academic:

· Bachelor's degree in Information Systems or Business Administration, or equivalent work experience in the IT industry.

Certified Business Analyst Profession (CBAPÂ©) or Project Management (PMI) certification desired.

Experience:

· 3-5 years experience in IT solution evaluation (including RFPs), project estimation, business systems design and technical focused analysis, business operations workflow design and implementation, systems testing and/or writing technical user documentation.
· 2-3 years IT experience in a healthcare/medical environment preferred.

Knowledge, Skills and Abilities Required:

· Strong analytical and product management skills required, including a thorough understanding of how to interpret customer business needs and translate them into application and operational requirements. This includes technical requirement gathering, specification development, data mapping, systems testing, and/or writing technical user documentation.
· Assist business customers in prioritizing business requests and challenges. Comfort level in developing strategies in partnership with business customers.
· Ability to understand technology projects and concepts as they relate to high-level business objectives, and ability to present those concepts to both IT and non-IT audiences in a concise and easily-understandable manner.
· Proven ability to drive conceptual and technical discussions and system requirements. This includes the ability to generate innovative ideas to resolve problems.
· Ability to perform analytical research and problem-solving for business critical issues.
· Proven abilities in problem management, process analysis and root cause analysis.
· Excellent verbal and written communication skills and the ability to interact professionally with a diverse group, executives, managers, and subject matter experts.
· Ability to work independently and in a complex, matrixed, and partially outsourced team environment.
· Ability to handle multiple projects (including a mix of small to mid-size) at any given time, set priorities, schedule and meet deadlines.
· Strong command of key desktop productivity applications; Microsoft Access, Project, Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Visio.
· Customer service focus. May serve as a liaison to key business stakeholders.
· Identification with and support of the Mission and Values of Catholic Healthcare West and its Sponsors.

KEY WORKING RELATIONSHIPS

The key working relationships of the IT Business Analyst II include:
· Key business and technology stakeholders/customers
· Business Solutions Center staff (primarily Business Analysts and Project Managers)
· Program Management Office
· Application Developers
· Application Support Analysts
· Database Administrators
· Infrastructure group
· Legal
· IT Contracting and Procurement
· CHW outsource partner

DIRECT REPORTS

· None


DUTY TO SUPPORT THE MISSION AND PHILOSOPHY OF CHW

The CHW Technology Solutions Analyst at all times upholds the best interests of CHW by adhering to the highest standards of ethical and moral conduct and by committing to the values of CHW's mission and philosophy.


COMPETENCIES ESSENTIAL TO SUCCESSFUL PERFORMANCE OF THIS POSITION:

1. Commitment to Mission and Values
Believes in Mission of CHW; demonstrates personal values consistent with the Mission and Philosophy of the organization; promotes organizational culture and development based upon CHW values and principles.

2. Gets Results
Exceeds goals; is constantly and consistently a top performer; very bottom-line oriented, steadfastly challenges self and others for results.

3. Strategic Ability
Sees ahead clearly, anticipates future consequences and trends accurately; has broad knowledge and perspective; is future-oriented; articulately paints credible pictures and visions of possibilities and alternatives; creates competitive and breakthrough strategies and plans.

4. Customer Focused
Is dedicated to meeting the expectations and requirements of internal and external customers; gets first-hand customer information and uses it for improvements in products and services; talks and acts with customers in mind; establishes and maintains effective relationships with customers and gains their trust and respect.

5. Leadership
Takes charge in crises and does whatever it takes to get the job done despite resistance; faces difficult situations with courage and tenacity; encourages direct and courageous debate with resolution and progress; is looked to for direction in a crisis.

6. Action Oriented
Enjoys working hard; is action oriented and full of energy for challenges; acts with the minimum planning necessary; seizes opportunities as they arise.

7. Deals with Ambiguity
Effectively copes with change; shifts gears comfortably; decides and acts without having the total picture; doesn't have to finish things before moving on; comfortably handles risk and uncertainty.

8. Timely Decision Maker
Makes tough decisions in a timely manner, sometimes with incomplete information and under tight deadlines and pressure; has a bias for action.

9. Manages Innovation
Uses good judgment about which creative ideas and suggestions will work; demonstrates good management of the creative process of others; facilitates effective brainstorming;

10. Learns on the Fly
Learns quickly when facing new problems; a relentless and versatile learner; open to change; analyzes both successes and failures for clues to improvement; experiments and will try anything to find solutions; enjoys the challenge of unfamiliar tasks; quickly grasps the essence and the underlying structure.

11. Listens
Practices attentive, active, and focused listening; patient and hears people out; accurately restates the opinions of others

12. Negotiator
Negotiates skillfully in tough situations with both internal and external groups; settle differences with minimum conflict; is direct, forceful and diplomatic; gains trust of others quickly; uses a good sense of timing.
