University of Texas System
Director of Information System Shared Services
The University of Texas System’s Shared Services department is seeking a qualified applicant for the role of Director of Information System Shared Services. This position reports to the Chief Information Officer in coordination with the Office of Business Affairs. The work location is flexible depending on the needs of the selected candidate and the University of Texas System. Possible employment locations include Austin, the Dallas/Ft. Worth area, and Houston.

Purpose of position
Provide project management and organizational direction for a variety of shared services projects and processes, including software application implementations, regional data center operations, and business processes consolidation.

Essential functions
Manages multiple large-scale projects simultaneously. Develops and manages a set scope of work within the projects to be delivered through effective expectations setting, communications, change management control, budget control and management, and overall business understanding of the project needs. Develops and implements a quality assurance process to ensure all objectives are met, milestones are achieved, and stakeholders are satisfied. Establishes repeatable and reusable project standards and procedures to ensure performance and quality consistent with policies and procedures. Oversees and manages all vendor interactions, communications, and relationships, and monitors and maintains project financial budget. Establishes, implements and monitors comprehensive communication plans to ensure appropriate and effective communication with all U. T. Shared Services internal and external constituent groups. Assists and advises the Executive Vice Chancellor for Business Affairs (EVCBA) and the U. T. Shared Services governing board in establishing policies and procedures to guide program operation. Assists and advises EVCBA and U. T. Shared Services governing board in creating Service Level Agreements to define specific services offered by U. T. Shared Services and conditions, parameters, and metrics used to control delivery of those services.

Marginal/Incidental functions
Other related functions as assigned.

Required qualifications
Bachelor's degree. Five years or more of complex project management experience in large-scale environments, including use of formal methodologies for scope definition, budgeting, resource scheduling, change management, issues management, risk management, and communications. Excellent written and oral communication skills. Ability to negotiate the political landscape of multi-institutional initiatives. Ability to effectively and comfortably communicate with all levels and types of management, including successfully conveying technical terms and concepts to non-technical stakeholders. Ability to work independently and assume ownership for project delivery.

Preferred qualifications
Master's degree or higher. Project Management Institute Project Management Professional certification. Ten years or more of complex project management experience in large-scale environments, including use of formal methodologies for scope definition, budgeting, resource scheduling, change management, issues management, risk management, and communications. Successful experience managing university Student Administration System, Financial Enterprise Resource Planning, and/or Human Resources/Payroll implementation and/or upgrade projects. Experience with enterprise system software vendor negotiations. Experience managing large-scale PeopleSoft Student Systems, Financials, and/or Human Resource-Payroll implementation or upgrade projects. Experience managing projects across multiple university institutions. University functional or information technology (IT) management experience at the director level or higher. Extensive experience with enterprise system software vendor and other IT software/hardware vendor negotiations.

Working conditions
May work around standard office conditions. Repetitive use of a keyboard at a workstation.
Moderate to extensive travel required.

About the University of Texas System
Established by the Texas Constitution in 1876, The University of Texas System consists of nine academic and six health institutions. The University of Texas System Administration (based in Austin, Texas and found on the web at www.utsystem.edu) is responsible for the central management and coordination of the 15 institutions.
