I.       Position Summary:  The Director of Awards and Processing will be responsible for the oversight and processing expert of all Title IV federal, state and institutional financial aid for all campuses throughout Daymar Colleges Group. Responsibilities include: Establishing effective awarding procedures and systems for campus financial services operations to ensure that federal Title IV funds,  state and institutional aid programs are processed in accordance with all Federal, State and institutional regulations,  collaborate with senior management and make recommendations for systematic improvements, identify training and procedural needs at the campus level, research and implement "best practices" for awarding, provide training on policy and procedural updates and regulatory changes associated with the financial services operation, assist with the hiring and training of new financial services personnel including making recommendations for employment and training financial services associates.  This position will fill in during the absence of the Executive Director of Financial Services and will serve as the functional expert on aid awarding and processing for the Campus Resource Center.  The position will also provide supervision of assigned CRC Financial Services Associates while leading and providing direction to the Campus Financial Services Directors by implementing policies and systems that better serve students and the organization.

MRA #1.        Administration.  Develops and implements awarding policies and systems that align with students' needs for financial services at all campus locations.

Duties:

  • Monitors, tracks and reports on active and inactive debit balances.
  • Monitors the financial aid disbursements to ensure they are received in a way that maximizes cash flow.
  • Interacts with the campuses' top-level financial services staff in a cooperative and professional manner.
  • Mentors the campuses' top-level financial services staff to become proficient in processing financial services to students.
  • Monitors daily cash balances and ensures the accuracy of general ledger balances.
  • Monitors and manages financial aid packaging and re-packaging.
  • Provides training/Backup to Campus Financial Services Staff.
  • Provide weekly accountability reports to Senior Management on award results including outstanding debit balances, 90/10 ratios and packs/repacks.
  • Coordinates, prepares and collaborates with senior financial services managers and academics on annual processing calendar.

MRA #2.      Financial Aid Systems Management.  Coordinates and monitors transactional data files and interfaces between the Campus Resource Center and all federal and state agencies. Including system requirements for Common Origination and Disbursement (COD), Student Status Confirmation Reports (SSCR), National Student Loan Clearinghouse file requirements, ISIR and ISIR Correction processing, FAA Access and transactional changes with the Central Processing  System (CPS) and all other electronic data exchange (EDE) requirements set by the U.S. Department of Education.

Duties:

  • Monitors the flow and loading of all EDE files into the financial aid management system.
  • Evaluates and recommends user security access and level for the financial aid management system, NSLDS, U.S. Department of Education and all other entities requiring system access.
  • Processes all electronic transaction files in a timely manner that meets compliance requirements.
  • Coordinates and communicates system interfaces and data requirements to the Information Technology department at the Campus Resource Center.
  • Monitors, troubleshoots and resolves financial system failures, interface errors and coordinates resolution with the Information Technology division of the Campus Resource Center.

                                                                                                   

 

MRA #3.        Mentoring and Leading.  Monitors the effectiveness of policies, programs and initiatives to continually improve the performance of the campus-level financial services department and Campus Resource Center awards division.

Duties:

  • Provides direction, guidance and leadership to the awards and processing division of the Campus Resource Center Financial Services division.
  • Provide training and procedures to assist the campus Financial Services Associates with financial aid packaging.
  • Trains Financial Services Associates on awarding practices, report monitoring and system usage for maximum effectiveness.
  • Ensures that all financial aid disbursements are scheduled timely in CampusVUE so that accurate cash flow projections can be made.
  • Monitors files sent transaction data interfaces to insure student files are cleared promptly for payment.
  • Monitors CampusVUE for the accuracy and timeliness of financial services information on students financial packaging.
  • Monitors the Colleges' cohort default rates.

 

MRA #4.       Support and Backup.  Helps facilitate employee communication, teamwork and involvement to maintain or enhance morale and improve quality of serve in the Financial Services Department.  Serve as a backup in the absence of the Executive Director of Financial Services

Duties:

  • Communicates regularly with top-level campus Financial Services Associates.
  • Collaborates with Senior management to develop comprehensive systems for awarding and processing.
  • Prepares weekly transactional and monitoring reports that demonstrates the effectiveness of award processing and compliance.

 

MRA #5.       Compliance.  Maintains a presence and active involvement in communal affairs to assure compliance with Title IV Student Financial Aid programs and all federal and state regulatory requirements.

Duties:

  • Works with third party agencies to ensure proper and accurate financial aid packages are completed, conveyed and funds from grants and loans are received on time.
  • Studies, understands, keeps up-to-date and ensures compliance with all regulatory guidelines for distributing financial aid to students.
  • Collaborates with top-level campus Financial Services Associates to ensure compliance with regulatory guidelines.

MRA #6.      State Aid Monitoring. Coordinate, monitor and train Financial Services Officers on state aid processing.  Analyze to ensure that all state aid programs processed timely and accurately for the disbursement of funds.

Duties:

  • Monitors state aid report in CampusVue that lists all students who are enrolled in an associate degree program and reports accordingly to the state aid agency.
  • Reconciles state awards with the appropriate state aid agency.
  • Reviews student eligibility in CampusVue to determine in house credit hours the student is taking (online classes do not count), for all eligible students
  • Post student aid eligibility with the appropriate state agency.
  • Coordinates state aid activity with the Accounts Receivable and Funds Management Division.

 

IV.      Job Requirements:  The following job requirements represent minimum levels of education, experience and competencies/abilities needed to perform this job successfully:

            1.         Education:  Bachelor's Degree.  Year to year supervisory financial aid experience may be substituted for degree.

            2.         Experience:  A minimum of five years managerial experience in a financial services department of an educational institution.  Extensive knowledge of Title IV financial aid awarding, policies and procedures.

            3.         Competencies:  Incumbent must (be) able to:

  • Must be a functional expert on financial aid management systems, awarding requirements and federal Title IV aid compliance.
  • Project a professional image and provide outstanding customer service
  • Demonstrate strong analytical and cognitive thinking skills
  • Keep commitments, meet deadlines and achieve demanding results
  • Organize and execute around multiple priorities
  • Must be able to communicate effectively, both orally and in writing
  • Must be able to lead, direct and guide a team of associates to positive results
  • Cooperate and collaborate as a member of a team
  • Demonstrate excellent computer/software skills
  • Use Microsoft Word, Excel and PowerPoint to prepare and maintain records, correspondence, reports and other data
  • Work days and some evenings and Saturdays if/when required
  • Embrace and demonstrate on an ongoing basis the Company's core values of Continuous Improvement, Excellence, Innovation, Integrity, Respect, Service, Teamwork and Having Fun.

            4.         Physical, Mental and Travel Requirements:

                        Physical Requirements:  Sitting, standing, bending, reaching, stooping, walking and moving intermittently during work hours.  Ability to maintain verbal and written communications with co-workers and outside business associates.  Ability to travel as required.

                        Mental Requirements:  Alert and attentive; ability to remember locations, names, people.  Analytically competent.  Works well under pressure and manage multiple tasks. 

                        Environment:  This job is normally performed in a clean, well-lighted, heated and cooled office area/classroom.

                        Travel Requirements:  Must be able to travel and work remotely up to 75% of the time and manage expenses accordingly.
