Responsibilities
Senior Associate Director 

Data Analysis:  The AD plays a critical role in the analysis of data used by senior management at the University.  He/she is responsible for complex and sensitive analyses and reporting such as tenure status analyses, faculty salaries, faculty workload, and surveys related to the interests of faculty and academic leaders.  The AD will update and maintain routine analyses that support academic planning and will develop additional complex analyses as needed.  He/she may conduct data analyses using extant institutional data and may also prepare analyses using external data sources, utilizing extensive data files on various computer platforms and software to produce the data needed for analyses and reporting.  These analyses will support and contribute to planning and management activities at the University. 

Data Reporting: The AD will prepare routine and ad hoc reports to fulfill the institution's reporting and compliance demands. The AD will work with various campus units to ensure data needed for reporting is available, accurate and is of high quality. Further, the AD will develop and maintain currency in areas related to data standards, industry reporting, government compliance requirements, and proper uses of data. 

Survey Research Projects:  The AD will be an active contributor to the institution's survey efforts; surveying efforts may cross many departments and functions within the University.  Work in this area may include some or all of these functions depending on the project: designing and implementing surveys; providing guidance for survey research; initiating, conducting and reporting on various survey projects; administrating surveys; meeting with clients to outline parameters of research; preparing reports and communicating results to constituencies.  

The AD will serve as the second most senior person in the Office of Institutional Research.  As such, this person will act on the director's behalf when needed.  The AD will participate in professional development activities in order to maintain currency in industry practices and trends, and institutional practices that affect data reporting and analysis. The AD will take advantage of training to maintain skills in areas that affect his/her job.  The AD must work effectively and productively as part of the Institutional Research team.  Superior analytical skills are essential as is the ability and willingness to constantly reevaluate and improve analytical, reporting, and survey activities.  The AD will perform other duties as assigned.
Qualifications
A Master's degree in a related field is required, a Doctorate is preferred. The position requires 5-8 years of experience with quantitative research projects, including prior understanding of inferential and descriptive statistics, data analysis principles, practices, and techniques, and regression analysis techniques. A demonstrated ability to conceptualize and carry out complex research and analytical projects, expertise with using technology to assist in the collection and analysis of data, and excellent communication skills are required. Proficiency with various software packages including MS Office applications, database tools, SPSS, and data query tools is required. 

The ideal candidate will have excellent analytical, quantitative and writing skills, including the ability to communicate technical information in oral, written and graphic form to individuals with varying backgrounds.  Familiarity with concepts, trends and issues in higher education is strongly preferred. Related experience in institutional research, higher education research, or a closely-related field is strongly preferred. The Associate Director will be able to prioritize and organize projects and will be able to manage multiple and changing priorities and meet deadlines.
