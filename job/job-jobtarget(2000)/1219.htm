Banquet Chef

The University of Iowa

Iowa City, Iowa, United States
Open Job Area Information Page Show Map of Job Area Print View Print View Save Job E-mail Job
Bigger map Smaller map Zoom to Street

This is not an entry-level position.

Job ID: 7388338
Job Duration: Indefinite
Position Title: Banquet Chef
Required Travel: 0-10%
Min Education: BA/BS/Undergraduate
Posted: November 18, 2010
Job Function: Executive Chef
Min Experience: 3-5 Years
Job Type: Full-Time
Salary: $40,708.00 (Yearly Salary)

University Housing & Dining is seeking a creative, innovative, and self-motivated individual to manage all aspects of food preparation, presentation and staffing for banquet and catering events held within the Iowa Memorial Union, Kinnick Stadium Brechler Press Box and Suites, and many other University facilities, and off campus venues.  Total revenues in excess of $2.5M.    The ideal candidate must have a strong commitment to team management, ability to work collaboratively to insure outstanding customer service and to maintain efficiencies in operations.  The candidate must also be customer service oriented with excellent leadership, organization and communication skills.

Salary starts at $40,708 and commensurate with experience, plus excellent University benefits.  To learn more about University Housing & Dining and a complete job description, including desirable qualifications, visit our web site at http://housing.uiowa.edu/.  More information about The University of Iowa and how to apply may be found at http://jobs.uiowa.edu/.  Refer to requisition #58669.

 

The University is an Equal Employment Opportunity and Affirmative Action Employer.  Women and Minorities are encouraged to apply.

 


Job Requirements

Requirements include a Bachelor’s degree in Hotel & Restaurant Administration, Dietetics, Foods and Nutrition, Institutional Management, or related field; or an equivalent combination of education and experience; considerable (3-5 years) experience in banquet planning, food preparation and presentation in a high volume culinary operation that includes inventory control, food service management, ordering, production, and supervisory experience; excellent written and verbal communications skills; must be proficient in computer software applications (i.e., Word, Excel, and Email); ability to obtain and maintain ServSafe certification; ability to plan, organize and work in a fast-paced environment and have excellent decision making and problem solving skills; and a valid driver’s license and meet and maintain the requirements of the University’s Fleet Safety Program.
