Newbury is an undergraduate college that integrates career education with the study of liberal arts.  We endeavor to graduate students who are professionally competent, ethically aware, socially responsible, and prepared for lifelong learning.

Newbury's campus is nestled in a desirable city neighborhood just minutes awayfrom downtown Boston's educational, cultural and social attractions. Newbury College strives to create a welcoming and supportive environment for our community. We are committed to the development of our faculty and staff and acknowledge that you are critical to our students' success and the delivery of our mission.

 

Position:  Executive Director of Student Financial Assistance

Reports to:  Vice President for Enrollment Management & Dean of Admissions

Educational Requirements:  Bachelor's Degree required, Master's preferred

Requisite Skills:

  • At least five to seven years of progressive experience in a college financial aid position.
  • An understanding and appreciation of small, tuition-driven institutions; knowledgeable of financial aid leveraging and econometrics.
  • Possess strong analytical skills and computer expertise -including PowerFaids.
  • Excellent oral and written communication skills, strong interpersonal skills, the ability to effectively interact with people of all backgrounds, and who demonstrates creativity, vision and sound judgment.

General Responsibilities:

  • Collaborate with the President and Vice President for Enrollment Management in the strategic planning for the allocation of financial aid resources and assistance in the formulation, implementation, and assessment of financial aid and enrollment strategies for traditional and continuing education students.
  • Collaborate with the President and Vice President for Enrollment Management in developing and monitoring the financial aid leveraging model to ensure student recruitment and retention goals are met annually.
  • Oversee the daily operations of a student service-oriented department that includes a high interaction with Student Accounts. Collaborate with the office to ensure a seamless student transition between financial aid and student account functions.
  • Assist in the preparation of the financial aid budget in collaboration with the President and the Cabinet.
  • Advise the President, the Cabinet and other administrators concerning financial aid policies and procedures; and provide regular statistical updates.
  • Manage the administration of all federal, state and institutional financial aid programs.
  • Interpret federal, state, NCAA and institutional regulations pertaining to financial aid and monitor compliance; analyze students' need for aid; consult with staff to resolve family financial aid problems; and counsel students and parents regarding financial aid issues.
  • Comply with all federal, state, NCAA and institutional reporting procedures.
  • Prepare reports involving analysis and interpretation for the President, Vice Presidents, and other offices on campus.
  • Communicate in a highly personalized manner with key constituencies including students, parents, government agencies, and other campus administrators.
  • Collaborate with the Chief Information Officer to develop and coordinate main frame data base modifications - including software soft-ware installations and upgrades.
  • Manage, evaluate and train the staff and oversee the office budget.
  • Manage Newbury's college work study funds and, in conjunction with the Placement Office, assure that those funds are expended.
  • To assist in providing a dynamic and caring educational environment.

Specific Duties:

  • Responsible for all the detail work required in determining a student's eligibility for federal, state, and institutional funds.
  • Comply with the detail regulations that govern eligibility and disbursement of state and federal aid programs.
  • Develop programs that help educate current and prospective students and their families about what is available, how to apply, and who might be eligible.
  • File applications, reports, and expenditures with the proper agencies at the designated times so as to comply with all state and federal requirements, such as FISAP, bi-monthly Pell Grant reports, etc.
  • Establish internal mechanisms that provide both the Admissions and Continuing Education staff with enough training so that they can accurately and effectively convey the possible financial aid available to a prospective students.
  • Assure that financial aid complements and assists the recruitment and retention within the disbursement guidelines.
  • Award, administer time sheet, and monitor expenses in the College work-study program. Work closely with the Placement Office to ensure that funds are properly expended.
  • Manage electronic Pell Grant program.
  • Develop and manage the College's student loan program.
  • Develop procedures, policies, and approaches that acknowledge the office as integral part of student recruitment and retention.
  • Other duties as assigned.
