The At Daymar College, we change lives one person at a time.  Daymar Colleges Group has a new opportunity at its headquarters in Owensboro, KY for a Director of Title IV Compliance and Training.  This senior level position will serve as the functional expert on Title IV compliance and default prevention for enterprise.  The job description is as follows:

 

Director of Title IV Compliance and Training will be responsible for Title IV compliance and training of all Title IV federal financial aid throughout Daymar Colleges Group. Responsibilities include: Collaborating with the Director of Awards and Processing and Director of Cash Management to establish Title IV compliance procedures and systems for campus financial services operations. Staying abreast of current and proposed federal student aid legislation and documenting internal procedures for training and guidance to all campus associates.  Coordinating with the Internal Audit department campus compliance reviews and working closely with senior management to make recommendations for systematic improvements. Managing default prevention and financial literacy initiatives for the enterprise. Identify training and procedural needs at the campus level, research and implement “best practices” for awarding, provide training on policy and procedural updates and regulatory changes associated with the financial services operation, assist with the hiring and training of new financial services personnel including making recommendations for employment and training financial services associates.  This position will fill in during the absence of the Executive Director of Financial Services and will serve as the Title IV federal financial aid expert for the Campus Resource Center.  The position will also provide supervision of assigned CRC Financial Services Associates while leading, training and providing direction to the Campus Financial Services Directors by implementing policies and systems that better serve students and the organization.


Job Requirements

Job Requirements:  The following job requirements represent minimum levels of education, experience and competencies/abilities needed to perform this job successfully:

 

            1.         Education:  Bachelor’s Degree.  Year to year supervisory financial aid experience may be substituted for degree.

 

            2.         Experience:  A minimum of five years managerial or Title IV compliance experience in a financial services department of an educational institution.  Extensive knowledge of Title IV financial aid awarding, policies and procedures.

 

            3.         Competencies:  Incumbent must (be) able to:

 

·               Must be a functional expert on financial aid management systems, awarding requirements and federal Title IV aid compliance.

·               Project a professional image and provide outstanding customer service

·               Demonstrate strong analytical and cognitive thinking skills

·               Keep commitments, meet deadlines and achieve demanding results

·               Organize and execute around multiple priorities

·               Must be able to communicate effectively, both orally and in writing

·               Must be able to lead, direct and guide a team of associates to positive results

·               Cooperate and collaborate as a member of a team

·               Demonstrate excellent computer/software skills

·               Use Microsoft Word, Excel and PowerPoint to prepare and maintain records, correspondence, reports and other data

·               Work days and some evenings and Saturdays if/when required

·               Embrace and demonstrate on an ongoing basis the Company’s core values of Continuous Improvement, Excellence, Innovation, Integrity, Respect, Service, Teamwork and Having Fun.
