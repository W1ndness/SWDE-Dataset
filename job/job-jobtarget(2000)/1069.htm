BASIC FUNCTIONS AND RESPONSIBILITIES:
The Director of Leadership Giving is responsible for the success of Shorecrest's Leadership Giving program. The Director sets strategic direction for Shorecrest's Major Giving, Planned Giving, Endowment, and Foundation Support plans, and leads Shorecrest's efforts to inspire transformational gifts and grants. The Director of Leadership Giving reports to the Director of Advancement.

CHARACTERISTIC DUTIES:

FUNDRAISING LEADERSHIP
* Leads the creation and execution of short-term and long-range major giving and grants plans that include overall strategy, proposed activities, donor service expectations, required resources, and revenue goals. Regularly assesses progress toward goals and reports to Director of Advancement.
* Provides strategic leadership to Shorecrest's staff and volunteers in designing and implementing development programs needed to sustain major gift and grant support. In coordination with the Advancement team, coordinates the participation of staff and volunteers in the school's fundraising activities.

*Provides leadership and support to the Major, Planned Gifts and Endowment sub-committee of the Advancement Committee.
* Helps to identify sources, build relationships with and prepare proposals to corporations, foundations and private granting agencies.
* Conducts prospect research and peer review screenings to identify potential leadership donors.
* Executes major and planned giving efforts that identify, assess, cultivate, solicit, and steward major donors for outright gifts, pledges and planned gifts.
* Develops a portfolio of approximately 200 donors and uses moves management principles to build donor relationships that result in gifts of $5,000 and more.
* Works with Director of Advancement, the Director of the Annual Fund and Alumni Programs, and Advancement staff and volunteers to identify major giving prospects from pool of annual donors.
* Prepares written proposals, information materials, endowment agreements, gift illustrations and other materials needed to secure major and planned gifts.
* In consultation with the Director of Advancement, establishes personal goals, objectives, and strategies.
* Oversees major donor and capital campaign cultivation and stewardship events.
* Works with Advancement Coordinator to identify and pursue Business Partner leadership funding opportunities.

CAPITAL CAMPAIGN
* During capital campaign, provide overall administrative support for the campaign, including prospect management, volunteer support, materials development, and volunteer training.

*Lead Shorecrest's efforts to prepare and execute its major giving and grant programs during capital campaigns.
* Participates in the development and management of the capital campaign.

CONSTITUENCY RELATIONS
* Works closely with Director of Advancement to create and support a vibrant Advancement Committee and related volunteer groups.
* Works with volunteers and others to identify, cultivate, solicit and steward prospective major donors and grantors.


Oversees and carries out other duties as assigned.

SUPERVISION RECEIVED:
Reports to the Director of Advancement.


Job Requirements

Skills

QUALIFICATIONS:
*Bachelor's Degree or equivalent; and five years of progressively responsible experience in institutional development, with proven ability in cultivating, soliciting and closing major gifts and grants of $25K and more.
*Ability to articulate fundraising vision and engender internal and external support.
*Strong interpersonal skills and an ability to communicate effectively in writing, by phone, or in person. Poised, professional manner with the ability to work well with diverse communities, individuals, and personalities.
*Ability to work independently, problem solve, take initiative, set priorities, handle multiple projects, and exercise good judgment in a fast paced, dynamic, deadline driven environment in an organized and professional manner.
*Exceptional ability to organize oneself and others as well as motivate external advocates and volunteers. High energy level, enthusiasm, initiative, and flexibility required.
*Ability to handle and protect sensitive, confidential material.
* Staff management experience including expert leadership and coaching skills.
* Proven ability to work collegially and effectively with development colleagues, administrators, creative staff and external constituencies.
* Commitment to maintaining confidentiality and a high degree of accuracy in donor records.
* Ability to work in a fast-paced environment and maintain a professional demeanor.
* Must be able to work an irregular schedule, evenings or Saturdays as needed, additional hours during peak times or as required.

*Ability to utilize a variety of technology and software to support the endeavors of the Advancement Office.
* Knowledge of Raiser's Edge helpful but not required.
