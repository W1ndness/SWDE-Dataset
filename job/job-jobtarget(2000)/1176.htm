  •  Job Summary: *This position is located in Murrieta, CA* The Clinical Nurse B, Emergency Services, is a professional care giver who assumes responsibility and accountability for a group of patients for a designated time frame and provides care to these patients via therapeutic use of self, the nursing process, the therapeutic environment, treatment, and modalities and interdisciplinary collaboration within the scope of practice set forth by the Nursing Practice Act. Performs other duties as needed. Job Specifications: Associate's Degree from an accredited school of nursing required. Minimum of one year of recent appropriate nursing practice (within past 4 years) or completion of emergency or critical care course. Minimum of one year of Emergency and/or Critical Care Nursing experience preferred. Active California RN license required. Current Healthcare Provider Certificate required. ACLS, PALS, and/or Pediatric Competency Program Certificate, NRP required. TNCC and ENPC preferred. Good communication and writing skills. Physical and Emotional stamina. Able to use a computer and/or learn hospital system. Ability to prioritize multiple tasks and demands. Demonstrate an understanding of normal growth and development of the infant, pediatric, adolescent and adult population and care of those patients as appropriate to the Emergency Department. Demonstrates an understanding of the normal aging process in the elderly/geriatric patient population and care of these patients as appropriate to the Emergency Department. Able to: read; write legibly; speak in English with professional quality; use computer, printer, and software programs necessary to the position; troubleshoot and calibrate patient care equipment; perform technical patient care activities. Able to: relate and communicate positively, effectively, and professionally with others; be assertive and consistent in following and/or enforcing policies; work calmly and respond courteously when under pressure; lead, supervise, teach, and collaborate; accept direction. Able to: communicate effectively in English in person, in writing, and on the telephone; think critically; perform basic math functions; manage multiple assignments effectively; organize and prioritize workload; work well under pressure; problem solve; recall information with accuracy; pay close attention to detail; work independently with minimal supervision. Able to: distinguish colors and smells as necessary for patient care; hear sufficiently for general conversation in person and on the telephone; identify and distinguish various sounds associated with the work place/patient care; see adequately to read computer screens, medical records, and written documents necessary to position; discern temperature variances through touch.
