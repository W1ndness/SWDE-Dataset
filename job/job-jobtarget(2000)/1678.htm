Traction Motor System Architect

 Primary Job Responsibilities
        As a member of the Powertrain architecture and controls team, you will model, design, benchmark, and lead the architecture and controls implementation of future Tesla traction motor drives.

Description

  • Lead definition of all aspects of motor and motor control requirements
  • Understand and drive design of motor electrical, mechanical, thermal, and motor control software architecture
  • Define implementation of motor controller functionality, behaviors, and algorithms
  • Maintain awareness of and influence research and development in key component technologies that influence future motor architecture and motor control algorithms
  • Research, model, specify, design, and help implement, debug, and test motor control algorithms
  • Advance Tesla IP in electric motor design and motor controls


Job Requirements

  • Prior experience optimizing induction motor electro-magnetic design for efficiency, torque constant, or high speed power, by changing winding patterns, slot and bar geometry, stack length and diameter, etc
  • In-depth understanding of torque control techniques such as slip-based control, field oriented control, direct torque control
  • Experience with various PWM schemes such as space vector, discontinuous, and sinusoidal modulations
  • System-oriented understanding of thermal systems, commanded torque, efficiency optimization, and firmware integration for electric drives
  • Model development and data analysis in Matlab/Simulink
  • Strong communication and preference for working in teams

 

Desired Skills:

  • Understanding of induction motor construction, including magnetic, conductor, electrically insulating, and thermally conductive materials
  • Experience with liquid-cooled traction motors
  • Capability of delivering high-quality code in an embedded environment

 

Qualifications

  • Greater than 5 years experience leading the engineering and/or controls system development for traction motor drives
  • BS/MS/PhD in EE/ME or equivalent

Powertrain Performance Modeling Engineer

Primary Job Responsibilities

As a member of the powertrain architecture, controls, and modeling team, you will be responsible for the development, operation, and validation of all component and system models. You will use the model(s) to predict component-, powertrain-, and vehicle performance to inform component specification and control algorithm development.

Description

  • Advance the capabilities of and intellectual property of Tesla’s in-house powertrain performance model
  • Work with the model to produce repeatable and dependable results for various stakeholders within the organization
  • Produce presentations of said results to inform tradeoff studies, specifications, and control algorithm design for thermal, motor, battery pack, power electronics, and vehicle systems
  • Develop a consistent framework for reporting progress of model and component development as components go from conception to prototype  to fully-production status

Job Requirements

Essential Skills:

  • Excellent first-principle physics understanding of electrical and mechanical systems, power delivery, energy storage and transformation, and basic thermo- and aero-dynamics
  • Hybrid or electric powertrain component model development and data analysis in Matlab/Simulink
  • Advanced Matlab script development and experience with large datasets and complicated models

Desired Skills:

  • Advanced Excel and Powerpoint skills
  • Strong communication skills and preference for working in teams

Qualifications:

  • Greater than 3 years experience utilizing the essential skills above
  • BS/MS/PhD degree in Physics/ME or equivalent

Systems Engineer – Vehicle Communication Networks (CAN)

Primary Job Responsibilities

As a member of the vehicle firmware development team at Tesla, you will translate your experience with automotive software, communication and diagnostic protocols, and CAN tools into requirements definition for Tesla’s young and dynamic firmware team and the suppliers we engage with.

Description

·        Responsible for ensuring integration of multiple electronics modules on a vehicle network.
Specify requirements for ECUs and select components and suppliers to produce a robust, comprehensive system

·        Work closely with suppliers and internal engineers to identify issues and resolve integration issues.

·        Carry responsibility for the release of the vehicle network architecture including CAN, LIN, and other data busses.

·        Main supplier interface for all network and interface related activities.

 

Job Requirements
Essential Skills:

·        Specification of CAN architectures and networking systems for automotive products.

·        Hands-on technical experience from design through production.

·        Expert in using CAN based tools such as CANdb++, CANalyzer, CANoe, CANape and VehicleSpy or similar.

·        Responsibility for in-house and vendor system design activities relating to specification, software/ hardware development and validation.

 

Desired Skills:

·        Understanding of automotive electrical design and embedded software systems.

·        Design experience with vehicle networks utilizing industry standard CAN protocols e.g. CCP/XCP, UDS and others.

·        Ability to work in a startup environment. Ability to work with a variety of tools including home grown. Flexibility to deal with and merge multiple network models rather than just require all modules to use one.

·        Familiarity with the specification and administration of CAN database files in Vector .dbc format.

·        Capable of producing project-related documentation, including functional and interface specifications, electrical and software design specifications and schedules.

·        Ability to resolve ambiguity and drive the specification of sub-systems to resolve interface issues.

·        Ability to participate in large engineering projects by taking responsibility for multiple sub-systems and efficiently completing many tasks in parallel.

·        Basic management skills, including planning and organization, decision making, problem solving, and project management.

Qualifications

  • Greater than 5 years experience with Automotive communication networks
  • BS or MS degree in CS/EE/ME or equivalent
