Web Producer - Public Insight Network

St. Paul, MN

Minnesota Public Radio® is one of the nation's premier public radio organizations. With state-of-the-art headquarters in the heart of downtown St. Paul, Minnesota Public Radio operates a regional network of 38 stations, covering Minnesota and parts of Wisconsin, the Dakotas, Michigan, Iowa and Idaho.  We are the largest owner and operator of public radio stations nationwide.


Our workplace is as dynamic as our programming!  Diverse, creative and compelling doesn't just describe what we do, but also who we are.  We tap into the life experiences of our employees for perspective and balance.  In addition to pursuing your talents in a creative, energetic workplace, you'll discover competitive pay and benefits, a culture that encourages learning and development, and opportunities for advancement.  A career at Minnesota Public Radio is your opportunity to inspire - and to be inspired!

Position Summary:

The Web Producer joins a multi-faceted team whose mission is to create a more relevant journalism of collaboration and community engagement, called Public Insight Journalism.TM This effort relies on a network of people - the Public Insight Network - who have agreed to provide their insights, first-hand knowledge, experiences and expertise to news organizations around the country, including regional public radio and television stations, online and print media, and national radio shows like Marketplace and Speaking of Faith. (See http://americanpublicmedia.publicradio.org/publicinsightjournalism/ for more information.)

The Network Journalism and Innovation team at American Public Media (APM) envisions, invents, operates and spreads tools that allow news organizations around the country to creatively engage the Network and to collaborate on stories, projects and other ventures.  Team members use knowledge management, database and Web 2.0 software (including online games and collaborative tools) along with e-mail, phone, public meetings and traditional reporting methods to manage and mine insight from the PIN, and to create and leverage collaborative relationships that lead to high impact reporting and fulfill the information needs of diverse communities.

The Web Producer will be an integral part of the Network Journalism and Innovation team. Addition of this role to the team will make it possible to ramp up the number of partners we add, and the quality with which we serve them.   The Web Producer will support the growth of the PIN and PIN partner newsrooms by designing new templates and web-based interfaces for partner customization of PIN pages and applications, and helping each new partner prepare their online materials when they launch PIN on their website.  In 2010 PIN software was implemented in Spanish for the first time, and we expect this support to grow.  The Web Producer will take a leading role in expanding multilingual support of PIN software and online engagement tools.  In 2010 a new source-access website for PIN sources will launch, and this person will contribute to the team continuing to develop and improve this service.

Position Responsibility:

  • Work with Partner News Organizations
  • Take a lead role in execution of the web deployment process for each new partner.
  • Provide support to personnel at partner organizations for the bulk email service, PIN questionnaire applications, the source access website, and the integration of PIN materials into their websites.
  • Provide support and guidance in the development of web-based techniques for gathering, sharing and publishing insight across newsrooms, particularly Local Journalism Centers.
  • Proactively facilitate the sharing of new tools and techniques for gathering insights and publishing content online among the partners.
  • Support the Public Insight Partners Manager in creating and curating an online depot of training materials for PIN partners.
  • Participate in training of new partners and staff as assigned.
  • Multilingual Support
  • Coordinate translation of PIN materials into supported languages, ensuring best practices
  • Lead the effort to ensure that non-English PIN materials meet or exceed the level of support for English PIN materials
  • Digital Special Projects
  • Generate and develop ideas - independently and in collaboration with other team members - for spotlighting insight-driven storytelling and growing the Public Insight Network with experiences that "hum." 
  • Manage projects where requested.
  • PIN Web Presence
  • Support the maintenance and content development of the PIN source access website.
  • Support the technology team in building the interface for database-driven web applications that utilize data from APIs, user submissions, and external data sources.
  • Support the technology team in designing and maintaining web-accessible customizable templates for PIN applications.
  • Identify and report production problems and assist with solutions.
  • Conduct research and collect multimedia assets from various sources for online productions.
  • Create and edit web graphics, shoot photos and video, and prepare them for web use.
  • Participate in marketing plans related to PIN's digital efforts.
  • Assure that work complies with MPR|APM online platform standards and best practices.
  • Other duties as assigned.

Job Requirements

 Required Education and Experience:

  • Bachelor's degree or equivalent experience.
  • Three years experience of building websites, Flash packages, and mashups, preferably for a news organization
  • Experience with Teamsite and other content management systems.
  • Experience with a commercial bulk email system.
  • Experience with HTML, CSS, Flash, and related mark-up and scripting tools, as well as Photoshop and other imaging tools.
  • Experience supporting others in their use of systems and tools, especially remotely.

Required Skills, Knowledge and Abilities:

  • Ability to meet high standards of accuracy, production quality and timeliness.
  • Passion for social media, content aggregation, and "what's-new" in online media.
  • Passion for news and knowledge of current events from following the news daily.
  • Experience in maintaining high journalistic standards and a proven editorial judgment with a commitment to standards of objectivity, balance and fairness. 
  • Strong writing, re-writing and editing skills.
  • Able to work well and productively in an environment where news is constantly changing and being updated and have the temperament to enjoy the process. 
  • Ability to effectively execute multiple assignments under deadline with minimal guidance
  • Excel in a fast-paced, deadline-oriented environment, utilizing rapid prototyping and other design techniques to quickly iterate and demonstrate concepts for/with other members of the team.
  • Fluent in writing and editing for the web.  Basic copy editing skills and knowledge of AP style.
  • Strong attention to detail in professional settings.
  • Flair and imagination to craft original multimedia material.
  • Ability to work collaboratively and effectively with colleagues in multiple departments/divisions.
  • Excellent communication, interpersonal, planning, and organizational skills.
  • Knowledge of available digital storytelling technology, products, and industry trends.
  • Knowledge of RSS feeds, XML, and other information and data dissemination methods.
  • Solid information architecture and GUI sensibility.
  • Understanding of semantic markup and accessibility standards

Preferred Skills and Experience:

  • Understanding of Javascript, PHP, and various AJAX technologies.
  • Prior experience in the field of journalism
  • Fluency in Spanish.
