Job Title:

Strategic Partner Compliance Manager ("SPCM")

Strategic Partner Business:

WebBank is a leading provider of national origination platforms for consumer and commercial private-label and bank card programs.  The Strategic Partnership Group works closely with partners to design, develop, implement, administer and fund customized consumer and/or commercial closed network financing program(s) through WebBank.  Private-label products supported by WebBank include:

  • Closed-end Installment Loans (Consumer & Commercial)
  • Revolving charge (Consumer & Commercial)

Similar to private-label, the Group works closely with partners to structure and issue bank card branded products under WebBank's charter and principal bank card association membership. Products supported:

  • Private-Label / Co-Brand / Dual-Line Cards (Consumer & Commercial)
  • General Purpose Credit (Consumer & Commercial)
  • Small Business
  • Fleet Cards

Job Description Summary

The SPCM will report to the WebBank VP/Compliance Manager.  Primary responsibility is to ensure that WebBank Strategic Partners maintain compliance with all federal and state lending regulations as well as all WebBank requirements.  The SPCM will have primary oversight for the compliance aspects of WebBanks Strategic Partner Business but will engage in various other compliance activities as directed by the VP/Compliance Manager or Chief Compliance Officer. This position involves planning, organization and control of the Bank's day-to-day administrative, lending and operational compliance activities with respect to Strategic Partners.  The SPCM will collaborate with the Compliance Team as well as the Strategic Partner Management Team in the overall administration of these directives for the Bank.  In addition the SPCM will participate in various committees, audits and examinations.  The SPCM may perform duties as the back-up Bank Secrecy Act/OFAC Officer, and back-up Community Reinvestment Act Officer.  The SPCM will assist with the development and continued enhancement of internal compliance related policies and procedures and will review policies and procedures drafted by Strategic Partners to determine acceptability.  SPCM will conduct compliance training for new hires and Strategic Partners including AML/BSA, OFAC, Privacy, and other training as designated.  SPCM will assist the Chief Compliance Officer or VP/Compliance Manager in responsibilities of notifying the Board of Directors, Senior Management and Bank personnel of emerging compliance issues and consults and guides the Bank in the establishment of controls to mitigate risks.

Responsibilities:

  • Act as the primary company contact for all Strategic Partner compliance-related information and activities.
  • Inform Senior Management and support teams of all regulatory and business developments related to regulatory compliance and determine the impact of those developments to the Bank.
  • Ensure that existing and proposed Bank and Strategic Partner business practices, policies, procedures, loan pricing, origination software systems, documents and disclosures comply with federal and state regulations.
  • Stay abreast of all federal and state regulatory changes related to consumer lending and privacy and ensure those changes are communicated to and addressed by company management
  • Assist Chief Compliance Officer, VP Compliance Manager and CFO in addressing internal and external audit findings and help prepare responses to state and investor auditors.
  • Assist in the refinement and execution of compliance monitoring programs to test the compliance controls and procedures.
  • Develop and execute mandatory compliance training modules
  • Ensure that new product development, system initiatives and other projects adequately address compliance related issues
  • Work closely with retained legal counsel and other compliance related resources to interpret new and existing laws and regulations and communicate those interpretations to the company.
  • On an as-needed basis, perform research on regulatory issues that is thorough, accurate and timely. Communicate the results of that research to management for action plans to be developed if applicable.
  • Attend compliance related industry conferences and meetings as directed.
  • Perform site visit reviews of Strategic Partner relationships.  Must be willing to travel at least one week per month

Job Requirements

Requirements:

  • Bachelor's degree and minimum of 5 years of regulatory compliance experience
  • Must have prior bank experience.  Industrial Bank experience preferred
  • Direct experience dealing with regulators (FDIC, Utah Department of Financial Institutions)is highly desired  
  • Strong background in federal and state lending laws
  • Strong knowledge of AML/BSA guidelines and requirements
  • Excellent ability to analyze and interpret legal and statutory materials as well as state and federal regulations
  • Strong self-confidence, good judgment and the ability to make sound decisions
  • Must interface effectively with other departments
  • Ability to handle a fast paced environment with minimum supervision and successfully meet established project deadline requirements.
  • Strong problem solver
  • Outstanding initiative, communication skills (verbal and written), attention to detail and organizational skills required
