Love your work. Live your life. Make a difference.



Northwestern Memorial Hospital, we've built a dynamic reputation for providing superior health care by first fostering an environment where the finest minds in the industry choose to work. Our professionals are afforded the challenges, resources and opportunities that not only enhance their skills, but allow them to pursue their own ambitions with success. We currently seek a Coding Compliance Analyst to join us at our Chicago, IL location.

Responsibilities:

  • Support the organization's Compliance Program by identifying and assessing coding compliance risks at the direction of the Coding and Billing Compliance Manager.
  • Maintain a current understanding of regulatory trends and changes in coding guidelines and reimbursement policies that affect NMHC and its subsidiaries by monitoring various resources to assess regulatory changes and determine organizational impact.
  • Apply knowledge of medical terminology, abbreviations, anatomy and physiology, major disease processes and/or pharmacology to investigate and respond to documentation and coding issues.
  • Apply knowledge of classification systems, ICD-9-CM nomenclature, CPT-4 and HCPCS nomenclature, coding rules, guidelines and proper sequencing to analyze medical record documentation.
  • Understand and monitor changes, to coding conventions and rules established by the American Medical Association (AMA), the American Hospital Association (AHA) and the Center for Medicare and Medicaid (CMS), for assignment of diagnostic and surgical procedural codes.
  • Apply knowledge of ethical coding principles and revenue cycle activities to evaluate coding compliance through coding audits.
  • Work with departments and/or physicians to monitor their compliance with coding standards and provide guidance to help meet requirements.
  • Assist departments in the development of corrective action plans involving coding issues when areas of opportunities are identified.
  • Receive, develop, coordinate, and/or oversee internal and external coding audits for the purpose of monitoring and detecting any misconduct or non-compliance to final resolution.
  • Assist with the development of coding educational plans, materials, and resources to educate employees with respect to the overall objectives of the corporate compliance program and specific substantive areas of compliance.
  • Communicate with the Coding and Billing Compliance Manager on a regular basis as to any identified coding issues and/or systemic issues.

Job Requirements

REQUIREMENTS:

  • Bachelor's degree and 2 - 3 years of coding experience in inpatient/outpatient coding.
  • Certification as a Certified Coding Specialist (CCS) required.
  • Proficient in reading and interpreting documentation and data in medical records, and finding and resolving documentation discrepancies.
  • Ability to operate various computerized medical coding and information processing systems.
  • Ability to operate a computer and utilize a variety of software applications, including coding software.
  • Proficient in understanding and applying medical/surgical coding procedures and protocols.
  • Proficient in linking diagnosis to services and applying appropriate codes to diagnosis, procedures and supplies.
  • Ability to analyze medical records and create audit reports that assist physicians, management, and hospital staff in understanding coding compliance issues.
  • Ability to interpret and apply ethical coding standards, federal and state laws and regulations, rules, policies and procedures, and professional practice standards for health care organization coding compliance program activities.
  • Effective verbal and written communication skills.
  • Skill in presenting information, writing reports, assisting with educational sessions, and other compliance materials.



Desired:

  • Advanced coding experience in all acute care patient types.
  • One year or more of coding/billing auditing experience.
  • Understanding of the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid and Illinois Department of Public Aid Coding Policy and Regulations.
  • Certification as a Registered Health Information Administrator (RHIA) or Registered Health Information Technician (RHIT).
