Director, Institutional Giving

St. Paul, MN

Minnesota Public Radio® is one of the nation's premier public radio organizations. With state-of-the-art headquarters in the heart of downtown St. Paul, Minnesota Public Radio operates a regional network of 38 stations, covering Minnesota and parts of Wisconsin, the Dakotas, Michigan, Iowa and Idaho.  We are the largest owner and operator of public radio stations nationwide.

Position Summary:

The Director of Institutional Giving contributes to the income of Minnesota Public Radio | American Public Media by leading grant related development and achieving revenue goals.  The position is responsible for leading a development team in identifying and pursuing opportunities for five, six and seven figure grants from foundations, corporations, and government agencies regionally, nationally and extending internationally.  Grant proposals encompass unrestricted operating support, restricted project funding, and capital and endowment gifts.  The Director will actively establish and foster relationships between American Public Media and the nation's leading philanthropies to build support for advancing the organization's mission.  In addition to working externally, this position works closely with the Managing Director of Development, the Senior Vice Presidents of each audience division - Minnesota Public Radio, American Public Media, Classical South Florida, Southern California Public Radio, program producers, project managers, financial analysts, and other cross-functional partners.

Position Responsibility:

Successfully manage and grow a portfolio of over $6 million in grant revenue from more than 100 different grantors in a fast-paced entrepreneurial nonprofit.  This position has significant external responsibilities in addition to leading the internal grant seeking process.

External Responsibilities

  • Lead Institutional Giving staff in significantly increasing financial support from relationships with foundations, both regionally and nationally.
  • Lead staff in the creation, implementation, monitoring and evaluation of annual and multi-year grant funding strategies designed to maximize financial support.
  • Broadly communicate the overall MPR|APM strategy for grant funding.
  • Cultivate relationships with major funders and prospects to broaden networks, identify potential funding opportunities, and solicit support.
  • Ensure regular communications, through both written materials and verbal conversations, with funders and prospects.

Internal Responsibilities

  • Work with content producers and senior management to identify ongoing and potential projects suitable for grant funding.
  • Lead development team in researching and identifying potential funders and creating strategies.
  • Lead the writing and editing of fundable proposals.
  • Facilitate cross-functional teams as necessary for grant development and fulfillment.
  • Provide overall oversight of the grant seeking and grants administration processes.
  • Develop and implement a process and materials for regular communications with funders and prospects.
  • Work synergistically with Major Individual Giving and Underwriting Sales departments to maximize potential for institutional support.
  • Oversee annual budget and forecasting for Institutional Giving department.
  • Work with managers in grant funded areas and their financial analysts to plan role of grant funded proposals in their overall budget and strategic business plans.
  • Work with managers in grant funded areas to ensure alignment between restricted revenue and expenses for grant funded projects.
  • Hire, supervise, train, and manage staff; provide on-going performance feedback; provide annual written review; provide on-going coaching and support; and develop individualized development plans.
  • Establish annual and multi-year goals for individual staff members.
  • Foster a creative and productive work environment. Create development opportunities for staff and training to enhance skills and foster growth.

Job Requirements

Required Education and Experience:

  • Bachelor's degree required -- English, Journalism, Business, Media, Communications or related liberal arts field preferred.
  • Minimum eight (8) years development experience in securing significant grants from corporations, foundations and/or government institutions.
  • Demonstrated track record in producing excellent written materials and successful solicitations, resulting in consistently winning six figure-plus grants.
  • Previous experience in researching and identifying major new funding prospects.
  • Proven success in building relationships with prospective funders resulting in funding support.
  • Significant financial and budget management experience and skills.
  • Prior experience managing a talented group or development department is required.
  • Management experience must demonstrate an ability to recruit and develop staff with high standards, demonstrating an ability to coach, inspire and lead.

Required Skills, Knowledge and Abilities:

  • Excellent demonstrated writing and editing skills.
  • Excellent verbal communication skills.
  • Leadership qualities of presence, confidence, judgment and integrity are required to command the respect and response of foundation personnel, colleagues and senior executive personnel.
  • Knowledge of the foundation community, current trends and staff, and their interests and guidelines.
  • Ability to develop and communicate strategies.
  • Ability to prepare and make presentations.
  • Must be capable of quickly synthesizing complex ideas and activities for coherent and compelling major grant projects in radio production, journalism, arts, outreach, Internet and more.
  • Motivated, creative, and organized self-starter.
  • Team player with demonstrated ability to collaborate to accomplish goals.
  • Able to handle multiple projects and conflicting priorities.
  • Comfort with ambiguity and constant change.
  • Ability to recruit, motivate, lead, coach, inspire, manage and retain talented development staff.
  • Must know how to build grant timelines and budgets.
  • Working knowledge of Windows-based software applications.
  • Available to travel frequently to meet funders and prospects.
  • Committed to and enthusiastic about the mission and programs and services of MPR|APM.

Preferred Skills and Experience:

  • Master's degree preferred - Development, Public Administration, English, Journalism, Business, Media, Communications or related field desirable.
  • CRFE or ACFRE designation is preferred.
  • Prior experience working with major national foundations in the arts and culture field.

Reporting To This Position:               

  • 2 Senior Development Officers
  • Development Coordinator
  • Development Assistant
