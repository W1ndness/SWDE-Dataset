With annual sales exceeding $12 billion, Parker Hannifin is the world's leading diversified manufacturer of motion and control technologies and systems, providing precision-engineered solutions for a wide variety of mobile, industrial and aerospace markets. The company employs approximately 62,000 people in 48 countries around the world. Parker has increased its annual dividends paid to shareholders for 52 consecutive years, among the top five longest-running dividend-increase records in the S&P 500 index.

Currently we have an opportunity available for an HR Representative (Entry-Level) in Lincolnshire, IL.

Position Summary:

Provides generalist support across several functional areas of HR including staffing and employment, AAP/EEO, employee and/or labor relations, job evaluation, compensation, benefits administration, training/development, talent management, HRIS, Lean HR, communications, personnel records administration, safety, and special projects. Will prepare reports in conformance with legislated requirements or company needs. Must have the ability to deal effectively with people at all levels of the organization. This includes the ability to communicate in verbal and written form to present information and respond to questions from groups of managers, employees, and the general public. Must maintain the highest level of confidentiality and integrity while providing daily flexibility in work schedule and hours (including overtime and weekends as needed) to support 3 shift operations and business/divisional needs.

Responsibilities:

  • Provide human resource generalist services, including compliance with regulatory agencies (i.e. EEOC).
  • Administer employment procedures including effective recruiting and hiring to support the facility's AAP/EEO goals. Includes developing, writing, and updating AAP/EEO programs.
  • Source, screen and recommend candidates for employment and conduct new employee orientation.
  • Assist in administration of benefit programs including eligibility, enrollment, and effective communications. Includes providing direction to employees in the resolution of problems, related to benefits, company policies, etc.
  • Administer compensation programs for hourly employees including, but not limited to, job analysis, salary surveys, compensation reviews, wage and benefit reviews, and performance review systems.
  • Administer local salary performance review process and employment related policies and procedures.
  • Responsible for HRIS administration and maintenance. Includes ensuring accurate and timely processing of payroll and other employee transactions.
  • Create and/or facilitate communications and employee involvement initiatives.
  • Conduct training sessions related to Ethics, benefits, safety, or others as required.
  • Implement and facilitate learning and development opportunities and training programs/plans.
  • Recommend and interpret company policies. Ensure company practices comply with federal and state employment laws and regulatory agency requirements.
  • Coordinate and conduct employee meetings and a variety of company social functions.
  • Support and lead change, as required, creating understanding among employees about the need to change and assisting in the modification of systems and structures to support needed change.
  • Maintain good rapport and working relationships with employees and supervisors in an effort to prevent, resolve, and/or cope with personnel issues affecting the workplace. Support issue-free environment. Assist supervisors and managers in handling and resolution of personnel and/or performance issues. Spend time on the production floor talking to employees and identifying employee needs.
  • Support the administration of Worker's Compensation and coordination of workplace safety programs.
  • Schedule and create project assignments to meet completion dates.
  • Assist in the development and implementation of human resources policies and procedures and their dissemination through employee booklets, communications, and/or meetings.

Job Requirements

Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's or Master's degree in Human Resources.
  • Exceptional communications, HRIS, and software (i.e. Microsoft Office; Org Plus; PeopleSoft) skills.
  • Effective teamwork and multi-tasking abilities.
  • Strong financial and business acumen.



Parker offers competitive, comprehensive salary and benefits packages. Based upon experience and performance, professional development and growth potential exists for rewarding career advancement opportunities. For consideration, please forward resume with letter of interest describing qualifications, experience, and salary history to: ihdrecruiting@parker.com
