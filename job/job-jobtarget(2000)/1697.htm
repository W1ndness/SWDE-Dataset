Position Summary:

The Senior Application’s Manager is responsible for the design, development, system testing, system support, and documentation functions for AAMVA’s Commercial Drivers License Information System (CDLIS). The Senior Application’s Manager position includes primary responsibility for ensuring AAMVA’s standards are met in the design, R & D, product engineering, and support of assigned applications. The Senior Manager position is responsible for day-to-day team management, 24x7 operational support, software corrections, and long term enhancement of software applications targeting a variety of platforms and operating environments.

 

Essential Duties and Responsibilities:

 

·         Project Management – Oversee the software support group. Translate business needs into projects; document the tasks, milestones, costs, durations, and implementation timelines.  Produce documentation such as impact assessment, system design, and system operations manuals.  Manage priorities and workload.  (25%)

 

·         Operations Support – Take ownership and responsibility for the operations of CDLIS.  Responsible for the quality of the data, the system health, security and performance.  Track issues, support requests and document resolution.  Prepare and maintain applications road-maps; introduce ideas for improvements.  Document maintenance procedures.  Participate in the user and technical support at the tiers 3 level.  (30%)

 

·         Technical Lead – Responsible for providing technical direction to the team.  Participate in the technical design and implementation of the application changes.  Enforce change control and release management.  Follow AAMVA software development methodology standards. (30%)

 

·         Vendor Liaison – Responsible for initiating and/or maintaining vendor relationships.  When applicable, responsible for monitoring the quality of the vendor’s deliverables and the vendor progress.  (10%)

 

·         Research and Professional Development – Responsible for researching new products and/or solutions that would meet the AAMVA and IRP needs/challenges.  Mentor the engineering team on new and advanced technologies.  (5%)

 About AAMVA

 

The American Association of Motor Vehicle Administrators (AAMVA) is a tax-exempt, nonprofit organization developing model programs in motor vehicle administration, law enforcement and highway safety. The association also serves as an information clearinghouse in these areas, and acts as the international spokesman for these interests. AAMVA operates a private secure network that spans North America which enables the transfer of information and data between the jurisdictions and other stakeholders including a number of Federal agencies.

Founded in 1933, AAMVA represents the state and provincial officials in the United States and Canada who administer and enforce motor vehicle laws. AAMVA’s programs encourage uniformity and reciprocity among the states and provinces. The association also liaisons with other levels of government and the private sector. Its development and research activities provide guidelines for more effective public service. AAMVA’s membership includes associations, organizations and businesses that share an interest in the association’s goals.

To learn more about AAMVA visit www.aamva.org.  Click on CAREER CENTER to see a listing of AAMVA employment opportunities and information on benefit programs.
