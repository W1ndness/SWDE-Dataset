MANAGER TECHNICAL DOCUMENTATION

MTA, Metro-North Railroad

New York, New York, United States

This is not an entry-level position.

Job ID: 7343444
Position Title: MANAGER TECHNICAL DOCUMENTATION
Min Education: BA/BS/Undergraduate
Job Function: Documentation Manager
Posted: November 2, 2010
Min Experience: 5-7 Years
Job Type: Full-Time
Apply URL: http://www.mta.info/mta/employment/
 



MTA Metro-North Railroad, an organization with a vital role in the economy of New York State and Connecticut, is a subsidiary agency of the Metropolitan Transportation Authority (MTA), the largest such authority in the United States. Metro-North is a dynamic organization with a strong focus on customer service, safety, new technologies, and service expansion. With a staff of approximately 5,900 people, Metro-North serves more than one million customers per week over 384 miles of rail that link historic Grand Central Terminal (GCT) in New York City and 120 stations in New York State and Connecticut. Metro-North rewards employee resourcefulness, encourages innovation and offers excellent job opportunities in a challenging and diverse environment.

The Manager – Technical Documentation ensures the quality and compliance of documents produced by Maintenance of Equipment (M of E) and reviews and approves all vendor supplied documentation for new and or existing rolling stock. They are responsible for the development, production, publishing and upkeep of all M of E documents and procedures.

RESPONSIBILITIES


  • Identify documentation needs and target audiences. Define and develop deliverables for target audiences. Coordinate and track multiple projects and documents. Institute forward thinking and industry best practices for documentation delivery systems.
  • Perform all the responsibilities of a senior technical writer. Review, edit, publish and/or develop all relevant M of E documents e.g. Technical Instructions, Notices, Alerts, Modifications, Workscopes and Specifications. Interview technical experts and review other external third-party information relevant to the subject matter.
  • Manage the Document Management System. Assist developing departmental standards including templates, guides, library, both digital and in print. Management of publication and distribution processes. Conduct independent tests of technical features and software/documentation usability. Monitor and manage the level of quality for all documents.

Job Requirements

REQUIRED QUALIFICATIONS


  • Bachelor's Degree in Business, Engineering or Library Sciences.
  • Minimum five (5) years of developing and creating technical documentation (e.g. Maintenance or Instructional Manuals, Illustrated Parts Catalogs and Specifications).
  • Three (3) years documentation development experience.
  • Three (3) years managing projects and/or a diverse staff.
  • Strong knowledge of cataloging and illustrating; Military Standards, principles of Task Analysis, Reading Grade Level techniques and principals.
  • Strong knowledge of Integrated Electronic Document, Manual Management and Application.
  • Strong technical, analytical and problem solving skills.
  • Excellent interpersonal and leadership skills with the ability to foster teamwork.
  • Knowledge of standard safety practices rules and regulations.
  • Knowledge of Microsoft Office and/or comparable applications.
  • Ability to write clearly and concisely, from logical instructions and brief abstracts, to book-length manuscripts.
  • Ability to convey complex information appropriately to a large user community and audience.
  • Able to recognize documents user audience and organize ideas in a seamless presentation.
  • Knowledge of publication process for a large corporation.
  • Ability to use graphics and illustration skills effectively.
  • Strong organizational and oral communication skills.
  • Must have valid driver's license.



PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS


  • Familiarity with FRA regulations and AAR standard specifications.
  • Knowledge of MNR Assets Management System (AMS) and Incident Management System (IMS).
  • High degree of engineering and government documentation standards comprehension (i.e. IEEE, MIL SPECS, etc.).
  • Understands correlation between technical publications and asset maintenance life cycle.
  • Working knowledge of transportation equipment maintenance and operations.
  • Working knowledge of equipment reliability centered maintenance principles.
  • Experience citing and critically analyzing the technical and scientific literature in written format.
  • Experience editing and proofreading Engineering and Maintenance documentation.



This position is safety sensitive subjected to toxicological testing.

We offer a competitive salary and a comprehensive benefits package.
