We are currently seeking a Critical Environment Manager to oversee the efficient operations and maintenance of all facility electrical, mechanical, structural, life safety, and critical back-up systems and to apprise the owner’s representative of any and all conditions affecting portfolio operations located in Washington, DC.

 

Responsibilities:

Develops and implements a Critical Environment Management program reflecting client requirements and industry best practices. Develops operational guidelines for client’s critical environments to be implemented by our Engineering and FM teams. Monitors performance against key performance indicators including uptime and business interruptions.

Monitors call center, work order, and MES performance across the portfolio, working collaboratively to address problem work orders and ensure closure times meet KPI targets. Works with the client and FMs to prioritize work to ensure uptime. Manages departmental staff, including selection, scheduling and job assignments; counseling, appraisals and recognition; recommending salary, promotional, disciplinary, termination, and other personnel actions.

Understands the engineering design and operational aspects of the facility’s systems and equipment. Must be able to immediately recognize system shortcomings and respond to operational and emergency situations. Works collaboratively with the Energy Management team to develop programs that reduce energy consumption.
Formulates and implements the preventative maintenance program.  Specific duties include but are not limited to: scheduling of preventative maintenance with a minimum disruption of building services, performing and/or delegating preventative maintenance tasks to the appropriately qualified maintenance staff member, orders parts and equipment required for repair, maintenance and installation of new equipment and facilities, and maintains inventory.

 


Job Requirements

Requirements:  

Education - Minimum of a bachelor’s degree or PE in mechanical, electrical engineering or equivalent of on the job experience directing and managing a facility engineering department.  Data Center engineering design andoperating training helpful.
• Job Experience - 10+ years of experience in facility/plant engineering and maintenance supervision.  Managerial experience required.
• Stationary Engineers license or equivalent.
• Personal characteristics - Good interpersonal skills, ability to communicate well in both oral and written reports.
• Special knowledge and skills - Exposure to HVAC, fluid handling/pumping, fluid filtering, mechanical systems, welding, and electrical systems engineering and operation, back-up power design and data center design.
• Must be proficient in basic computer applications and software such as MS Office, Lotus, E-Mail etc.
