PRIMARY CARE INFORMATION PROJECT

 

HEALTH INFORMATION EXCHANGE (HIE) TECHNICAL LEAD

 

The NYC Department of Health and Mental Hygiene (DOHMH), Primary Care Information Project (PCIP) is devoted to improving population health in underserved neighborhoods through health information technology. The Primary Care Information Project (PCIP) is the largest project of its kind nationwide, focusing on extending prevention-oriented electronic health records (EHRs) to primary care providers in NYC. Our mission is to transform the quality of health care services in NYC, especially in medically underserved communities, by helping providers to use an electronic health care delivery system to improve the quality of care. To find out more about this cutting edge project, visit www.nyc.gov/pcip.

 

The Fund for Public Health of New York (FPHNY) is a program partner to DOHMH.  FPHNY was recently awarded a Healthcare Efficiency Affordability Law of New York (HEAL NY) 17 grant.  One focus of this grant is the development of a "Patient Platform" health care data accessible by patients and which can receive data from multiple sources, wherever patients may receive health care.  These sources may include RHIO's, medical devices, electronic health records, patient entered data, prescription data and others.  Giving patients ownership and transparency into their health care data is paramount. 

 

We envision the development of patient-centric public health functions including aggregate data views, clinical decision support and public health interfaces to be a critical piece of this project. 

 

We are currently recruiting for a Health Information Exchange Technical Lead.   In collaboration with a technical project manager, the incumbent will be primarily responsible for:

 

§         providing technical support and intensive collaboration on the development, definition, design and vision of a complex information system

§         standardization of functions in alignment with national and local requirements,

§         managing vendor relationships

§         development of use cases, functional and business requirements, user interface development, authentication/authorization and all aspects of the software development lifecycle

 

The position will work across internal teams and multiple data sets with latitude for the exercise of independent judgment and initiative. 

 

This position is grant funded and begins on or after January 1, 2011.

FPHNY offers a competitive salary and benefits package.  If you would like to be considered for

this opportunity, please submit your resume, with salary requirements to publichealthjobs@fphny.org  Please indicate the “title of this position” in the subject line.

 

The Fund for Public Health in New York, Inc., is an Equal Opportunity Employer and encourages a diverse pool of candidates to apply
