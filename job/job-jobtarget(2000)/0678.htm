Provider Relations Director
(Job Code PRD)

The Provider Relations Director is responsible for the development and maintenance of a comprehensive provider network in the following areas: Hillsborough, Pasco, Pinellas, Polk, Citrus, Hernando, Marion, Lake and Sumter counties and must meet state, federal and accreditation requirements and Plan standards. Responsibilities include provider contract negotiations and initial and ongoing education and support.

Specific responsibilities include:

  • Responsible for network development and retention of comprehensive provider network, which meets state and federal time distance standards for access and availability and attainment of the Company's goals and objectives in an assigned geographical region.
  • Responsible for performing gap analysis and developing overall network development strategy for the assigned region for all lines of business.
  • Management of Provider Relations staff, including Provider Relations Representatives. This includes training, oversight, annual and periodic evaluation, payroll and time management.
  • Preparation of reports which document adequacy of network in the format specified by regulatory agencies, accrediting organizations, and company guidelines.
  • Responsible for Provider education, training, support and management to ensure compliance with regulatory, accreditation guidelines and the successful implementation of Company's goals and objectives in the assigned territory in accordance with established Company policies and procedure.
  • Cross-walking of CMS and NCQA requirements for model sub-contracts in preparation for internal, regulatory and accreditation audits.
  • Analysis of Service Fund & Utilization information for physicians and recommendations to Vice President of Network Operations/Business Development and CFO.
  • Meeting with physicians to educate and to help change practice patterns and refine network accordingly to meet fiscal and compliance targets.
  • Meeting with large volume and other important providers personally.
  • Working with PCPs and/or UM to make out of network arrangements as necessary.
  • Developing and maintaining preferred PCP and Specialists list for PCPs.
  • Submitting contracts pending to VP Network Development/Business Expansion for review by CEO.
  • Responding to letters of interest.
  • Review cap report for AFC and submit any necessary corrections.
  • Review PCP error report monthly and re-assign members to valid PCP and give to Member Services.
  • Oversight of the Annual PCP auditing processes for appointment availability and accessibility in the assigned geographical territory. Reporting results to Quality Assurance Manager.
  • Pre-delegation and annual audits of Delegated Entities.
  • Annual HEDIS audit collection of chart copies.

Job Requirements

Qualifications include:

  • An undergraduate degree from an accredited institution of higher learning and at least four years of experience in the managed care industry with a focus on contracting is preferred. Experience and skill set may be considered in lieu of education requirements. Management experience is required.
  • Ability to navigate and master proprietary software programs.
  • Ability to read and interpret documents
  • Excellent communication skills, along with good grammar, voice and diction
  • Proficiency with MS Office, basic computer and keyboarding skills.
  • Ability to formulate and articulate persuasive dialogue and to understand and convey complex financial information to providers.
  • Ability to travel 30percent.
