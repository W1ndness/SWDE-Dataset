Position Title: Interior Designer II

NELSON is a unique, “one stop shop” providing Interior Design, Architecture, Engineering, Strategic Planning, Information Services and Workplace Services in the corporate and residential real estate markets.  We have 36 locations in the United States and overseas and offer the resources of more than 350 professionals worldwide.


Selected by Interiors & Sources magazine as one of the five “Great Places to Work” and ranked among the “Top Four Giants” by Interior Design magazine, NELSON is always looking for people with the skills, vision and character who can go beyond a job description to find new and innovative ways to contribute to our company's success. If you are looking for a growing, highly professional work environment and enjoy working with creative minds, NELSON is the place for you. For more information on NELSON, please visit our Website at www.nelsononline.com.

 

We are seeking an Interior Designer II to participate as a team member and help in  the design, documentation and execution of tenant development related interior design and architecture projects, The candidate will participate, as required, for all phases of projects, meeting or exceeding the client’s business objectives; from programming, schematic design and design development, construction documents and furniture specification and to a limited extent oversight of design intent during CD and CA phase with a professional alignment of project schedule and budget.  Collaborate with the Project Manager and assist as appropriate on the regular and periodic NELSON Design Reviews.

 

Responsibilities

  • Function as a designer on designated projects, generally smaller in scope tenant development related projects which vary in complexity and are standards based.
  • Participate with the Project Manager and/or Senior Designer to understand/establish project goals and objectives including but not limited to work plan, schedule and budget parameters.
  • Attend meetings with clients, consultants, contractors and vendors as delegated.
  • Participate in the development and maintenance of the project standards library, including CAD and detail standards and procedures, and assist in maintaining the resource library.
  • Participate during trade or industry events or manufacturers product introductions proactively as needed.
  • Act as a team resource during the programming and design phases, and the coordination of documents.
  • Participate in aspects of project development from programming through conceptualization, design development and construction documents.
  • Participate in existing conditions surveys and architectural base and/or interior record plans preparation.
  • Participate in furniture and equipment inventories, document and prepare appropriate reports when appropriate.
  • Assist in or prepare programming questionnaires for use during interview process and set up project files as needed.
  • Develop space plans that are responsive to and conform to the program requirements and client’s goals and objectives.
  • Prepare conceptual design sketches that illustrate the architectural treatment of the space, develop concept statements and develop concept and presentation in the NELSON presentation formats as needed..
  • Design and assemble presentation boards, including furniture and finishes, concept statement and design abstract within the NELSON presentation standards.
  • Attend client presentations when appropriate.
  • Research products and systems that accomplish the design intent.
  • Prepare furniture and finish recommendations and alternatives, including budgets.
  • Develop plans, elevations and architectural details that illustrate the design intent and use of materials.
  • Participate in the development of construction documents and specifications to insure compliance with design intent.
  • Prepare furniture drawings and specifications as needed.
  • During the construction phase assist in the preparation of document revisions such as addenda, bulletins and clarification sketches.
  • As requested by PM or Senior Designer assist in the review of the contractor’s submissions for compliance with the design intent.
  • Assist in review as requested of the production of furniture, millwork and equipment contract shop documents.
  • During the construction phase attend job site meetings, on “as requested” basis to review elements of the construction and furnishing placement to ensure compliance with the design intent of the approved construction documents.
  • Participate in the punch list process as needed.

Job Requirements

Qualifications

  • Bachelor’s degree in Architecture or Interior Design, and 4 – 5 years’ experience in Architecture or Interior Design
  • Tenant space development skills, and client facing skills required
  • Proficient in Microsoft Office Applications, MS PowerPoint, MS Outlook, proficient in AutoCAD 2007 and REVIT; Familiarity with MS Project, Photoshop, rendering software and CAFM systems preferred 
  • Knowledge of programming, design and documentation processes, procedures and standards
  • Certificate or Registration of Interior Designer preferred.  LEED AP accreditation preferred.  Professional membership and active participation in Industry Associations ie. IIDA, and ASID, preferred
  • 3-D Rendering skills a plus
  • Capable of working in a fast paced environment and juggling multiple projects at a time.

 

We offer competitive wages based on skills and experience, excellent benefits package including; Paid Time Off, medical, dental, 401K, pre-tax Flexible Spending Accounts, and more. NELSON is an Equal Opportunity Employer.
