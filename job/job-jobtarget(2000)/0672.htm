Vice President for Finance

Providence College & Seminary

Otterburne, Manitoba, Canada

This is not an entry-level position.

Job ID: 7320720
Position Title: Vice President for Finance
Job Function: Vice Presidents / Deans
Posted: October 26, 2010
Job Type: Full-Time

Providence College and Seminary invites applications for the position of Vice President for Finance. The Vice President serves as the Chief Financial Officer of the Institution and supervises the business office. Providence is a Christian academic community in the evangelical tradition. Candidates will agree with the Providence Covenant of Faith found on the About Us page at www.providencecollege.ca or www.providenceseminary.ca.

In addition to appropriate training and an accounting designation (CA, CGA, CMA), the successful candidate will have significant experience in corporate non-profit finance. He/she will have a history of positive working relationships, clear communication, and professional conduct.  The Vice President is a member of the President's Cabinet and has a significant role in the overall development of the Institution. All candidates will be considered, however Canadians and permanent residents of Canada will be given priority. A resume may be sent by December 15 to:

David H. Johnson, Executive Vice President
Providence College
10 College Crescent
Otterburne, Manitoba R0A 1G0
Phone: 1-204-433-7488, ext. 266
Fax: 1-204-433-7158

Job Requirements

General Summary:
- The VP for Finance is an active member of the President’s Cabinet and is responsible for the development and implementation of short and long-term strategic planning with that group.  To support these responsibilities the person in this position must have excellent communication skills and a proactive approach.
- The VP for Finance is responsible for all budgeting and financial reporting.


Essential Duties and Responsibilities:
- Oversees the preparation of the annual budget and exercises general budgetary control over all accounts.
- Supervises proper record-keeping and accounting of all financial transactions of the Institution.
- Represents Cabinet on the Audit & Operations Committee of the Board
- Schedules and facilitates the annual audit with the audit firm and Audit Committee. 
- Provides regular reports on the status of the Institution’s finances to the President, President’s Cabinet, Board and others.
- Negotiates loans, bank fees and other banking arrangements as required.
- Is familiar with GST, PST and all charitable reporting required for the organization.
- Ensures provision of adequate and effective business service to the PC/PTS employees in areas such as purchasing, printing, banking, telephone and data processing.
- Is familiar with all financial aspects of the Blackbaud enterprise software.
- Supervises all salary and payroll procedures, as well as the financial arrangements with respect to employee benefit programs.
- Reconciles and analyzes balance sheet and income statement accounts including preparation of associated journal entries and working papers.
- Prepares divisional and organizational financial statements or reports
- Compiles, analyzes and interprets a variety of financial and transactional information to review and improve business and accounting processes as well as system setup.
- Prepares working papers and other associated documents for year-end and various audit requirements.
- Reviews and verifies accounting entries and adjustments to help maintain the integrity of accounting and reporting systems.
- Develops and produces routine and ad hoc management information reports for a varied audience.
- Provides a variety of business support, project assistance and analysis for various Departments.
- Prepares and remits reports to various governmental and non-governmental agencies such as Canada Revenue Agency, Statistics Canada, Canadian Council for Christian Charities, Association of Theological Schools, Association of Biblical Higher Education, and others as required.
- Assists other departmental employees with advice, training, and expertise on the use of financial systems.

Job Specifications/Qualifications:
- Spiritual qualifications should include references which indicate this person has a mature, consistent walk with the Lord, and has had an established fellowship with a local body of believers.  Candidate must read and sign complete agreement with the Doctrinal Statement and be able to give a clear, verbal testimony of their personal faith in Jesus Christ as their personal Saviour.
- Canadian Professional Accounting Designation (CA, CGA, CMA)
- Appropriate administrative experience.  This person should present him/herself in an executive image in both appearance and conversation.
- Five years recent related progressive accounting experience in a computerized environment including experience in developing spreadsheets, financial statements, analytical reports as well as general accounting responsibilities.
- Job requires sitting at desk for long periods of time; general office conditions.
- May include competency requirements in the areas of knowledge, skill, ability, or characteristics associated with high performance on a job.

Skills/Knowledge:
- Knowledge of the Corporation's business environment and mandate.
- Knowledge and understanding of corporate systems.  Ability to use automated data systems effectively and to contribute to their design and improvement.
- Exceptional analytical skills and apply them through deductive reasoning to both empirical evidence to the statistical output of controlled data systems.
- Deal with highly sensitive files, impacting upon the credibility of both the Corporation and the federal government.  Sense of diplomacy and tact in working in sensitive and demanding areas.
- Ability to take initiative and work independently on policy and product improvement.
- Organization skills to manage several ongoing and competing tasks at one time.
- Ability to work in a multi-disciplinary team environment.
- Above average writing and communication skills are a prerequisite.
