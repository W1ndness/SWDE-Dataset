MAJOR FUNCTION:

An administrative exempt staff position for The University of Montana Foundation, the Director of Development and Alumni Relations provides leadership and management of the comprehensive fundraising activities of the College of Health Professions and Biomedical Sciences.  In partnership with the dean, the DOAR defines fundraising priorities and develops an annual fundraising plan based upon these priorities.  Specific responsibilities include the identification, cultivation, solicitation and stewardship of major gift prospects.  One focus will be on the identification and solicitation of major gifts from individuals, corporations, foundations and organizations; but an equally important focus will be upon strengthening the overall fundraising program of the College (most specifically annual giving) and working with college alumni, including communications, stewardship, President's Club members and planned giving donors.  This position may also include supervisory duties.

Responsibilities:

1.   Create opportunities for increasing major gifts:                                       

Identify, develop strategies, cultivate, and manage solicitations and stewardship for major gift prospects in support of the College's priorities.

Manage a portfolio of current and future major gift prospects ($10,000+)*; make an agreed upon face-to-face visits annually*; develop and maintain written strategies for "Top 25" prospects* (strategies for these prospects may include utilizing volunteers, dean, faculty, or the campus leadership team including the president of the University); and initiate activities that will bring major gift prospects closer to the University.  Develop strategies that include planned giving opportunities for donors in partnership with the Director of Gift Planning.

2.      Enhance the College's advancement efforts                                                 

Oversee the college's annual giving program in conjunction with the Director of Annual Giving*; oversee and provide coordination for volunteer committees established in support of the College's fundraising priorities; direct the creation and design of a comprehensive advancement-related communications plan designed to support fundraising for college priorities; identify, manage and motivate volunteers that will support the College's fundraising efforts.

3.  Support the Dean:                                                                                              

Develop an annual fundraising plan that includes goals and objectives for the College's fundraising priorities; include the dean and his/her leadership team when appropriate for solicitations; prepare all necessary briefing materials and talking points for solicitations; provide leadership for all cultivation, solicitation and stewardship follow-up that includes the dean; help to identify volunteers to serve on the Dean's Development Advisory Board and other boards; and staff the committees as appropriate.

4.   Create synergy for increasing the level of private support to the University through partnership with other units:                                                                   

Develop collaborative development strategies with other units (e.g. colleges, schools, and campus centers) for prospects with multiple interests in order to be donor-centric and maximize gift potential; work with the central development office, UMAA and volunteers to expand the prospect base and to identify opportunities for donor cultivation and stewardship; participate as a member of the development staff in prospect management meetings and other strategy sessions.

5.    Perform other duties as assigned:                                                                   

Includes, but is not limited to, providing staff and volunteer training for college development efforts, and participating in professional development activities.

* - denotes measurable indicators of success


Job Requirements

MINIMUM Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's degree
  • 5 years experience in fundraising or related sales experience
  • Excellent written and oral communications skills
  • Possesses and demonstrates excellent interpersonal relationship skills; an ability to work independently and in a multi-layered team environment while fostering positive relationships, teamwork, and collaboration
  • Willingness to travel extensively, and at times, work odd hours and weekends
  • Familiarity with personal computer programs and database software applications

ADDITIONAL DESIRABLE QUALIFICATIONS:

  • Advanced degree
  • Major gift fundraising experience
  • Record of securing major gifts
  • Strong knowledge of development activities, preferably in higher education
  • Experience leading and motivating volunteer boards
  • Public relations or marketing experience
  • Experience in one of the college's disciplines
  • Demonstrated ability in strategic planning
  • Experience in grant writing
