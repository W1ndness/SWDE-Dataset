Position Summary:

Responsible for providing support for all information systems employed by the Office of Financial Assistance, while remaining current on federal, state and institutional policies regarding the awarding and disbursement of all forms of financial aid. Develop, maintain, and optimize use of information systems for all levels of staff. Serve as primary contact for problem resolution for all information systems, both hardware and software related.

This position should provide leadership and direction for the exploration and implementation of new technologies that will assist the work processes of the Office of Financial Assistance (OFA).

This position has primary responsibility for data gathering and reporting for all internal and external financial aid reports.

Serve as member of Information Management Group (IMG) for Enrollment Management as well as the Banner Coordination Committee and myNova committee with primary responsibility to technical needs and process improvements for the Office of Financial Assistance as well as Enrollment Management. Participate in meetings and retreats held by the Office of Financial Assistance.
Duties and Responsibilities:

Primary responsibilities include:
1. Serve as primary support for all processing and reporting related to the Banner Financial Aid/Student system both internally and externally. Maintain documentation on report information sources and production processes. Responsible to train staff members on Banner and related reporting tools related to FA.
2. Act as primary contact for all financial aid issues with SunGard Higher Education and all technical issues with U.S. Department of Education and Pennsylvania Higher Education Assistance Agency (PHEAA), COD, NSLDS, ACS, College Board and other third party vendors.
3. Responsible for staying current on upgrades and releases, including notifying programmers and database administrators regarding the availability of those from SunGard. Test and sign off on upgrades and new releases.
4. Assist and support the information processing staff within the Office of Financial Assistance, whose responsibilities include letter generation, FAFSA and College Board dataload, online award notifications, email communication, file creation and maintenance, and NOLIJ imaging of all financial aid documents.
5. Provide data and analysis for internal and external reports and surveys as required by the University and external interests including for example: IPEDS and US News and World Report.
6. Act as intra-office contact person for any computer related questions or issues, including support with SunGard products, Financial Aid web site, Oracle Discoverer, EDExpress, and Microsoft Access.
7.Maintain operational knowledge and support of the following software items and web-based programs:
Microsoft Office Suite, Banner Student and Financial Aid Modules, NOLIJ Imaging, Web Tailor (NOVASIS), myNOVA, Oracle Discoverer, EDConnect, EDExpress, Cognos, FAA Access to CPS Online, COD, NSLDS and College Board.
8. Act as liaison for outside agencies as well as UNIT for any and all technical issues as related to financial assistance.


Job Requirements
Minimum Qualifications:

Baccalaureate Degree required, preferably in Information Systems
Knowledge of BANNER Financial Aid/Student is highly desirable
Knowledge of UNIX based relational database, specifically Oracle
Knowledge of imaging technology
Basic PC hardware familiarity (PC's, printers, peripherals)
Knowledge of World Wide Web and associated technologies
Knowledge of computer related networking
Knowledge of database query language (SQL)
Knowledge of Microsoft applications
Knowledge of financial aid interface systems including PHEAA systems and others as defined above
Knowledge of USDE and College Board software
Must be proficient in population selection and report functions both within Banner and through third party software such as Discoverer, Access and Cognos.

3-5 years of experience in information technology and systems
2-4 years of experience in Financial Aid

Ability to effectively communicate with a variety of constituents including the ability to train functional users on new and existing technologies as applicable to their positions

Ability to develop full use of existing software systems including; BANNER, NOLIJ and web applications

Ability to problem solve systems problems as related to applications
Strong analytical and planning skills
Preferred Qualifications:

1-2 years of experience in web development desired
