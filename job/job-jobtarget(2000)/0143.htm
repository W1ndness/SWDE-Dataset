University Information Technology (UIT) is a university-wide service organization dedicated to the strategic planning, implementation, and support of technology products and services that anticipate and meet the academic, research, and business needs of the Tufts community. UIT offers technical leadership and services to our customers with a focus on providing innovative solutions, delivering exceptional customer service, and creating a reliable infrastructure that demonstrates value to the students, faculty, staff, and alumni of Tufts University. UIT works in partnership with schools, business units, and other academic support organizations to provide campus-wide services in the areas of academic and research technology, enterprise application systems and services, networking and telecommunications, information security, data center operations and user support, training, and outreach.

Within UIT, the University Information Technology Support Center (UITSC) provides a broad range of technical and IT service functions for the University community as well as second-level services for Tufts local IT support organizations. The department tests, documents, communicates, and supports major university-wide IT initiatives.

The IT User Support Specialist focuses on providing superior first-tier support to end users on a variety of issues including ad-hoc training, technical troubleshooting, customized IT solutions, and any other related tasks as assigned. The position reports to the Manager of the UITSC and works within the UIT Support Center.

Working as a client representative within the UITSC, the IT User Support Specialist identifies, researches, and resolves technical problems. She/he responds to telephone calls, email, and personnel requests for technical assistance and documents, tracks, and monitors issues to ensure a timely resolution. The IT User Support Specialist develops technical documentation on IT-related tools and processes. Successful candidates must be familiar with a variety of IT concepts, practices, and procedures including security, networking, desktop and application support.


Job Requirements

Basic Requirements: Bachelor's degree OR High School graduate with three (3) years of directly applicable computer/networking support experience will be considered equivalent. Candidates must have a background in the configuration, installation, and troubleshooting of the following: Microsoft Windows (all versions), Mac-OS 8.0 and above, Mozilla email and web clients, Microsoft Office Suite, Telnet clients, SSH clients, Antivirus software, TCP/IP, and Imaging software and well as experience troubleshooting peripherals (printers, scanners) and handheld devices (PDAs), knowledge of desktop security and standards (security/networking), sound technical judgment based on experience and standard troubleshooting with an ability to be innovative to resolve complex problems. The IT User Support Specialist must possess excellent customer service and communication skills, client relationship management and negotiation skills, ability to communicate/work with colleagues and clients at all levels of technical ability, and have a high degree of integrity/ethics, superior customer focus, attention to detail, solid analytical and problem solving skills, ability to travel around the Medford campus and to and around both Boston and Grafton campuses as needed. The IT User Support Specialist will collaborate with project groups and other technical support staff in resolving technology related issues. The selected candidate must be pro-active, and able to work independently as well as collaboratively.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Demonstrated time and project management skills, and the ability to work consultatively as part of a team are preferred.
  • Experience with the following applications/products/protocols preferred: Trend OfficeScan, IMAP, LDAP, Palm OS, Microsoft ActiveSync, BlackBerry OS Apple iOS, LANDesk, Active Directory and group policies, data recovery tools, Eudora, Mozilla, Microsoft Exchange, patch management solutions, backup strategies, WINS, DHCP, DNS, TCP/IP
  • If direct professional experience is substituted for a degree, some college work and a commitment to obtain a four-year university degree is strongly preferred. Possession of, or a commitment to obtain, major, industry recognized professional certifications.

Special Work Schedule Requirements:On-call and work after hours, as needed.

Tufts University is an AA/EOE employer and actively seeks candidates from diverse backgrounds.
