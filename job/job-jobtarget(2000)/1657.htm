Executive Administrative Assistant

Medical College of Georgia

Augusta, Georgia, United States

This is not an entry-level position.

Job ID: 7245537
Position Title: Executive Administrative Assistant
Required Travel: None
Min Education: BA/BS/Undergraduate
Industry: Health Care/Health Services
Posted: December 6, 2010
Job Function: Executive Assistant
Min Experience: 5-7 Years
Job Type: Full-Time
Apply URL: http://www.mcg.edu/jobs
 

 The Medical College of Georgia (Georgia Health Sciences University) is seeking a highly professional and articulate individual with excellent written and verbal communication skills and strong attention to detail to serve as Executive Administrative Assistant to the President. The ideal candidate will have the ability to function independently with minimal supervision, prioritize tasks and manage multiple projects simultaneously. This includes the ability to handle confidential matters, data, and information. The candidate should be able to assess and anticipate the needs of the President's office and be comfortable with dealing with a diverse set of people on the telephone, email, and in person in a friendly, professional manner. This position will work under the supervision of the Chief of Staff and the President. This position will oversee and manage all office procedures and other tasks as assigned in order to facilitate the Office of the President and CEO of the Health System functions and operations.


Job Requirements

Bachelor's degree and seven years' executive level office management experience in a professional office setting.

Excellent writing, analytical and problem solving skills.

Ability to communicate effectively and to follow oral and written instructions. 

Ability to make independent decisions and handle multiple competing priorities. 

Must possess excellent time management skills; maintain strict confidentiality; honest and trustworthy; respectful and possess cultural awareness and sensitivity.

Strong knowledge of personal computers and various software packages, specifically Word, Access, Excel and Power Point; must possess excellent bookkeeping skills; must be flexible and demonstrate sound work ethic.

Responsibilities

Coordinate work flow and meetings;

In coordination with the Executive Schedule Coordinator, maintain the President's calendar, schedule appointments, and brief the President on the schedule; Arrange travel for the President including purchasing of airline tickets, hotel reservations, and car rental; creating and maintaining schedule for all trips, including meetings, logistics, and events; Manage the President's email in box by screening all incoming mail, determining how best to route certain questions and inquiries, and responding to selected messages; Respond to incoming calls and requests. In the President's absence, ensure that requests for action or information are relayed to the appropriate staff member; Organize and maintain efficient filing system for the President's office, including subject, index, and/or cross-reference files; Maintain the Office of the President's budget and serve as liaison to the Finance Department on budget issues. Handle and assure timely payment of all President-related invoices; Compose routine correspondence on own initiative or from brief notes or oral instructions, referring to other sources for detailed information; Assemble materials or reports for distribution; Proofread typed or printed work for grammatical construction, completeness, clerical, arithmetical, and typing accuracy, and for compliance with established standards and special instructions; Create, manage, and edit all content in the President's presentations; Serve as liaison between the President's office and other offices and organizations; Monitor and assist with maintenance of the President's Website; Manage academic support needs for the President including, but not limited to, working with important publications, editing those publications and ensuring that they are submitted for publication in accordance with the applicable deadlines.

Prepare agendas and make arrangements for cabinet/board meetings; Interact with the organization's leadership; Attend meetings as requested and record minutes; Compile, transcribe and distribute minutes of meetings; Answer telephones and transfer to appropriate staff member; Support staff in assigned project-based work; May conduct research, compile data and prepare documents for consideration by and presentation to the Chief of Staff and President/CEO; Other duties as assigned.


Shift/Salary

Shift: M-F/Days

Annual Salary Range:  Minimum - $60,058, Midpoint  - $81,078, Maximum -$102,099 Salary to be commensurate with qualifications and experience of candidate.

Pay Grade: 21

Recruitment Period: 9/27/2010 - Until Filled.

 

Conditions of Employment

All candidates are required to successfully pass a Background Check review. 

Other Information

MCG is a tobacco free environment and the use of any tobacco products on any part of the campus, both inside and outside, is strictly prohibited.

This position is also responsible for promoting a customer friendly environment and providing superior service to our patients, students, faculty, and employees.  "MCG is a patient-and-family-centered care institution, where employees partner everyday with patients and families for success."
