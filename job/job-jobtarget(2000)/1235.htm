TITLE:                  DIRECTOR OF INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY

SALARY:              $83,458 - $111,277

DEPARTMENT:     MANAGEMENT AND BUDGET

DIVISION:           INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY

 Applications are available in person at City of Jolet, 150  W. Jefferson Street, Joliet, IL  60432 or at the website: www.cityofjoliet.info. 

APPLICATION DEADLINE IS DECEMBER 3, 2010 OR UNTIL FILLED

 

GENERAL PURPOSE

 This position directs the City’s IT operations, including computer operations, technical support, systems analysis and programming, telecommunications, and microcomputer technology.  Ensures sufficient systems capacity for organizational needs.  Oversees telecommunications systems including voice and data.  Manages the planning, design, installation, and maintenance of networks.  Supervises and directs all IT personnel.

 ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

 Manages and supervises the Information Technology Division to plan, direct and coordinate all activities for the development, implementation, and maintenance of the City’s information and communication systems, services, and network facilities.

 Plans, directs and participates in the IT development activities of the City, including the analysis, research and acquisitions for improvement and expansion of IT services.

 Advises and coordinates with all City departments on IT matters; prioritizes and assigns requests from users; evaluates and implements hardware and software solutions that address department needs to ensure conformance with system standards.

 Plans and manages the IT budget, including acquisition, implementation, training, operations and support for all IT systems and projects.

 Maintains, records and documents system designs, configurations, vendor agreements and solutions that are developed.

 Installs new equipment, cabling and programs; directs in-house trouble shooting and repair activities; instructs/educates users, managers and IT staff in new or changing systems and technologies.

 Performs leadership role on technical and advisory committees; prepares long-range plan for technology services in coordination with all City Departments.

 Evaluates and monitors the performance of outside consultants involved in developing systems for the City.

 Provides 24-hour support for all City Departments.

 Performs other related duties as required.


Job Requirements

MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS

 Bachelors Degree in Computer Science or related field;

 Masters preferred; 

Ten years of progressively responsible experience in an IT environment;

 Five years of significant supervisory and administrative experience;

 Any equivalent combination of education and experience which provides the required knowledge, skills and ability.

 Comprehensive knowledge of information technology systems and networking theory, principles, and current practices and developments, including machine capabilities, data applications and database support;

 Considerable knowledge of the application and integration of wireless technology systems;

 Considerable knowledge of server and desktop operating systems, including Windows and DOS, and protocols, including TCP/IP, MS SQL Server and MS Access, C++, Visual Basic, and other programming tools; IBM iSeries (AS400, i5) computer;

 Considerable knowledge of word processing, spreadsheets, database, and graphics applications;

 Considerable knowledge of the principles and techniques of systems design, develop, program planning, implementation and evaluation;

 Skills in communication (both in technical and non-technical terms) to professionally and respectfully interact with coworkers, managers, the public, and government officials to exchange or convey information, both orally and in writing;

 Ability to plan, organize, direct and evaluate the work of subordinate employees;

 Ability to document procedures and provide training for IT staff and users on implementation and operation requirements of the system(s).

 Ability to use the required tools and equipment that may come into common use or be necessary to perform needed tasks.

 Ability to move and/or lift up to 35 pounds.

 *A complete job description is available in the Human Resources Division.

 SPECIAL REQUIREMENTS

 Valid State of Illinois Class D driver’s license.
