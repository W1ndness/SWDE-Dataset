*1. Analytics:
a. Serves as a member (along with the Medical Informatics Medical Director and Actuarial Informatics  Vice President) of the Medical Cost Management Team. Team is responsible to assure that GIO maintains a pulse on any and all Medical Cost Trends. Responsibilities also include the direct enhancement and development of Key Performance Indicators and Metrics. Develops and assist with presentation to the Medical Cost Management Team Agenda in identifying trends of interest and direct support to assure actionable initiatives are focused and measurable. Tackles high impact business and operational problems with large financial impact $600 - $700 million in annual medical expenses and $100 million in annual pharmaceutical expenses.
b. Responsible for financial and business performance monitoring, auditing and analyzing cost and pricing models utilized by executive management in Geisinger Insurance Operations. In addition provides performance analytics to Clinical Enterprise initiatives (CEO Sweet Spot Initiatives) including but not limited to ProvenCare, ProvenHealth Navigator (Medical Home), Skilled Nursing Home (Transitions of Care), and Physician Group Practice. Assures drilldown availability in the information structures for the performance of ad-hoc analyses. Utilizes CDIS, Geisingers Integrated and multi-dimensional database.
c. Develops and maintains Analytic standards necessary for interpreting financial recast models (Membership, Premium, Expense, and Medical Loss Ratio reporting) and utilization and expense trends for GHP. Directly responsible for the Re-Cast business rules and change management prioritization. Develop procedures for data integrity for a complex series of audits and reviews. Results are used to create the secondary data warehouse (Re-Cast) that provides a true picture of historical financial and clinical results. The secondary data warehouse is the information repository standard used by the GIO to support strategic analytics. Directly responsible for claim experience stratification for managing utilization and expense trends in a way that ensures appropriate rating. Results of the Financial recast are used in GHP - Finance for our Long Range Financial Models and Budgeting.
d. Develops guidelines for risk stratification by product line and market segment levels and formulas for use in strategic decision making and incorporation of our predictive modeling software (Medai). Medai is used to provide forecasted, concurrent, acute and chronic risk at a variety of levels.
e. Supports the Vice President of Health Plan  Actuarial Services with Business Intelligence for Geisinger Health Plan current Medical and Pharmaceutical Trends.
f. Produces Key Performance Indicators used to measure and quantify the Health Plans experience through Early Indicator Reporting using our Authorization System (Wisdom) and CDIS.
g. Performs statistical significance testing using SAS for Generalized Linear Models (Normal, Poisson, Lognormal, and Gamma Distributions). As well as mean and standard deviation testing on key performance indicators (i.e. Admissions, Risk Scores, Expenses, etc.)
h. Directly responsible for all efficiency analytics for Geisinger Health Plan ProvenHealth Navigator Initiative. Responsible for Target creation, Efficiency Scoring, Key Performance Indicators presented to Physicians within PHN sites, and Quality Indicators on a Monthly basis. Target development is comprehensive of historical experience trended to predict future experience, with consideration of any future changes along with a sites risk change.
i. Responsible for the oversight, integrity, and Scorecard of the Health Plans Medicare Membership Analytics. Must maintain and implement the membership business rules accurately. Provides actionable Business Intelligence to the Medicare Sales and Marketing, Operations, and Senior Leadership to address items such as New Enrollment, Voluntary and Involuntary disenrollment, and Net Gain changes within Product Line, County, Region, and Broker/Sales Representative.
j. Supports of the Medicare Rate Filing which determines the premium that will be set for our Medicare membership every year. Provides follow-up support to questions posed by CMS in order to assure our Medicare business rates are adequate and just.

*2. Communication:
a. Identifies data element and other related topics for training within the Actuarial Informatics team and serves as an active member of the training team. Participates in all required meetings, educational overview sessions, product implementation and review sessions as communicated by the Vice President of Actuarial Informatics.
b. Effectively serves as a liaison with external consultants and vendors. Proactively educates third parties of any complexities associated with requests and works collaboratively to achieve success in most productive way possible.

*3. Leadership:
a. Directly conducts as well as guide and train the Actuarial Informatics Trends/Metrics team in advanced analytic modeling, best practice tool usage, and review business intelligence to support strategic initiatives. Responsible for managing team, communicating and assigning tasks as appropriate, performing yearly performance evaluations, etc.
b. Serves as the liaison with Vice Presidents, Medical Directors, Medical Management, Provider Network Management, Actuarial Services, Finance, Underwriting, Sales & Marketing, and other Health Plan departments. Must maintain a comprehensive understanding of operational processes and ability to evaluate impacts on data compilation, reporting, and performance analytics.
c. Directly assists in meeting the financial, operational, and performance goals of the health plan. Reviews benefit plan changes, pricing changes and opportunities for cost reduction and in conjunction with GHP Finance, models their impact. Assist analyst in written analytic summaries used to communicate financial performance and trend analysis to GIO and GHS executives. Summaries include highlighting of trends and performance metrics of interest as well delving into the reasons and drivers as to why the trends are occurring.

*4. Continuous Improvement:
a. Maintains proficiency and expertise in Health Plan data application, state of the art business intelligence software and reporting tools that include, but are not limited to, MS SQL Server Components (Query Analyzer & coding, Analysis Server, Stored Procedures, Jobs, and Functions), Business Objects (Crystal Reports, Crystal Enterprise, Crystal Analysis, OLAP, and Xcelsius), SAS, and Microsoft Office (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Access) to prepare complex multi-factorial analysis. Synthesizes and Interprets analyses.
b. Continues development/enhancement of skill sets for self and direct reports. Actively pursues industry knowledge growth by researching text contained throughout the Health Plan. Encourages certification from the Academy for HealthCare Management


Job Requirements

SKILLS AND ABILITIES:

Demonstrates strong mathematical, statistical, financial, technical, interpersonal, organizational, project management skills, and ability to maintain confidentiality.

Demonstrates effective communication skills (oral and written).

Demonstrates the ability to work independently, be a problem solver and decision maker, identify key business issues and develop appropriate action plans.

Demonstrates literacy of health plan data and statistical analysis tools e.g. Business Objects, Crystal Reports, SAS, SQL, Excel, PowerPoint and Word.

Demonstrates managerial and leadership abilities and maintain effective working relationships throughout all levels throughout GHP

EDUCATION AND/OR EXPERIENCE:

Masters degree preferred. Bachelors degree in Business, Mathematics, Statistics, Actuarial Science, Computer or other data relevant field required.

Minimum five years related professional work experience in a progressive healthcare setting required.

Passage of at least two (2) Society of Actuarial (SOA) exams required. Membership in the American Academy of Actuaries and Associates of the Society of Actuaries or the Casualty Actuarial Society preferred.
