United Way of Treasure Valley has two job openings for a Community Engagement Coordinator and a Community Impact Coordinator.

The Community Engagement Coordinator provides assistance to the VP of Community Engagement on issues related to volunteer management and volunteerism. This position works with the Vice President of Community Engagement to create volunteer opportunities to advance UTWV's Community Impact Goals and activities in Education, Income and Health. Essential duties include

 Administrative

  • Primary administrative support to the Vice President of Community Impact
  • Greets the public and visitors in person or over the phone, and obtains (or provide) information
  • Sorts and distributes mail to department employees; folds and stuffs documents to process outgoing mail; receives and sends documents via fax machine; and sends mail
  • Manages the Volunteer Solutions software and the Volunteer Center Database
  • Schedules Agency Speakers per request

Community Engagement

  • Create and manage corporate volunteer opportunities
  • Assist with identifying and pursuing opportunities to collaborate with other community groups to promote involvement and awareness of volunteerism, including opportunities with United Way
  • Develop new relationships by engaging people from specific market segments - university and high school students, retirees, business/company employees, people in non-traditional workplace settings - in meaningful volunteer opportunities
  • Assist with UWTV committee and initiative project volunteer recruitment, job descriptions and orientation
  • Consultant and advisor to senior staff on all United Way Volunteer recognition programs/activities/evaluation
  • Seek funds through grant writing and relationships to support Volunteer Center activities
  • Track volunteer hours, placement, satisfaction, etc. for effectiveness
  • Assist in developing and management of an effective internship/ in house volunteer program

 Stewardship

  • Work with senior staff to develop appropriate recognition of individuals, companies, partners, and volunteers
  • Event planning, as a key component in United Way's relationship building strategy with donors, volunteers, partner organizations and the community

 Marketing and Communication

  • Social media; Face Book, Twitter, blog
  • Composing articles for UWTV's monthly Newsletter

The Community Impact Coordinator provides high-level administrative support to the VP of Community Impact by conducting research, preparing statistical reports, handling information requests, and performing clerical functions such as preparing correspondence, receiving visitors, arranging conference calls, and scheduling meetings.  Essential duties include:

 Administrative Support to VP of Community Impact

  • Calendar management; requiring interaction with both internal and external individuals to coordinate a variety of meetings
  • Create, read and analyze incoming memos, submissions, and reports to determine their significance and plan their distribution
  • Provide follow up and status reports on assignments delegated by the VP of Community Impact to other team members
  • Prepare responses to correspondence containing routine inquiries

 Community Impact Coordination

  • Provides administrative support during Investment process
  • Coordinate meetings and documentation for the Vision Council
  • Take meeting minutes and maintain current records
  • Represent UWTV to the public, businesses and Vision Council members at the request of the VP of Community Impact
  • Coordinate department activities and schedules as needed
  • Grant tracking and general information collection in Andar (Community Building Module)

Job Requirements

Community Engagement Coordinator minimum job requirements:

  • Bachelor's degree in a related field and/or equivalent work experience
  • Ability to successfully manage multiple projects simultaneously
  • At least 3 years of experience in Marketing and Communications
  • Ability to speak and write clearly and concisely for a variety of audiences
  • Passion for helping people become involved and make a difference through volunteerism and community engagement
  • Flexibility to work as required by the project, including evenings and weekends.
  • Proficient in Microsoft Office applications, databases, social media outlets, can manage web-based tools and database programs

____________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________

Community Impact Coordinator minimum job requirements:

  • Bachelor's Degree in Business Administration and/or equivalent work experience
  • At least three (3) years experience in general office responsibilities and procedures
  • Must be computer literate in MS Office
  • Knowledge of principles and practices of basic office management and organizational procedures

Evaluation & Selection Process

Successful applicants should submit a resume, at least three references with contact information, and a cover letter that summarizes why he/she should be considered for the position.  Applicants are encouraged to use their cover letter to address their skill-set as defined above and why they feel they would be the best candidate for the position.   The employment package includes a competitive salary and benefit package.
