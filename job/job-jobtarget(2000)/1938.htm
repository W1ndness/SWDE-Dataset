Information Technology Support Specialist
Graduate College
University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign



The Information Technology Support Specialist works closely with all Graduate College academic professional staff, nonacademic staff, and graduate assistants to support their information technology needs. The Information Technology Support Specialist provides technical support and develops new functionality for the Graduate College's public web site. The Information Technology Support Specialist develops, deploys, and maintains software solutions for streamlining Graduate College business processes for students, staff, and faculty.

Responsibilities

  • Implement new functionality for the Graduate College's Drupal web site.


- Develop forms.
- Assist with template and theme development.
- Update and maintain style sheets.
- Assist with integration of graphical and video content.

  • Develop, deploy, and maintain software applications to support business processes of the Graduate College
  • Implement version control for the Graduate College.
  • Set up and monitor Graduate College's email lists.
  • Provide user support for Graduate College software applications.
  • Assist the Director of Information Management Systems in the documentation of the Graduate College policies, applications, and user instructions.

Job Requirements


Qualifications

  • Bachelor's degree in Computer Science or other technical field required.
  • Experience with web application development, preferably in Drupal environment.
  • Familiarity with one or more programming languages including PHP, ColdFusion, and SAS.
  • Experience working with databases in application development and writing basic SQL queries
  • Strong verbal, written, interpersonal and presentation skills. Demonstrated ability to work with users of varying technical skills and understanding.
  • Demonstrated ability to work with users of varying technical skills and understanding.
  • Ability to work in a team environment.
  • Ability to take initiative and act independently within policy guidelines.


SALARY: Commensurate with experience.

STARTING DATE: As soon as possible after closing date.

APPLICATION PROCEDURES: For full consideration, please create your candidate profile at http://jobs.illinois.edu and upload a letter of application and resume, along with the names and contact information of three references by October 18, 2010. The position will remain open until filled. All requested information must be submitted for your application to be considered.

For further information regarding application procedures, you may contact Lynn Schaefer at axlschae@illinois.edu or by phone at 217-244-5948. You may also visit grad.illinois.edu for more information.
