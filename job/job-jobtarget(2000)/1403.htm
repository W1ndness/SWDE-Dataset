Position Summary

Direct and oversee all BSA/AML investigative and transaction monitoring functions of the Corporate BSA Department. The scope of responsibility includes the entire life cycle of detection of unusual activity, ongoing monitoring and preparation of investigative summaries and accurate SAR narratives for submission to the Quality Control group. Ensures investigative and due diligence functions are performed accurately, timely and in accordance with regulatory and agency requirements and bank policy.

Major Areas of Accountability

A.Analyzes and adjusts work procedures governing investigative functions within the BSA Department for maximum efficiency and effectiveness. These include processes designed to identify, review and report terrorist financing, money laundering schemes, structuring, high-risk customers, and accounts prohibited by bank policy (e.g. illegal money service businesses). This includes but is not limited to: Internal AML investigations, monitoring accounts and transactions for suspicious activity, enhanced due diligence functions, and Quality Control and SAR Filings.

B.Directs a staff of 40-50, including managers, supervisors, AML investigators and due diligence specialists. Interviews candidates for positions to be filled in the organization; approves appointments, selections, or reassignments to positions. Exercises the usual authority concerning staffing, performance appraisal, promotions, salary recommendations, and disciplinary actions.

C.Works closely with the supervisors and coordinators to plan and prioritize daily work load to meet department goals and objectives and regulatory timelines. Designs management reports for this purpose.

D.Establishes performance standards for investigators and due diligence specialists. Monitors productivity and periodically reviews investigative summaries. Identifies and addresses training needs. Maintains strong quality control protocols.

E.Assists in the development of a case management system. Once implemented, ensures the case management system is utilized to its full extent.

F.Serves as the primary resource for investigative techniques and advice on the disposition of alerts/escalations. Oversees decision making related to identified scenarios, to include whether or not to file a SAR is appropriate and whether additional action, such as contacting law enforcement or regulators or recommending the cessation of a customer relationship is necessary.

G.Directs interaction with other departments within the bank, as necessary, to coordinate the investigation of cases involving potential criminal matters relating to theft, fraud, embezzlement, and bank robbery, or other violations of law.

H.Directs interaction with the Internal Audit department, others performing reviews and testing of the Bank’s AML program and regulators regarding the AML investigations and enhanced due diligence processes.

I.Reports trends in new suspicious activity to the BSA/AML Officer and Technology and MIS Manager in order to adjust resources and detection software.

J.Effectively manage the department functions while meeting and adhering to corporate, legal, and regulatory policies, guidelines, and requirements regarding compliance. Work with the Compliance and Legal departments for overall updates, procedure changes or development, and resolution of issues.


Job Requirements

Minimum Qualifications

Education

Required:
•Bachelor’s degree in Criminal Justice, Business Administration, or related experience or equivalent work experience

Preferred:
•CAMs certification

Work Experience/Skills:

Required:
•Six to eight (6-8) years experience in investigations, security operations and/or fraud prevention with at least 5 years of dedicated AML experience.
•Five years of prior management/supervisory experience required.
•Strong technical knowledge of AML Compliance, the BSA and USA Patriot Act.
•Solid foundation in conducting AML investigations. Extensive knowledge of money laundering and terrorist financing typologies.
•Highly analytical and organized. A high degree of good judgment is required.
•Strong interpersonal skills are critical due to the cross-functional team environment and interaction with senior compliance, bank operations and line of business management responsible for the execution of other AML-related functions such as account opening and high-risk customer management.
•Demonstrated managerial experience, with the ability to provide work direction, coaching and leadership to employees with varying levels of experience.
•Excellent verbal and written communication skills.
•Knowledge of and proficiency in the use of systems, databases and technology.

Supervisory Responsibilities

•Direct supervision of exempt level staff; indirect supervision of non-exempt staff (approximately 40-50 ees)
