Department Overview
 
Pacific Gas & Electric Company, incorporated in California in 1905, is one of largest combination natural gas and electric utilities in the United States, delivering safe, reliable and responsive gas and electric service to approximately 15 million people across a 70,000 square mile area of northern and central California. Its service area stretches from Eureka in the north to Bakersfield in the south, and from the Sierra Nevada in the east to the Pacific Ocean. Based in San Francisco, the company has approximately 20,000 employees  
 
Pacific Gas and Electric Company is a wholly owned subsidiary of PG&E Corporation. In 2009 PG&E had approximately $40.8 billion in assets and generated revenues of approximately $14.6 billion. 
 
The Information Security and Compliance (IS&C) function is responsible for IT security across the organization as well as the related implementation of appropriate controls for regulations such as SOX, HIPAA, CA1386 and NERC/CIP. The function has direct responsibility for establishing IT Security standards, toolsets and processes and then matrixing them to the delivery, operation and maintenance areas across PG&E. The Security Intelligence department is responsible for ensuring that PG&E proactively identifies and assesses threats to its network and data, investigates intrusions and other relevant events, and has a sophisticated and detailed understanding of the evolving threat landscape. 
 
Position Summary
 
The Intelligence Analyst (Logical) is responsible for providing intelligence analysis in order to identify threats, quantify vulnerabilities, and reduce risk to the corporation. Focused on threats originating in information technology environments, the Analyst will produce actionable information in a clear and concise manner.  The ideal candidate will possess a strong background in Information Security, ideally gained in a public utility. He/she will apply analytical rigor to all work but remain open to non traditional information or conclusions.  The analyst will have excellent writing and presentation skills and be able to defend his/her analytical conclusions in logical and persuasive terms. The Analyst will be able to work to tight deadlines and be able to function calmly and efficiently in crisis situations. 
 
Responsibilities
  • Provide cogent intelligence analysis to PG&E decision makers in both written and oral form
  • Maintain a high state of situational awareness regarding threats to the Corporation, the industry and US critical infrastructure
  • Initiate pertinent analysis in accordance with established intelligence priorities
  • Alert decision makers to emerging threats in a timely manner
  • Identify, vet and develop new information sources
  • Leverage existing expertise and knowledge in order to improve the intelligence deliverable
  • Leverage and expand the capabilities of existing analytical tools and technologies; recommend new technologies as appropriate
  • Support crisis events with intelligence collection, analysis and/or dissemination
 

Job Requirements
Minimum Qualifications
  • Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science, Information Systems or related degree; or equivalent work experience. 
  • CISSP (or ability to obtain one within 12 months of appointment)
  • 5 + years experience in Information Security, gained in a hands-on technical role
  • 5 years experience with Industrial Control Systems, commonly called SCADA, with vendor or with a utility
  • Ability to work under pressure
  • Strong analytical ability with readiness to defend analysis in the face of countervailing opinions
  • Strength of conviction without being dogmatic 
  • Ability to work effectively with an incomplete data set
  • A quick study of new technologies, industries and scenarios
  • Willing to apply logic and academic rigor to make sound analytical leaps
  • Ability to seek out new, unique information sources and make sound judgment regarding source reliability and validity
  • Strong, concise communication skills with a brisk writing style; able to build a compelling and effective narrative
  • Strong presentation skills; comfortable in front of corporate leadership
  • Ability to prioritize work and move swiftly toward strong analytical conclusions
  • An outgoing and engaging personality supported by an inquisitive, analytical mind
 
Desired
 
  • CPP a plus
  • Other Certifications considered desirable include ECSAP, EnCE, GCFA Security, SANS certificates
  • Proven ability to network across law enforcement and homeland security communities.
  • Experience conducting enterprise or industry risk assessment and mitigation
  • Experience in Applied Research or Investigations
  • Previous experience with analyst software tools e.g. i2™ analyst notebook
  • Proven ability to network across law enforcement and homeland security communities
  • Experience working in cross-departmental task forces, ideally with Government homeland security or intelligence community experience
  • History of participation in industry or technology information sharing groups, formal or informal a plus
