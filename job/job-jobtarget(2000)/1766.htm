InfoComm International® seeks an experienced Director of Exposition Marketing to plan and direct marketing of organization's products and services for its U.S. trade show and events.  This position reports directly to the Vice President of Marketing and Communications. 

 

Primary duties and responsibilities include but are not limited to the following:

  • Establish and execute marketing plans to achieve the objectives of InfoComm’s U.S. trade show events, including attendance and exhibition goals and onsite promotion of events and conferences.
  • Research, analyze and monitor financial, technological and demographic factors to capitalize on market opportunities and minimize effects of competitive activity.
  • Develop and manage trade show marketing operating budgets.

·         Develop and implement direct mail campaigns including copywriting, design creation, and management of vendors including list brokers, mail houses and others contracted to support elements of marketing plan.

·         Track campaign efforts through a comprehensive coding system and conduct extensive marketing analysis of campaigns and audience demographics.

·         Create and execute comprehensive marketing timelines and action calendars, cost tracking spreadsheets, attendance projection spreadsheets and marketing plan templates to ensure efficient and cost-effective marketing strategies.

  • Plan and oversee advertising and promotion campaigns including print, online, social media, electronic media and direct mail.
  • Oversee development and production of promotional and collateral materials in concert with InfoComm’s outside marketing design agency and in-house marketing services team.
  • Monitor competitive exhibition offerings and marketing activities.
  • Work with internal and external data lists to mine appropriate customer selections for promotional purposes.
  • Maintain marketing relationships with strategic in-conjunction partner groups.
  • Resolve conflicts and facilitate changes in InfoComm trade show marketing to ensure objective fulfillment and swift response to marketing problems and opportunities.
  • Guide preparation of marketing activity reports and presents to executive management.

·       Perform other duties as assigned by executive management.

 

Supervisory Responsibilities                            

This position has no direct reports.  The position works under the direction of the Vice President of Marketing and Communications with consultation from the Sr. Vice President of Expositions in determining the overall marketing strategy and tactics for InfoComm’s U.S. expositions.  Additionally, the position must work collaboratively with other marketing and communications department staff including the Director of Marketing Services, Production Coordinator, Web Content Coordinator, Marketing Editor and Director of Public Relations, as well as Director of Education Marketing and the Director of Membership Marketing to integrate association messaging and produce overall show marketing needs.

 

Competencies                               

The successful candidate must demonstrate the following competencies:

Strategic Planning, Analytical, Problem-Solving, Project Management, Financial Management, Customer Service, Relationship Development, Interpersonal Skills, Oral and Written Communication Skills, Teamwork, Visionary, Leadership, Quality Management, Business Acumen, Organizational Support, Motivation, Planning/Organizing, Dependability, Initiative and Innovation


Job Requirements

Education and/or Experience                                   

Bachelor's degree (B. A.) or equivalent; or four to ten years related experience and/or training; or equivalent combination of education and experience. Trade show and conference marketing experience is required.

 

Language Skills                                   

Ability to read, analyze and interpret general business periodicals, professional journals,

technical procedures, or governmental regulations.  Ability to write reports, business correspondence and procedure manuals.  Ability to effectively present information and respond to questions from groups of managers, clients, customers, and the general public.

 

Mathematical Skills                               

Ability to work with mathematical concepts such as probability and statistical inference.  Ability to apply concepts such as fractions, percentages, ratios, and proportions to practical situations.

 

Computer Skills                                  

To perform this job successfully, an individual should have knowledge of Contact Management systems; Database software; Graphic Design software; Internet design and Content Management software; Project Management software; Social Media platforms, Spreadsheet software and Word Processing software.

 

Other Skills and Abilities                                    

Experience managing the outsourcing of major parts of the marketing program is necessary. Experience with planning and implementation of comprehensive marketing campaigns is necessary, including direct mail, e-marketing, advertising, list selection, data mining, relationship management and website management is critical.

 

Travel

Position requires approximately 10 to 15 days of travel per year for site visits, exhibitor committee meetings, industry meetings and 8 to 10 days during the InfoComm event.

 

Fax resume and cover letter with salary req’s to Christy Jones, Human Resources Generalist, at #(703) 273-8432 or via e-mail to HR@infocomm.org. EOE.
