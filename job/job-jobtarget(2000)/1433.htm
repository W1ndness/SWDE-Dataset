About the Department:
PLEASE NOTE: EMAIL CONTACT ADDRESS CHANGE:  nchavez@fhcrc.org

The IT Project Manager will report to the Manager of IT Projects and Business Operations as part of the IT department.  IT Project Managers at the Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Center coordinate or manage IT projects to support the Center and partner goals and initiatives and collaborate with process owners and key stakeholders to identify project scope, goals and deliverables.  Projects may correct, enhance or change existing processes, systems or services. Project Managers work consultatively  with multidisciplinary teams composed of employees from various IT departments and disciplines, vendors, and other departments and partner institutions brought together to accomplish specific goals.  They assure that project scope, schedule, and budget objectives are achieved as well as appropriate project management methodology is applied.


To Apply:  You MUST submit a formal cover letter and resume directly to: nchavez@fhcrc.org to be for considered.

About Us:
Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Center, home of three Nobel laureates, is an independent, nonprofit research institution dedicated to the development and advancement of biomedical research to eliminate cancer and other potentially fatal diseases. Recognized internationally for its pioneering work in bone-marrow transplantation, the Centers four scientific divisions collaborate to form a unique environment for conducting basic and applied science. The Hutchinson Center, in collaboration with its clinical and research partners, the University of Washington and Childrens Hospital and Regional Medical Center, is the only National Cancer Institute-designated comprehensive cancer center in the Pacific Northwest. Join us and make a difference.

Equal Employment Opportunity:
The Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Center and The Seattle Cancer Care Alliance are equal opportunity employers, committed to workforce diversity.

Qualifications:
The ideal candidate will possess the following qualifications:

Project Management:
Demonstrates skill in applying project management concepts and tools in the successful planning, execution, controlling and closing of IT projects
Determines which process or deliverable is best for the project at hand
Develops high quality and appropriate deliverables
Synthesizes complex or diverse information
Collects and researches data; uses intuition and experience to complement data
Adapts to changes in the work/project environment and the ability to deal with frequent change or delays
Changes approach or method to best fit the situation
Sets expectations, delegates work assignments and monitors delegated activities
Facilitates large, complex work groups; strong meeting facilitation skills
Demonstrates strong organizational skills
Proficient with common office applications (Word, Excel, Outlook, etc.) and project management applications and tools, such as MS Project and Visio.

Technology Skills:
Stays knowledgeable in a broad spectrum of information technologies
Strives to continuously build knowledge and skills
Experienced in managing a broad range of IT projects for the full life cycle including Software Development and Infrastructure Implementations projects
Collaborates with vendors to ensure technical solutions and supports project requirements, timelines and cost objectives.

Problem Solving:
Skillfully identifies, analyzes, resolves or escalates problems/issues in a timely and appropriate manner
Develops alternative solutions independently or through the project team
Facilitates resolution by working well in group problem solving situations
Uses reason even when dealing with emotional topics.

Interpersonal Skills:
Demonstrates interpersonal and social skills required for positive interaction with others
Utilizes behaviors that establish trust, minimize conflict, maximize cooperation
Keeps emotions under control; remains open to others ideas
Exhibits objectivity and openness to others views
Contributes to building a positive team spirit; supports others efforts to succeed
Displays passion and optimism
Empowers others to fulfill project goals
Effectively influences actions and opinions of others; gives appropriate recognition to others
Works successfully with multi-disciplinary project team members and with different levels of staff, partners and organizations.

Oral & Written communication:
Speaks clearly and persuasively in positive and negative situations
Listens well and solicits information and gets clarification; responds well to questions
Demonstrates group presentation and facilitation skills
Conveys complex or technical information effectively to technical and non-technical folks; effectively conducts meetings.
Writes clearly and informatively; edits work for spelling and grammar; varies writing style to meet needs; presents complex data effectively.

Professionalism & Quality:
Reacts well under pressure
Treats others with respect and consideration regardless of their status or position
Accepts responsibility for own actions; follows through on commitments
Exhibits sound and accurate judgment includes appropriate people in decision-making process
Demonstrates accuracy and thoroughness
Looks for ways to improve and promote quality.

Education
A bachelors degree in a business or technical discipline
Minimum of 6 years program/project management experience in leading IT Projects
Experience managing multiple projects with varying complexity and scope
PMP or equivalent certification desired.

Responsibilities:
Major Duties:

1.Work with project sponsors, stakeholders and team members to define project requirements, work plans, timelines, budgets, scope, risks, staffing requirements, organization and approach.
2.Appropriately and effectively use the departmental standard methodology and templates.
3.Conduct business analysis to document existing and planned processes and procedures.
4.Plan, direct and coordinate activities of project personnel to ensure project progress on schedule and within budget.
5.Track and manage project budgets and issues; escalating issues to the appropriate level in a timely manner.
6.Prepare status reports and keep project sponsors, team, stakeholders and others informed of project status and related issues.
7.Manage requests for changes to scope, schedule, and budget according to defined change control procedures.
8.Serve as project liaison representing the project to other center departments, senior or scientific management, affiliated individuals or institutions, and outside organizations.
9.Effectively close projects ensuring that all project work and documentation is complete and that all project work has been successfully transitioned to operations.
10.Lead Lessons Learned meetings at the conclusion of other IT projects or events.
11.Provide project management advice to technical project leads as assigned.
12.Contribute to project planning by developing project justifications and estimates and/or proposals.
