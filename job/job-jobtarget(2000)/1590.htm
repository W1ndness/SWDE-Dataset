Apply URL: http://jobs-airdc.icims.com?&sn=careers.aera.net
 
The American Institutes for Research and Learning Point Associates have merged to become the leading education research company in the nation providing a continuum of education research, assessment, technical assistance and policy analysis from the school level up to the national and international levels. Our programs apply science to address real world issues on behalf of clients that include federal and state agencies, international development agencies, leading foundations, school districts and private businesses and our experts are a frequent source of information and guidance for policymakers, clients and the news media. We are currently seeking a Senior Researcher who may be located in either our Washington, D.C., Naperville or Chicago, Illinois office.
Responsibilities:
1.Manage medium to large projects and/or high-profile or high-risk projects (tasks, timelines, outputs, budgets, risk, scope, team members, and conflict and risk management)
2.Lead the design of policy, research studies, and evaluations that apply valid scientific techniques and efficient analyses
3.Serve as a lead expert to the organization applying special, in-depth knowledge
4.Design data collection frameworks and/or data crosswalks
5.Review and edit summaries, analyses, and results
6.Provide advice and guidance to staff in terms of appropriate research methods and reporting
7.Develop and manage daily relationships with clients to plan, implement, and present the results of the study while maintaining appropriate professional objectivity
8.Monitor, coordinate, and provide work direction to assigned project staff
9.Lead teams in writing proposals and/or grants and evaluation reports, including recommendations
10.Maintain AIR’s national visibility and presence through conference presentations and writing for refereed journals
Additional research responsibilities include:
1.Analyze, interpret, and report complex quantitative data using appropriate statistical and/or psychometric methods (multiple linear regression, HLM, Structural Equation Modeling, Rasch, and so on)
2.Evaluate the statistical methods and procedures used to obtain data to ensure validity, applicability, efficiency, and accuracy
3.Conduct and lead studies using rigorous designs such as randomized control trials, regression discontinuity, and quasi-experimental designs with tight controls

Qualifications:
•Doctorate in education or Master’s degree and substantial relevant experience
•5-plus years of demonstrated experience working on research and/or evaluation projects, including experience as principle investigator
•Demonstrated skills and experience in quantitative and qualitative instrument development as well as in data collection and analysis
•Demonstrated skills and experience in project management, including designing, budgeting, managing, and conducting large scale research and/or evaluation projects, preferably of education programs, improvement initiatives, services, and products
•Demonstrated ability to lead and manage research teams; strong project management and staff supervision skills
•Strong oral and written communication skills, including the ability to produce concise, high-level summaries and briefings
•Ability to work in a collaborative, team-based environment
•Demonstrated skills and experience in all aspects of proposal writing
•Proficiency in various analytic software, such as Microsoft Access and Excel, SPSS, SAS, Stata, and Nvivo
•Ability to work with and manage a variety of educational stakeholders and audiences

TO APPLY:

AIR offers an excellent compensation and benefits package, including a fully funded retirement plan, generous paid time off, subsidized garage parking, tuition assistance, and more.  For more information, please visit our website at www.air.org.  To apply, please go to http://jobs-airdc.icims.com?&sn=careers.aera.net and conduct a job title search.  EOE.
