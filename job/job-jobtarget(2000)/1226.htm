VW Credit, Inc.

Wholesale Documentation Specialist

Location: Herndon, VA

Role Summary: Responsible for ensuring legal documentation is accurately & thoroughly completed for the Billion Dollar Wholesale Loan Portfolio and ensuring and protecting the Company’s first lien position on all of the assets of the Dealer, to the extent possible.


Job Requirements

Role Responsibilities:

• Research, review & analyze regulatory & legal issues relative to public records of Uniform Commercial Code (“UCC”) Financing Statements/liens, tax liens, judgments & corporate status by utilizing CT Lien Solutions & Dun & Bradstreet reports for Regions in order to obtain & maintain VCI’s Lien priority status. • Ensure & maintain compliance of the UCC, Revised Article 9 (“RA9”), Secured Transactions & appropriate state statutes pertaining to corporations, limited liability companies, partnerships & sole proprietorships in support of VCI’s wholesale lending programs. • Manage nation-wide jurisdictional filing requirements for filing UCC Financing Statements. • Interface with & support the Regions (Sales and Risk Management), Legal Counsel, Controller, & the Dealer Credit Committee in the identification & development of a plan of action to resolve legal risk, including negotiation of disputed terms in intercreditor agreements with competing lenders. • Support Commercial Underwriting Managers to identify documentation requirements for wholesale floorplan, capital loan and line of credit & lien priority status & effectively communicate identified areas of legal risk within the wholesale lending process to VCI. • Provide guidance & analysis to the Region Managers, Area Team Leaders & Business Development Managers, & Wholesale Specialists in responding to Dealer inquiries regarding legal issues relating to loan documentation. • Provide on-going training to Regions on loan documentation, UCC & RA9 compliance in regard to lending programs, • Participate in VCI meetings to communicate & develop tools & processes to effectively maintain team workflow. Identify supporting or improvement objectives to minimize deviations from the process plan. Develop and improve processes to ensure the prompt execution & return of loan documentation. • Track loans from request & approval through documentation & closing. Insure that loans are closed timely & accurately. • Verify & ensure compliance with Risk Management procedures for executed & returned loan documentation. Verify closing documents comply with loan approval standards. Control all closing documents & prepare loan closing binders. • Manage & maintain wholesale loan documentation. Take leadership role in the execution and oversight of graphic imaging of loan documentation. • Produce loan documentation promptly to Legal Counsel for litigation with dealer or other parties. • Conduct audits of current accounts as required & update loan documentation as needed. • Responsible for timely delivery, accuracy & accountability of all closing documents.

Skills:

• Analytical and conceptual thinking – using logic and reason, creative and strategic • Communication skills – interpersonal, presentation and written • Computer savvy – skilled in the use of software • Integration – joining people, processes or systems • Influencing and negotiation skills • Problem solving • Resource management

Specialized Skills Required:

• Working knowledge of Business Law • Thorough understanding of Revised Article 9, Secured Transactions of Uniform Commercial Code • Experience with preparation & filing of Entity Formation documentation, drafting documents from forms, collecting/organizing closing & due diligence documents & creating Closing Binders • Extensive experience in reviewing legal commercial loan documents • Extensive usage of CT Lien Solutions, Public Records, Lexpedite, Dun & Bradstreet, & research tools in general (e.g. Internet/legal websites) • Demonstrated computer skills using Windows, Excel, Word, Power Point, Access.

Specialized Skills Desired: Notary Public Paralegal Certification

Qualifications: 3-5 years experience

Education: Required: Bachelor degree or equivalent related experience; Paralegal Certification Desired: Currently working towards or intends to pursue a law degree.

We are proud to be an EEO employer M/F/D/V. We maintain a drug-free workplace and perform pre-employment substance abuse testing.
