The major purpose of this position is to expand and administer a comprehensive Planned Giving Program that includes outright gifts of real estate and all forms of deferred giving, including estate planning, bequest donations, charitable remainder trusts, and gift annuities.

 

ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:

The Director will be responsible for managing a major and planned gift prospect portfolio, generating and closing major and planned gifts and assisting other college personnel with Planned Gift proposals.  In particular, the Director will be responsible for:

 

  • establishing overall objectives and priorities for planned gifts and for executing that plan;
  • developing marketing materials for print and online use (including educational materials for younger constituents) developing written marketing, engagement, solicitation, and stewardship strategies; moving prospective donors through the traditional cultivation and solicitation and stewardship cycle, soliciting and closing major, deferred, and reunion gifts;
  • identifying potential deferred giving donors, visiting donors both independently and with major gift colleagues, developing planned giving proposals and reviewing trust, will, and gift annuity agreements, and life insurance contracts;
  • monitoring and reviewing probates in which the College is a beneficiary and consulting with the College's legal counsel and financial department as appropriate;
  • overseeing administration of the College’s planned gift portfolio of about $5 million in trusts, gift annuities and pooled income fund gifts;
  • administering a 50th Reunion Gift Program and assisting in the management of the College's Planned Gift Recognition Society.

 

A significant amount of time is spent in direct visits, telephone calls, email, and written correspondence.  The Director will also represent the College as a major gift officer and will meet stated visitation goals.

 

WORKING CONDITIONS:

 

Person typically works in an office environment.  Frequent travel is required.  Evening and weekend work is occasionally required.  May occasionally lift 10/15 lbs., or may walk and/or bend in the performance of the job functions.

 EOE

 


Job Requirements

 Bachelors degree required, Juris Doctor (JD) degree with specialization in Estate Planning, Taxation or related areas or, at a minimum, a graduate-level degree in a directly-related field are preferred.

·         Excellent negotiation and interpersonal skills and the ability to work independently and as part of a team environment

·         Ability to plan strategically, set priorities, and efficiently manage resources along with meeting goals

·         Strong project-management skills and organizational abilities

·         Demonstrated analytical and strategic thinking capabilities

·         In-depth knowledge of taxes, finance, legal policies, and planned giving vehicles, (including charitable gift annuities, charitable trusts, life estate agreements, real estate and gifts-in-kind).

·         Experience in the use of computer software for planned giving calculation

·         Ability to work effectively with Board members, colleagues, donors, and Senior Staff

·         Excellent oral and written communication skills

·         Knowledge of Raiser's Edge software is preferred.
