Essential Functions:
Oversee a teaching and research Machine Shop laboratory facility. Provide engineering services to faculty, post doctoral, graduate and undergraduate students, including but not limited to, the design, fabrication and testing of scientific laboratory research apparatus, instrumentation systems, and processes, utilizing solid modeling and other computer based engineering modeling and design programs. Serve as an engineering consultant to the faculty, post doctoral, graduate and undergraduate students. Additionally engage in the repair of teaching and research equipment and supervision of the operation of a full service Machine Shop. This facility is of critical importance to the College of Engineering and its research objectives.

Job Requirements

Minimum Requirements:

  • Bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering or related field and/or an equivalent combination of education and experience.
  • The ability to analyze the feasibility of engineering designs.
  • The ability to design, fabricate, and test scientific apparatus, instrumentation systems, devices, and processes including those which must withstand extreme pressures, temperatures, and forces.
  • Demonstrated ability to act as an engineering design consultant with knowledge of the design of standard machine components, material selection, tolerance selection, design for manufacturability/assemblability, etc. The ability to provide advice on the design of complex engineered devices including establishing design requirements.
  • The ability to transform designs and concepts expressed through oral instructions, written instructions, sketches, and/or blueprints into completed projects.
  • Possess judgment, ingenuity, and problem-solving skills necessary to complete a variety of open-ended design and fabrication tasks, both independently and with guidance from a supervisor.
  • Proficiency in the use of computer-aided design and manufacturing (CAD/CAM) software such as SolidWorks, MasterCam, Surfcam, etc.
  • Operating knowledge of materials used to construct scientific laboratory apparatus, instruments, devices, and processes, including knowledge of hardness, toughness, strength, welding characteristics, machining characteristics, corrosion resistance, etc., and how these characteristics influence material selection. The ability to select appropriate machining processes for a given material.
  • Demonstrated knowledge and skill in the safe operation of a machine shop. Ability to safely test scientific apparatus, instrumentation systems, devices, and processes, including those which must withstand extreme pressures, temperatures, and forces. Knowledge of techniques and procedures for safe handling of various materials.
  • Skill and experience in using traditional (manual) machining equipment including, but not limited to, drill presses, band saws, mills, and lathes.
  • Skill and experience in using CNC machining equipment including multi-axis mills and lathes and five-axis wire EDM machines. The ability to program complex three-dimensional parts with CAM software and simple parts using the machine control.
  • Skill and experience in using precision grinding equipment including surface grinders and universal grinders.
  • Skill and experience in: making very high precision fixtures and holding devices, designing and constructing small one-of-a-kind cutting tools, and designing and constructing other specialized tools and gages.
  • The ability to perform compound angle setups on milling machines.
  • Experience in working with close tolerance such as +/- 0.00025 for mills and lathes and +/- 0.00005 for surface grinders.
  • Ability to troubleshoot, repair, and maintain machine tools.
  • Ability to troubleshoot, repair, and maintain mechanical and electromechanical systems.
  • Experience with maintaining records in an accurate and organized manner.
  • The ability to provide effective instruction and training in the safe and effective use of machine shop equipment.
  • Good oral and written communication skills. Skill and experience in writing and editing technical documents using correct grammar and punctuation in a concise and logical style.
  • Excellent interpersonal skills. Ability to interact effectively with faculty, staff, and students.
  • Hired applicant must successfully pass a background check through the Department of Justice.


    Preferred Qualifications:
  • Experience working in a research and/or instructional laboratory in a higher educational setting.
  • Experience managing a machine shop and supervising machine shop personnel.
  • Experience at maintaining inventories of stocks and materials necessary to enable effective operation of a research machine shop.
  • Experience at preparing budget estimates and maintaining operating budgets.
  • Ability to weld using TIG, oxygen-acetylene, and arc welding equipment.
  • The ability to fabricate sheet metal parts using brakes, shears, etc.
  • The ability to install and operate strain gages.
  • Ability to design, construct, and troubleshoot basic circuits such as instrumentation amplifiers for strain gages, pressure transducers, etc.
  • Skill and ability to troubleshoot and repair electrical systems.
  • Skill and ability to repair and maintain vacuum pumps.
  • Ability to set up and operate data acquisition systems such as those manufactured by Omega and National Instruments.
  • Knowledge and experience in programming with a high-level language such as C++ and Visual Basic.
