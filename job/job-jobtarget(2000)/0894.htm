Technical Writer I

Job Responsibilities
A Technical Writer’s responsibilities include, but are not limited to, the following:

  • Work independently or as part of a team to author a suite of airplane technical manuals (AFM, AMM, CMM, IPC, WDM, SRM) from engineering, manufacturing, flight, and supplier data.
  • Research technical information and interview SMEs to develop technical documents.
  • Develop documentation in structured Adobe Framemaker to maximize electronic technical data reuse and authoring efficiency.

Job Requirements
Requirements

Position Requirements:

  • 0 - 5 years of technical writing experience.

·         Ability to proactively research complex source data, read and interpret blueprints and engineering specifications; also extract and comprehend typical engineering data (bill of materials, schematics, etc.).

  • Experience with MS Office suite.
  • Professional attitude toward work assignments; willing to accept responsibility if needed.
  • Self-starter and eager to learn new methods of accomplishing tasks.

 Preferred Experience and Abilities

  • Demonstrate strong attention to detail, good communication skills, and personal organization.

·         Familiarity with aircraft systems and technical documentation, including illustrated parts catalogs, airplane maintenance manuals, operator manuals, and installation instructions.

·         Familiarity with Industry Writing Specifications (ATA and GAMA).

·         Experience authoring documentation in structured Adobe Framemaker (SGML, XML).

  • Knowledge of ISODraw software and editing CAD/CATIA illustrations.
  • BS/BA degree or A&P or Pilot’s License.
