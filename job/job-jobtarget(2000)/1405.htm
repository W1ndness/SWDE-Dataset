Description: This is a full-time, 40-hour evening position.  Hours are 4 p.m. - 12:30 a.m. with rotating two out of every three weekends as well as holiday coverage.

Interviews and registers all Emergency Department patients. Obtains demographic
and insurance information. Uses Patcom registration system. Checks and verifies
insurance using NEHENLITE. Advises patients regarding free care and follow up on
motor vehicle accidents and Workers Comp. Works in hectic, fast paced clinical
environment. Produces accurate, detail oriented documentation. Uses strong
interpersonal skills and customer service skills to assist and interact with patients,
and families, as well as clinical staff.



Please see below for full job description:

GENERAL SUMMARY/OVERVIEW STATEMENT

Under the general direction of the Team Leader of ED Registration, the incumbent insures that the ED patient registration and billing information is complete and accurate.  Provides support as needed to all ED staff.  Must be able to function in both central and pod/treatment area environments.  Must have excellent customer service and organizational skills.


PRINCIPAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

Interviews patients entering the Emergency Department for treatment. Orients patients to registration process.  Conducts bedside interviews when appropriate/necessary.

Obtains, verifies and records personal, demographic, financial, visit-specific clinical information.

Must be fully trained in all Hospital registration systems and have familiarity with ED documentation system.

Issues medical records numbers when necessary.

Collects information relevant to ED visits from outside parties as necessary (i.e. family members, transferring facilities, ambulance companies, employers, police and/or correctional departments).

Understands utilization review, Medicare procedures, MGH contractuals, secondary payor rules and Mass. Health and Free Care eligibility.

Verifies insurance plan eligibility using POS devices, EDI software and Web based technologies.

Documents patient's PCP in the ED Registration system.

Obtains patient signature on all appropriate forms.

Conducts quick screen for Mass. Health.  Refers patients who qualify on screen to Patient Financial Services and facilitates contact between the patient and a Financial Services Counselor.

Determines patient financial responsibility as it relates to Emergency Services.  Collects co-payments and deposits against visit charges.  Records transaction via receipt and Emergency Registration logbook.
Reconciles cash collections and makes daily deposits.

Assists nursing staff with the expeditious transfer of patients to appropriate treatment areas.  Keeps triage/nursing staff appraised of any change in waiting patients clinical condition.

Maintains census of patients awaiting treatment area assignment.

Informs the charge person of reportable injuries required by law (i.e. gunshot wounds, stab wounds, animal bites and/or burns).

As outlined, assists in implementation of disaster plans.  Is required to remain on site during any and all disasters.

Must be knowledgeable in all State and Federal Legislation, particularly EMTALA and COBRA as they apply to Emergency services. 

Displays exemplary customer services skills.  Respects patient confidentiality.

Participates in intra- and extra-department project teams as appropriate/assigned.

Provides support to supervisor on miscellaneous projects as appropriate/assigned.

DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES  Specific to Major Ambulatory Muli-Purpose (MAMP) treatment area

Receives patients entering the MAMP and Pedi treatment areas in the Emergency Department.

Collects and logs in patient valuables.

Supplies back-up support to Coordinator in treatment area (i.e. phone, tracking board maintenance, faxing, etc.).

Must be cross-trained in some functions of ED treatment area coordinators.

QUALIFICATIONS

High school diploma required.  Associates/Bachelors Degree and prior health care experience preferred.
Incumbents should possess medical terminology and basic computer skills.

SKILLS/ABILITIES/COMPETENCIES REQUIRED

Incumbents are required to have exceptional customer service skills.
Strong interpersonal and communication skills.
Ability to exercise judgment in dealing with sensitive and confidential information
Ability to handle stressful situations that may arise in facilitating requests.
Individuals with previous medical experience preferred

WORKING CONDITIONS

Registration area of busy emergency room.  Very fast-paced, sometimes chaotic environment.  Exposure to sight of moderately to severely ill and injured patients.
