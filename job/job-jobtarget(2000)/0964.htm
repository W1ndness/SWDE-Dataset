About the Metro Ethernet Forum (MEF)

 

The Metro Ethernet Forum (MEF) is a global industry alliance comprising more than 165 organizations including telecommunications service providers, cable MSOs, network equipment/software manufacturers, semiconductors vendors and testing organizations. The MEF’s mission is to accelerate the worldwide adoption of Carrier-class Ethernet networks and services. The MEF develops Carrier Ethernet technical specifications to promote interoperability and deployment of Carrier Ethernet worldwide. The MEF also conducts extensive marketing programs on a global basis.  

 

Most of the work of the MEF is conducted by individual representatives of member companies that volunteer their time on activities that are synergistic with their company’s business strategy and market activities. Work is conducted through two primary committees, Technical and Marketing.  MEF members meet quarterly, with approximately 200 people attending meetings held twice annually in North America and once each in Europe and Asia-Pacific

 

Work of the Technical Committee occurs through conference calls, email  and  at the noted quarterly meetings. The Marketing Committee is much more divers in terms of activities and geographies. Marketing runs a “Global Program”, developing key MEF positions and content used worldwide. Specific implementation of the programs are decided by the Americas, European and Asia-Pacific marketing teams. Country teams reporting to these regions also exist. Examples of marketing activities include conference participation, public relations activities, membership recruiting and webcasts.

 

 For more information about the Forum, visit the MEF web site at http://www.MetroEthernetForum.org/.

 

 

Position Overview

 

To enable the volunteer work of the MEF, led by the MEF COO, a small team of part and full-time contracted professionals work closely with the members to coordinate and implement key program support functions. Examples include quarterly meeting management, marketing events coordination, content development, website management, press and analyst relations, membership recruiting, retention and administration.

o     Manage contact lists, coordinate and moderates bi-weekly conference calls

·         Membership Recruiting and retention activities; works closely with COO and Regional team to develop new member recruiting strategies including;; sending follow-up materials via email, coordinates member and non-member briefings, tracks and sends out invitations to Quarterly Meetings, maintains member tracking database.  Responsible for undertaking member retention efforts designed to encourage renewals of existing members.
