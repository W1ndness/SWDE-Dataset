Oracle Applications Developer

Harvard University

Cambridge, Massachusetts, United States

This is not an entry-level position.

Job ID: 7413619
Position Title: Oracle Applications Developer
Job Function: Information Technology
Posted: November 29, 2010
Job Type: Full-Time

Duties & Responsibilities:  Harvard University's Office of the University CIO (UCIO) is seeking an Oracle Applications Developer to be part of the Finance and Procurement Systems practice.  This practice is part of the Office of Administrative Systems (OAS) and is responsible for maintaining Harvard University's enterprise-wide applications that support the Finance and Procurement business community campus-wide.

We seek to hire a team-oriented developer with recent implementation and/or support experience in a Release 12 E-Business Suite environment.  The successful candidate must be a developer with deep experience with Oracle's Application Framework (OAF), including both extending and personalizing one or more of Oracle's self-service modules.  In addition, proven working knowledge of BI Publisher is another skill we are seeking in this candidate.

A strong working knowledge of Oracle's E-Business Suite applications is required for this position.  Our current portfolio of modules includes:  AP, GL, AR, iProcurement, Purchasing and Cash Management.  We also develop, support and maintain a number of major custom-developed applications built to integrate with the Oracle Application infrastructure (P-Card, Web Voucher, Chart Request System, Capital Project Approval System).  We also provide technical support for a number of smaller 3rd party applications (Paybase/Bottomline, Glacier, SciQuest integration, Credit Card integrations, InputAcel (Imaging).)

The key responsibilities for this position include the following:

  • Working with other developers on the team to develop, support and maintain customizations and extensions to Harvard's E-Business Suite Applications
  • Working with business analysts to design and develop technical solutions to new business problems
  • Providing level of effort estimates for technical activities to assist with planning efforts
  • Writing technical specifications in response to business requirements
  • Developing and testing technical solutions
  • Prototyping concepts to assist with feasibility analysis
  • Troubles-shooting production issues
  • Participating in day/night on-call support rotation

Other activities include but are not limited to the following:

  • Researching bugs and issues with Oracle Support Analysts
  • Browsing online forums, etc. to find additional information related to issues or products
  • Sharing ideas and experiences with colleagues at other Higher Education institutions
  • Implementing new modules in the future to address the University's business needs. (iExpense, iSupplier, Fixed Assets, Approvals Management, User Management to name a few modules that we are considering.)

Job Requirements

Basic Qualifications:  To be considered for this role, candidates must have the following experience and skills:

A bachelor's degree is required, preferable in computer science.

Applicants must have strong demonstrated programming experience (at least 5 years) in support of Oracle's E-Business Suite.  To be considered you must be able to demonstrate strong proficiency in various aspects of Oracle Applications technology and architecture, with a strong emphasis on:

OAF (Priority)

BI Publisher (Priority)

J-Developer (Priority)

XML

PL/SQL

Java/J2EE

AOL

Unix Shell Scripting

Full Software Development Lifecycle

You will be responsible for streamlining the system's processes, and enhancing its performance.  You will be required to adhere to production support processes and implementation change management procedures.

Additional Qualifications:  In addition to the required skills, the ideal candidate will have these highly desirable experiences and/or skills:

  • Support and maintenance experience with two or more of the E-Biz modules implemented at Harvard (AR, GL, AP, iProcurement, Purchasing, Cash Management)
  • On-the-Job experience with Version 12 of E-Business Suite.
  • HTML
  • Workflow
  • BPEL
  • Open Interfaces and Oracle API's
  • Oracle Developer tools 10g (nice to have)
  • PVCS or another Source Code Control utility
  • Eclipse (nice to have)
  • Oracle Applications Server

Web Logic / Apache

Additional Information:  Please note:  Harvard University requires pre-employment reference and background screening.  The Office of the University CIO is unable to provide work authorization and/or visa sponsorship.   This position has a 180-day probationary period.
