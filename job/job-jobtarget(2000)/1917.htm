Clinical Systems Analyst - Upstate South Carolina 

McKesson’s Computerized Physician Order Entry (HEO, HOM)

Provide expertise and support in the implementation of Horizon Expert Orders (HEO) software.  Services may include application build, process analysis, process design, fit gap analysis, interaction with vendor support services, report development, and progress reporting to management and stakeholders. Ensure customer is properly educated, trained and advised on the clinical aspects of our products and capable of implementing the health care solution within their organizational framework. 

Position Summary:  Plans, conducts, and directs the analysis of clinical healthcare business problems to be solved via automated systems and/or business processes.  Responsible for designing, developing, modifying, evaluating, and supporting programs, applications software, and/or services for clients.  Partners with clients and/or business users to identify, evaluate, and develop products, procedures, and/or systems which are cost effective and meet business needs. 

 

Desired Skills:  Solid applicants shall have 2+ years recent build experience with McKesson’s HEO, HED, HHS and/or HOM applications and 4+ years experience in clinical/healthcare field, computer applications, or business analysis.  You must have documented experience with software implementation, end user training,  and XML, HTML design coding.  iForm (Standard and VGR) experience is a plus.  Describe your organizational and communication skills related to IT projects.

-    Education:  A bachelor’s degree in information technology or a healthcare related field is strongly preferred.  Equivalent experience in software implementation and support, particularly as described above, will be considered in lieu of the formal education requirements.

Full time, great compensation and a sign on/relo option is available.  Founded in 1951, Self Regional Healthcare is a regional healthcare center serving the upstate Lakelands region of South Carolina.  Recognized the past 3 years with the Gallup Great Workplace Award, singling the hospital out as one of the 23 best workplaces in the entire world.  The hospital's mission is to improve health by bringing advanced care closer to home for the more than 250,000 living in its service area. Licensed for 414 beds, the hospital employs 2,400 health professionals and has a medical staff of more than 200 representing almost every major medical specialty.  Only qualified applicants will be considered.  Please alpply at www.selfregional.org.

-    Education:  A bachelor’s degree in information technology or a healthcare related field is strongly preferred.  Equivalent experience in software implementation and support, particularly as described above, will be considered in lieu of the formal education requirements.
