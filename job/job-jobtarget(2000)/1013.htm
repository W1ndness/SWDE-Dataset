About the Department:
The HIV Vaccine Trials Network (HVTN) is part of the Vaccine and Infectious Disease Division (VIDD) at Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Center. HVTN is an international collaboration of scientists and educators searching for an effective and safe HIV vaccine. The HVTN's mission is to facilitate the process of testing preventive vaccines against HIV/AIDS. Our organization conducts all phases of clinical trials, from evaluating experimental vaccines for safety and the ability to stimulate immune responses, to testing vaccine efficacy.

About Us:
Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Center, home of three Nobel laureates, is an independent, nonprofit research institution dedicated to the development and advancement of biomedical research to eliminate cancer and other potentially fatal diseases. Recognized internationally for its pioneering work in bone-marrow transplantation, the Centers five scientific divisions collaborate to form a unique environment for conducting basic and applied science. The Hutchinson Center, in collaboration with its clinical and research partners, the University of Washington and Seattle Childrens, is the only National Cancer Institute-designated comprehensive cancer center in the Pacific Northwest. Join us and make a difference!

Qualifications:
Bachelors degree in related field.  Excellent written and verbal communication skills.  .  Ability to adjust communication and working styles to effectively interact with outside organizations, internal operational staff, scientists, and site staff. Strong organizational skills.  Ability to think critically, work independently, and act as a team member.  Ability to multi-task in a fast-paced environment.  Proven attention to detail, Extensive experience with Access Database (minimum 1 year).  Extensive experience and knowledge of Microsoft Word, Excel, and Power Point (minimum 3 years).  Three years of progressively complex administrative project coordination experience.  Experience with training/education and interest in Public Health or Clinical Research preferred.  Experience with webconference software helpful.

Major Duties:
Provide administrative support to the Associate Director of Training (10%)
Coordinate administrative activities, ensuring all projects are completed according to project timelines.
Assist in the development of program materials which may include questionnaires, data collection instruments, and training/educational materials. Develop, review, edit, and revise administrative materials.
Carry-out moderately complex research assignments in support of various projects
Serve as Training Unit liaison, representing the Unit/projects to other Center departments, funding sources, affiliated individuals or institutions, and outside organizations. 
Track program data and compile data reports.
Assist in the analysis of project data. 
Conduct literature searches, seeking and recording information relevant and helpful to the project/program.
Coordinate logistics and evaluation of HVTN training activities held in the US and internationally (i.e., blended-learning workshops, face-to-face sessions, web conferences).
Work with the HVTN Travel Department and others to coordinate and assure that all arrangements are made for trainers and trainees. Communicate with venue staff. Coordinate the production, printing, and distribution of training guidelines and materials.
Coordinate translation of training materials into multiple languages.
Establish and maintain relationships with partner organizations providing training opportunities. Assist with course registration, and coordinate participation of HVTN staff.
Assist with the creation and monitoring of consulting agreements with external vendors
Represent the Training Unit as a member of various committees and working groups.
Maintain the HVTN training database. Utilize the database to document activities, create training certificates and produce reports.
Maintain filing system for training records
Compile training evaluation data.
Produce special and recurring reports.
Communicate program information
Prepare minutes for committee and working group calls and meetings.
Develop systems to increase efficiency and standardize training program activities
Conduct specific research tasks  as required
Assist with budget development, grant writing and producing reports to help secure funding.
Facilitate or co-facilitate select trainings
Maintain Training Program calendar
Assist with special projects.
Perform other duties as required.

Responsibilities:
Coordinates research and administrative activities in support of the HIV Vaccine Trials Network (HVTN) Training Program, developing procedures, and administrative materials, carrying-out moderately complex assignments and assisting in project implementation.

The incumbent will work under the direction of the HVTN Associate Director of Training, referring project issues on and/or soliciting guidance as necessary. Special skills and knowledge are applied in coordinating training projects and administrative activities and in carrying-out moderately complex assignments. May coordinate the work of other project/study staff.
