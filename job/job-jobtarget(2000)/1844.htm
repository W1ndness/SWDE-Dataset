Managing/Copy Editor

Journal of the American Planning Association

University of California, Los Angeles

 

Description and Contract Terms

The Journal of the American Planning Association (JAPA) is the scholarly journal of the American Planning Association (APA). The JAPA office, a two-person team comprised of the editor and the managing editor, is funded by APA and UCLA. 

This is an annual, full time contract position, paid monthly.  This is not a regular university position and there are no benefits outside the agreed upon monthly contract fee. Telecommuting within the continental USA is feasible.  If telecommuting, the contractor must provide their own computer, software, internet access, telephone service, and other incidental costs of carrying out these responsibilities.  Office space may be available on the UCLA campus.  The position is available immediately.

The initial payment for services will depend on skills and experience, where the higher end requires substantial copy-editing experience.  (We may consider managing editor applicants lacking copy-editing experience at the lower fee range, and then contract for copy-editing in a separate position within this budget.)

Please email a cover letter and resume, including contact info for references, to jobs.japa@gmail.com.  No phone calls or faxes unless requested.  

 

Education/Experience Requirements

The ideal candidate has graduate training in social science methods, a minimum of two years managing a scholarly journal, and experience copy-editing research articles in the social sciences and design fields.  Experience producing a scholarly journal and copy-editing are the highest priorities.

Requirements include ability as a team member to perform administrative and editorial work in conformance with professional editing standards; ability to manage daily office operations in the production of the journal; ability to use the computer (appropriate word processing, spreadsheet, data base, graphics, and communications software) to prepare correspondence, monitor the office budget, and maintain a data base of authors, referees, and editorial board members. Intermediate to advanced skill with MS Word, database, and internet software is strongly preferred.

We may use a comprehensive web-based software package to handle at least some of the flow of manuscripts, reviews, and correspondence, so comfort with new technologies is essential.  However, the new managing/copy editor will be trained in the Taylor & Francis options.  Telecommuting is acceptable, though location in the southern California metropolitan area for occasional meetings is preferred.  (No relocation or commuting expenses are covered.)

Duties and Responsibilities

1.              Report to the JAPA editor; 

2.              Coordinate communication among the editor and associate editors and 1) authors who submit articles for publication in the journal; 2) scholars who serve as anonymous referees of articles submitted for publication; 3) the editorial board; and 4) the publisher and journal production operations, Taylor & Francis;

3.              Oversee manuscript review process. Supervise the process for submission and review of manuscripts to be considered for publication, including adherence to deadlines and communication of decisions to authors. Supervise the maintenance of a comprehensive database and office filing system (of both paper and digital files) for manuscripts and general editorial communication with authors and referees. Support the Editor as needed in making decisions about manuscripts;

4.              Manage the review of 100 to 200 submitted articles per year, of which 24-30 are published upon successful review;

5.              Oversee the publication of accepted manuscripts and book reviews. Copyedit manuscripts and book reviews and prepare manuscript graphics for publication. Copyedit and coordinate the inclusion of various special materials in each issue. Receive advertisements from APA staff to be included in each issue and prepare them for publication. Coordinate production schedule with publisher. Check proofs produced by the typesetter and printer for accuracy. Have authors and review editors check proofs prior to printing;

6.              Check galley and page proofs prepared by the compositor for accuracy;

7.              Facilitate the selection and processing of cover art;

8.              Prepare monthly reports for the editor and bi-annual reports for the editorial board, which meets twice yearly at the Association of Collegiate Schools of Planning (ACSP) and APA conferences.  Reasonable travel expenses covered to attend these.

9.              Manage the JAPA virtual office. Monitor the office budget. Coordinate semiannual meetings of the editorial board, including preparing agendas, scheduling meeting rooms, and writing minutes. Be available to travel in October and April for two annual meetings.  Work closely with the JAPA book review editor, APA director of publications regarding various issues concerning the journal, and with Taylor & Francis staff. Answer queries/requests from authors, referees, APA staff, Taylor & Francis staff, readers, and others as appropriate;

10.           Ensure that each of the four-issue volumes are completed and delivered to the printer according to schedule.

Find JAPA at http://www.tandf.co.uk/journals/rjpa.
