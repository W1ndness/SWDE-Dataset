RESPONSIBILITIES

This is an important administrative and professional accounting position with many multi-program responsibilities and a high level of accountability.  

Work includes but is not limited to:

·          Compliance to asset management and Public Housing financial requirements of Dept of Housing and Urban Development and Section 42 IRS code requirements related to tax credits

·          Maintenance and detailed review of fiscal records; preparation of financial statements, internal and external financial reports and budgets

·          Application of professional accounting principles and methods to a wide variety of accounting problems and transactions

·          Excellent analytical skills required to prepare financial analyses in several areas

·          Frequently works with auditors and assists in formulation and implementation of improved systems, policies, procedures and regulations involved with fiscal control

Continuous education is required to ensure appropriate knowledge to remain in compliance with all federal, state and local financial requirements that may apply.  In addition, work includes frequent supervision of work performed by professional, para-professional, and clerical accounting employees.  Independent judgment is required of daily tasks; while general guidance is provided on special projects or new assignments.  Subject to frequent deadlines, work is submitted to the Controller upon completion for review and is subject to an independent audit.

Benefits:       Company contribution to retirement plan, health, dental, and disability insurance coverage, company provided life insurance with the option to purchase additional, paid time off

To apply:      All applicants must submit an official KCDC employment application forms, resume, professional references, and salary history along with college transcripts to the Human Resources Office at 901 N. Broadway, Knoxville, TN 37917, Fax: 865-594-8743, or via e-mail to humanresources@kcdc.org by November 29, 2010.  Visit www.kcdc.org for more details.

 


Job Requirements

MINIMUM REQUIREMENTS

Must possess a Bachelor’s degree from an accredited four-year college or university with major work in accounting, finance, or a related field or equivalent combination of education and experience, at least 5 years, that would provide considerable experience in GAAP, governmental accounting and financial reporting, federal, state, and local laws, rules and regulations.

Must possess strong organizational and problem solving skills, and a strong knowledge of automated accounting systems including word processing/ Word, Excel and the Internet.

Must possess experience in implementation of good internal control procedures, good time management skills and the ability to work with multiple priorities and deadlines.

Must demonstrate good supervision principles and practices.

Applicants with financial experience working with tax credit projects will be given preference.  In addition, preference may be given to applicants with knowledge of HUD financial requirements/regulations, capital fund grants, and fixed assets and depreciation.  Working knowledge of EmPHAsys and/or Elite software programs or other housing software also a plus.
