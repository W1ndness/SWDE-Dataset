SUMMARY

 

This position is responsible for all aspects of installation and maintenance of equipment within a cable television headend. Required to optimize incoming signals from wide variety of sources and process for redistribution to cable systems. Reception and distribution mechanisms include; satellite, off-air antenna, microwave, coaxial cable, fiber. Required to work on-call.

ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES include the following. Other duties may be assigned. 

  1. Work closely with field and office employees in determining source of signal impairments and facilitates repair of problems as they are located.
  2. Install and align satellite dishes. Includes fine tuning of feedhorns and replacement of low noise block converters. May include multi-feed assemblies.
  3. Install, replace, and align various types of off-air antennas for VHF and UHF television and radio reception.Maintain and document video carrier levels, audio video ratio, depth of modulation, audio deviation.
  4. Able to measure and set video levels with waveform monitor.
  5. Utilize spectrum analyzer for variety of measurements including: satellite alignment, RF levels, carrier to noise, low frequency disturbances, composite triple beat, composite and discrete second order beat, depth of modulation, audio deviation.
  6. Proficient in setup and operation of basic headend equipment including but not limited to: signal processors, modulators, demodulators, satellite receivers, integrated receiver descramblers, digital receivers, transcoders, combining and splitting networks, laser transmitters, fiber optic receivers.
  7. Familiar with setup and operation of cable modem termination systems, cable modems, and basic networking equipment.
  8. Able to work closely with network engineers to facilitate proper operation, maintenance, and restoration of high speed data network.
  9. Responsible for headend cooling requirements. Coordinates with air-conditioning specialists to maintain proper system operation.
  10. Install, maintain, and test emergency alert systems.
  11. Test and maintain backup powering systems- including generators, uninterruptible power supplies, temporary feeds.
  12. Responsible for insuring the proper tower and site maintenance schedule is followed.
  13. Responsible for insuring the proper OTN cabinet maintenance schedule is followed.
  14. Familiar with optical splice patch panels, optical couplers, and appropriate optical connector handling and cleaning procedures.
  15. Utilize optical time domain reflectometer to perform splice acceptance tests as well as fault location.
  16. Proficient with software programs for storage and archival of test equipment results.
  17. Able to maintain, and troubleshoot terrestrial microwave systems.
  18. Compliance with system operating specifications as well as applicable governmental regulations.
  19. Assist in design and planning of headend facilities.
  20. Maintain accurate and complete headend documentation.
  21. Responsible for maintaining the headend in a clean and orderly manner.
