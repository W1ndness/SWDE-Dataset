Ambulatory Applications Manager

Salem Hospital

Salem, Oregon, United States

Position Title: Ambulatory Applications Manager
Min Education: BA/BS/Undergraduate
Industry: Hospital, Multi-Hospital System, Integrated Delivery
Posted: November 29, 2010

Job Type: Full-Time
Apply URL: http://salemhospital.contacthr.com/17885989
 

Salem Health is a not-for-profit regional health system in Oregon’s mid-Willamette Valley that includes Salem Hospital, along with West Valley Hospital, a critical-access hospital; Willamette Health Partners (medical group comprising primary-care and specialty practices), and other affiliated healthcare organizations. Salem Hospital, a Magnet®-recognized facility, is a regional referral center and one of the largest acute-care hospitals in Oregon.   Our hospital is licensed for 454 acute care beds, and is guided by a 15-member volunteer Board of Trustees. The hospital is the city of Salem's largest private employer, with approximately 3,500 full and part-time employees.

 

Salem Hospital is seeking a dynamic, experienced, team-oriented and quality focused information services professional to assume the role of Ambulatory Applications Manager. This position reports to the Director of Information Services, and is responsible for supervising, directing and evaluating all activities supporting Salem Health ambulatory practice initiatives. 

 

General Responsibilities:

  • Assists in defining measurable unit objectives consistent with organizational objectives.
  • Fosters staff participation in assessing, planning, implementing and evaluating department activities and goals.
  • Develops and monitors annual budgets which provide sufficient, qualified staff to meet department and hospital objectives and ensure the availability of needed materials, supplies and equipment.
  • Staff management responsibilities:  Provide ongoing coaching and feedback.  Confront behaviors that violate values or standards.  Acknowledge and celebrate excellent performance.  Provide timely and thorough performance reviews.  Ensure that employees are informed about department and health system goals, strategies and action plans.  Create opportunities for employees to develop their skills and abilities.
  • Understand, interpret and consistently apply the organization’s policies and procedures.
  • Use performance improvement tools and processes to improve quality.
  • Work collaboratively across the organization -- consider the impact of decisions and actions on other departments.
  • Continue own education to strengthen knowledge and meet current and future goals.
  • Develop and manage an effective plan for the future direction of areas of responsibility.
  • Understand regulatory issues and ensure compliance.
  • Understand the changing governmental push for electronic health records and interoperability.
  • Works with managers, staff, and physicians to assess information needs and expectations.
  • Assess physician and patient information needs in collaboration with the Chief Medical Information Officer and Willamette Health Partners Leadership.
  • Assesses available computerized clinical and other information systems for ability to meet the needs of clinical staff, financial staff, and physicians.
  • Collaborates to integrate projects into house-wide IS plan.
  • Develops work plan for implementation of computerized clinical and financial applications, in an ambulatory setting.
  • Monitors progress of implementation and communicate progress to project stakeholders.
  • Assesses systems for impact on clinical practice and looks for opportunities to enhance practice or use newly available tools.
  • Recommends and oversees modifications to system to rectify problems identified.

 

 

Additional Information:

In order to be considered for this position, your resume must reflect all relevant licenses, certifications, education, and experience noted in the requirements and minimum qualification sections of this job posting. Without this information, candidates cannot be considered for positions to which they may be qualified.

  

Upon date of hire, applicants will be required to complete the U.S. Department Homeland Security’s I-9 form confirming authorization to work in the United States. Salem Hospital is not able to support VISA sponsorships.


Job Requirements

Minimum Qualifications:

  • Undergraduate degree, preferably in Information Technology, Health Systems or Management, required.
  • Minimum of five (5) years of information systems experience, preferably in a health care environment, required.
  • Minimum of three (3) years experience in Ambulatory Applications with at least two (2) years of supervisory, team lead or project lead experience required.
  • Working knowledge of Epic or similar HIS software vendors, specific to Physician Practice Management applications required.
  • Knowledge of ambulatory clinic practices from both Clinical and Practice Management perspectives required.

Working Conditions:

Must be capable of light physical activity.

 


Special Skills, Knowledge, or Ability:

  • Knowledge and application of principles of administration, supervision and management.
  • Above average communication and human relations skills; ability to relate well with others.
  • Extensive knowledge of information systems structures, support models and practices to ensure highly reliable systems.
  • Knowledge and experience with Project Management principles.
  • Strong leadership, organizational and communication skills.

Salem Health is seeking candidates who demonstrate the following strengths, as defined by the Gallup Organization.

Core Strengths:

  • Service: A drive to be of service to others.
  • Ethics: A clear understanding of right and wrong which guides your actions.
  • Responsibility: A need to assume personal accountability for your work.
  • Problem Solving: An ability to think things though with incomplete data.
  • Empathy: An ability to identify the feelings and perspectives of others.
  • Team: A need to build feelings of mutual support.

 

Leadership Strengths:

  • Achiever: A drive that is internal, constant and self-imposed.
  • Vision: A drive to paint value-based word pictures about the future.
  • Performance Orientation: A need to be objective and measure performance.
  • Strategic Thinking: An ability to play out alternative scenarios in the future.
  • Interpersonal: An ability to purposely capitalize upon relationships.
  • Activator: An impatience to move others into action.
