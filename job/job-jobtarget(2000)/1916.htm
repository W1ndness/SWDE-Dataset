The National Center for Computational Sciences (NCCS) in the Computing and Computational Sciences Directorate at Oak Ridge National Laboratory is seeking a highly qualified individual to play a key role in the administration and support of a massive parallel distributed file system, along with the accompanying storage subsystems and underlying Infiniband and Fiber-channel based infrastructure. The ideal candidate will perform complex analysis tasks, testing, and troubleshooting of current systems, as well as contribute to the design,installation, and management of future file system and SAN-based storage resources. Major Duties/Responsibilities Duties of the position include, but are not limited to: -Installing, configuring, and maintaining computer system hardware and software -Monitoring of systems and storage performance, up to and including network components -Diagnosing system operational problems quickly and effectively -Coordinating with vendors to resolve hardware and software problems -Porting and writing system management tools -Building and Deploying open source software and software from vendors/partners -Providing reliable and efficient backups/restores for all managed systems -Setting up and maintaining host and network based security for all managed systems -Documenting system administration procedures for routine and complex tasks -Participating in a 24-hour, 7-day on-call support rotation and off-hours maintenance windows -The position is also responsible for system implementation/integration into the NCCS environment and systems performance analysis.

For more information about computing at ORNL, see: http://computing.ornl.gov/
and http://nccs.gov/


Job Requirements

Qualifications Required
The successful candidate will have a B.S. in Computer Science or closely
related field (or an equivalent combination of training and experience) and a
strong working knowledge of UNIX and Storage system fundamentals.
-Minimum of 3 years experience managing Unix/Linux operating systems in a large
scale system environment
-Experience configuring, administering, and supporting storage
subsystems (EMC, NetApp, DataDirect, IBM, LSI, etc.)
Knowledge of basic SAN administration concepts (zoning, multipathing, fabric
topologies, etc.)
-Ability to develop and maintain programs and scripts that aid in the operation
and automation of administrative tasks using various shell and scripting
languages (bash, Perl, Python)
-Solid understanding of networked computing environment concepts

Preferred Qualifications
-Experience with one or more distributed file systems (Lustre, GPFS, GFS,
IBRIX, etc.)
-Experience installing and maintaining diskless clustered environments, and
provisioning systems using automated installation methods (like kickstart)
-Direct experience working with Infiniband (must at least be able to
demonstrate a working knowledge of Infiniband concepts)
-Strong interpersonal and communication skills
-Ability to work as a team player
-Ability to plan, organize, prioritize tasks, and complete assigned projects
with minimal supervision

Special requirements:

It is required of this position the ability to obtain and maintain a clearance from the Department of Energy. As such, this position is a Workplace Substance Abuse program (WSAP) testing designed position which requires passing a pre-placement drug test and participation in an ongoing random drug testing program in which employees are subject to being randomly selected for testing. The occupant of this position will also be subject to an ongoing requirement to report to ORNL any drug-related arrest or conviction or receipt of a positive drug test result.
