Global Product Manager
St Paul, MN


The Global Product Manager integrates the functional organization in the development of the market requirements for specific portfolio of products and services in accordance with the Company's strategic plans and objectives.

Specific responsibilities include, but are not limited to:

Marketing lead for all New Product Introduction (NPI) activities

  • Identification, evaluation and recommendations for market opportunities for specific product and services within assigned product portfolio.
  • Marketing lead to coordinate with Center of Excellence (COE) functions
  • Global Launch Strategy Development
  • Product Claims and Product Positioning Development
  • Customer Requirements Documentation
  • Customer Input and Validation



Marketing lead for the development of the World Wide Product Plans and Business Unit Strategic Plans

  • Business Performance Reporting and Analysis
  • Market Trends and Drivers
  • Global Revenue and Margin
  • Global Forecasting



Marketing lead for Product Portfolio Stewardship

  • Lifecycle Management
  • Market Surveillance
  • Cost Reduction Initiatives
  • SKU Management

    Perform global voice-of-the-customer and voice-of-the-market
    Define and deliver customer requirement specifications product requirements documents with prioritized features, prioritized global markets and corresponding justification
    Work with external third parties to assess partnerships and licensing opportunities
    Work with downstream/outbound marketing and provide input into go-to-market plan (i.e., positioning, messaging, sales agreements, forecast, sales tools, collateral, etc.)

Job Requirements

Qualifications include:

  • Bachelor's degree in Business Administration, Marketing, or technical discipline such as Computer Science or Computer Engineering (MBA preferred)
  • Clinical background a plus (RN, BSN, or other clinical degree/education)
  • Seven years product management in the health care industry and at least three years experience managing projects and budgets.
  • Demonstrated success defining and launching excellent software or combination software/hardware products
  • Technical background, with experience in software, hardware, or computer systems, specifically hospital or healthcare information systems
  • Experience developing or managing health information technology systems (e.g., CPOE, EMR/EHR, eMAR, etc.)
  • Strong interpersonal, verbal and written communication skills.
  • Ability to work on extremely complex problems where analysis of situations or data requires interpretation
  • Ability to interface at a technical level with engineering team
  • Excellent teamwork skills with the proven ability to influence cross-functional teams
  • Must be able to travel 50percent of the time
