The Position

 Seattle Public Utilities is seeking a senior level water quality analyst to perform the more advanced water analysis and testing in order to ensure that the results meet state and federal drinking water requirements.  This position reports to the Chemistry and Limnology Supervisor in the Laboratory Services Division. 

 Job Responsibilities

·         Perform mandated chemistry testing of drinking water

·         Calibrate and maintain instrumentation and troubleshoot, as well as make minor equipment repairs

·         Analyze and report routine and regulatory samples

·         Understand how chemical processes are affected in the water distribution system and make recommendations concerning that information

·         Review data for reasonableness and quality control acceptance

·         Peer review other analysts data and cross-train other analysts

·         Provide technical assistance to other staff in fields of expertise

·         Plan and coordinate work in the section for routine and special projects

·         Clearly communicate orally and in writing about technical aspects of water quality issues to technical and non-technical staff within and without the utility

·         Familiar with a wide variety of computer software such as LIMS, MS Office, and various other packages

·         Perform the more technically complicated tests and act as a lead analyst.

 


Job Requirements

Required Qualifications

 Education:  Bachelor's degree in Microbiology, Chemistry, Chemical Engineering or a related field

Experience:

·         Three years experience in the chemical or microbiological analysis of water, or

·         Three years experience in the limnological monitoring of lakes and streams related to drinking water supplies

Equivalencies:  Combination of education and/or training and/or experience which provides an equivalent background required to perform the work of the class
 

Licensing Requirements:  Current State of Washington driver’s license or evidence of equivalent mobility

 

Desired Qualifications:

·         Five years experience in a laboratory setting analyzing drinking water

·         Experience working with a variety of testing techniques such as:  Atomic Absorption Spectroscopy, Gas Chromatography, Gas Chromatography/Mass Spectrometry, Segmented Flow Analysis and/or Inductively Coupled Mass Spectrometry

·         Knowledge of drinking water regulations and maximum contaminant levels (MCLs)

·         Understanding of online water analyzers

·         Ability to develop new methods for analysis and reporting

·         Ability to train and mentor analysts

·         Ability to work within the organizational framework of SPU Workplace Expectations including our values on Safety, Respect, Diversity, Trust, Humor, Productivity, Integrity, Innovation, Partnership and Accountability

·         Ability to communicate and work effectively within a multicultural workforce serving a diverse customer base.
