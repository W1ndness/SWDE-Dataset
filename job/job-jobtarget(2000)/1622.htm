Information Assurance Analyst 3 (FISMA & HIPAA) Compliance Controls Specialist
 
Job Description
POSITION OBJECTIVE
Working under general direction and with a high degree of independence, provide technical and process security leadership for the university through the implementation and maintenance of a program for Federal Information Security Management Act (FISMA) compliance for the National Children's Study within the Case School of Medicine, and a similar information security controls program to assure Health Information Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) compliance. The person in this role supports the National Children's Study at 50 percent of the effort.

ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS
1.  Exercise programmatic responsibility for the two major compliance activities (FISMA and HIPAA) in support of research data activities at Case Western Reserve University, primarily within the School of Medicine.
2.  Ensure, through mentoring and coaching of IT staff, researchers, and faculty, all security and confidentiality requirements are understood by the customers.
3.  Lead systems and research program development of data management processes that address security and confidentiality compliance requirements. Develop and implement action plans to effectively mitigate top risks.
4.  Manage information security controls and policies for the customer organizations.
5.  Direct and support the National Children's Study FISMA Compliance planning and implementation of IT security controls, business operations, and local system defenses against risks such as data breaches and cyber intrusions.
6.  Perform auditing of existing security controls. Perform or provide regular risk assessment activities for FISMA certification and accreditation (C&A) package delivery and submission.
7.  Direct the administration of security policies, activities, and compliance to FISMA control standards.
8.  Assist the customer in completing, maintaining, and updating certification and accreditation packages.
9.  Take responsibility for independent Contingency Plan testing, security control testing and conducting evaluations of C&A packages according to the customer C&A package checklist.
10.  Coordinate implementation of enterprise security controls and training activities with Case ITS.
11.  Work with subcontractors as needed to assist in their FISMA compliance as it relates to their work on the National Children's Study.
12.  Coordinate appropriate security HIPAA compliance activities to maintain privacy and confidentiality of ePHI used for research purposes within the scope of the university, but primarily focused on activities within the School of Medicine.
13.  Ensure implementation, management, and enforcement of information security policies and directives as mandated by HIPAA, as appropriate for the university research context.
14.  Develop long term data protection strategies for research data from human subjects research.
15.  Develop Institutional Review Board security protocol standards for patient privacy and data security.
16.  Lead incident response activities to contain, investigate, prevent future information security breaches with respect to patient health information.
17.  Advise and consult with leadership and researchers in HIPAA standards of practice, business relationships with clinical facilities.
18.  Define requirements and develop solutions for healthcare and research-based security functions.
19.  Adhere to Administrative Applications standards, policies and procedures for information security controls.
20.  Some public speaking involved in security and HIPAA education and awareness activities.
21.  This position, like all Information Assurance personnel, is required to be available to respond to emergency security issues and incidents on a 24/7/365 basis.

NONESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS
Perform other duties and responsibilities as assigned.

CONTACTS
Department: Daily contact with the National Children’s Study principal investigators and research staff, School of Medicine IT staff. Daily contact with Information Security Office staff.
University: Daily contact with IT staff from IT Services. Contact with IT systems administrators, staff, and faculty. Monthly contact with IT leadership.
External: Frequent contact with subcontractors working with Case Western Reserve University on the National Children’s Study. Occasional contact with staff and clinical faculty from University Hospitals, Metro Health System, Cleveland Clinic Foundation, and Louis Stokes Veterans Administration Medical Center.
Students: Occasional contact with medical school students in IT support and incident response roles

SUPERVISORY RESPONSIBILITY
This position has no direct supervision of staff employees, but has organization wide compliance responsibility.

QUALIFICATIONS
Experience: 8 years progressive work experience required, at least 5 years in the information security field, with a concentration on Certification &Accreditation as it applies to FISMA and/or HIPAA. 2 of these years must be in a lead security design or technical task/program leadership position. Past supervisory experience will prove essential to address needs of various high-profile research groups. Additional desired fields of experience include information security, healthcare information technology, computer science, computer engineering, software engineering, chemistry, mathematics, mechanical engineering, statistical analysis, operations research, life sciences, or allied health sciences.
Education/Licensing: Bachelor's degree required, preferably in an analytical field with computer-related experience. Security certification required, such as SANS GSEC, CISSP, HISP, or equivalent DoD certification.

REQUIRED SKILLS
1.  Must possess demonstrated experience in all phases of preparing and reviewing complete certification and accreditation (C&A) packages for information technology systems and/or applications as defined by the Federal Information Security Management Act of 2002 (FISMA) and implemented by the guidance of the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST).
2.  Must possess a strong background with NIST, in particular security controls based on NIST SP-800-53.
3.  Must possess direct experience in HIPAA program security controls implementation.
4.  Good analytical skills to support IT security and information assurance functions.
5.  Be able to effectively manage multiple tasks simultaneously; coordinating resources and ensuring scheduled goals are met.
6.  Demonstrated ability to support customers with care and diligence, work to resolve root cause of problems.
7.  Strong organizational skills required. Ability to analyze and troubleshoot issues in a timely manner.
8.  Strong interpersonal skills with an emphasis on oral and written communication.
9.  Good knowledge of threat and vulnerability to identify solutions to security issues.
10.  Ability to exercise meticulous care in the maintenance, control and accessibility of sensitive information.
11.  Must be able to successfully pass a criminal background check on an annual basis.
12.  Basic network management skills (TCP/IP) at the host level. (required)
13.  Prefer past supervisory experience will prove essential to address needs of various high-profile research groups.
14.  Prefer the ability to conduct effective vulnerability assessments of networked and stand-alone information systems to the extent of conclusively validating all technical controls found within NIST SP 800-53.

WORKING CONDITIONS
General office environment supports mobile workforce. Limited manual dexterity required. Work requires moderate physical exertion (walking to various areas of campus). Little or no exposure to hazards.
