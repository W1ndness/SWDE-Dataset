Plans, administers and coordinates the programs for the purchase, receiving, storage and distribution of materials including capital equipment that will insure the maintenance and/or improvement of quality patient care at the lowest possible cost.  Develops policies and procedures and recommends operating and capital budgets.   All duties are performed in a manner consistent with the standards and values of KGH.

 

POSITION REQUIREMENTS

 

1.     Education:                Bachelor’s Degree preferred with an emphasis in business, purchasing, merchandising, accounting or related field.  Related experience may be substituted for formal education.

 

2.     Certification:             N/A

 

3.     Experience:               Five or more years medical equipment and supplies purchasing experience preferred.  Supervisory experience is preferred.  Must have numerical ability and ability to converse and understand technical terminology.  Lawson Software experience highly desirable.

 

4.     Interpersonal:            Must have the ability to work efficiently and responsibly in an environment with multiple work demands and short time frames.  Must have the ability to effectively communicate orally and in writing with staff, department managers, other hospital personnel, volunteers, vendors and other clientele to obtain the correct products at the most cost-effective price

 

5.     Physical:                   Must have ability to: tolerate long periods of sitting, standing or walking; read computer screen text; communicate verbally with individuals in person or on the telephone; read and write text, solve complex problems; respond quickly to department or hospital emergency.  Some lifting of supplies and equipment up to 50 pounds is required.

 

 

ESSENTIAL DUTIES

 

·        Understands, supports and promotes the organizational values inherent in the philosophy of KGH.

 

·        Performs job duties in a manner that demonstrates the values inherent in KGH.

 

·        Cooperates with other departments to accomplish department/hospital objectives.

 

·        Maintains department records, policies and job descriptions according to department procedures.

 

·        Operates the department within approved expense and capital budgets and within productivity measures.

 

·        Establishes goals and objectives for the department.

 

·        Operates department in compliance with The Joint Commission (TJC) and/or other regulatory agency requirements.

 

·        Provides for and administers an effective department safety program.

 

·        Ensures adequate department equipment, materials and supplies are available.

 

·        Maintains effective communication with department employees.

 

·        Maintains managerial competence.

 

·        Complies with KGH Human Resource policies and key procedures.

 

·        Evaluates employee performance and recommends salary changes.

 

·        Supervises orientation of new employees.

 

·        Directs the purchasing and receiving functions for the hospital in a manner consistent with the hospital and department policies and procedures. Oversees the negotiations and authorizes all agreements and contracts for the acquisitions of materials. Good vendor relationships are maintained at all times to assure prompt attention, up-to-date data on new products and equipment and quality workmanship. Serves as a liaison between hospital personnel and supplier to procure specifically what is needed in a cost-effective manner.

 

·        Serves on key hospital committees for the review of material resources with the purpose of creating a cost-effective environment. Acts as advisor to administration, department managers and staff on issues concerning purchasing, contracts, inventory control and other materials related items.

 

·        Directs the operations of printing services to ensure timely and cost-effective production or outside acquisition of all hospital requirements for printed matter.

 

·        Directs Central Stores and distribution functions to ensure proper inventory control and timely delivery of basic supplies.

 

·        Protects confidentiality of patients, employees, and hospital.

 

Please note: The applicant who is offered this position will need to pass a physical capacity post offer screening test.
