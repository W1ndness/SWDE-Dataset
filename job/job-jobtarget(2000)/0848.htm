Boston College is a leading national Jesuit, Catholic university, enrolling 9,000 undergraduates and close to 5,000 graduate and professional students. Located six miles from downtown Boston, the University has approximately 680 full-time faculty and 2,300 full-time employees, an operating budget of $767 million, and an endowment of $1.5 billion.

Founded in 1863, Boston College is one of the oldest Jesuit, Catholic universities in the United States. U.S. News and World Report rank it 34th among national universities. Boston College has experienced tremendous growth in recent years, including a 75 percent increase in undergraduate applications over the past decade. During the same period, a remarkable increase in revenue from voluntary giving has helped to move the University's endowment to approximately $1.4 billion, among the 40 largest in the nation. For more information about Boston College, its people and programs, visit www.bc.edu.

The University is currently engaged in the most ambitious fundraising campaign in the history of Jesuit, Catholic education-in terms of both its financial goal and the impact that support will have on its future. The Light the World campaign goal is $1.5 billion and is scheduled to conclude in 2015. As part of the 150th Anniversary campaign for Boston College the University recently announced the Alumni Participation Challenge, a multi-year effort that will raise Boston College to an elite level of alumni loyalty.

Boston College seeks an experienced, senior professional for the position of Senior Principal Giving Advisor.  The Senior Principal Giving Advisor will personally manage a portfolio of principal gift prospects, working to maximize their philanthropic commitments to the University through effective cultivation, solicitation, and stewardship strategies.  The expectation is that this individual's work will result in gifts at the highest levels to Boston College.

The successful incumbent will bring experience in a sophisticated fund raising environment, including success in personally soliciting gifts at the $1,000,000+ level, or will have demonstrated experience at a leadership level in a role outside the development profession that would have direct correlation to success as the Senior Principal Giving Advisor at Boston College.  He/she will possess superior leadership skills, including the ability to influence those who are not direct reports but who play an important role in the relationship between the prospect and the University.  Exemplary oral and written communication abilities, with specific application to successful development communications, are required.  As a senior member of the University Advancement staff, the Senior Principal Giving Advisor must be adept at managing volunteer activities and working directly with senior members of the University community, including the president, vice presidents, faculty, and board members, for maximum effectiveness.

An understanding of the nature of work in a complex organization, an ability to work persuasively to advance the cause of Jesuit, Catholic education at Boston College, and demonstrated judgment and decision-making capabilities-including an appetite for undertaking appropriate risk-are required.

The position requires a bachelor's degree and 10 years or more of direct or related professional experience, including demonstrating superior results in environments that point to the individual's potential for success in soliciting and closing gifts of $1,000,000 and above.  Successful applicants will have performed at the highest levels in their professions to date.  Some travel and evening or weekend work is required to successfully meet the job responsibilities of this role.  Proficiency with technology and relevant software applications, such as e-mail, the Microsoft Office Suite is expected. Familiarity with Millennium is desirable.

Boston College is an affirmative action, equal opportunity employer.
