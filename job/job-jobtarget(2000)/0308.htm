Massachusetts General Hospital

, Massachusetts, United States

Job ID: 7412694
Position Title: CLINICAL RESEARCH ASSISTANT II
Job Function: I.D. Administration - Weekly
Posted: November 29, 2010
Job Type: Full-Time
Apply URL: https://careers.partners.org/psc/EA/EMPLOYEE/HRMS/...
 
Location: MGH Main Campus - Boston

Department: I.D. Administration - Weekly
Regular/Temporary: Regular
Standard Hours: 40.00

Full Time/Part Time: Full-Time
Job ID: 2206903

Responsibilities: The HCV Immunology group at Massachusetts General Hospital seeks a research assistant to join a group of researchers conducting translational research that investigates the genetics, clinical manifestations, and immunology of Hepatitis C infection.

In carrying out his/her duties, the Data Manager for the HCV Group will be expected to participate in different levels of multi-disciplinary collaborative research projects carried out between the Division of Infectious Diseases and the Gastrointestinal Unit of the Massachusetts General Hospital, and associated collaborators. Organizational and computer skills must be excellent, as the Coordinator will also be expected to manage data from over 1000 subjects involved in various studies.  Excellent interpersonal skills and the ability to work on a team are essential.  Clinical research experience, and knowledge about infectious diseases, and/or immunology are each a plus.

His/her primary responsibilities will be (1) further development of the HCV database at Massachusetts General Hospital; (2) coordination of a large international consortium of DNA samples and associated genetic data; (3) development of HCV-related data management in a large international of HIV-1 controllers.  This will involve the linking between clinical data and immunologic, genetic, and virologic data.  Commensurate with experience, the data manager will assist Dr. Arthur Kim and his collaborators in study design, data collection, data entry, data analysis, manuscript composition, and conference presentation.  The coordinator may, in conjunction with other research assistants, study nurses, and investigators, be asked to assist with submission of IRB-related materials.  S/he may participate in the organization and analysis of other datasets for research purposes.
Principal Duties and Responsibilities:
Enter and track clinical and scientific data regarding several ongoing studies
Development and maintenance of several databases of over 1000+ individuals
Provide investigators with data requests and sample pull lists for approved collaborative sub-studies, assist with sample pulls.
Perform systematic literature reviews and synthesize relevant data
Track large collection of DNA and other tissue samplings, freezer logs
Attendance and participation in HCV group meetings both at the Massachusetts General Hospital
Assist with manuscript composition, figure preparation, conference presentations
Data analysis commensurate with experience / capabilities
Interact and coordinate with Clinical and Research staff for all of the above duties
Comply with all study regulations, especially regarding confidentiality

Qualifications: B.A./B. Sc. Required. 
Prior clinical research experience preferred. 
Proficiency in computer skills, including knowledge of Microsoft Windows, Word, Excel, Access, and Internet applications.
Knowledge of medical terminology preferred
Experienced in or knowledge of data entry application. 
Familiarity with other software such as Stata, SAS and ACCESS are desirable
Well organized and flexible
Able to work independently and as part of a team
Able to work on multiple concurrent projects
Interact professionally at all levels
Excellent English language and communication skills
Ability to respect confidential medical information

Other information:
The data manager will be responsible for covering other research assistants/coordinators within the group in the event of illness or vacation
Duties are performed in an office environment in a hospital setting

Contact:
Please forward resumes and letters of interest to:
Arthur Kim MD, Principal Investigator.
E-mail: akim1@partners.org; Phone: 617-724-3230

Applicants with clinical/research experience is preferred. Experience with statistical and database software (including SAS, STAT, Excel) is preferred).  Experience and knowledge of infectious diseases and/or immunology are also assets.  Candidates with an M.P.H. or equivalent degree are invited to apply.
