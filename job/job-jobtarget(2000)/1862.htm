DATABASE, REPORTING & WORKFLOW DEVELOPER
HARVEY MUDD COLLEGE

Harvey Mudd College, one of the premier private liberal arts colleges of math, science and engineering in the nation, is seeking a Database, Reporting & Workflow Developer. This is a full-time, 12-month, exempt, benefits-eligible position reporting to the director, Data and Software Engineering Services. Starting salary is dependent upon qualifications with a minimum $5,166.67 per month. HMC offers a competitive benefits package.

The Database, Reporting & Workflow Developer is a member of the Data and Software Engineering Services team in Computing and Information Services (CIS), and exercises a high level of discretion and independent judgment to analyze, develop, and implement reports and workflows for all College departments. This position is an expert in the College’s Business processes; creates cross system solutions to make those processes more effective; and designs reports to provide timely and relevant data to faculty, staff, and students. The position requires a mix of strong interpersonal skills and strong technical skills, with the ability to focus on College priorities. The College is currently using IBM Cognos and Hyland OnBase for reporting and Enterprise Content Management. The position involves becoming expert in these and other software tools.

Job Responsibilities:
- Collect and analyze information about reporting needs within and across College departments using various methods (i.e. interviews, surveys, report assessments, etc.) to obtain information from faculty, staff and students about reporting needs and the rationale behind them. Develop proposals for reporting improvements, incorporating the information obtained from constituents, that will make use of IBM Cognos and other reporting tools; vet the proposals with appropriate stakeholders, asses feedback and revise proposals as necessary. Lead the implementation of proposals, providing follow-up to campus constituents and, applying technical judgment, making appropriate adjustments and improvements.
- Collect and analyze information about business processes within and across College departments using various methods (i.e. interviews, surveys, report assessments, etc.) to obtain information from faculty, staff and students about current business processes and the rationale behind them. Develop proposals for business process improvement, incorporating the information obtained from constituents, that will make use of the Hyland OnBase workflows and document management system; vet the proposals with appropriate stakeholders, assess feedback and revise proposals as necessary. Lead the implementation of proposals, providing follow-up to campus constituents and, applying technical judgment, make appropriate adjustments and improvements,
- Actively, participate in the Data and Software Engineering Services unit meetings and collaborate with colleagues on an ongoing basis. Develop a general understanding of the services provided by the unit, including the Sakai learning management service, Jenzabar JICS portal, Jenzabar CX and others. Assist in the delivery of these services as appropriate and needed.
- Design and provide training programs for faculty and staff on reporting and business processing tools.
- On an ongoing basis, analyze the College’s data needs and make recommendations for improvements to the Director and CIS management team. Design solutions, develop strategies, and lead the implementation of approved recommendations.
- Stay abreast of developments in areas of primary responsibility by carrying out research and attending and presenting at conferences. Place special emphasis on staying expert in:
- Cognos and other similar reporting tools
- Hyland OnBase and other similar enterprise content management/workflow systems
- Extraction, Transformation and Loading (ETL) techniques and tools
- On an ongoing basis, report back to DSES team and others in CIS about developments in these areas.
- Undertake special projects and perform other duties and tasks as assigned by the Director.

Required Qualifications:
- Bachelor's degree in a related field, such as Management Information Systems or Computer Science, or an equivalent combination of training, education and related experience.
- Three to five years of experience in positions of increasing responsibility, preferably in a computing and/or higher education environment.
- Ability to acquire technical expertise quickly and independently
- Excellent written and verbal communication skills
- General working knowledge of relational databases, report writers, and SQL
- Demonstrated ability to work effectively both independently and in a group
- Demonstrated ability to carry out projects with minimal direction
- Demonstrated ability to define specific goals and objectives and prioritize them.
- Demonstrated ability to collect and document information about business processes and reporting needs and clearly communicate this information verbally and in writing
- Demonstrated ability to perform research and distill findings for other technical users and management
- Demonstrated skill in presenting information for groups of people, such as conference presentations and training
- Ability to work in a flexible, fast-paced environment
- Exceptional follow-through and customer-service driven focus
- Effective and constructive participation in team-oriented design discussions and team meetings
- Ability to communicate and work effectively with individuals at all levels of the college community, exercising tact and discretion
- Demonstrated strong interpersonal skills
- Strong technical aptitude
- Highly organized and experienced in multi-tasking
- Strong understanding of confidentiality and discretion
- Ability to work both evenings and/or weekends as required
Preferred Qualifications:
- Education and Experience: Master’s Degree, MBA or equivalent; HTML, JavaScript, and VBScript experience
- Knowledge of Hyland OnBase or other enterprise content management/workflow tools; IBM Cognos or similar reporting tools; ETL techniques and tool; and Java, JBoss and GWT widgets

Why work at Harvey Mudd College:
Located in the heart of Claremont, California, 35 miles east of Los Angeles, Harvey Mudd College is one of seven institutions that comprise The Claremont Colleges. With just over 750 students, HMC provides the collegial working environment of a small college, while providing access to numerous events and activities across all the campuses.

Application Procedures:
In addition to a Harvey Mudd College employment application available at http://www.hmc.edu/employment, please submit a resume and a cover letter describing your qualifications. All required documents must be submitted with your application in order to be considered for this position.

Deadline to apply: Open until filled.

Harvey Mudd College is an Equal Opportunity Employer.
