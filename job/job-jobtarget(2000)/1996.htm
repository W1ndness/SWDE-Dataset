With a mission to lead, serve and advance amateur golf in Southern California, the SCGA keeps its 155,000 members and 1,200 clubs informed through various communications.  Its recently redesigned Web site provides up-to-date association news and tournament results, and its member magazine reviews standout golf course restaurants, the latest apparel and accessories, and great golf travel destinations. Visit scga.org for more information.


Job Requirements

JOB SUMMARY:

Provides leadership for managing all digital content to further improve the brand, increase revenue and support and promote, via digital communications, the SCGA’s various programs and services; and exercises discretion and independent judgment when analyzing and solving problems by performing the following duties. 

ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES include the following:

  • Assists with the design of the SCGA’s Web site marketing plan, including the SCGA Foundation.  Plans and implements the SCGA’s Web site marketing plan to achieve strategic business objectives including, but not limited to, analyzing and interpreting site traffic, formulating web content policies and practices, recommending and developing outreach strategies, producing daily content, providing consultation and advice to management, etc.
  • Plans, establishes and manages the digital branding identity manual, interacts with internal departments to ensure branding standards are met on all digital communications and plans, and administers the SCGA’s Web-based budget within approved specifications.
  • Develops and manages content and design for scga.org and all SCGA electronic communications, including eNews, eBlasts, and eRevisions.  Ensures all communications meet the organization’s strategic needs and maintain a consistent look and feel.
  • Analyzes all Web site and online newsletter metrics, reports on analysis and issues, and recommends strategies to Communications and Marketing staff and SCGA sales team.
  • Researches, recommends and advises management on new technologies, trends and web/interactive practices.
  • Manages electronic calendar for all digital advertisements and sponsorships.
  • Implements plans in supports of digital communications objectives for internal departments.  Initiates meetings, anticipates issues, and provides exceptional internal customer follow up.
  • Manages Web-based archived information for future needs and reference.
  • Promotes and executes SCGA Foundation donor program, fundraising initiatives, and spokesperson opportunities.
  • Handles and performs special projects and other duties as may be assigned by the Director of Communications and Marketing.

 

PERSONAL COMPETENCIES:

To perform the job successfully, Web Content Manager must demonstrate the following competencies:  performs multi-tasks under pressure; adheres to time deadlines; analyzes and processes complex and diverse information; identifies and resolves problems in a timely manner; listens to others and solves conflicts; develops and maintains strong professional relationships with others; integrates diverse viewpoints and opinions working with staff, volunteers and industry stakeholders; speaks clearly and persuasively and demonstrates group presentation skills; conveys a demeanor that is professional yet approachable; builds morale and teamwork; adapts well to changes in the work environment and implements effective changes to improve results; looks for ways to improve and promote quality and increase productivity; works within approved budget; develops strategies to help achieve SCGA’s business objectives; prioritizes and plans work activities; demonstrates accuracy and thoroughness; and meets productivity and quality standards of the Communications & Marketing Department.

 

 

QUALIFICATIONS:

To perform this job successfully, an individual must be able to perform each essential duty satisfactorily. The requirements listed below are representative of the knowledge, skill, and/or ability required. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions.

 

 

Education and Experience:

Bachelor’s degree (B.A.) in communications from a four-year college or university, and a minimum of two or more years of relevant Web and communications experience are required.  Understanding and knowledge of the game of golf is preferred.  Must have a demonstrated ability to interact well with employees, volunteers and others.  Must possess strong work ethic as well as analytical and problem solving skills, have strong oral and written communication skills, have strong interpersonal skills, and be detailed oriented and well-organized.

 

Communication Skills:

Ability to read and comprehend written or oral instructions, correspondence, and memos.  Ability to read, analyze, and interpret general business or trade periodicals, Web analytical software, technical procedures, and governmental regulations.  Ability to write memos, reports, correspondence, procedure manuals, and other documents.  Ability to effectively present information and respond to questions from Board members, managers, employees, members, donors, and the general public. 

 

Mathematical Skills:

Ability to add, subtract, multiply and divide in all units of measure, using whole numbers, common fractions, and decimals.  Ability to calculate figures and amounts such as discounts, proportions, percentages, etc.  Ability to apply concepts such as fractions, percentages, ratios, and proportions to practical situations.

 

Reasoning Ability:

Ability to apply common sense understanding and carry out oral or written instructions.  Ability to define problems, collect information and other data, establish facts, and draw valid conclusions.  Ability to solve practical problems and deal with a variety of concrete as well as abstract variables in situations where only limited standardization exists. Ability to interpret a variety of instructions furnished in written, oral, diagram, or schedule form.

 

Computer Skills:

To perform this job successfully, an individual must have experience in computers both information systems and hardware, and be proficient in using word processing, spreadsheet, and contact management software, including Microsoft applications, e.g., Excel, Word, PowerPoint, Outlook, and other Windows software.            

 

Other Qualifications:

Requires ability to operate a car safely, and must have a valid driver’s license and provide proof of insurability.  The job requires minimal travel.
