 ANNOUNCEMENT OF POSITION VACANCY FOR  Director (Foundation Financial Management)

 

Category: Exempt staff, full-time, 12-month appointment (# 109838)

University of Maryland:  The flagship campus of the state of Maryland, the University of Maryland, College Park, is one of the nation’s premier public research universities.  Founded in 1856, the University of Maryland is strategically located 9 miles from Washington and 30 miles from Baltimore. The University is one of only 61 members in the prestigious Association of American Universities. Its programs include 91 ranked nationally in the Top 25, 61 in the Top 15 and 31 in the Top 10. The University attracts $350 million annually in sponsored research grants and contracts. Our diverse community includes 25,000 undergraduates, 8,000 graduate students, and 12,000 faculty and staff.

 

The Division of University Relations inspires excellence and achievement.  We are a leader in illuminating the University of Maryland as a world-class institution with global impact.  Our mission is to advance the goals of the University by increasing resources and support, enhancing awareness and affinity, and strengthening and developing relationships with the Maryland family and the greater community. Our core values are excellence, communication, and collaboration.

 

Position:  The purpose of this position is to provide accounting expertise for all General Ledger and Reporting Functions of the University of Maryland College Park Foundation and related entities. The position will report to the CFO. The position will be responsible for ensuring all general ledger accounts in all funds accurately reflect the financial operations and responsibilities of the UMCPF, are reconciled monthly, properly maintained for accounting and fiduciary purposes, and provide for prompt and accurate internal and external reporting for the Foundation Board of Trustees. The position will supervise and coordinate the activities of the Accountant for Disbursement/Accounts Payable, and the Assistant Director for Receipts/Accounts Receivables, and the Accountant for Investments.

 

This position oversees the recording of transactions in the general ledger, and reconciliation thereof, to afford for the proper and prompt, generation of internal and external reporting for management, fund holders, auditors, and regulatory bodies. The position will be responsible for maintenance of the chart of accounts, establishment of new and suspension of outdated/unused accounts, structure of the general ledger and reporting formats, opening and closing of fiscal periods, and management of updates to the accounting software. The position will be the approver of all journal entries generated by other functional areas within Foundation Finance, except for entries generated by the CFO. The CFO will be the approver of all journal entries generated by this position. This position will be responsible for monthly reconciliation of all cash accounts and investment accounts, as well as any annuity or similar accounts. The position will initially review all new account agreements, Memorandums of Understanding for Endowments, contracts for services, and grants. The position will also be responsible for all grant reporting and adherence to grant requirements and grantor guidelines. Preparation of financial statement and notes for annual audit, as well as all supporting schedules for the UMCP Foundation and all related organizations.  


Job Requirements

Qualifications:

Education: Bachelor's Degree with a major in Accounting, CPA certificate required.

Experience:  Minimum of 15 years professional accounting experience with a minimum of ten years direct supervisory experience. Prior general ledger experience required in a non-profit organization of at least $50M in assets with major and direct responsibilities for the maintenance of the general ledger, generation of GAAP based financial statements, supervisory experience in an accounting environment. Experience with preparation of 990 required.

Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities: Knowledge of Fund Accounting, GASB and FASB standard, financial reporting requirements for non-profit organizations, internal control standards and procedures, and fiduciary responsibility of Foundation officials, Direct Knowledge of FAS 116, 117, 124 and related accounting standards.  

Physical Demands/Work Environment: Traditional office environment with majority of time using the computer.

 Compensation:  Salary range is low $117,000s –mid $ 146,000s.  The University also offers a comprehensive benefits package, including 22 Days Annual Leave; 15 Days Sick Leave; 3 Days Personal Leave; 15 Paid Holidays; Tuition Remission; Health, Dental, Vision and Prescription coverage.

 

Position is open until filled, however, for best consideration, please apply by December 10, 2010.  To apply, please visit the University of Maryland Human Resources website:  https://jobs.umd.edu/ and upload cover letter of application, resume and names and phone numbers of three professional references in PDF format.  Please include complete mailing address in your application.  Candidates must be able to provide proof of eligibility to work in the USA.  No H1-B Visa sponsorship is offered for this position.  For questions relating to this position, please contact urcareers@umd.edu. 

For more information about the University of Maryland, please visit www.maryland.edu.
