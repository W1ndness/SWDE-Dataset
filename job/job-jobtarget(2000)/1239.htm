Avionics Manager

Crownair Aviation

San Diego, California, United States

This is not an entry-level position.

Job ID: 7388749
Position Title: Avionics Manager
Job Function: Other
Posted: November 18, 2010
Min Experience: 5-7 Years
Job Type: Full-Time
Email Address:
 
  • 1. Responsible to assign qualified personnel to jobs performed and ensure that all repairs are accomplished by approved methods and techniques in accordance with the specifications set forth by the manufacturer in the service manuals, FARs, Service Bulletins, Air Worthiness Directives and other data approved by the FAA administrator.
  • 2. Responsible for providing the inspection department with training records for all personnel within the Avionics Department as required and will notify the inspection department when there is a change in personnel within the avionics department.
  • 3. Ensure that proper tools and equipment are available and maintained in serviceable condition and ensure that any tool which is found unserviceable for any reason will be removed from service.
  • 4. Ensure personnel are adequately trained and competent to perform assigned duties.
  • 5. Ensure proper handling of parts and the preservation of all units or parts during the work process through the Avionics Department.
  • 6. Be thoroughly familiar with the requirements of the repair station manual, FAA Regulations, Airworthiness Directives and Advisory Circulars, Manufactures Service Letters/Bulletins and engineering orders.
  • 7. Overall scheduling and planning of aircraft maintenance into this facility.
  • 8. Evaluate and over see scheduling of aircraft avionics and update the customer on the progress of avionics repair/installation.
  • 9. Approves and tracks all parts ordered for each aircraft in maintenance and ensures the part is ordered for the correct delivery.
  • 10. Responsible for the overall performance and duties of the avionics personnel.
  • 11. Provide customers with estimates and work continuation authorizations for work to be completed.
  • 12. Has direct supervisory authority over the avionics personnel.
  • 13. Communicates with the avionics personnel concerning expectations of work to be accomplished.
  • 14. Responsible for giving guidance to the avionics personnel when necessary.
  • 15. Ensure that all work order authorizations are signed by the customer when the aircraft arrives for maintenance.
  • 16. Ensure that all discrepancies are signed off with all the information completed, such as part numbers, maintenance/service manual, ATA and Chapter etc...
  • 17. Responsible for updating aircraft status.
  • 18. Creates work priorities and time lines, and tracking of maintenance.
  • 19. Responsible for Avionics Supervisor, Avionics Lead Technician and Avionics Technician evaluations in conjunction with the Director of Maintenance.
  • 20. Responsible for employee counseling and performance issues in conjunction with the Maintenance Manager.
  • 21. Responsible for performing weekly crew meetings and communicating with management any employee concerns.
  • 22. Displays and promotes a positive attitude toward work and other employees.
  • 23. Use experience and technical expertise to assist when necessary to meet completion and production schedules and produce the quality our company has come to expect.
  • 24. Assure that all safety precautions are taken and that available protection devices are properly used at all times when applicable to the tasks performed, in order to reduce the risk of possible injury and health problems resulting from performing assigned tasks.
  • 25. Identify any employee's problems and concerns.
  • 26. Participates with management to strategize and implement new maintenance tactics.
  • 27. Provide leadership and communication to subordinates and employees.
  • 28. He may delegate his responsibilities to any qualified assistant as he deems necessary, however, such delegation does not relieve him of his overall responsibility.
  • 29. Performs related work as assigned. 

Job Requirements

  • Education:

    • Five or more years related experience and/or training; equivalent combination of education and experience.
    • Airframe and Powerplant (A&P) Certificate.
    • Federal Communication Commission (FCC) License.

    Experience:

    • Five or more years demonstrated experience in managing avionics inspections, repairs and installations.
    • Strong understanding in 135 and 145 repair station operations/regulations required.
    • Experience in managing five or more employees.

    Qualifications/Special Skills and Certifications:

    • Results oriented - Proven ability to achieve goals. Follow through and meet deadlines; Strong organizational, multi-tasking and time management skills; Strong problem solving and analytical skills.
    • Customer Service - Manages difficult or emotional customer situations; Responds promptly to customer needs; Solicits customer feedback to improve service.
    • Communication - Oral Communication - speaks clearly and persuasively in positive or negative situations; Listens and gets clarification; Responds well to questions; Participates in meetings. Written Communication - Writes material in clear concise terms.
    • Leadership - Exhibits confidence in self and to others; Inspires and motivates others to perform well; Effectively influences actions and opinions of others; Inspires respect and trust; Accepts feedback from other. Displays passion and optimism; Mobilizes other to fulfill the vision; Ability to maintain absolute confidentiality.
    • Computer - Knowledge of MS Office Software, TFBO.
    • Airframe and Powerplant (A&P) Certificate.
    • Federal Communication Commission (FCC) License.
    • Overall knowledge of aircraft that are worked by this location.
