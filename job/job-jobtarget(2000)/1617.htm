Sr. Analyst I -APC

National Association of County and City Health Officials

Washington, Dist. Columbia, United States

This is not an entry-level position.

Job ID: 7428491
Job Duration: Indefinite
Position Title: Sr. Analyst I -APC
Min Education: BA/BS/Undergraduate
Industry: Non-Profit / Charity
Posted: December 3, 2010
Job Function: Professional Services
Min Experience: 7-10 Years
Job Type: Full-Time
Contact Person: No Phone Calls Please
Email Address:

 

The National Association of County and City Health Officials (NACCHO), a national organization representing approximately 3000 local health departments (LHDs) nationwide, has an immediate opening for a Senior Analyst I position to performing a range of responsibilities related to the Advanced Practice Center (APC) program, supporting the Public Health Preparedness Team.  This position is funded through a cooperative agreement from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) Coordinating Office of Terrorism Preparedness and Emergency Response (COTPER).

The Senior Analyst I position is 100% dedicated to the Advanced Practice Center Program, and requires a degree of specialized training and/or knowledge.  The work involves significant collaboration with Federal agencies, local and state public health agencies, regional, state and local coordinators, NACCHO partners, and NACCHO staff.  The work also requires the exercise of extensive discretion, sound independent judgment, and a high level of accountability.  Working in conjunction with the NACCHO project management team, APC sites, and consultants the Senior Analyst I will fulfill the following work duties, which may include, but are not limited to:          

Evaluation, Analysis, and Writing                                                                                                                                                                                                                               

  • § Execute a rigorous evaluation protocol to measure performance objectives and longitudinal impact of APC objectives.
  • § Work in tandem with fellow APC Senior Analysts' to develop corrective action recommendations for APCs as required.
  • § Develop, assemble, and distribute project-related reports, articles and educational materials/tools in collaboration with advisory committee members, other staff, and other project or program partners.
  • § Write issue briefs, research briefs, and white papers on relevant preparedness issues, and publish findings in appropriate professional journals and NACCHO media outlets.
  • § Communicate with local APC site leaders and health department staff through appropriate outlets, regarding meetings, funding, conferences, training opportunities and other timely information related to public health preparedness.
  • § Summarize findings, translate, coordinate and prepare written summaries of project meetings.
  • § Assist in the preparation of proposals, periodic reports to funding partners and other relevant documents.

Technical Expertise and Program Support

  • § Design, implement, and track evaluation protocols through access and working knowledge of research software including, but not limited to SAS, SPSS etc...
  • § Assist in the collaboration with external contractors and consultants working on NACCHOs APC program activities.
  • § Represent NACCHO and APC at external meetings, accurately and adequately supporting the association's positions in various settings
  • § Develop and conduct professional presentations and abstracts at conferences, workshops and training sessions.
  • § Provide high level public health all-hazard technical assistance and consultation to NACCHO staff, members, and partners.

Project Planning and Coordination

  • § In conjunction with the APC Program Manager strategize and coordinate activities across the NACCHO all-hazard division.
  • § Serve as lead staff liaison to workgroup members, regional coordinators, partner and collaborative organizations, Federal partners and funding agencies, as necessary.
  • § Perform other duties as assigned.
  • § Travel required (intermittent approximate 3 to 4 days/month).

Compensation: $52,369 - $64,938 commensurate with experience. 


Job Requirements

Qualifications:

Education/Experience

  • § Master's degree in Public Health or Public Policy; or related field with a minimum of 5 years of experience in a public health setting; or Bachelor's degree with a minimum of 7 years of such experience, is required.
  • § A Working knowledge of all-hazard initiatives (preparedness, mitigation, recovery and response) in public health systems is strongly preferred.
  • § Special consideration will be given to candidates who have assumed a leadership role in the creation and coordination of local, regional, state, or national all-hazard training programs and/or disaster personnel deployment initiatives.

Knowledge

  • § Demonstrated ability to plan and execute evaluation and analysis protocols
  • § Working knowledge and understanding of all-hazard issues and public health practice.

Abilities

  • § Demonstrated ability to independently design and manage project activities
  • § Ability to establish effective working relationships and maintain credibility with diverse groups; including co-workers, personnel in partner agencies, and external stakeholders
  • § Ability to interpret and explain research findings and project outcomes both orally and in writing
  • § Ability to effectively represent NACCHO in a variety of settings
  • § Demonstrated ability to complete high quality work within time requirements\
  • § Ability to work independently as necessary to achieve high performance in position
  • § Demonstrated individual accountability for work performance and outcomes

Skills

  • § Excellent oral and written communication skills and presentation skills
  • § Skills in establishing and maintaining interpersonal relationship
  • § Skills in providing technical assistance
  • § Skill in preparing and presenting research findings in oral and written form
  • § Strong organizational skill
  • § Detail-oriented; high degree of accuracy in all aspects of work
  • § Strong meeting facilitation skills
  • § Customer service orientation, for both internal and external customers
