The Art Institute of California - San Francisco

San Francisco, California, United States

Responsible for providing counseling, information, and support to student and parent borrowers consistent with federal requirements and the school default management plan. Incumbent must assure that the EDMC philosophy: quality services to clients; development, growth, involvement, and recognition of employees; sound economic principles; and environment which is conducive to innovation, positive thinking and expansion - is considered in carrying out the duties and responsibilities of this position.

Key Job Elements
  • Conduct entrance interviews with, or manage completion of online entrance interviews for Federal Stafford loan, Federal Perkins Loan and alternative loan borrowers.
  • Conduct exit interviews in person with graduating borrowers, or manage completion of online exit interviews.
  • Conduct exit interviews with students who are withdrawing, or manage completion of online exit interviews.
  • Handle post-enrollment follow-up activities, in accordance with the EDMC default management plan.
  • Maintain appropriate records.
  • Handle telephone, mail and in-person inquiries.
  • Advise college management of pertinent issues and concerns relating to default.
  • Other duties as assigned

Reports To:
Director, Student Financial Services
Directly Supervises:
None
Interacts With:
Other members of the student financial services team as well as students, and the Accounting, Admissions and Registration departments; outside contacts include lenders, loan servicers, and default management servicers

Job Requirements

Job Requirements

Knowledge:

  • Bachelor's degree in a related field required.
  • Experience with Federal student loans or consumer credit or student financial aid preferable .

Skills:

  • Excellent written and verbal communication skills.
  • Strong interpersonal skills with both faculty and student populations.
  • Superior organizational and problem resolution skills.
  • Strong basic computer software (MS Office) skills as well as exposure to more concentrated financial aid/EDE software programs such as Banner.

Abilities:

  • Ability to read, comprehend, and comply with EDMC policies pertaining to the provision of student financial aid.
  • Ability to interact effectively as a member of a team and work collaboratively with other departments.
  • Ability to listen to customers (e.g. students, staff, etc.) and to understand and respond positively to their requests
  • Ability to work without close supervision and to set one's own priorities.
