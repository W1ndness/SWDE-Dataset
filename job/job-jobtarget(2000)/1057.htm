About the Company

EMD Serono, Inc. is a leader in US biotechnology. We discover, develop, manufacture and market innovative medicines in key therapeutic areas; neurodegenerative diseases, oncology, fertility, endocrinology and cardio metabolic care. With headquarters in Geneva, Switzerland, US operations in Rockland and Billerica , MA, and field sales locations throughout the US, and over 17000 people worldwide, we offer brands in 150 countries.

Description

The position seeks an expert in imaging of small animal CNS to investigate mechanism of action of novel medicines for the treatment of Multiple Sclerosis. Imaging modalities such as MRI, MRS, DTI and MTR will be used to qualify exploratory and conventional non-invasive imaging biomarkers in the context of novel therapies for their use in clinical development.

Functional Tasks

  • Provide imaging expertise in MRI and other modalities as a translational readout for preclinical studies in animal models of MS.
  • Participate in and manage extramural academic collaborations and CRO imaging investigations.
  • Assist with and lead the development and implementation of in vivo imaging in neurodegeneration therapeutic team, including hands-on experimentation.
  • Collaborate with neurobiologists in the generation of novel animal models, imaging biomarkers, and drug efficacy read-outs.
  • Represent Neurodegeneration therapeutic area team in biomarker planning for clinical research and development of molecules.
  • Produce timely and comprehensive internal and external reports, publications, and presentations of research.
  • Participate in project leadership, strategy development, and in-licensing evaluations.
  • Maintain a direct and close relationship with the clinical teams.

Managerial Tasks

  • Recruit, train and manage up to 3 direct reports.

Job Requirements

Qualifications

EDUCATION/LANGUAGES

  • Must be fluent in English.
  • PhD with 3+ years of relevant experience in in vivo imaging in small animals, preferably in neurological models.

PROFESSIONAL & TECHNICAL SKILLS

  • Understanding of biology, pharmacology, small animal imaging in the context of pharmaceutical research.
  • Small animal MRI experience preferably with a knowledge of EAE and MS.
  • Extensive knowledge of imaging application. Animal skills, such as handling, restraining, anesthesia and injections (IP, IM, IV) in rodents.
  • Proficiency in data analysis using Excel and statistical software, image processing; computer proficiency.
  • Good interpersonal, communication skills, and working with diverse and talented scientific teams.
  • Excellent written and oral communication.
  • Experience in advanced microscopy techniques (confocal, EM) and advantage but not a requirement.
