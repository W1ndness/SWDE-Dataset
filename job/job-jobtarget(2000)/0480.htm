Description:

The Office Manager reports to the Deputy Director of Operations and Finance.  Broadly stated, the Office Manager is responsible for the administrative functions that support the daily life of the organization. Additionally, s/he works closely with all Directors and coordinates special projects for the Executive Director.  This position is designed to be part time, however, it may evolve into a full-time position with expanded responsibilities should business needs dictate.

Key Result Areas:

Administration

  • Answer telephone, direct calls, obtain and distribute messages and greet guests
  • Respond to inquiries from public, disseminate information and promotional materials accordingly
  • Sort, distribute, and send all incoming/outgoing mail
  • Screen and prioritize incoming messages and mail for the Executive Director
  • Prepare outgoing correspondence on behalf of/for Executive Director signature
  • Coordinate travel, meetings, staff events and other logistics
  • Organize, coordinate, and communicate the Executive Director's calendar
  • Analyze systems and work flows within SOP and internal control guidelines. Recommend and implement improvements as needed.
  • Organize and maintain all electronic and paper organizational files, archives, etc.
  • Develop systems to manage inventories, materials and supplies. Ensure office supplies and collateral materials are always stocked and ready for use
  • Keep list of hardware and software needs and repairs and see that they are resolved
  • Work with internal users and external technology consultants to troubleshoot computer issues
  • Coordinate administrative aspects of regular events (staff meetings, Board of Director calls, etc.) to include agenda, confirming participation, venue, material distribution, and recording of minutes
  • Update and maintain event planning & procedures books, timelines and after-action reports
  • Support Deputy Directors of Operations and Finance, Programs, and Development, as well as the Executive Director, as needed.
  • Other tasks as assigned

Director Support

  • Coordinate bi-weekly collection of staff timesheets and expense reimbursement forms
  • Maintain databases with donor & grantee contact info
  • Interface with donors, grantees, and the not-for-profit community for admin matters
  • Work closely with the Director of Communications to streamline and simplify communication among geographically distant staff
  • Enforce brand integrity; staying up to date with current messaging, logos, forms, PSA's, videos, etc.
  • Collaborate with the Executive Director on special projects; be prepared to perform research, develop content, and create draft presentations for discussion and review

Job Requirements

Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's degree preferred
  • 3-5 years of administrative experience
  • Must have excellent written and verbal communication skills
  • Strong computer skills (Microsoft Word, Excel and PowerPoint)
  • Familiarity with bookkeeping and/or accounting a plus
  • Familiarity with social networking media (Facebook, Twitter, etc.) a plus
  • Must think pro-actively, have a desire to improve processes, and be a problem solver
  • Must be able to work independently, multi-task and work under tight deadlines
  • Commitment to social justice and the mission, and
  • Supervisory experience preferred
