Mercy Medical Center is a fully accredited Joint Commission, 25-bed Critical Access regional medical facility which serves approximately 60,000 people from Western North Dakota and Eastern Montana.  Mercy Medial Centers is a rural regional center and offers a large array of services.  Mercy hosts a 24-hour Emergency Department, Surgical Services, Cancer Treatment Center (IMRT and BAT), Home Health & Hospice, Renal Dialysis, Rehabilitation Services, sophisticated Imaging Services including MRI and 16-slice CT scanning.  Mercy also has a full service OB program with close to 500 live births annually.  Mercy has a net revenue budget of $40 million.  There are approximately 425 employees at Mercy Medical Center.

 Surgical Services department provides in-patient and outpatient surgical services.  The OR Suite hosts 4 operative rooms to provide endoscopy, urology, OB/Gyn, ENT, General Surgery, and orthopedic services.  Surgical Services cariies a high case load that is expected to increase over the next few months.

Mercy also provides clinic services to community. Primary care services are provided through Craven Hagan Clinic with specialty services offered via Mercy Health Partners. These clinics generate approximately $9,000,000 in revenue.  Craven Hagan Clinic employs 6 physicians and 1 physician extender.  Mercy Health Partners offers ENT, General Surgery and Orthopedic services.  Our Occupational Health Clinic also provides services to our growing number of community employers. 

 

In 1996, Mercy Medical Center became part of Catholic Health Initiatives.  Catholic Health Initiatives (CHI) is a faith-inspired national non-profit corporation based in Denver Colorado.  It is comprised of 75 hospitals, 22 Critical Access Hospitals, 40 long-term care, assisted, independent living, and residential facilities serves 68 rural and urban communities, and has more than 66,000 employees.  CHI is spread across 19 states and is untied by a common mission, vision and core values.

 

Mercy Medical Center is located in Williston.  Williston is a growing community in the northwest corner of North Dakota, just 60 miles from the Canadian border and 18 miles from the Montana border.  There are a variety of landmarks and activities in Williston that contribute to a remarkably high quality of life.  The region boasts Fort Union Trading Post National Historic Site, Fort Buford State Historic Site, and the Missouri-Yellowstone River Confluence.  Williston boast many recreational opportunities with miles of paved bike trails, six new USTA regulations size tennis courts, recently constructed large community playground with splash park, a very large recreational lake full of water, some of the best golfing in the country (The Links of North Dakota is ranked one of America’s 100 best modern golf courses), and great opportunities for hunting and fishing.  Williston ranked 24th in a recent edition of Outdoor Life Magazine for being in the “Top 200 Towns for the Outdoorsman.”  Williston is also just an hour away from scenic Theodore Roosevelt National Park North Unit.

 

The Williston community is presently going through a significant economic boom due to the Bakken Oil and Gas Field growth.  North Dakota is one of the few areas in the U.S. where oil production is actually increasing.  A recent Forbes article reported that North Dakota’s oil production is expected to approach 350,000 barrels in 2010.  The unemployment rate is extremely low and the local economy is very strong.  This is expected to continue into the Twenty First Century, so Williston and the surrounding area will continue to grow and develop into the foreseeable future.
