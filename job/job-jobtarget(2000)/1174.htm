The Aging & Disabilities Division (ADS) of the Human Services Department (HSD) is seeking an experienced Case Manager to join our team! This an exciting opportunity to be part of a department dedicated to connecting people in the Seattle community with resources and solutions during times of need, and improve the health and quality of life for seniors and adults with disabilities.

 

The Case Manager in-home comprehensive functional assessments for frail elderly and disabled adult clients receiving DSHS Medicaid funded personal care and other services. Case Managers develop an individualized service plan based on the functional assessment with the client that identifies priorities, desired outcomes, and the strategies and resources to be used in attaining the outcomes. Reporting to a Human Services Supervisor, the Case Manager will coordinate and monitor 80-100 client care plans. 

 

 Responsibilities include:

§  Conduct in-home functional assessments and reassessments.  Confirm diagnosis, skin condition, medications, treatment, psych/social information with healthcare providers and others as needed.  Review financial eligibility annually and as needed.  Work with interpreters to provide services to persons who do not speak English.

§  Document all client activities and contacts (referrals, additional contacts, and other in-home visits, etc.) and maintain confidential client record.

§  Develop and evaluate a plan of care with each client utilizing paid and unpaid resources available.  Link clients and caregivers to services available to meet their needs, including referrals to appropriate services (i.e. caregiver support programs and educational programs, mental health, adult protective services, drug and alcohol treatment, nutrition, adult day health, self-directed care, nurse delegation, etc.) and other informal support networks. 

§  Advocate with agencies or persons to help clients receive appropriate benefits or services.  

§  Assist clients with recruiting, contracting and termination of their Individual Personal Providers (IP) and/or home care agency. 

§  Authorize or reauthorize payment for client approved Medicaid funded services or terminate services in WA St. SSPS (social services payment system).

§  Conduct placement (i.e., Nursing Home, Assisted Living, Adult Family Home, etc.) assessments and/or termination planning.  Close case management services when indicated.

§  Maintain work standards to ensure compliance with DSHS Quality Assurance expectations. 

As a City of Seattle employee, you'll enjoy outstanding benefits which support work/life quality, including a comprehensive and generous benefits package which exceeds 40% of base salary.


Job Requirements

Required Qualifications:

§  Bachelor's degree in Social Services, Psychology or a related field (or a combination of education and/or training and/or work experience which provides the ability to perform the work of the class)

§  Three years social services experience involving interviewing, counseling, or crisis intervention

 

Preferred Qualifications:

§  Second language fluency in Russian/Ukraine, Somali, Spanish and/or Punjabi with the ability to effectively communicate verbally and in writing in English and second language

§  Experience working in major social service, health and/or government agencies

§  Experience working with elderly or disabled adults who have unstable health conditions and risk factors including multiple physical and/or mental health problems, long-term care needs, and/or drug/alcohol problems

§  Experience conducting comprehensive medical/psychosocial assessments while administering and monitoring service delivery

§  Experience developing and implementing individualized service plans with proven ability to maintain confidential records

§  Demonstrated proficiency and use of electronic mail and industry standard computer software including Microsoft Office (i.e. Word, Outlook, etc.)

§  Ability to work effectively within diverse workgroups and populations with proven ability to collaborate with community based and governmental agencies

§  Comfortable working in a fast-moving, collaborative, team-oriented environment

 

This position closes on TUESDAY, NOVEMBER 30, 2010!
