Min Qualifications

Bachelor¿s Degree or equivalent; RHIT, RHIA or CCS Certification through AHIMA required; and four years supervisory experience and ICD-9-CM and CPT-4/HCPCS Coding experience.

JOB DESCRIPTION:

The Coding Manager is responsible for all necessary decisions relating to the daily activities of the coding functions of the Coding Department and determining that all functions are being carried out appropriately and in a timely manner. Oversees inpatient and outpatient coding operations within the Hospital Patient Financial Services department (HPFS) and also serves as a resource to all other coding functions within the organization. The Coding Manager has direct supervision of all Coding personnel and serves as a member of the Hospital Patient Financial Services department (HPFS) Management Team. Coding Manager will oversee coding compliance initiatives, Case Mix index management, and assist in coding as necessary.

Salary Range

Salary starts at $61,887.00.  Actual salary is commensurate with experience.

Specific Job Related Duties

* Develops, implements, and maintains data quality for coding, reimbursement, and medical record documentation.

* Responsible for the daily operations of coding department including monitoring quality and quantity of workflow to ensure completion of work assignments.

* Ensures compliance for areas of responsibility with all regulatory requirements for HSCRC, CMS, JCAHO and all other licensing and regulatory organizations.

* Serves as primary Coding department representative for resolution and improvement of revenue cycle initiatives/issues

* Serves as a Coding and Reimbursement resource to Hospital Patient Financial Services.
   Maintains a working knowledge of all current rules and regulations as they pertain to coding  and reimbursement practices as appropriate and mandated by all governmental agencies.

* Establishes and maintains good working relationships with Health Information Management,
   Hospital Patient Financial Services, Charge Description Master, FGP Financial Services, Medical Staff, and other customers both internal and external.

* Allocates tasks to staff and directs their daily performance, supervises the special project work, reviews work papers and resolves questions and verifies that conclusions are supported.  Keeps supervisor and department informed of progress, so that time is efficiently used and departmental quality management standards are met.

* Provides information or advice to supervisor and department.  Formulates alternative solutions to problems, sells ideas, and obtains acceptance and action on audit findings.

* Develops and maintains a current procedure manual for all employees that out lines all aspects of coding and reimbursement for third party payers.

* Reviews payment denials to identify trends and opportunities to ensure maximum reimbursement for third party payers.

* Conducts performance appraisals on all Coding personnel with input from the appropriate staff.

* Identifies appropriate internal controls for department; provides mechanisms to monitor and
   enforce compliance.

* Recruits and trains qualified applicants to ensure all positions are filled with the best possible
   employees

* Adheres to internal controls and reporting structure.

MARGINAL OR PERIODIC JOB FUNCTIONS:

* Assists and manages special projects/assignments as needed.

* Performs related duties as required.

Preferred Skill/Competency

* Thorough understanding of ICD-9-CM, CPT-4/HCPCS Coding, DRG and APC reimbursement and CMI management.
* The ability to interpret clinical and statistical data. Knowledge of healthcare system applications including encoder software
* Thorough knowledge of current requirements of the JCAHO, coding and compliance regulations related to the Prospective Payment Systems
* Excellent verbal and written communication skills

Preferred Work Experience

* Experience with concurrent coding and physician documentation monitoring and education preferred.
