Employment in the world of education can be a very fulfilling and meaningful career path. We all realize the value of education and the importance it can play in helping to change people’s lives. Westwood College has a rich history that can be traced back for many decades. Whether you have an interest in working in an administrative environment or directly on a campus interacting with students and faculty, we offer a variety of job openings that allow you to help shape the lives of thousands of students each and every day.
 
Our 18 campuses, located in California, Colorado, Georgia, Illinois, Texas and Virginia, hire energetic and committed professionals as both full-time and adjunct faculty members. Opportunities are available periodically in a variety of subject areas. Our Westwood College – Online Campus is also always interested in experienced faculty and adjunct faculty to teach online. Online instructors can reside anywhere in the country.

If you love learning, teaching and helping students, then Westwood College is the place for you. Whether you are a top-notch sales professional, enjoy marketing strategy and execution or seek a position helping with the administrative and management side of an organization, Westwood College is always looking for talent to help drive the institution forward.

Westwood College in Chicago, O'Hare campus is seeking an Assistant Academic Dean.

POSITION PURPOSE
Working under the supervision of the Campus Academic Dean, the Campus Assistant Academic Dean has responsibility for the delivery of educational programs at the campus, including implementing the Westwood Mission and Westwood Teaching and Learning Model, ensuring effective teaching; and faculty hiring, evaluation, training, and development. The Campus Assistant Academic Dean maintains high levels of quality and student satisfaction in order to retain and successfully graduate students, while operating in compliance with Westwood College policies and procedures and all state, federal, and accrediting body regulations.

ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

  • Assumes all duties and responsibilities of the Campus Academic Dean in his or her absence.
  • Assists Campus Academic Dean in all other tasks required of the Campus Academic Dean, including:
    • Campus Level
      • Maintain and oversee all aspects of the education department
      • Maintain high levels of student satisfaction as measured by program completion, retention, and satisfaction surveys.
      • Ensures adequate resources are available to faculty and students to deliver curriculum including classrooms, labs, books, materials and technology.
      • Collaborates in the development and ongoing improvement of curriculum.
      • Evaluates and recommends resources including textbooks, materials, equipment and technology.
      • Monitors student satisfactory academic progress and initiates appropriate actions.
      • Ensures compliance with state and federal regulations and licensing requirements.
      • Participates in Program Advisory Committees and seeks employer input on curriculum.
      • Monitors submission of source documents for entering attendance and grades into student records (including processing of transfer credits).
      • Generates Student Action Reports to accurately represent student status.
      • Assists in managing the Master scheduling.
      • Provide academic advising to students.
      • Participate in the orientation of new students.
      • Manage external training contracts (Aviation)
      • Assists in planning of information related to graduation including the actual ceremony.
    • Corporate Level Interaction
      • Assist the Campus Academic Dean in efforts:
        • To work with the Department of Academic Affairs and in collaboration with Education Departments at the other campuses
        • To manage the ongoing development and maintenance of common curriculum
        • To define faculty skills necessary to deliver curriculum
        • To oversee research and development of new programs of instruction
        • To oversee the development and maintenance of education policies and procedures
        • To communicate changes in curriculum to ensure accurate marketing and delivery of programs of study
        • To manage academic catalog and to develop academic calendar

OTHER DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

  • Attends meetings and prepares reports as needed.
  • Assists as assigned in the preparation and conducting of employee reviews in a timely manner.
  • Assists as assigned in the preparation of the annual operating and capex budget

JOB QUALIFICATIONS
At the time of hiring Westwood College faculty and education department managers are required to hold a completed graduate degree from a U.S. regionally or nationally accredited university.  Ph.D. or Ed.D. from an accredited institution/university is preferred.   In addition, state licensing and national accrediting criteria apply. Faculty with international credentials are required to present an official U.S. degree evaluation by AACRAO or NACES recognized evaluation agency.

  • Meets accrediting agency, state and federal standards for formal education and certification/licensing requirements
  • Working knowledge of institutional, state and federal guidelines and regulations
  • Working knowledge of spreadsheets, databases, and word processing software
  • Strong oral and written communication skills
  • Strong attention to detail

PHYSICAL REQUIREMENTS

  • Standing
  • Walking
  • Sitting
  • Carrying – up to 50 pounds
  • Normal vision/talking/speaking
