The Brookings Institution is a private nonprofit organization devoted to independent research and innovative policy solutions. Established in 1916, Brookings analyzes current and emerging issues and produces new ideas that matter - for the nation and the world.

Brookings seeks to fill the position of Assistant Controller.

Basic Function:

The Assistant Controller plans, directs, and organizes all financial accounting operations including: overseeing preparation of financial statements and reports that accurately depict the current financial position of the Institution; ensuring the timely, accurate, and efficient analysis, communication, and reporting of financial data in accordance with GAAP, funding contracts, and internal policy; and providing support to the Controller and representing the Controller as needed in various internal/external meetings. Responsible for execution of annual audit; trains and educates staff on accounting issues; and researches accounting issues as requested by the Controller. Significant written and verbal interaction with program administrators, guests and affiliates, and external parties, including auditors and banks.

Education/Experience Requirements:

Bachelors degree in Accounting or related field required; advanced degree or CPA preferred. Minimum 8 years of accounting experience in positions of increasing complexity, including 5 plus years of supervisory experience required. Focus on financial reporting required. Non-profit accounting and grant allocation experience required.

Knowledge Requirements:

- Dynamic individual with strong managerial, leadership, interpersonal, problems-solving, and written and oral communication skills needed.
- Thorough knowledge of non-profit accounting with extensive knowledge of GAAP, FASB, Sarbanes-Oxley, fund accounting, and financial management.
- Knowledge of grants management as it relates to compliance and reporting of government, corporate, foundation, and individual grants is essential.
- Experience with PeopleSoft or similar software required.
- Ability to prioritize and handle multiple assignments concurrently; proven ability to operate successfully in a demanding environment; and demonstrable track record of high performance.
- Detail-oriented and able to work in a meticulous, thorough, and detailed manner.
- Must possess the ability to assume non-routine tasks and to take initiative where appropriate, and possess a strong follow-through on all projects with the ability to bring projects to timely completion.

Principal Duties and Responsibilities:

10% Other Projects:

- Additional projects and analysis as assigned by the Controller and/or requested by the Vice President for Finance & Administration.

90% General Accounting:

- Direct daily operations of accounting department, including accounts receivable, accounts payable, general ledger, payroll, fixed assets, sales and use tax, cash flow management, billing, and sales order entry.
- Oversee preparation of monthly and annual financial statements and audit, and monthly reconciliation of all income received.
- Oversee the accounting and reporting for the endowment and operating fund, and related assets and liabilities.
- Oversee the maintenance of accurate inventory of all assets and the maintenance of reconciliations of balance sheet accounts, including supporting documentation as required by auditors.
- Review and maintain accounting department policies and procedures.
- Analyze internal controls and take corrective action as needed.
- Manage staff overseeing preparation of bank reconciliations and recommend opportunities for improved cash flow.
- Provide quality assurance and customer service of accounting and financial services provided to the various users both internal and external.
- Respond to accounting inquiries from program personnel.
- Plan, assign, and direct work of managers and various direct reports.
- Ensure timely and accurate recording of all transactions that impact the general ledger by accounting staff.
- Interviews, hires, trains, and promotes the ongoing development of staff: (1) Prepares and delivers periodic and timely performance appraisals (both formal and informal); (2) Mentors staff in driving efficiencies in close process, grant allocation, and general process improvement; and (3) Seen as an advocate of change management and forward looking as organization continues to grow.
- Develops, implements, and monitors an appropriate system of internal controls and monitors departmental goals and objectives.
- Prepares or directs and reviews the preparation of reports and analysis that demonstrate and communicate the financial impact on operations. Develops and communicates recommendations related to such reports and analysis.
- Establishes and promotes a service oriented “can do” operational philosophy within the department that is both proactive and responsive.
- Develops, maintains, and reports against a departmental schedule of deliverables and due dates.
- Makes periodic inquiries to ensure that needs are being met and to identify opportunities for improved service.
- Establishes and maintains an appropriate image of the department in order that it might function effectively while preserving the high-quality of the financial reports, statements, and analyses.
- Maintains a strong senior financial manager mindset while becoming an integral member of the team.
- Provides timely and ongoing communication to Controller regarding all areas of responsibility including personnel/performance issues and highlights.

Only applicants meeting minimum qualifications for the position will be considered. No phone calls please. Brookings welcomes resumes from all qualified applicants, particularly women and minorities.
