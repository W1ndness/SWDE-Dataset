Research Scientist

Center for Advanced Study of Teaching and Learning

Charlottesville, Virginia, United States

This is not an entry-level position.

Job ID: 7379440
Job Duration: Indefinite
Position Title: Research Scientist
Required Travel: 10-25%
Posted: November 15, 2010
Job Function: Research
Job Type: Full-Time
Apply URL: http://jobs.virginia.edu
 

The Center for the Advanced Study of Teaching and Learning at the University of Virginia seeks a motivated research team leader who is dedicated to quality and experienced in the conduct of large-scale educational research studies for a Research Scientist position. The research scientist will work as a part of a team of scientists on the CASTL portfolio of research in education science, which include rigorous multi-site studies of interventions, large-scale longitudinal studies relating classroom and family experiences to child outcomes, and studies focusing on large-scale observational assessments of classroom quality and children’s social adjustment and learning. This person will excel in the coordination of multiple complex projects, in creative, flexible problem-solving and in the ability to communicate advanced level analyses into user-friendly formats. The successful candidate will conduct extensive data analysis and prepare publications using any of several data sets available through CASTL, manage and direct components of ongoing studies, and write grants and design new studies. 


Job Requirements

Applicants must have a Ph.D. in Developmental Psychology, Education, or a related field and a strong interest in school-based research. Applicants must have substantive knowledge and background in the field of education, evidence of potential for obtaining external funding, and demonstrated leadership, management, and communication skills.

Additional required qualifications include:

Manage all aspects of research implementation, data collection, and data management and analysis; Serve as lead researcher, project manager and collaborator on multiple grant-funded projects, guiding the work of multiple individuals filling various roles on the research team and implementing flexible problem-solving skills; Coordinate multiple simultaneous tasks of varying complexity, with demonstrated accountability and excellent attention to detail; Employ strong organizational and time management skills, to work independently and with minimal supervision and deliver high quality results to meet regular deadlines; Provide clear, concise reports to the funding agency and for oral and written presentations; Lead and participate in the preparation of research publications and grant proposals; Effectively employ software tools for office productivity, presentations, and statistical analysis; and, master new technology as needed.

This position requires education, training, and prior experience in grant-funded early childhood education research. This includes independently designing and overseeing aspects of a complex research protocol, ability to implement randomized control trials, knowledge of child and classroom observation systems, ability to design effective data tracking and processing systems, and quantitative statistical skills including familiarity with hierarchical linear modeling.

This position is fully funded for two years, after which time the successful candidate would be expected to have generated partial support through external funding. This position is for forty (40) hours a week with full-time benefits. Additional information pertaining to the duties of this position can be found under employment at the following website: http://curry.edschool.virginia.edu.

To apply, candidates must complete a candidate profile under job posting number 0606750 at http://jobs.virginia.edu. Candidates are required to attach a cover letter, curriculum vitae, and the contact information for three professional references. Screening of candidates will begin December 6, 2010; however, the position will remain open until filled.

The University of Virginia is an Equal Opportunity/Affirmative Action Employer. We are committed to building a multicultural work force and strongly encourage women, racial/ethnic/gender minorities and persons with disabilities to apply.
