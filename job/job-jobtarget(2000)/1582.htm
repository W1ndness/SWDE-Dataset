Company Description:

Yeshiva University, ranked in the top tier of universities nationally, offers rewarding and challenging employment opportunities to qualified candidates in a wide range of disciplines. Founded in 1886, Yeshiva University has a strong tradition of combining Jewish scholarship with academic excellence and achievement in the liberal arts, sciences, medicine, law, business, social work, Jewish studies, education and psychology. The University offers an excellent compensation package, and a broad range of employee benefit plans, including immediate participation and full vesting in the University's retirement plan. Staff members are typically eligible for four weeks paid vacation each year.

Yeshiva University is an equal opportunity employer committed to workforce diversity.

Job Description:

The faculty of the Albert Einstein College of Medicine (Einstein) is a diverse group of preeminent scholars in fields of basic, clinical and translational research. We are seeking an individual who can design and implement novel computational solutions to the equally diverse research problems of the faculty. Leveraging a depth of knowledge in high performance computing and/or information exchange, this individual will have the opportunity to grow and develop our information technology (IT) infrastructure to meet the faculty needs and enhance research capability at Einstein. Reporting to the University Vice President and Chief Information Officer, this individual will be given seed capital to invest in high performance resources to expand faculty research capabilities, and furthermore will be asked to advise the executive leadership of Einstein in additional IT investment in support of the research mission. She / He will need to communicate effectively with the research faculty to establish their needs, set priorities and advocate to the executive leadership of the college.

The responsibilities of the selected individual include:

  • Work with the research faculty to create the vision and strategic plan for Faculty Research Computing at Einstein and implement the plan to advance and expand the research possibilities within the College;
  • Efficiently utilize the existing capital for the expansion of Faculty Research Computing, and create a compelling business plan to secure additional funding for the realization of the faculty goals;
  • Be the faculty’s advocate for the allocation of IT resources in support of research;
  • Facilitate communication between the faculty and Einstein’s IT specialists;
  • Guide and support Einstein IT functions for the research endeavor;
  • Advance the use of IT, such as high performance computing, data processing, transfer and storage in support of the faculty research community;
  • Plan, develop and implement high level IT solutions to meet faculty research needs such as high performance computing and data security and backup;
  • Organize and manage a program of technical support in the use of computing resources in support of the faculty’s research;
  • Help the faculty secure needed IT resources including providing expert advice and research for the submission of extramural research grants.
  • Direct and develop current staff of 3 professionals, and additionally build the team as necessary and appropriate for the expansion of the department.
  • Advise faculty as to available off-the-shelf software and/or hardware solutions relevant to their inquiries.

Einstein is an open and engaging community of physicians and researchers that embraces strong and creative leadership. The selected individual will have the opportunity to make a difference in the health and well being of the people of the world. Qualified candidates applicants should have demonstrated accomplishment in the computational, physical or biological sciences (not necessarily in an academic settings) with extensive experience at a high level of IT leadership. Successful applicants must appreciate the breath and depth of the biomedical sciences and be fluent in the application of high level IT solutions and high performance computing to the challenges posed by contemporary biomedical science.

The Albert Einstein College of Medicine is an Equal Opportunity Employer


Job Requirements

A Masters Degree in a related field is required; a Ph.D. is preferred.
