I.   JOB SUMMARY

     

     A.  POSITION PURPOSE:

The OR Technician is responsible for assessing, planning, implementing and evaluating the care delivery to assigned patients.   Works closely with and leads other ancillary personnel in the care of patients.  Professional practice must be consistent with Mercy philosophy.   Provides prescribed medical treatment and personal care services to ill, injured, convalescent, and disabled persons.  The OR Technician must be able to demonstrate the knowledge and skills necessary to provide care appropriate to the age of the patients served on his or her assigned unit.  The individual must demonstrate knowledge of the principles of growth and development over the life span and possess the ability to assess data reflective of the patient's status and interpret the appropriate information needed to identify each patient's requirements relative to his or her age-specific needs, and to provide the care needed as described in the department's policies and procedures.

 

Is held accountable for following the policies and procedures of the department, organization, and CHI.  Additionally, the position serves as a role model for ethical behavior and promotes an awareness and understanding of positive ethical and moral principles consistent with the Mission, Vision and Behavior Standards of Mercy Medical Center.

V.        ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS:  (40%)

1.   Assists with the nursing care of patients in the OR by performing routine and delegated duties according to the standards of practice and policies of the hospital department.

2.   Inventories and requisitions supplies.

3.   Stocking, replenishing, preparing, and/or selecting supplies and equipment for storage or use during surgical procedures.

4.   Perform competencies as listed for the OR Competency Checklist.

5.   Maintain aseptic technique at all times.   Report breaks in technique to the RN circulator and surgeon.  Correct them immediately.  

6.   Anticipate the needs of the surgeon regarding instruments and equipment according to technique procedure and special requirements. 

7.   Conscientiously maintain correct count of needles, sponges, and instruments with the RN circulator.

8.   Alert to identifying emergency situations and use sound judgment in instituting established procedures.  

9.   Follow correct procedures for gowning and open and closed gloving.

10. Organize the straddle/mayo table with instruments, supplies, and equipment according to the surgical procedure before the surgeon enters the room.   Maintain an orderly instrument table. 

11. Demonstrate a proficiency in surgical draping according to  physician preferences and aseptic guidelines.  

12. Demonstrates competency and understanding of local anesthetics and all solutions used during the procedure that have been placed in the sterile field.  

13. Care for and properly handle cultures and specimens.

14. Properly dispose of all sharps and blades.  

15. Use correct PPE, clean instruments for resterilization.

16. Check all supplies for outdates to assure no unsterile supplies are used.  

17. Willingly and thoroughly restock the operating room to predetermined par levels.  

18. Exercise stewardship by dealing responsibly with hospital resources at all times. 

-Meeting all meets standard.

-Exceeds standard by meeting all standards & participating in two hospital committees.

Mercy Medical Center is a fully accredited Joint Commission, 25-bed Critical Access regional medical facility which serves approximately 60,000 people from Western North Dakota and Eastern Montana.  Mercy Medial Centers is a rural regional center and offers a large array of services.  Mercy hosts a 24-hour Emergency Department, Surgical Services, Cancer Treatment Center (IMRT and BAT), Home Health & Hospice, Renal Dialysis, Rehabilitation Services, sophisticated Imaging Services including MRI and 16-slice CT scanning.  Mercy also has a full service OB program with close to 500 live births annually.  Mercy has a net revenue budget of $40 million.  There are approximately 425 employees at Mercy Medical Center.

 Surgical Services hosts 4 OR suites and provides services for endoscopy, urology, general, orthopedic, OB/GYN, and ENT procedures.  This department has a busy case load that is expected to increase over the next few months.

Mercy also provides clinic services to community. Primary care services are provided through Craven Hagan Clinic with specialty services offered via Mercy Health Partners. These clinics generate approximately $9,000,000 in revenue.  Craven Hagan Clinic employs 6 physicians and 1 physician extender.  Mercy Health Partners offers ENT, General Surgery and Orthopedic services.  Our Occupational Health Clinic also provides services to our growing number of community employers. 

 

In 1996, Mercy Medical Center became part of Catholic Health Initiatives.  Catholic Health Initiatives (CHI) is a faith-inspired national non-profit corporation based in Denver Colorado.  It is comprised of 75 hospitals, 22 Critical Access Hospitals, 40 long-term care, assisted, independent living, and residential facilities serves 68 rural and urban communities, and has more than 66,000 employees.  CHI is spread across 19 states and is untied by a common mission, vision and core values.

 

Mercy Medical Center is located in Williston.  Williston is a growing community in the northwest corner of North Dakota, just 60 miles from the Canadian border and 18 miles from the Montana border.  There are a variety of landmarks and activities in Williston that contribute to a remarkably high quality of life.  The region boasts Fort Union Trading Post National Historic Site, Fort Buford State Historic Site, and the Missouri-Yellowstone River Confluence.  Williston boast many recreational opportunities with miles of paved bike trails, six new USTA regulations size tennis courts, recently constructed large community playground with splash park, a very large recreational lake full of water, some of the best golfing in the country (The Links of North Dakota is ranked one of America’s 100 best modern golf courses), and great opportunities for hunting and fishing.  Williston ranked 24th in a recent edition of Outdoor Life Magazine for being in the “Top 200 Towns for the Outdoorsman.”  Williston is also just an hour away from scenic Theodore Roosevelt National Park North Unit.

 

The Williston community is presently going through a significant economic boom due to the Bakken Oil and Gas Field growth.  North Dakota is one of the few areas in the U.S. where oil production is actually increasing.  A recent Forbes article reported that North Dakota’s oil production is expected to approach 350,000 barrels in 2010.  The unemployment rate is extremely low and the local economy is very strong.  This is expected to continue into the Twenty First Century, so Williston and the surrounding area will continue to grow and develop into the foreseeable future.
