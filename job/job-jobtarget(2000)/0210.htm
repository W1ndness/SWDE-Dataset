Statistical Programmer/Analyst(113550)

Northeastern University

Boston, Massachusetts, United States

Bigger map Smaller map Zoom to Street

This is not an entry-level position.

Job ID: 7378626
Position Title: Statistical Programmer/Analyst(113550)
Industry: Education / Teaching / Administration
Posted: November 15, 2010
Job Function: Other
Job Type: Full-Time

Responsibilities
Experienced statistical programmer/data analyst needed for an exciting new social epidemiology research project, examining how housing policy and neighborhoods influence the health of urban adolescents. 

Responsibilities include data analysis of large multilevel databases in SAS and STATA (and other software programs as needed); executing latent variable models and multilevel multiple regression analysis, and other more sophisticated analysis; coding variables and building regression models. Data analyst and management responsibilities include building multilayer datasets from several large neighborhood and individual databases; compiling residential histories for individuals across time; organizing analytic files; aiding the management of an interdisciplinary collaboration; aiding the writing of reports, presentations, and articles for publication. 

Specific responsibilities include: 
Statistical programming. The programmer will spend most of his/her time executing data analysis in both SAS and STATA (and other software programs as needed); will execute latent variable models and multilevel multiple regression analysis, as well as some more sophisticated analysis; will research new statistical models and run validation analyses as needed; will build analyses with the guidance and supervision of the Principal Investigator and Investigator team; will present and review statistical analysis results with team. 

Data management. Data management responsibilities of programmer includes building multilayer datasets from several large neighborhood and individual databases; compiling residential histories for individuals across time; coding variables and building regression models; organizing analytic files; documenting analysis. 

Assist with writing of publications, presentations and reports. The data programmer will assist with the writing of reports for the funding agency, and contribute to conference presentations and papers to be submitted for publication. 

Management and coordination. Helping to manage and coordinate an interdisciplinary collaboration research team of co-investigators.
Qualifications
Expert statistical programmer/data analyst sought. Extensive experience (7+ years) with statistical programming and data analysis required, including programming in both SAS and STATA, and excellent data management skills for designing and constructing large databases for research analysis. Masters Degree required; Doctoral degree or doctoral training preferred. Desired discipline: epidemiology, biostatistics, public health, quantitative social science, economics, education, or related field. Strong detail orientation required, including keeping projects on timeline. Knowledge of statistical methodology and analysis. Preferably has experience manipulating complex survey designs. Knowledge/experience examining health and mental health is a plus.
Additional Information
One of 2 open positions as a result of collaboration between Health Sciences and the Institute on Urban Health Research. Bouvé College is one of eight colleges at Northeastern University and is housed in the new Behrakis Health Sciences Center.  The College, comprised of three schools, Nursing, Pharmacy and Health Professions, prepares students for health science careers in 5 undergraduate and 29 graduate majors.  The comprehensive array of skills, expertise and professions present in the Bouvé College of Health Sciences are found at few institutions. 

The Department of Health Sciences is home to Northeastern's new MPH Program in Urban Health.  In addition the department consists of an undergraduate program in Health Sciences and a Masters program in Exercise Sciences.  These programs are designed to prepare undergraduate students for post graduate education in medicine, public health, and the health professions by giving them a strong foundation in the basic sciences, liberal arts, and urban/public health.  The Department is enhanced by the presence of collaborating organizations including the Institute on Urban Health Research [IHUR] and the Center for Community Health Education Research and Service (CCHERS) -- a network of 15 academic health centers in Boston.  The University also houses the Northeastern Environmental Justice Research Collaborative. 

IHUR is devoted to knowledge discovery and its practical application to improve personal and public health within urban communities.  The IUHR is particularly focused on understanding the social and environmental conditions of urban living in order to inform public health intervention strategies, policies and professional training. 

Salary will be commensurate with education, training, and experience and includes an outstanding benefits package. Evaluation of candidates will begin immediately and applications will be accepted until the position is filled. 

Interested applicants must completely fill out the on-line application and direct letter of interest and relevant experience to Dr. Theresa Osypuk to upload along with CV or resume and reference list. Information requests about the position can be directed to Vanessa Farias at v.farias@neu.edu.
