This is not an entry-level position.

Job ID: 7354501
Position Title: System Tech I
Job Function: Technican
Posted: November 5, 2010
Job Type: Full-Time
Contact Person: Megan Rutherford
Email Address:

Position Summary:  Supports and facilitates the customer focused activities of all customer contact personnel by performing various field technical work involving routine maintenance and repair of CATV trunk and distribution systems, including activation and proof of new-build areas.  Is involved with system troubleshooting due to equipment or power failure; assist with training and guidance of Installers, Installer Technicians and Service Technicians.  System Tech positions filling a night or weekend shift are eligible for a shift differential pay of $1.00/hour.

 

Essential Duties and Responsibilities:

  • Performs routine maintenance on power supplies: Including testing, changing out batteries when needed and bi-annual physical inspection and cleaning of the cabinet and power supply modules.
  • Sets forward and return trunk levels, sets-up and changes out trunk amplifiers, calculates signal level losses in cable and equipment for trunk and distribution lines
  • Assumes full responsibility for troubleshooting and maintenance of the entire trunk and distribution system, including Fiber Optic Cable, Fiber Nodes, forward and return amplifiers, coax cable, couplers, splitters, and taps. Also assisting in trouble shooting Escalated Customer premise issues up to the house box/MDU.
  • Uses OTDR, TDR, Spectrum Analyzer, FSM, Digital Test Equipment, Stealth Sweep Receiver and Cable Fault Locator throughout the scope of duties. Occasionally uses waveform monitor and frequency counter when testing / proofing Headend performance.
  • Conducts system tests such as: carrier/noise, hum modulation, 24 hour tests, forward and return sweep, return ingress levels, and fiber transmitter and receiver operation. Performs all FCC mandated Proof of Performance tests.
  • Reads and understands system design maps. Assists and effects interface for required revisions to strand and design maps.
  • Prepares estimates for projects, providing designs and bill of materials.
  • Is responsible for project through completion, including monitoring contractor progress up to and including processing of invoices from contractors to assure Company specifications are met.
  • Performs customary CATV aerial and underground construction, including installation of U.G. cable, vaults, pedestals or equipment used to house CATV equipment. Locates existing U.G. cable, identifies bad cable with use of a TDR or fault locator. Performs aerial construction including make-ready, pulling and lashing cables, placing all passive and active devices found in trunk and distribution. Sets up GNA and Line Extenders, including calculating pads and equalizers as required, using a field strength meter. Assists with fiber construction and initial forward fiber optic receiver and return fiber optic transmitter splicing and setup.
  • Participates in emergency fiber restorations, including fiber preparation, fusion splicing and planning permanent repairs.
  • Keeps records and renders reports relative to system operations as required
  • Perform System Standby duties in the On Call rotation. During this 7 day period the tech must be available 24 hours a day to respond to system outages and or degradation of service issues in the plant.
  • Responsible for monitoring and repairing CLI leaks within assigned maintenance area/entire plant to reduce customer impacting ingress and maintain greater than 98% on annual CLI flyover.
  • May be required to participate in early morning hour cutover projects to lessen the customer impacting service interruptions during peak hours.
  • Keep up with rapidly changing technology and services by continually developing technical skills through industry organization memberships, completing new certifications, and vendor specific training
  • Collaborate and communicate with other departments to ensure customer satisfaction. Works closely with various technical, sales, and engineering teams. Guide and mentor teammates in Business Services and cross departmentally.
  • Demonstrate high ethical standards, strong work ethic with an emphasis on team commitment, show respect for others, acknowledge and build on others ideas, and support group decisions
  • Keep accurate records and renders reports relative to system operations on or before deadlines
  • Other functions that may be assigned

Preferred Prerequisites:

  • 2 to 4 year degree in electronics or a related subject or equivalent training through military experience in radios and electronics, trade schools, and programs offered by equipment and software manufacturers
  • 3 or more years progressive system tech experience and certifications in the cable and telecommunications industry; will consider 3 or more years progressive service tech experience and certifications
  • Must complete one of the following certification options:
    • A) SCTE Broadband Distribution (BDS) Certification, or;
    • B) NCTI Service Technician Course
  • Satisfactory passing of practical knowledge exam administered by the employer demonstrating practical ability to perform the required skills of the classification
  • Strong judgment, decision making, and critical thinking skills
  • Advanced written, verbal and customer relations skills with acute degree of social perceptiveness
  • Must be highly organized and manage time well
  • Must be team oriented and customer focused
  • Intrinsic motivation; understands and supports company goals
  • Understanding of computers preferred (PC and/or MacIntosh), including DOS, Internet, IE4.0, Excel and Word programs
  • Ability to interpret and correctly fill-out various work order forms provided by the company
  • Strong work ethic with an emphasis in team commitment a must
  • Valid driver's license with satisfactory driving record
  • Successful criminal background and reference check is required
  • Final applicants are required to take and satisfactorily pass a pre-employment drug test within 24 hours of notification

Physical Requirements:

The work environment characteristics described here are representative of those an associate encounters while performing the essential functions of this position. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions.

  • Position requires frequent walking, bending, crawling, and reaching above shoulder level
  • Frequently lifts and carries 10-70lbs
  • Occasional climbing of ladders and gaffing poles while utilizing proper safety equipment
  • Frequently drives a company vehicle; Occasional use of bucket truck
  • Occasionally works within limited confines such as crawl spaces and attic
