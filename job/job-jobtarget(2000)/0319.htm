About Framingham State University:

Framingham State University is a vibrant, comprehensive liberal arts institution offering 25 undergraduate degree programs in arts, humanities, sciences, social sciences, and professional fields. Nearly 6,000 students attend Framingham State, including nearly 2,000 graduate students. Framingham State University offers graduate degrees in 23 fields, including an extensive graduate program for teachers in international schools. The University takes pride in its 28,000 alumni, most of whom live and work in Massachusetts. The University is just the right size - small enough for learning to be personal and collaborative, yet large and diverse enough to broaden students' understanding of their world. This broadening of perspectives and appreciation of diversity is one of the hallmarks of a first-rate undergraduate education.

Job Description:

Manage Office of Institutional Research and synchronize full spectrum of data reporting internally and to federal, state, and private education agencies. Establish, sustain, and assume responsibility for consistency, accuracy, relevancy, and timeliness of institutional data to all consumers, including President, senior administrative staff, sister institutions and the Massachusetts Department of Higher Education, and state and federal agency reporting agencies. Enforce data standards, source selection, business practices, and reporting methodologies across the University. Analyze state, regional, and national competitive peer institution statistics. Increase faculty and staff understanding of student demographic, persistence, and engagement trends. Oversee NEASC review in 2013. This position is accountable for ensuring that affirmative action, equal opportunity and diversity are integrally tied to all actions and decisions in areas of responsibility.

Requirements:

Required qualifications include five years minimum of relevant work experience in institutional research, with three years in higher education; master’s degree in a related field; excellent interpersonal skills with ability to collaborate with staff across campus; excellent project management and organizational skills and familiarity with US Department of Education IPEDS reporting requirements

Preferred qualifications include experience with NEASC or other regional accreditation boards; proficiency in Microsoft Office Suite; familiarity with Commonwealth of Massachusetts public higher education system and reporting requirements; familiarity with Banner and Banner-related electronic reporting program software; experience with database management and knowledge of quantitative and qualitative research and evaluation methods and statistical package software such as SPSS

Additional Information:

This is a full time, benefited position.

Framingham State University is an affirmative action/equal opportunity employer. Members of underrepresented groups and those committed to working in a diverse cultural environment are encouraged to apply.

Application Instructions:

Please apply online by submitting a resume, cover letter, and contact information for three references.
