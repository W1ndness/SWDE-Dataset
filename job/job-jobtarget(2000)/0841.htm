Position Summary

The Director of Member and Guest Services is responsible for the design, development, implementation, and supervision of the Member and Guest Services Department. The priorities are two-fold: 1) the planning, management, and implementation of a comprehensive membership program that provides a stable base of membership support; and 2) the planning, management, and implementation of the guest services unit that provides excellent customer service. Responsibilities include the effective training and management of key membership and guest services staff for a seamless front of house operation that ensures a welcoming experience and proper on-site servicing of members and guests. The Director will prepare and manage the departmental budget, annual projections, reports on visitation and membership trends, and ensure accurate and timely management of revenue, receipts, and reporting. This position will hire and supervise the Member and Guest Services Department staff and oversee a large team of volunteers. The position requires proven personnel management and customer service skills, strategic planning expertise, and marketing experience. The ability to work in collaboration with administrators, staff, and volunteers and to communicate effectively with members and guests is essential. This position reports to the Deputy Director of Museum Relations.


Job Requirements

Principal Responsibilities (Essential Functions)

  • Define and administer strategies for building, launching and maintaining the inaugural membership program including strategies to identify and solicit member prospects; draft departmental action plans including financial goals; and design of members’ benefit structure.
  • Work with Marketing Services to plan and implement membership campaigns (including but not limited to direct mail, renewal, on-site) in a timely, creative and strategic manner.
  • Manage and maintain Raiser’s Edge membership database, including list management, data entry, merges, segmentation, and list orders.
  • Create engaging and effective membership opportunities and benefits that attract new members and retain existing members.
  • Design and build the Member and Guest Service Department by effectively hiring, supervising, and evaluating well-qualified staff. Work with team to create a positive guest experience at the Museum through the consistent and reliable use of effective communication, accurate information and gracious hospitality. Demonstrate a high level of professional courtesy in all interactions with members, visitors, and co-workers. Train and lead all staff in the use of proven techniques to properly service guests, promote membership, address issues and solve problems.
  • Oversee admissions administration and determine the levels of staff and volunteers required to service guests based on projected visitation.
  • Work with Marketing Services to generate member and on-site guest marketing materials and communications. Coordinate and manage Museum representation and membership promotions at community and civic events, as well as onsite Museum events. Supervise the planning and execution of member events.
  • Maintain a thorough understanding of all levels of membership and each level’s respective benefits. Uphold and sustain policies tied to membership benefits.
  • Keep abreast current and future exhibitions, events, educational programs, lectures, etc., and create efficient ways to clearly relay information to members and guests.
  • Coordinate volunteer needs and duties relating to guest services and membership.
  • Provide analysis and reporting of visitation trends, sales, promotions, and projections to the Deputy Director of Museum Relations.
  • Manage department’s operating budget and provide regular department financial reports.
  • Work collaboratively with Museum Relations leadership and department managers to deliver excellent service to the Museum staff, members and guests; work with other museum staff to effectively integrate membership into related museum strategies.

Minimum Qualifications

Education, Training, and Traits:

  • Bachelor’s degree from an accredited institution of higher education
  • Ability to use good judgment, focus attention on detail, and demonstrate skills in organization and recruitment required
  • Must be able to work under pressure and able to work in a fast-paced environment
  • Ability to understand and maintain the highest levels of confidentiality
  • Highest ethics as they relate to all aspects of museum practices
  • Knowledge of museum operations is preferred
  • Academic training and/or specific work experience in marketing, customer service and promotions preferred.
  • Belief in the Museum as a cause for social good and a positive, dynamic force for economic development

Work Experience:

  • Five or more years of senior management experience
  • Five years of membership-related experience
  • Previous sales/customer service experience required with demonstrated outstanding customer relations skills.
  • Supervisory experience
  • Proven experience in cash management and budgetary accountability.
  • Working knowledge of development, marketing, public relations and museum operations preferred.
  • Volunteer management experience preferred

In regard to education and experience, an equivalent combination of relevant education and/or experience will satisfy the minimum requirements.

Licenses and Certifications:

  • Valid driver’s license

Skills and Abilities:

  • Creative, energetic attitude with an aptitude for innovation
  • Strong and effective written, verbal, and listening communication skills and a demonstrated ability to communicate clearly and professionally.
  • Strong public speaking skills
  • Demonstrated organizational planning, problem-solving and collaboration skills
  • Demonstrated understanding and use of diplomacy
  • Cash management
  • Proficient computer skills in MS Word, Excel, and PowerPoint. Database and additional software knowledge preferred.

Physical Demands and Work Environment:
The physical demands and work environment characteristics described here are representative of those that must be met by an employee to successfully perform the essential functions of this position. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions.

  • Physical demands: While performing the duties of this job, the employee is required to travel independently (approximately 25% of time) within museum office and spaces, construction site and in the communities served. In the work environments described below, position requires verbal and written conversation with others, sitting, reaching, lifting/moving objects up to 10 pounds, and use of hands to finger, handle, or feel objects, tools, or controls and the ability to move throughout the museum and grounds regularly. Vision abilities required by the job include close vision.
  • Work Environment: Work will be performed in an office environment, museum spaces, construction site, and in the communities served. Occasional overnight travel is required. While performing the duties of this job, the employee is exposed to weather conditions prevalent at the time. The noise level in the work environment is usually low to moderate.


Compensation
Crystal Bridges offers competitive salary and a comprehensive benefits package.

If submitting by mail, please reference Director of Member and Guest Services position opening. No phone calls please.
