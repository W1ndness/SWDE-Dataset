School of Business Administration

Department of Decision and Information Sciences

Special Instructor of MIS (Position #989191)

The School of Business Administration at Oakland University seeks to fill a Special Instructor of MIS position. The person who fills this position will teach Management Information Systems courses at the Undergraduate level, provide counseling for the students enrolled in the Masters of Science in Information Technology Management program, and assist in the administration of the Experiential Learning and Innovation program, a graduate internship program. The normal teaching load is seven/eight classes per academic year. Class sizes are moderate. Summer teaching is available.

The position begins August 15, 2011. To be considered, applications must be submitted by December 15, 2010.

If interested, please email an application package that includes your resume, evidence of teaching effectiveness, and three letters of recommendation to Professor Mark Isken at isken@oakland.edu

Compensation

Oakland University offers a competitive salary and excellent benefits including University contribution to TIAA/CREF and medical and dental insurance.

The School of Business Administration

The School of Business Administration (SBA) is AACSB accredited at both the undergraduate and graduate levels. It has 1,600 undergraduate and 550 graduate students. The business school building opened in August, 2000, incorporates state-of-the-art information technology and has several computerized instructional classrooms. For more information about the School of Business Administration see www.sba.oakland.edu

Oakland University

Oakland University is a state-assisted institution that enrolls over 19,000 students and offers a wide range of majors in the arts, sciences, education, engineering, health services, and business. It is located 25 miles north of downtown Detroit in Rochester, Michigan. Rochester has 75,000 residents, the characteristics of a small town, and provides an excellent family environment. For more information about Oakland University see www.oakland.edu .

                              

Oakland University is an affirmative action/equal opportunity employer and encourages applications from women and minorities.


Job Requirements

Required Qualifications

  • M.S. in Information Systems, MBA, or equivalent
  • Evidence of effective teaching of information systems courses at the undergraduate level
  • Knowledge of management information systems concepts
  • Ability to develop curricular materials

Desired Qualifications

  • Relevant professional experience
  • Experience with online teaching and course management software such as Moodle, Elluminate, and myITlab
  • Experience supervising student organizations
  • Experience as an academic administrator
  • Ability to collaborate effectively with other faculty members
  • Experience interacting with business professionals
  • Ability to mentor new part-time instructors
  • Ability to communicate effectively to a variety of audiences
