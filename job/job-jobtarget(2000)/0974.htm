Health Care Policy and Costs Expert

McGill Institute for Health and Social Policy

Montreal, Quebec, Canada

Bigger map Smaller map Zoom to Street

This is not an entry-level position.

Job ID: 7369822
Position Title: Health Care Policy and Costs Expert
Min Education: Master's: MPA, MPP, MPM, etc.
Industry: Health
Posted: November 11, 2010
Job Function: Research/Analysis
Job Type: Full-Time

Position Context:

The Institute for Health and Social Policy at McGill University is working to build the first centre of comparative data on health care costs and quality in the world’s leading economies.  This initiative will provide information on national investments intended to address social determinants of health, encourage public population health approaches to prevention while also evaluating the cost-effectiveness of medical care.  The goal of this data centre is to markedly enhance our ability to analyze which approaches to population health and health care most effectively combine cost control with high quality health outcomes.

 

A team of researchers will help build the data centre and carry out a longitudinal analysis of health care costs that is comparative across provinces and countries, looks at historical trends in different sectors within health care, and examines projections for the future.

 

The Health Care Policy and Costs Expert will lead the longitudinal analysis of health care costs and quality, as well as cross-national medical care policy analysis for the world’s leading economies. In doing so, he/she will guide an exciting and unique effort to build this resource to understand what all leading economies are doing to provide quality health care, while controlling medical costs. The Health Care Policy and Costs Expert will play a vital role at the Institute in enhancing health and social policy research and policy capacity at McGill, as well as in translating our research findings into action at the global, national and local levels.  

 

Key Responsibilities:

  • Collaborate on project conceptualization and guide building of a longitudinal comparative database on health care costs;
  • Provide content expertise on health care systems and policies as well as health care expenditures during project conceptualization, construction and analysis;
  • Analyse the relationship among medical care, public health, and social policies and health outcomes;
  • Supervise a team of analysts;
  • Disseminate and translate findings to policymakers;

 


Job Requirements

Education/Experience:

Graduate degree in public health, health economics, health policy, or related discipline.   At least five years of relevant work experience in health policy or health economics. All applicants must have substantive knowledge and understanding of health care systems, expenditures, and costs and familiarity with data analysis. Strong writing and English language proficiency required. Preferred experiences include: Experience working with or understanding of government budgets; experience with Microsoft Access and/or statistical software such as Stata or SAS; and experience managing a team. French and/or other language proficiency an asset. 

 

To apply, send a cover letter, CV, 2 research articles, and contact information (telephone and email) for 3 references to the attention of the Search Committee at the Institute for Health and Social Policy, 1130 Pine Avenue West, Montreal, QC, H3A 1A3, or to applicants.ihsp@mcgill.ca.  Electronic submission is encouraged.  Please specify “Health Care Policy and Costs Expert” in the subject heading. 

 

Review of applications will begin November 30, 2010. Only complete applications will be considered and only candidates selected for an interview or to gather additional information will be contacted.

 

All qualified applicants are encouraged to apply; however, Canadians and permanent residents will be given priority.
