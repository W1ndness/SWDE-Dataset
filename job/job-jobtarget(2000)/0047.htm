The Great Atlantic & Pacific Tea Company, Inc

Montvale, New Jersey, United States

Position Title: Director of Treasury Services

POSITION SUMMARY:

Provides strategic planning and technical direction and management on all treasury issues including corporate finance and capital market transactions, treasury policies and procedures, treasury systems and operations, corporate cash management and cash forecasting, debt management, credit risk, investments, real estate transactions and insurance. Develops and maintains credit and banking relationships to support strategic objectives and treasury operations. Provides ongoing communication with the investment community and prepares quarterly earnings press releases and presentations for all bank and analyst conferences and meetings.

 

KEY ACCOUNTABILITIES:

  • Evaluates and executes corporate finance and capital market transactions including debt and equity issuance, real estate financing, lease transactions and hedging arrangements; provides support for financing strategies through quantitative analysis and integrated financial modeling, presentation development and due diligence management.
  • Prepares reports and analysis for quarterly Finance Committee meetings of the Board of Directors.
  • Prepares financial forecasts and analyses including pro-forma financial statements, cost of capital, covenant analysis, and buy vs. lease analysis.
  • Develops and controls budget for net interest and bank expenses.
  • Evaluates treasury procedures and systems, and develops project plans to enhance operational efficiencies and integrity of the processes.  Resolves critical business issues and initiates treasury process and infrastructure improvements.
  • Oversees all cash management functions; including short-term investments, bank borrowings, cash concentration and disbursements, forecasting and reporting, and performs liquidity analysis.
  • Designs and manages strategic changes in cash management systems and services and related financial systems; manages the effective implementation of new treasury software products and evaluates new product offerings for their benefit to A&P.
  • Develops cash flow metrics and drives the continuous improvement of the metrics to secure the best borrowing and investment rates.
  • Manages banking relationships and negotiates pricing; evaluates service levels and systems, reviews banking fees, measures performance, and continuously identifies opportunities to streamline procedures, improve processes, reduce expenses, and upgrade technology.
  • Provides Treasury support to the Human Resources department for defined benefit and defined contribution plans and monitors fund management performance.
  • Develops and maintains positive relationships within the financial community, including regular communication with the stock exchange, rating agencies, institutional investors, individual investors and research analysts. Drafts Q&A’s and associated analysis for quarterly earnings releases. Ensures consistent communications with the investment community.
  • Remains up-to-date on industry trends, current events, perceptions of A&P within the market and shares knowledge, information, and investment community feedback with executive management.
  • Assists with the management of the Investor Relations website content and message.
  • Assists the VP, Treasury with various insurance related financial analysis.
  • ·        Oversees the selection, hiring, training, and development of the Treasury and Cash Management team and manages the Performance Management process for direct reports. Communicates performance objectives and manages to performance expectations for staff.  Manages a succession plan for the department.
  • Exhibits the highest degree of personal and professional integrity at all times, consistent with the Company’s Core Values.

                        Measures of effective written and verbal communication

                        Measures of effective Succession Planning for department

                        Measures of improvement in cash flow metrics

                        Measures of improvement in bank processes and service levels.

SPECIAL SKILLS, TRAINING, OR EXPERIENCE:

  • Bachelor’s Degree in Finance, Accounting, or Economics.   MBA preferred.
  • 5-9 years of Cash Management or Corporate Treasury experience
  • Extensive knowledge in banking practices, cash management, and treasury operations and all applicable rules and regulations.
  • Knowledge of capital markets and investment instruments
  • Advanced proficiency in Microsoft Office products and Oracle
  • Proficiency in Treasury management systems and bank software products
  • Excellent written and verbal communication and presentation skills.

CORE MANAGEMENT LEADERSHIP COMPETENCIES:

  1. Situational Leadership: Matches leadership style to the needs of individuals and teams; delegates and provides guidance and coaching accordingly.
  2. Motivating Others: Connects with and inspires others to perform at their best. Instills the desire to win in others. Establishes a challenging, stimulating, productive, fair work environment that supports both individual and team accomplishment. Recognizes others who take initiative and act with urgency. Makes things happen in the organization.
  3. Performance Management: Sets high performance for her/himself and her/his team members and holds those standards. Clearly communicates roles, responsibilities and expectations. Provides direct, candid performance feedback to her/his team in a balanced, constructive manner. Balances being demanding with being supportive. Rewards excellent performance and moves with deliberate speed to take action with under-performers.
  4. Talent Development: Creates a learning culture by supporting associate training and development, and encouraging associates to be self-directed learners. Demonstrates an accurate understanding of all of her/his team members’ strengths and development needs. Seeks out opportunities to stretch, grow and promote talent.
  5. Two-Way Communication: openly shares information and creates an inclusive work environment by seeking input, listening and demonstrating respect for others. Tailors language and messages to better connect with others’ communication styles. Encourages an active and open exchange of ideas with others, and invites and responds to others’ questions. Uses professional language with others.
  6. Change Leadership: Leads change efforts through careful attention to stakeholders and the impact on people. Assist people through change; build their understanding, skills and confidence so they can be successful in the new environment. Addresses resistance by clearly communicating the change, explaining that it means to people and engaging them in the change wherever possible. Models flexibility, adaptability and patience in and with ambiguous situations, responding positively to new demands and challenges.
  7. Business Acumen: Demonstrates sound knowledge of "the business," including general business concepts, the grocery industry, and her/his functional area.  Appropriately prioritizes business problems.  Focuses the organization on what brings the most value to stakeholders.  Understands and utilizes a range of levers and options available to achieve success and build shareholder value.  Monitors the market place and capitalizes on business or industry trends.
  8. Problem Solving and Decision Making: approaches problem using appropriate facts and information, and makes sounds decisions, taking into account both short- and long-term implications. Thinks nimbly and flexibly, envisioning multiple solutions to business issues. Determines the root causes behind issues and develops process improvements to eliminate or decrease recurring problems.
  9. Drive For Results: Aggressively drives for outcomes that are beneficial to multiple stakeholders (shareholders, customers, associates) to positively EVA. Focuses on the critical factors that drive productivity, revenue growth and bottom line results. Demonstrates a sustained track record of consistent, verifiable results that balance the need for short-term performance with a view towards long-term success. Actively seeks out new or better ways of doing things to achieve results more efficiently and effectively.
  10. Total Store Perspective: Address issues and problem with a corporate-wide perspective. Seeks to expand her/his knowledge about the A&P enterprise. Optimizes company performance by consistently and effectively executing company programs. Collaborates with other and readily shares ideas and best practices to help drive overall company success. Competes with external companies, not internal colleagues.

 

CORE COMPANY VALUES:

  1. Think Customer First: Our Customers are the reason we’re in business. So we set priorities based on the question: “How will this serve them?” We owe Customers our very best efforts. That means doing whatever it takes to keep them coming back. When we interact with them, we make Customers feel Welcome, Important, and Appreciated. For some of us, the “Customer” is our stores. We treat fellow Associates with the dignity and respect deserving of any member of the A&P family.
  2. Integrity: People want to do business with those they can trust. To earn that trust, we are true to our word and keep our promises. We are honest to a fault. We tell the truth, even when it hurts. Because we hold ourselves to the highest ethical standard in our words and deeds, and we welcome scrutiny from our business partners, our shareholders, and the general public. We are committed to being an upstanding corporate citizen in the communities we serve.
  3. Accountability: Each of us makes a daily contribution to the success of our Company. In that sense, we are all owners. We show our pride in ownership by taking responsibility for all aspects of our work. We stay accountable until the work is complete. Each Associate has the responsibility to look, see, and act to make our Company stronger. When we see a problem, we not only fix the problem — we fix the cause. We know our Customers, know our numbers, and reach our goals.
  4. Sense of Urgency: We act as if our livelihoods depend on it. Because in the retail world, they do. We marshall our resources, analyze information, and make decisions as quickly as possible. We act with urgency — at the “speed of retail” — to bring our ideas to life.
  5. Culture of Continuous Improvement: Change is constant. To stay profitable, we must constantly evolve our thinking to adapt to and — wherever possible — drive change. Our pursuit of excellence is relentless. We continually seek out the best practices that will give us the competitive edge. A culture of continuous improvement starts with the individual. We are a Company of responsible learners who continually seek out fresh ideas and approaches. Building our knowledge base and sharing it with others will help our Company innovate and grow.
  6. Teamwork: We work as a team, because while any individual idea or effort may be great, collaboration will only make it better. The most important job of any manager is to develop people to be experts and leaders themselves. This is how we will build strong teams. Company leaders keep an “open door” to suggestions and new ideas from Associates. It’s more fun to win. When a job is well done, we celebrate successes and share in the rewards — together.
