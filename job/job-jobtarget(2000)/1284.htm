Min Education: BA/BS/Undergraduate

The Brookings Institution is a private nonprofit organization devoted to independent research and innovative policy solutions. Established in 1916, Brookings analyzes current and emerging issues and produces new ideas that matter - for the nation and the world.

 

Brookings seeks to fill the position of Assistant Director of Financial Planning and Systems.

 

TO APPLY and for Complete Job Description: http://www.brookings.edu/about/employment/FM10165.aspx

 

Basic Function:

 

Drives, develops, and manages the business planning and budgeting function of a 90+ million dollar, nonprofit operation; responsible for preparation and day-to-day management and oversight of the Institution’s capital, operating, department (program), and project budgets; and drives the annual budget process comprised of developing planning assumptions, compiling budget submissions, and developing analysis surrounding both operations and capital requirements.

 

Note: This position reports to the Director of Planning and Analysis and works closely with the VP and CFO. The Office of Planning and Analysis is responsible for the integration and oversight of Brookings decision support comprised of financial planning, budgeting, forecasting, reporting, and analysis, as well as the tracking of interdisciplinary and strategic initiatives.

 

Education/Experience Requirements:

 

- Bachelor’s degree in Accounting, Finance, or Business Administration required; MBA or CPA preferred.

- Minimum 8 years work experience required, with 3 to 5 years of proven experience developing organization-wide operating and capital budgets, and successfully managing the budget of a department or organization of similar size.

- Experience in a nonprofit organization, preferably a research organization or university/academic institution preferred.

- Experience defining and institutionalizing budget processes within an organization, and demonstrated leadership managing a budget organization through significant growth required.

- Experience assessing, evaluating, selecting, and implementing financial planning, budgeting, and performance reporting software required.

- Experience with identifying critical information needs of both organization and department management for decision support, pinpointing the sources of information and utilizing the financial systems or chosen technologies to present data into meaningful management reports required.

- Experience with PeopleSoft preferred.

 

Knowledge Requirements:

 

- Solid working knowledge of budgets and accounting principles; must have a strong understanding of GAAP and fund accounting, as well as a strong understanding of how business transactions are recorded into the financial records of an organization.

- Strong interpersonal, organizational, and critical thinking skills; keen attention to detail.

- Ability to document the processes, quality control data, and to analyze and troubleshoot problems, and to communicate effectively with all levels of staff throughout the organization.

- Strong technical skills in Windows; proficiency in Microsoft Office (Excel, Access, Word, PowerPoint).

- Experience with Visual Basic for Applications (VBA) managing an Excel based budget process with integrated VBA macros.

- Ability to develop and manage databases and develop reporting from large datasets, including building Pivot Tables.

- Strong analytical, problem-solving, written and oral communication skills, as well as customer service orientation and positive attitude.

 

Principal Duties and Responsibilities:

 

40% Prepares, Manages, and Oversees Institution's Budget:

 

- Assists with development and maintenance of a comprehensive planning calendar capturing financial closes, planning activity, and Brookings management meetings as well as Board and Board Committee meetings.

- Acts as principal support to development and implementation of the Budgeting and Reporting System (BRS).

- Holds primary responsibility for maintenance of BRS ensuring its full functionality at all times.

- Updates templates in BRS prior to budgeting and forecasting season kick-off utilizing VBA macros.

- Supports programs during budget and forecast updates, ensuring timely and accurate submission of project budgets by the programs into BRS.

Works closely with the ITS department to troubleshoot issues encountered by users of BRS.

- Consolidates program budgets and ensures budget process integrates program plans and budgets into Institutional planning, forecasting, and budgeting, in order to assure transparency between program and Institutional levels.

- Ensures Accounting maintains actual planning rates in the financial system.

Provides oversight of program business management staff in regularly monitoring program budgets, forecasts, and actual results with variance explanations.

- Prepares effective reports and analysis to monitor performance of operations and capital expenditures against budget plans, including business insight into any resulting variances to those plans.

- Seeks continuous improvements in operating procedures and internal controls in order to minimize the operational risk.

 

30% Reports and Reporting:

 

- Produces performance reports which incorporate historical trends, current year, actual results, project to-date status, and any variances to budgets.

- Incorporates in budget reports financial and operational data, status of fundraising, revenue, operating expense, capital expenditures, major projects/initiatives, employment, etc.

- Spearheads report development at Institutional, program, and/or major project level.

- Produces all relevant financial materials in preparation for presentation to Executive Office, Steering Committee, Board of Trustees, Board Committees, and external entities (e.g. Bond trustees, rating agencies, foundations).

 

30% Other Duties and Responsibilities:

 

- Leads regular financial review meetings with program business management staff for the purpose of: (1) instilling accountability and controls at the program level; (2) connecting the financial operations of the programs to the Institutional budgeting and planning process; and (3) providing financial transparency between the Institutional and program levels.

- Develops financial models for decision support, setting assumptions, and business analysis.

- Researches and recommends effective tools and models to enhance the capture of budget data as well as continually seeking opportunities to enhance and streamline the budget and/or planning process.

- Works to build a community of program business management professionals through improved recruiting, training, communication, and career development opportunities for business staff.

 

Only applicants meeting minimum qualifications for the position will be considered. No phone calls please. Brookings welcomes resumes from all qualified applicants, particularly women and minorities.
