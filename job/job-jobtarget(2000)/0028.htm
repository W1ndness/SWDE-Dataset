Chicago, Illinois, USA

This is not an entry-level position.

Job ID: 7388358
Position Title: Product Support Analyst
Min Education: BA/BS/Undergraduate
Posted: November 18, 2010
Job Function: Other
Job Type: Full-Time

The American Theological Library Association (ATLA), a high-tech professional association of theological libraries & librarians that produces electronic products in all fields of theology and religion seeks qualified applicants for a full-time Product Support Analyst position and for a full-time Digitization and Product Support Associate to join the Department of Electronic Products and Services (EPS). 

(For more information about the other open position in the department, the Digitization and Product Support Associate position, please visit our listing at: http://www.atla.com/member/job_openings.html#ATLA_Product_Associate).

 Essential Duties:

  • Performs quality control tests on MARC21 index records, digital files, and other ATLA products or data. Collects pertinent data and performs corresponding analyses to ensure timely detection of quality concerns and identification of their causes and coordinates the tracking of quality control analyses for each product to ensure consistency with each product release and with ATLA standards while meeting firm deadlines.
  • Proactively works with database aggregators and partners on implementation, data, product content / information, and customer support issues.
  • Tracks, analyzes, and troubleshoots support issues and devises prompt, accurate and strategic answers to ATLA product inquiries from customers; database users; and partner data producers, publishers, and database aggregators.
  • Creates documentation, training materials, user guides, title lists, FAQs, and other materials about ATLA products and data releases.
  • Prepares and ensures delivery of data and product according to production schedules.
  • Collaborates with other ATLA staff to improve the quality of products and to invent and implement improved methods for supporting ATLA products.
  • Serves as administrator of ATLA product accounts for ATLA staff, partner data producers, and partner publishers.

 

Salary and Benefits: 
ATLA is an equal opportunity employer located in downtown Chicago, offering a competitive salary, excellent benefits, work/life balance, and a collegial culture. For more information about ATLA, please visit our web site at www.atla.com <http://www.atla.com>.


Job Requirements

 Qualifications:

      Education & Experience:

  • Bachelor's degree with minimum of three years of professional experience required.
  • Extensive writing skills (documentation, fact sheets, user guides, tutorials, etc); proofreading / quality control / editing skills required. Writing samples will be required and a proofreading/quality control exercise will be administered during interviews.
  • Quality control / assurance experience a plus.
  • Technical support, reference, bibliographic instruction experience, or experience in a public-facing role a plus, especially experience working with database aggregators or supporting products and services for libraries, librarians, scholars, students, clergy, or laypeople.
  • Familiarity with features, functionality and administration of database aggregator products for the library market a plus.
  • Advanced degree or work towards advanced Library & Information Science or related degree from ALA-accredited institution a plus.     

 

Technical Skills:

  • Experience and proficiency with computers and new technology, including intermediate skills with Microsoft Programs such as Word, Outlook, Excel required.
  • Experience creating and executing quality control tests; experience with automated software or software testing methodologies and repeatable processes; experience running or creating perl scripts; experience running software for analysis of MARC21 datasets such as MARC Report a plus.
  • Familiarity with library and/or digital publishing standards / guidelines such as MARC21, XML, PDF, Unicode, KBART, OpenURL a plus.
  • Experience editing web content via SharePoint or other CMS a plus.

 

Successful Candidate Will Have These Qualities:

  • Conscientious and meticulous, with a proven ability to operate at a consistently high level producing quality, detailed, accurate and timely work in an atmosphere with multiple firm deadlines.
  • Effective interpersonal and diplomatic skills, and strong verbal, telephone, listening, and communications skills.
  • Ability to understand and communicate complex technical issues to a non-technical audience.
  • Highly motivated and strong customer service orientation, including interest in supporting, improving and developing products for libraries, library patrons, and other users of religion and theology materials.

 

TO APPLY: 
Review of applications will begin on November 29th and continue until the position is filled.  Send a resume and a cover letter with career goals and salary expectations via e-mail to PSA@atla.com.  Required: Also include as an attachment a short (less than 5 pages) academic, business or marketing writing sample or a link to the material.

 No phone calls or recruiters please.
