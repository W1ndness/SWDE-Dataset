Position Summary

 

The Director of Public Affairs plans and directs the work of the Public Affairs department staff and is responsible for establishing the objectives and priorities of the department.

 

Essential Duties and Responsibilities include the following. Other duties may be assigned.

The duties listed here are typical examples of the work performed; not all duties assigned are included, nor is it expected that all similar positions will be assigned every duty.

 

Develops and maintains a proactive marketing and communications program that positions the First 5 LA as a leader in the field of children and family issues in LA County.[mm1] 

 

Develops and implements communications and media strategies that successfully deliver information and key messages to the public.

 

Develops and maintains a strong and productive relationship with both print and electronic media;  educates the media on the organization and its strategies.

 

Develops and executes public information strategies relative to new or developing programs and advises the Chief Executive Officer and Chief of Program on the public-relations aspects of such programs.

 

Develops and implements effective paid and earned media strategies.

 

Ensures that all special events are planned to capture the attention of the intended audience and the media.  Manages the production of each event ensuring all appropriate collaborations and/or sponsorships are achieved and managed in a creative, timely and cost-effective manner.

 

Evaluates communication activities and recommends future actions.

 

Develops strategic relationships with other Proposition 10 Commissions in order to support First 5 LA’s marketing and communication strategies and goals.

 

Plans and administers the department’s budget. 

 

Supervises the RFP/RFQ protocol process to ensure compliance with organizational policy.

 

Serves as the spokesperson for First 5 LA, and the Chief Executive as assigned.

 

Oversees First 5 LA’s electronic marketing efforts, including the supervision of web site design and maintenance.

 

Performs research, compiles data and writes reports, talking points and speeches for the Chief Executive Officer, members of the Senior Staff and other Commission members as needed..

 

Provides on-going support and media training to the management team and staff as needed.  Ensures management has the skills and confidence to deliver appropriate messages during interviews with the media or in presentation to the public.

 

Keeps abreast of changing demographics and other relevant issues in order to evaluate marketing and communications activities and to monitor emerging issues.

 

Manages the use of external resources (e.g. vendors, consultants) in keeping with organizational policy and in compliance with regulations.

 

Develops and conducts strategies for program communication for parents, the public and stakeholders taking LA County’s diverse communities into consideration.

 

Supervises the production of all collateral materials related to First 5 LA’s communication’s strategies which include newsletters, pamphlets, brochures and annual reports.

 

Represents the Commission at meetings, conferences and other public events.

 

 

 

Supervisory Responsibilities

Directly supervises 14 employees in the Public Affairs Department, including 4 subordinate managers.  Carries out supervisory responsibilities in accordance with the organization’s policies and applicable laws.  Responsibilities include interviewing, hiring and training employees; planning, assigning, and directing work; appraising performance; rewarding and disciplining employees;  addressing complaints and resolving problems.


Job Requirements

           

QUALIFICATIONS

 

To perform this job successfully, an individual must be able to perform each essential duty satisfactorily. The requirements listed below are representative of the knowledge, skill, and/or ability required. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions.

 

Education*

Bachelor's degree (B. A.) from an accredited four-year college or university in the fields of social science, health, education or a related academic field.  Master’s Degree (M.A.) preferred.

 

Experience*

A minimum of fifteen (15) years of professional experience in marketing, communications or public affairs in a senior management role. A minimum of seven (7)  years of supervisorial experience.

 

*An equivalent combination of training and experience may be substituted for the educational requirement.  Two (2) years of professional experience will be credited for every one (1) year of post-secondary education required for this position.

Mathematical Skills

Ability to utilize mathematical formulas; to add and subtract; multiply and divide; utilize decimals and percentages; and to apply the principles of algebra and geometry.

Core Competencies

 

Politically astute and tactful; attentive to the perspectives and competing interests of various internal and external stakeholders.

 

Ability to participate in a team, manage multiple and often simultaneous tasks, and in create a work environment that is recognized for a high level of ethical integrity, organization, timeliness, cost-effectiveness, accuracy, and results.

Highly effective leader who works with others collegially and is a proven team builder. 

 

Inclined to coach and teach in order to improve the knowledge and ability of others, but objectively and equitably holds people accountable for results.

 

Understands and values quality improvement, applies managerial and technical skills to measure and improve efficiency and effectiveness. 

 

Demonstrated commitment to diversity and inclusion; valuing a diversity of perspectives and encouraging contributions by all team members.

 

 

Knowledge & Abilities

Comprehensive knowledge of program management in the areas of public relations, marketing, information and publicity techniques, publication production, broadcast and print media, design and printing concepts.

 

Knowledge of crisis communications strategies.

 

Knowledge of effective media and presentation training strategies.

 

Application of Internet technology to acquire and publish information via the World Wide Web.

Principles and practices of staff organization, supervision, budgeting, training, effective discipline.

 

Strong writing skills.

The Los Angeles media market and the structure and operations of print and broadcast news organizations.

Knowledge of effective Paid and Earned Media Strategies.

The ethnic, cultural, geographic, and socio-economic diversity of the populations served by the Commission.

Current issues regarding children and families of Los Angeles County, including public health, childcare and early childhood development issues.

Computer software programs used for Desktop Publishing.

Management principles and practices, including work planning, evaluation and supervision.

Project management techniques and team-building skills.

 

ABILITY TO:

Present information regarding Commission programs in a proactive, complete and media-sensitive manner.

Write clear, concise reports, articles, speeches/remarks, position papers, statements and talking points.

Analyze and communicate legislative changes and their impact on Commission.

Research and compile information to develop proposed legislation to maximize the effectiveness of the Commission’s programs and objectives.

Analyze emerging situations and assess their public relations and policy implications.

Develop and maintain effective relationships with news media, community leaders, administrators, and staff.

 

Develop and maintain effective relationships with regulators, government agencies, policymakers and their staff, business and corporate leaders and child development organizations.

Develop effective media and communications strategies for non-English speaking populations.

Communicate effectively in Spanish a plus.

Demonstrated ability to interpret and implement policies, conduct research, and to make sound recommendations and decisions.

Work independently and possess a high degree of creativity.

Act judiciously under pressure.

Supervise staff effectively.

Use computer software programs such as MS Office, MS Outlook, and the Internet.
