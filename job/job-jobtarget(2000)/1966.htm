The Energy Systems Division at Argonne National Laboratory is seeking a candidate to fill the position of Principal Electrical Engineer. Primary responsibilities will be assisting in the design and implementation of experiments that advance the state of the art for electric vehicle charging infrastructure and Smart Grid interconnectivity technologies.  This role will support the U.S. Department of Energy’s mission to provide technical guidance to utilities and industry suppliers towards the adoption of SAE codes and standards related to charging control systems for electric vehicle supply equipment (EVSEs) and their communications methodologies with the Smart Grid. Essential duties and responsibilities include the following:

·         Apply embedded systems programming skills to enable the communication and desired functionality of related subsystems for the evaluation of vehicle to grid interconnectivity demonstrations.

·         Gather and analyze data collected from complex testing of charging equipment, grid interconnectivity, power electronics, energy storage devices, and motors, in a vehicle systems context.

·         Assist in the preparation of technical reports and papers that describe the results of R&D on Smart Charging interconnectivity and the evaluation of advanced energy storage devices.

 

Job requirements are a Ph.D. and 2-5 years experience/equivalent or a Master's Degree and 4-7 years of experience/equivalent or Bachelor's Degree and 9-11 years experience/equivalent. 

The ideal candidate will have:

·         Demonstrated expertise in embedded software programming skills and considerable

proficiency in using LabView and MATLAB software;

·         Considerable knowledge and experience with hybrid electric vehicles, battery systems and

component testing.

·         Considerable knowledge and skills with data analysis methods and reporting.

·         Considerable knowledge of quality assurance procedures and applying to current work.

·         Considerable skill using computer software such as AutoCad, LabVIEW, MATLAB, Excel, and HTML.

·         Considerable skills in oral and written communications.

 

Candidates should submit a resume through the Argonne job website at http://www.anl.gov for

Requisition #316027.
