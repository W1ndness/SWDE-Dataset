COMPANY OVERVIEW

The Society of Hospital Medicine (SHM), a non-profit membership association, is the largest organization in the nation representing hospitalists and the practice of hospital medicine. It is our mission to promote the highest quality care for hospitalized patients, as well as provide opportunities and support to hospitalists. We are committed to enhancing the practice of hospital medicine by promoting education, research and advocacy.

SHM provides ongoing education and industry updates through its publications, The Hospitalist, and the Journal of Hospital Medicine. SHM also provides a forum for professional networking and education through its Annual Meeting and other events.  Located in Philadelphia, SHM employs approximately 40 employees. 

JOB SUMMARY

 Under the direct supervision of the Vice President, Business Services & Technology, this position serves as the primary database administrator for the Society's SQL Server databases.  This individual manages the development, maintenance and support of all databases and iMIS, SHM's association management system. The Database Administrator will be responsible for the development and integration of new database systems and services.  This position will also be responsible for designing, administering and maintaining complex database projects as well as ensuring highly efficient operations of SQL Server and other database applications/services.

DUTIES & ESSENTIAL JOB FUNCTIONS

This person will support the management and administration of SQL server databases for SHM's Website, CMS, SharePoint, Great Plains and other business applications. This individual will work collaboratively with staff, senior leadership, consultants and service providers to manage the successful implementation and management of all database services, systems and applications.

The Database Administrator must have demonstrated experience managing SQL Server 2008 & 2005 Database Systems.  This person will work with the VP, Business Services & Technology to develop, maintain, and administer database end user training and support, as well as oversee governance of policies & procedures.

The Database Administrator will have demonstrated experience in project delivery, and previous experience with database implementation and administration. This position requires strong technical and organizational skills with an emphasis on leading the translation of business requirements into solutions. Excellent writing and communication skills are a must, as well as the ability to problem solve in a quick-paced environment and work collaboratively with others.

 The responsibilities of the Database Administrator also include:

  • Perform the installation, configuration, testing, maintenance, monitoring, troubleshooting and reporting metrics for assigned databases;
  • Performs timely analysis, diagnosis and resolution of complex database issues.
  • Prepare database documentation, including data standards, processes and procedures;
  • Provide technical consulting in identifying, implementing and supporting new database functionality;
  • Create and modify reports and reporting tools for staff to query against SHM's database using Crystal Reports, SQL and IQA;
  • Recommend improvements to services and systems policies and procedures;
  • Assist the BS&T staff in establishing and maintaining clear conduits of information and communication;
  • Proactively follow and actively promote all department policies and procedures and conduct yourself within the framework of the Society's Core Values. Meet assigned commitments and hold self accountable for delivery;
  • Manage multiple tasks with changing deadlines and priorities;
  • Performs other related duties and assignments as required.

Job Requirements
QUALIFICATIONS
  • 3+years' experience in SQL 2008/2005 database administration, utilities and tools;
  • Proficiency in database design, validation, operating systems, database maintenance and reporting strongly preferred;
  • Proficiency in SQL security, backup and recovery procedures preferred;
  • Experience managing iMIS or a similar AMS (Association Management System) or CRM highly desirable;
  • Working knowledge of relational databases and advance knowledge/programming experience with SQL and Crystal Reports is required;
  • Experience with SQL Reporting Services is highly desirable;
  • ASP, VBScript and .NET knowledge is a plus
  • Understanding of content management systems, e-commerce solutions, Great Plains (accounting software) and SharePoint a plus;
  • Demonstrated project management skills and the ability to manage multiple, changing priorities;
  • Proficiency with MS Office applications; experience with MS Project a plus;
  • Ability to quickly learn new systems, technology, and methods;
  • Bachelor's degree or equivalent required.
