POSITION SUMMARY:

Working under the direction of the Health Systems Development Director, the EHR Manager/ Integrated Care Coordinator works to strengthen CRIHB’s and member Tribal Health Programs (THPs) quality improvement and reporting activities. S/he works on projects to comply with an increasing number of external reporting mandates and quality improvement initiatives. The incumbent is responsible for leading the Electronic Health Record (EHR) implementation team; assisting with the display and interpretation of data; and assisting with reporting the data to a wide range of clinical and administrative leaders in THPs. The EHR Manager/Integrated Care Coordinator will work with the CA tribal health programs, as well as with the American Indian/Alaska Native Regional Extension Center to spearhead efforts towards achieving meaningful use with commercial EHR systems.

The EHR Manager/ Integrated Care Coordinator provides expertise to all areas of EHR implementation and will assist in developing clinical guidelines, templates and informational tools for major outpatient disease management programs. The EHR Manager/ Integrated Care Coordinator must influence and gain support at all levels of the THP organization(s); work closely with the THP EHR Project Leaders, managers and staff in planning, directing and implementing the EHR. The incumbent provides, or arranges provision of EHR training services to THPs to ensure product development, implementation, and enhancements meet user specifications.

In addition, the EHR Manager/Integrated Care Coordinator assists THPs to improve access to care, redesign processes, build capacity and provide quality patient care by coordinating Institute for Healthcare Improvement (IHI) collaborative with member THPs. Incumbent will develop educational events; maintain a centralized repository of QI/IHI tools, resources and data to share with member THPs; assist programs in analyzing individual and population data to identify trends, patterns, problems, and process/care improvement opportunities.

The EHR Manager/Integrated Care Coordinator acts as the point of contact for the EHR software vendor, NextGen. S/he is responsible for maintaining vendor contract (which CRIHB holds for the benefit of member THPs), ordering licenses and services, negotiating prices and discounts, advocating for software enhancements, and approving invoices. The EHR Manager/Integrated Care Coordinator is responsible for managing and coordinating, or supervising coordination of the Tribal Health NextGen Consortium (THNC), a national THP users group. S/he will coordinate an annual THNC meeting, identify and pursue funding opportunities, collect and manage any dues/assessments, and supervise any additional staff employed specifically for THNC activities.

ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS:

1. Provide clinical expertise for NextGen EHR and/or RPMS/EHR implementation.

2. Evaluate EHR system performance, with other members of the implementation team. Identify problems, recommend solutions.

3. Participates in the implementation of NextGen or RPMS/EHR software.

4. Coordinates with THP EHR project manager and appropriate staff, in managing project timelines and taking appropriate actions when required.

5. Leads development of electronic guidelines, protocols, and reports.

6. Leads development of IHI processes within THP; identifies, pursues, and obtains resources for THP to implement IHI collaboratives and attend IHI conferences.

7. Evaluate clinic work flow and patient flow systems. Assist clinics in change management and planning processes related to major changes in clinic activities, including EHR implementation and clinic redesign.

8. Maintains written reports of findings and activities. Reports status of projects and activities to supervisor.

9. Assist in development of performance improvement processes for THPs, including use of EHR data in performance improvement processes. Working with HSD staff, evaluate clinic needs and abilities. Participate in planning TA activities to meet individual clinic’s needs.

10. Works with THP quality leaders to investigate quality concerns or issues arising from external payer reviews, external clinical benchmarking efforts, or tracking and surveillance of outcome measures.

11. Works with health program quality leaders/representatives to develop annual quality measurement and improvement plans, including identification of measures to be tracked. Acts as a resource to health program quality leaders.

12. Responsible for writing and submitting grant applications, grant reporting, grant budget management, project management and communication with granting foundations.

Job Requirements

QUALIFICATIONS:

 

1.     Prefer a Masters degree in nursing, pharmacy, or similar clinical field, and 2 years experience in EHR implementation

                                    OR

Bachelors degree in healthcare field and 5 years experience in EHR implementation Two years experience in an ambulatory care environment.

2.     Experience in quality measurement and/or improvement, and IHI processes highly desirable.

3.     Ability to work and communicate effectively with a wide variety of people within health care settings, State and Federal agencies, and private organizations.

4.     Ability to exercise independent judgment, to coordinate a number of projects, personnel, and clinics to meet specified objectives and to maintain detailed records of these activities and projects.

5.     Knowledge of state and federal regulations specific to external reporting mandates.

6.     Superior organizational and interpersonal skills with ability to work independently.

7.     Excellent communication and follow-up accompanied by the ability to analyze and present data in a way to influence and change behavior.

8.     Comfortable interacting with a vast array of administrative and clinical staff, ranging from the Medical Director and department directors to frontline care providers.

9.     Experience with performance improvement methods, including statistical concepts and applications.

10.  Five years project management experience required with strong knowledge of healthcare quality improvement models/strategies and health information technology preferred.

11.  Experience in a clinic or community health center is highly desirable.

12.  Strong computer skills (Word, Excel, and PowerPoint applications), with ability to use spreadsheet and graphic programs to produce bar graphs and charts.
