Manager of Billing and EDI CBO - FT (days)

Job Description

Manager of Billing and EDI CBO - FT (days)-1000014927

Description

Job Summary:

Oversee daily operations of Billing/EDI area.  Assist Director in management of outside vendors, and overall billing and collection of accounts receivable for all Mercy Central Billing Office accounts.

Essential Duties:

* Demonstrates professional, appropriate, effective and tactful written, verbal, and nonverbal communication with patient, families, medical staff, colleagues, vendors, and other departments throughout the continuum of care to promote continuity of care and services and enhance clinic image.

* Demonstrates positive professional customer service in all patient, staff, and visitor contacts.

* Utilizes interpersonal skills to establish and maintain healthy interpersonal relationships with healthcare members, patients, and families.

* Acknowledges patients rights on confidentiality issues, maintains patient confidentiality at all times, and follows HIPAA guidelines and regulations.

* Participates in staff development activities and orientation as requested.

* Proactively acts as patient advocate, responding to and working to resolve patient concerns.

* Responsible for following equipment maintenance protocol, identifying problems, and coordinating appropriate repairs.

* Operation of various office machines (i.e. fax, copier, phone, and computer).

* Participates effectively as a team member in the clinic

CBO MANAGER/BILLING/EDI:

* Supervise Lead Billing Representative, Billing Representatives, Support Services Clerks, and Clinic Liaison. * Identify clinics with recurrent registration problems by utilizing the Billing Specialists tracking mechanism tools. * Contact Clinic Managers if return of requested information have been delayed. * Report inconsistencies in workflow between CBO and clinics to Director. * Evaluate and present recommendations to management regarding enhancement to training for front-end users. * Perform quality control review of paper claim submission to secondary and tertiary insurance, and correspondence. * Facilitate staff meetings. * Promotes working as a team to achieve strategic and financial goals and the objectives of the department and corporation by recognizing problems and presenting them openly and professionally with other Managers and staff. Helps coordinate solutions interdepartmentally. * Assists the Director to assure that the Mission, Vision and Values of Mercy Clinics, Inc. and subsidiaries are visual in the operation and direction of the assigned responsibilities. * Schedules and supervises personnel in assigned area. Ensures the policies are understood and followed by staff. Assists the Director with the responsibility for personnel issues (i.e., interviewing, motivation, discipline and performance appraisals.) * Performs quality control review on each staff member assigned to their department on a systematic basis. Monitors and reports productivity by posting weekly results. * Evaluate individual staff members training and make appropriate recommendations if additional training is required. * Assist in daily operations to ensure that protocols conform to accreditation, licensing and regulatory standards set forth by federal, state and local agencies. * Consistently notified the Director of outstanding issues and concerns related to the billing business process. * Pursues professional development to ensure currency of present practices and to stay abreast of trends in health care. * Collects and summarizes, for upper management, statistics which are pertinent to the accounts receivable process. * Ability to understand and utilize a complex and changing highly sophisticated billing system. * Responsible to improve adverse public relations issues. * Has authority to write-off accounts at the discretion of the Director up to $100.00. * Performs other work-related duties as required by the Director.

Qualifications

Education:

* Professional level of knowledge in accounting, business management, or like fields, which is equivalent to that which would be acquired by completing a two year college program.

Experience:

* At least two years of successful experience in billing, customer accounts, or insurance claims. * Proven verbal communication and interpersonal skills. * Proven written communication skills. * Proficient personal computer skills

License/Certification:

* None required.

Additional Responsibilities:

* Demonstrates a commitment to service, organization values and professionalism through appropriate conduct and demeanor at all times. * Adheres to and exhibits our core values: Reverence: Having a profound spirit of awe and respect for all creation, shaping relationships to self, to one another and to God and acknowledging that we hold in trust all that has been given to us. Integrity: Moral wholeness, soundness, uprightness, honesty and sincerity as a basis of trustworthiness. Compassion: Feeling with others, being one with others in their sorrows and joys, rooted in the sense of solidarity as members of the human community. Excellence: Outstanding achievement, merit, virtue; continually surpassing standards to achieve/maintain quality. * Maintains confidentiality and protects sensitive data at all times. * Adheres to organizational and department specific safety standards and guidelines. * Works collaboratively and supports efforts of team members. * Demonstrates exceptional customer service and interacts effectively with physicians, patients, residents, visitors, staff and the broader health care community.
