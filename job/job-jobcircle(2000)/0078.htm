Engineering Consultant

Posted:  December 3, 2010

Engineering Consultant

Add to Job Cart |  View Job Cart

Description

At Honeywell, we look for people driven by a desire to contribute, be challenged and grow. Our people make Honeywell a special company and are a key competitive advantage. We are seeking an Engineering Consultant for Houston, TX facility. Honeywell International is a $31 billion diversified technology and manufacturing global leader. Honeywell has more than 125,000 employees in 120 countries around the world and has a demonstrated heritage of innovation and achievement. Honeywell Process Solutions (HPS) is a strategic business unit in Automation and Control Solutions (ACS). HPS improves the safety, reliability, efficiency and sustainability of industrial facilities on every continent around the world. With more than 12,000 employees in 120 countries, HPS offers a full range of industry-leading automation and control solutions and advanced software applications to key vertical industries, including oil & gas, mining, refining, pulp & paper, power, chemical, and life sciences. Competitive salary and excellent benefits await the successful candidate. The HPS strategic intent is to profitably grow the ACS Industrial Channel to $5 Billion by 2012. This means that HPS will incorporate the entire ACS portfolio in developing solutions that improve our customers business performance on safety, reliability and efficiency. In alignment with this strategy, Turbomachinery control has emerged as a key STRAP initiative. The Turbomachinery New Growth Initiative is designed to address this key technology trend and opportunity facing the Automation Industry and use HPS technical lead and solution delivery capability to emerge as a leading player in this exciting new area. The Turbomachinery Engineering Consultant will develop and grow the Turbomachinery business across HPS Americas. The role will have engineering consultant responsibility and will lead a technical team to take a holistic view of the business including sales support, engineering consulting and operations support, working closely with regional management teams and all operations functions within those regions. Turbomachinery is a Global key strategic initiative for Honeywell Process Solutions within the 5-year strategic plan (STRAP). The Turbomachinery Engineering Consultant will develop, own and execute the engineering plan, setting the technical direction, strategy and actions to support HPS Americas in reaching the Goals and Objectives set in the Business plan itself. The span of responsibility for this engineering consultant within HPS is Americas Pole. The person will work closely with the New Growth COE Leader and will interface with and provide technical leadership to all (Pole) Marketing, Sales and Operations personnel dealing with Turbomachinery in HPS (Pole). We are looking for a high performing individual to directly lead a small core team and coordinate a wider (Pole) virtual team to build a sustainable multi-million dollar Turbomachinery solutions business within a few years. This role reports directly to the Americas New Growth Initiatives COE Leader, who is the Executive Owner for the Turbomachinery Growth Program. Specific Roles and Responsibilities: Develop, own and execute the Operations plan for Americas Turbomachinery Growth Initiative, as part of it: • Design, develop and coordinate a Turbomachinery cross functional team owning all the needed competencies to successfully engineer, support and deliver the Turbomachinery Solution in Americas • Americas Service and Technical support: identify and select across the Americas HPS functions the qualified people to become member of the Turbomachinery Delivery Team in order to reach self-sufficiency and autonomy at Americas Level in the following Turbomachinery related activities, like, Project Engineering and services quotations, Commissioning, Field Services, Sales Support, Identify and select external service partners to ensure back up and flexibility to the internal structure. • Lead Americas Communications effort: Drive consistently the Americas activities such as, Coordinate Certifications and approvals, Support Product Marketing activities, prioritize efforts towards the Global product roadmap, Develop and select the proper Training material, Trade show activities support (articles development, road-shows, demonstrations) • Support Turbomachinery Business Development and/or Solution Consultants Remain focused on the assigned area, goaled with relevant deliverables, support other solution consultants to integrate the Turbomachinery products by supporting the development and execution a effective training program for HPS sales and HPS indirect Channels and drive HPS engagement. Proactively drive, together with AMs, Corporate Accounts approach, focusing on a predefined list of accounts, tailoring specific strategies for each account. • Drive HPS sales force to explore new customers and new markets using Turbomachinery market as door opener. • Customer presentations and meetings at various hierarchical and functional levels, practical demonstrations, actively attend the main Power Generation Events in Americas and participate in Customer round tables, Conferences, Congresses, and events such as Road shows and HUG. • Develop metrics for progress measurement and regular updates including a monthly action plan for execution of the business plan • Work with the RGMs and SMs to define and execute regional action plans in order to reach and exceed regional targets • Investigate for Americas partnerships and alliances in order to maximize the Turbomachinery Exposure • Participate in regular progress update meetings from HPS President, Pole VP/GM and other leaders/stake holders in the business • Work with regions to achieve AOP orders and Gross margin targets

Qualifications

Basic Qualifications: Technical Degree 5 years of working knowledge with NERC 10 years of sales/marketing/consulting experience in the industrial sector Desired Requirements: Working knowledge of the Process Industries market with a focus on O&G, refineries, chemicals, power and pharmaceutical and of the related L1-L4 offering. Advanced Degree a plus Minimum 5 years turbine control engineering experience, preferably gained in a major global supplier of Turbine equipment. Strong leadership skills, ability to motivate and manage a team in a highly matrixed organization Good insight of Turbomachinery control systems and related technical topics Ability to think and act with integrity across Regions and Functions Ability to lead change and establish metrics/key indicators A strong bias for action and results oriented strategic and tactical behavior; strong conflict management and influencing skills Proven leadership skills Excellent communication skills and relationship building skills, at all levels of the organization Global Mindset Six Sigma certification preferred Multilingual skills (mandatory English language),and cross-cultural experience preferred Honeywell has been selected as one of the Most Admired Companies in the world by Fortune magazine. As an Equal Opportunity Employer, Honeywell is committed to a diverse workforce.
