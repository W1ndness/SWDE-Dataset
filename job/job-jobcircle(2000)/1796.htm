Department Administrator

Posted:  November 27, 2010

Position Information Job Title: Department Administrator Department Ecology & Evolutionary Biology Employment Category Full-Time Requisition Number 11127 Posting Date 11-16-2010 Closing Date Open Until Filled Rice University Standard of Civility Serves as a representative of the University, displaying courtesy, tact, consideration and discretion in all interactions with other members of the Rice community and with the public. Position Summary The Department Administrator assists the department chair in maintaining operation of the department through financial planning, monitoring budgets, internal and external accounting and forecasting, human resource management and development, and professional management for the department. This position assists with strategic development for the department in accordance with the universitys mission, vision, and long-term goals. Education Required Bachelors Degree May substitute additional related experience, above and beyond what is required, on an equivalent year for year basis in lieu of the degree requirement. Experience Required 5 years Experience in a highly responsible administrative position, that includes budget, project and personnel management. Education may not be substituted for the experience required. License/Certification Required Other Skills Required - Excellent leadership and management skills, ability to motivate and train staff. - Experience with financial accounting and management of multiple accounts. - Excellent communication skills, both verbal and written. - Demonstrated experience in using interactive computer systems and in generating and analyzing spreadsheets and graphic presentations of data. - Strong interpersonal skills needed to work successfully with faculty, students, and university officers. - Must be well organized, able to meet deadlines, often under short time-frames and with multiple tasks assigned. - Must be able to work independently and with little direct supervision. - Must be able to deal with difficult situations in a diplomatic manner; must be discreet about confidential information; and must have sound judgment so as to make appropriate decisions independently. Education Preferred Bachelors degree in management, accounting, finance, or other related field. Experience Preferred Three years experience in a university setting in the role of department administrator or coordinator with research grant management and supervisory responsibilities. License/Certification Preferred Other Skills Preferred - Experience with Banner Financial System. - Familiarity with Federal OMB circulars A-21 and A-110 and university research accounting guidelines. Physical Demands Ability to move around campus. Must be able to lift up to 25 pounds. Working Conditions (e.g. Weekend/evening work, travel, inclement weather, hazards) Occasional evening or weekend work. Grade 15 Salary Information Salary Commensurate with Experience and Qualifications Benefits Eligible Benefits Eligible Eligible for Overtime No Work Schedule Monday through Friday, 8 a.m. to 5 p.m.* *Possible weekends or after normal working hours when necessary. Eligible for Shift Differential Not Shift Differential Eligible Security Sensitive Yes Special Instructions to Applicants Job Category Professional/Managerial Job Duties 4 Records Description of job responsibility/duty: - PERSONNEL MANAGEMENT - Supervise two department coordinators to ensure sufficient training, oversight, and compliance with departmental and university standard operating procedures. - DEPARTMENTAL OVERSIGHT - Responsible for strategic planning of departmental resources and finances, internal financial reports, and internal and external audits. Act as the departments financial representative in interactions with the deans office, relative administrative offices of the university, and affiliated institutions. Maintain a working knowledge of the departments financial operations, funding sources, and policies, including documentation. - FINANCIAL AND RESEARCH ADMINISTRATION - Plan, manage and monitor departmental budgeting activities; create and analyze financial reports; and develop budget forecasts for research and university funds working closely with the chair and faculty to ensure compliance with the universitys policies and procedures and federal government, state, and other research project sponsor guidelines. Support faculty with proposal submission and grant monitoring throughout the life of the award to include budget preparation, internal signature processes, gathering non-scientific data as required by funding agencies. Reconcile expenditure transactions to the university banner system monthly. - FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT - Provide administrative guidance, oversight, financial management and logistical support for the department. Initiate, oversee, and/or approve departmental and research expenditures including travel statements, check requests, purchasing card charges, purchase orders, internal purchases, and special work orders. Prepare re-budgeting, IDT, and expense transfer paperwork for the department in accordance with university policies and the availability of funds. - RECORDS MANAGEMENT - Create records management systems to ensure the accurate maintenance of financial records for all departmental and research funds housed within the department. This includes the management of proposal and grant documentation in accordance with departmental, university, and governmental policies, rules and regulations. - PAYROLL - Create, implement, monitor and enforce departmental payroll processes and procedures to ensure appropriate paperwork is completed accurately, timely, and according to policy for all human resource issues, i.e., appointments, promotions, salary actions, and terminations. Generate and manage all faculty personnel paperwork and records. Ensure all personnel records are current and organized. Ensure that the departments employment practices are in compliance with the personnel policies and procedures of the university and the department. Make recommendations on hiring, staff retention, and termination of employees. - COST CENTERS - Maintain cost centers and accounting systems. Oversee and control financial expenditures and operation of departmental cost centers such as the department vans and office equipment. - OPERATIONS - Prepare written notices, policies and procedures establishing the departments position on financial matters and operations. - Ensure compliance with university fire and safety codes. - Delegate and monitor departmental projects and responsibilities. - Develop and implement appropriate internal controls. - Assist chair and faculty with building management and maintenance, including renovations and repairs of the building and facilities that support the department. - Oversee and assist with the annual faculty report to the provost. - Oversee and assist with the departmental annual report. - Mandate policies and procedures regarding property control and inventory. - Complete all university and governmental surveys related to the department: NSF, NIH, NRC, GRE survey, space survey, etc. - Process the annual and bi-annual effort reporting forms. - Work with facilities and engineering, faculty and chair to maintain the building, greenhouses, and office spaces as suitable environments for the work required and to ensure that all aspects of the facility are in working order. - Responsible for the completion of all time allocation reports for faculty. - Responsible for monitoring cost sharing commitments and preparing appropriate paperwork to comply with university requirements. - Oversee preparation of promotion dossiers, faculty searches, etc.
