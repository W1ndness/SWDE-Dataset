Hardware Design Engineer RF Analog

Posted:  November 27, 2010

Description: As the worlds premier measurement company, Agilent works in close collaboration with engineers, scientists, and researchers around the globe to meet the communications, electronics, life sciences, and chemical analysis challenges of today and tomorrow. The company operates two primary businesses -- electronic and bio-analytical measurement -- supported by Agilent Research Laboratories, its central research group. Agilent is committed to providing innovative measurement solutions that enable our customers and partners-- the leaders in their • elds -- to deliver the products and services that make a measurable difference in the lives of people everywhere.

Santa Rosa, in the heart of Californias beautiful wine country, is the R&D headquarters of Agilents Microwave and Communication Division (MCD). MCD develops and produces the worlds highest performance signal generators and signal analyzers. If you• re up to the challenge, join our high caliber team. As an RF/Analog Engineer, you will apply your Electrical Engineering coursework to a variety of focused, hands-on projects. You will be part of a product development team that designs, develops, and tests RF and microwave test equipment. This is your opportunity to join a team thats bringing new products and technologies to the market faster and more efficiently than ever before.

Job

Your responsibilities will include developing a variety of RF and analog circuitry depending on project needs. Designs could cover amplifiers, filters, interface and control circuits , phase lock loops, fast switching attenuators, low noise/low distortion front ends, broadband baluns, low distortion multipliers, high power broadband outputs, low phase noise VCOs, and frequency conversion covering frequencies from DC to 26 GHz. Additionally, you will implement product measurement functions, assist in instrument architecture decisions with senior engineers, assist with vendor and component selection, and troubleshoot problems partnering with other functional areas. Accomplishing your design goals will require the use of simulation, schematic capture and layout tools. Qualifications:  Qualifications:

Bachelors, Masters Degree or equivalent

• Demonstrated ability to design microwave & rf circuits up to 20GHz (via work experience, lab work or simulation)

• Familiarly with Advanced Design Simulation (ADS) a plus

• Comfortable using Network Analyzers, Spectrum Analyzers and Signal Sources to measure/debug a design

• Demonstrated ability to work in a team

• Strong oral & written communication skills

Experience and Skills:

• A solid educational foundation in analog electronic circuit design and high frequency transmission line concepts

• Experience with RF/analog circuit analysis and design, CAD schematic tools, circuit simulation tools, and design evaluation

• Ability to evaluate alternative circuit approaches and reduce the most appropriate to practice

• Ability to effectively communicate ideas and concepts in both written and oral form

• Measurement experience in one or more of the following areas: signal analyzers, signal generators, I/Q generation, time domain analysis, and vector network analysis

• Knowledge of communication systems, time and frequency domain analysis including architectural tradeoffs and partitioning impacts

• Experience with Baseband through RF/ Microwave measurement systems

• Commitment to excellence, dedication, team player, self-motivation, and good communication skills

Additional Desired Skills:

• Experience in RF/Microwave design

• Experience in block diagram design for up converter & down converter type products

• Knowledge /experience with DSP, digital, and programmable logic design
