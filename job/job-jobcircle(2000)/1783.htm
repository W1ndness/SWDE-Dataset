Education Testing/Accountability Consultant II

Posted:  November 27, 2010

PLEASE REFER TO VACANCY NUMBER P10111901 / POSITION #60091283 ON YOUR APPLICATION. Working Title: Charter School Testing Consultant This position involves specialized and professional work in the implementation of assessment projects in the North Carolina statewide testing program. The position will provide specialized professional assistance and support to charter schools in the assessment of student performance (grades K-12) and the collection and management of student data in the implementation of the North Carolina Statewide Testing and ABCs Accountability Programs. Other duties include providing the necessary training, interpretation, and enforcement of the standards for uniform statewide test administrations and the implementation of the ABCs and AYP Accountability Programs. This position provides leadership to charter schools which span across multi-counties for the delivery of accountability services on issues related to statewide testing, operational procedures, policy interpretation, training activities, use of testing and accountability software applications and data, and technical support for the scanning and scoring of state tests. This position will also be responsible for preparing and monitoring quality assurance standards and data collection procedures that ensure that test data collected and reports generated are accurate by conducting data audits and verifications using sophisticated proprietary statistical analysis software applications. A high degree of skill in data management is required. This position will be trained by other Accountability staff to have knowledge of new and existing proprietary software programs, laws, policies, procedures and established rules. This position will be based in Raleigh, NC and will have regional responsibility over the Central and Southwest Regions. KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS, AND ABILITIES: An ideal candidate will possess considerable knowledge of federal, state, and local provisions, regulations, and objectives pertaining to the statewide testing program, the ABCs Accountability Program, NC Statewide Student Accountability Standards, the federal No Child Left Behind Education Act of 2001 including revision to Title I and Title III, the Individuals with Disabilities Act (especially the reauthorization and amendments of 1997), and the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), especially the authorizations under Section 504 and their implications on the testing of students with disabilities and testing students with limited English proficiency. The candidate should have considerable knowledge of methods of gathering, analyzing, interpreting, and evaluating data, and test results and reports; the ability to analyze and interpret organizational and procedural problems and to make alterations in existing systems; the ability to plan, design, and promote measures of evaluation, particularly with respect to tests, research projects, statistical analysis, and other research in statewide large-scale assessment projects; and the ability to effectively convey ideas related to the testing policy, rules, and procedures, in oral or written form, to state and local education agency personnel, federal personnel, and the general public. Minimum Requirements: Minimum requirements include a masters degree in Education, Tests and Measurement, Education Research, Education/Quantitative Psychology and Statistics, or Statistics, and three years of directly related experience needed to perform the work; or an equivalent combination of education and experience. All degrees must be received from appropriately accredited institutions. Management prefers candidates with experience working in a charter school, as well as experience working in test administration and related procedures. Any employment offer may be less than the maximum of the range due to salary equity with similarly-situated employees or the selected candidates related education and experience.
