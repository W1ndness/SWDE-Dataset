Solution Analyst (SAP Business Analyst)

Posted:  December 3, 2010

I Choose Opportunity.

Whatever job youre looking for, you can probably find it at Cargill.  Our diverse partnerships across a variety of industries mean countless career opportunities for you.  Its your choice.  Its your future.  And you can make it happen at Cargill.

Cargill is embarking upon a journey to design all of its key business processes in over 70 business units within 66 countries. This will include creating the approaches, tools and technology to support those processes.  Be on the ground floor as a part of a team that will change the way Cargill works. This is a major global effort with significant impact. Join us to make this successful company even stronger, more focused on the customer and more profitable.  Be a part of it.

The Process and Application Team is responsible for the enterprise wide designing of processes and technology applications that will optimize the depth and breadth of Cargill. Much of the work Cargill does today will benefit from common processes that are not dependent on technology: establishing strategic intent, Cargills brand strategy, and managing internal controls are just a few examples of processes that are expected to designed, but not technology enabled. However, many process areas must be enabled by technology to achieve the aspirations of SI 2015.

The Solution Analyst will work in both project-based environments and operational/support environments.  The percentage of time spent in each setting will vary over time.

In a project setting, the Solution Analyst is responsible for understanding business needs and designing, configuring and testing solutions to meet those needs.  The Solution Analyst is a key resource with business experience in relevant processes and applications.  He/she works with the other project team members to translate business requirements into solutions that enable the organization to achieve its goals.

In an operational environment, the Solution Analyst is the key resource troubleshooting, diagnosing and solving production issues. The Solution Analyst adheres to established methodologies and processes for operational support.  He/she provides the in-depth knowledge of functionality and configuration of the applications.

In both environments, the Solution Analyst is expected to understand and enforce established quality standards, enterprise architecture standards and compliance controls. The Solution Analyst is capable of in-depth analysis of existing solutions (or legacy applications).

40%

-Assist Process Architects in assessing requests for solution enhancements from end users or other project teams.

•Troubleshoot, diagnose and solve production issues.

•Identify, analyze and validate requirements for approved projects.

•Translate business requirements into functional design specifications. In some instances the Solution Analyst may also support the creation of technical designs.

-Work with other project or support teams to create integrated solution designs.

Ensure design specifications meet policies and standards.

30%

-Teach other project and/or support team resources about specific application functionality - this could be formal training, or more informal knowledge transfer.

-Support deployment teams during implementation of solutions.

-Develop in-depth knowledge of the specific applications.

20%

-Document solution designs. Ensure that changes to designs are reflected in project and support documentation.

10%

-Write test scripts and use cases. Develop the test scripts in alignment with the test plans.

•Execute test scripts and use cases.  Document test results.

•Follow quality management policies, procedures and testing related guidelines when executing or creating the solution test scripts.

•Complete solution testing documentation.

Qualifications

Required:

• Bachelors degree with 5+ years of business experience OR 9+ years of business experience.

• Experience working on at least one full lifecycle SAP implementation.

• 2+ years experience with business process design and improvement projects.

- Experience in gathering business requirements.

• Experience working in at least one of the Major Processes or depth in another area of technology or data.

- Ability to translate business process requirements into process, data and technology solutions.

- A proven track record of performance with at least 3 years of increasing technical responsibility.

• Demonstrated ability to excel in ambiguous situations

• Active inquiry skills

• Strong written communication skills

- Ability to travel up to 20% on a regular basis, with ability to travel up to 75% on a short-term basis to support specific project needs.

Preferred:

• Experience modeling business processes, i.e. ARIS modeling.

• Past experience working in a global team

Professional Development:

Employees that take advantage of the many opportunities to work as a member of the Tartan team can expect to gain experiences and new skills, which will enhance professional development and increase marketability within Cargill and the global marketplace.

Many roles will provide the following experiences:

• Effectively manage / lead significant organizational change and achieve desired results.

• Acquire project experience, which align common processes, consolidate software portfolio and upgrade technology infrastructure to meet SI 2015 aspirations.

• Demonstrate broad perspective of business environment.

• Apply knowledge to new and more complex situations.

• Leverage knowledge, perspectives and experience from working with globally diverse team.

• Be part of new or unfamiliar role, building / being part of a new global team, dealing with ambiguity.

• Influence people without direct authority.

• Create a process from the ground up.

• Collaborate across process areas and across the organization.

• Increase business-partnering skills.

Cargill is an international producer and marketer of food, agricultural, financial and industrial products and services. Founded in 1865, the privately held company employs 131,000 people in 66 countries. Cargill helps customers succeed through collaboration and innovation, and is committed to applying its global knowledge and experience to help meet economic, environmental and social challenges wherever it does business. For more information, visit www.cargill.com.
