PMO Analyst

About the Job

Job Purpose: Assists the overall PMO Lead for a business unit or program area perform PMO responsibilities to ensure project adherence to enterprise project delivery processes, governance routines, and reporting requirements.

Primary responsibilities include:


- Onboard PMs to company processes
- Mentor in Project Management Tools
- Coordinate and support GPMO Enterprise Governance (Tollgates, Resource Allocations, Enterprise Change Controls, Gap Management, Execution Deep Dives, Local Governance).
- Monitor & review enterprise reporting packages (Dashboards, Adherence, etc.) and proactively engage with PMs to take corrective action and escalate when necessary.
- Facilitate enterprise requests: Portfolio Exercises (where applicable), Special Projects & Fire-drills (i.e. Demand & Capacity, FRB Requests, Budget Cuts, ad-hoc reports)
- Approve Tailoring and Kick-Off/Integrated Project Plan. Ensure all tailoring approvals are gained from horizontal process areas. Assist project teams in filling roles on RASIC.
- Conduct Process Assessments before Tollgates 2 & 3 and at Close
- Accountable for Clarity data integrity/quality and adherence to Enterprise Standards
- Escalate process and governance issues & submit Process Improvements
- Maintain program level documentation like org charts, vendor makeup etc.
Process Clarity requests, new ideas

Level Scope: Work is assigned and general instructions given for routine work; more detailed instruction is provided for new projects. Applies knowledge of company policies and standard practices to resolve problems. Analyzes issues and uses judgment to make decision.

Education and Experience: BS and 2 + years or equivalent

Capabilities: (1) Knowledge of Project Management methodologies and tools in relation to tracking progress and reporting project status results

Organizational Competencies

Customer Service Orientation: Understands the Impact of Customer Satisfaction. Values the importance of customer satisfaction and understands how one’s actions impact customer satisfaction. Gauges how quality, reliability and timeliness affect the customer’s work/issues and their perception of company.
Communications: Communications demonstrate concern and interest in the customer’s problems and needs. Written communications are coherent and accurate (e.g., correctly states issues and actions). Communicates frequently with the customer about the status of activities.
Teamwork: Values others' input and expertise and is willing to learn from others. Promotes team cooperation and supports team decisions even if different from own views.
Knowledge Application: Applies new technical approaches to best resolve customer problems. Keeps current with new approaches in the field (e.g., via classes, reading, working with experts).
Business Acumen: Considers cost of various business actions or alternatives in determining customer solutions. Understands the customers of the IT organization and demonstrates a clear commitment to their satisfaction.

Level Specific Competencies

Flexibility: Understands how to work flexibly within organizational policies and procedures.
Problem Solving: Breaks down a problem into smaller parts. Makes multiple causal links: Several potential causes or events, several consequences of actions, or multiple-part chains of events (A leads to B leads to C leads to D). Analyzes relationships among several parts of a problem or situation. Anticipates obstacles and thinks ahead about next steps.
Initiative: Identifies opportunities or problems and acts quickly and decisively to respond to the situation. Takes the lead in gathering necessary data to improve unit performance or solve customer problems.
Professional Development: Actively seeks feedback in order to improve his/her performance. Seeks out information and expertise from individuals in other function groups who have more experience and expertise.
