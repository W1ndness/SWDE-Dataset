Application Analyst, Clinical Systems

About the Job

The Application Analyst, Clinical Systems will play a key role working with nursing and other clinical end users in the implementation, enhancement and support of Meditech and its modules.

The Application Analyst participates in the support, enhancement and implementation of vendor supplied packaged software solutions to meet the needs of CHE and its Regional Health Corporations (RHCs).  This position will participate in the evaluation, planning, design and implementation of application systems for CHE and its RHCs following the CHE project management methodology.  The Application Analyst will assist with the transition of new application systems to support ensuring that any unique requirements are incorporated into the IT Shared Services support model.  The Application Analyst will provide on-going support for installed application systems as assigned by the Manager/Director Application Systems.  This on-going support includes resolution of application system incidents, root cause analysis of problems, impact analysis and planning of proposed system upgrades and patches, analysis and development of enhancements to meet changing customer business requirements, and execution of IT Shared Services change control procedures to ensure all enhancements, upgrades and patches are thoroughly tested and planned before implementation in the production environment.

Requirements

Education, Certifications or Licenses:

§  Bachelor’s degree in a clinical area, such as Nursing, Medical technology, or Pharmacy preferred. A degree in business, healthcare administration, computer technology, information management or related field acceptable.

§  Training in system analysis with special emphasis on system methodologies, project management, and business process reengineering related to information systems.

Experience

§  Minimum 3 to 5 years experience supporting and/or implementing packaged software application systems in a health care setting preferred.  Prior hands-on experience with CHE defined standard systems or similar products from other vendors.

§  Ideal candidate will have hands-on experience with Meditech Client/Server, basic NPR skills, and an understanding of security access and MIS.

§  A clinical background is desired.

Skills

§  Demonstrated knowledge of application systems and the business processes they enable.  Track record of improving business processes using information technology.

§  Proven track record in application system implementation and support.  Creative, innovative and flexible in responding to rapidly changing needs and priorities.

§  Effective interpersonal skills, with the ability to articulate verbally and in writing.  Ability to develop effective working relationships with all levels of staff and management.  Comfortable working in a team-oriented, enterprise organization.

§  Ability to work on multiple assignments simultaneously and maintain composure in highly stressful situations.

§  Computer literacy with strong knowledge of Microsoft Office Applications (i.e. Word, Excel, Power Point, Project, Visio).

§  Strong analytical and problem solving skills.

§  Attention to detail.

§  Knowledge of IT functions and their interdependencies.

§  Ability to demonstrate a high degree of independent judgment, discretion, and confidentiality.

Language skills:

§  Candidate must have the ability to read, analyze, and interpret general business periodicals, technical procedures, or governmental regulations. 

§  Ability to write reports, business correspondence, and procedure manuals.

§  Ability to effectively present information and respond to questions from groups of managers, clients, customers, and the general public.

Physical demands:

§  While performing the duties of this job, the employee is regularly required to sit for long periods of time; use hands to finger, handle or feel; reach with hands and arms; and talk or hear.

§  Specific vision abilities required by this job include close vision, depth perception, and ability to adjust focus.
