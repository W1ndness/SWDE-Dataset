NPO Information Technology Director

About the Job

NPO Information Technology Director

National Procurement Office

93,827-117,283-146,604

NPO01

 

 

The National Procurement Office has an immediate opening for a Director, reporting to the Vice President of this team.  In this critical role, you will direct the information systems operations, analytical, financial and administrative activities of the department including the integration of personal computers, mainframe, and telecommunication resources to assist this department of 15 and the Federal Reserve System Procurement community in accomplishing everyday activities.  You will analyze, develop, test and maintain software from external vendors as well as maintain broad knowledge of cost-effective application of state-of-the-art data processing activities.  This role will monitor new and future developments in information technology as well as develop, coordinate and assist in the completion of automation projects and information security programs for the Federal Reserve System as it relates to National Procurement initiatives. 

***The deadline for applying on line is 5:00PM on Thursday, December 16, 2010

***The market range for this opening is $80,784-$126,225 annually (hiring range will be based on the selected candidate’s skills and qualifications as they relate to the job description)

***Selected candidate subject to special background check procedures

 
Additional Responsibilities

·         Provides technical expertise and consultation to other areas of automation, senior management, and end users to resolve operational problems and promote operational effectiveness. Meets with user departments to determine the information technology needs for the business function.

·         Manages and provides consulting services for automation projects. Assists other technical support staff with the integration of automation, information security, network, and communication systems.

·         Performs and reviews automation evaluation studies of new technologies. Coordinates the development of automation project plans for new systems and automation backup/recovery and contingency plans. Monitors testing and implementation to ensure that standards and procedures are followed. May coordinate with vendors to resolve problems.

·         Maintains current knowledge of automation systems, technology, and products to provide guidance on automation, information security and access control issues. Attends seminars and formal classes, and reviews various technical education material.

·         Manages the resolution of software malfunctions on vendor provided software. Coordinates area activities in the research and solution of these failures. Provides technical assistance in evaluating and correcting application malfunctions. Assists in the creation and testing of system backup/recovery and contingency procedures.

·         Recommends changes in staffing and/or equipment to maximize operational effectiveness. Allocates resources to ensure District goals and objectives are met.

·         Develops policies and procedures for the department and other districts to promote effectiveness and compliance with established procedures and standards.

·         Participates as a Bank representative in special studies, projects or assignments which have District, System, community or industry impact.

·         Directs the development, improvement, maintenance and implementation of new software.

·         Develops subordinates. Ensures that all staff are adequately trained and developed to effectively perform their duties, and that employee training and development activities are effective. Evaluates, counsels, and provides guidance to subordinates concerning job performance and development.

·         Recommends and initiates associated personnel actions. Administers disciplinary actions as necessary.

·         Proficiency with Independent Judgment/Complexity - Works with strategic guidance from officer but clearly leads NPO IT efforts.  Must accommodate technical support and training needs of NPO, business leaders, and Districts.  Consequence of error is significant.

·         Controls expenses within department and is responsible for meeting budget goals and objectives. Provides input to policy level direction regarding standards and budget constraints. Confers with subordinates on administrative policies and procedures, technical problems, priorities and methods.  Consults with personnel in other Districts or business lines to coordinate activities. Prepares activity and progress reports regarding information systems operations.  May represent the District in system level conferences, workgroups or significant audit discussions.

·         Develops department goals and plans to meet those goals. Prepares and administers the department budget. Recommends and approves performance goals, standards, procedures, and project objectives. Develops and approves methods to monitor effectiveness and compliance with established standards and procedures.

 

Education/Technical Competencies/Requirements

·         Master’s degree in any business discipline (strongly preferred)

·         5-7+ years of related work experience (required)

·         Previous supervisory/leadership experience to manage/develop and mentor a team of 4 direct reports (preferred)

·         Proven interpersonal skills including Group leadership and management (required)

·         Strong analysis and decision making abilities along with verbal/written communication skills and self management (required)

·         Strong analytical competency and project management skills (required)

·         Working knowledge of competitive bidding processes (preferred)

·         Working knowledge of generally accepted acquisition policies, procedures, and methods (preferred)

·         Extensive knowledge of e-sourcing application technical requirements (preferred)

·         IT and project management background (required)

·         Business line management experience or graduate education (strongly preferred)
