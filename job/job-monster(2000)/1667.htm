Digital Lab Assistant

About the Job

 
 

DATE AVAILABLE: October 2010

 

JOB TITLE: Fine Arts: Digital Lab Assistant (Part-Time)

 

DEPARTMENT: Fine Arts

 

STATUS: Non-Exempt

 

REPORTS TO: Fine Arts Digital Systems Administrator and Department Chair

 

POSITION OVERVIEW:  The Fine Arts Digital Lad Assistant will aid students and faulty with their projects as needed, perform maintenance tasks to help the lab operate smoothly, and oversees the equipment cage.

 

 

DUTIES & RESPONSIBILITIES:

 

 

  • Assist students and faculty with their projects as needed.
  • Provide technical instructions to students in the form of tutorials and workshops.
  • Assist Faculty in their use of the lab resources during classes that are run in the lab.
  • Ensure that all lab equipment is in working order to include computers, projectors, software programs, cameras, lighting and sound equipment.
  • Oversee equipment cage and track equipment repairs.
  • Assist with the daily operations of the Digital Lab and CNC room by performing systems maintenance tasks assigned by the Systems Administrator. This includes installing software, running network services, installing and trouble shooting hardware, resolving hardware and software issues
  • Build documentation of the Digital Lad resources and produce instructional tutorials (video ad PDF) to populate the Digital Lab Knowledge base wiki.

 

 

QUALIFICATIONS:

 

  • BFA in Fine Arts or a related art discipline.
  • Thorough Knowledge of digital video production workflows and related issues
  • Knowledge of Apple Final Cut Pro Studio, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe After Effects, Max/MSP/Jitter.
  • Knowledge of 3-D modeling with Maya, 3DS MAX, or solidworks is a plus.
  • Should be comfortable working with Apple OS X operating system, including networking, installing, and troubleshooting.
  • A strong interest in an experimental, non-traditional approach to the use of digital technology in a Fine Arts context is essential.



 
 
The School of Visual Arts (SVA) in New York City is an established leader and innovator in the education of artists.
From its inception in 1947, the College has instituted numerous educational innovations, 
including the selection of professionals working in the arts and art-related fields as instructors. 
SVA provides an environment that nurtures creativity, inventiveness and experimentation, enabling students 
to develop a strong sense of identity and a clear direction of purpose.
 
Find out what it’s like to work at SVA.  Visit www.sva.edu/workingatsva.  
 
To apply for this position, please send a cover letter and resume.  No walk-ins please.
