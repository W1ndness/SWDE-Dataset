Information / Network System Specialist

About the Job

Job Summary:  The Information/Network Systems Specialist is responsible for the administration of the system network; and installs, configures, and troubleshoots the network system and other in-house computer equipment; and for understanding the Interactive Television Network, with limited trouble shooting responsibilities.

Essential Job Duties and Responsibilities

¨        Install, upgrade, and configure network printing, directory structures, rights, security, and software on fileservers.

¨        Evaluate and recommend acquisition of computer and software products.

¨        Perform network troubleshooting to isolate and diagnose common network and Internet problems.

¨        Prepare documentation such as instructional or procedural manuals.

¨        Modify hardware or software.

¨        Design and implement network configurations, both internal and external.

¨        Serve as a technical resource for agency information systems planning.

¨        Serve as liaison to vendors and suppliers.

¨        Provide training and technical support in use of software products, i.e.: Word Processing, Spreadsheets, E-mail.

¨        Provide technical support for the Network Management Administrator (NMA) and for the E-911 system.

¨        Troubleshoot and solve hardware and software problems.

¨        Respond to the needs and questions of users concerning their access of resources on the network.

¨        Establish network users, user environment, directories and security for networks being installed.

¨        Troubleshoot CPE equipment associated with special circuits.

¨        Build special reports as needed.

¨        Responsible for paperwork associated with software licensing, inventory and reference material.

¨        Perform trouble shooting of the computer system with the assistance of the Computer Technician.

¨        Work closely with VisionNet and Nemont Communications, Inc. employees to operate and trouble shoot the interactive television systems within the company area.

¨        Provide technical support for the Sun Work Stations, Uninterrupted Power Sources, and Application Peripheral (AP).

¨        Provide Network design for outside customers.

¨        Assist with design of Internet network.

¨        Provide back-up Internet support.

Knowledge Skills and Abilities

¨       Must have a good working knowledge of information systems and the ability to maintain all company systems.

¨       Working knowledge of UNIX a plus.

¨       Must learn the interactive television systems.

¨       Capable of working with and understanding network problems.

¨       Able to communicate well to assist company personnel with hardware/software problems.

¨       Able to learn and apply new technologies as they become available.

¨       Able to pay close attention to numerous details. 

¨       Able to maintain pleasant manner and possess ability to work with internal/external customers and interpret problems they may have with computer work stations and software programs.

¨       Able to work well in stressful situations and/or under pressure.

¨       Able to obtain and maintain a valid Montana driver’s license, have a good driving record, and be insured under the Cooperative’s insurance policy.
