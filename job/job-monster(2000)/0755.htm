About the Job

Project Manager

IT-Application Development
86,971-108,714-135,892

ITAD40

 

The Information Technology department has an immediate opening for a Project Manager on the Application Development team.  In this critical role, your focus is primarily on planning and being responsible for making decisions in such a way that risk is identified and controlled and uncertainty minimized.  This position is a facilitator. You will do whatever it takes to ensure that the members of the software project team can do their work. This means working with senior management to ensure that necessary resources are available as well as dealing with team issues that are negatively impacting a team's productivity. The ideal candidate must possess a combination of skills including the ability to ask penetrating questions, identify unstated assumptions, and resolve personnel conflicts along with more systematic project management skills.

A Project Manager is also a resource manager, working to ensure that her/his Application Development projects are staffed sufficiently.  In this role, she/he is expected to oversee her/his team’s Career Development Plans and provide guidance in the creating and maintaining of Performance Objectives for each individual.  This person will also create and maintain the necessary direct reports budget as well as perform an ongoing assessment of team’s training needs

 

***The deadline for applying on line is 5:00 PM on Friday, December 17, 2010

***Hiring range is $86,971-$108,714 annually

***Candidates subject to special background check procedures

 

Additional Responsibilities

·         Project Planning - participate in requirements gathering and create project planning documentation such as: Project Management Plan; Project Estimation, including WBS based work effort and project budget; Project Scope Management Plan; Project Schedule; Project Staffing Management Plan; Project Responsibility Assignment Matrix; Risk Management Plan; Risk Register; Implementation Plan; Procurement Management; and Release Planning.

·         Project Monitoring, Control and Reporting - maintain communication with the appropriate parties, ensure that project progresses as planned and manage issues, utilizing project documentation and processes such as: Track and update Project Schedule; Track and update Risk Register; Issue Management; Change Control, including Scope Management; Create and maintain Sprint Backlog; Testing Strategy; and Post-implementation Review.

·         Resource Planning - Understand current and future work to ensure that the appropriate staff is available; Allocate resources to proper projects; Assess current resource allocation and recommend any changes to senior management; Ensure effective utilization of resources; Timesheet approval; Budget creation and management; Skill set analysis; Employee acquisition and departure; Disaster planning; Staff Development.

 

Education/Requirements/Technical Competencies

·         6+ years of experience in software development projects (required)

·         1+ years experience managing projects (required)

·         1+ years supervising and managing resources (required)

·         PMP Certification required within 1 year (required)

·         Bachelor's Degree, or equivalent experience (required)

·         Subject to extended hours 3-5 times per month for problem resolution and completion of priority projects

·         Overnight travel averages 5-10 nights per year for training requirements, project related meetings, and/or District/System meetings

·         May need to be reachable 24 x 7 via blackberry or phone for crisis management purposes

 

Core Competencies

Organization – Must be detail oriented and capable of handling multiple tasks at the same time.
Knowledgeable-Possess basic understanding of PMBOK best practices and demonstrates flexibility and adherence to departmental standards.


Visionary – Maintains a long-term, big-picture view and identifies emerging trends and opportunities, articulates clear strategies to achieve intended results, and provides the resources team members need to be successful in achieving the Bank’s vision.


Teamwork – Solicits and is receptive to input and feedback from team members, pursues continuous learning with others in teams, works to instill and maintain cooperation among team members, and adapts behavior and communication style to be effective in the situation.  Treats all team members with respect.


Effective Communication – Actively listens to others to gain an understanding of different perspectives, provides useful and consultative feedback in a respectful manner, and communicates to appropriate parties immediately upon discovery of potential problems and ensures “no surprises”.


Customer Focus – treats all customers with dignity, respect, and a welcoming attitude, keeps promises and commitments to customers/stakeholders, seeks and obtains mutual agreements to deadlines, quality standards, and other expectations important to the customer, and shows ownership by taking action to ensure customer needs are met.


Inspirational – influences others to align and commit around a common goal.


Change Agent – designs processes and programs that produce visible improvements in capabilities and results.


Talent Builder -   Conducts assessments to determine the talent needed to achieve results, provides team members with challenging development opportunities that are aligned with the organization’s strategic needs, provides useful feedback to team members, and develops diverse teams with complementary capabilities and varied styles.
