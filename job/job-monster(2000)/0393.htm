Enterprise Architect

About the Job

Our ideal Enterprise Architect is a strong technical leader with in-depth and extensive expertise in a broad discipline of systems and technologies, including, but not limited to: database management and access; operating systems management and monitoring; software engineering with various programming languages, methods, and orientations; data design, management and governance principles; Service Oriented Architecture (SOA) and other architectural orientations; and, process modeling and engineering. The Enterprise Architect will leverage their expertise to specify the high-level architecture, design, technology and standards of a system. The Enterprise Architect will collaborate with project stakeholders to build systems to enable the integration of Enterprise and Line of Business information and business processes.

The Enterprise Architect will be highly familiar with common technologies and terms including SOAP, REST, XML, WSDL, WS-*, SAML, BPEL, JDBC, SQL, OWL, RDF, and SPARQL, as well as Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC), systems management and performance optimization. The Enterprise Architect will apply his or her knowledge of open standards, software development, technology, and architectural patterns to develop solutions that satisfy customer requirements aligned with the time and cost aspects of the project.  The Enterprise Architect will provide strategic technical direction for system integration efforts and serve as an evangelist of the architectural vision, strategy, and principles to internal staff, customers, analysts, and leadership.

Other areas of responsibility include the following:

1.      Technology Oversight: Maintain an ongoing awareness of emerging developments and trends in technology that could impact current projects and influence new projects.

2.      Vendor Tool Analysis: Lead evaluation of software vendors targeted for possible integration into the systems or environment, including strategic applications, tools, and utilities.  Provide oversight and guidance of new product initiatives ensuring adherence to architectural principles.

3.      Evangelism: Create and deliver detailed technical presentations of architectural vision, strategy, and principles to staff, customers, analysts, and leadership.

4.      High-level technical consultation with and mentoring of development staff.

5.      Architecture Implementation: Plan and implement technical Enterprise Architecture, including both short and long term strategies for maintaining, integrating or retiring existing systems, and implementing new systems.  Refine, document, and communicate architectural objectives. 

6.      Customer Technical Resource: Serve as the technical resource and reference for customer integrations, help define appropriate customer support responses, and work directly with customers on high-level issues.
