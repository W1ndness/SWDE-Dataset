Cyber Infrastructure Engineer

About the Job

TO APPLY CLICK HERE: http://jobs.ucsd.edu/bulletin/job.aspx?cat=information&sortby=post&jobnum_in=51971


What is known about the world’s oceans comes largely from ship-based scientists taking samples and making measurements at periodic intervals. Now researchers led by Scripps Institution of Oceanography and the California Institute for Telecommunications and Information Technology at the University of California, San Diego are creating the blueprint for the Cyberinfrastructure that will allow ocean scientists to observe, analyze and interact with ocean in real time 24 hours a day, seven days a week.

The Consortium for Ocean Leadership of behalf of the National Science Foundation, has selected UC San Diego as the implementing organization to design and construct the information technology and networking for the Ocean Observatories Initiative (OOI). The initial Cyberinfrastructure (CI) award is for five years, with renewable funding for an additional 5 years. The OOI's four implementing organizations are UCSD for the CI, University of Washington and Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution for the deployment of the interactive marine networks at multiple scales, and a “still to be awarded” fourth organization for the Education and Public Engagement program.

As the Infrastructure Engineer of the OOI Cyberinfrastructure Operations and Maintenance team, this individual is responsible for executing the OOI Cyberinfrastructure operations and maintenance program. The position includes software system administration, federation of information resources, deployment of the system middleware software on all real and virtual servers, monitoring of all systems, end user administration support, data management, and guaranteeing the security and integrity of our systems. This position works under the direction of the Cyberinfrastructure Operations Manager for the OOI Cyberinfrastructure program at the California Institute of Telecommunications and Information Technology (Calit2). This position helps support the research and engineering objectives of the OOI Program in partnership with its leadership, implementing organizations, and research communities.

This position will work closely with the CI Network Engineer to validate that the technical implementation and operation of the information and control integration framework interoperates with incoming data from multiple sensor, computing and information networks at institutional, national and international scales. This person will be responsible for analyzing, developing, implementing, and maintaining a complex computer-based administrative information systems serving the OOI cyberinfrastructure environment. Applications are developed in a large multi-cpu/host, cross-platform networked environment. Development and computing support is performed using various compiled languages, interpreted languages, systems utilities and relational database tools on an Internet client/server environment accessing multiple database servers and various platforms. This IT position is dedicated to core/mission critical needs and must provide high availability of all systems on a 7 x 24 basis. This individual will administer a geographically distributed, enterprise level virtual computing environment to facilitate core production services, development activities, and staging computing resources. The incumbent supports all aspects of the engineering environment throughout the development phases of the project. Supported development tools include Enterprise Architect and DOORS. Collaboration tools and resources include: Confluence; Alfresco; Jira; WebEx; Google docs; EtherPad; Jabber and other chat protocols and clients; LDAP; and various plug-ins for Confluence including security, code review, and status evaluation as well as in-house plug-ins for group deployment.

For more information on the project and its positions, see http://ci.oceanobservatories.org/jobs .

QUALIFICATIONS:
* Bachelor of Science Degree or higher in Computer Science, Electrical Engineering or closely related field or comparable education and experience typically obtained by 7+ years related work experience.
* Demonstrated expertise in designing, building and managing complex computing systems based on service-oriented architectures. Working experience in operations or development of electronic service provisioning, identity management, and supporting virtual environments.
* Demonstrated experience with Java, C, Python, Perl, Bash, XML, Http/Https, database support, complex shell scripting and create cron jobs.
* Demonstrated experience in understanding network protocols, DNS, DHCP, NFS, Apache, Tomcat, SSH, network routing and electronic messaging.
* Proficiency with database use and administration, particularly mysql/mysqladmin and PostgreSQL. Specifically, this individual most know how to complete the installation and configure the databases. The individual must also be able to issue SQL statements to trouble shoot problems and extract information from the databases.
* Specific expertise in implementing distributed systems, cluster computing and expertise with diverse networking, computing and storage systems, installations, and vendors. Specific example technologies include: Globus, VMWare, cloud computing (Amazon/Azure), iRODS, Condor and Nimbus.
* Demonstrated ability in releasing and maintaining high-quality, high-impact web or grid services software. Experience with cloud service providers like Amazon (EC2) and Microsoft (Azure). Experience with deployment and operation of virtual systems.
* Demonstrated work experience in designing, implementing and maintaining security for large-scale computing environments.
* Demonstrated knowledge of hardware and software RAID options for disk storage and ability to manage hundreds of terabytes of storage.
* Comprehensive understanding of computer internal architecture and design to include working knowledge of specific details and design of Linux/Unix operating systems.
