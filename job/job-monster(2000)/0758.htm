ABOUT THE CALIFORNIA MARITIME ACADEMY:
The California Maritime Academy is a specialized campus of the California State University (CSU) system serving a student population of approximately 850. The campus, situated on the shore of the Carquinez Strait 30 miles northeast of San Francisco, currently offers baccalaureate degrees in five disciplines and includes licensing programs for future merchant marines, coast guard and naval reserve officers. The Information Technology Department supports the vision and mission of The California Maritime Academy (Cal Maritime) through the application and integration of technology across the curriculum and business processes. IT provides secure, reliable and stable network and digital services to support the Academy’s business practices, enterprise activities, and its central work of learning, discovery, and engagement. The Information Technology Department welcomes applications for a Systems Administrator.

MAJOR DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:
• Deploy and maintain server and network configuration and monitoring services as part of a proactive approach to server and system maintenance.
• Install server hardware and software platforms and assist in developing best practices for server management lifecycle.
• Maintain, administer and support print, email and file servers.
• Develop and execute automated deployment technologies for server administration.
• Plan, design and deploy services to build and maintain a Microsoft domain environment including LDAP/Active Directory, Windows DNS, Group Policy, WINS, DHCP and related domain services.
• Support the specification, installation, configuration and maintenance of network connectivity devices and services.
• Technical documentation.
• Provide status reports, metrics and project plans for IT infrastructure projects.
• Conform to CMA’s Change Control policies and procedures. Assure proper installation and registration of hardware and software with respective manufacturers and publishers.
• Proactively pursue new technologies that can benefit the University by providing operational cost savings, increased efficiency, enhanced communication, etc. Present technology proposals to the IT NOC Manager for internal review and approval.
• Provide support resolving Network Operations Center (NOC) trouble tickets.

SPECIAL REQUIREMENTS:
Work and visit foreign ports in the summer such as Chile, Japan, and Australia aboard our Training Ship! May be required to provide IT support for up to two months on the annual training cruise aboard the Training Ship Golden Bear. Incumbent will be subject to an annual cruise physical and drug screening prior to participation. The incumbent must also have or be able to obtain, a U.S. Passport to participate in the training cruise.

MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS:
• Bachelor’s degree in Information Systems, Business Administration, Computer Science, or a related field, OR equivalent work experience at the career level.
• MCTS and MCTIP Certifications (or equivalent) required.
• Three or more years of experience in definition, design, deployment and maintenance of enterprise computing platforms including network devices and software, server hardware and operating systems and applications.
• Demonstrated ability to support a multi-platform, networked environment.
• Working knowledge of hardware and software specifications, configurations, capacity and performance planning.
• Ability to administer/deploy Exchange 2003, 2007 or 2010.
• Demonstrated ability to provide support for Windows Server 2003/2008, Windows XP and Windows 7.
• Ability to support Window XP and/or Window 7 desktops.
• Ability to support a diverse environment of third-party applications.
• Possess excellent presentation, written and oral communications skills
• Experience with desktop and server virtualization is a plus

DESIRABLE QUALIFICATIONS:
• Ability to support VoIP server.
• Working knowledge of Windows server scripting and server penetration testing.
• Skilled in the use of network tools to analyze network issues.
• Experience with configuring Cisco switches and routers.
• Working knowledge of tape backup and replication solutions.
• Knowledge of firewalls and wireless controllers.
• Knowledge of Ghost imaging or other similar technology.
• Customer-focused and enjoys working in a technical and consultative role.
• Ability to work effectively with all members of the team or work group and take actions that respect the needs and contributions of others. Strives for the success of the team and can manage priorities and disagreements among project stakeholders with a positive and professional attitude.
• Attentive to details and takes pride in providing high quality of work. Ability to produce work results that are accurate, through and dependable.
• Ability to be a self-starter, who takes initiative, conveys a sense of urgency and drives issues to closure. Ability to analyze and resolve problems in a fast-paced, dynamic business environment.
• Takes ownership in upholding the client's recognized reputation among customers and peers.
• Prior experience in a higher education and/or professional services organization is a plus.
