About the Job

 NEW YORK CITY COLLEGE OF TECHNOLOGY/CUNY


Title: College Laboratory Technician (CLT)
Computer Lab/Shop Specialist

 

Architectural Technology


Position Type: Full-Time Support Staff

Salary:  Commensurate with Experience

Web Site: http://www.citytech.cuny.edu

Closing Date: Open Until Filled

 

Position Description and Duties: 

Manage the operation of model shops and computer laboratories. Provides crucial support for both faculty and students of a rapidly growing and vibrant architecture department, which is one of the largest in the United States. Our Department’s mission is to stimulate and motivate as well as to foster confidence and ensure students’ scholastic and professional success.

  • Supervises Adjunct CLTs working in the labs and model shops, helps develop training materials and guidelines, and carries out training
  • Oversees/spearheads troubleshooting for computer labs and shops
  • Manages and oversees student account administration
  • Sets up HTML or other web-based support pages for the computer labs and shops
  • Assists faculty and students, providing basic training in equipment use
  • Coordinating with the Dean’s office and CIS, assists with computer lab administration and networking, including documentation of system operation
  • Working with Dean’s office, installs and troubleshoots hardware and software for the architecture field, currently including AutoCAD 2010, 3DS Max, Maya, Rhino 4, Adobe Creative Suite 3, Solidworks, Bentley Generative Components, ArcView GIS and Microsoft Works
  • Works with and assists the oversight of computer aided manufacturing lab and wood shop, including system documentation, and development of student tutorials for machine use
  • Maintains familiarity with new developments in architectural related software and hardware, and assists in evaluating options

 

Must understand and implement the informal policies, procedures, and practices necessary to conduct the normal operation of the computer laboratories and shops. Must be familiar with configuring and maintaining computer-based networks.  Strong interpersonal skills necessary for managing and training, as well as good organizational skills are essential.  Have the ability to carry out duties and responsibilities with limited supervision. Makes decisions and establishes work priorities for the operation of the lab and shops.  Supervises Adjunct CLTs and work study students, and works under the Department Chair.

 

Qualifications:

Undergraduate Degree and five years of computer-related experience, or Undergraduate Degree and particularly fitting qualifications/skills for the position.

 

This description is a general statement of required duties and responsibilities performed on a regular and continuous basis. It does not exclude other duties as assigned.
