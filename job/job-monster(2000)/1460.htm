IT Helpdesk Analyst

About the Job

Founded in 1906 as a one-man scrap metal operation, Schnitzer Steel Industries, Inc., has become one of the nation's largest recyclers of scrap metal, a leading provider of used and recycled auto parts and a manufacturer of finished steel products.

We are a stable company who has been in business for over 100 years.
 
With corporate headquarters based in Portland, Oregon, the company is comprised of three vertically integrated business units: the Metals Recycling Business, the Auto Parts Business and the Steel Manufacturing Business.
Schnitzer Steel common stock is traded on the NASDAQ Stock Market under the symbol SCHN. Please refer to our website www.schnitzersteel.com for detailed information regarding our company profile.

General Position Summary:

The Helpdesk Analyst reports to the Helpdesk Manager.  Functions as a point of contact, for the acceptance and ownership of all information technology issues, inquiries and requests submitted by Schnitzer internal company users via the telephone and email.

Job Scope:

The primary function of this job includes responding to requests for service from internal customers.  Manages priorities, understanding requirements, developing solutions, and implements solutions.  This job demands a significant amount of multi tasking and the ability to work under pressure.

Essential Functions:

1.    Assist end-users in identifying and solving problems related to computers, hardware, software, and other related items via the telephone.
2.    Support, and troubleshoot Windows desktop operating systems and Microsoft Office applications for users.
3.    Provide accurate and complete descriptions of problems, inquiries and requests in a problem management ticket database.
4.    Create, dispatch and manage service requests.
5.    Escalate problems and requests according to established procedures.
6.    Maintain accountability and ownership of tickets.
7.    Analyze operating system and hardware anomalies and provide for problem resolution.
8.    Monitor desktop/laptop performance and identify issues for resolution
9.    Provide rotating 24 x 7 on-call support for hardware/software problems and priority outage notifications.
10.    Creation and maintenance of knowledgebase documents as needed.
11.    Provide occasional one-on-one training on Windows application and email usage
12.    Report progress and status to management
13.    Responsible for developing and implementing process improvements
14.    Establish and document internal procedures
15.    Provide coordination between infrastructure, operations and application development teams.



Qualifications:
1.    Education:  Preferred AS or AA in Computer Science; Electronic Engineering or equivalent experience, and applicable training across multiple hardware/software platforms.
2.    Experience: Minimum of two to three years of Helpdesk experience and/or equivalent in technical support role.
3.    Certifications: Preferred Comptia A+ (Hardware & Software), Comptia N+, MCP (Microsoft Certified Professional), MOUS Advanced (Microsoft Office User).
4.    Make appropriate use of technical documentation in resolving technical problems
5.    Possess an in-depth understanding of communications and network applications
6.    Apply a systematic approach to troubleshooting and problem solving
7.    Possess a highly developed understanding of multiple product specific hardware, software and operating systems in a complex computing environment
8.    Prioritize and manage own workload
9.    Courteous, helpful and tactful in all communications
10.    Assist and instruct Schnitzer in the use of his/her End-User Machine/Software
11.    All personnel must have patience in communication and well developed oral and written English language skills
12.    Advanced Customer Service skills


Physical Activities Required to Perform Essential Functions:

Ability to: sit for up to eight hours per day; type and/or keyboard for a few hours per day; be mobile within a confined, crowded area; communicate by speech and hearing in person and by Phone in a professional manner.  Visual acuity to perform close detail work, operating a computer.  Ability to: write by hand and/or keyboard for extended periods of time, up to 6-8 hours per day; bend at waist, twist upper body, stand, walk, and negotiate uneven surfaces; assemble papers, stable, and file; apply sufficient force to open and close file drawers.  

Supervisory Responsibility:
This position involves no supervisory responsibilities.

Interpersonal Contacts:

This position involves phone, written, and face-to-face contact with all levels of employees from entry level clerks to executive officers.  Communication ranges from answering simple questions to discussing complex problems.   External contacts by phone, in writing or face-to-face include management and executives, consultants, and other IT professionals.  Work effectively with others and build relationships both within Schnitzer and among various levels of customers


Job Conditions:  
Normal office environment.  On-call and occasional overtime required.  Extreme pressures at times when dealing with deadlines and volume of work.
