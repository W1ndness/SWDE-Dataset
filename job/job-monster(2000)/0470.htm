Epic Program Director

About the Job

 

The Epic Program Director is responsible for the successful planning and execution of a multi-year, multi-facility, enterprise-wide clinical and revenue cycle systems implementation. The Epic Program Director reports directly to the CIO and directs the work of the project leadership and staff in their respective applications/departments. The Epic Program Director maintains the scope and work plan and ensures that all milestones and deliverables are on time and budget. The Program Director is tasked with the internal communication of project strategies, high level deliverables, plans, and schedules. Ensures all outcomes and deliverables meet organizational goals, objectives and quality standards. Major responsibilities include the definition, adoption and consistent execution of system requirements, communication, change management, issues management, and post-implementation support. S/he must propose measures of success and targeted outcomes and execution strategies. The Epic Program Director, will direct, lead and manage the development and implementation of this high-profile information system transformation project that will have a strategic impact on the core operations and processes of the organization for both the inpatient and outpatient environments.

 

Job summary:

- Epic Program Director will have in depth knowledge of hospital & ambulatory operations in multi-facility health system.

- Epic Program Director will be experienced in the full implementation of the enterprise wide electronic medical record system (EPIC Experience Preferred).

- Epic Program Director will be responsible for completion of project tasks within a timeline and budget.

- Epic Program Director will be the primary contact for the EMR technology portfolio and will coordinate projects and communications to all impacted stakeholders.

- Requires the ability to communicate with project leadership, clinicians and technical staff.

- Must be a champion for all projects in his/her portfolio across the institution and serve as a positive role model for encouraging others to embrace operational changes associated with the implementation and other application technology projects.

 

Requirements:


- Demonstrated success in Program Management in full life cycle, enterprise wide, implementation of both clinical and revenue cycle applications in both the acute care and ambulatory environments.

- Exceptional leadership, organizational and project management skills.

- Understanding of health care industry service delivery processes, preferably in a multi-facility health system.

- Ability to establish and maintain project management methodologies for large, complex projects and ensure effective utilization across all project teams

- Experience with oversight of multiple simultaneous complex projects.

- Previous leadership experience with management of multi-million dollar project budgets.

- Six years of progressive IT experience or a combination of education and experience in an IT environment required

- Must have direct managerial experience supervising and managing staff of 50+ professional and technical staff.

- Ability to establish and maintain project management methodologies for large, complex projects and ensure effective utilization across all project teams

- Demonstrated ability to solve highly complex technical, organizational and operational problems.

- Significant competency in vendor management

- Experience in establishing and maintaining appropriate and effective project governance structures for large, complex organizations

- Demonstrated ability to communicate, collaborate and consult effectively with all levels of the organization

- Demonstrated knowledge of outcomes management and process improvement methods

- Demonstrated understanding of enterprise wide healthcare applications suite with a focus on the acute care, clinical and revenue cycle applications is required; Epic experience is preferred.
