SOFTWARE ENGINEER

About the Job

Weill Cornell Medical College, New York, NY

SOFTWARE DEVELOPER

Weill Cornell Medical College seeks a highly motivated software developer to work on the development, implementation and testing of a C/C++ Linux software system for controlling cardiac and neuronal experiments. The system is known as RTXI (www.rtxi.org). Job role includes: serving as the main developer for the custom-developed C/C++ real-time experiment interface system; testing RTXI software with experiment apparatus and working with experimentalists to ensure that the software is user friendly and fully functional; assisting experimentalists in writing experiment-control code modules; working with end users in other laboratories to enable them to install and use RTXI and ensure that RTXI is widely adopted and used; and performing other job related duties as required.

Requirements: Bachelor's degree in computer science or a related field is required; experience programming in C/C++ in the Linux environment preferred; previous experience coding real-time or embedded systems, Qt, kernel module development, scientific computing, numerical methods are all pluses; excellent organization and communication skills.

Excellent benefits package (includes tuition reimbursement).

Please use our online application system and submit a cover letter, contact information (including email address) for three references, and resume at:

http://weill.cornell.edu/hr

Please refer to Job Number 13548

Only applicants selected for interviews will be contacted.

Founded in 1898, and affiliated with what is now New York-Presbyterian Hospital since 1927, Weill Cornell Medical College is among the top-ranked clinical and medical research centers in the country. In addition to offering degrees in medicine, Cornell also has Ph.D. programs in biomedical research and education at the Weill Graduate School of Medical Sciences, and with neighboring Rockefeller University and the Sloan-Kettering Institute, has established a joint MD-PhD. program for students to intensify their pursuit of Cornell's triple mission of education, research, and patient care.

Weill Cornell Medical College's educational mission emphasizes the importance of combining a strong foundation in the medical sciences with extensive clinical training in patient care. By promoting a true social commitment, stimulating creativity, and fostering independent thought and study, Weill Cornell Medical College continues to cultivate the best of tomorrow's leaders in the field of medicine.
