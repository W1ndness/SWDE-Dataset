Chief Information Officer

About the Job

The Chief information Officer provides both visionary and pragmatic leadership for accountability, research, and technology within the Division, working collaboratively with the Superintendent, Assistant Superintendents, Director of Secondary Education, and Director of Elementary Education to support the Division’s vision, mission, and goals.  Supports division-wide use of advanced technologies to improve student learning, accelerate and scale up the adoption of effective practices, and use data and information for continuous improvement as outlined in the National Education Technology Plan 2010.  Responsible for direct supervision and oversight of assessment and technology functions.  Essential functions include, but are not limited to the following:

  • Works with the School Board, Superintendent, Assistant Superintendents, Director of Secondary Education, and Director of Elementary Education to assure the organization achieves its mission, goals, priorities, and measurable targets;
     
  • Works with administrative and instructional leaders to identify and promote the use of current and emerging technologies to support excellence in student learning and to improve services for all stakeholders;
  • Provides strategic and tactical planning, development, evaluation, coordination, and maintenance of information, technology systems, and related infrastructure throughout the Division,  examining ongoing methods to effect improvements, reduce cost, manage risk, and enhance efficiency in order to leverage maximum return on investment;
  • Assists in development of Division priorities and measurable targets outlined in the Division's Strategic Plan; 
  • Evaluates, forecasts, and monitors total cost of ownership (TCO) and return on investment (ROI) for administrative and instructional technology initiatives;
  • Works with the Board, Superintendent, and Assistant Superintendents throughout the budget process, looking for innovative ways to minimize cost and maximize results;
  • Leads the development and implementation of the Division’s comprehensive Educational Technology Plan to ensure efficient, effective, and innovative use of technology resources in support of the Division’s vision, mission, and goals;
  • Directs and/or consults with others on the governance, policies, processes, and best practices related to the collection and management of data;
  • Supervises administration of local and required state testing programs, development and maintenance of division databases and information management systems; data collection, data management, and production of reports;
  • Supervises development of data for effective monitoring of progress toward division goals and measurable targets, supporting  Division- and school-based achievement teams with relevant information and data;
  • Supervises development of annual progress report, balanced scorecard, and key metric reports;
  • Directs the review, analysis, and interpretation of Division, school and student performance data, working with leaders to aggressively turn data into actionable information in support of Division and school improvement;
  • Works with key leaders to evaluate and manage organizational risk, establishing the appropriate information security measures and risk management plans to ensure business continuity and legal compliance;
  • Summarizes and reports Division, school, and student performance data to the Board, Superintendent, Division and school personnel, and community groups;
  • Directs review and approval of internal and external research projects;
  • Submits reports to the Assistant Superintendents, Superintendent, School Board, and Virginia Department of Education;
  • Participates in organization teamwork concept; and performs other task as assigned.

QUALIFICATIONS:  Master's degree in appropriate area of study is required, doctorate preferred; considerable professional and successful experience (at least 10, with at least 5 years of supervision) in a position responsible for organizational change management, information management, comprehensive planning, research, evaluation, and/or organizational improvement; knowledge of state and federal laws, regulations, and requirements regarding school accreditation, achievement testing is preferred. 

Experience in leading organizational change management initiatives through the effective use of data and technology; demonstrated success and multi-year experience leading complex organizational improvement initiatives; demonstrated ability to efficiently and effectively manage both fiscal and human resources; successful track record working with internal and external constituencies (including vendors); extensive experience in evaluating and managing risk; proven experience in forecasting return on investment and total cost of ownership; demonstrated ability in effective reporting, written and oral communications, and interpersonal relations; ability to develop and execute a strategic plan; ability to operate in a collegial environment; demonstrated analytical skills, conceptual skills and problem solving capabilities; demonstrated ability to articulate a vision and motivate people to act with a shared vision; high level of energy; enthusiastic commitment to excellence and  student learning.  Comprehensive knowledge of K-12 curriculum, instruction, and assessment, including knowledge-based decision making and school improvement planning is preferred.

PHYSICAL CONDITIONS AND NATURE OF WORK CONTACTS:  Work is typically performed in an office setting primarily sitting at office equipment and files. Frequent operation of computer office equipment is required. Frequent travel to school sites throughout the county and occasional statewide, regional, or national travel is required. Attendance at meetings outside the normal duty hours is frequently required. There is regular involvement with the superintendent, assistant superintendents, directors, coordinators, principals, and teachers. Usually there is need to motivate, establish rapport, gain support, persuade or influence individuals or groups. Contacts often require tact and discretion. Contacts with state and federal agencies, the Albemarle County School Board, advisory councils, faculties, students, parents, and the general public are regularly required. Debate is often characteristic of the contact and may be non-cooperative.
