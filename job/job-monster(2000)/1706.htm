IT Help Desk Administrator

About the Job

Primary Job Function:

Provide administrative and secretarial support for the IT Department. 


Maintain and support all aspects of the local and wide area networks. 

Handle help desk requests and provide extensive and detailed technical support to the staff.

 

Responsibilities:

Coordinate and assist in the management of the IT Help Desk process.  

Handle incoming service requests. 

Provide support as needed including password management, security level adjustments, network account creation and deletion, file transfer and deletion, etc. 


Provide 24/7 support for users. 


Disperse service requests to the IT staff as required. 


Ensure that responses and service are provided in a timely fashion. 

Maintain open communication with the operating departments so that the users are kept up-to-date on status of requests. 

Provide administrative duties for the IT Department staff including tracking and distributing mail, producing documentation and reports, answering incoming phone calls and responding via voice or E-mail. 


Maintain inventory of all PC and network hardware and peripherals. 


Track equipment distribution, serial numbers, statistics, life cycle, etc. for all equipment.  

Maintain software licensing for all departments. 


Track purchases, review reports and ensure that credit union is fully compliant. 

Place and track purchase orders for hardware, software and PC peripherals for each operating department. 

Ensure that all requests are properly documented and approved by the Accounting Department. 

Collect and distribute software applications for IT and operating department staff. 

Search and download files and documentation from Internet and bulletin board sites.  

Maintain software inventory for IT at all MCU locations specifically for disaster recovery needs.  

Coordinate the tape backup distribution process for all locations including MCU’s disaster recovery site.  

Maintain and support the local and wide area network onsite as required.  

Keep entire IT Department abreast of new and emerging applications and technology, particularly in the financial services industry, utilizing all types of media including print and online.  

Perform other duties as assigned.

 

Requirements

College degree preferred.

Combination of education and work experience.  

Local and Wide Area Network experience.  

Comprehensive understanding of the functionalities of the credit union’s operating departments.  

Excellent oral and written communication skills.  

Excellent analytical skills.  

Superior problem solving and reasoning ability.

 

Municipal Credit Union (MCU) is an Equal Opportunity Employer.
