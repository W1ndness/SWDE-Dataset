About the Job

Computer Network Defense (CND) Auditor

Adelphi, MD

Description:

As a Computer Network Defense (CND) Auditor, the incumbent applies technical and functional expertise to provide support to the High Performance Computing Modernization Program (HPCMP) Computer Network Defense Service Provider (CNDSP) customer base. 

Responsibilities include:

§  Perform monthly and ad hoc vulnerability scans of unclassified and classified network subscriber enclaves.

§  Maintain knowledge of latest Information Assurance Vulnerability Alerts (IAVAs) to ensure subscribers are remediating against known threats.

§  Assist subscribers with vulnerability remediation as necessary.

§  Conduct trending and correlation of monthly results to detect low-visibility vulnerabilities impacting the network.

§  Test and evaluate new technologies, specifically related to network vulnerability scanning.

§  Maintain knowledge of applicable CND policies, regulations, and compliance documents specifically related to CND auditing.

§  Prepare reports for subscribers to assess technical configurations and compliance.

§  Generate monthly capture of the network(s) security posture and provide to Program Office for situational awareness.

§  Document policies and procedures for the use of vulnerability assessment tools.

§  Provide support to Vulnerability Assessment Support (VAS) Lead as necessary.

Must obtain Security+ certification and Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH) certification within six (6) months.

Required Experience:

   Understanding of Information Assurance (IA) and Computer Network Defense (CND) concepts, practices and tools to design and administer classified and unclassified DoD computer networks and systems.

§  Ability to communicate complex technical and programmatic information, often in the form of verbal and visual operational updates, situation reports and briefings.

§  Computer helpdesk and customer service experience.

§  Familiarity with CJCSM 6510 and DoD 8570.1

§  Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT) or Security Operations Center (SOC) operations

Education Requirements:

Undergraduate or Graduate degree in computer science, information security, information systems management, or technology management.

Additional:

WareOnEarth Communications, Inc., an SMS company, leverages our vast background in Information Assurance (IA) and Security (InfoSec) for cutting edge, mission critical government network solutions (e.g. Department of Defense [DoD], Department of Treasury, DEA). WCI provides a unique blend of extensive experience, exposure, knowledge and expertise that puts us ahead of the pack and our clients ahead of the curve when it comes to infrastructure protection.

We enable our clients to mitigate risks before incidents occur and interrupt business. WareOnEarth provides a wide range of Information Assurance and Security services including Vulnerability Assessments, which are designed to evaluate the effectiveness of our clients’ infrastructure protection so they can discover and address vulnerabilities before someone else does.

Due to the nature of work performed in our facility, U.S. citizenship is required.

Candidate must possess and active Secret Clearance or be eligible to obtain an Interim Secret Clearance.


WareOnEarth Communications, Inc. offers outstanding compensation and benefits including matching 401K plan, up to $6,000 annual Tuition Reimbursement, 10 vacation days and 10 paid holidays per year.

Replies will be sent only to those candidate meeting position requirements.
