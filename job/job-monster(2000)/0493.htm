About the Job

The Department of Anesthesiology at Washington University in St. Louis strives to foster an academic culture in which clinical care, research and education are seamlessly integrated. We are organized in subspecialty-based divisions, all of which share a common research and educational platform. The department places a strong emphasis on innovative education and comprehensive, personalized clinical training. Our goal is to develop leaders in all aspects of anesthesiology, including clinicians, educators and investigators. Our ACGME-approved, subspecialty fellowship programs in critical care, cardiothoracic anesthesiology, pain management and pediatric anesthesiology are particular strengths of the department. Through its leadership, professionalism, commitment to service, academic productivity and clinical robustness, the Anesthesiology Department at Washington University commands the respect of colleagues in other specialties, of the hospital communities which we engage, and of the community which we serve.  The Department of Anesthesiology has three major missions:  We are dedicated to providing state-of-the art patient care in the areas of preoperative evaluation, intraoperative anesthesia, post-operative critical care and pain management.
We are committed to providing the highest quality of undergraduate and postgraduate medical education. We provide a well-organized and comprehensive residency program for training both consultants in clinical anesthesiology and clinician-scientists in anesthesiology.
We strive, through our basic science and clinical science programs, to make major contributions to the science of anesthesiology and to spearhead major advances in the clinical practice of anesthesiology.


Essential Functions

The Website Developer will expand and enhance the Department of Anesthesiology's online presence through new website content development, feature sections, and timely postings. The position uses technical and relationship skills that will allow broad functionality to be developed and implemented. The Developer will "own" the site and inherently understand the requirements of site content and develop it accordingly.
Essential functions -
-Has the relationship and technical skills to take the Department Marketing Vision of the website and translate it to the finished product.
-Technically develops the site content using Microsoft Visual Studio, IIS, ASP.net, VB.net, JavaScript, Photoshop and other department standard software.
-Ensure that all web site content is uploaded to the site on a timely basis, refreshed and updated as needed and that information and its design flow adheres to department specifications.
-Maintain cross-platform and cross-browser compatibility so that the web site is accessible from a variety of different environments.
-Evaluate all content for its value in search engine optimization according to key word frequency, key word density, formatting, etc. 
-Produce site traffic analysis and monthly senior management reports and aid in interpreting results and potential impact.
-Design, program, test and document web applications used by and for the department or division
-Perform other related duties as necessary.
-Will work with Chairman and Division Chiefs.


Required Qualifications

Bachelor's degree in Computer Science or equivalent and one to three years of HTML experience in an online production environment.


Preferred Qualifications

-Experience in launching new websites and/or redesigning existing websites (portfolio requested).
-Knowledge of Web development, applications and technology, including Microsoft Visual Studio and IIS, ASP.net, VB.net, HTML 4.0, Java Script, Photoshop, PHP, and HTML editing tools.
-Knowledge of basic principles of interface usability. 
-Written and verbal communication skills, proven ability to develop websites, ability to manage multiple tasks simultaneously.
