Business Solutions Analyst

About the Job

The Business Solutions Analyst is responsible for closely partnering with business to identify business process, functions, and procedures that can be enhanced, streamlined or eliminated through technology solutions. Specifically, this role is responsible for documenting requirements, processes and associated deliverables to design, implement and support Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) modules. Acting as an extended working member of the customer’s organization, the Business Solutions Analyst is responsible for proposing to the customer, technology solution options which will enhance business processes. In addition, the Business Solutions Analyst is responsible for end-to-end management of all associated business technology projects within their function. From initial discussions with the end user/customer about a general business problem, to the final rollout of a new technology solution, the Business Solutions team will act as the coordination point on every aspect of MDC’s business technology efforts.


ESSENTIAL JOB FUNCTIONS (including but not limited to):

1) Customer Interface/ Communication Meet frequently with the end users/customers to ensure that IT is meeting the customer expectations. Understand business process, and work closely with the customer to identify areas of technology opportunities. Work with the customer to prioritize requests and needs, and create/communicate dates and deliverables.
2) Planning Evaluate dynamic business goals, drivers and influences. Partner with the business and other IT teams in order to prepare project proposals for business technology projects, and assist in designing solutions. This includes development of project proposals, vendor analysis, detailed project plans, ROI analysis, project budgets and recommendations to the business.
3) Budgetary Responsibility Prepare and maintain project budgets.
4) Usage Metrics Implement, analyze and distribute system metrics to determine appropriate course of action. Prepare system/business change recommendations based upon analysis of metrics.
5) Corporate Standards Verifies and validates requirements for new projects to ensure alignment with the business, IT strategies, and standards.
6) Training Interface with training department to develop and/or develop effective training programs for new and existing technology systems. Ensure that end users have the tools necessary to complete their responsibilities on systems in a timely manner.
7) Process Re-engineering Performs strategic business research and defines requirements in support of standardizing business processes and the integration of information systems and contributes to continuous process improvement.
8) Travel Requirements This position will require 25 – 30% travel, with the exception of deployment efforts which may require more travel (with the exception of implementation which will require extensive travel through the end of 2010
9) Internal Contact Will interface extensively with business managers, field personnel and Information Technology team members.
10) External Contact Will interface with IT consultants
11) Direct Reports No direct reports.


PREFERRED REQUIREMENTS (unless otherwise indicated):

1) Five or more years experience in Construction Industry, Business Consulting and/or IT. Consulting experience strongly preferred.
2) Knowledge of principles and techniques of information processing related to IT or strategic planning.
3) Strong knowledge of structured analysis and design techniques.
4) Strong project, leadership, problem-solving and people management skills.
5) High-level expertise in written and oral communications.
6) Ability to examine business processes from an IT and business perspective and translate business needs into system requirements.
7) Ability to lead and facilitate effective consensus-building meetings.
8) Ability to multi-task and manage multiple priorities simultaneously.
9) Experience developing IT strategy and implementing enterprise-wide standards.
10) Strong computer skills. Experience with IBM compatible computers and the Windows operating systems preferred. Knowledge of spreadsheet, word processing, and presentation software.
11) Ability to function well in a team-centered environment.
12) Minimum Education Requirement: Bachelor’s degree. MBA preferred.


EQUIPMENT TO BE USED:

Personal computer and related peripherals, telephone and other typical office machines.


TYPICAL PHYSICAL DEMANDS:

Requires sitting, standing, bending and reaching. Requires manual dexterity sufficient to operate standard office equipment such as computers, keyboard, calculator, telephone, and other office equipment. Requires normal range of hearing and vision.


WORKING ENVIRONMENT:

Works in a typical office setting. Occasionally called upon to work evenings and weekends.
