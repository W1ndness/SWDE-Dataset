Programmer Analyst

About the Job

This is a highly technical, professional application software and hardware planning work in support of Treasury information systems implemented on various platforms in the client/server computer environment. The employee must have an in-depth knowledge of application design, program coding, hardware and software installation, document writing, troubleshooting, maintenance of systems and system monitoring. The employee will analyze, plan and recommend new and/or enhancements to application systems including emergency recoveries, migrations, conversions and other transitions. Interfaces with users and vendors of software products.

Programmer Analyst, TA-20 (full performance level) serves as expert in one specialty and performs intermediate to journey level work in secondary specialty to backup primary person on the secondary specialty for limited functions. The needs of the Authority will determine which specialties are required. Directs and trains other Programmer Analysts and approves contractor work. The position reports to the Supervisor, Systems and Technology Development - TRES.

MAJOR DUTIES:

Performs all duties listed in the lower grades within this series plus:

Frequently involved with technical problem-solving and troubleshooting of application systems and provides assistance to junior level programmers in program coding and debugging.

Suggest and implement cost-effective automation and reduction of manual and labor-intensive Authority functions.

Independently designs and codes programs and modules, including database file maintenance and tuning.

Attend to end-user requests regarding any legitimate application system enhancements, issues and resolution.

Assumes senior level support responsibility for a project, either major or small scale, including user interface, scope of work estimates and project preparation planning.

Prepares initial lead time estimates, scope of work and schedules for changes or enhancements to existing systems. Prepares biweekly activity logs and work plans for all those tasks

Adheres to and has considerable knowledge of standards set by the Department of Information Systems.

Implement, install and upgrade major software packages.

Responsible for the maintenance and program enhancement of all in-house developed or packaged software applications.

Develops test scripts and test plans and coordinates with user acceptance or parallel testing requirements and procedures. Utilize end-user involvement during the course of systems development and or enhancement.

Keeps abreast of current developments in the field of Information Technology; develops innovative solutions to a wide range of applications.

Ensures project life-cycle is in compliance with WMATA standards and procedures.

Understand strategic priorities and the ability to anticipate the Authoritys requirements.

The above duties and responsibilities are not intended to limit specific duties and responsibilities of any particular position, nor are they intended to limit in any way the right of the Authority to assign, direct, and control the work of employees under its supervision.

QUALIFICATIONS AND EXPERIENCE:

This class specification series covers application programmer analyst duties on a variety of platforms and with several specialties:

Packaged software
iSeries/System I Applications (COBOL, SEQUEL, SQL, CL, OS400 commands)
Client/Server applications (Windows Server 2003/2008, NT, XP)
Server maintenance and upgrade (security patches and system updates)
External database sources such as ORACLE, DB2, SQL SERVER 2003, ACCESS and others.

Within each specialty there are a variety of sub-specialties. The choice of specialty and sub-specialty will depend on the needs of the Authority. All Programmer Analysts will be expected to specialize in at least one of these areas when hired, depending on the needs of the Authority at that time. The Programmer Analyst will specialize in three or more of these areas in order to reach grade TA-20.

The needs of the Authority will determine which platform/technology will be used and how many Programmer Analysts are required to support each one.

Qualification Requirements

To be considered for the position, you must meet minimum qualifications. It is, therefore, very important for you to include on your application any education/experience you have had that is described in the minimum qualifications. Incomplete information may delay the assessment proces

Qualifications

Graduation from an accredited college with Bachelors degree in Computer Science or Mathematics or other closely related science. A minimum (5) years of progressively responsible experience in the iSeries/AS400, UNIX/AIX, SEQUEL Viewpoint v9.0 computer environment doing program analysis, and/or systems programming directly related to assigned area(s) of responsibility is required.

Or, an equivalent combination of post-high school and education and more than seven (7) years of progressively responsible experience in the iSeries/AS400, AIX/Unix, SEQUEL Viewpoint v9.0 computer environment doing program analysis, and/or system programming directly related to assigned areas of responsibility is required.

The incumbent must be able to perform the essential functions of this position either with or without reasonable accommodation.
