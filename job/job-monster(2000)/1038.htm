About the Job

Web/Print Designer

Job Description
The University of Minnesota is seeking a creative, experienced Web/Print Designer to join our award-winning, in-house design studio. Our creative staff produces a wide range of print and electronic materials for a diverse client base of colleges and departments at the University. The position requires a candidate with experience creatively working with large and small budgets, successfully managing multiple projects under tight deadlines, and effectively communicating with clients throughout the design process. Candidates must combine a strong creative approach with detail-oriented technical skills and must be highly motivated to produce effective, award-winning communications.

Major areas of accountability

Web and Technical Design (40%)
> Creatively design, build, and launch client websites, developing creative solutions within the framework of University brand guidelines; develop information architecture; ensure proper usability as it relates to utilizing design to influence and direct user actions; apply search engine optimization (SEO); apply all best practice approaches.
> Work with other designers at Printing Services to build and launch websites based on their designs.
> Develop HTML e-mail and other forms of electronic communications.
> Develop web templates, including CSS, for University clients.
> Enhance and maintain Printing Services’ website, including coordination of the addition of state-of-the-art storefront ordering capabilities; improve interactive functionality and user experience.

Print Design (30%)
> Design brochures, posters, forms, magazines, annual reports, newsletters, identity systems, and fully coordinated print/web campaigns for new and existing University clients.
> Support other designers with basic design/layout as needed.
> Make corrections/adjustments/updates to previously produced print pieces.
> Prepare print projects for production, including meeting with production staff to determine the most efficient and cost-effective method of production.

Visual Design/Client Relationships (20%)
> Meet with University clients to discuss, conceptualize, and plan print, web, and electronic communication projects, including making expert recommendations on approaches that will improve efficiency, reduce costs, support branding policies, and elevate the effectiveness of University communications.
> Apply creative thinking and problem solving skills to provide professional design appropriate to the target audience for educational and promotional materials on a diverse range of content topics.
> Develop creative solutions, including graphic development and layout/design, while actively supporting the institutional brand in order to leverage the strength of the institution’s identity and advance the mission of the University.
> Work closely with other members of creative team, including designers, customer service representatives, and freelance photographers/writers, as well as coordinate project execution with members of a diverse production staff.
> Meet with and present concepts to colleagues and clients and openly discuss opportunities to evolve and enhance creative concepts in order to improve effectiveness of communications and develop strong, lasting relationships.

Project Management (10%)
> Independently manage multiple projects; set and meet critical dates within the design and execution process; communicate effectively with both internal and external clients; coordinate with colleagues and external vendors; develop and maintain strong relationships with clients.

Minimum Qualifications
> Education: Bachelor’s degree in design or related discipline and two years experience in visual and web design, communications, or similar position.
Any combination of relevant education and experience may be substituted for the educational requirement.
> Experience with graphic design, such as concepting, thoughtful use of typography and color, layout, and visual communication hierarchy.
> Experience with web-based tools, technologies, and practices such as web authoring software (preferably Dreamweaver), HTML, and cascading style sheets (CSS); mastery of the Adobe Creative suite toolset (InDesign, Photoshop, Illustrator); experience with Microsoft Office programs.
> Strong written and verbal communication skills with the ability to listen, articulate your design, and advocate for best practice solutions.
> Strong online and print portfolio which shows versatility in design solutions.

Preferred Qualifications
> Five years experience in visual and web design, communications, or similar positions.
> Interest and enthusiasm for emerging technologies; openness and passion for design trends and new approaches to communicating; solid grasp of latest graphic and web design principles and aesthetics.
> Demonstrated ability to design creatively within a brand visual identity system and manage brand standards.
> Experience and knowledge of web-based tools and technologies including user-centered design, user experience, information architecture, web usability principles, accessibility, web analytics, capabilities of different platforms/browsers, web content management systems, and social media strategy.
> Understanding of current web technologies such as HTML (advanced knowledge), CSS (advanced knowledge), XHTML, Flash, and javascript libraries (jQuery, MooTools).
> Knowledge of design and development of HTML e-mail marketing pieces, including testing to ensure deliverability
> Print production and vendor experience.
> Familiarity with both Mac and PC platforms.
> Ability to proactively self-manage time and tasks with minimal guidance and maintain high, sustained levels of productivity under tight deadlines.
> Experience designing for mobile platforms; experience in designing and executing social media campaigns.
> Experience working in a large institutional or corporate environment.
Salary/Benefits
> Salary determined by experience and education
> Excellent health and dental benefits
> Generous vacation, sick, and holiday leave
> Regents Scholarship (attend classes at the University of Minnesota)
