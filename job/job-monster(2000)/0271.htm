Technical Support Analyst

About the Job

JOB FUNCTION

Configure, install, and repair PC and printer hardware, and support end users in the use of PC hardware and software; serve as on-site liaison with the rest of IT and assist with network and telecom administration.

 

JOB ACTIVITIES/DUTIES

 

1.      Deploy, troubleshoot, and repair PCs and printers in primary facility and remote facilities as necessary.

2.      Assist end users with hardware, software, and network problems through direct contact and through Help Desk

3.      Serve as a primary initial contact for PC hardware and software problems for users on-site

4.      Administer and support mobile devices including PDAs, cell phones, smart phones, etc.

5.      Serve as on-site liaison between local staff and IT staff

6.      Perform administration of phone and voice mail systems

7.      Work with IT staff to maintain adequate parts and supplies for the support of PCs and printers, UPS units, network hardware, etc.

8.      Work with other IT staff to support end users and in the deployment of new applications, systems, and directives; assist with data backups

9.      Other projects as assigned by IT Infrastructure Manager

     

 JOB REQUIREMENTS

Education:     Associate Degree or equivalent experience. Thorough knowledge of PC hardware operation and maintenance; in-depth understanding of MS Windows operating system, knowledge of printer repair, familiarity with LAN concepts.

Experience:    Five years PC and LAN support.

Skills: Ability to diagnose and solve hardware problems, operating system and applications software problems. Ability to configure PC hardware peripherals including modems, printers, network interface cards, disk and CD-ROM drives.

Ability to install, clean and repair printers. Ability to troubleshoot LAN connectivity issues. Ability to work with IT staff to support network devices, UPS, etc. Ability to support phone systems and mobile devices. Good communication and organization skills important for end-user support.

 

 

 

JOB COMPLEXITY

Relationships:

Internal: Heavy contact with PC users on-site for problem resolution and knowledge transfer. Contact with IT staff in Tremonton, Minneapolis and Northfield.

External: Contact with software/hardware vendors to resolve issues. Contact with professional peers to remain current in computer field.

Problem Solving:

Reactive: Troubleshoot PC and LAN hardware and software problems; use available resources to determine appropriate solution; be available in a first-contact role for

        end users.

Proactive: Set up PCs with standard hardware software to avoid problems, keep spare parts and equipment organized for efficiency.

 

JOB ACCOUNTABILITY

Work Structure: Works under direction of the IT Infrastructure Manager in an environment that may require confidentiality. Configures, repairs, and deploys PCs and printers as appropriate for departmental directives. Prioritizes users' requests and responds accordingly. Repairs broken equipment in a timely manner as requests come in. Administers phone system and voice mail, and ensures backups, as well as assisting with corporate data backup.

Responsibilities: Responsible for establishing a consistent PC software and hardware in keeping with corporate standards environment through the implementation of new hardware; for providing on-going support to PC users; for being available to end-users in a help-desk role; for backup assistance; for telecom support; for being the on-site liaison between users and the rest of IT.
