Technician 1

About the Job

This position is located in Tampa, Florida
SUMMARY
Support H.D. group with regard to simulator systems during the Hardware Test, Hardware/Software Integration (H.S.I.), and site installation phases of a program. Provide technical support for In-plant Testing and On-site Testing of simulator devices.

ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES include the following:  Other duties maybe assigned.
• Generate documentation for device/drawing corrections.
• Provide support during Hardware/Software Integration.
• Identify and implement changes to correct hardware deficiencies and test discrepancies.
• Support Manufacturing during tear down, pack, and ship phase.
• Support Manufacturing during initial phase of simulator installation at site.
• Support device power up and system checkout at site.
• Support On-site testing activities.

ADDITIONAL REQUIREMENTS
• Extensive travel.
• Implement basic modifications to existing simulators.
• Provide limited Administrative and Off-device Technical assistance.
• Provide training to junior members of the department.
• Ability to work with little supervision.
• Shift and weekend work.


SUPERVISORY RESPONSIBILITIES
• May be assigned limited responsibility for simulator devices.

SECURITY RESPONSIBILITIES include the following. 
Compliance with all company Information Systems security policies and procedures.  Personally responsible for proper classification and marking of all information and materials, in any form, produced by the incumbent. Shall be assigned specific responsibility for Information Security by immediate supervisor or department management. Shall not divulge any information, or afford access, to other employees not having a need-to-know. Shall not divulge information outside company without management approval. Incumbent shall execute company non-disclosure agreement prior to access to any controlled information.

QUALIFICATIONS To perform this job successfully, an individual must be able to perform each essential duty satisfactorily.   The requirements listed below are representative of the knowledge, skill, and/or ability required.   Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions.
Incumbent must be eligible for DoD Personal Security Clearance.

Education and/or Experience
• Associate’s degree in a technically related field or equivalent
• 1-2 years experience in a related field with a minimum of 1 year in a simulator manufacturing environment.
• Ability to use company standard personal computer applications.
• Familiar with various simulator operating systems.
• Proven electronic component level troubleshooting experience.

Language Skills
Ability to read and interpret technical documents. Good written, verbal, organizational, and interpersonal skills. Ability to make effective verbal and written presentations on technical topics to supervisor and peers.

Mathematical Skills
Ability to calculate figures and amounts such as proportions, percentages, area, circumference, and volume.  Ability to apply concepts of basic algebra and geometry. Ability to apply concepts such as fractions, percentages, ratios, and proportions to practical situations.

Reasoning Ability
Ability to define problems, collect data, establish facts, and draw valid conclusions. Ability to interpret an extensive variety of technical instructions in mathematical or diagram form.

Certificates, Licenses, Registrations
• Ability to obtain a valid U.S. passport
• Ability to obtain Industrial secret security clearance
