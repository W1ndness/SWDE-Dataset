About the Job

Advancement Systems Analyst

Department: University Advancement

Position Reports To:
Director of Advancement Services

Classification:
Information Technology Consultant - Foundation

PURPOSE OF POSITION:

University Advancement has a unique function on campus in that it deals with high level dignitaries and donors to our university. The incumbent must be able to interact with staff, administrators, and high level external constituents in a fast-paced environment while remaining flexible, proactive, resourceful and efficient. A high level of professionalism and confidentiality is crucial in this position.

Under general supervision of the Director of Advancement Services, the Advancement Systems Analyst provides functional and operational support for the Blackbaud Raiser’s Edge database system, which tracks gift/financial and biographical information on alumni, friends and donors. Create and process various data reports, maintain database integrity, develop automated processes to enhance service delivery, and provide support and training to end users at all levels within the university. This position will also provide back-up support for and represent the University at meetings and events as needed.


MAJOR RESPONSIBILITIES: % of Time
• Data manipulation, report development and report production - 55%
• Systems maintenance, training and operations support - 40%
• Other Support for University Advancement - 5%


LIST OF TASKS FOR EACH MAJOR RESPONSIBILITY:

Data manipulation, report development and report production
• Oversee the daily operation of the Blackbaud Raiser’s Edge development database, which tracks gift/financial and biographical information on alumni, friends and donors
• Monitor, analyze, and verify data to ensure database integrity
• Coordinate data quality and integrity procedures with other members of the advancement staff.
• Document and resolve user-identified data issues
• Review data integrity and perform data cleansing as necessary
• Develop, maintain and produce scheduled and ad-hoc reports
• Determine reporting needs for various development programs
• Develop reports that provide relevant and timely data to identify trends and assist in making informed decisions in support of advancement initiatives.
• Coordinate data reporting with various campus departments as needed

System maintenance, training and operations support
• Provide functional support; train and assist staff with Blackbaud Raiser’s Edge database use, policies and procedures as needed
• Maintain configuration tables to fit within the University business requirements.
• Review and analyze issues, including system documentation and output to identify solutions and /or make appropriate referrals of issues.
• Educate and engage advancement staff in the optimal use of data processing, report generation to promote best practices.
• Assist with implementation of new processes and systems integrating with the Blackbaud Raiser’s Edge database
• Coordinate future data conversions/upgrades as needed
• Review business processes and compare with the Blackbaud Raiser’s Edge system and work with designated staff and managers to identify appropriate changes.
• Design, develop and implement automated processes that are consistent with office policies and procedures and enhance the delivery of services
• Analyze and interpret technical and operational processes to develop and facilitate staff training
• Create and/or update system documentation such as business process guides, end-user training guides, and report requests
• Work with Instructional and Information Technology Services (IITS) staff and vendor on hardware, software and operating issues as needed

Other Support for University Advancement
• Serves as a member to staff Special Events on the day of a given event as needed
• Runs Raiser’s Edge reports to obtain data to assist with projections for event planning

N/A

REQUIREMENTS OF POSITION:

• List education and experience required

• Bachelor’s degree in computer science, information systems, educational technology or related field; or an equivalent combination of training/education and technology-related work experience.
• Demonstrated experience with relational databases
• Demonstrated experience supporting and utilizing integrated systems, preferably within a university environment.
• Experience with Blackbaud Raiser’s Edge database system preferred.
• California Driver’s License, Class C, preferred

• List knowledge, skills, and abilities required for this position.

• Ability to quickly learn the application as it relates to the advancement industry in general and more specifically, fundraising, development, alumni and donor relations, and communications.
• Proficiency in data organization and manipulation, with close attention to detail.
• Skill in analyzing functional requirements of new projects to develop functional and technical specifications for programming and implementation.
• Ability to provide exemplary customer service and demonstrate a high level of professionalism, both in person and over the telephone.
• Ability to interact with staff, administrators, and high level external constituents in a fast-paced environment while remaining flexible, proactive, resourceful and efficient
• Ability to analyze and interpret technical and operational processes to develop and facilitate staff training
• Ability to create and/or update system documentation such as business process guides, end-user training guides, and report requests
• Excellent consultative, interpersonal, oral and written communication skills required to assess user needs, provide training, and for interaction with a variety of users including faculty, staff and students.
• Ability to establish and maintain cooperative working relationships and work effectively as a member of a work team
• High level of knowledge of computer applications and operating systems to serve as a resource for users.
• Ability to demonstrate an awareness and appreciation of the cultural diversity of the University community, and establish and maintain cooperative and effective relations with University employees, students and the public
• Familiarity with complex federal and state regulations and their relation to office processes and procedures in order to develop solutions, resolve problems and improve services
• Ability to take initiative based on broad goals and general communication to meet internal and external timelines
• Ability to concentrate with numerous interruptions and to prioritize work to assure the completion of tasks within acceptable deadlines.
