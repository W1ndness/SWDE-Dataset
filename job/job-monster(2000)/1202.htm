Project Manager

 

Our Olympia, WA customer needs a senior/expert level Project Manager to assist them with a project that will replace an antiquated legacy desktop application written in Borland Delphi.

 

The selected Project Manager shall develop, maintain and execute the project plans necessary to see this project through to completion.  It is anticipated the Project Manager will be available to allocate full-time hours to this project.

 

Mandatory Experience:

• Five (5) years experience working within an Information Technology (IT)

environment.

• Three (3) years of Information Technology Project Management experience.

• Successful experience with previous project of similar size and complexity.

• Knowledge of the principles, practices, and disciplines of systems analysis and system application development to include planning, analysis, design, development, testing, training, and implementation.

• Well developed, high quality written and verbal communication skills.

• Experience developing project schedules including defining work breakdown structures, estimating resources, task scheduling and maintaining schedules throughout an entire project lifecycle.

• Experience defining, developing, implementing, and monitoring critical project processes such as change management, communications management, risk management issue management, stakeholder management and resource management.

• Proven ability to define, communicate and maintain an effective project organization and governance structure throughout the entire project lifecycle.

• Proficient in the use of MS Project, Microsoft Office Suite, Outlook, Excel, PowerPoint and Visio.

 

Highly Desired Experience:

• Fluent in project Management principles and best practices.

• Project Management Professional (PMP) or similar certification.

• Problem solving and conflict resolution.

• Decision making.

• Fluent in system/application development principles and best practices.

• Fluent in IT contract management and procurement policies and procedures.

• Familiarity with State government IT policies and procedures.

• Ability to plan, direct, control and implement successful Information Technology projects on schedule, within budget and scope.

• Skills to analyze data and situations, make decisions, proactively solve problems, organize and consult to resolve challenges.

• Coordinate, schedule, negotiate for and prioritize the use of shared project resources within multiple Information Technology projects executing concurrently.

• Experience building cohesive, collaborative teams within a complex and political environment.
