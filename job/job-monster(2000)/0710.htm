About the Job

ERP Analysts is seeking a Application Developer to assist with an implemetation of the following:


SCOPE OF WORK: The contractor shall provide technical application and database developer services in the Injury Biomechanics Branch, particularly with the Biodynamics Data Resource (BDR), of the USAARL. Work will be complex, requiring critical and independent judgment.

PERSONNEL: The Contractor shall provide a fully trained and qualified individual to perform these services. The individual must be able to work independently with minimal strategic and application direction and with no technical direction to solve analytic, theoretical, and technical problems. The individual also be able to work in a multi-disciplinary team and with SMEs to determine system and application requirements, formulate plans to meet or exceed the requirements, and implement the solutions.

Minimum qualifications of the individual performing these services are as follows:

a.                Computer science studies at the collegiate level.

b.                At least 10 years of experience in database development.

c.                At least 10 years of experience developing data-driven software applications, including multiple years with web-based applications and interfaces. Development includes design, coding, and testing based on self-initiated evaluation trials and on user and potential user input.

d.                At least 5 years of programming experience in application development languages.

e.                At least 2 years of demonstrated experience with applications containing or designed to contain PHI, PII, and/or FOUO data.

f.                Experience with batch processing, system architecture, system maintenance, system administration, .NET, Visual Basic (and its variations), Visual Studio, Microsoft products for application/interface/database, HTML, and/or XML.

g.                Experience with or the demonstrated ability to learn Linux and cluster administration for scientific research applications.

h.                Experience with or the demonstrated ability to learn server and client administration for scientific research.

i.                Demonstrated experience working with subject matter experts to develop and implement application and architecture requirements.

j.                Demonstrated experience working in team environments.

k.                Experience with or the demonstrated ability to work in start-up environments or newly created projects/programs.

l.                At least two years of application and complex database experience in the military setting preferred.

HOURS OF WORK: The Contractor is required to provide these services up to

40 hours per week and not to exceed a total of 1920 hours during each year of performance. Initial period of performance is for one year with options for additional efforts during a second and a third year. Allowance will be made for time off to compensate for overtime hours as necessary.


TASKS: The tasks to be performed are as follows:


architecture, and implementation strategies to meet the needs of the Biodynamics Data Resource (BDR), a multimedia biodynamics data repository, particularly by working within a larger injury biomechanics research team and working with military research laboratory IT support and guidelines.

b.                                  Provide technical services for development of system, architecture, and database requirements for an application and data store for the storage, preservation, and use of Injury Biomechanics research data, particularly in the BDR. These services should include, but are not limited to, incorporation of industry best practices and military standards and the development of written requirements, written justifications, and written requirement evaluation.

c.                                  Provide technical services for development of system technical specifications based on developed requirements from end users, available data, functional metadata, and user interface and access needs. These specifications shall include the design of hierarchical, relational, and/or multidimensional database(s) as appropriate and the design of end user and system management functionality.

d.                                  Provide technical services for development of application and data store for the storage, preservation, and use of Injury Biomechanics research data, particularly in the BDR. These services should include, but are not limited to, implementation of requirements and specifications, coding, testing, assessing application usability, assessing application/database performance, and preparing written documentation to industry and military standards, such as user manuals/instructions, system documentation, and other industry best practice documentation.

e.                                  Provide technical services to incorporate, meet, and/or exceed industry best practices and military standards for accreditation, assurance, and protection of PHI/PII.

f.                                  Provide technical services for ongoing development, defect repair, improvement, and maintenance of the systems/applications described by the requirements and technical specifications developed above.

g.                                  Provide services related to the specification, installation, and maintenance of current and future server systems and client systems to support development and internal deployment of the designed research systems. Responsibility also includes research server systems for external deployment.

h.                                  Provide cluster and client administration services for a Linux cluster and Linux workstations including, but not limited to, system specifications, software installation, and network configuration based upon research user needs.

i.                                  The contractor may be required, on written authority of the Laboratory Commander, to fly on military aircraft or ride in military vehicles in the official conduct of duties of the position.


Research Laboratory, Building 6901, Fort Rucker, Alabama, and offsite locations in order to efficiently accomplish research goals (particularly to test applications and web-based interfaces), and TDY locations as required for research purposes. The Government will provide access to the building and use of office space, desk, chairs, and office equipment.

OTHER DIRECT COSTS: The Contractor will provide supplies and travel costs or other direct costs beyond that provided by the Government to allow incumbent to accomplish research purposes as defined in the SOW. At a minimum, this will include travel expenses and conference/course registration/fees to allow incumbent to accomplish research purposes at CONUS or OCONUS places of performance.

QUALITY ASSURANCE SURVEILLANCE PLAN (QASP)

1.                                  All tasks as per Scope of Work and Statement of Work will be performed under the direction of COR.

2.                                  Technical application/database development and system administration services will be monitored for quality (evaluated by number of technical errors or remaining resolvable bugs) and timeliness of completion (by length of time to develop a technical resolution strategy and by length of time to implement the solution) by the COR through random spot-checking of research applications, databases, systems operation, and documentation by the COR, Injury Biomechanics Branch Principal Investigators (IBB PIs), and Injury Biomechanics Branch Chief.

3.                                  Status of recurring tasks (e.g., development of requirements, implementation of technical solutions, determination of technical specifications, system maintenance) will be reported to the IBB PIs and/or COR on a weekly basis or otherwise as necessary to the IBB PIs and/or COR for guidance.

4.                                  Draft requirements and specifications documentation, implementation strategies, user manuals, system documentation, and contributions to team research/progress reports, presentations, and documentation will be monitored for quality and timeliness.

5.                                  Services related to incorporation of end user research requirements in applications, databases, and systems to enable ongoing research studies and future scientific studies will be monitored by the COR through reports of the Injury Biomechanics Branch Principal Investigators supported by the incumbent.

6.                                  Administrative duties and training will be conducted as per USAARL guidelines and procedures.
