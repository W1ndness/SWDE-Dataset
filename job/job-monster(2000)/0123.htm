Computer Systems Analyst

About the Job

Job Responsibilities –

The Engineer will work as part of a Biometrics Capabilities office, on a System Engineering and Technical Assistance (SETA) task order,  to support the overall vision of NGIC biometrics tool evolution over a given 24-36 month period. The Engineer (Requirements) is an information technology professional who has detailed knowledge of the biometric mission and can provide efficient and effective solutions to manage the evolution of customer requests to and through implementation. The professional will work with internal and external customers to translate mission needs into desired features for the supporting IT System. The professional should possess a wide range of technical knowledge.

 

Environment - Fast paced, growth oriented company with collegial staff focused on building one of the best companies in industry.

Required Skills –

·         Authoring requirements and use cases based on customer interaction

·         Facilitate and lead multiple meetings with stakeholders at all levels

·         Coordinate and Control detailed software specification development with designated development contractors

·         Provide Subject Matter Expertise regarding biometrics, as well as current and future technologies

·         Support program integration activities that cut across the acquisition, program management and technical domains, including technology studies, evaluation of alternative solutions, and support in aligning program efforts with organizational objectives;

·         Interact with NGIC personnel and contractors, coordinate conferences, visits, and SVTCs;

·         Develop and deliver program briefs

·         Support development and maintenance of stakeholder requests and software features baseline

 

The Engineer should provide the following skills and experience.

·         Breadth and Depth of experience in System and/or Software Engineering tools and methodologies. Examples include: Functional Allocation, Release Management, Software Design and Scheduling.

·         Knowledge of Requirements Development and Management processes and tools; including elicitation and validation techniques. Proficiency with IBM Requisite Pro a plus. Experience in system verification/test a plus. 

·         Knowledge of Project & Program Management processes and tools, including schedule and cost analysis and management, earned value, and earned schedule. Proficiency in Microsoft Project a plus.

·         The candidate must be results-oriented, self-motivated, and an effective communicator. Experience with biometrics preferred.

·         Experience with Department of Defense Architecture Framework (DoDAF). DoDAF 2.0 preferred.

 

Clearance – DOD Top Secret/SCI


Education & years of experience -

BS in Systems Engineering, Computer Science, Information Systems, Mathematics or similar degree. MS preferred. At least 5years experience in Systems Engineering and/or Requirements Management.

 

Company Overview -
Join an established, reliable mid-tier government contractor with a reputation for excellence.  For more than 25 years, Pragmatics, Inc. has provided state-of-the-art IT services and solutions for the federal government.  We provide software engineering and systems integration, systems engineering, information assurance, program management support, and IT infrastructure management.  Pragmatics has many diverse contracts with both defense and civilian agencies.  We have been externally appraised at CMMI® Level 3 and ISO 9001:2008 and 20000:2005 certified.  

Pragmatics strives to provide the highest level of customer satisfaction, as well as rewarding careers for our employees, who are hardworking, enthusiastic, and dedicated to superior performance.  We encourage you to find out more about the company and our benefits at: www.pragmatics.com.
