About the Job

Director in the Department of University Computing Services
University Computing Services

McNeese State University invites qualified applicants and nominations for the position of Director in the Department of University Computing Services. This is a full-time, 12-month, unclassified position. The appointment begins January 3, 2011.
 
Position Description and Responsibilities: The Director is responsible for functions related to the Banner ERP System. As such, the Director will work closely with members of the entire campus community on a variety of projects.
 
The successful candidate will have responsibilities, including but not limited to:

  • Advising and assisting the Chief Information Technology Officer in developing objectives, policies, and procedures for university computing.
  • Maintaining an effective liaison with the administration, faculty, staff, and students to ensure that the total computing needs of the University are met.
  • Planning and implementing administrative computing needs.
  • Managing a staff of both classified and unclassified employees.
  • Preparing and being responsible for budgetary and financial planning for University Computing Services.
  • Tracking and reporting all costs and expenditures for the department.
  • Maintaining a technical knowledge of existing as well as newly developing hardware and software.
  • Planning and administering the physical plant needs of the department.
  • Maintaining an effective liaison with hardware and software vendors.
  • Serving on designated university committees.
  • Any other duties as assigned.

Qualifications: 
 
Required Skills

  • Ability to develop and promote collaboration among departments at McNeese State University.
  • Client-focused orientation and exceptional communication skills.
  • Both technical and managerial experience, a minimum of 10 years relevant experience with at least half in a management position. 
  • Demonstrated expertise in current and emerging technologies used in higher education.
  • Outstanding judgment, sense of humor, keen understanding of the motivations and needs of others, and an ability to balance self-reliance with team dynamics.
  • Bachelor’s degree.

Preferred
  • Academic credentials or IT service experience in a college environment.
  • Participation in regional and/or national higher education IT organizations.
  • Advanced degree.

Salary: $ 73,500
 
Deadline: Review of applications will begin on November 29 and will continue until the position is filled.
