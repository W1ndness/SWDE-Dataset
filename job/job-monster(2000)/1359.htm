NERC Coordinator, Test, Substation & Maintenance

About the Job

The United Illuminating Company, headquartered in New Haven, Connecticut is an investor-owned regional electric utility that provides service to more than 320,000 customers in the greater New Haven and Bridgeport area. We are seeking an exceptional individual to join our team in our Shelton facility.


The NERC Coordinator will manage and report on all aspects of NERC standards, including assembling and maintaining evidence that demonstrates compliance. Develop, revise and communicate all NERC related policies, procedures, standards and guidelines. Liaison with UI's NERC Compliance Group and with other utilities on NERC Standards. Collaborate with all areas of the Company on NERC related issues and participate in NERC Compliance team meetings. Participate in external organization meetings (e.g. NPCC, ISO-NE). Prepare documents for training and awareness on NERC standards to employees. Collaborate with Test, Substation & Maintenance to prepare documentation, evidence, and policies related to applicable NERC PRC Reliability Standards. Collaborate with Test, Substation & Maintenance to prepare documentation, evidence and policies related to NERC Critical Infrastructure Protection (CIP) Reliability Standards. Support all related areas of the company in an annual review of maintenance plans, procedures, testing and schedules to ensure compliance with NERC Standards and Procedures. Verify that maintenance and testing documentation provides sufficient evidence that the required testing was completed according to NERC standards. Obtain required documentation for annual NERC reporting from other related areas of the company to ensure that it is stored and readily available to support the requirements of NERC's reliability standard auditing.


This position requires a Bachelor's degree in Engineering, Science or Computer Science and five years experience performing compliance/engineering duties. Knowledge of the North American Electric Reliability Council (NERC) Reliability Standards desirable. Ability to interpret NERC or other applicable compliance requirements. Demonstrated strong technical writing skills. Ability to perform complex data analysis. Proficient in providing training, presentations, and instructions. Possess experience, knowledge and skills in Test, Substation & Maintenance. Able to explain difficult compliance issues and work to establish a consensus. Able to work in a cross-functional team and provide compliance guidance Test, Substation & Maintenance group. Strong SCADA background preferred.


We are proud to be an EEO/AA employer M/F/D/V. We maintain a drug-free workplace and perform pre-employment substance abuse testing.
