COMPUTATIONAL PHYSICIST 008954

About the Job

Our Next Breakthrough is You.

 

Research and development in the national interest thrive at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory.  By encouraging a competition of ideas, instilling respect for individual contributions, and embracing a diverse and motivated work force, we make breakthroughs that have global impact.  Make history by being a vital part of our future.

 

NATURE AND SCOPE OF JOB
The Plasma Theory Group within AX Division of the Weapons Directorate seeks a computational physicist.  The selected candidate will work as a team member providing analysis on massively parallel numerical modeling, post-processing, and analysis of laser-plasma interactions.  The ideal candidate will work well in an innovative environment at the frontier of computing.  Will report to the Plasma Theory Group Leader. 

IN ADDITION AT THE MTS.3 LEVEL
The selected candidate will take the team lead on massively parallel post-processing of numerical modeling, fully optimizing and parallelizing the suite of analysis tools.

ESSENTIAL DUTIES
- Maintain and further develop massively parallel plasma theory group codes.
- Develop new code diagnostics and optimize the computational efficiency of post-processing of massively parallel simulations.
- Participate in exascale research to advance computational physics models future architectures.
- Participate directly in physics applications, modeling laser-plasma interactions for the National Ignition Facility.
- Provide computational physics support for other group members.
- Present and disseminate research results at scientific conferences and in peer-reviewed publications.
- Responsible for day-to-day code operations.
- Perform all assignments in accordance with ES&H, security, and business practice requirements and policies.

IN ADDITION AT THE MTS.3 LEVEL.
- Specify strategies to advance computational modeling of laser-plasma interactions.

ESSENTIAL SKILLS, KNOWLEDGE, AND ABILITIES
- Ph.D. in computational physics/plasma physics or closely related field or equivalent level of demonstrated knowledge.
- Experience developing and using massively parallel codes, preferably with computational experience at the petascale.
- Experience working independently as well as in a team research environment.
- Demonstrated verbal, written, and presentation skills necessary to author numerous technical and scientific reports, publications, invited papers, and to deliver scientific presentations.
- Demonstrated problem solving, decision-making and analytical skills necessary to independently advise and recommend solutions.

IN ADDITION AT THE MTS.3 LEVEL
- Significant experience leading independent work through the development and use of massively parallel codes, preferably with computational experience at the petascale.
- Experience leading in a team research environment.
- Demonstrated advanced verbal and written communication and presentation skills.
