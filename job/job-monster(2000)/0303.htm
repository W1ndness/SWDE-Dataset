MASTER SCHEDULER

About the Job

The Consortium for Ocean Leadership is pleased to announce the search for a Master Scheduler. This is a regular, full-time, exempt position. The position will report to the Senior Manager, Project Process Control.

The Master Scheduler is primarily responsible for maintaining the program schedule and ensuring the Integrated Master Schedule (IMS) is fully integrated with the Implementing Organization’s master schedule and the other program management tools, i.e., Budgets, EVMS, WBS, SOWs, Risks, Critical Path Method modeling and Schedule Risk Management.  This position develops and maintains program overview schedules; coordinates with internal and external organizational entities and insure that all detailed work plans are consistent with the IMS; develops and maintains program schedule, earned value and health metrics; models, performs and analyzes schedule risk assessments and critical path analyzes; provides Program Management and Customer with schedule related status and reports.

 

Qualified applicants must possess a degree in a relevant field (e.g., business, operations/project management, engineering, etc); advanced degree preferred; a minimum of ten (10) years of experience with at least five (5) years of experience in MS Project, EVMS, Critical Path development, and schedule performance analysis.  This position also requires effective project management skills and significant experience in Microsoft Project and Microsoft Project Server; excellent communications skills (verbal and written); particularly in a technical environment, among system engineers, technical consultants, programmatic and engineering staff from all parties involved in defining and scoping program tasks to meet desired delivery needs; acquisition planning, change management execution, and schedule risk assessment models and methods including schedule projections and analysis.  Qualified candidates must possess expertise in Microsoft Project, earned value software and Risk+ software; experience in budget and cost analysis as well as schedule/cost integration; keen attention to detail; significant experienced with Project Server, schedule assessment tools, EVMS, and reporting with tools such as wInsight, Cobra, etc.  The role requires proficiency with Microsoft Office tools, including MS Word, MS PowerPoint, and MS Excel and MS Access; ability to work as a team member in a diverse group; ability to deal effectively with both Government and contracted workforce members; and ability to enable teams through effective use of diplomacy and tact.

 

To apply, please forward cover letter, resume, salary requirement, and any other relevant application materials to jobs@oceanleadership.org with the subject line, “MSTR SCHDLR”. Materials may also be mailed to Human Resources, The Consortium for Ocean Leadership, 1201 New York Avenue, NW, 4th Floor, Washington, DC 20005.

 

The Consortium for Ocean Leadership represents 95 of the leading public and private ocean research education institutions, aquaria and industry with the mission to advance ocean research, education and policy. Located in the heart of our nation’s capital, Ocean Leadership offers a collegial work environment and excellent benefits. To learn more about us, please visit www.oceanleadership.org .
