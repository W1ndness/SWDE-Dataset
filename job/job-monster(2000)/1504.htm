Web Developer, Database and Web Initiatives

About the Job

The Office of Alumni and Development is the central fund-raising and alumni relations unit of Columbia University. We are currently engaged in a $4 billion capital campaign, along with a Trustee-mandated effort to increase alumni engagement. The Web Initiatives group works closely with a team of database programmers and reporting specialists to service the technology needs of frontline fund-raisers, alumni relations officers, and the University’s nearly 300,000 alumni. We strive to be end user focused and deliver attractive and intuitive products in a timely fashion.


Reporting to the manager, Web Initiatives, the Web developer provides programming support for a variety of applications utilizing the Microsoft .NET Platform. The incumbent requires a solid foundation in C# and ASP.NET 3.5 technologies.


Our applications vary greatly in complexity and scope, and priorities shift frequently. Current major initiatives include Athena Web. This is a Web-based interface to Advance, the University’s alumni and donor database of record. Athena Web’s fundamental purpose is to streamline access to Advance’s highly complex data structures, allowing fund-raisers to focus on their development duties. It also enhances data integrity by providing guided data entry and validation (both of which are lacking in the base Advance product). Planned enhancements include end-user personalization, accessibility from mobile devices, and a more robust system of workflows.


Bachelor’s degree and/or its equivalent required. Minimum of 3–5 years of related experience. Programming ASP.NET 3.5 Web sites with C# in Visual Studio 2008. Expertise in HTML, CSS, and Javascript, including AJAX technologies. Familiarity with relational database design and querying. Solid written and verbal communication skills. Experience with Oracle databases and PL/SQL programming highly desirable. Experience with Telerik ASP.NET controls. Graphic design, production of Web graphic elements with Macromedia Fireworks, Adobe Illustrator, or similar.
