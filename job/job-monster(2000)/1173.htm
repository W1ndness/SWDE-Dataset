Senior Security R&D Engineer

About the Job

The role of a Sr. Engineering Consultant is to optimize the security of information technology infrastructure for Nuspire’s customers to ensure the confidentiality, availability and integrity of their information. Methods of accomplishing this vary in day-to-day responsibilities and include, but are not limited to, staying abreast of new threats and vulnerabilities, monitoring market conditions and the managed security market space, learning new products and technologies to aid in the above goal, consulting with clients and providing advice, and working with co-workers to educate them on the threats and vulnerabilities our prospects and existing clients are potentially exposed to.

  • Continuously evaluate, identify and implement opportunities to enhance the information security profile of our clients and Nuspire. Work with security tools, engineers, and processes to investigate and resolve intrusion detection/prevention alerts, network traffic anomalies, and system vulnerabilities.

  • Lead Research and Development efforts and communicate build requirements to Development staff for updates and new network monitoring and IDS/IPS tools.

  • Assist in development and creation of marketing material, security blogs, white papers, and other research documentation for publishing to news organizations, clients, and/or the company website.

  • Proactively maintain a thorough knowledge of technologies, processes, controls, and monitoring of information technology security systems.

  • Maintain and enhance expertise in security systems, hacking techniques, penetration methodologies, security risk assessments, and forensics investigations.

  • Identify system configuration discrepancies and inadequacies that may compromise infrastructure security levels.

  • Conduct scans on servers, develop profiles for alerting, and utilize preventative tactics for hackers.

  • Maintain the security related process and procedure documentation for Nuspire and templates for clients, including but not limited to security event detection, tracking, trending, alerting and incident analysis.

  • Lead information technology groups in the infrastructure security related patching and hardening process and priorities based on industry best practices.

  • Candidate will train employees and clients to run security systems.

Certifications preferred but not required include:

  • ISC2 Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP)
  • EC-Council Certified Ethical Hacking
  • EC-Council Computer Hacking Forensic Investigator
  • GIAC Certified Forensics Analyst
  • GIAC Certified Intrusion Analyst
  • GIAC Security Essentials Certification  
  • QSA – PCI

5 to 10 years experience in network security preferred
