About the Job


Research Computing Administrator
UC Berkeley - Main Campus


Haas Computing Services is responsible for the provision of computing and technology services for the business school, supporting over 3000 faculty, staff, students, and research affiliates. Services include PC support, student computer lab services, e-mail services, instructional technology support, applications development, administrative database services, research computing, learning management systems and instructional technologies, remote connectivity services, network and back-office/systems services on Windows XP/2003/2008/7, BSD, and Linux.

This position is the primary UNIX server System Administrator for the Haas school of Business, responsible for the administration of most of the schools production UNIX servers. The incumbent is responsible for the research computing infrastructure, including the research computing cluster which provides the computing platform to support the research activities of the Haas faculty and PhD students. This position analyzes the requirements for research computing, evaluates alternatives for providing the support, and designs the infrastructure to implement the solutions. Once the design is approved, the incumbent procures, installs, configures, integrates, and tests the hardware and services needed. After testing, the System Administrator puts the system into production, maintains and upgrades the hardware and software, and adjusts the design to accommodate the evolving requirements of the Haas researchers. The position must ensure that the research services integrate with the complex, heterogeneous Haas computing environment; ensuring server, domain, and network security, interoperability, and reliability. The incumbent administers over 15 servers, 12 of which operate as a high performance clusters running research applications. The incumbent also administers the backup server and coordinates the backup of 75 production servers at Haas, and 25 servers in the campus data center.
