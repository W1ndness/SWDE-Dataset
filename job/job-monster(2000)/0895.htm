Software Engineers, Test and Validation, Custom Implementations Team:


Numonyx (now part of Micron) is now actively hiring energetic software development and validation engineers to join our rapidly growing Professional Services team, who will help us integrate Technology for Memory Optimization products in customer systems.  TMO is a new category of product and service that helps our customers deliver more cost effective solutions by reducing the memory used by our customer’s embedded devices, primarily mobile phones. 


Responsibilities:

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 


 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 

Successful software implementations require that our CMO software development and validation engineers should be effective in communicating with internal and external engineering teams, have a holistic understanding of lower-level software systems, have good judgment under pressure, and be excellent debuggers. Some of the custom software development and validation work will be done on-site with our customers and partners all over the world, giving you the opportunity for travel too.  Our best and brightest experienced engineers will mentor you in this challenging and varied work that will offer you hands-on experience working directly with the biggest names in electronics, the latest technologies, and with many diverse cultures. 


Specifically, you will:


 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 


•    Test/Validate Numonyx TMO memory optimization products and solutions.

 

•    Evaluate system level performance, memory usage, and effective use of memory technologies.

 

•    Assist in the development of validation plans and validation protocols.

 

•    Execute, and complete validation plans, validation protocols, and test cases necessary for a successful validation process including evaluation of validation results and writing final validation reports.

 

•    Find and accurately report SW and/or HW bugs and performance issues and recommend fixes or workarounds.

 

•    Interact with team members in Asia and Europe, and customers as required. 

 

•    Install/build/maintain Test/Validation tools, and platforms. Includes installing hardware, operating systems, and automated tools.

 

•    Design and develop test automation artifacts (scripts, functions, scenarios, processes) for simple to complex testing situations using various tools.

 

•    Manage testing artifacts to ensure proper versioning, backups, organization and retention.

 

•    Participate in the definition and design of overall testing strategy in partnership with the project teams. This includes defining: testing objectives, scope, test methodology.

 

•    Analyze test results for baselines, trends, and anomalies to facilitate problem solving, operational and performance improvements, and reporting. This includes analysis of raw data, and determination and root cause of issues. Contribute ideas and recommends action based on that analysis.

 

•    Report project status and work to project management to resolve issues.

 

•    Prepare and report test results both formally and informally. Communicate results information to technical and non-technical audiences.

 

•    Help define and establish new test methodologies, processes, and best practices particularly related to testing and contribute both ideas and effort toward continuous improvement.

 

•    Collect, organize, and analyze statistical information from a variety of sources (both technical i.e. web logs, and non-technical i.e. product management interviews) to support high-quality test design.


**This position can be based either in San Jose, California, or in Folsom, California.

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 


Desirable Experience and Skills:

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 

 
 
 
 
 


•   2-5+ years experience in high tech industry related to memory storage products

•   Experience in SW test/validation and/or SW development

•   Knowledge of SW development processes and tools (VCS, Bug Tracking, etc.)

•   Understanding of hardware systems, and computer architecture

•   C programming experience

•   Scripting languages (Perl, Ruby, Python, Bash)

•   Version Control (Git/Mercurial/SVN)

•   Understanding of programming tools including compilers, linkers, and debuggers

•   RTOS development/testing experience (Nucleus, ThreadX, vxWorks) 

•   Trace32

•   ARM architecture

•   Ability and willingness to travel internationally 

•   Ability to visualize and develop innovative solutions to detected problems and issues

•   Any Korean language ability very helpful

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 


Education:


•           BS or higher in CS, EE, CE or equivalent.

 


About Numonyx

 

Numonyx (a Micron company) is changing the face of memory. And we’re looking for smart, creative professionals to help us do that. Because memory is all we do, we offer unparalleled focus and broad manufacturing capacity to deliver memory solutions globally. In fact, you’ll find our memory at the heart of many of the world’s communications, electronic,automotive and industrial devices. If you’re passionate, committed and enthusiastic about a company with a focus on innovation, reliability and customer-orientation, we want to hear from you. We offer competitive salaries, a generous benefits package, and a collaborative, results-focused culture.  Numonyx has locations around the world, including  design centers in San Jose and in Folsom, California.
