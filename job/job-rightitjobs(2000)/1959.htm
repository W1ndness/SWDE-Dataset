General statement of duties:

The Network Engineer role is to ensure the availability, Stability, and integrity of the organization data, voice, video and wireless network services.  This is achieved by planning, designing and implementing local area networks (LANs), wide area networks (WANs) and wireless LANs (WLANs) across the organization. In addition the Network Engineer will participate in the installation, maintenance, support, and optimization of all network hardware, software, and communication links.  Monitor network performance, troubleshoot problem areas as needed and perform network problem resolution.  Perform system tuning and make recommendation for improvement.  Assist in developing architectures and planning for tactical and strategic requirements, system definition, and feasibility determination.  Plans, Analyzes, and coordinate site equipment requirements. Participate with the installation, monitoring, maintenance, support and optimization of all network hardware, software and communication links. Operate both as an incident and problem response contact.

Job Requirements

The ideal candidate will possess extensive knowledge, ability and experience related to:

[] Design principles for local area networks, Wide area networks and wireless LANs.

[] Installation, Configuration, troubleshooting and maintenance of network hardware, including routers, switches, firewall and wireless access points and VoIP equipments.

[] Switching and routing protocols

[] WiFi Technology

[] Network testing tools

[] IP addressing and subnet schemes

[] Cisco routers and Sonicwall firewalls.

[] IP traffic flow, protocol analysis, capturing and monitoring.

[] Windows and Apple servers and the integration of Open Directory and Active Directory.

[] Microsoft Exchange server in a distributed Enterprise environment.

[] Protocols such as SMB, AFP, NetBios,  and TCP/IP.

[] Cooper and fiber cable topologies.

[] Layer 3 switches, VLANS and fiber switch configuration.

[] Microsoft office suite.

[] Installing and troubleshooting local area network including wireless systems.

[] Reviewing and evaluating IT system designs for compliance with industries standards.

[] Configuring servers, computers and a variety of network equipments.

[] Thorough understanding of Active Directory, Group Policies, etc.

[] Symantec Anti Virus Enterprise/ Corporate Editions.

[] Intrusion detection

In addition to the extensive technical competency required, the ideal candidate will posses analytical and problem solving skills and strong written and oral communication skills, great customer relation skills focus, highly self motivated and directed with keen attention to detail and maintain composure in stressful situations and adjust priorities as situation change.

 Education:

Graduation from a recognized college or university with a bachelor’s degree in computer science or information systems, electrical engineering, or related field.  Qualifying experience may substitute for the required education.

Cisco Certified Network professional (CCNP) or Cisco Certified Design Associate (CCDA).BICSI Registered Cable Distribution Designer (RCDD), CISCO Certified Design Professional (CCDP), Advanced Wireless LAN for System Engineers (AWLANSE), Cisco Advanced Wireless LAN Field Engineers (AWLANFE) and Cisco Certified Voice Professional (CCVP) are preferable.
