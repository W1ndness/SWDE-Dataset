Job Description 

Rating Systems Analyst II – Jacksonville, FL

 

Position Summary

Under direct supervision, analyzes, implements and maintains system changes to the Personal Lines Rating System including rating algorithms and rate tables. Identifies changes to the interface programs. Makes recommendations to improve systems effectiveness and efficiency. May provide project work direction to PL Rating Systems Technicians.

 

The position requires regular contact with technicians to discuss project work to be completed and to advise them of completion dates. Regular contact with PLG business units, IS and regional offices for project implementation, problems and questions relating to the rating system. Occasional contact with ISO, bureaus and rating vendors for interpretation or to clarify new rating methodologies.

 

Position Responsibilities

  • Serves as primary contact between IS and business functions for systems changes to ensure all Personal Lines Rating System issues and the proper interpretations of rules are considered in program designs.
  • Provides input into system design for the implementation of new programs as they relate to the rating and the Personal Lines Rating System.
  • Serves as vendor liaison in the transfer of information and customer issues.
  • Provides project time estimates to IS and the business units for changes to the Personal Lines Rating System.
  • Develops and maintains rating algorithms and rate tables. Tests and reviews the output for format, accuracy and completeness.
  • Develops and runs test cases for policy level testing to ensure rate accuracy on the more complex changes.
  • Notifies IS of problems detected with test or production rate levels and makes recommendations to correct the problem.
  • Updates and maintains base test file and test case criteria as needed. Performs extensive testing on all updates made to the files.
  • Makes recommendations to hold processing of policies if rating problems occur.
  • Responds to inquiries from regional offices, PLG business units, IS and outside rate vendors regarding rating procedures or interpretations of ISO or company manuals.
  • Automates rate examples and other information as requested for use by insurance departments, the media or PLG business units and communicates with bureaus and insurance departments for information relating to consumer publications.
  • Authors change requests in the Personal Lines Tracking System for rating problems or change requests.
  • Investigates problem logs, make any necessary changes to the rating system and provides notification to the Personal Lines Help Desk, Information Systems and field offices.
  • Determines criteria and requests selection runs as needed for rating errors.
  • Publishes rating information in agreed upon mediums, including paper, LAN based databases and mainframe systems for distribution to vendors and Kemper personnel.
  • Contributes to the development and achievement of department and company objectives and goals.
  • May assist in the training of new Rating Systems Analysts.
  • Keeps manager informed verbally and in writing of activities and problems within assigned area of responsibility; refers matters beyond limits of authority and expertise to manager for direction.
  • Performs other related duties as required or requested.

 


Job Requirements 

Position requirements

 

  • Education equivalent to graduation from college or the equivalent in work related experience.
  • Math degree a plus.
  • Proficiency with Microsoft Excel.
  • Working toward IIA Certificate or demonstrates equivalent insurance knowledge.
  • 3 or more years of insurance experience, demonstrating sound knowledge of the Personal Lines Rating System, Personal Lines coverages, underwriting and marketing concepts, or the equivalent in related work experience.
  • Must be able to travel as required.
  • Demonstrates the ability to develop and deliver information and respond to questions from all levels of the organization and external sources in a clear, concise, organized, and diplomatic manner.
  • Demonstrates the ability to gather and analyze data, and draw correct inferences from the information; weigh alternatives to solve problems.
  • Demonstrates the ability to review and proofread work for accuracy and to review detailed reports in order to identify and correct questionable data.
  • Demonstrates the ability to initiate independent action as needed to accomplish work.
  • Demonstrates the ability to efficiently manage own time, activities, and resources to ensure department standards are met on a consistent basis.
