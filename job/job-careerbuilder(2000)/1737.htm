Company Overview 

LMI Aerospace, Inc. is a leading provider of structural components, assemblies and kits to the aerospace, defense and technology industries. We fabricate, machine, finish and integrate formed, close tolerance aluminum and specialty alloy components and sheet metal products primarily for large commercial, corporate and military aircraft. We manufacture more than 30,000 products for integration into a variety of aircraft platforms manufactured by leading original equipment manufacturers, referred to as OEMs, and Tier 1 aerospace suppliers.

Founded in 1948 as a manufacturer of components to the large commercial aircraft market of the aerospace industry, LMI Aerospace went public in 1998. In recent years we have expanded our capabilities and diversified our operations through a number of acquisitions and business initiatives.

In 2001 we acquired the operating assets of Tempco Engineering, Inc., which expanded our aerospace product line and added technology components used in semiconductor and medical equipment. LMI acquired Versaform Corporation and Southern Stretch Forming and Fabrication, Inc., producers of large formed metal components for the regional jet, business jet and military markets of the aerospace industry.

Since 2003 LMI has expanded into assembling and kitting and opened a distribution center in Savannah, Georgia. In 2006, we opened a 23,000 square foot manufacturing center in Mexicali, Mexico, that will support our U.S. operations.

In 2007 LMI Aerospace acquired San Diego-based D3 Technologies, Inc., a premier design and engineering services firm. The company performs structure and sub-structure design work for manufacturers of commercial, business and military aircraft, for Boeing, Spirit AeroSystems, Vought Aircraft, Airbus North America, Lockheed Martin, and others including key roles on the JSF for Lockheed and Boeing's 747-8, 777, and 787 programs. D3 Technologies' engineers have worked on a range of design and analysis projects including airframe, electro/mechanical and hydraulic/pneumatic systems and have expertise in mechanical, structural, and system design; stress and finite element analysis; tool design and engineering; numerical controlled programming; logistics and program support; and avionics software and hardware development.

In addition to aerospace products, we produce components and assemblies for laser equipment used by semiconductor and medical equipment manufacturers in the technology industry. We also provide prototyping and design capabilities to these customers to support new product development.

Job Description 

DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:  The Project Business Analyst (PBA) will assist the Project Manager in developing the project plan, schedules, budget, resource plans, risk assessments, and reporting methodology. The PBA will provide data and or graphs to show schedule and cost performance. Additionally, on new projects, the PBA will coordinate with the Project Manager to organize Hand Off/Kick Off Meetings and regularly scheduled Project Status Review meetings.

 

Essential Duties and Responsibilities:

Specific job responsibilities include but are not limited to:

Ø  Responsible for developing an initial task level project plan using Microsoft Project or Excel.

Ø  Responsible for developing a schedule to complete the planned tasks.

Ø  Responsible for developing the resource plan to support the planned schedule.

Ø  Responsible for providing risk assessments related to the plan.

Ø  Responsible for providing schedule and cost performance measurement to the planned tasks utilizing Gantt charts, percentage of completion, Earned Value Measurement or other analytical methods as may be appropriate to the Project Manager.

Ø  Provide comparison of planned task completion to actual task completion, planned cost to actual cost, and identify risks and opportunities.

Ø  Provide support to the Project Manager to define and monitor the ‘Statement Of Work’ and subsequent changes for the project.

Ø  Interface with Supply Chain Management for subcontractor schedule performance.

Ø  Responsible for collecting actual performance data from various sources and loading this data into the ‘performance measurement tool’ selected for a project.  

Ø  Interface with finance to determine cost categories to be tracked, organize cost collection and issue charge numbers.  

 

Qualifications:

To perform this job successfully, an individual must be able to perform each essential duty satisfactorily. Job Requirements 

Education and/or Experience:                                            

A minimum of four years related experience or equivalent in education, training and/or experience. Experience performing task planning, scheduling, resource planning, budgeting and risk assessment in support of Project Management, as defined by Project Management Book of Knowledge (PMBOK), within an engineering or manufacturing organization required. Knowledge of engineering or manufacturing operations associated with aerospace sheet metal manufacturing or assembly a plus.

Bachelor’s degree in Engineering Management or Business Management preferred. PMI certification a  plus. 

 

Computer Skills:                                                      

Proficient in Microsoft Project, Word and Excel, including creating formulas and various types of charts. Knowledge of Jobscope a plus. Ability to learn and use Jobscope query required.

 

Competencies:                                              

Ø  Identify and resolve issues in a fast-changing environment with a focus on process improvement

Ø  Proven analytical ability

Ø  Ability to organize large amounts of various data types in a simple and logical format for presentation to others.

Ø  Deliver quality work on a timely basis

Ø  Work in a team environment

Ø  Work effectively with all co-workers, vendors and customers and consistently interact in a positive and professional manner with customers, suppliers and other employees. Carry out all department functions in a manner consistent with LMI’s values and code of ethics.

Ø  Ability to maintain confidentiality

Ø  Effectively establish and communicate policy, procedure and performance analysis in both written and presentation formats

Ø  Work independently and with minimal direction

Ø  Initiative/self motivation

Ø  Follow through/follow up skills

 

Physical Demands:

The employee must be able to sit and use a computer for extended period of time, up to 8 hours with periodic breaks.

Employee may occasionally stand and/or walk and occasionally lift and/or move up to 10 pounds. Employee must have the ability to remain calm under pressure and stress.

 

Work Environment:

The employee will normally work in an office environment. Co-workers’ work station may be relatively close in proximity.

 

 

 

Special Requirement:

The ability to work extended hours, including weekends, when necessary to meet deadlines. Some travel may be required, up to 20%.
