Company Overview 

At Money Management International (MMI), our mission is to improve lives through financial education.

Since 1958, we have been helping consumers find the tools and solutions they need to achieve financial freedom. We provide professional financial guidance, credit counseling, community-wide educational programs, debt management assistance, bankruptcy counseling and education services, and housing counseling assistance to consumers via phone, Internet and in-person sessions. MMI is a member of the National Foundation for Credit Counseling (NFCC) and The Association of Independent Consumer Credit Counseling Agencies (AICCCA).

Job Description 


Position Summary: The Database Administrator�s role is to design, install, monitor, maintain, and performance tune MMI�s databases while ensuring high levels of data availability. This individual is also responsible for defining standards and models for database architectures which includes developing, implementing, and overseeing database policies and procedures to ensure the integrity and availability of databases and their accompanying software. In addition this individual will evaluate and select all technology components, such as software, hardware, and networking capabilities, for database management systems and applications.

Primary Responsibilities:
� Ensure the stability and reliability of data access and data quality across the organization via ongoing database support, problem resolution and maintenance.
� Establish appropriate end-user database access control levels and develop routines for end users to facilitate best practices database use.
� Work with application development staff to develop database architectures, coding standards, and quality assurance policies and procedures.
� Install and configure relevant network components to ensure database access as well as database consistency and integrity. Perform database transaction and security audits.
� Monitor database system details within the database, including stored procedures and execution time, and implement efficiency improvements.
� Monitor, optimize and allocate physical data storage for database systems and perform database transaction and security audits.
� Create and document models for new database development and/or changes to existing ones through data flowcharting.
� Assist with the installation and configuration of relevant network components to ensure database access, consistency, and integrity, and advise on the allocation of physical data storage for database systems.
� Develop, implement, and maintain change control and testing processes for modifications to databases. Plan and coordinate data migrations between systems.
� Analyze user requirements for reports, forms, queries, and data extraction and create, or support creation of, required reports in response to business user needs.
� Under the direction of the Director, Data Services implement and administer control and sharing strategies and goals for company database resources and services.

Additional Responsibilities:
� Conduct research and make recommendations on database products, services, protocols, and standards in support of procurement and development efforts.
� Provide end-user training as necessary with regards to the effective and efficient use of database tools and resources and develop and deploy end-user practice tools
� Provide input and feedback solicited from data owners and department managers to aid the Director, Data Services and Vice President, IT in the development of long-term strategic goals for production databases and the development of strategies for the control and sharing of company database resources and services.
� Work with Business Continuity Manager to identify, design and implement redundant systems, policies, and procedures for disaster recovery and data archiving to ensure effective protection and integrity of data assets.
� Support and maintain information security measures as appropriate to the position.

NOTE: The duties listed above are intended only as illustrations of the various types of work that may be performed. The omission of specific statements of duties does not exclude them from the position if the work is similar, related, or a logical assignment to the position.
