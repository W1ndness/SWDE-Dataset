Job Description 



The technology program manager provides leadership and expertise to deliver IT programs and initiatives on schedule and within budget. They plan and administer both projects and the overall program, and ensure that deliverables are completed correctly and on-time. The technology program manager delivers the technical solution to support an organization's vision and strategic direction. They are leaders in the IT organization, and are held accountable for the success of enterprise-impacting technology initiatives.


Accountabilities and Activities


  • Oversees the delivery of large, complex technology programs. Typically, these large-scale efforts comprise parallel projects and cover software development, IT infrastructure systems installation, business process engineering and an organization's functional capabilities.
  • Defines the program governance structure, which provides program leadership, oversight and control. The executive sponsor, steering committee and technology program manager are represented within the program governance structure.
  • Identifies the program's individual component projects and estimates the resources required to deliver the program.
  • Identifies the connections and dependencies among the individual projects and collects this data in a master program plan.  Continuously monitors the plan throughout the program lifecycle, defining the planned outcomes for each component project, the resources required and the work schedule.
  • Manages, monitors and controls work efforts, while ensuring that the output of each component project satisfies its specified requirements. Continuously interacts with stakeholders to set expectations, review objectives and coordinate resources across the individual component projects.
  • Communicates relevant information, monitors systems integration activities, negotiates deviations in plans and adjusts resources as needed.
  • Manages the program budget to conform to policy, and develops processes for requesting, spending and reporting capital expenditures. Oversees the creation of project budgets for the individual project components, and prepares cost estimates for the program based on this information.
 


Job Requirements 


  • Bachelors Degree
  • 5 - 10 years project and program management experience, preferably in a healthcare setting
  • 2 - 3 years of experience in IT business process engineering
  • Excellent oral and written communication skills, including ability to communicate with information technology professionals as well general business professionals
  • Effective presentation skills to delivery information to senior decision makers
  • Able to function effectively in a matrix-team environment
  • Proficient in MS Project, Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Visio and SharePoint

Judgment Skills


  • Able to function as a facilitator for senior business and technology leaders
  • Strong problem-solving and business requirements analysis skills to organize and motivate team members to action
  • Able to effectively adapt to new work assignments, team members and environments
  • Able to initiate work independently as well as collaborate in team environment
  • Able to communicate difficult/sensitive information tactfully

Freedom of Action


  • Expected to identify and document process improvement initiatives related to projects and organizational development at the department and/or enterprise level.
  • Expected to facilitate the creation of plans to deliver business capabilities and use their knowledge to influence decisions in the best interest of the entire enterprise.
  • Expected to use positive leadership techniques to develop highly productive project teams.

Dimensions


  • Provides leadership to a matrixed group team of up to 12 project managers and technical leads (no direct reports).
  • Manages project budget through estimating and tracking labor hours and physical assets to support project delivery

Environment


Light office work required with 10% travel to HD sites in New Enlgand.
