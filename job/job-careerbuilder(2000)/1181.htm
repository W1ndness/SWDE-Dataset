Job Description 

SCOPE:

Develops strategies for the practice to increase revenues and achieve business objectives focused on and including expansion of specialty referral programs. Responsible for developing referral development training programs for network liaisons. Works directly with marketing to create referral development specific tools to aid practices in growth.  Supports and adheres to the US Oncology Compliance Program, to include the Code of Ethics and Business Standards. ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:

-Develops and directs training curriculum for oncology liaison programs designed to educate sales and marketing on emerging products/services and pro-active competitive strategies. Standardizes content, design, delivery and packaging of training. Designs metrics that will be used to evaluate the effectiveness of program deliverables. - Identify, develop, and implement effective specialty referral sales plans and strategies. Develops specific revenue plans, as needed, to ensure growth in targeted sales by the organization.-Forecast and track annual, quarterly, and monthly sales revenue streams accurately.  Analyzes sales results to identify market and environment concerns and advises on solutions. Analyzes network practice growth needs and works in conjunction with sales management team and sales analyst to identify liaison coverage needs for network practices. -Coordinates and works in tandem with the marketing department projects to plan and create strategies, tactics, activities, and materials for the purpose of conveying the most effective and meaningful marketing message to target physician referral markets. Ensures that marketing materials position the organization and its services for high visibility with appropriate physician audiences and media.-Enforces adherence to USON policies and ensures equity.  Coaches and develops staff where appropriate.  Provides on-going job related education and training of staff.  Keeps senior leadership and field abreast of key program initiatives and represents the field as the point of contact in corporate for those key initiatives.-Other duties as requested or assigned.

MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS:

-Bachelors degree in Marketing or other related discipline.  At least twelve (12) years of healthcare market sales, growth and development, and/or territory management experience.  At least five (5) years supervisory experience coaching, developing, and managing staff.  Excellent presentation skills, and ability to influence peers, staff and physicians.  Proficiency in Microsoft Office (Outlook, Excel, Word, and PowerPoint).
