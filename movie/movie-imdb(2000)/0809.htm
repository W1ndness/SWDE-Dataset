Storyline

In an exotic divided land, Delgo, an adventurous dreamer but naive teenager, must rally his group of troubled youth and some unlikely friends to protect their world from conflict between the terrestrial Lockni people, who harness the mysteries of the land. And the winged Nohrin people, who rule the skies. They live - segregated - in an uneasy peace until Delgo was framed for a crime he did not commit. After enlisting the help of a unlikely friend to escape a Nohri prison, Delgo discovers Imhoff is in danger so he must unite the two races against a common foe. User Reviews

 
Lots of replay value for children
1 September 2009 | by Thomas Kase (United States) – See all my reviews

My two daughters - one soon to be 3, and the other 7.5 - both have requested this as a repeat view. At least 4 times so far.

That's a spot where Cinderella, 12 Dancing Princess, Aladdin, and Nemo take the top awards - but it says something when the girls want to see it again and again.

Personally, I thought it was OK fare - typical plot of course, but well executed. Seriously, how many different ways can you twist a "reluctant and unlikely hero saves girl who turns out to be a princess and wins her heart" story? Novelty is not reasonable to expect in this genre.

Enjoy the visual feast - I thought it was worth watching - we made a family evening with popcorn session out of it and all had fun.
