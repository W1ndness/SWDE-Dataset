Storyline

A young boy fighting cancer writes letters to God, touching lives in his neighborhood and community and inspiring hope among everyone he comes in contact. An unsuspecting substitute postman, with a troubled life of his own, becomes entangled in the boy's journey and his family by reading the letters. They inspire him to seek a better life for himself and his own son he's lost through his alcohol addiction. Written by Anonymous

Fun Facts

Trivia

Olivia says to Benjamin at one point, "Shall we accept good from God and not trouble?" This is a reference to Job chapter 2. God gives all of Job's status and health in the devil's possession. His wife tells him, "Curse God and die!" Job replies, "You are talking like a foolish woman. Shall we accept only good from God and never trouble?" See more »

User Reviews

 
There is nothing wrong with having a little faith.
30 September 2010 | by robk-18 (United States) – See all my reviews

I felt compelled to do this review after reading one of the last ones. I noticed the review contained more hate towards those who do not believe in god than the actual movie. I thought it was unfair that the user attacked those who do not believe for giving the movie lower ratings on that reason alone. First of all, how does the user know those who graded the Movie didn't see it? It seems to me that the review was just an attack from a very religious person that was not happy with the current rating on the film and wanted to blame it on those who are not religious. Well I do not believe in god but I decided to watch this movie and I will give it a review and rating on its merits, not my beliefs. Also want to point out that I don't believe in ghosts either but I loved the movie Ghost.

This movie was not only well written but an inspiration to those who believe or are losing their faith. I loved the Sam character and how she was such a great friend and protector. It's about a families struggle not only with cancer but with faith. Not only did it affect the family but all those close to them. I would recommend this film to anyone who enjoys a good family film. It brought tears to my eyes and if you have half a heart it will do the same to you.

While I may not believe in god, I do have faith in human beings, and hope someday we can all work together regardless of our beliefs. Thanks for reading my review, I hope it helps.
