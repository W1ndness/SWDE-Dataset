User Reviews

 
A great comeback attempt from Singleton
24 February 2007 | by Green-Sabre – See all my reviews

Jody Summers is an immature young man still living with his mother in South Central, LA. Without taking any responsibilities or actions in his life he still continues to cheat on his girlfriend, fathered a child with another girl and remains unemployed. Despite this unlike his peers he stays away from the criminal life they've adapted and does the best in his somewhat ignorance to be good hearted and legit to the people he knows and atmosphere around him. However with his mother's new boyfriend Melvin who is a ex con moves in, this causes hostility with Jody and to makes things worse his girlfriend's ex, Rodney who is a gang-banger also moves in to his girlfriend's flat. Jody is an weak complex man afraid of growing up and at times we feel he is the bad guy but he's just a miscarried youth who is the victim.

John Singleton's films after Boyz N the Hood always felt weak and couldn't match the touch he had with that, "Poetic Justice" and "Higher Learning" were disappointing. Although Baby Boy isn't going to be a classic this is probably a great attempt from Singleton but will be probably be the highest he'll ever get (I'll haven't seen "Four Brothers" just yet). Baby Boy might have been criticized for it's bad portrayal of young African Americans and ethnic stereotypes but Singleton himself comments on this in the DVD commentary. It's very tough to watch from a cultural point of view. Like "Poetic Justice" the film often tends to resort to too much swearing but Baby Boy thankfully is way better than it because the strong moments in this film make Poetic Justice look like a soap opera. The film doesn't make much comment on the white supremacy as it said in the opening moments, one might assume it's too racist and sexist.

Acting is generally good. Gibson is one of the few rappers that can act and he is good as Jody, Henson is alright, Johnson is good with the material she's given, Rhames is great and Snoop's not bad as the evil Rodney. Even if it's flawed, Baby Boy was a interesting watch and look of struggle and hardship and I applaud Singleton for doing his best. 7/10
