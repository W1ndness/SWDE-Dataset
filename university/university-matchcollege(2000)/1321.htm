California State University, Fullerton is popularly referred to as Cal State Fullerton, CSU Fullerton, or CSUF. The California State University maintains several campuses and the Fullerton branch is the largest in terms of enrollment. It is located in Orange County, California.

 

Through the years, the university has undergone many improvements and renovations. Many additions occurred in 1993 when the Steven G. Mihaylo Hall, Titan Student Union, Dan Black Hall, Grand Central Art Theater, University Hall, and Student Recreation Center were launched. The school also maintains a full-service hotel - the Fullerton Marriot - in its southeastern area of the ground.

 

The Cal State Fullerton was authorized as a degree granting educational organization by the State Legislature in 1951. During the next few years, plans were underway to develop a site in northeast Fullerton. In 1959, Dr. William Langsdorf was designated as the founding president of the institution. Originally, the school was called the Orange County State College.

 

The university started with 452 students in 1959 and has expanded rapidly since then. Take note though that the school was already established prior to that year, in 1957. The last school name change occurred in 1972 when the school formally became known as the California State University, Fullerton. In August 2007, it celebrated its 50 years of existence.

 

Similar to other universities, the California State University, Fullerton also have a mascot. In fact, students are proud of their elephant mascot that they fondly call "Tuffy the Titan". This character dates back to the 1960's in the event "The First Intercollegiate Elephant Race in Human History".
