Campus SummaryTOP
History
In 1915, a Denver school teacher named Emily Griffith told Frances "Pinky" Wayne, a Denver Post features writer, that she had a dream of opening a school to serve people of all ages and interests. Wayne wrote about her dream in the Post, and the notion of a new �Opportunity School� caught fire. The Denver Board of Education gave Emily the old, condemned Longfellow School located at 13th and Welton Streets.

On September 9, 1916, Emily's dream became reality and she opened the doors of Emily Griffith Opportunity School. She hoped for a few students; more than 1,400 registered the first week! Emily's philosophy, "For all who wish to learn", still encompasses our school�s commitment to learning for people of all ages, backgrounds and interests.

During World War I, the school trained soldiers in radio communications and civilians for special mathematics, drafting, tractor and ambulance driving, and gas engine work. New programs were added after the war that gave marketable skills to Denver citizens during the depression. Under the government's War Production Board, the school operated around the clock during World War II, training more than 24,000 people for defense work with programs in war production training, food conservation, and victory gardening. Since then, the school has evolved to keep pace with today�s technologies and job demands.

Emily Griffith Opportunity School is still located at 1250 Welton St. on the spot where it was founded. The school occupies an entire block in the heart of downtown Denver. The school has assisted over 1.5 million students in achieving their goals and has positively impacted generations of families in our community. Today�s teachers and staff carry on her mission of enabling people "to succeed at meaningful work and a purposeful place in the community".

Mission
The mission of Emily Griffith Opportunity School is too provide knowledge, skills, and values that enable students to compete successfully in the workforce and to be self-sufficient citizens and lifelong learners.

Vision
It is our vision to serve as the premier college of excellence in the delivery of career and technical education for the workforce.

Success
* 1.5 million students served since 1916 � over 90 years of experience.
* 11,000-12,000 students are served annually.
* 90% employment rate for graduates.
* 69% average completion rate for students.
* 99% average employment rate for apprenticeships.
* We provide education in six of the fastest growing occupations according to the US Dept. of Labor Occupational Employment Projections.
* We help 350 high school drop outs graduate each year, infusing the Denver economy with an estimated $2,520,000 in wages.

Location
Emily Griffith Opportunity School is located in the heart of Denver just 40 minutes East of the epic Rocky Mountains. Being within walking distance to the Convention Center, light rail, 16th street Mall, Capital buildings, Elitch Gardens Amusement park, Invesco Field, Coors Field, and a variety of art and history museums you can be sure Opportunity School is always in the middle of something big.


Unique/Special ProgramsTOP
We are Colorado's most unique technical college. We offer an extensive, yet affordable, curriculum and our staff upholds a personal commitment to each student's success.
* Open year round: Day and some evening and Saturday courses available.
* Affordable tuition: Cost per credit hour is the lowest in the region.
* Many credit hours are transferable to other Colorado community colleges.
* 19 Apprenticeships available.
* Most Certificate Programs can be completed in one year or less.
* Four colleges encompass over 45 study programs and nearly 500 individual courses.
* Open admissions for some programs: Start whenever you are ready.
