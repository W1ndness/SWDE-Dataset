Campus SummaryTOP
For more than a century, LDS Business College has specialized in career training. Classes are small--an average of 20 students--and our expert faculty and focused curriculum provide you with real-world experience. In fact, our mission statement focuses on the individual.

You'll use up-to-date computer technology and study courses designed to move you from classroom to career in two years or less. Our Career Services Center has maintained a career placement rate that exceeds 95 percent for more than 10 years.

You'll gain professional experience in your field while you study. For example, LDSBC's Interior Design students have swept the International Interior Design of America (IIDA) portfolio competition three years running. Most programs require an internship experience your last semester.

The Student Body Council and other campus organizations sponsor dances, talent shows, movie nights, picnics, barbeques, hikes and a host of other events. In addition, the College is located within minutes of Salt Lake City theater and ballet, professional basketball, Church and other museums and some of the best skiing in the world.

All of this happens in an LDS Setting. General Authorities and corporate leaders regularly address students at devotionals and forums, and you'll study with others who share your values.

We invite you to visit campus at 95 North 300 West in Salt Lake City to experience the College's friendly atmosphere for yourself.


Unique/Special ProgramsTOP
Students in the Interior Design major graduate with a strong portfolio and with experience in their major. LDSBC students swept the four-year division of the International Interior Design of America (IIDA) competition in 2003 and 2002 and has placed first four of the past six years.

Students in Business majors may choose two tracks--one for entrepreneurship or one for transfer to a four-year university.

In the Information and Office Technology majors, students develop technical expertise and certifications through technology support. Legal and executive administrative assistants graduate with experience through the internship program.

In the Medical Careers area students can prepare for professional certification exams in medical assisting or coding, gain experience through externships, and even choose a major which lends itself to working at home (medical transcription).
