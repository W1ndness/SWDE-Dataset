Seton Hall Says...

Since its founding by Bishop James Roosevelt Bayley in 1856, Seton Hall University has dedicated itself to providing "a home for the mind, the heart, and the spirit."

FOR THE MIND: We offer more than 60 majors and concentrations taught by 860 full-time faculty members across six undergraduate schools with practical, real-world emphasis...Since its founding by Bishop James Roosevelt Bayley in 1856, Seton Hall University has dedicated itself to providing "a home for the mind, the heart, and the spirit."

FOR THE MIND: We offer more than 60 majors and concentrations taught by 860 full-time faculty members across six undergraduate schools with practical, real-world emphasis. FOR THE HEART: We offer a compassionate, diverse and collaborative environment that focuses on academic and ethical development. FOR THE SPIRIT: Our faith and spiritual commitments inspire faculty to educate students to become servant leaders in their personal and professional lives, and in a diverse society.

"There is pride and excitement in being part of the Seton Hall community. There's a spirit, a passion for the place."
Leonard Jones
Political Science and English
Hillsdale, NJ

Seton Hall's campus spans 58 acres and is home to 5,300 undergraduate students, with 82 percent of its freshmen living on campus. While Seton Hall certainly enjoys a reputation of being a large institution, our campus community is actually close-knit and inclusive. Students receive personal attention with an average class size of 25 to 30 students and a student-to-faculty ratio of 14:1. Students, faculty and staff come from around the world, representing all 50 states and 67 countries, creating a diverse yet unified campus environment. More Less
