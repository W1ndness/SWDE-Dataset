The Basics About Arkansas State

Arkansas State is a public institution that was founded in 1909. It has a total undergraduate enrollment of 10.024, its setting is urban, and the campus size is 1.474 acres. It utilizes a semester-based academic calendar. Arkansas State's ranking in the 2011 edition of Best Colleges is Regional Universities (South), 58. Its in-state tuition and fees are $6,640; out-of-state tuition and fees are $14,860.

Mission (as provided by the school)  

Overview Arkansas State University-Jonesboro began its centennial year in 2009, celebrating 100 years of powering the minds of students. It began in Jonesboro, Ark. in 1909 as an agricultural high school and has since developed into a comprehensive university offering top-quality academic programs to students from throughout the United States and the world. More than 12,000 students attend the university, which includes students from 58 foreign countries. Academics Arkansas State University-Jonesboro's 11 colleges offer 42 degree programs and 153 fields of study at the doctoral, specialist, master's, bachelor's and associate degree levels. The university is the state's leading provider of nursing graduates, early childhood educators and agriculture business graduates. In addition, ASUJ has top-ranked programs in journalism, radio-television and speech communication. Degree programs in nursing and health professions are greatly in demand, with ASUJ having clinical affiliations with more than 500 healthcare facilities. ASUJ offers management education including international business, technology, entrepreneurship, and economic development through action-based learning. Most of ASUJ's faculty hold the highest possible degrees and are recognized leaders in their fields. ASUJ also has the state-of-the-art Arkansas Biosciences Institute, where undergraduates can conduct cutting-edge research. Student Life Students will find more than 100 campus organizations, 17 men's and women's intramural sports, 17 national Greek organizations, seven men's intercollegiate athletic programs and nine women's intercollegiate sports. There's always something to do at ASUJ, whether it's just hanging with friends at the Student Union, grabbing a bite at the food court, watching a current movie in the on-campus theatre, working out in a fitness center, catching an athletic event or seeing exciting programs and concerts. Living on campus at ASUJ gives you the chance for lifelong friendships and easy access to campus. You'll find monitored card access, 24-hour reception desks, high-speed Internet, cable, personal telephone and voicemail, laundry facilities and computer labs. ASUJ also offers convenient meal plans. From traditional dorms to garden apartments, each residence hall at ASUJ offers a unique living environment, including first-year residence experience, co-ed and single-sex halls, sorority suites and family housing. ASUJ also offers the Honors Living-Learning Community (HLLC). The HLLC is composed of three separate residential buildings and an Honors classroom that creates a distinct sense of identity for Honors students, while signifying ASUJ's continued commitment to quality education. The HLLC lets Honors students thrive in a residential environment designed with their educational pursuits in mind. Students will have "one howl of an experience" at ASUJ. See student videos and information on financial aid, including ASUJ's Arkansas Promise Scholarship at http://www.onehowlofanexperience.com/. Athletics The ASUJ Red Wolves compete in NCAA Division I-A, the top level, with 16 men's and women's intercollegiate sports. Men's sports include baseball, basketball, cross country, football, golf and track. Women's sports include basketball, bowling, cross country, golf, soccer, tennis, track and volleyball.
