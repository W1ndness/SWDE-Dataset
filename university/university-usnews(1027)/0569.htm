The Basics About SUNY Plattsburgh

SUNY Plattsburgh is a public institution that was founded in 1889. It has a total undergraduate enrollment of 5,906, its setting is suburban, and the campus size is 300 acres. It utilizes a semester-based academic calendar. SUNY Plattsburgh's ranking in the 2011 edition of Best Colleges is Regional Universities (North), 84. Its in-state tuition and fees are $6,102; out-of-state tuition and fees are $14,002.

Mission (as provided by the school)  

Mission: The State University of New York College at Plattsburgh prepares a diverse population of undergraduate and graduate students for a wide range of careers based on a strong foundation in the liberal arts. The college is a selective public institution offering a high-quality and affordable education. SUNY Plattsburgh supports students in the discovery and cultivation of their potential for leadership in their chosen careers and communities. We challenge students to think, learn, and excel through both close interactions with an outstanding faculty and staff and participation in rewarding experiential learning opportunities. We are committed to academic excellence, ethical values, lifelong learning, and responsible citizenship in a sustainable global community. SUNY Plattsburgh contributes to the cultural enrichment and economic development of the Northeastern New York region. Attributes: Our distinctive location, near Lake Champlain, the Adirondack Mountains, and Canada, is embraced in academic programs, recreational activities, and community involvement. For instance, through our connection with the W. H. Miner Institute, students have access to 8,600 acres of forests and agricultural land to use as an outdoor laboratory, working on issues like preserving the Adirondack Park, as they study earth and environmental science. Others work in the Lake Champlain Research Institute, where they fight invasive species, identify insects and more. They have even helped develop and write the computer code for an Algal Bloom Mapper, which locates potentially hazardous algae blooms in the lake. The Expeditionary Studies program also makes use of the landscape to teach advanced skills in rock and ice climbing, back-country skiing, and sea kayaking. These lessons are combined with materials from different disciplines, including history, leadership, land management, environmental science, ethics, and education. Proximity to Canada makes the college a perfect place to study the growing field of global supply chain management. In fact, the college was the first in the SUNY system to offer this program. The Plattsburgh State Art Museum is comprised of over 4,600 works of art and artifacts ranging from antiquities to contemporary art. Through its Museum Without Walls, students are able to view art across the campus. The college also houses what may be the largest collection of skeletal remains from the ancient Maya, housed with permission from the Belize government. Combined with a DNA sequencer, these allow for unique opportunities to unlock mysteries of the past. Dr. Nancy Elwess, winner of the Presidential Award for Excellence in Science, Mathematics and Engineering Mentoring, has been working with undergraduates on this and more. For five years in a row, her students have won national and international awards for this work. In biology, our students founded the first-ever student chapter of the Botanical Society of America and won awards from that group. One student traveled to Australia with faculty where she helped them discover a new plant species. Our students are also winning awards for video and audio productions. They won 20 out of 34 regional awards and three national awards from the National Broadcasting Society this year. Our students take part in unique experiences through our Spectrum clinics. These allow them to apply classroom learning to a clinical setting and do meaningful research in speech and hearing, Alzheimer's, traumatic brain injury and autism. SUNY Plattsburgh has a long tradition as a teachers college, with education alumni recently winning awards from the president of the United States, the New York State Department of Education, the School Administrators Association and more. Now, our redesigned program has reset the bar, adjusting it to the needs of the 21st century, with an emphasis on inquiry-based teaching and programs in which students may be working in schools the first semester of their freshman year.
