The Basics About Mary Washington

Mary Washington is a public institution that was founded in 1908. It has a total undergraduate enrollment of 4,397, its setting is suburban, and the campus size is 176 acres. It utilizes a semester-based academic calendar. Mary Washington's ranking in the 2011 edition of Best Colleges is Regional Universities (South), 13. Its in-state tuition and fees are $7,862; out-of-state tuition and fees are $19,590.

Mission (as provided by the school)  

The University of Mary Washington, long a highly respected and sought after institution for pursuit of academic excellence in the liberal arts, has this year added new colleges of business and education to complement its existing college of arts and sciences. Solidly grounded in the liberal arts tradition, students in all three colleges have the opportunity to work directly with professors to achieve their academic and career goals. The UMW experience is based on a culture of honor, individual recognition, and dedication to community and global service, and the university places great value on diversity within its student body. An education at UMW, with multiple opportunities for student research and service learning, prepares graduates for outstanding careers or for entry into graduate school. A wide range of programs includes strong majors in political science and international affairs, English, biology, psychology, earth and environmental science, history, visual and performing arts, economics, and business. Other distinctive offerings include historic preservation, a concentration in creative writing, and one of the top debate programs in the country. With its classic Jeffersonian-inspired architecture, beautifully manicured central campus walk, spacious grounds, and residential dorms located amidst traditional lecture halls, UMW truly conveys the uniquely American college experience. At the same time, renovation of heritage dorms, classrooms, performance centers, and new construction of a 624-bed residential community and 2,000-seat convocation center provide students with superb facilities. The campus is centrally located halfway between Washington, D.C. and Richmond, Va., making access to national monuments, museums, government facilities, and vibrant social scenes just a short trip away. These two capitals also serve as a rich resource for undergraduate student internships as well as a promising job market for graduates. Historic Fredericksburg, Va., the university's host city, abounds with interesting shops, great restaurants, quaint bookstores and coffee shops, all typical of the college scene and within easy walking distance from campus. One of Virginia's newest and largest modern shopping and entertainment complexes also is nearby. The campus itself is brimming with activity throughout the school year. More than 100 student organizations and clubs not only provide opportunity for leadership but also are active in arranging events for the entire student body. Whether at the new "Underground" snack bar and coffee house, on the lush green at Ball Circle, or at spirit rock near Campus Center, there is always something going on. UMW Eagles teams regularly compete at the championship level in NCAA Division III, and there are numerous intramural and club sporting activities available for students to compete at all levels. Getting to and from the UMW campus and around the area is easy. The local commuter bus service, FRED, serves the campus and the surrounding area, the AMTRAK station is located just downtown, and international airports in Washington D.C. and Richmond connect the university with the rest of the world. UMW students are bright, multitalented, and involved. Accomplished professors provide individualized attention to their students, and the university environment is friendly and welcoming.

Student Reviews Reviews provided by:

The best things about UMW are the amazing friends you will make, the professors who always have their doors open, and the beautiful campus. In my opinion, UMW is just the right size. It's small enough so that you can run into your friends strolling down campus walk and large enough for you to comment to your friends that you "have never seen any of these people in your life" when different students show up at a school sponsored event or activity. When I tell someone who is not familiar with our school that I go to Mary Washington, the response I get most often is, "Oh isn't that an all girls school?" Occasionally I get responses something like "Oh now that's a very good school. You must be really smart." I spend most of my time in Melchers Hall, which is the studio art and art history building, and in Chandler Hall, which is the Business building. A popular pasttime for many students, however, is benchsitting along campus walk and hanging out around the fountain. I think Fredericksburg is slowly becoming more of a college town. I wouldn't have considered it a college town when I first went to Mary Washington, but with the University's purchase of more land to build residence halls and shops geared toward students, the area will definitely become more student friendly. The bars downtown are also not known for being centralized hang-out spots for students either and usually cater to an older crowd. The administration is probably the largest downfall of the university. With the exception of those faculty who make a point to get to know students like acting President Richard Hurley, Dean of Student Life Cedric Rucker, and Vice President of Student Affairs Bernard Chirico, the administration and the amount of red-tape students have to go through to get anything done are the biggest complaint causers among students. Some unusual things about UMW are that we don't have a football team and there is no Greek life at UMW. There is a decent amount of school pride considering we don't have a football team. Students line up to participate in school sponsored cook-outs and events such as Devil/Goat Day, an annual campus tradition where the even-numbered classes compete against the odd-numbered classes. Of course the line is probably more for the free food and tee-shirts ;) Homecoming weekend always show Eagle pride at it's best and the annual Underwear Run down campus walk is also rapidly gaining popularity.

Heather , Alum University of Mary Washington
