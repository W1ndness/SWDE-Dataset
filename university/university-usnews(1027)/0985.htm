The Basics About Brooklyn College

Brooklyn College is a public institution that was founded in 1930. It has a total undergraduate enrollment of 13,069, its setting is urban, and the campus size is 26 acres. It utilizes a semester-based academic calendar. Brooklyn College's ranking in the 2011 edition of Best Colleges is Regional Universities (North), 61. Its in-state tuition and fees are $5,051; out-of-state tuition and fees are $12,901.

Mission (as provided by the school)  

Founded in 1930, Brooklyn College is one of the eleven senior colleges of the City University of New York. The College has been consistently ranked for the past six years by Princeton Review as among America???s Best Value Colleges. In Princeton Review's 2009 guidebook, The Best 368 Colleges, Brooklyn College was ranked third in the nation for diversity. Located in what many consider to be the most culturally vibrant borough in the city, Brooklyn College is a comprehensive liberal arts institution with nationally recognized programs and world-class faculty in the arts and sciences, education, and business. The student body reflects the city???s rich ethnic and cultural diversity and global connections. The college has continued to raise entrance requirements, supports vibrant honors programs and numerous student research opportunities, and provides robust and ongoing advisement for all students. The College enrolls more than 16,000 undergraduate and graduate students. Over the last eight years, the college has appointed some 273 new full-time faculty members, more than half of its full-time teaching faculty, who hold higher degrees from Harvard, Stanford, Yale, Oxford, and similarly prestigious universities. Among them are Pulitzer Prize-winners, Fulbright and Guggenheim Fellows, and national grant researchers. What attracts students to the College are its innovative programs, strong internship opportunities, and extensive study-abroad selections. The College's admissions criteria are increasingly selective, and high-achieving high school students apply in ever-larger numbers for admission to the Macaulay Honors College at Brooklyn College, where they join other strong students who make up the College's prestigious Honors Academy, a federation of six honors programs. Perhaps the best measure of an excellent college is the success of its students. The College ranks nineteenth nationally in the number of undergraduates who have gone on to receive Ph.D.'s. The Core Curriculum is the College's signature program. Long a national model, it was revised and strengthened in 2006 and offers students an unrivaled general education. The On-Course Advantage (TOCA) enables dedicated students to graduate in the traditional four years by giving them priority at registration, guaranteed access to required courses, and one-on-one advising throughout their course of study. Brooklyn College is a student-centered campus; it has expanded its Center for Academic Advisement and Student Success in order to make individual, pro-active advising available to everyone. As a public institution, Brooklyn College takes seriously its public responsibilities. It has actively broadened its engagement with the surrounding neighborhood and with Brooklyn's cultural and social organizations and is most proud of its partnership with high schools throughout the borough. A major capital construction and renovation program is currently in progress at Brooklyn College, more than $500 million allocated to the College by New York State in capital funds. The West Quad Building, the first new building on campus since the 1970s, opened in fall 2009 and houses extensive student services and state-of-the-art physical education and athletic facilities. A new performing arts center is due to begin construction later this year, and a new science complex is on the drawing boards of well-known architectural firms.
