The Raps have been in three straight blowout games, so it's tough to read the rotations, but with the wing so crowded in Toronto owners need to be quick to move on if a guy isn't getting it done. Weems falls into that category right now.
Nov. 26, 2010 - 11:59 p.m. ET
Sonny Weems missed all nine of his shots and failed to score in 18 minutes on Friday.
He played 35 minutes in his last one but still scored just eight points, after scoring in double digits in eight straight before that game. Things are getting very crowded on the wings in Toronto and our guess is different guys are going to take turns having big lines every night. In other words, Weems may be too inconsistent going forward to keep on your roster.
Nov. 21, 2010 - 4:13 p.m. ET
Sonny Weems started and played 36 minutes on Sunday, going 8-of-20 from the field to score 16 points with five rebounds, one assist and two turnovers.
The most important thing to take from Sunday is that his minutes were not impacted by the return of Leandro Barbosa, who played in his first game since Nov. 8. At this juncture, Weems is clearly the guy to own between Barbosa and Linas Kleiza. Kleiza came off the bench on Sunday and scored just two points with three rebounds in 21 minutes.
Nov. 17, 2010 - 10:23 p.m. ET
Sonny Weems got another start over Linas Kleiza and scored a career-high 25 points on 10-of-18 shooting in 42 minutes on Wednesday.
He added four boards, two assists and a 3-pointer on the night, and may have permanently locked up the starting job over Kleiza (1-of-7, three points). If Weems is available in your league, he should be picked up immediately after starting and hitting double figures in his sixth straight game.
Nov. 17, 2010 - 6:50 p.m. ET
Sonny Weems will make another start for Linas Kleiza, who is back and healthy for the Raptors.
OK, we're going to go ahead and bump Weems in front of Kleiza on the depth chart, but this is still anyone's job. If you were on the fence about picking up the red-hot Weems, this could be your sign to go ahead and do it.
