Dec. 2, 2010 - 1:06 a.m. ET
James Harden had 16 points, seven rebounds, three assists, two steals and a 3-pointer on 4-of-11 shooting as the Thunder held off the Nets in triple-overtime Wednesday.
Harden came off the bench and was helped by two key factors: An extra 15 minutes of action and the absence of Durant. Once Durant is healthy and the Thunder go back to playing 48-minute games, Harden's value will plummet.
Nov. 23, 2010 - 12:44 a.m. ET
James Harden went back to his bench role on Monday, scoring five points on 2-of-6 shooting (including a three) with one rebound, two assists, and three steals in 18 minutes.
He's back to being waiver wire fodder, but is worth keeping an eye on if he starts to heat up.
Nov. 21, 2010 - 2:57 a.m. ET
James Harden set season-bests in scoring, rebounding, and 3-pointers made on Saturday, with 23 points on 6-of-13 shooting (6-of-8 from downtown) with nine rebounds, four assists, and a steal in his spot-start for injured Kevin Durant (ankle).
We wish we could say that this would buy him some more playing time, but in reality we can't. He will return to his bench role once Durant returns, and then he will need to improve his defense and stay consistent to start earning consistent playing time.
Nov. 19, 2010 - 10:21 p.m. ET
James Harden moved into the starting lineup on Friday for injured Kevin Durant and had 12 points, six rebounds, four assists, a steal and a 3-pointer in the win.
He's got talent, but as long as Durant, Jeff Green and Russell Westbrook are healthy, he's going to struggle.
