Sep. 27, 2010 - 8:22 p.m. ET
Kendrick Perkins, who thinks he's ahead of schedule as he rehabs from serious knee surgery, said that he fully expects to get his starting job back when he returns to action.
"My spot is not up for grabs," he said. "So when I come back I will be in my spot, everybody else will just have to adjust to me after that." While there's a chance the O'Neal boys will be injured when Perkins finally returns from his injury, we doubt he's just automatically inserted into the first unit. And given the fact he's expected to miss about half the season, we don't see any reason to draft him in fantasy leagues, either.

Aug. 22, 2010 - 10:13 a.m. ET
Celtics center Kendrick Perkins (ACL surgery) hopes to resume playing by January, but the team doesn't expect him back until February or March.
Perkins can't run or do any basketball activities, but the swelling in his leg has gone down and he's working hard to stay relatively in shape. To his credit, he praised the team for acquiring Shaq and didn't say anything negative about the possibility that he won't have his starting job when he gets back.
Source: Boston Globe
Aug. 14, 2010 - 6:15 p.m. ET
Celtics GM Danny Ainge said this week that Kendrick Perkins will have to earn his starting job back once he returns from ACL surgery.
The addition of Jermaine O'Neal and Shaq gives Boston a surplus of slow-footed centers, and Perkins may not even be ready to play until February. On the bright side, Rajon Rondo said that Perkins is "trying to take care of his body. He's getting a lot of treatment on his knee. He's actually walking. He's in good spirits. He's a warrior."
