Nov. 19, 2010 - 4:14 p.m. ET
A league source said on Thursday that the Trail Blazers will pursue a 'Disabled Player Exception' following Greg Oden's season ending knee surgery.
Such an exception requires NBA approval and can be used to sign a healthy replacement player, or accept salary in a trade. As noted by BlazersEdge.com, "If the Blazers wanted to use the exception to sign a free agent, they would have to waive a player (probably Sean Marks?) to create a roster spot."
Source: BlazersEdge
Nov. 18, 2010 - 10:58 a.m. ET
The Blazers may not extend a qualifying offer to Greg Oden, speculates CBSSports' Ken Berger.
The Blazers have until June 30 to make the $8.8 million qualifying offer, so there will be plenty of time to monitor Oden's rehab. But that is an awfully large number to give someone that has now had three season-ending knee injuries. It's a brutal decision for the Blazers, who may be forced to simply give up on the No. 1 overall pick in the 2007 draft.
Source: cbssports.com
Nov. 17, 2010 - 10:30 p.m. ET
Greg Oden will undergo microfracture surgery on his left knee and miss the entire 2010-11 season.
The bad luck continues for Oden and the Blazers, so go ahead and drop him if you were holding him in hopes of a comeback. Marcus Camby and Joel Przybilla will hold down the center minutes for the Blazers the rest of the way - that is, once Przybilla returns from his own knee surgeries. It's tough not to feel bad for Oden and the Blazers, as Kevin Durant continues his dominant ways in OKC.
