Dec. 7, 2010 - 12:14 a.m. ET
Darren Collison left Monday's game with five minutes to go with a leg or foot injury, and did not return, finishing with 17 points on 6-of-7 shooting with three rebounds, one assist, and one steal in 25 minutes in the Pacers' 124-100 blowout win over the Raptors.
There was no reason for him to be in the game that late, so Jim O'Brien will have some egg on his face if this is serious, and at this time multiple post-game reports have not touched on any injury. If not for these factors, we would be more aggressive in telling owners to grab T.J. Ford, but for now he should not be picked up for anybody of value.
Dec. 4, 2010 - 4:16 a.m. ET
Darren Collison had just five points, two rebounds, four assists and five turnovers in Friday's loss to the Suns.
He was benched in favor of T.J. Ford due to his turnovers, and the Pacers committed 24 of them tonight. Blame it on Steve Nash, but look for Collison to turn it around on Monday against the Raptors. That is, as long as Jim O'Brien hasn't soured on his top offseason acquisition.
Nov. 23, 2010 - 10:17 p.m. ET
Darren Collison started at PG and played through an ankle injury on Tuesday, scoring 18 points on 5-of-11 shooting (including a three) with six rebounds, seven assists, and a steal in 34 minutes.
The big question has been about T.J. Ford pushing him for playing time, and it may have been answered tonight with Ford's 14-minute outing that yielded no points, no rebounds, and two assists. If you took the risk and bought low on Collison you might have pulled a fast one.
Nov. 23, 2010 - 7:28 p.m. ET
Darren Collison started on Tuesday against the Cavs, one day after returning from an ankle injury that Pacers blog Indy Cornrows said he tweaked in Monday's game.
At least he has a tiny excuse for getting outplaying by T.J. Ford in Monday's game. At any rate, he's starting and gets a chance to redeem himself. Collison's value could change a good amount, depending on how things turn out tonight.
