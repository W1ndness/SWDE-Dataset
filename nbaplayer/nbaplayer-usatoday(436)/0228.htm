Dec. 7, 2010 - 1:39 a.m. ET
Jeff Green and Kevin Durant were benched for most of the third quarter in Monday's loss to the Bulls, but Green's fantasy line was hurt the worst as he finished with just seven points on 1-of-9 shooting with six rebounds and two steals in 33 minutes.
Durant scored 29 points on 7-of-18 shooting (14-of-14 from the foul line) with five rebounds, two steals, and two blocks, but both played without energy and smarts to the extent that Scott Brooks made the move, saying he "needed a spark." The Bulls were +10 with them off the court, but the message was sent and Durant, for one, endorsed the move after the game. We wouldn't get too worried about this as it relates to either player just yet.

Dec. 2, 2010 - 1:00 a.m. ET
Jeff Green hit 12-of-21 shots and four 3-pointers for a season-high 37 points to go along with five rebounds and four assists in Wednesday's triple-OT win over the Nets.
He clearly benefitted from the absence of Kevin Durant (sprained knee), but is a fine fantasy player when Durant is healthy. His three clutch free throws sent the game to a third overtime, when Russell Westbrook took over the game and won it for the Thunder.
Nov. 30, 2010 - 12:16 a.m. ET
After playing through his ongoing ankle injury in Sunday's game with 15 points and seven boards in 39 minutes, Jeff Green followed it up with a similar outing Monday, scoring 13 points on 4-of-14 shooting with seven rebounds, one assist, and one block in 41 minutes.
The numbers aren't eye-popping, but the result is good enough for owners hoping that they can put his ankle injury in the rear-view mirror. He gets a day off before Wednesday's game against the Nets.
Nov. 28, 2010 - 6:39 p.m. ET
Thunder beat writer Darnell Mayberry reports that Jeff Green (ankle) will be in the starting lineup for Sunday's game against the Rockets.
Green aggravated his ankle injury on Friday, but is ready to go for Sunday. Nenad Krstic (back) has been ruled out, which means more run for Serge Ibaka and Nick Collison.
Source: Darnell Mayberry on Twitter
Nov. 28, 2010 - 5:13 a.m. ET
Jeff Green, who aggravated his previously-injured ankle in Friday's game, missed Saturday's practice and is questionable for Sunday's game.
"He was feeling pretty good (Saturday)," said Scott Brooks. "It's day-to-day. We'll see how he feels (Sunday) and then make the decision." Serge Ibaka would get the start in his place if he can't go. Nenad Krstic (back) also said he hopes to play, with Nick Collison starting if he can't.
