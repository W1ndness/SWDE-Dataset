Austin Daye G/F Detroit Pistons
Height: 6'11'   Weight: 205   DOB: 6/5/1988  Age: 22
Dec. 8, 2010 - 1:36 a.m. ET
Austin Daye got 18 minutes of run with Charlie Villanueva (personal) out on Tuesday, and unsurprisingly hit 4-of-6 shots from the field (2-of-2 from downtown) on his way to 12 points, one rebound, and not much else.
He has now hit 18-of-37 (49%) of his 3-balls, and John Kuester gets roasted on a daily basis by anybody covering the team for his inability to find time for the preseason MIP candidate. We understand if you don't have space, but he's a solid roster stash knowing the Pistons will likely move their vets and go young as the losses mount -- even if it is months too late.

Nov. 22, 2010 - 9:52 a.m. ET
After Austin Daye picked up a DNP-CD on Sunday, Pistons beat writer Dave Pemberton wrote, "There is no reason [Daye] should not leave the bench for a Pistons team clearly in rebuild mode (even if they won't admit it)."
Amen. Daye was benched in favor of Jason Maxiell for matchup purposes, and Detroit did win the game, but we're not sure why he couldn't even crack the rotation. Pemberton goes on to call Daye "a matchup nightmare...He's a 6-11 guy that can handle the ball and shoot the 3 (51.7 percent this season)." If you can afford to keep him stashed away, it should pay off when (or if) Daye routinely plays starters minutes.
