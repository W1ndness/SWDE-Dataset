Dec. 4, 2010 - 11:50 p.m. ET
Mo Williams and the Cavs saw their franchise fall further into the gutter on Saturday, when the lowly Wolves ran them out of the gym by the score of 129-95.
Mo had four points with four assists in 19 minutes, Anderson Varejao had four points and three boards, and Anthony Parker had five points, four boards, and three steals. How Bryon Scott keeps his job if this continues is anybody's guess.

When LeBron left Cleveland, he essentially said that the players on the current roster weren't good enough, and the Cavaliers proved him right on Thursday. J.J. Hickson was equally incompetent as Williams, hitting 3-of-9 shots to score six points with four rebounds and two turnovers. Nov. 27, 2010 - 11:19 p.m. ET
Mo Williams hit a clutch 10-footer with 6.5 seconds left in the Cavs' 92-86 win on Saturday, and also passed out a season-high 12 assists to go with 25 points on 10-of-21 shooting, five rebounds, four treys, two steals, and a block.
He has answered the bell for fantasy owners with a big week, and hopefully he has turned the corner from both the injury and personal issues he has struggled with to start the year.
