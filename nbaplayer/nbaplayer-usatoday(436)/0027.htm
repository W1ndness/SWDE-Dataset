Nov. 28, 2010 - 12:04 a.m. ET
C.J. Watson left Saturday's game during the fourth quarter after dislocating his left finger and may not return.
He had logged just six minutes with only an assist to his credit, so needless to say his one-game run as a fantasy stud has come to an end.
Nov. 27, 2010 - 2:20 a.m. ET
C.J. Watson started for injured Derrick Rose on Friday and hit 11-of-22 shots and 9-of-12 free throws on his way to 33 points, three rebounds, three assists, two steals and two 3-pointers.
He was fantastic, but was mysteriously pulled late for John Lucas, who was signed just before the game. Lucas missed a pair of free throws and then Carmelo Anthony ended the game with a clutch jumper, the 14th game-winner of his career. Tom Thibodeau said he pulled Watson in order to get a good free throw shooter in the game, but it was simply a terrible call. Lucas was cold and has been sitting at home since training camp, while Watson was hot and had only missed three of his 12 free throw attempts on the night. Tough loss for the road-weary Bulls. If Rose misses any more games, Watson is worth a pick up for a one-game start.
Oct. 16, 2010 - 10:59 p.m. ET
C.J. Watson got 30 minutes of run in the Bulls' blowout loss to the Magic on Saturday, and scored 13 points with three rebounds, four assists, and a steal.
If you glanced at the box score and saw the 30 minutes, disregard them. He's buried behind Derrick Rose, Ronnie Brewer, Kyle Korver, and even Keith Bogans on the depth chart. He needs injuries to matter in fantasy leagues this year.
Oct. 13, 2010 - 11:30 a.m. ET
C.J. Watson (quad) returned to action on Tuesday night.
Watson missed two games with that sore left quad. He came off the bench for 12 points in 12 minutes. He'll need injuries in front of him to be a fantasy factor.
