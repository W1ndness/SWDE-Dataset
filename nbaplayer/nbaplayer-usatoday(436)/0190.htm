Nov. 27, 2010 - 3:50 p.m. ET
Jamal Crawford hit 8-of-18 shots and three 3-pointers on his way to 21 points and three assists in a tough win at New York.
Crawford, who could end up playing for the Knicks if the Hawks don't agree to extend his contract, had a lot of open looks today and hit several daggers to stop Knicks runs. He's scored 21 in his last two games and is averaging 19 ppg over his last three, meaning he might be heating up for Atlanta, who has now won two straight after losing three in a row.
Nov. 23, 2010 - 11:00 p.m. ET
Jamal Crawford busted out of his shooting slump with 21 points off the bench on Tuesday, hitting 8-of-14 shots from the field (3-of-3 from deep) with five rebounds, three assists, and a steal in 37 minutes.
Hopefully this is the spark that gets him going, because he has been ice-cold to start the year, whether that be due to the new motion offense, his contract situation, or the time off with a back injury. We've called him a buy low candidate, and that window could be shutting soon.
Nov. 22, 2010 - 4:08 p.m. ET
Jamal Crawford, who is averaging just 13.3 points per game after averaging 18 last season, says that his unresolved contract extension is partly responsible for his poor play.
"I think with everything going on, I'm just thinking too much and not just playing basketball," he said. "I'd be lying if I said it hasn't crossed my mind, you know, the future...Everything's been a little bit of a distraction." Crawford is making more than $10 million this season and would become an urestricted free agent if the Hawks don't extend him.
