Dec. 8, 2010 - 3:05 a.m. ET
Gilbert Arenas returned to the starting lineup in Tuesday's loss to the Lakers, and scored 23 points on 8-of-17 shooting (2-of-8 from downtown) with two rebounds, four assists, two steals, and a whopping seven turnovers in 38 minutes of action.
He slowed down to start the month of December with just a 12-point, five-assist average entering tonight, so tonight's result is a welcome sight. The loss drops the Wizards to 0-11 on the road, but a game in Sacramento on Wednesday could get them off the schneid.
Dec. 6, 2010 - 9:43 a.m. ET
Gilbert Arenas admitted that his new role off the Wizards bench is still "a learning process."
"I'm better with the ball," Arenas said. "I'm trying to learn to play without the ball." He's had ups and downs this season, but he's finally healthy which is more than half the battle in fantasy leagues.
Source: Washington Post
Dec. 1, 2010 - 10:36 p.m. ET
Gilbert Arenas hit just 1-of-10 shots for seven points as the Wizards were blown out by the Raptors on Wednesday.
Arenas had been rolling over the last couple weeks, but this was just not his night. Nor was it the Wizards' night, who were soundly beaten by a team coming in with a 6-11 record. The Wizards are now 0-8 on the road and it will be interesting to see when, or if, they get their first.
Dec. 1, 2010 - 5:08 p.m. ET
Wizards owner Ted Leonsis flatly denied rumors that the Wizards are attempting to trade Gilbert Arenas.
In addition to saying that the rumor "isn't true," Leonsis said, "The team is trying to rebuild and Gilbert is one of the leaders on the team. Gilbert is the best player on the team right now." Whether he's actually on the block or not, fantasy owners are reminded once more that testing Arenas' trade value while he's playing well is a wise idea given his history.
