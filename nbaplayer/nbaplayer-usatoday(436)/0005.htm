Aug. 10, 2010 - 11:01 a.m. ET
Celtics forward Luke Harangody officially signed his rookie-scale contract on Tuesday.
Harangody will come off the Celtics' bench and won't have any fantasy value in average leagues. His signing moves Boston to the maximum 15 player roster, though another spot could open up whenever Rasheed Wallace retires.
Source: Celtics via Twitter
Jul. 25, 2010 - 10:40 a.m. ET
Celtics second-round pick Luke Harangody will likely make the roster, as GM Danny Ainge was thrilled with his play at Summer League.
Ainge compared the Notre Dame product to other second-round players he has drafted, including Glen Davis, Leon Powe, and Ryan Gomes. The 6'8" Harangody showed an ability to shoot from the outside, which could seal the deal.
Source: Boston Globe
Jun. 24, 2010 - 11:37 p.m. ET
Celtics selected Notre Dame PF Luke Harangody with the No. 52 pick in the draft.
One of the most productive players in college basketball over the last two seasons, Gody could have a chance to replace Brian Scalabrine. But don't expect anything huge -- Gody's game doesn't translate to the NBA.
