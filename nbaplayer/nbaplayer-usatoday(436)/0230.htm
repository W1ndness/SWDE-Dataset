Nov. 30, 2010 - 12:27 a.m. ET
Russell Westbrook scored 25 points on 9-of-19 shooting with five rebounds, 11 assists, five steals, and eight turnovers in the Thunder's 95-89 win over the Hornets on Monday.
He keyed a 14-0 run in the fourth quarter, scoring 12 of his 25 points during that time. Hornets coach Monty Williams called him an All Star, saying he "plays at a level that causes teams to scout him 10-15 minutes during practice." Thabo Sefolosha tied a season-high with 13 points, five boards, two steals, two treys, and a block on perfect 4-of-4 shooting in the win.

Nov. 28, 2010 - 9:39 p.m. ET
The Thunder got 23 points and 10 assists from Russell Westbrook on Sunday, but their comeback bid fell just short as they lost in Houston 97-98.
Westbrook had four rebounds, two steals and zero turnovers in 36 minutes. He has established himself as one of the NBA's premier point guards, and from a fantasy perspective he's been a top-20 player thus far. Fortunately, there's no reason to think he can't keep up this blistering pace.
Nov. 27, 2010 - 1:15 a.m. ET
Russell Westbrook hit 13-of-24 shots and 17-of-18 free throws on his way to a career-high 43 points to go along with eight rebounds, eight assists, three steals and a block in Friday's overtime win at Indiana.
Yeah, he missed his only 3-point attempt, but after the numbers Westbrook has put up in the first month of the season, we don't expect to be hearing many more complaints about his lack of threes going forward. If you slept on Westbrook on draft night because of the lack of threes and poor shooting (43% this season), don't do it again next year.
Nov. 19, 2010 - 10:23 p.m. ET
Russell Westbrook committed seven turnovers and missed all seven of his field goal attempts in the fourth quarter, but still led the Thunder to a tough win at Boston with 31 points, four rebounds, six assists, two steals and two 3-pointers Friday.
What a win for the Thunder, who were without Kevin Durant and Jeff Green, who are nursing ankle injuries. Westbrook is a fantasy force this season and will be for many years to come.
