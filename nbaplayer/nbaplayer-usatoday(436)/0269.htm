Dante Cunningham F Portland Trail Blazers

Nov. 19, 2010 - 2:05 a.m. ET
Dante Cunningham played 19 minutes off the bench on Thursday, recording just one point, two rebounds and one assist.
The Blazers' big-man shortage has forced Cunningham into 15-25 minutes per game for most of this season, which is about 15-25 minutes per game more than he would be logging for almost any other NBA team. He has done little with his opportunity, and he'll become a fantasy afterthought once Joel Przybilla (knee) returns next week.

Dante Cunningham started again for Marcus Camby (hip) on Monday, and finished with eight points on 4-of-7 shooting with four rebounds, one assist, two steals, and a block in 26 minutes.

Oct. 17, 2010 - 5:08 a.m. ET
Dante Cunningham got another spot start for injured Marcus Camby (hip) on Saturday, and scored 10 points on 4-of-9 shooting with eight rebounds and two assists in 39 minutes.
By the time the season starts the Blazers will have a few more options at their disposal, so Cunningham probably won't be needed by then. But with all of the Blazers' bigs wearing the injury-prone label keep him in mind for spot duty during the year.
Oct. 9, 2010 - 12:38 a.m. ET
Dante Cunningham continued to benefit from the lack of viable big men options in Portland on Friday, scoring 15 points on 7-of-11 shooting with 10 rebounds and four steals in 36 minutes.
He was literally the last man standing at center for the Blazers tonight, but when the games count he still has the walking triage unit of Marcus Camby, Greg Oden, and Joel Przybilla ahead of him. He may post a few good lines here and there, but don't waste a draft pick on him outside the deepest of leagues.
