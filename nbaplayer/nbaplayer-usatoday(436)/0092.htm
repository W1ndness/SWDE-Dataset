Jul. 29, 2010 - 11:59 a.m. ET
Wizards first-round pick Kevin Seraphin reached a buyout agreement with his French team, and is set to sign with the Wizards.
His new contract, expected to be for two years, is pending validation by the league office. The Wizards agreed to pay a $500,000 buyout to free him, and the move will put the Wizards at 12 players. Seraphin will be looked to for rebounding off the bench, but isn't likely to make much of an impact this season.
Source: Washington Post
Jun. 24, 2010 - 9:13 p.m. ET
The Bulls took big man Kevin Seraphin from France with the 17th pick in the draft. The pick used to take Seraphin is expected to go to the Wizards once the Kirk Hinrich trade goes down on July 8, so he won't play for Chicago.
He's dealing with a knee injury currently and wasn't able to work out for teams because of it, and this feels a little early to us. He's not going to make an impact for at least a year or two, so plan on ignoring him in fantasy next season.
Jun. 4, 2010 - 4:38 p.m. ET
Kevin Seraphin is dealing with a knee injury right now, but is still in the NBA Draft.
The injury will keep him sidelined for a couple weeks, meaning he won't be able to work out for teams. As of now he's still in, but could withdraw if his stock is hurt too badly by the injury.
Source: ESPN.com
Jun. 2, 2010 - 11:43 a.m. ET
Draft prospect Kevin Seraphin will miss 2-3 weeks with a minor knee injury, which essentially prevents him from working out for NBA teams.
Seraphin, who played in the French league this season, recently measured 6'9" without shoes. He weighs about 260 pounds, has a crazy 7'3" wingspan, and will likely be snapped up in the first round.
