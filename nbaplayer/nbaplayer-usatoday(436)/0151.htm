Nov. 25, 2010 - 12:55 a.m. ET
Chuck Hayes started and played 29 minutes Wednesday, finishing with 16 points, seven rebounds and five assists in a win over the Warriors.
Hayes was 4-of-5 and an amazing 8-of-8 from the line, but had a high of just eight points in November coming in. He might be worth a look in deeper leagues, but could easily lose minutes to Brad Miller without notice. Miller, who was getting solid minutes prior to this one, was not injured as far as we know, but a victim of the Rockets going small against the Warriors.
Nov. 23, 2010 - 12:27 a.m. ET
Chuck Hayes started at center for the Rockets and had eight points and eight rebounds in Monday's loss.
He started for matchup purposes with the Suns, but this is about his ceiling. We see no reason to pick him up. Brad Miller came off the bench for 12 points, six boards and four assists in 21 minutes, while Jordan Hill actually stayed out of foul trouble for 14 points and a career-high 10 rebounds. Miller is still the guy to own here with Yao Ming out, but Hill also deserves some attention. Hayes could start the rest of the way for all we care, but is still not worth owning in most fantasy leagues.
