Jeffrey (Jeff) Demarco Teague (born June 10, 1988 in Indianapolis, Indiana) is an American professional basketball player for the Atlanta Hawks of the National Basketball Association. He was selected 19th overall in the 2009 NBA Draft by the Hawks. Teague played college basketball for Wake Forest University in Winston-Salem, North Carolina. He played competitively for Pike High School in Indianapolis. Teague is known for his shooting ability and ability to drive to the basket.[1]

[edit] College career

[edit] Wake Forest

[edit] 2007–2009

As a freshman in 2007–08, he was 2nd on the team in scoring, scoring 13.9 points per game, just behind fellow freshman James Johnson's 14.6.[2] Teague was the leading scorer for the 4th ranked (as of January 26, 2009) Demon Deacons, averaging over 21 points per game. He scored a career-high 34 points on January 11, 2009 in a 92–89 victory over North Carolina.[3]

[edit] NBA career

[edit] Atlanta Hawks

[edit] 2009–present

On April 8, 2009, Teague announced that he planned to forgo his final two seasons at Wake Forest University and make himself available for the 2009 NBA Draft, where he was drafted 19th overall by the Atlanta Hawks.

[edit] Personal

Jeff Teague is the cousin of David Teague, who played for Purdue University from 2002 to 2007, and the older brother of Marquis Teague, a five star recruit who has committed to play point guard for the University of Kentucky men's basketball team starting in the 2011-2012 season.
