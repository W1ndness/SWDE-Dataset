Trevor Booker (born November 25, 1987) is an American professional basketball player. He was the starting power forward at Clemson, and now plays for the NBA's Washington Wizards.

[edit] High school career

Booker attended Union High School in Union, South Carolina. In his senior year (2005–06), Booker was named the Gatorade Player of the Year for South Carolina after averaging 21.9 points, 16.4 rebounds and 3.5 asstists per game. Booker was also named the AAA state player of the year and a first-team All-State selection. Booker left Union as its career leader in rebounding and blocked shots[1]. He was coached in High School by Joe Pitt.

Booker played AAU basketball with the South Carolina Celtics.

[edit] Collegiate career

Upon completion of his high school career, Trevor Booker signed with coach Oliver Purnell and the Clemson Tigers. He started every game of his freshman season on a team that went 25–11 and ended the season as runners-up of the 2007 National Invitation Tournament.

In his sophomore and junior seasons, Clemson had its first back-to-back NCAA appearances in nearly ten years. In Booker's junior year he led the ACC in rebounding (9.7 per game) and field goal percentage (.571), and finished second in blocked shots (2.0 per game)[2]. For his efforts, Booker was named second-team All-ACC and was named to the 2009 All-Defensive Team[3]. On the National level, he was named USBWA All-District[4] and NABC second team All-District[5]. Following his junior year, Booker announced that he would return to Clemson for his senior year[6].

He spent the summer following his junior campaign playing for Team USA in the World University Games, helping the team win a bronze medal.

[edit] NBA

He was selected by the Minnesota Timberwolves with the 23rd overall pick in the 2010 NBA Draft, and was the first senior to be picked.

The rights to Booker were traded to the Washington Wizards on draft night.
