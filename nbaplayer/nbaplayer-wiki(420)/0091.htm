Samardo Samuels (born January 9, 1989 in Trelawny) is a Jamaican professional basketball player for the Cleveland Cavaliers of the NBA. He is a 6-foot-8-inch, 265-pound power forward/center who attended college at the University of Louisville.

[edit] High school career

Samuels attended Saint Benedict's Preparatory School in Newark, New Jersey, United States. Samuels was ranked 9th in the nation by Rivals.com[1] and 2nd in the nation by Scout.com.[2] He, alongside class of 2010 star Tristan Thompson led St. Benedicts's Prep to a 24–1 record in the 2007–2008 season. At seasons end St. Benedict's was ranked #2 in the nation by USA Today.[3] He played in the two top all-American games, the McDonald's All-American Game and the Jordan Brand Classic.

[edit] College career

Samuels decided to attend the University of Louisville for college. He picked the Cardinals over Connecticut, Florida, Georgetown and North Carolina. Samuels played well while in college, starting every game that he played in and leading the 2009-10 team in scoring and rebounding. In a somewhat shocking and certainly controversial decision he decided to forgo his final two years of college eligibility and enter the 2010 NBA Draft.

[edit] Professional career

Samuels went undrafted, but played for the Chicago Bulls in the 2010 NBA Summer League.[4] He averaged 12.6 points and 7.4 rebounds. On August 17, 2010, Samuels signed a 3-year, $2.3 million dollar contract with the Cleveland Cavaliers. A portion of the deal is guaranteed.[5]
