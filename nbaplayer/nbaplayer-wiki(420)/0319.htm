Elliot Jerell Williams (born June 20, 1989 in Memphis, Tennessee) is a professional basketball player for the Portland Trail Blazers of the NBA. He was selected with the 22nd overall pick in the 2010 NBA Draft by the Blazers.

[edit] Collegiate career

Williams received an NCAA waiver in August to play immediately for the Memphis Tigers in 2009-10 after playing for the Duke Blue Devils and coach Mike Krzyzewski in 2008-09. He was and All-academic selection in 2006 and 2007. Playing in a total of 68 career games and 46 starts, Williams Averaged 11.1 points, 2.3 rebounds, and 2.2 assists.

[edit] 2008-09 season (as Freshman at Duke)

For the Blue Devils, Williams saw action in 34 of Duke's 37 games, starting in 12 contests, with 11 coming in the Blue Devils' final 12 games of the season (including ACC and NCAA Tournaments), averaging 4.2 points and 2.3 rebounds. Willams also recorded 23 assists and 21 steals, shot 44.1 percent from the field, 25 percent from the three-point arc and 50 percent from the foul line in helping lead Duke to a 30-7 overall record, an ACC Tournament crown and an NCAA Tournament Sweet 16 appearance.
