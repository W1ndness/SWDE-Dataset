Brian Scalabrine (born March 18, 1978) is an American professional basketball player who is currently playing for the Chicago Bulls. A 6 ft 9 in (2.06 m), 235 lb (107 kg; 16.8 st) forward from University of Southern California,[1] Scalabrine was selected by the New Jersey Nets in the second round (35th overall) of the 2001 NBA Draft.

He attended high school at Enumclaw High School in Enumclaw, Washington.

[edit] Career

On August 2, 2005, Scalabrine signed a five-year contract with the Boston Celtics worth $15 million.[2]

On September 21, 2010, Scalabrine agreed to a non-guaranteed contract with the Chicago Bulls.[3]

[edit] Career highs

  • Points: 29 at Golden State, on January 26, 2005[4]
  • Rebounds: 11, twice[4]
  • Assists: 5, six times[4]
  • Steals: 3, four times[4]
  • Blocks: 3, vs. New York, February 28, 2007[4]
  • Minutes played: 45, at Toronto, April 15, 2005[4]
