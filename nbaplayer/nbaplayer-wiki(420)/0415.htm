Nikola Peković (Montenegrin Cyrillic: Никола Пековић; born January 3, 1986 in Bijelo Polje, Montenegro, ) is a Montenegrin professional basketball player for the Minnesota Timberwolves of the National Basketball Association (NBA). Peković is 2.11m (6 ft 11 in)[1] tall and he plays the center position. He previously played for powerhouse Panathinaikos of the Greek League and the Euroleague.

[edit] Professional career

[edit] Europe

Peković began his basketball career playing with the Budućnost Podgorica club's junior team department in the 2001-02 season. From there, he went on to go play for KK Atlas in 2002 and he played for them until 2005, when he moved to Partizan Belgrade. With Partizan, he won 3 Serbian League Championships (2006, 2007, 2008), 2 Adriatic League Championships (2007, 2008), and the Serbian Cup in 2008. He was named the MVP of the Adriatic League Final Four in 2008.

In the summer of 2008, Peković joined the Greek League club Panathinaikos. He signed a 3 year contract worth €4.6 million euros net income. He averaged 13 points and 3.8 rebounds per game, in just over 18 minutes per contest, in helping Panathinaikos win the Euroleague 2008-09 title.

[edit] NBA

Peković was selected 31st overall in the 2008 NBA Draft by the Minnesota Timberwolves.[2] Many draft experts considered him a top 10 pick, but due to his contract situation with his team in Europe he was selected much lower. He was considered a lock to be drafted with the first pick of the second round because players selected in the second round are not subject to the rookie scale contracts of the first round. This meant that the team that drafted him would be able to pay him more than those players selected later in the first round. Due to the amount of money Pekovic was making in Europe already, he likely would not have made the jump to the NBA for the amount of money that could be paid to a late first round player. It was reported on June 30, 2010, that Pekovic had agreed with the Wolves to a 3 year, $13 million gross income deal.[3]

[edit] International career

[edit] Serbia and Montenegro national team

In 2005, while playing with the Under-21 Serbia and Montenegro national basketball team, he received the bronze medal in Russia at the FIBA Europe Under-20 Championship. At the 2006 FIBA Europe Under-20 Championship held in İzmir, he won the gold medal with his national team.

[edit] Montenegro national team

Peković is currently the captain of the senior men's Montenegrin national basketball team.
