The former No. 1 overall pick will be a restricted free agent next summer and the Blazers will be able to match any offers for him.
Analysis: The Oregonian reported Friday that the Blazers and Oden's representatives agreed there would be no extension, making the oft-injured center the first top pick since Kwame Brown not to get one at the end of his rookie contract. Teams have until Monday to reach deals with first-round picks from the 2007 draft. But Portland hasn't seen enough of Oden, who has played in only 82 games. He has been sidelined with a fractured left knee since last Dec. 5.

Analysis: Oden was arguably having the best season of his injury-prone career when he broke his right kneecap during a game last December against the Houston Rockets. The Blazers won't put a target date on his return, and neither would coach Nate McMillan.
